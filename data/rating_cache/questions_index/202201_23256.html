\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562075\n 1562075\n \n 1562075\n Strengths are that the professors treat us as adults, they give us the readings and the assignments once a week and they do not baby us but during the lectures or throughout the week, the class is engaging and if you want to participate you do need to do at least some of the readings which also forces you to learn. They take every question no matter how naive or "stupid" it may be. They also include good tangential conversations and stories to reduce the monotony. They are fun to listen to and fun to engage in.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses include that the professors I think end up talking with each other a little more than with us than I expected. Additionally, because of the size of the class and the wide range of skills (from grad students in philosophy and cogsci to first years) there wasn't always enough time to ask questions especially as the conversations and topics changed quickly. But this is a weak weakness as there is not much you can do about this and really it just means that I wanted more time in the class which is a good thing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1577971\n 1577971\n \n 1577971\n The concept for the class is creative, and I thoroughly enjoyed discussion every week. That said, the ways that the fields of philosophy and cogsci interacted were not always super clear to me, and I generally had trouble with understanding the metaphysics side of things.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1595159\n 1595159\n \n 1595159\n Our class discussions were incredibly interesting and substantive, though they could be disorganized at times.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1595476\n 1595476\n \n 1595476\n Laurie and Brian are unquestionably the biggest strengths of this class. At times, it didn't seem necessary for the class to be a seminar at all, for their arguments with each other were so intellectually stimulating that we could have sufficed with just their cross-questioning to explore certain topics. Another strength is the diversity of opinion that the class attracts, from undergraduates to PhD students strictly focusing in philosophy, psychology or even further removed subjects.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1595799\n 1595799\n \n 1595799\n Guest speakers are a big strength for the course, as is the teaching. \r\nOnly weakness is that it could be fun to have more robust discussions in class with back and forths among the students.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562075\n 1562075\n \n 1562075\n ABSOLUTELY! If you don't think you like philosophy, especially if you are a STEM person, PLEASE take this class. Brian Scholl is an amazing professor, seminar professor, and advisor. Laurie Paul is also an amazing professor and will make sure that everything is questioned but questioned correctly. You will spend a lot of time on readings but they are worth it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1571410\n 1571410\n \n 1571410\n I personally was not a huge fan of this course despite loving psychology. I was looking for some exposure to philosophy/metaphysics and hoped this course could give me that. At times it did, but I was generally disappointed with it. Brian and Laurie are probably both great individual instructors, but together they spend most of the class talking to each other, resulting in a pretty inactive and boring seminar. Also because they're presenting different sides / approaches to something, it often seems like they're battling for the attention of the class to discuss their side of psychology or metaphysics, which is also not super interesting. The guest speakers were great, but again spent a lot of time just talking with Brian and Laurie, so don't quite expect a normal active seminar.
