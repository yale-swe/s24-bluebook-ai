\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537405\n 1537405\n \n 1537405\n This class was phenomenal! I learned a lot and improved my Hindi writing and speaking skills. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537415\n 1537415\n \n 1537415\n The class taught me how to read and write and also made me more comfortable speaking.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537446\n 1537446\n \n 1537446\n We had a lot of opportunities to practice our oral hindi which helped us become more comfortable with speaking. The teacher explained grammar concepts really well, and class pacing was great. However, the quizzes often covered content not covered in class and were quite challenging in general, especially since we were just learning to read and write.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537760\n 1537760\n \n 1537760\n At first, I really, really struggled in this class, especially in the first quarter. I was spending considerably more time on this class than others, which was a problem. I felt I wasnt learning the language because my speaking wasnt improving, and it seemed everyone came into the class already knowing how to speak Hindi. The quizzes were asking us to write in full sentences that included perfect tense, present and past progressive tense, present and past habitual tense, but we had not learned any tenses. Once we learned our first tense (present habitual) after fall break, I felt a huge improvement. All of a sudden, I could remember more verbs and vocab and started gaining more confidence speaking in class. I think the class would work better if we learned the alphabet, then simple sentences (nouns, adjectives, and the verb to be) and maybe imperatives, then present habitual, and only THEN postpositions, possessives, the oblique case, and other grammatical concepts that add detail. It didnt make much sense to learn ingredients of more complex sentences when we didnt even know how to write in basic tenses. I think the class would also benefit from more instruction on sentence order and tense formation, (there was only a bit of that towards the end), rather than just going through the books exercises. All of that being said, Savita Ji is incredibly kind and understanding! So was Hiba Ji! The class wasnt a gotcha environment, and it was okay and accepted to make mistakes. For that reason, I think it was overall a good environment in which to learn.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537853\n 1537853\n \n 1537853\n Strengths: focuses on literacy skills \r\nWeaknesses: too much book-reading, not enough engaging discussions or assignments. Homework felt tedious and lacked creativity except for final project.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538165\n 1538165\n \n 1538165\n Strengths: In class went around doing the practice questions together, which forced me to practice each new concept that we were learning. The workload of the class was not too high and as long as you are doing what you are supposed to, you can end the class with a good grade. \r\nWeaknesses: While we learned the alphabet, we were forced to learn the spelling of most words by ourselves. The class worked under the assumption that because we could speak, we knew the small differences in spelling, which was not always accurate. Also, we had weekly quizzes at the end of the week to test the skill that we learned in the week. However, more of the students' focus went towards figuring out the other random words in the sentence, than actually testing the one skill that we learned.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538810\n 1538810\n \n 1538810\n I felt as though the beginning of the course could have been organized a bit better since most students were not familiar with the Devanagari script and we were kinda thrown into the deep end. Also, the class environment was a bit stressful at the start since we were put on the spot and asked to do textbook exercises that were beyond our initial abilities. Apart from that, everything went smoothly and I felt like we were given a lot of practice to improve our reading and writing skills, which I really appreciate.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541727\n 1541727\n \n 1541727\n I think there could be more emphasis on developing vocabulary and making sure old grammatical concepts are being practiced rather than moving through them as units.
