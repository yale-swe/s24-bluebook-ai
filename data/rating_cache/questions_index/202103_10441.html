Howard really challenges his students and isn't afraid to share his own views, but he always listens to and believes in each one of us. Every student left this class more knowledgable about the world and more aware of nuanced perspectives.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The final group project can be a bit of a pain, and isn't relevant to the work we do throughout the semester. It is not the most academically challenging class (although the combination of the reading list and graduate students makes for some incredible conversations, some of the best, intellectually challenging conversations I've witnessed at Yale), but it is the best community-focused class I've taken at Yale. Howard interviews every candidate for the class personally, and hand-selects a really diverse, well-rounded class. If you get into it, you should take it, for tons of reasons not least compelling of which is Howard, himself. He is who he is, and still remains the most engaged professor I've ever had at Yale. He held weekly office hours buying students food, hosted dinners for the class, and made himself incredibly available to his students. In my book, he is one of the best, most community-oriented, most helpful, most generous, and most knowledgeable professors at Yale.
