\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1522200\n 1522200\n \n 1522200\n Garsten's H&P class provided us with an excellent balance of examining specific historical figures and commenting on broad themes that capture human comportments and tendencies throughout the span of time. We learned to tackle big questions step-by-step because our professor's strategy was to launch us into the discussion with a general query. We were then motivated to look back into the text to support our findings, but also permitted ourselves to stray into digressions. Moving back and forth between deep reflection, perspicacious textual analysis, and engaging class conversations was very useful for building a historical and political foundation.

I actively looked forward to section meetings as I knew Professor Garsten would always come with engaging ideas and allow us to really participate constructively. The lectures were also generally thought-provoking and insightful.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1522200\n 1522200\n \n 1522200\n The course is not afraid to challenge ideas that are regarded as widely accepted in the contemporary United States. The majority of the authors we read lambasted democracy, which I was totally not expecting when I entered the course. The ability to speak openly about this type of idea and try to get at the root of the author's perspective was enlightening. The syllabus was incredibly well structured for the time frame we had while there were certain jumps in historical continuity, the texts flowed together nicely and the introduction of the Bible at the beginning of the year before transitioning to the Greeks and Romans was an appreciated component of the course. \r\nThe only weakness of the course was when a lecture spent too much time dwelling on a summary of the text instead of connecting it to other works or drawing a decisive conclusion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537399\n 1537399\n \n 1537399\n Strengths: great introduction to the Western canon, prompted thought\r\nWeaknesses: a lot of discussions were just people repeating one another
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538995\n 1538995\n \n 1538995\n One of the great strengths was the fascinating discussions. I just wish there was more help on writing improvement.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538996\n 1538996\n \n 1538996\n Strengths: everything\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: none
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539022\n 1539022\n \n 1539022\n The course overall does an excellent job of accomplishing its purpose of furthering your writing and giving you an overview of Historical and Political Thought in the Western tradition. My main complaint is that we do not spend enough time on some material (just two crammed weeks at the end of the semester on the Middle Ages), but this is largely unavoidable given how much this survey course has to cover. I would tweak the curriculum a bit to spend slightly less time on The Republic. Also, there is a TON of reading to do for the course which some people might want to avoid. You can get around doing a good amount of it, but I recommend doing as much as possible to get as much as you can out of the class. If you trust yourself to do most of the reading and show up, you will get a lot out of DS generally and especially H&P.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539052\n 1539052\n \n 1539052\n Seminar sections facilitated by Professor Garsten were incredibly enriching since he asked intellectually stimulating questions and encouraged us to consider various (sometime conflicting) interpretations of the text. As a result, this course helped me grow as a writer and thinker. I also enjoyed the diverse array of authors that we read this year. This allowed me to gain greater knowledge about the western canon through the lens of history and politics.\r\nThe only weakness is that I wish we had more time to spend on each text. This would allow us to discover more about each text and discuss more sections that we were assigned to read.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539089\n 1539089\n \n 1539089\n I think the curriculum is a little disorganized, in terms of the specific materials we read being a mix of philosophy and history (although it is easy to see the connection between the works in the contexts they were written in). However, the strengths of this course lie in the ways the examination and discussions of these texts enable students to understand greater concepts like justice, knowledge, and power in the context of organized society and government.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539124\n 1539124\n \n 1539124\n Strengths: very broad coverage of ancient political thought in the Greek and Roman eras. Acquaints you with foundational ideas that are still highly relevant to politics today. Teaches you to read efficiently but also engage with the reading's challenging sections. Teaches you to write well!\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: the curriculum is focused on western thought, but bringing in just a bit of wisdom from places like China, Japan, or India could add a lot of value. Suggestion has been fielded, and I get the sense that changes in curriculum could gradually happen in the future.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539185\n 1539185\n \n 1539185\n This class is phenomenal. The readings can at times be overwhelming, but the material is fascinating, and Bryan Garsten does a great job leading discussions. He sees H&P (and DS as a whole) as one of the only times in life you get to ask the really hard questions---eg. what is the best form of government? What distinguishes a good leader from a bad one? How can we apply historical evidence to modern event?-- and expect an answer.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539204\n 1539204\n \n 1539204\n A strength of this course was its breadth (the weakness that goes hand-in-hand, of course, is the lack of depth in each text). Professor Garsten, however, did a good job of making sure we extracted the most from each text
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539247\n 1539247\n \n 1539247\n The classroom discussions Professor Garsten led and his insightful comments on DS papers were what made the class interesting - he has a unique, ponderous approach to history and politics that he inculcated in us over the semester and for this I am quite thankful!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539542\n 1539542\n \n 1539542\n Strengths: Prof. Garsten (!!), pre-disciplinary nature of subject, profundity of texts, classmates\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: vague instruction on how to write a paper that feels at once literary, philosophical, historical, and political
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539602\n 1539602\n \n 1539602\n Strengths: the discussions, the essay feedback was really detailed, the books\r\nWeaknesses: I think some of the readings could be cut down (especially the latter parts of Herodotus, for example) since DS already has so much reading, and I think (and this goes for all of DS) students should receive more support throughout the process of writing their first essays.
