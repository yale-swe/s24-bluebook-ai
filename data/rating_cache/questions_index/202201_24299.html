\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589265\n 1589265\n \n 1589265\n As someone who had never formally studied film, this class opened my eyes to the political and artistic significance of film. I feel like I now approach film with a completely different understanding of what it can be. I also now know how to write about film in a more technical and nuanced way that I hadn't had the opportunity to develop before.\r\n\r\nThis class is about Black film. As such, our readings focus on Black liberation and political aspirations of Black people and Black directors. I felt that we discussed an important political history through media representation that I hadn't learned about before. Professor Forster is impressively knowledgeable and does an excellent job of situating the films we watched within history.

Learned about multiple new lenses to view and analyze film through, including Afropessimism and sociological analysis. Deep analysis of short films that was fun and challenging.

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1588415\n 1588415\n \n 1588415\n The course gives equal weight to the story of each film's production (i.e. asking "how is Black art made in America?") as it does to an actual formal analysis of the films themselves, which I really enjoyed. Professor Forster made an effort to introduce some creative activities in the second half of the term (e.g. making two-minute short films during class) it would have been helpful to have those sprinkled throughout, especially earlier on, when we were watching newer "experimental" works by artists like Arthur Jaffa and Keisha Rae Witherspoon.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1588889\n 1588889\n \n 1588889\n It is an incredibly well-organized course. The readings + films are paired really well, and the thematic organization is clear. I do think the readings can be a lot sometimes, but I suppose that is expected of a junior seminar. Prof. Forster really does a good job of introducing us to ideas and themes as much as we just discuss movies. I feel like I left with a much deeper understanding of Black politics + movements. I appreciate the concept of blog posts an informal, low-stakes way for students to synthesize their ideas and learn from one another. However, I'm not sure that they quite do that in practice.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589265\n 1589265\n \n 1589265\n Strengths of this class included the selection of films (which were all excellent and impactful), the selected readings (all were relevant and enlightening in some way) and the blog post assignments (which gave us creative room and an opportunity to improve throughout the semester). Strengths of this class also include the visits from Black directors -- it was incredible to be able to speak with them after seeing their work.\r\n\r\nOne weakness of this class was often the discussion aspect. I felt like Professor Forster could have provided a little more direction or commentary to our discussion particularly when the discussion fell flat, when students were quiet or when students went off on tangents with little relevance. That being said, when our discussions were strong, they were very strong.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1599406\n 1599406\n \n 1599406\n Amazing syllabus selection spanning a wide array of time periods, genres, films, and kinds of work that engage with each of the films. The course includes lots of casual and low-stakes writing such as the pre-class worksheets and blog posts, providing many opportunities for students to engage in conversation with the films and each other without much pressure. Class this semester included a variety of collaborative and creative small projects/assignments, which were very fun and illuminating as we had to create our own short films. Class had a strong emphasis on the various kinds of labor involved in the production and distribution of film, and not just from the director, which is an inside perspective that doesn't seem to get discussed enough elsewhere. Class discussions were always fun, engaging, and well-facilitated.
