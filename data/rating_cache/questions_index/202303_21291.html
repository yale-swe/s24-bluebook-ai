RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702241\n 1702241\n \n 1702241\n Strengths: for a class taught for the first time, this class was remarkably well organized. The readings were varied and manageable, and the professor is great. It was also great to pick the topic of the penultimate class meeting and the structure of the final exam as a class. \r\nWeaknesses: Student engagement in class discussions was low (just like it is in every EP&E seminar), but that was really despite the professor's best efforts to the contrary. Otherwise, the main weakness of this course is its grading structure. It does not make sense to give three 6-page writing assignments over the course of the semester and then give the sum of the three the same weight in the course grade as the final exam, which was basically a lower-grade version of the same type of writing assignment. 18 pages of polished essays should not get the same weight as two short essays written over the course of two hours. It was also a little excessive to have the final writing assignment due on Wednesday of reading week and then have the final exam the following day. \r\nIn the future, I think this class could do away with the final exam altogether and just have the final writing assignment be a critical reflection about the course themes, using the exact questions that we had on the final. Also, the class should probably just be called "Fundamentals of Market Theory" to better reflect its content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1704331\n 1704331\n \n 1704331\n Strength - Professor Kevin Elliot 100%. Engaging, funny, TAUGHT YOU, made the 9:25am two hours worth it, went for him. The content was neccesary, it when chronologically in order, the reading was not overbearing - it was specific and not broad. Elliot made sure that we would understand the material, essays were very interesting and tied to the three sections of the class - which made it helpful. Feedback was helpful. Weakness - there's only so much we can limit ourselves to learning in a semester, there are infinite things to discuss and unfortunately it was reasonable that we couldn't mention every thing that exists in the world to discuss, but Elliot did the best way to include as much as possible. I think the final should be more split between identifications and short response and then 1 longer response - the only reason it was not this is because us students voted with a majority saying 2 essays in person as the final - but either way was just as effective and doable, and so the students will have a reasonable input to what can work well for us to be evaluated. I think maybe just for fun, it could be interesting to add a low stakes first essay at the beginning of the class where the student makes an argument just from their own thoughts, and then the 3 class essays of the term, then maybe another low stakes essay of their own thoughts at the end to see their growth.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714527\n 1714527\n \n 1714527\n Strengths: Prof. Elliott is incredibly knowledgable. He has a wealth of knowledge about the authors that we read and so we always got a great understanding of the texts we were discussing. The texts we read were also connected to one another, and they would reference one another as the course continued. I appreciated this because it felt like there was a sense of continuity! I also really appreciated the flexibility on the deadline for our weekly reading responses. Even though we had a 9pm deadline every Tuesday, Prof. Elliott was gracious and just asked that we submit before the end of the day. Weaknesses: the last final paper overlapped with the in-class 2.5 hour final at the end of the course (paper due Wednesday night, final on Thursday night). I would have appreciated having either a final paper OR a in-class final, and it made the end of the semester extra stressful with 2 important assignments for one class being due back-to-back. I also would have appreciated a little more structure to the discussions/lectures. Though I appreciated the interest in hearing what we wanted to discuss, at times it would have been better to have Prof. Elliott simply explain and elaborate on the texts, which could then have sparked deeper discussions after we got clarification. On the last day of class, Prof. Elliott also mentioned themes that we were supposed to have picked up throughout the semester, but I feel like it would have been better to know the themes or ideas to look out for before the end of the semester. Overall, it was a great course, seeing as it was the first class Prof. Elliott taught at Yale, and if I could, I would take Classics again with him!
