\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1696540\n 1696540\n \n 1696540\n I found it a bit frustrating how often there were overlaps of having multiple assignments due in the same week, and even one case where two assignments were due on the same day right before fall recess (although I recognize in a rigorous class this may be unavoidable). I was also split on how I felt about all assignments being due at midnight with a 3-hour grace period. On one hand I was glad for the leniency, but on the other I feel it strongly encouraged me and other students to stay up until 3am to squeeze in work. I know it's the student's own fault for pushing things to the last minute, but I couldn't help thinking there could be a healthier way to incentivize turning in assignments on time. One better solution may be to give a few late days that could be expended during the semester, rather than a universal grace period that leads to people frequently staying up until 3am. This way, students would feel a strong incentive to finish their work at a reasonable hour (as only a limited number of late allowances would be available), and if they needed to spend some of their overtime allowance then they could make that choice and then have normal daylight hours to do their work instead of invariably destabilizing their sleep schedule. Just a thought! Overall, I do appreciate the clear intention goal of giving students flexibility.
