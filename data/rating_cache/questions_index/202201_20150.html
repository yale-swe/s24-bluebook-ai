Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562173\n 1562173\n \n 1562173\n Strengths: The timing of the class: one long discussion class a week, is literally perfect. The workload, at least one rather long book of poetry that you only have to read as much as you can get through, is literally perfect and flexible. The discussions are interesting and well lead, and the in-class poetry analyses are fantastic. The explications were fun to write and pretty intuitive, and Prof. Connolly gave good critique while also being considerate of my rocky french ability. The final essay was a complete marathon but so rewarding when I turned it in. No Prerequisites required, basically a perfect class.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The one class sometimes feels long because... it is long. Sometimes discussion can take a bit to ramp up, but it's good once it gets going.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1566268\n 1566268\n \n 1566268\n The section was always very well lead and the readings were very well organized and in tune with the discussions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575154\n 1575154\n \n 1575154\n Strengths: The course is very well organized and comprehensive: students learn not only about the works but also about their historical and literary contexts. I definitely improved my writing and reading abilities in this course. Prof. Connolly is a great professor and class was always interesting and informative. Weaknesses: none
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593482\n 1593482\n \n 1593482\n Everything about this course is great. Prof. Connolly is extremely knowledgeable and passionate about poetry of the Maghreb; he guides class discussions so that each student has a chance to participate, while still making his own (extremely insightful) contributions. I love the way the class is structured, as each week we cover a new poet and read one of their anthologies as well as some secondary literature on them. I cannot really think of any way to improve this course, other than the fact that we often run out of time at the end of each class so we had to rush a bit.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593588\n 1593588\n \n 1593588\n I truly believe this course had no weaknesses. It was very well organized and Prof. Connolly was great at communicating his expectations to us. The class size was small so everyone always had a chance to speak, and Prof. Connolly ensured that there was a welcoming environment, even for those like myself who had no experience with French poetry or the Maghreb. A great breadth of poets were covered, but I still felt that we were able to talk about each poet with decent depth during weekly seminars. Prof. Connolly himself is also such a kind and passionate professor, which made my experience very enjoyable.
