\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538210\n 1538210\n \n 1538210\n Strengths: The sheer amount of time spent writing the 5 English papers will improve your writing. The different essay genres force you to acknowledge the nuance of each one and contemplate what a good essay in said genre looks like and why. The assigned reading comes in handy there. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The course really facilitates your improvement as a writer. But if you actually want to get better, you have to take your own path. Find someone to read over your essays (a really smart friend or the Poorvu center) and discuss things with. Find people in the class to brainstorm with, etc. \r\n\r\nSometimes I also found that the in-class discussion got a little redundant. Possibly more guidance from the instructor on the nature of the discussion would help.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539494\n 1539494\n \n 1539494\n One strength was the class environment. I really enjoyed getting to know my peers.\r\n\r\nOne weakness was immediate feedback for my writing. One way this could be improved would be to have peer tutors where each student partners up to read each others works and give them feedback on them. It's an easy way to make sure every student has had someone else look at it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543036\n 1543036\n \n 1543036\n A strength of this course is the sense of community it builds among writers and students. In most English classes I have taken, writing (especially creative non-fiction) was treated as something so individual, and while the content is individual in English 120, the sharing of ideas and workshopping allows students to see the process of other students and receive a variety of feedback. The essays chosen for my class to read were all great fits for each of the five units, and the open participation was really great, too. I cannot think of much that would improve the course, aside from maybe more time for early workshopping or the sentence-level analysis we did in the second quarter.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1548174\n 1548174\n \n 1548174\n Professor Saraceni is a strength. She is incredibly kind and gives a lot of detailed feedback on papers.\r\nThe course is also organized well. We write several types of essays. At the end of the semester, when other classes are cramming information for finals, we had a shorter humor essay.\r\nI think it could be helpful to name more of the techniques we discuss, since we usually talked about things the author did well, and I sometimes didn't know how to apply those things in my own writing.\r\nI know some people find workshops great, but I never felt that I had much expertise to advise my peers on their essays, so I didn't always think I was helpful in workshops and didn't always find workshops super useful for myself.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550876\n 1550876\n \n 1550876\n I really loved every aspect of this course: the structure worked so well and made it easy to learn from the essays we read, our discussions were lively and engaging, the essays themselves were wonderful, the writing assignments felt meaningful and fulfilling, and Professor Saraceni was just such a delight. \r\n\r\nHonestly, it's hard to think of weaknesses!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550879\n 1550879\n \n 1550879\n I loved the organization of this class! The five units really helped us narrow our focus and work on specific skills. I love that we had to submit a first draft and a final draft this made us learn from our mistakes! We got such specific feedback and really knew what we had to work on to improve. Additionally, the selection of short stories were incredible and I always looked forward to reading them.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550882\n 1550882\n \n 1550882\n Prof Saraceni is so sweet and positive and gives incredible feedback - she really creates an amazing class environment. I was so intimated by the idea of workshops, but they were the coolest part of the class. It was really cool to hear what people thought of my writing and I got so many great ideas from my classmates. The workload is heavy in that you have to turn in a draft for an essay every week so you are alwayssss writing, but I thought that was the best challenge of the class. I didnt always keep up with the readings but they were always interesting and more fun to read than any other classs readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550901\n 1550901\n \n 1550901\n Strengths were definitely my professor (Prof. Saraceni) and her infectious excitement as we read essays, wrote and revised our drafts, and workshopped our peers' essays. Also the wide range of essay topics that we got to cover and her encouraging us to challenge ourselves. Weaknesses are that sometimes there doesn't seem to be enough time for breathing room between essays, but she is so kind about extensions and genuinely cares about her students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550915\n 1550915\n \n 1550915\n I thought the organization of the course was one of its greatest strengths. In my opinion, learning by example is one of the most effective ways to improve one's writing. Reading and discussing works by great writers helped me a great deal both in honing my craft and finding inspiration for my own writing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1551007\n 1551007\n \n 1551007\n The class was a warm and tight knit community which helped me learn and made class lovely. The feedback I received on my writing was detailed and very helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1551119\n 1551119\n \n 1551119\n Strengths: Passionate professor who leads an engaging discussion that was sometimes the highlight of my week; terrific readings; easy bonding with the other kids in the class because everyone is super into it; clear course structure (the syllabus you get on the first day of class details each subsequent class meeting's agenda!)
