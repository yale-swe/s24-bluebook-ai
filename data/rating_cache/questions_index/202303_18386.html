Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1705944\n 1705944\n \n 1705944\n A strength of the class is the great variety of topics we cover in the class. Each week had a different theme so it was always interesting and we were able to learn a lot of material this way. Another strength of this course was the seminar format where we balanced discussions with lectures from the professor to provide background knowledge. We frequently had guest speakers which each provided unique perspectives on the topics we were discussing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1708228\n 1708228\n \n 1708228\n Thought the seminar was well-organized for learning and allowed ample opportunity for questions and discussions. However, the time we used to break apart in small groups and talk about our papers (especially early on) was rather chaotic and could have been used for more lecture-based material.\r\n\r\nI felt like the term paper was kind of overplayed in the sense that we were expected to talk about it every week, but we didn't submit parts of it in stages. We only were asked to submit an outline prior to the official deadline, and we never got feedback on those outlines, so it seemed a bit pointless. I think that incremental deadlines (ex: submit topic by this date, submit outline by this date, send draft of lit review section by this date) with feedback on each would better match the professors' desire for students to work on and be engaged with their papers for the whole course of the semester. Without those sorts of benchmark deadlines, it felt like (myself included) there was a lot of redundancy in what students would say about their papers each week, since they weren't really working on them at the time.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1713539\n 1713539\n \n 1713539\n weaknesses: it was a bit difficult to communicate with professors via email, which made it difficult to schedule office hours; i found the only way to schedule office hours was to speak with professors in class. we also never received feedback on our media analysis essay which made it difficult to understand what the professors were looking for for the next essay; strengths - the teaching staff is incredibly passionate about the subject material, which makes the class evermore engaging
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1717800\n 1717800\n \n 1717800\n In terms of strengths, both Dr. Beitel and Dr. Cutter complement each other's areas of expertise very well. Dr. Beitel clearly has an extensive background as a therapeutic practitioner, and his training in psychoanalysis offers a perspective that supplements well Dr. Cutter's more orthodox biomedical approach. Dr. Cutter, meanwhile, offers fairly robust first-hand accounts of patient perspectives throughout treatment. Both, meanwhile, are clearly versed in the literature available to such a niche area of clinical practice, and both demonstrate clear relationships with the people impacted by this field's development, whether as practitioners or patients. Additionally, they are very attentive to each students' personal development in the course and provide ample room within each seminar for students to develop their independent papers and obtain feedback from one another.\r\n\r\nThe only weakness I can name clearly has largely to do with the available literature. Namely, some of the sources seem to preserve outdated or controversial metrics, and more supplemental perspectives could be provided to challenge or reground those metrics in more contemporary critique. That said, this need not assume much priority at all. The course is clearly intended as a survey, and given its uniqueness in subject matter, that alone suffices. Any issues with sources are purely incidental and a result of the field itself remaining under-resourced and underdeveloped, for reasons beyond the urgency of the issues in question and the efficacy of its practitioners.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1718459\n 1718459\n \n 1718459\n Strengths of the course include the built-in support for writing a literature review and guest speakers with great insights.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1719169\n 1719169\n \n 1719169\n Overall, the course is strong. I think it does what it sets out to do well. The presentations were sometimes slow and the discussions could have been more engaging, but I think that is partially on students and the professors did incredibly well to balance student input and lecturing.
