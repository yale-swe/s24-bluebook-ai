\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722320\n 1722320\n \n 1722320\n I cannot think of any particular weaknesses that this iteration of the course has had. Despite the fact that this is the first time that the course has ever been taught at Yale, it was INCREDIBLY well organized, and only left positive emotions after each and every section. It was clear right from the get go that both Marijeta and Spencer cared deeply about both the topic and the way we discussed all of the information, and I particularly enjoyed the weekly routine we established as the semester progressed. Whereas Tuesdays were dedicated to providing context for the videogame of the week, Thursday were spent reflecting upon the game night sessions we had ever Wednesday. Speaking of those, I absolutely LOVED THEM. I found those nights to be the strongest reason behind my constant engagement with the course and its material, as it allowed to not just engage with the material, but also discuss it right away and form strong bonds with my classmates.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724356\n 1724356\n \n 1724356\n Honestly, I have almost no complaints about how this course was structured. The discussions were lively and deeply engaging, with the professors beautifully weaving in our discussion posts, the readings, and relevant examples. Almost every time class ended there were people waiting to say more because there was just so much we had to say. Speaking honestly, I do not always do the readings for every week for most humanities classes, but I went out of my way to do every reading for this class. I never felt like my time was being wasted; whenever we were asked to read something, it was for a purpose and clearly helped progress our thinking about the topics. I absolutely loved the class's "game nights" too; they allowed for a lot of cross talk and helped us experience the games we were analyzing in a way that gave us a lot to think about. Compared to the average film class, I learned a lot more about each game than I would from the equivalent film screening because it wasn't just silently consuming the media; the professors helped guide our thinking. Truly, this was a near perfect humanities course.
