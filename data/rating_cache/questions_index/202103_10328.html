\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535970\n 1535970\n \n 1535970\n strengths: the topic is inherently interesting, especially to gen z. the conversations were relatable.\r\n\r\nweaknesses: the class had a lot more potential. the readings weren't the most engaging and felt a bit out-dated. i didn't feel like i was critically thinking about internet language. essay prompts were weird and difficult to write about.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537491\n 1537491\n \n 1537491\n Strengths: Obviously, the topic is interesting to many in various different wayseach student engages with memes, fandom, and the internet in vastly different ways, giving diverse opinions. Feedback from Holt was helpful and consistent. Most of the journal articles we read, especially in the second half of the semester, were extremely interesting and yielded some amazing discussions. Holt was very agreeable, you could tell that she was very passionate about linguistics and that she was truly trying to help you. We got a lot of freedom in our writing assignments, which was nice. \r\nWeaknesses: Class discussions were either extremely interesting or mind-numbingly boring, and this was due to the readings we would be assigned. We spent the majority of the first half of the semester doing readings on linguisticsthese readings were unnecessarily convoluted to the point where they were nearly impossible to comprehend without an underlying knowledge of linguistics, which many in this introductory English class did not have. As such, most of the class was spent trying to figure out what the reading was actually trying to get across instead of engaging with those ideas. Picking different readings on the same subject, finding abridged versions, or simply cutting these readings altogether would have greatly improved the class. Additionally, Holt, while a gem, can be boring at times during class. She would often go on linguistic tangents that were barely related to our days discussionthese tangents no doubt would come out of a passion for linguistics, but she would attempt to explain things that were relatively advanced in the world of linguistics without giving us any basic knowledge. While it was apparent that Holt had a lesson plan for every class, when she would go on these tangents, 1. we would run out of class time and 2. we wouldn't be able to understand what she was trying to teach us because she had not planned to teach it and was going off of her head on the blackboard.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539146\n 1539146\n \n 1539146\n Strengths: English 114 is a well-structured class with a manageable workload and engaging seminar style. Professor Holt was always willing to answer questions and provide feedback, and her commitment to the class helped us all to become better writers.\r\nWeaknesses: The writing assignments were vague and difficult to interpret.
