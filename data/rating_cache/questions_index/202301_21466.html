I have lost a significant number of points for incredibly small errors, and in many cases, I've lost points for not mentioning things the questions didn't ask for. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1651560\n 1651560\n \n 1651560\n I think the biggest strength of the class was the variety of labs that we performed and the different perspective that they gave me on topics that were covered in my physics lecture as well. I wish this was implemented more into the course, especially since many labs that do research at Yale use this program or something similar.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1652780\n 1652780\n \n 1652780\n The strength of this course is in the structured approach to most tasks. Often, if only I knew what the homework wanted me to talk about, I would have been able to, but some questions are so vague that it is almost impossible to answer them as intended.

I think a weakness was the grading of some of the pre-labs and post-labs. The instructions were not always super clear or detailed, and I would lose points because a question was not concise enough.
