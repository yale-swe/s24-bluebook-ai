\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521312\n 1521312\n \n 1521312\n This course was remarkably engaging, both in terms of the curriculum and the class discussion. Mario is an excellent discussion facilitator, and he really went out of his way to make sure everyone was involved and that the discussion was open, inviting, and interesting. The curriculum itself was clearly designed with care, and each week was a new adventure into interesting philosophical works, controversial films, or contrarian works of fiction. The only weakness was that I wished we'd met more, as I really enjoyed our discussions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1522120\n 1522120\n \n 1522120\n The strengths of the course came from the in-class discussions. It was riveting getting to work through issues with peers and having debates on contentious philosophical issues. I found it really helpful. \r\nThere weren't any significant weaknesses per se, but I would have enjoyed more diversity in paper prompts. Thesis defense is only one type of philosophical essay, and I think it could have been beneficial to consider adding other types of assignments to broaden how we think about art and morality. I also would have like for class discussions to have spent more time talking about the texts. Again, these are not necessarily weaknesses but rather different things that I would've liked to see.

So, I wish we spent fewer weeks on moralism at the beginning because it wouldve allowed us to spend more times on different topics at the end.

I loved doing our homework--since we read such a fascinating mixture of philosophical texts and stories and lectures and plays and scientific studies. We also watched wonderful movies. And the classroom discussion was the perfect balance of getting context and knowledge from Professor Attie Picker while also allowing room for everyone to participate in engage--and I learned a great deal from my classmates. But not in a way where it was just one person talking--Professor Attie Picker did such a great job in creating a classroom environment in which I wanted to participate in it--and I think others felt this way too.
