The workload was not overwhelming- there were no exams and no huge stressful projects. Rather, each class was a discussion, and the homework was to prepare for the next discussion (in addition to a short weekly writing assignment and a biweekly speaking assignment). \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1674298\n 1674298\n \n 1674298\n Strengths: awesome classroom environment, all due to our fun group of classmates and also Jorge being a lively, intelligent, dedicated teacher.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1674354\n 1674354\n \n 1674354\n The variety of units in this course offer something for everybody. The 200-word writing assignments were long enough to generate substantive feedback but short enough to be manageable; I almost wish that there had been more. Group discussion in class was significantly more active than in lower levels, with dialogue, disagreement, and discussion filling the air in most meetings. The layout of units and lessons made sense and was interesting. The variety of sources (articles, documentaries, speeches, essays) helped us interact with the language in a variety of ways.\r\n\r\nThe last unit, on constitutional design, could use some refinement. It's tough to write somebody else's constitution, because constitutions should have a special relationship with the history and tradition of the people who live with them. As an outsider, it can be difficult to pay appropriate respect to these dynamics; one way to get a little closer would be to pick a single country instead of trying to do all of Latin America at once. Alternatively (and I think that this is more in line with the spirit of the unit) the project could focus on a declaration of rights, which are usually meant to be more universal.
