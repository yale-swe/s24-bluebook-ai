RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1653344\n 1653344\n \n 1653344\n Strengths: Lots of practice! Both in and out of class, all of the assignments were directly relevant to strengthening my grammar abilities. The content of the reading was also quite engaging, as well as the podcasts and videos and language partners. The class, rather unexpectedly, started with more basic grammar, which was super appreciated. The professor is amazing, kind, and helpful.\r\nWeaknesses: the online assignments were sometimes excessive, as well as frustrating because it was hard to tell what you did wrong. The due dates for most assignments are not flexible, which is understandable but adds stress.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1656593\n 1656593\n \n 1656593\n Strengths: lots of engagement with Spanish due to the number of assignments that were all topically relevant to the class periods. Professor Seymour is super passionate about the subject and gives so much individual feedback which is incredible. Weaknesses: not really any focus on speaking ability (which might be a strength depending on your perspective)
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1660426\n 1660426\n \n 1660426\n There was a lot of homework.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1660495\n 1660495\n \n 1660495\n I think this is a great class to practice a lot of grammatical concepts. I also think that given the volume of assignments and the fact that with few exceptions none of them were even graded quickly, a slightly more lenient policy on late work would better encourage students to do it all. If you fell behind, that discouraged getting in the good practice of going back and redoing it. Yes, if that happens too much youll struggle to participate, but I think that the Spanish department in general should think about whether its policies are consistent with principles that those who study education would endorse. I think the answer broadly is no, and I think that changing some policies wouldnt change the amount of effort put in by students but it would just give them room to be a person with ups and downs.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1660730\n 1660730\n \n 1660730\n Strengths: concepts are well-explained, and while a few times over the semester, we learned some grammatical concept just is the way it is, almost every concept we learned had some logic behind it, which made things easier to metabolize. Weekly conversations are useful/fairly relaxed, and the readings are interesting/engaging. The professor was approachable/happy to answer questions, and gave helpful answers.\r\nWeaknesses: This isn't a weakness, exactly, but the material is difficult. We cover many different concepts in a fairly short period of time, and it's hard to hang on to everything we learn. I think the course compensates for this by making every assignment cumulative: ser vs. estar is the main focus for the first week or so, and then shows up every once in a while on unrelated assignments throughout the rest of the course, because ser and estar are important enough verbs that you need to use them frequently.\r\nImprovements: The class wouldn't function if it wasn't a lot of work, but sometimes I think it was a bit much. I would maybe draw the line at three unrelated assignments being due on the same day.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1661123\n 1661123\n \n 1661123\n The course at times was formulaic and repetitive in its format. VHL Central was more frustrating than helpful. Nonetheless, it was an organized and easy to follow course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1664353\n 1664353\n \n 1664353\n strong teaching of grammar and support at office hours. lively collaborative class environment. way too much work each night
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1671014\n 1671014\n \n 1671014\n Strengths: Tons of feedback, Prof Seymour is super engaged with students, it's a collaborative class.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I think the class would be improved if it was more focused on helping us get our point across with new forms of grammar, rather than just testing us on particular contexts in which the word or tense might make sense. Like helping understand when to use a certain tense or construction, and then giving us the freedom to articulate more complex topics or sentences with that grammar.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1677008\n 1677008\n \n 1677008\n I felt that I walked away with a lot of knowledge that I will retain about the Spanish language. The course requires a lot of work outside of the course, and although this made it a challenge for time management, it did help reinforce concepts well. I would advise assigning films over the weekend rather than during the week, but it was otherwise a pretty manageable workload.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1682460\n 1682460\n \n 1682460\n It is a lot of work! But you get out what you put in and this course really offers an opportunity to commit yourself to the tedious every day work of learning a language and learning it well. If you keep up with the work, your spanish will really improve. Professor Seymour is wonderfulpatience, non-judgemental, flexible.
