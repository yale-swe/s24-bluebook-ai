\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1610367\n 1610367\n \n 1610367\n The course does a fantastic job of moving you through the central philosophical texts of the Western canon, but it could do better at connecting the different schools of thought and helping the students compare and contrast them.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624255\n 1624255\n \n 1624255\n Strengths: the syllabus is very ecumenical and you get a great broad overview of the main schools of thought; Weaknesses: it feels rushed at some points
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1626009\n 1626009\n \n 1626009\n I think we go through too many texts at a time, but the discussions around it are extremely fun
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627151\n 1627151\n \n 1627151\n Strengths: incredible professor, engaging readings and thought-provoking discussions.\r\nI cannot think of any weaknesses other than that it only lasted for one semester!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627346\n 1627346\n \n 1627346\n I think some of the readings were a bit difficult to parse and having a reconstruction of the writer's argument might've been helpful in untangling the text.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627369\n 1627369\n \n 1627369\n Professor Winkler is definitely a strength. He was highly engaging and amazing at explaining philosophical ideas. I always felt like I left class with a much better understanding of the week's reading. He also left extremely detailed comments on papers (over 1 page long) that were very helpful for improving my writing. One weakness was that there is so much material to cover in the reading that often we would not get through all the main points in seminar discussions. However, we definitely had interesting and detailed discussions about the points we did get to.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627652\n 1627652\n \n 1627652\n I appreciate the small seminar layout of the course, and Professor Winkler facilitated discussion such that everyone had a chance to share their thoughts. As with other Directed Studies courses, I think the curriculum could stand to be diversified--I found the Arabic philosophy we read to be quite compelling, and I can't help but to wonder if there were woman philosophers or philosophers outside of the ancient Greek and Christian traditions from the same time period that could similarly enrich our understanding of the Western Canon.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627742\n 1627742\n \n 1627742\n Strengths: DS philosophy in general is a good intro philosophy course, Professor Winkler's teaching and feedback is excellent, robust discussion sections. Weaknesses: although there is an informative handout on how to write a philosophical analysis, how to write philosophy essays in general could have been taught more (although this is likely a DS issue in general). As a student without a background in philosophy, I found myself googling "how to write a philosophy paper" and reading a guide from...an unnamed school in Cambridge the night before our third paper was due because I had never been consciously trying to generate my own philosophical ideas before beyond reconstructing and criticizing those of the authors we read. DS does have a philosophy tutor available; however, she was never mentioned again after a brief announcement at the beginning of the semester, so I don't know how many students actually received help from her. The other writing support available are the Poorvu center tutors, but these range from DS-specific residential college tutors to other students and all have varying levels of familiarity with philosophy. Your best bet for help is probably your professor's office hours; Professor Winkler was wonderful about offering many of these sessions, but general instruction for philosophy papers probably would have still been helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629769\n 1629769\n \n 1629769\n It is generally a very strong course. It teaches well, about interesting subjects, builds upon previous knowledge and within the course builds on each previous text. Its two weaknesses are: lack of diversity in the philosophers (though I'm not sure this is possible to remedy given extant materials from ancient times, and perhaps this is more of a concern in the second half of DS) and the speed of the course. Since we covered so much, if I had an off day I felt like I completely missed a huge chunk of each philosopher; however, it did allow me to gain tons of knowledge quickly.
