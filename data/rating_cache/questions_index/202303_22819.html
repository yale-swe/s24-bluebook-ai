\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702436\n 1702436\n \n 1702436\n S: Fantastic teaching, great discussions which other students that exposed me to different points of view about Dylan's music, very accessible professor who was always hosting open office hours where we could develop our ideas and go into more depth with him, and a fantastic selection of assigned readings that contributed to an understanding of the meaning of the songs, as well as the history behind them. \r\nW: as this was the first time this course was taught, the framework for the assignment prompts was a little bit underdeveloped, but I think that after teaching the course over more and more semesters, an intuitive framework for prompts and the schedule for listening to the songs will come naturally.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1705396\n 1705396\n \n 1705396\n The strengths of this course are the in-class discussions and writing assignments. One weakness is that it occasionally can be hard to find opportunities to speak in class. The course could be improved by limiting the time a student has to make a point. Such a limit would make it easier for everyone to speak.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1705400\n 1705400\n \n 1705400\n The class was very strong on the discussion side of things, as every class was just an open talk about whatever the assigned listening was that week. It was definitely not hard to participate and when you did, you always felt like your ideas were really being taken into consideration. It was a very cool class to have taken because everyone in it was so passionate about the class, so discussions were always very interesting. I thought the only weakness was the lack of feedback when it came to discussion posts. I would have liked a little bit more guidance on whether or not I was approaching analysis correctly and how to improve my writing skills.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1711619\n 1711619\n \n 1711619\n This course was fascinating and extremely comprehensive as we studied dozens of Dylan albums and likely hundreds of songs. The class discussion was lively and Professor Barasch guided the discussion in fresh directions with supplemental readings and media. All participants were very involved and the caliber of discussion greatly increased over the course of the semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722434\n 1722434\n \n 1722434\n Strengths: I love the linear structure of the course, quite the opposite of much of Dylan's songwriting. The course followed Dylan's entire discography in chronological order. This allowed for much comparative study of Dylan's music across albums and periods. I loved the weekly song/theme analyses in the class. It allowed for me to hone in my writing and be more concise in my analysis of poetry. I also loved the major papers for the class, especially the final paper because I was able to talk about a theme which I saw across many songs throughout Dylan's career. To track a theme is to also see how it changes, and as a way to end the class, the paper succeeded in allowing for me to evaluate my appreciation for Dylan's craft as a result.\r\nWeaknesses: The course could have included more aspects of media to a certain extent. The purpose of the class, at its core, is to appreciate the craft of Dylan's words, not to evaluate his character through his actions. However, certain documentaries like Don't Look Back, Eat the Document, and No Direction Home allow for those to get into the mind of Dylan on a much more personal basis. The class could have evaluated these movies and how they succeed or not succeed in allowing the audience into Dylan's world.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722585\n 1722585\n \n 1722585\n Strengths- fun, interesting, engaging\r\nWeaknesses- slow at times, grading was sketchy
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723280\n 1723280\n \n 1723280\n The class is incredibly well structured and allowed us to grow as critical readers of text along with Dylan's personal and musical growth. That is the class' genius. We are able to more astutely dissect his work as it grows more complicated.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724947\n 1724947\n \n 1724947\n Strengths: great classroom discussions, open-format engagement, the concert (real world application of course material!!!!!!), office hours and the conversations they prompted\r\nWeaknesses: class sometimes bordered on hero-worship of Dylan, and I wish we had looked at him as more of a nuanced figure and recognized some of his downsides/faults

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722434\n 1722434\n \n 1722434\n I would recommend this course to another student under the premise that they appreciate poetic verse and lyrical content. This is not a "gut" course. It is not a survey of an artist's career in regards to their relation to the socio-cultural structures of their heyday. As previously mentioned, it is at its core a class teaching collegiate-level close reading through Dylan's verse. A student does not need to be a diehard of Bob Dylan or familiar with all of his music, but the student should be open to exploring the history of popular music in the 20th and 21st century in general and lyrical content on the level of Dylan in particular. The student should not be fooled with the course looking like a way to just listen to music as assignments. In-depth analysis of a multitude of songs per album is required for the class to operate, so there is a lot expected of students to be able to have a coherent class.
