\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1646925\n 1646925\n \n 1646925\n Strength: the syllabus is awesome, and just doing the readings, you learn so much.\r\nWeakness: the seminar was way too many people, which really decreased the quality of the conversation. It should have been limited far more, because having so many people just made everyone's comments feel dispersed and non-connected. I wanted to have a more engaged conversation figuring out the texts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1652030\n 1652030\n \n 1652030\n I think the only weakness of this course was trying to squeeze three of the most profound thinkers within Western philosophy into the same class. I believe the depth of these philosophers can only be grasped in a seminar focused solely on them alone. Aside from that, I believe we accomplished about as much as we could within the time span we had for each thinker and our conversations were well structured. Every other aspect of the course was fantastic.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1653078\n 1653078\n \n 1653078\n The course was great! Darwall is amazing, obviously, and the class discussions were interesting enough to keep me awake on Tuesday nights. The course was very well organized, as we got handouts each week with a list of topics we were going to cover. But Darwall also wasn't completely tied to the handout, so if something more interesting came up we'd pursue it. I think that's how seminars should run. One thing is that there were probably too many people, probably because a lot of people audited, which made it feel crowded and sometimes I had to wait a really long time to speak. But I didn't really mind because everyone else had such interesting things to say.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1658366\n 1658366\n \n 1658366\n There should be less reading because we never got through the material in any class. Also, the format is not great because we just end up summarizing the reading instead of discussing anything.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1659241\n 1659241\n \n 1659241\n The course is an excellent way to start to get into Marx, Kierkegaard, and Nietzsche's thought. You read a large selection of the works given that it is a single semester course and I felt I understood their philosophies more leaving the course. Professor Darwall is also great at welcoming everyone in the class to contribute and making sure people understand the readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1671421\n 1671421\n \n 1671421\n This course was absolutely wonderful. My one suggestion is that it be taught as a seminar that meets twice per week next time, instead of once per week. It was disappointing to never be able to get through the material, especially since our class discussions were so great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1672980\n 1672980\n \n 1672980\n It was a great class, but the large class size was a mixed bag for me. I felt that it was nice hearing many different views, but it also made it harder to participate. \r\n\r\nI also wished that we met more than once a week
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1674289\n 1674289\n \n 1674289\n Decent introduction to the philosophers. More of an exposition course, little critique. \r\n\r\nReadings on Kierkegaard not ideal -- too much time on Either/Or vs later works. \r\n\r\nWould have been interesting to have at least one summary lecture to link them all together.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1674654\n 1674654\n \n 1674654\n The breadth of the readings was simultaneously a strength and a weakness. Members of the class could find a variety of topics for response and discussion, almost always without repetition. This brought a diversity of perspectives and offered broad exposure to the readings. But because the readings were so broad, it felt impossible to get through all of them. If it takes me 10 minutes to read a page of Hegel (which it usually does), and the reading for the first class was 76 pages of Hegel, then I'd need 13 hours to fully prepare for class, plus a few more if I had a response paper due that week. I never got that much time together. I did, however, become a better reader through the class, and acclimated to each author's style after few weeks with their work.\r\n\r\nThe reading load also shaped the form of our class discussions. It seemed like there were a few people who could speak on the entire reading (very impressive, honestly), but most of us tended to speak on only a few sections--so different groups of people could speak in different portions of the class. This was different from the Yale seminars I'm used to, though not necessarily worse. If more cohesion is wanted, I would love to be be able to read one another's response papers* or see Professor Darwall's handout before class. That way, everyone could prepare some thoughts (response to the response).\r\n\r\n*This kind of accountability might help the significant portion of us who struggled with deadlines, too.
