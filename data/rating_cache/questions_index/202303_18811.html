\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1701850\n 1701850\n \n 1701850\n Depends. IF you pure Math major taking this class to learn how integration, differentiation, and manifolds are rigorously defined in R^n then I would say yes, this class covers some really cool material that's incredibly useful for application to other branches of Math and theoretical Physics. Prof. Wang is incredibly sweet and a great Professor if you try to get to know her. She responds to emails and offers to schedule appointments outside of her office hours if you cannot make it to those. Lectures were a bit dry, and it did feel like we spent a bit too much time on integration in R^n to the point where multilinear algebra, tensors, and forms felt a bit rushed. Unfortunately, class enrollment was less than 40% by the end of the semester, but if you went to lecture, Prof. Wang would give you all the necessary Definitions and Theorems you needed, which made struggling with the (in my opinion, not the best) textbook (Munkres) less necessary. Grading on the Psets was a bit harsh, but it was more to do with the graders we got stuck with rather than it being the Professor's fault. HOWEVER, IF you are thinking of taking this class just to learn Calc III... Don't. Take Math 120 instead. Trust me. This class will NOT teach you Calc III (in fact, I feel like Math 120 should be a prerequisite, because I have no idea how I would have gotten through some of the material had I not taken it!) It's not a Professor thing, but rather how this class *is*you do proofs and no applications (for example, if it's taught in the way it was this semester, you will nevernot even oncehear the words gradient, divergence or curl in this classthey aren't taught. Neither is Green's Theorem, nor Lagrange multipliers, nor the ordinary Stokes's Theorem in the form it's taught in Math 120). So if you are expecting to know how to deal with the vector calculus needed in other STEM classes like E&M or Econ after taking this class... you won't! I promise you that. But of course, applications aren't the point of this classthis is more of a warning for anyone thinking of this class as a "more serious" Math 120it's not. They are completely different. Although the PSets were mostly computational, it was Proof-based computation that doesn't teach you how to actually do Calc III, but rather the type that teaches you how to apply the theorems in the book to book-specific problems. This was a bit challenging to do, because very rarely did we do examples in class and Munkres is an awful textbook when it comes to actually showing you how to apply the theorems it gives you (I have never seen a math textbook with LESS examples in my entire life!). The Proofs in that textbook are also quite awfulmost of them are several pages long and very difficult to follow, which can be quite overwhelming, especially at the start of the semester. TA's weren't the most helpful and office hours were at a very awkward time (last weekly office hours were the morning 2 days before the Pset deadline). BUT the class was overall doable and a good experience.
