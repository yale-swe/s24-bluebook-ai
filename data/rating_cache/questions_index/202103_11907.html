I found that I had never taken a multivariable calc class before and about half of the people in the class had in high school, making it frustrating since they had learned much of the material and I was struggling to learn it at such a fast pace. I felt that the pre-class videos and work was lengthy, though good to get a brief introduction and the problem sets had very difficult problems that were harder than what we had done in class. I thought I didn't get enough repetition solving problems on the homework, since the problems were often lengthy and difficult and it made preparing for exams difficult. I would have liked to have a large lecture hall taught by one experienced professor so everyone is learning equally and getting fed the same content, or having one experienced lecturer's class recorded and posted so that I could go back and listen to them teach in a different manner. \r\n\r\nI am on a varsity athletics team and on days I missed class it could be difficult to learn content. I found I could get the class notes and watch the introductory concept videos but I didn't have as good of an understanding of the material or ability to ask questions. My instructors' office hours were during practice and the only help I could get was from peer tutors during their sessions mostly geared towards the homework. I would have really appreciated a recorded class to be able to both have when missing class for athletics and to be able to go back to and watch since I didn't like my instructors teaching style and would have liked to learn the content again, or get additional clarification.
