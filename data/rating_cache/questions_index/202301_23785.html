\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1671530\n 1671530\n \n 1671530\n Do you know anything about the archeology of ancient Eurasia? How about Mongolian culture of the past and present? Probably not, or at least, I didn't until taking this amazing course. I don't know why I signed up for it, but the reviews looked good. So I did, and I am so happy I did. This course is one of the best I have ever taken at Yale. Professor Honeychurch is a unique, highly-energetic, and passionate professor who will have you caring about the tiniest details of archeology drama. Don't be afraid of the archeology part of this class! I'm an English major. There were econ and pre-med and history majors in there, and we all did well. You will also have the unique experience of meeting NELC majors and grad students who know so much stuff about archeology it's scary. They can just read all these ancient scripts on the tablets we discuss, and it's very very impressive and fun. Take this class! But be warned! It is a decent amount of work, but for some reason, I found myself wanting to do it. Archeology papers and essays never seemed so tempting. Also be ready to laugh during the class. Professor Honeychurch isn't the most serious guy.
