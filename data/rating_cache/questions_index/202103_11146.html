\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521183\n 1521183\n \n 1521183\n Strengths: The readings for this class are so interesting and fun. They covered a wide variety of cultures, time periods, genres, and tones and were always a joy to discuss in class. Professor Jordan is absolutely amazing and always knew how to stimulate the conversation and help us to understand deeper elements of the texts. We had lots of freedom with the assignments and always got super helpful feedback on our first drafts. \r\n\r\nWeakness: The only weakness was that the syllabus was sometimes unclear or would reference a text that we didn't have access to. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527328\n 1527328\n \n 1527328\n Strengths: Maria is a truly one of a kind teacher!! \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527355\n 1527355\n \n 1527355\n Its a great course if you want an intensive curriculum based around improving your literary criticism skills. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527406\n 1527406\n \n 1527406\n There is not a single weakness. The strength is the professor. I wish every professor at Yale was like Mara. I also love how she changed the syllabus as we knew each other better to accommodate everyone and fulfill everyone's expectations, covering a wide range of material taking into account everyone's interests.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527482\n 1527482\n \n 1527482\n If you have mostly taken instructional Spanish classes, this course is a great opportunity to apply your knowledge in a creative space. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527586\n 1527586\n \n 1527586\n I love we were allowed so much creative freedom. I also loved sharing our stories in class. Some of the lectures on poetry toward the end were a bit tedious... it would have been fun to do a project involving all the authors or writing in the style of our favorite author from class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527722\n 1527722\n \n 1527722\n Strengths: Professor Jordan is the kindest and most invested professor I have ever had at Yale. She put so much thought into reading all of our work and was understanding when we needed extra help or extensions. I really enjoyed all the readings selected for the class and reading my peers writing. One strength was the group of students itself- it's a lot of fun when people are engaged. \r\nWeaknesses: You shouldn't expect to improve your grammar and overall proficiency much in this class because the content of the work is much more important that your syntax and grammar errors. The poetry unit felt overly long but I don't like poetry.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528272\n 1528272\n \n 1528272\n Great readings and fantastic assignments. Sometimes it was a little confusing what we had to turn in and when.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538411\n 1538411\n \n 1538411\n Strengths: Profesora Jordn, the feedback from Profesora Jordn, and the workshops!! I found her comments and feedback from workshops very helpful in developing as a writer and learning how to best tell my stories in Spanish. The setup of the class with one day of literary analysis to learn methods, techniques, and have exposure to how different genres of creative writing are in Spanish and then having a chance to put them in practice with writing and workshopping is perfect. I loved how eager everybody was in participating in class; I have taken some Spanish courses where few people jumped in but the seminar setup really was conducive to learning. I genuinely loved this class and would take another like it in the future. I liked how I could also reach out to Profesora Jordn without problem; she was always so excited and willing to speak with me about both literature and writing!\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I would say that the workload was a lot at times, with the written pieces due the day before class and then having blog posts and readings on top of that. However, Profesora Jordn was very understanding when other courses or commitments interfered with our ability to hand in assignments on time. I would have also liked to switch up the groups we worked in a bit more, so that we would be able to hear the perspective of other students, although that also happened in class when we all shared our ideas with everybody. For the poetry unit, I think it would be useful to include more poetry samples that are longer in the mix as well. Overall, these are minor, and I really really don't have complaints about the course.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538411\n 1538411\n \n 1538411\n YES! Everybody at Yale should take a creative writing course at least once. Creative writing in Spanish presents you a novel experience with writing than what you are used to in English (if you are L2 or heritage Spanish and not a native speaker). It's a challenge not only with writing but organizing and transforming your mentality to one in another language and culture. I always find writing in Spanish liberating, although it isn't easy, and find that I am better able to convey my thoughts in another language than I can in English. This is because I have to slow down, take the time to actually think about what I want to say and figure out how to best say it. In English, I quickly and sloppily jot down anything that comes to mind, but in Spanish, I take the time to search for synonyms and appropriate words that will best suit the type of theme, plot, character development, and moral I am going for. In the end, when you see all that you have created with your own hands (and with the enriching comments of Profesora Jordn and your classmates), there is a feeling of satisfaction that is unmatched. You will also have a polished portfolio of various kinds of Spanish creative writing pieces as sample works for any future endeavors. Long story short: if you're thinking about taking this course, take it! It would be amazing if this course was the introductory course for other, specialized creative writing courses in the department as well -- there is an energy and class dynamic particular to creative writing courses that simply CANNOT be match in technical/analytical essay-focused or cultural-focused language courses.
