RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521873\n 1521873\n \n 1521873\n Very very few strengths that are greatly outweighed by the weaknesses. Lab point deductions are completely arbitrary. Post and Pre lab quizzes without open notes are just plain unnecessary. Why should I need to memorize and rigorously study each lab report so that I have it memorized? If the grading process was improved maybe I would feel neutral about this class. Taking two semesters of chem lab is tedious. Most other undergraduate institutions only require one. \r\nI often found myself dreading the walk up to science hill each day before class as I knew I had four hours of misery ahead. The labs are somewhat interesting, but I wish students had more of a say in what they could do. Its really just regurgitating clumsy procedures before hand, that can actually be simplified/better by the student.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525284\n 1525284\n \n 1525284\n This class was helpful in deepening my understanding of chemistry because it covers each topic/experiment very thoroughly. Additionally, Dr. G is extremely kind and genuinely wants everyone to understand each concept, which creates a very encouraging learning environment. \r\n\r\nThe one weakness of the class was the workload. Although the course is only worth half a credit, I spent at least double the amount of time per week on this class than I would spend in a full credit course. I found the pre-lab lectures and videos to be helpful, and I thought the lab reports were very useful in building a full understanding of the material. However, I thought that the post-lab quizzes ended up being more stressful than helpful; I was already working on the lab report to gain the same understanding of the previous week's experiment, so the additional quiz seemed redundant. Additionally, it was stressful to juggle studying for both the pre-lab and the post-lab quizzes in the same 24 hour time span there was just a lot of material to cover. It might be helpful to eliminate the post-lab quiz, or to just make it an assignment graded for completion rather than for correctness.
