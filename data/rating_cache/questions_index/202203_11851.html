\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1620164\n 1620164\n \n 1620164\n I think that the major strength of this course was that it was a first year seminar; one was really able to see how all the students grew and developed in their knowledge and abilities over the semester. I also think the seminar format was excellent; a class on ethics such as this really benefits from conversation, rather than lectures. I think the reading selection was well-done, and most, if not all of the readings, developed my understanding of the topic that we were studying in a nuanced way. Professor Rosas really brought the course together and was superb, I don't think the course could have been as good without him. His great strength is guiding the conversation, by not really inputting his own comments, but facilitating dialogue between the students. He also makes sure that every student who wants to speak is able to, which is very nice for more introverted students. I would say that one way for this course to be improved would be to add in another essay or two (there are 3 essay assignments currently). Now, one may complain that this creates more work, which it would, but I think the benefits would far outweigh the negatives. First of all, my writing definitely improved (especially in structure) from the first essay to the last, but I imagine I could see even more improvements with one more essay. I also think that if there were more essays, then this course could fulfill a writing requirement. Also more essays would make the course grading easier, since less weight would be put on each essay. I understand there would be logistical complications to this though, since Professor Rosas is the Dean of Murray, and is extremely busy, so adding more essays to his schedule may not be feasible; Professor Rosas also spends a lot of time grading each essay and gives extensive feedback which is a huge strength. Excellent course!
