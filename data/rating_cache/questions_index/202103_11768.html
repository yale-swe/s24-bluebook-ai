\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521987\n 1521987\n \n 1521987\n Through taking this course, I developed a genuine love and appreciation for writing, one that I did not have before. I always wanted to get into reading modern essays, but before this course, I never made a point in beginning to sift through the genre. The essays that Rachel chose were all diverse in essayists, style, and topic which made it clear to me how there is no specific way to write or be a writer. The only boundaries are what you create for yourself.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1529710\n 1529710\n \n 1529710\n Writing
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530285\n 1530285\n \n 1530285\n We read a range of wonderful essays this semester, many by authors I had never heard of before but am so glad to have read. Through these readings, we learned and developed strategies to craft effective prose in our own work. This course (and Rachel specifically!) has made me realize that good writing takes practice and thought, but that anyone can be a writer.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530999\n 1530999\n \n 1530999\n I learned so much about what writing could be. Especially non-fiction writing. I learned that I am the authoritative voice on what is and what is not allowed in my writing. And I learned how to look at other writing styles and readings and how to use the aspects of those styles that I like. I also learned that writing is revising.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532129\n 1532129\n \n 1532129\n Skills in close reading of non fiction texts, personal essay writing, seminar discussion format, providing helpful peer feedback
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536091\n 1536091\n \n 1536091\n I have never written a non-academic essay for a class before, and this class has been the best possible introduction. I gained knowledge of a wide range of different writers and styles, and learnt how to implement their styles and techniques in my own writing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544478\n 1544478\n \n 1544478\n ENGL 120 is one of my favorite classes this semester. Through reading and writing essays on 5 different topics, I explored them deeply and also discovered more about myself. My writing skills have also improved through the process.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544529\n 1544529\n \n 1544529\n This course places a strong emphasis on honing in on your unique voice as a writer through the personal essay. I learned to value short and long-form essay writing that captured the emotive truths of its authors lived experiences and witnessing. I learned to love drafts and move away from expecting my first attempt to be anywhere near the final product. Mostly, I learned to write with clarity, heart and conviction.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544569\n 1544569\n \n 1544569\n I learned about different types of writing, both how to produce my own works and how they are part of the modern world. I was brave enough to BE vulnerable and saw the impact that had on my writing and its audience. \r\nI learned about different techniques used to evoke certain emotions from the reader and portray a specific image, such as omission, exaggeration, defamiliarization, specific uses of punctuation, admitting doubt, leaving the ending without a "bow".

RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521987\n 1521987\n \n 1521987\n I don't feel like there is any weakness in this course. Rachel is such an attentive and perceptive teacher, who successfully made the class environment one where everyone felt comfortable to share things that they might not have otherwise. I didn't particularly enjoy our workshop discussion classes, but I know that other students did, and they were necessary and helpful when it came down to it. Whether you need an extension, more direction/clarity, or assistance in anyway (related to the class or not), Rachel was so easy and welcoming to go to.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1529710\n 1529710\n \n 1529710\n Fun topics and a lot of freedom are good. Sometimes unexciting readings are not so good.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1529955\n 1529955\n \n 1529955\n The units are well designed and strategically placed. However, the experience within each unit varies depending on which pieces your prof. chooses for that essay.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530285\n 1530285\n \n 1530285\n The essays we read, the professor, the workshops, and the feedback on our work were all great! The only weakness of this course for me was that the schedule for turning in our essays was sometimes a little strange or inconvenient. I think it might make more sense to have essays due on class days (when possible), instead of days between classes.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530999\n 1530999\n \n 1530999\n (Prof. Rachel Kauder Nalebuff, section 14, fall 2021) I really liked the pace of the course. A lot of my stem-oriented friends thought the amount of reading and writing seemed scary, but I enjoyed all of it. The readings never felt long at all because they were so interesting and well-written. The class workshop seems intimidating but it was one of my favorite parts. We started doing in-class writing exercises toward the end of the semester which was amazing, so I wish we had started those sooner.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532129\n 1532129\n \n 1532129\n The strengths of this course include the workshops where your writing is discussed by peers, the discussion format, in-class writing assignments and excellent professor feedback. No weaknesses.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536091\n 1536091\n \n 1536091\n I think this is a very good choice of a course should you choose to engage with it fully, do the readings and think about how they should impact your writing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544478\n 1544478\n \n 1544478\n Strengths: 1) Rachel is definitely the highlight of this course. She is a wonderful instructor - she is very knowledgeable, nice, and encouraging. I especially appreciate her way of teaching because class discussions are never limited to content of the readings; Rachel tries to let us learn from the readings and apply those techniques to our own writing. 2) Although this course involves quite a lot of writing, I think it is amazing that we get to explore 5 different topics. 3) It is wonderful to workshop different classmates' essays. I feel that I learned as much from their writings as I did from course readings. Additionally, I think Rachel developed an effective workshop mechanism! 4) In the last unit (Humor), Rachel invited 2 guest speakers to join us, which also added light to the course experience.\r\n\r\nHow the course might be improved: Maybe invite one guest speaker in the Culture Commentary unit as well?
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544529\n 1544529\n \n 1544529\n The wide range in essay themes, diverse mix of writers in our readings and collaborative and communal nature of this course are all exceptional strengths. I cannot think of any weaknesses.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544569\n 1544569\n \n 1544569\n A huge strength of this course was the peer feedback. My piece that was workshopped by everyone was the strongest of the pieces I wrote for the class. I would even take it a step further: what if for each draft, you could get one or two classmates to give feedback on it? \r\nSomething that was a bit complicated was the submission process of our pieces on Canvas. The Collaboration feature worked fine until we had to submit multiple drafts, and keep the edits from the past drafts. Just a minor inconvenience, but the process could be streamlined. \r\nThe structure of roughly an essay a week was a strength. The pieces we read usually gave interesting examples that reflected the unit we were in (even if I personally didn't connect with a piece, I could see why Rachel chose it). \r\nSometimes, our class was very quiet. People could have talked more when discussing the readings. This was not Rachel's fault, but perhaps instead of letting us sit in silence for so long (because for a bit is very useful), she could break us off more in pairs / small groups and then ask us to share. Sometimes, she lingered on topics that were saturated and it was time to move on to other things. \r\nOverall: way more strengths than weaknesses.
