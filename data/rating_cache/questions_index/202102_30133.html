\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1514892\n 1514892\n \n 1514892\n Strengths: The class possessed a lot of intellectual infrastructure and support with regards to the teaching staff when it came to students that were pursuing research in the biological sciences. On a related note, the class exposed us to the EndNote citation manager software, which in retrospect, was a useful way to compile a centralized bank of scientific literature that could be readily available to cite in any assignment that we were doing for the class. Additionally, the class ultimately did facilitate the development of the endurance and skillset necessary to present a 15-minute talk on scientific research, albeit these presentations did occur virtually. \r\nWeaknesses: This class is not really built for folks who are not involved in research pertaining to the biological sciences. Expectations when it came to slide and graphic design, presentation delivery, and harshness of grading were very imbalanced and inconsistent across all three of the sections. I would say that the project was a huge distraction from the actual purpose of the program and should not be included in future iterations of the class. I still personally do not understand what the point of the project was, considering we did not learn the software in anywhere near enough detail to use for anything more than personal side projects. While I do understand that ArcGIS can be useful for certain applications, like epidemiology and climate change/sustainability, the software is best suited for a semester-long course and not really a six-week period in a course that is already operating on somewhat of a compressed schedule.
