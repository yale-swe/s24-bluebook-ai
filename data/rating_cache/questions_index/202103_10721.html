\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521604\n 1521604\n \n 1521604\n I learned about the IAD and SES frameworks, polycentricity, Elinor Ostrom's and others' brilliance, and a lot about the Maine lobster fishery. \r\n\r\nI became a better writer. There's a lot of writing in this class, from short weekly memos to two medium-length papers. I also became a better thinker in the context of environmental policymaking. I underestimated the power of the "what is really going on here?" question. \r\n\r\nThis class transformed the way that I view economics (speaking as an economics major who took this class for related course credit). The essence of the IAD -- zooming in on actors, understanding community characteristics, analyzing rules in use, and summarizing outcomes -- seems like a pretty obvious thing to do when developing policy, but it seems like economics at the intro and intermediate level simply stresses "utility maximization" or "cost minimization" without 1) understanding how such objectives can be achieved through institutions; and 2) justifying why the "max" and "min" approach is the best policymaking strategy. It's clear from this class that people themselves (rather than markets and governments) can solve their own problems. Broadly, there's a whole lot of incredibly valuable things that I learned in this class -- beyond the ideas that I've tried to articulate here.
