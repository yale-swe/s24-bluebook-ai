Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521241\n 1521241\n \n 1521241\n One strength of the course is the ability to chose your labs for the semester. You can work on experiments which you find truly interesting. Another strength is that you have the time to delve deep into each lab. Unlike 205L/206L, you have time to really understand what's going on in each lab. A third strength is that there is a lot of room for flexibility. Many of the labs will not take the entire 24 hours of class time allotted, so you can explore extensions to what is in the lab handout. You will get out what you put in, so if you do not take the opportunities to do additional projects you will not get much out of the course. A final strength is that the professors are very kind, knowledgable, and patient. They will remind you why you love physics! One weakness of this course is that some of the labs are unbalanced in workload, although this is not too much of an issue because you will find opportunities for more work even in the lighter labs.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528557\n 1528557\n \n 1528557\n The strengths of this course include the excellent help we received from Professor Lamoreaux and the exciting quantum cryptography lab. The main weakness of the course stems from the unclear lab handouts, often containing unclear wording and/or typos. This caused confusion often that was cleared up by the instructors, but the handouts should still be updated to read better. Further, not much insight is given in conducting the data analysis, which required help from the instructors or outside sources.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531325\n 1531325\n \n 1531325\n Strengths: fun labs, cool physics, very reasonable workload. Profs. Maruyama, Lamoreaux and Cahn were all helpful and supportive. Weaknesses: Although this course is a writing credit, I felt that we did not receive a level of feedback on our lab reports comparable to that in other WR courses. I would have liked to have gotten more specific suggestions and guidelines on how to produce good scientific writing. The Ising Model lab is kind of tedious.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536148\n 1536148\n \n 1536148\n Overall, this was a very good course. Strengths: The labs were interesting and challenging. I had the chance to experience in-depth labs in areas of physics that I am less familiar in. Plus, there were only three labs in the semester, so I had the time to fully develop each experiment and analysis instead of rushing to get a lab report out every week like in 206. This lab is a more mature, better paced version of 206. Weaknesses: There was not much instruction on how to go about estimating uncertainties, so we were largely left to figure this out on our own. Also, there was no instruction or guides on how to use Python or Matlab, so I had to pick up a lot of those programming skills on the fly. It ended up working out, but it would have been nice to know going in. Also, the "how to give a talk" talk felt rather patronizing
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537057\n 1537057\n \n 1537057\n Strengths: expertise of the instructors and willingness of the instructors to help you out, enough flexibility to accommodate for experiments taking longer than usual or things going wrong or unexpected, and the way you have to work independently with your group to troubleshoot things
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1542184\n 1542184\n \n 1542184\n The experimental labs were very interesting and well-organized, but the computational lab was a little confusing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1542189\n 1542189\n \n 1542189\n Strengths: This course is VASTLY better than the intro sequence. The labs are (mostly) enjoyable and interesting, and that they allow one an entire month to work on them actually allows one to get a deeper understanding of what is going on. Professors Maruyama and Lamoreaux really care about the labs and will go out of their ways to help you (especially Lamoreaux). And it gives one a writing credit! Go straight to the professor leading the lab. Most of the labs are well-outlined and explained, but some are a little bit less so. I was excited about the concept of the mini-lectures, but they were extremely surface-level, poorly done, and generally a complete waste of time.
