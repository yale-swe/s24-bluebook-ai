Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512476\n 1512476\n \n 1512476\n Some of the strengths include the opportunities for participation, opportunities to work with other students, talking to guest speakers, and the professor. \r\n\r\nSome of the weaknesses include very long lecture periods. There were several classes where I felt like the class was dragging on. We each had to go over our first pages and then our entire scripts in front of the class, and they were a little hard to get through.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512780\n 1512780\n \n 1512780\n This course is great because it demands a lot from its students. Writing a screenplay is a challenging task and requires students to dig deep for a story that they want to tell and read a lot of screenplays. We were required to do script coverage and plot point analysis which gave us a sense of cinematic timing, dialogue, plot development, and action. The group critiques were also a great way to get exposure to others' stories and style of writing which improves everyones' writing. There were also great speakers who came in to speak about what it takes to forge a career in the film industry. \r\n\r\nThe weaknesses are that there was not much guidance on how to complete the midterm and students are thrown into the process of writing without much hand holding. This can be daunting and learning how to do script coverage without any instructions is difficult if a student doesn't have much confidence in their own writing abilities. The workload is also a lot and can get overwhelming. Another weakness is that Professor Lapadula's feedback can be too heavy handed at times.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513034\n 1513034\n \n 1513034\n Strength: It is perfect for new beginners to start writing screenplays. The professor teaches all the basics that though seem simple but is actually a very important habit to form. \r\nWeakness: Maybe we can analyze some movies from this century?
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513114\n 1513114\n \n 1513114\n The strength of the course was in its interactive nature. We were watching stuff and commenting on it, as well as bringing in our own material. Professor Lapadula also provides comprehensive feedback on all of your work. He also brought in interesting speakers, including one of the producers of La La Land. It was a tad bit long and had a tendency to go overtime though.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513443\n 1513443\n \n 1513443\n The strength was that while the course required a lot of work, by the end of the process I really learned a lot about screenwriting.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513638\n 1513638\n \n 1513638\n No improvementsawesome course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513641\n 1513641\n \n 1513641\n Strengths: every class was challenging and engaging, so even when they went overtime I was happy to stay and listen/chat.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I wish we had more instructions about what to discuss with our small groups? Mine only met twice.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513775\n 1513775\n \n 1513775\n The strengths were the professors passion for the topic. He treats every student as though they plan to go into screenwriting as a profession, which is very useful for learning. I think the class could be improved by having more class discussions. Because it was on zoom, it was harder to stay engaged for the length of time without constantly having discussions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1516125\n 1516125\n \n 1516125\n The structure of the class itself felt somewhat disorganized at times and I think it could be really helpful to have smaller deadlines throughout the course rather than the complete midterm folder at once.\r\n\r\nSome strengths of the course include film analysis as a class (ex: Rebel without a Cause), the assignments of the midterm folder (helpful for constructing the final screenplay), and the collaborative atmosphere of the class (in-class critiques and group meetings outside of class). The guest lecture with Fred Berger was also really interesting.
