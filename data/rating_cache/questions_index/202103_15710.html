\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1533674\n 1533674\n \n 1533674\n A strength of this course was that it wasn't too focused on teaching us grammar, and rather letting us speak ourselves, gradually improving and gaining confidence. What I didn't like about this course was that I felt that the topics we were covering were too large and nuanced. It is hard to offer a cohesive take on capitalism or racism when our speech is limited. Also, when we learned about latin American culture, I sometimes felt that we were focusing on really heavy topics of suffering and discrimination, when I, as a Mexican student, wanted us to learn about the richness of the culture and the good parts of latin American life. Also, when we spoke in Spanish and made mistakes, our professor never corrected us. I think she did this because she wanted to create a safe environment, which I really appreciate as speaking in an other language can be intimidating. However, I would've like some feedback on my errors made so that I could have corrected myself, because there were times where I just didn't remember what word or what tense was appropriate in a given situation. Also, I wish I would've known at the beginning of the semester that this was an experimental Spanish L4 class, because I'm not sure if I would have decided to enroll in it over a more traditional course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535106\n 1535106\n \n 1535106\n Strengths: There are plenty of opportunities to exercise and develop your Spanish-speaking abilities and the class allows you to apply your Spanish knowledge rather than simply memorizing tables of conjugations and vocabulary. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: None.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535439\n 1535439\n \n 1535439\n This course really excels at improving fluency, composition, and comprehension. I'd say the one weakness in the course is how grammar is taught. For me it was much more valuable to practice speaking and listening over re-learning the same grammar I'd been taught in past classes, but it might have been helpful if the grammar instruction was slightly more formal. The professor's strategy was to correct our grammatical errors as we spoke either by correcting us verbally or by writing the correct form on the blackboard. This worked fairly well, but it might be even more helpful if there were a brief, more formalized review of some of the mistakes that were made during the course of the class at the end of each period or something like that. As it was, even though we were being corrected, it would be easy to make the same kind of error repeatedly. This, however, was minor, as like I said, fluency was more important to me and I definitely improved in that respect.
