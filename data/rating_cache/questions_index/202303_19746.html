\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1711090\n 1711090\n \n 1711090\n This course is unlike any I'd ever taken at Yale; you get assessed on something completely different from exams, P-Sets, and papers. That is both a liberating and challenging experience. I say challenging to note that it can be outside of a lot of people's comfort zones. But most people, including me, seemed to adapt well. The course teaches you how to approach art in a new way; I found myself critically analyzing the styles of many artists after taking it. It also makes you confident about your skills. You gain a lot of ideas just by working with so many other people who approach the subject matter differently. At the same time, most of the people in the class were beginners; I never felt judged negatively but still picked up on many new areas of improvement.\r\n\r\nSince this class is meant to develop skills, so you definitely get what you put into this course. You could take it easy and not improve much over the semester or do extra work and benefit from it. The assignment can be challenging to ideate about, which I personally found fun. There aren't very solid guidelines on what you should or shouldn't do, so the way your art develops is up to you. I say that in case you expect to be drawing Renaissance-esque sketches by the end of the class--that will probably not be the case. But the class will help you understand how you can get where you want to be in terms of your artistic skills.
