\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616586\n 1616586\n \n 1616586\n The readings in this class were engaging, and Craig also provided helpful feedback, particularly in office hours. I liked the pacing of the assignments; I had enough time to edit my first drafts and turn in final drafts I was satisfied with. One way this class might be improved is if we had more line-by-line feedback, but overall I was very happy with the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1620180\n 1620180\n \n 1620180\n You are exposed to a lot of different types of writing, so you aren't stuck to mimicking one style of writing. You really get to find a style that works for you. I wish there was more one on one interaction with the instructors in order to get a more personal sense of how to improve.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622861\n 1622861\n \n 1622861\n Strengths: The example essays, the reading we'd do for class, the student-led discussions we'd have over the "how" portion of the essays beyond the "what"\r\nWeaknesses: I think more emphasis needs to be placed on the "personal narratives" portion of the class. I interpreted this as an opportunity for students to explore their own stories and get a better grasp on how to tell their narratives. I tried doing this, but I don't think this was expected despite being a personal narratives class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1623153\n 1623153\n \n 1623153\n This course developed my skills as a writer through its heavy emphasis on revision. Additionally, by reading so many different types of essays from different authors, I was prompted to explore different writing styles within my own texts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624789\n 1624789\n \n 1624789\n I think this course definitely has a lot of strengths. I really enjoy the conversational energy of the classes. I enjoy being able to break down our readings in detail as a class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1626130\n 1626130\n \n 1626130\n English 120 gave students the opportunity to engage with bite-sized pieces of great, modern literature and pick it apart to improve their own writing. The only weakness I can think of is only having one rough draft for each essay; something that could've helped me improve even more would have been having the opportunity to write and receive feedback on two drafts before turning in the final.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629386\n 1629386\n \n 1629386\n The format of reading one, two, or three relatively short essays and discussing them in class worked well. The system of turning in a draft for feedback before turning in the final version was very helpful. And the class workshop structure was extremely useful. I would not change the class structure, workload, or essay topics.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1631008\n 1631008\n \n 1631008\n The strength of this course is the vast amount of writing, reading, and feedback we do. This allows the student to consistently improve throughout the semester.\r\nThe weakness of this course is that, because of the amount of content, it could feel rushed at times.\r\nIn the future, I might recommend cutting one of the sections, and allowing students to either produce a third draft for an essay, or to pick a category for their final essay. For example, after writing my Place essay, I had a lot of new ideas about Place, but because we only produced one essay in that category I was never able to test those ideas out
