For example, we learned about how Emerson emphasizes that man is inherently good; Dickinson dwells on the externalization of the self; and Melville asserts the realism of his fictional characters.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593702\n 1593702\n \n 1593702\n This class allowed me to dive into the work of three incredible American authors. I grew as a writer, and gained confidence in evaluating arguments. The class discussions were very insightful and it was a pleasure to learn from such a soulful and intelligent group of my peers.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593891\n 1593891\n \n 1593891\n I learned a lot about three important figures in the American literary canon and got a great overview of the breadth of their works. I also explored how they can be in conversation with each other.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594066\n 1594066\n \n 1594066\n This course improved my analytical skills on a specific and broad scale. It helped me to read sources in conversation with each other, without exclusively looking at one particular tradition. Specifically, this class improved my asking of questions: I learned how to interrogate small sections of a text and frame them in various different lights. It also helped me develop as a participant in a seminar, as the conversations were engaging and highly collaborative.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594754\n 1594754\n \n 1594754\n This course will teach you how to think about texts ranging in theme but unified in stakes and history of influence. You will practice not just noticing and articulating patterns but determining their grander significance. Professor Deming is a big fan of the question, "So what?" This philosophy guides class discussions. This helped the entire class feel greater than an intellectual exercise. The texts and conversations matter. \r\nAs a student, you will write two papers (a midterm and a final) as well as a discussion paper and a set of discussion questions (used throughout the semester). None of these assignments have specific prompts, so you will also learn to craft arguments around your interest. This was a huge skill I took away from the class. Since these works are so famous, it can be hard to find new/personal readings. However, by teaching the class in SUCH an open/individual way, students had to find their own path into the text.
