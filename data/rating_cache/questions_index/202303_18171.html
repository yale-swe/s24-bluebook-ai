\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1698324\n 1698324\n \n 1698324\n Deb has a non-prescriptive teaching style, so while she pushes you to ask important questions about yourself, your characters, and your writing, the class does not offer much in terms of structure, feedback, or guidelines. I personally could have used more organization, instruction, and specific feedback, though it was a fun, creatively stimulating, and welcoming class environment.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1700658\n 1700658\n \n 1700658\n The main strengths of this class are Professor Margolin and the high engagement level. I found the freedom of the class to be very helpful to my creative process, though I can see why some students might prefer more oversight (specific due dates, pre-scheduled workshop dates, etc).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1707346\n 1707346\n \n 1707346\n More checkpoints and pages required throughout the semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710412\n 1710412\n \n 1710412\n Learning from your peers and from actual published, produced playwrights (Deb and Stef, the TF) is invaluable. You really have to be on top of yourself or make an accountability partner out of some friends in class so you don't fall behind in your actual writing of the play.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710721\n 1710721\n \n 1710721\n It was excellent. Deb is a wonderful professor, resource, and human being and her passion for the theatre and ability to teach the art by making us reflect on ourselves and our lives is unparalleled. No weaknesses!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1711511\n 1711511\n \n 1711511\n Strengths: You get to work with Deb, you learn to trust yourself, you get to hear some hillarious stories from Deb's carreer. I think a great strength is also that you get to hear your work being read out loud and decide what you need to change or if it's actually good. You get feedback on the spot as well and I found that really helpful.\r\n\r\nWeakness: I think the only weakness is that I never got some direction on tone, style, or any basic roadmap on how to write a play. I had never written a play and found it difficult to just start. I think some discussion on what tone is, what style you should be writting in would be helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722314\n 1722314\n \n 1722314\n Wonderful course with lots of feedback and time to workshop. Deb is a huge pro for the course, she is a truly kind and knowledgable guide through the playwriting process. The only downside was my own lack of time management skills. As there are only two deadlines in the course, it is easy to put off writing, which in turn makes writing more difficult. Maybe a few more deadlines would help others like me.
