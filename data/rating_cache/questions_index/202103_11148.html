\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1520854\n 1520854\n \n 1520854\n I gained greater insight into the complexities of Spanish grammar, increased my fluency in spoken Spanish, and practiced reading and writing in Spanish at a college level.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530463\n 1530463\n \n 1530463\n This course is aptly named. You will learn Spanish grammar, and I want to emphasize the fact that this course is indeed advanced. For people who have taken L4 courses in the past (or even L3), you will recognize a lot of the basic concepts: subjunctive, imperfect/preterito, etc. However, this course takes it to a much further level, and it moves quite quickly. \r\nAlthough this course is focused on written evaluations, you will have ample opportunity to practice speaking. The entire course is taught in Spanish, including office hours, emails, etc. As part of the curriculum, you will practice with native speakers in two ways. The first is a multi-week online coach whose weekly lessons correspond to one of the grammatical concepts you have been learning about. The second is a "pen pal" of sorts. You will have four one hour meetings, half in English half in Spanish, with a college student living in Ecuador.

I also got to practice speaking with a language coach and a student from Ecuador. I learned the official rules of Spanish which I didn't really know all too well before.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1547556\n 1547556\n \n 1547556\n Specific facets of Spanish grammar really well; lots of subjunctive and some work with adjectives/pronouns that I'd always wondered but never known.

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530463\n 1530463\n \n 1530463\n For strengths, all of the course materials are very well laid out to help you develop a thorough understanding of each topic, even though they can be hard. The lectures, combined with textbook explanations, homework, readings, movies, are timed perfectly so that you build upon each new topic. The course does move very fast, but the timing of assignments and assessments in the syllabus was never outrageous. It is also a perfect size of course to be able to ask questions and have enough time to practice speaking. In short, if you are willing to give this course enough time (and I am talking 1-2 hours for homework between each class plus any meetings or office hours), you will absolutely improve your understanding of Spanish language. \r\n\r\nSpeaking of weaknesses, I believe we could have had more structured conversations to practice the grammar spoken instead of just written. The only formal spoken assessment we had was a short debate at the end of one class. Although I greatly improved my grammar when writing, I need more practice in a classroom setting speaking what I have just learned and getting feedback on the spot when I made mistakes. Things like the language conversation exchanges were nice, but the other university student was not going to correct my grammar every time I made a mistake in Spanish.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535673\n 1535673\n \n 1535673\n The strengths are definitely the language partners to practice speaking, the professor is very supportive and knowledgeable, the textbook is really well written and we follow it along very closely so it was a good read. Another strength are that the assessments are fair, both the exams and essays although sometimes I feel as though we had too many assignments and it did feel a like a constant grind that was a little frustrating. Exams are really simple if you just read the textbook, they basically test exactly what the textbook mentions. The weaknesses are probably that a lot of the time in class we just review the assigned textbook reading so if you did the reading then basically class was a little redundant although it seemed many people didn't do the reading anyway. I think this class could have a little less homework but besides that great class,.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1542839\n 1542839\n \n 1542839\n Great variety of interaction with Spanish culture, ranging from movies to articles to stories. Also had the opportunity to have an exchange program with students in Ecuador to practice speaking. The workload is heavier due to being an L5 course however manageable as the professor was organized and pre posted many assignments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1542856\n 1542856\n \n 1542856\n strengths:interactions with native speakers. weaknesses: overwhelming amounts of work that did not really seem relevant for advancing communication skills
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1542881\n 1542881\n \n 1542881\n Strengths- frequent practice (homework and in-class discussions and activities) improved my fluency; tests and assignments were straightforward and explained well; conversation partners were fun and helpful\r\n\r\nWeaknesses- honestly, aside from my own personal failings, I dont think this course is flawed in any significant way. Its basically a continuation of L1-4, but in a way that will improve your Spanish significantly. I think it could be boring for some people, but for me the intellectual challenge of the language learning process was enough!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1547556\n 1547556\n \n 1547556\n Strengths: lots of practice speaking and writing\r\nWeaknesses: not enough practice with the topics after them being explained in class. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555087\n 1555087\n \n 1555087\n The course was definitely demanding and required a lot of work, but that is what forced me to become better at Spanish. All of the writing practice helped me understand the grammar rules a lot better. The homeworks due before class were helpful for getting familiarized with the content and getting some practice in for the content of the week. It was great that they were graded based on completion, as it really was just meant as practice rather than being a stressful assignment. I appreciated the variety of assignments given, which meant your grade did not heavily rely on one single element. The exams were reasonable, the papers were about fun topics, and the lectures were very engaging. The professor was very understanding about conflicts with deadlines and class, and was always willing to help you in the course in one way or another. She was a great teacher and person overall which made her class conducive to learning as well as enjoyable. It was also great to get a taste of Latin American culture through the stories we read and the movies we watched for the course.
