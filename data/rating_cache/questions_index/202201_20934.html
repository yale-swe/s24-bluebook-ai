Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1561858\n 1561858\n \n 1561858\n Strengths: you get a ton of background on human rights, both the theory and the international mechanisms that promote and enforce it\r\nWeaknesses: it's probably too broad for one semester. I understand the need to cover all of theories, practices, and politics, but I would have been able to engage with the material more critically if it had focused on one of those areas. Also, sometimes it felt like the course was structured strangely. For example, we covered international legal mechanisms before bodies of rights. How am I supposed to understand the courts' work if I don't know what laws they're referencing? I also think the class would benefit from reworking the grading scheme. Right now, almost 50% of the grade is based on the final paper, and that's really our only major assignment. How am I supposed to apply feedback if the only assignment on which I receive feedback is at the end of the course? I think it would be advantageous to have a midterm paper and a shorter final paper instead.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562653\n 1562653\n \n 1562653\n I wish this course got to the "bodies of rights" portion earlier in the semester, perhaps incorporating some of the theory/history/structure material into that study. I did enjoy the diversity of readings and felt there was always at least one I could resonate with.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1573741\n 1573741\n \n 1573741\n Strengths: Engaging lectures, sections were very informative\r\nWeakness: none
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1592482\n 1592482\n \n 1592482\n Strengths: Professor Beckerle and her experience in this area was the greatest strength for the class. The professor has vast knowledge of the course material and presented excellent, informative lectures every week. Additionally, the reading selection was generally very good, with several readings providing interesting and compelling arguments on the issues. Points for improvement: there were some moments in the semester that could have benefited from a lighter reading load in exchange for more in-depth conversations on the material.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1592483\n 1592483\n \n 1592483\n The course could be improved by having more major assignments throughout that allow for us to get a sense of what the professor and TAs are looking for in terms of style and substance of writing. It felt a little off to have one major essay worth nearly half of our grade and no other major papers throughout the semester that could accustom us/expose us to the grading style of the instructors. \r\n\r\nI would also decrease the amount of reading for the course and instead focus on the most important parts of readings to assign to students. I was unable to complete all of the reading most of the time and wouldve appreciated some guidance on what were the most important parts instead.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1592824\n 1592824\n \n 1592824\n Its an overview so lots of breadth with less depth. I really enjoyed the mix of formats for lectures, and the sections. I wish less weight was placed on the final paper, because it felt like the course was so engaging leading up to finals but that engagement/work was worth less.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593557\n 1593557\n \n 1593557\n Some of the strengths of this course include: \r\n-The lecture format, which allowed participation as well as learning \r\n-Professor Beckerle's interesting lectures and well-organized presentation \r\n-The very helpful TAs \r\n\r\nWeaknesses \r\n-The long reading that though helpful and interesting, sometimes consumed too much time and was not addressed during lectures \r\n-The 50-minute lecture format (I believe that with a longer lecture, we would have been to cover the wide variety of topics with a little more depth)
