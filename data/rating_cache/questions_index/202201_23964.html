We also had a small introduction to art history and its interaction with literature, which I greatly enjoyed.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1586215\n 1586215\n \n 1586215\n Became more fluent in modern Western literature
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1587427\n 1587427\n \n 1587427\n This was my favorite Literature class I have taken so far at Yale, and it's in part because Professor Spitz encourages students not to over-analyze literature. When you approach a text purely from an analytical lens, and lose sight of how a text makes you feel, you lose sight of what makes reading literature so enjoyable. Thus Professor Spitz encourages students to really allow themselves to feel emotions as they read and think about how the relationships and human experiences depicted in the texts relate to their own lived experiences. I really learned how to grapple with character's psychology this semester, how to connect what we read to art and music of the time, and to really immerse myself in the lives of these characters in order to try and empathize with them. I also learned how to discern why characters were motivated to act in the way that they did -- what emotions drove their actions? I also learned how to write personal essays that are based around my experience reading a particular text. That was something I had never done before! Another cool thing that I learned was how to prepare and lead a literature seminar discussion. Professor Spitz has every student in the class lead one discussion, and it was a really cool thing to learn how to pick passages and themes that would help foster discussion among my peers.
