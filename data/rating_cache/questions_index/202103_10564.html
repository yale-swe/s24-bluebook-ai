\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525449\n 1525449\n \n 1525449\n Strengths \r\n- really cool experiments in theory (anesthetizing/shocking worms to see their nerve responses, working with grasshoppers and flies, cutting out a tiny nerve in a frog leg, etc) \r\n- they seem really chill with grading/scoring the lab notebooks (overall, just a much more chill lab class than any other I've taken)\r\n\r\nWeaknesses\r\n- the experiments/equipment never actually end up working, so you have to just stay there for 4 hours fiddling with stuff/waiting for one of TAs to help you (even though they don't know how to do it successfully either lol), oftentimes just to get no data...can be very frustrating/boring during those 4 hours per week
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528517\n 1528517\n \n 1528517\n While the lab was very interesting and allowed us to study a variety of different neurobiological processes, it was very reliant on all of the equipment working (which was often not the case). So, a lot of time in lab was spent sitting and waiting for one of the TAs or Prof. Keshishian to come look at the setup, and even then, the recording would often not work. Therefore, it was hard to accomplish all the goals for the assigned lab in a given week, which became frustrating when trying to put together the lab notebook (where we have to write our observations/discussion for the lab). If all the pieces of equipment were tested before lab by the TAs, then lab might run more smoothly and this course would be more enjoyable.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534843\n 1534843\n \n 1534843\n I just looked at my transcript, and there are no other classes I have taken that are worse than this class. Out of the eleven labs that we did, my lab partner and I obtained usable data from just TWO of them. If most of the students cannot get the labs to work, I think there needs to be serious change to the curriculum. I understand that scientific experiments don't work every time in the real world, but they SHOULD in a lab class that I am taking for credit.\r\n\r\nOne thing that made the class inefficient was spending two hours watching the professor demonstrate the lab that we were doing, then doing it ourselves.
