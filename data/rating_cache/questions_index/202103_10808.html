\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544873\n 1544873\n \n 1544873\n This class was amazing! I really enjoyed the different activities and we did in class, and I think that Mr. Saunders really modeled the kinds of pedagogy and care that I would want all teachers to show their students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545455\n 1545455\n \n 1545455\n Even though we only examined 4 geographies, I sometimes felt that we didn't have enough time to fully understand that place before we moved onto the next. For example, I really enjoyed the section of the course that focused on Dine Nation, but because I had very little prior knowledge about Navajo Nation I felt like I was only able to make surface-level observations. I would have enjoyed a little more time spent on each geography even if that meant fewer geographies. Especially because this course criticizes how academic writing is wrongly valued above other ways of knowing, the readings were quite academic. I would have liked more opportunities to learn about place, race, and memory outside of books and academic articles. For instance, some of the reading could have been substituted for videos, stories, artwork, photos, music, etc. \r\n\r\nOften we were assigned a large amount of reading (such as a full book each week) and while they were interesting it was difficult to keep up with that much reading. Mr. Saunders usually cut down the reading by suggesting chapters to read if we were not able to do all of the reading, but that made class discussions awkward because it was unclear what reading expectations were. Sometimes I would do all of the reading and we discussed very little of it in class, and other times when I was able to do less of the reading I felt uncomfortable contributing to class discussions. Often large group discussions were very broad-level, and I would have liked to go into more depth on a smaller amount of reading. Mr. Saunders often apologized when he would talk for longer periods of time but I actually really enjoyed hearing his knowledge and insights! I think having small lecture overviews for each geography could have helped place our discussions into a clearer context. \r\n\r\nI think the best part of the course was the assignments, which gave students the opportunity to learn more about a school geography from their own lives. It did take a very long time to get feedback on assignments and even when we did get feedback it was not very detailed -- I don't think the feedback and evaluation fostered student growth and learning but I do think the assignments themselves were interesting and valuable.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546514\n 1546514\n \n 1546514\n Strengths: the energy of Professor Saunders, the varied ways of interacting with the texts through discussion, visuals, small group talks, mini presentations, close reading. The texts themselves were also a strength of the course as there was a mix of locations, writing styles, and narratives as well as a lot of agency in choosing about which parts to read about for the Chicago guidebook. A strong strength throughout the course was the framework given to ensure that students all knew about the theories and movements encountered in class through discussion and presentation.\r\nWeaknesses: I think one area this course could improve is in feedback, at times it felt as if an assignment were coming up without feedback on earlier writings which sometimes produced hesitancy in the writing process.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1548878\n 1548878\n \n 1548878\n It was really great. Professor Saunders is an incredible instructor, and clearly put in an immense amount of work to ensure that the readings, class activities, and assignments were all designed to further our learning.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549056\n 1549056\n \n 1549056\n The discussions and creative post it activities were amazing. The slow timing for feedback on our papers made it really hard to improve my writing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555487\n 1555487\n \n 1555487\n The first three weeks of this course each introduce the ideas of 'race', 'place' and 'memory'. By introducing and explaining these concepts with sociological and philosophical frameworks, the course sets a foundation for understandings of 'race' 'place' and 'memory' that is then expanded upon over the course of the semester. This common ground helps create class discussions that are focused and productive. The course focuses both on local examples of education, including an optional fieldtrip, but also draws heavily on the environments of Chicago and NYC. The weekly reading responses helped me remain engaged with the course. The course also draws heavily on the idea of 'close-looking', especially in looking at the built environment of architecture and seeing how the physical enacts the ideological.

Also, we didn't get grades back until almost the end of the semester, which made improvement challenging.
