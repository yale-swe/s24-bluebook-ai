\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1561891\n 1561891\n \n 1561891\n Strengths: Professor De La O is a very clear lecturer, and there are generally clear expectations on the assignments\r\nWeaknesses: it tries to introduce empirical papers, but it doesn't require statistics/econometrics as a pre-requisite. The course would probably be a lot stronger if everyone had a solid baseline knowledge in regression analysis. You can't go halfway with the empiricism; either require stats and introduce more empirical work, or don't require it and don't assign empirical papers. We spent a lot of time in section going over statistics and game theory that could have been avoided if those classes were required.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1561982\n 1561982\n \n 1561982\n I think it could go more into broad concepts and then apply them onto case studies. I sometimes felt like we were getting too specific instead of understanding general concepts first.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562348\n 1562348\n \n 1562348\n From my own experience and from speaking to other students in the class it felt somewhat like the course was a little set up for us to do poorly, at least in the beginning. I think the writing responses were fine although perhaps graded a little harshly for what they were trying to accomplish, but it was truly the pop quizzes that were a journey to figure out. They were extremely not intuitive and asked hyper-specific questions about 60 page readings while also transitioning from closed to open notes and more. It just felt like there was a bit of a learning curve for the class trying to figure out how to handle this quizzes and even though they changed the format several times, I do not believe we are being rewarded for improvement on them which I feel like should be given seeing as the admin of the class took some time to settle into how to give us quizzes, it makes sense to me that our later quizzes would be weighted more heavily. I also think less dense readings, a slower lecture pace, releasing lecture slides before class, allowing us to see what mistakes we made on quizzes, etc. would all be helpful towards promoting learning in the class.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1577307\n 1577307\n \n 1577307\n The layout of the course was excellent with a good balance between textbooks and research papers.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1591902\n 1591902\n \n 1591902\n Strengths - interesting informative \r\nWeaknesses - sometimes redundant and readings were hard
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1591987\n 1591987\n \n 1591987\n One strength of the course was its heavy emphasis on interpreting political science research critically. Prof. De La O went into detail on the methods of the studies we were reading and encouraged the class to think about their limitations/what their regressions did and did not show. I really enjoyed this aspect of the class, and think it will stick with me going forward. \r\n\r\nThe class curriculum was another strength. I found many of the weeks extremely interesting, particularly those covering theories of state formation, the roles of corruption and clientelism in democracy, and whether democracies or autocracies promote more development. Although I also know that some students disliked it, I thought that the APSE textbook was clear and did a good job summarizing research that was relevant to each of the questions that the class addressed. \r\n\r\nA final strength was Prof. De La O's use of specific examples to illustrate concepts that we learned about in class. Prof. De La O frequently provided detailed case studies that did a great job demonstrating what much of what we learned about looked like in practice. \r\n\r\nOne weakness of the class was the reading quizzes. I did the readings every week and annotated most of them, but even then sometimes had trouble with the reading quizzes because they would ask about parts of the readings that sometimes seemed arbitrary and only vaguely relevant to the theme of that reading/week. That said, Prof. De La O was lenient about grading the quizzes, and I understand that they were an accountability mechanism that was necessary to make students do the readings. \r\n\r\nAnother slight weakness was the pacing of the lectures. The lectures were quite good overall, but Prof. De La O sometimes rushed through specific slides, making it hard to write everything down. That said, the slides were later posted, so this also wasn't a huge issue.
