Professor Gee is also a very engaging and thoughtful instructor, whose lessons, points, and relationship with the class left everyone feeling understood, appreciated, and empowered.\r\n\r\nThe only weakness of the course that I might suggest an improvement for is the initial reading response. I think for many students this response was the most nerve-wracking, mostly because it was our first real grade and our first real "assignment." To go along with the idea of proposals that this class left us all in (which is a state of mind I still find myself responding to situations with), I propose that Professor Gee should make the first discussion paper a "practice grade." Meaning, students are still expected to complete it with the same degree of care, thoughtfulness, and attention to detail, but they don't have to worry about this paper being the grade that will be "dropped" because it may inevitably be lower than all of their other papers. The idea here is to allow students to receive direct feedback on their structure, ideas, content, and general approach from Professor Gee without the worry of their performance negatively impacting their grade. If having a specific number of graded papers is necessary for the core structure of the course, perhaps Professor Gee could consider including an extra paper, maybe a week earlier, or ask students to write a concluding response paper just based more broadly on the conversations they've had throughout the semester.
