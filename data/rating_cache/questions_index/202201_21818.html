I learned the value in precision within poetic translation, in the concision of my language and the telling nature of form. I began to crave poems that were drunks with nouns, that disquieted me, that offered up unexpected catharsis, that got under peoples skin.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1596719\n 1596719\n \n 1596719\n Professor Cole is an exceptionally kind and committed professor, and he is a real expert on these poets. His approach is that of a learned poet, so the seminar conversation is driven more by excitement about the material than an interest in assigning poems to categories or analyzing them through a particularly academic lens. I feel that this is a strength in many ways, but it's something to be aware of if you're hyper-focused on one concern, such as Marxism and poetry. I also really appreciated the diverse pedagogic approaches, which ranged from facilitated discussion to student-direct presentations. I would have preferred a smaller class (closer to 12 than 18), as the larger class size seemed to get in the way of class bonding and made it harder to have a cohesive conversation in class. It rarely felt like we were addressing each other in conversation about the text--it felt more like one person at a time speaking to Professor Cole, with the occasional reference to a previous comment. I like it when professors draw connections between students' comments or ask students to call on the next speaker after speaking themselves, and I think that seminar discussion could have been even more robust if there had been more emphasis on students talking to each other. The other weakness was that while we focused on deep engagement with individual poems in class, the reading load was heavy such that it was difficult to pause and re-read individual poems. The Canvas posts also felt a bit onerous and I'm not sure how much they really helped us engage with each other before class.
