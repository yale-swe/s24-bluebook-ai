\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521000\n 1521000\n \n 1521000\n This course suffers from a large number of critical issues that could be easily improved upon if even slightly more time were given to doing so by the instructors. Even at the start of the semester, it was clear how poorly run the class was. Further, these office hours were held by the TAs, who are first year graduate students. The professors in fact did not hold office hours the entire semester. Lectures were poorly organized and did not reflect homework assignment material at all. Homework questions were terribly worded, and homework problems were entirely unreflective of class material or exam work. No formal review sessions were held for the first two examinations, and for both the professors only posted 1-3 practice problems the day or two before the exams that were not reflective of test material. This resulted in incredibly low averages for the class in both midterms. Homework and lab grading criteria were also very unclear and rubric consisted of postings that were clearly gathered from a general course rubric (i.e., from the Poorvu center). The final exam time was not communicated until the last week of classes. To be frank, it felt like the professors could care less about this course. I certainly know that the manner in which this course was run negatively portrayed the major (E&EB/EVST).
