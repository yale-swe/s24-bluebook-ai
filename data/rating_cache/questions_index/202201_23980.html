\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589148\n 1589148\n \n 1589148\n I think that class structure was sometimes a challengethere were times when the class discussion was really good, but other times when the issues were so confused that it was hard to have good class discussions because I felt like nobody really understood what was going on (including me) but everyoen wanted to prove to everyone else that they absolutely did understand.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589198\n 1589198\n \n 1589198\n The syllabus is interesting, if packed. I wish we had more time for the later thinkers, like Beauvoir and Anscombe. I was grateful for Professor Billet's handouts, which she had clearly worked very hard to craft for us. They were crucial in preparing for the exam, and I think they should be distributed to every DS student. \r\n\r\nI felt that classroom discussion in this class was a bit unproductive and hard to break into. Professor Billet kindly insisted on letting everyone be heard and encouraged students to direct the discussion. At times, however, the discourse felt like a clash among students eager to hear themselves talk, and I wish more had been done to prevent that. The "lightbulb" moments always came during Professor Billet's explanations, not during group discussions.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1591500\n 1591500\n \n 1591500\n Strength - the syllabus, the writing curriculum\r\nWeakness - the token women philosophers. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1591589\n 1591589\n \n 1591589\n Strengths: reading these texts in the order they are presented was thrilling.
