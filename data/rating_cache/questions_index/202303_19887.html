Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697127\n 1697127\n \n 1697127\n Strengths: the Professor is very enthusiastic about helping his students learn and deeply cares about our learning. the class was also a wonderful group of people, I don't think I ever had this nice of a group in a seminar, everyone was super engaged and interesting and the class environment was wonderful. Weaknesses: assignments are very unclear, my classmates and I would always find ourselves asking a ton of questions to the professor and each other, because instructions were not clear and the professor would not really clarify much. It wasn't even clear what the purpose of the paper was at the beginning, which made some of us choose our topic kind of blindly. The readings were simply too many for no reason, I think lots of them were very dry and not engaging. It'd be nice if future syllabi included more up-to-date examples of resource management. Some grades came back very slowly, and feedback wasn't extensive so it was hard to find guidance for improvement. On the other hand, when some of us asked for extensions we always had to get a dean's excuse. It'd be nice to have one chance of delayed submission per semester because life happens.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1700715\n 1700715\n \n 1700715\n The seminar is a wonderful mix of so many interesting subjects, but sometimes I fear it is almost too broad. Shifting from China's one-child policy to lobster fishery management is a pretty big jump (though you see the connections between the two eventually), but manageable with engagement in discussion. Still, the breadth got a bit overwhelming at points. Class discussions were really well-facilitated.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1700835\n 1700835\n \n 1700835\n The strength of this course is its great depth of analysis. The weekly memos were also helpful in digesting heavier readings. Something that could be improved is having slightly more engaging discussions by not having a queue and rather letting people just speak out.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1715985\n 1715985\n \n 1715985\n The directions were sometimes confusing, as Prof Fotos would assign the weekly work in the language of the IAD framework before you really understood what it was so I had to simultaneously try to figure out what Prof Fotos was asking for and also do it and it was challenging sometimes. It was hard to see the pieces of the IAD framework as they were supposed to come together at the beginning because I just had no idea what I was supposed to be doing. On the other hand, I gradually realized that Prof Fotos was intentionally a little vague to give us as much creative freedom as we could do work through the unique problems of each case. Professor Fotos did a good job of exposing us to a lot of concepts and having good class discussions and investing in each student so that we all cared about what we were doing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723309\n 1723309\n \n 1723309\n Strengths: IAD framework was cool to learn about, left feeling more optimistic about the possibilities of collective action, improved my writing to be more concise, very short discussion posts every week, had different activities every seminar\r\nWeaknesses: barely any readings from the past 20 years - would love to see an updated syllabus where at least one week or unit can address the role of science/policy in current affairs. readings can be dry also, especially Ostrom's entire book of governing the commons. People were also confused about how to use the IAD framework in their paper
