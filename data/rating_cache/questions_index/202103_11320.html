The way the writing assignments were organised (three smaller essays, each a part of a longer history essay) were extremely helpful for the final paper. Workload was manageable and fair.\r\n\r\nI do not have many complaints about the course. The only aspect of which I was not quite sure were the weekly reading responses. While I understand that they were meant to make sure everyone did the readings, apart from a few mentions of them in class, they often felt quite disconnected from the section discussion. Reading them, it felt like we were all talking past each other. Maybe a different reading response format that would make it feel mroe like a discussion woudl ehlp the responses feel more integrated. Maybe having part of the class write a response and then having the rest respond to those would work. Or maybe a format similar to "The Conversation" in the NYT where students meet in groups of 2-4 each week and write a log of their conversation on the readings.
