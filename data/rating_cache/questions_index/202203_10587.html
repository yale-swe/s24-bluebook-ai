\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1610182\n 1610182\n \n 1610182\n This course could be improved by offering feedback on drafts of papers.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1621871\n 1621871\n \n 1621871\n Strengths: Professor Levene, both in class and in office hours. She pushes students to develop their own interpretation of the material, but makes sure they remain faithful to the text. \r\n\r\nWeakness/Suggestion: I think it would be helpful to introduce slightly more structure in class discussions to make sure everyone's interests are taken into account. For example, students could be asked to put up questions and concepts they would like to discuss on the board before jumping into a free-flowing discussion. While the relatively unstructured nature of the seminar allowed for interesting and unexpected lines of inquiry, it also meant that some students tended to dominate the discussion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1623532\n 1623532\n \n 1623532\n This course is unique. Prof Levene chose some fantastic texts, and these texts and the SHORT weekly readings are the key strength of the course. I would often spend 5+ hours on a 15 page Kierkegaard reading for a week. I cannot describe how intellectually satisfying reading his amazing work in such a slow, methodical manner was. That was the highlight. Discussion sections were, I thought, hit or miss. 40% of discussions were so thought-provoking, and thought-provoking about such grand topics with such enormous relevance to my life, that I would leave class emotional from the intellectual ecstasy I was experiencing. I would say 60% of the sections were kind of meh because they proceeded in the following form: 1) student raises question and points to a passage that might be useful in answering it 2) another student affirms that they have the same question, points to another passage that might answer it, and raises another question borne out of that passage. 3) 2 repeats several times. I found myself frustrated when a student would read a brilliant, rich passage and raise a profound question, and rather than spend the 20-30 minutes of discussion owed to that passage and question, we would move on the another passage and eventually another question before answering the first. Still 40% is a great rate when those 40% are as truly stellar as they were in our section. And not being able to answer such challenging questions is understandable; if wed waited to answer them before moving on we might have been stumped/silent for long periods. It may be that the discussions were as good as they could have been. So overall I consider the discussions a strength, along with the principle strength, the readings. Professor Levene is brilliant and caring and is another strength in the very strong course she has created. She is a tough grader and justifies it by arguing the inflated grading system at Yale may be dysfunctional. Interesting argument and I appreciate her pushing back against that system. Still, her class is a class in that system, and I often found myself confused about whether my paper was good like the comments said if my grade was 1-2 letter grades below what I would have received in another class at Yale for the same quality of work. The grading left a bit of a sour taste in my mouth as a fair evaluation system but those grades sure were an effective motivational tool! My writing became more precise over the course of the semester. What more can you ask?
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627179\n 1627179\n \n 1627179\n I really loved discussion in this course and the general concept of the course. However, the structure of the course is tough. I get the policy about no participation grades, but for a class where we are developing most of our ideas together and in class, it feels weird for that work to suddenly be negligible and have a single final essay is worth more than half of our grade. Although I didn't, more than half of the people I know in this course Credit/D/Fail-ed it, so although I liked the course a lot, I think there should be room to both take the course and feel okay about one's grade in it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1630701\n 1630701\n \n 1630701\n I think the course's strength is its content and the passion brought by those teaching it -- Prof. Levene put a lot of work into fitting together this class, and it shows! I feel like the course was shrouded in this supposition, but I would have appreciated a bit more explicit discussion of what that idea means, and why this syllabus reflects that.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1630968\n 1630968\n \n 1630968\n I think its strength is in its format and in the texts which complement each other very well, it is not a weakness really but prospective students should know it is a fairly difficult course.
