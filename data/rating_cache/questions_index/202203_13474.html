Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1615072\n 1615072\n \n 1615072\n Strengths: taking this class is an opportunity to be immersed in a culture and history that most students don't ever have exposure to. You will be surrounded by a huge amount of expertise - in my class there were two professors, several grad students who'd done research in the field, and several Brazillian students familiar with Amazonian politics, so there are lots of people who have interesting contributions to make. The course makes the field of Amazonian ethnography seem approachable and small enough to really understand the nuances of, which is a result of great reading selections. It's nice to bring in politics, art, and religion as well and there is room for all of it in the course.\r\nWeaknesses: The readings vary wildly in approachability, and each student will probably struggle with some of them depending on their interest and background, but this is sort of inevitable in a course that treats with essentially five different academic fields.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618982\n 1618982\n \n 1618982\n This course had a ton of reading, and you really needed to do all of it to be able to follow class and do well on the exams. If I were to improve this course I would add some kind of note-taking or homework assignment to make sure I'm getting the important information out of the readings every week.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1623809\n 1623809\n \n 1623809\n The course was well organized into multiple chapters. Mr. Herrmann always communicated with students in a timely manner when there would be a change to the syllabus. He would upload the new syllabus onto Canvas. He also was timely about what readings we should focus on for class discussion. Another strength of the course was the diverse group of scholars that we were able to hear from. We benefitted from the two visiting scholars, Mr. Estanislao and Mr. Thiago as well as the guest lecture by Dr. Shephard who happened to be the father of one of our peers. I'm aware that the same guest scholars can't be there each time this class is offered, but we really did benefit from hearing more from people doing active work in the Amazon. \r\n\r\nBecause this was the first time that this course was organized, I felt that I was missing crucial information that would have made the readings and discussions more fruitful. I would recommend further fleshing out the second week's topic on Amazonian ecology. As much as reading about the major biomes in the textbook was somewhat helpful, I think future students would benefit from spending time looking at a physical map of Amazonia. We could have a better mental picture of where the major rivers, ethnic groups, and countries that include the Amazon are. I don't assume that everyone knows South American geography. \r\n\r\nI wonder if this course could be split into two related courses over the course of one year. I was discussing with Mr. Herrmann once on how much context I was missing simply because I did not know the history of the South American nation states that include the Amazon. This is important because each country's government has had differing relations with Indigenous Amazonians and vice versa. I'd completely support a fall offering that focuses mainly on Amazonian archaeology and a spring course focusing on the colonial history integral to understanding how the Amazon got to where it is today.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624572\n 1624572\n \n 1624572\n Strengths: the breadth of topics and readings covered, the amount of knowledgeable people and instructors in the seminar, the variety of readings (articles, movies, stories, etc), not an insanely high workload or stress purely from grades. Weaknesses: in-class discussion was sometimes not as active as other seminars, lots of emphasis placed on the midterm and final exam, some context for the readings provided after the readings were due
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1638082\n 1638082\n \n 1638082\n The course was very well organized and readings were well selected. The course was designed to help students understand the material. The instructors always had interesting things to say so I would have appreciated if they gave more formalized presentations/lectures.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1638138\n 1638138\n \n 1638138\n The course is very strong and I like the dynamic seminar style of it, but one improvement could be putting it in a bigger classroom -- it was a tiny space and many people wanted to take the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1638575\n 1638575\n \n 1638575\n This is a wonderful class overall. I would have, however, appreciated a slightly closer look at how archaeology impacts modern political movements in the Amazon.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1640266\n 1640266\n \n 1640266\n Lots of interesting readings and perspectives but I really needed more basic background information on geography and tribes of the amazon
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1640646\n 1640646\n \n 1640646\n The expansive course materials and discussions allowed for a deep and diverse engagement with the topic of the Amazon. Offering the class for the first time, the professors did an impressive job of providing so many angles of this complex subject. This is such an important course, and I hope it keeps being offered in the future.
