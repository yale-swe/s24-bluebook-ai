Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1703305\n 1703305\n \n 1703305\n There was a good balance of seminar-type discussion and lecturing. Professor Herring was very open to hearing our ideas and letting everyone contribute, and then he would go through the slides he had prepared to give us more historical context or queer theory background to our readings. The small class size (16) was definitely a strength. The presentations for different US cities/regions were really great. This allowed the whole class to explore the queer history of different US regions, and the fact that students got to make the presentations was a cool way to switch it up from just looking at Professor Herring's powerpoint. I would say a weakness was that the structure of the class could become a bit monotonous. We didn't have many assignments throughout the year (which was nice because it reduced stress), so almost all of the days had the same structure: read for homework and discuss in class. These discussions were usually really engaging, and the city presentations did break up the monotony a bit. Sometimes in our class discussions, there would be issues or interpretations of the readings that people respectfully disagreed on. I think it could be interesting if Professor Herring incorporated some kind of debate into the course where students would have to build their "claim" on material from the syllabus. That could be a fun activity that would also encourage high-level thinking.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706044\n 1706044\n \n 1706044\n Strengths: the class environment built by Prof. Herring and the peers, I feel like I got to know many people in the class; lecture / analysis Prof. Herring provides for the material is SO thought-provoking, concrete, amazing, loved Rubyfruit Jungle and On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous. Weaknesses: the seminar isn't as lively as I hoped, I wish there is more conversation happening between the students instead of just everyone talking to the professor; the more academic readings can get dry and the discussions around them are less engaging
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710156\n 1710156\n \n 1710156\n Strengths: like other first-year seminars, this class was a great way to find community. Also, the readings were balanced and well-chosen, truly widening my perspective of the course's themes.\r\n\r\nWeakness: the way seminar discussions were run did not generally lead to conducive, engaging conversations. I wish we didn't essentially answer questions in a single-file line, and instead responded to each other's thoughts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1720848\n 1720848\n \n 1720848\n Strengths: Organized, intriguing, and most importantly, fun. I giggled more in this class than I did any other, yet I learned so much!\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: At times, the readings can be a bit overwhelming to read thoroughly.
