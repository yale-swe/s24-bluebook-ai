RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1611984\n 1611984\n \n 1611984\n One of the strengths of the class is that it provides insight into how centrism inhibits social movements and shows up in our daily lives. The class also does a good job of showing how centrism shows up in works of literature. The lectures are always engaging and the workload is manageable. Although the course includes works by Martin Luther King Jr., Audre Lorde, Kim Scott, Pablo Neruda, etc, I wish that it included more political philosophy outside of the white Western cannon, especially in unit 1. For the first half of the term we spent a lot of time reading Wallerstein and Burke so the midterm essay was heavy on the Western cannon. It would also be interesting to read some more critical theory articles in this class (perhaps articles on the books/poems we are reading) because that could add to the level of depth in the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1612586\n 1612586\n \n 1612586\n This course does a really great job of blending politics and literature to show how the two might be more intertwined than we might realize. I appreciated working through both the political theory and literary analysis sides of the class--Professor North is an amazing lecturer who brings the room to life with his passion. In an age that needs more radical action, the class illuminated the subtler dangers of centrism and the ways in which our art can often perpetuate these political attitudes by cultivating certain implicit aesthetic tastes. One drawback is that the mandatory discussion sections were often not very engaging.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1615802\n 1615802\n \n 1615802\n The lectures were incredibly engaging, and the readings were all well-tailored to the message of the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616305\n 1616305\n \n 1616305\n The professor doesnt adhere to the schedule in the syllabus very well, which makes it hard to plan ahead.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618103\n 1618103\n \n 1618103\n This course is really engaging and I felt very good about the way lectures were presented with enough time for questions at the end. There were no slides which sometimes made things difficult to follow but I think that the "no laptop" rule made the class engagement much better. I think one thing that could be improved is the number of essays assigned. On the one hand, only having two essays made the course load a lot lighter, but it left few opportunities to receive feedback on writing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625000\n 1625000\n \n 1625000\n The readings at the end of semester are lengthy and a grind, but even if you arent able to complete them, the lectures and sections are extremely insightful. Professor North lectures a lot, but I enjoyed his commentary and perspectives.

They got boring quickly and didnt feel like they introduced anything new.
