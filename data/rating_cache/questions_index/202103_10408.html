\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538686\n 1538686\n \n 1538686\n So many strengths!!! What he says is not to be taken as absolute truth, and he jumps from one concept/person/event to the next in a way that may seem chaotic at first, but gradually comes together over the course of a lecture. He is inspired by Paulo Freire's Pedagogy of the Oppressed and always strives to respond to and learn from the student; he also encourages people to come to his office hours, even though it is a large class. The TAs were spectacular - Josh, Katy, and Aron - and I imagine that, from year to year, this course tends to attract TAs who are thoughtful, radical, and supportive of the students. The assignments consist of weekly discussion posts, an in-class midterm exam (which the TAs are very helpful in preparing for), and a final reflective paper that consists of locating yourself within the social formation. A lot of community was cultivated through this class, and I've met so many people who I love and learn from. It's hard for me to think of weaknesses, but if you are someone who prefers highly structured/chronological lectures, this may be an adjustment - it's still totally worth it though! The final paper also asks a lot from you (8-10 pages to locate yourself within the social formation), but if you are interested in this class, these are probably questions you have already been considering. You can only grow from this class!
