\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1646955\n 1646955\n \n 1646955\n An introduction to representation theory: the representations of finite groups in general, the symmetric group in particular, and also associative algebras. It's an interesting perspective on algebraic structures that differs from what you find in 350/370, for example.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1652517\n 1652517\n \n 1652517\n I learned about representation theory of groups and associative algebras. Representation theory is the study of groups through a homomorphism to a linear map. When a group or algebra is considered as a linear map we can learn a lot of new information about the group or algebra itself.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657565\n 1657565\n \n 1657565\n I developed a really strong understanding of representations and the implications for other areas of mathematics. Additionally, I felt the problem sets challenged me to become a better problem solver and a more mature mathematics student in my ability to digest different ideas and apply them to solving problems.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1659291\n 1659291\n \n 1659291\n Basics in representation theory: representations of finite groups, symmetric representations, induced representation, representations of associative algebras, skew-fields.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1659838\n 1659838\n \n 1659838\n Stronger understanding of algebra and tensor products, problem solving skills
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1662001\n 1662001\n \n 1662001\n Representation theory of groups and of associative algebras. Completely reducible representations, characters, representations of symmetric groups, simple & semisimple algebras. But most important, if you have a typical undergrad's background in algebra, this course will give you a lot more maturity in algebra---you'll become familiar with important algebraic constructions, notation, and patterns of thinking.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1664711\n 1664711\n \n 1664711\n Introduction to representation theory for finite groups (first half) and associative algebras (second half). An important skill I learned was to review the course notes even after attending the lecture to make sure I understand.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1665050\n 1665050\n \n 1665050\n Quantum mechanics is written in the language of representation theory, where time evolution is governed by a symmetry group that acts on Hilbert space. This course gave me further insight into this construction. In a broader sense, learning representation theory showed me how to use an unfamiliar mathematical construction to answer all kinds of interesting questions. This class tied together much of group theory and linear algebra, with the classification of Abelian groups, the class equation, orbit-stabilizer theorem, Jordan normal form, generalized eigenspaces, and direct products playing central roles in the study of representations.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1665389\n 1665389\n \n 1665389\n Representation theory of finite groups and finite dimensional associative algebras.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1667441\n 1667441\n \n 1667441\n General introduction to representation theory
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1667532\n 1667532\n \n 1667532\n Very excellent introduction to the basics of group representations and modules.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1669250\n 1669250\n \n 1669250\n Basic theory of representations of groups and algebras, characters, representations of S_n and A_n, theory of semi-simple algebras and their modules.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1669730\n 1669730\n \n 1669730\n Representation theory: the general idea is that we can get an idea of how a group behaves/looks based on how it acts on vector spaces. So turn group theory into linear algebra. We learned a lot about representations (vector space + group action on the vector space), classifying/constructing representations, and notable examples, e.g. for S_n. Prof. Loseu talked about some applications, such as to harmonic analysis and Lie groups. Later on we talked about algebras (vector space with multiplication) and skew fields. My sense is that this kinda continues down the path of abstract algebra after Math 350.
