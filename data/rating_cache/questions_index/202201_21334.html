\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1567069\n 1567069\n \n 1567069\n I wish lectures were more engaging throughout rather than just at the end. More discussion of the reading assignments. The midterm report was super helpful!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1572195\n 1572195\n \n 1572195\n I absolutely loved this course! The lectures were extremely interesting and generative. The sections were well structured to help us develop our concepts and execute our deliverables, and we were given ample time for each assignment. Although some students expressed projects having too quick of a turnaround, I felt that we had more than enough time when we worked progressively - the projects just weren't conducive to being done last-minute. Each project was both extremely challenging and rewarding, and I loved seeing everyone's varied end products. I was so impressed by the high level of engagement of the students this semester, which is a testament to the course structure and content. \r\n\r\nI have a few critiques about how the class could further be improved. Firstly, the assignment descriptions were wordy and theoretical, which could translate to them being quite vague. I needed repeated clarification on the parameters of some assignments, and would appreciate a bit more concision, or at least a concise through-line of the theoretical part as opposed to just the deliverables. Also, I wish there was a better way to form teams for assignments 3 and 4. We were asked to form teams extremely quickly (sometimes in a 24 hour turnaround) for those assignments with people in our sections, but we had no way of contacting each other for the most part. I would have wanted a partner for the final project, but I had no way to contact anyone until after the deadline for team formation.

Sometimes felt deadlines were crunched. Projects that used Rhino or other software were mostly self-taught.

Another strength is the chance to learn and be inspired by classmates. The course asks that students share their work at various stages in the creative process, allowing you to marvel at and draw from the talent of a creative group of classmates.\r\nA minor weakness of the class was that I would have liked a bit more practical training in the various methods/softwares beyond the tutorials.
