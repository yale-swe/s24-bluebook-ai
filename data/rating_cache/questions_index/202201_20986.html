\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1564715\n 1564715\n \n 1564715\n This class could have been great. I took this one over Listening to Music because Listening to Music seemed to focus on a lot of weird stuff; I thought this class would focus on classical music from the 1800s. I was terribly wrong. You spend 2ish weeks studying Beethoven, Bach, Shubert, Mendelson, etc but it quickly goes into opera where you stay for several weeks. While I gained a possible appreciation for opera and went to see my first opera due to this class, It was not my intention to academically study opera; if it was, I would have taken an opera class. After 3-4 weeks of exclusively opera, you then pivot to impressionist and modern music (i.e. weird stuff that a layperson wouldn't consider music). A month and a half of this stuff and you realize that while you hoped to learn a lot about the great classical composers so you could have educated discussions about classical music and heighten the enjoyment of famous symphonies, you will instead be beating your head with your headphones as you're forced to listen to atonal noises and train sounds. Some more notes. After 4 years at Yale, this is the only class I've been in that takes lecture attendance, yes, you will be treated like a middle schooler and forced to sign the sign-in sheet at every class, causing a stir as half the class shows up late. It's also published as great for non-musicians, accepting to all, but while that may be what you think, it entirely depends on your TA grading your work. My section TA was great and made me feel (as a musical outsider) welcome, but the TA grading my work was extremely harsh and nitpicky, critiquing my use of words that weren't even musical terms and basically telling me my subjective opinion of music was wrong. Don't take this class for a grade (or at all) because guess what...at the end there is a listening quiz where you have to learn 90+ pieces, the year composed, the composer, and attributes about them (The 90+ include the crappy atonal stuff and train sounds). Overall, if you are like me, hoping to take a fun class outside a stem major and wanting to learn about classical music this is NOT your class.
