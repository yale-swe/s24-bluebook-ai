Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1565668\n 1565668\n \n 1565668\n The hands-on components of this course are phenomenal. I loved working with Maishe and working with collections from the Peabody Museum. It would have been cool to have done some pXRF as well, since the department has access to that. The readings selected were interesting, but conversations were a bit lacking. I wish my classmates had been more engaged in the group discussions, but I don't think that is directly an issue with the class itself.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1569289\n 1569289\n \n 1569289\n The first third of the class was great because we worked with a professional potter for a number of weeks, creating vessels that we had begun to study. That was really fun. Looking at ceramics that had already been found and already been studied, and being asked to measure them and other small observations about them was not interesting. Studying the sherds was difficult and felt like busy work.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1570586\n 1570586\n \n 1570586\n This course's best components were the hands-on work and analysis we did, along with the weekly class discussions and the time spent making our pot. All of these components interacted with the readings to make my learning better!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1572305\n 1572305\n \n 1572305\n Strengths: Anne Underhill is the nicest professor I've ever had. She is definitely one of the biggest strengths of the course. She always has a smile on her face and is supportive of and enthusiastic about the comments students make. The hands-on exercises are also an amazing aspect of the course--they are so engaging and I feel I learned so much through them. I learned a lot with the final research paper too. I liked that discussions were short and more time was spent on other aspects of the class. The readings were sometimes dense, but were ultimately manageable. Weaknesses: I felt the exercises would have been better as individual rather than group projects. Coordinating with someone else creates a lot of stress and sometimes workloads are not distributed evenly among two people.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1573586\n 1573586\n \n 1573586\n Professor Underhill is an amazing professor. She cares so deeply about her students and is incredibly knowledgeable. The readings are interesting and I always look forward to her lessons. She also gives helpful feedback on assignments and always makes herself available to students if they have any questions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1588372\n 1588372\n \n 1588372\n The level engagement required for the course was its biggest strength. Whenever I came to class, I knew there was something to be done and something to be learned during the process.
