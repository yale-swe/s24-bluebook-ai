Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1646960\n 1646960\n \n 1646960\n Prof Allen is a huge strength of this course. She gives so much constructive feedback and really encourages students to meet with her about their writing. Also, her selection of readings was excellent and varied. I do think this course was overly stressful, and Prof Allens expectations seemed designed for graduate students rather than undergrads. I found myself discouraged in the middle of the semester because I felt like I could not please her with my writing. That said, I felt like Id improved a lot by the end of the semester, and I do think the course is very much worthwhile if youre prepared for some of the stress that comes with it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1647408\n 1647408\n \n 1647408\n Very high workload in terms of readings for each week; I feel that if the readings were slightly reduced, we would be able to look at each one in greater depth and ensure that all students are realistically on the same page in class discussions, since (especially toward the end of the semester) students are generally so busy and it becomes very difficult to devote the same amount of time to as much reading.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657775\n 1657775\n \n 1657775\n The course was incredibly well structured and taught there are very few ways I could imagine it being improved. The assignments were well-thought-out and interesting, the feedback was pointed and helpful, and the readings, week-to-week, were fascinating. The one area I thought had any room for improvement was class discussion sometimes I felt we were simply bouncing between differing students' views on a given text and it felt like we were not gaining as a collective but simply speaking our minds.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1659257\n 1659257\n \n 1659257\n The course is very demanding, which is both a strength and weakness depending on who you are. Prepare to be challenged.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1661183\n 1661183\n \n 1661183\n My biggest issue with the course was that it felt like we didn't actually talk very much about the Nazi period itself. A ton of time was spent on the Weimar period, which was probably my favorite part of the class, the last few weeks were about memory politics, but there wasn't actually any time devoted to how the Nazi state functioned.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1663876\n 1663876\n \n 1663876\n Strengths: I felt lucky to learn from Professor Allen -- she's kind, thoughtful, and incisive in her analysis of your work (and her comments are very helpful). She puts so much into the class and it shows -- discussion level was always very high and I managed to come away from every seminar meeting feeling like I had really learned something. Allen gives really comprehensive feedback on work that genuinely improved my writing (even if it intimidated me at first) and she challenged me to think about Nazi Germany in ways that will influence my future, non-Germany-oriented History coursework. She, and the high level of investment from the students in seminar, make this a really great experience. I thought most of the assignments for the class were well-structured and well-placed (ie assigned at the right time and with enough time to complete). \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: take this class if you know you have the time/motive to put in the work for it -- you'll realize quickly that it's an awesome class that you want to engage with and do all the readings for, and it just feels like a loss if you can't afford to do that because you have a hard set of other classes. The assignments are demanding but not busywork, and I wrote some of my best writing at Yale for this class -- in short, the work level is very high but worth it. The only caveat I'd make to this is that I really wished that the final paper was due later in the semester (for our year it was due April 25th, with a prospectus due a week earlier). I thought it would be nice to have the final due earlier because it's obviously way less work during finals period but this wound up being a mischaracterization -- it's pretty hard (and kinda annoying) to have to get into a final paper mindset in the middle of April, and I thought this just felt very rushed. I think a rough draft due around this point, or perhaps a fuller prospectus, with the actual final due at a normal date would let students put a lot more time into it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1672079\n 1672079\n \n 1672079\n I think Professor Allen leads an extremely engaging seminar and is thought provoking. She makes sure that every single person is engaged in the class. I do think, however, that she is unfairly harsh on the assignments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1677091\n 1677091\n \n 1677091\n Professor Allen is an excellent teacher. The only thing is I wish sometimes I heard more of her opinion, but she does a great job of facilitating discussion. I really enjoyed my time in the course.
