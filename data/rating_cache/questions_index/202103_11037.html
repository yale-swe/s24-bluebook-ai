\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536220\n 1536220\n \n 1536220\n Strengths: if you're fully engaged, you get so much out of this course. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545121\n 1545121\n \n 1545121\n The course places particular focus on applying new vocabulary terms and grammar structures to oral practice, and students learn to utilize these newfound skills at a rapid pace. However, at times, class discussions moved too quickly from subject to subject, requiring quick thinking and a somewhat competitive approach, as students prioritized the number of vocabulary words or grammar structures they could incorporate into their contributions, rather than depth of thought.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545426\n 1545426\n \n 1545426\n Strengths- the course packet is so useful, also the vocabulary\r\nWeaknesses - do not expect to learn from a diverse range of authors. Also the last book, one of the only pnes written by a non-white author, doesnt appear outwardly to have nearly as much resources or time poured into it
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545427\n 1545427\n \n 1545427\n The class forced me to work on my French on a daily basis during and outside of lessons. This consistent practice meant I made a good amount of progress throughout the semester. I found the grammar exercises especially helpful and thorough. \r\n\r\nThe workload of this course was very high, but in my opinion it was worthwhile because I was very committed to improving my French.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1556417\n 1556417\n \n 1556417\n The professor was a major strength of this course. I appreciate that she would make an active effort to solicit responses from all students in any given class period, so that everyone had an opportunity to practice their spoken French. Of course, this meant that conversations could sometimes feel quite forced, but did ensure that everyone had an opportunity to practice speaking within the short class period. Furthermore, the professor was very enthusiastic about the course material, and so the class was never boring. I also appreciated that the professor was always willing to make herself available for office hours, and was patient with students' questions. \r\n\r\nAdditionally, the major writing assignments (the "rdactions") were split up into two parts: an initial draft, which would receive commentary from the professor, and then a final edited/corrected version. This structure provided an invaluable opportunity for students to receive constructive feedback and corrections on the rdactions as we worked on them. Personally, I loved this aspect of the course. Had I not had this opportunity for direct feedback from the professor in between drafts, I would have been reluctant to experiment with more complicated sentences and grammatical structures, and higher-level vocabulary. I feel like I was able to push my bounds a bit, and receive constructive feedback, which helped to advance my writing. \r\n\r\nThis course could be improved by placing greater emphasis on developing students' spoken French. The only time that students actually got to speak the language was within the short class period on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays. Because of the number of students in the class, this amounted to very little time actually speaking the language. In previous French courses at Yale, teachers incorporated spoken/pronunciation practice into homework assignments by having students send in audio recordings of their response to a prompt, for example. Incorporating something like this could help students receive clearer feedback on their pronunciation.
