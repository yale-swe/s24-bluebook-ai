\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1529145\n 1529145\n \n 1529145\n Very well organized and all the material both fits together and is broken into manageable pieces. I even wished there'd been a little more weekly vocab.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1547893\n 1547893\n \n 1547893\n This course was fabulous and truly a lot of fun if you are willing to put in the time and effort.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1552090\n 1552090\n \n 1552090\n No weaknesses. Loved it. Super fun.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553320\n 1553320\n \n 1553320\n I loved Padlet. Everything was very organized and it was easy to see what we had to do for the entire week.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553607\n 1553607\n \n 1553607\n This course is an excellent foundation for anybody looking to get started with Hebrew. Its main flaw is that many of the students already have a background in the language. True newcomers can feel lost and sometimes left behind in class material due to their peers demonstrating a much more solid grasp of the language.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554020\n 1554020\n \n 1554020\n Padlet was a great tool, that was definitely a strength, although I think too many assignments/texts were assigned daily sometimes and it became overwhelming. Weekly vocab lists were a great tool and helped organize learning for each week and what we were being held responsible for. Material built on itself well. The course did ramp up quite a bit at the end with a new binyan every week, and I think the "et" accusative should be taught a bit earlier in the semester; it's not super hard to understand and I think we could have benefited from being able to solidify it more into our knowledge and speaking by learning it maybe before the roots/binyanim. I also liked doing some assignments as a class so that we can hear real time corrections, but doing them again at home if they were homework should be optional. I do wish this course touched on past tense since that's the one thing missing that really makes you conversational, just in case you want to get that full foundation from only L1 and not L2 (where it's taught).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1556155\n 1556155\n \n 1556155\n Strengths: Very very organized professor, coupled with the excellent Padlet interface! Predictable course-load, weekly assignment, and class material. It's a 1.5 credit so there's certainly work to keep you occupied, but very adaptable to your semester if you easily set aside time to work on the daily/weekly assignments (Vocab memorization, Google Doc assignments, etc.).\r\n\r\nWeakness: Could be a lil' too draining at some point in the semester to go to class EVERY 10:30AM morning of your week. Honestly, it may be possible to make one of the days asynchronous/just on Zoom. Maybe.
