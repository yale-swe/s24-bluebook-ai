\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594116\n 1594116\n \n 1594116\n Yes! Even if you are a beginner to Philosophy/Psychology (as I was). Professor Knobe is excellent, and the course covers a lot of basics in the areas of Happiness, Altruism, Virtue, Morality, and Belief in God. We study the works of many influential philosophers like Aristotle, Plato, Descartes, Marx, etc. We also learn to write Philosophy papers. The weekly reading responses are low stakes (you don't need to spend more than 10 minutes writing them, as per the syllabus). The 2 papers over the semester can be long (6-9 pages) but I found I could do them pretty quickly if I was passionate about my topic. I got away without doing 80% of the readings in the class, but you definitely need to be familiar with what each author is saying (because the exams have questions where you are given a passage and have to identify the author). That being said, I wouldn't avoid the course because of this if you don't like doing readings - it is definitely manageable to do well without doing them.
