\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538450\n 1538450\n \n 1538450\n This class was probably the best class I've taken at Yale. Your experience in the class probably depends a lot on who's teaching it, because we had two sections of painting basics running this semester and they were doing completely different things. It seems like the professor is given a lot of flexibility over the curriculum. I can't speak for other professors, but I absolutely loved the class under Oscar. Even though there's a lot of differing levels of ability in the class (people with no experience vs. art majors who have been painting their whole lives), Oscar leveled the playing field by making us paint using these super old, antiquated forms of painting like fresco and egg tempera, which don't behave the way you're used to. Instead of just accepting the paint the way a modern-day canvas will, these surfaces will fight against you (almost like a living organism), and you have to really understand the medium in order to coax out a painting. This means that you have to really think about what you're doing instead of just going on autopilot. The thing I liked most about this class was probably how much Oscar encouraged us to be truly thoughtful and intentional with art. He once said something along the lines of "you can learn these painting techniques in any class, but I'm trying to teach you how to think - how to generate ideas when you're staring at a blank canvas and don't know what to do." As a STEM major with limited artistic experience, I really appreciated that. Besides being a super unique class (The stuff we did in this class eg. fresco, egg tempera, going to the woodshop is stuff you probably won't get to do in any other class), this class allowed for a space where I could reflect on my emotions and draw inspiration from my environment and experiences and use that to create something that's meaningful to me, even if it doesn't look amazing from a conventional perspective. I think even if I were to never paint again in my life (which I don't think is true), I've still taken away something valuable from this class in that it's changed the way I view art. When I first signed up for this class, I was expecting to come in twice a week and paint some still lifes or whatever, but I ended up learning some real life lessons that I think will stick with me for quite some time.
