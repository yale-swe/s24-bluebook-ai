RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702420\n 1702420\n \n 1702420\n Strength: Eun-joo is the kindest person ever and she made the seminars excellent. Course content was interesting and the final project was a good chance to culminate knowledge of the course.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Readings got a bit repetitive in the middle of the course before becoming interesting again. Was not a 50/50 humanities to science split as advertised - not enough science.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1708849\n 1708849\n \n 1708849\n Strengths: The course is very well structured to cover many facets of Asian Americans in STEM. Plenty of time is given to work on the Final Research Project and there are several checkpoints along the way to help with progress during the ample amount of time. There were two guest lectures and one Wright Lab visit, which were very fun. There are weekly assignments which consist of short answer responses for some weeks and problem sets on physics concepts for other weeks. The assignments are very reasonable, and help with gaining a better understanding of different fields and perspectives in STEM. All students contribute to class discussion, every time the class meets.\r\nWeaknesses: The amount of readings vary by week some weeks can be very heavy on reading, while some very minimal.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1709145\n 1709145\n \n 1709145\n I think that it was a very well organized course, however the 9am class time was a bit early and I sometimes found myself struggling to wake up on time for class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1712962\n 1712962\n \n 1712962\n I think a strength could be how there are guest speakers in the class which brought new insights. A weakness may be that this class isn't very engaging, felt like the discussion questions can be answered by one person, and there's not much to build from there. I also felt like the lecture was a lot of reiteration from the readings; I think it might be good to introduce something new or add more to the readings. I think discussions can also happen organically by just starting with how everyone thought about the readings and that can trail off to important things that we got out of it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1718098\n 1718098\n \n 1718098\n Strengths: The course is interdisciplinary so it was cool to see some of the connections. Also, the weekly assignments helped me formulate my thought process for my final paper. Grading was also pretty easy.\r\nWeaknesses: I think it focused a lot more on the humanities side than the STEM, and the STEM was very physics-heavy (not difficult though).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1720461\n 1720461\n \n 1720461\n I feel that it could be more inclusive of diverse Asian backgrounds like South Asians and Middle Easterners.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724242\n 1724242\n \n 1724242\n I think this course had a great balance of humanities (through readings and writing assignments) and sciences (concepts and problem sets), and it goes to show the significance of the crossover between the two.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1725029\n 1725029\n \n 1725029\n Strengths:\r\nThe course content was very interesting and refreshing; the history of science, especially that of Asian Americans scientists, is something that is rarely touched upon even among Asian Americans in STEM.\r\nReadings are generally digestible (with the exception of some scientific papers) and not too long.\r\nThe science problem sets felt connected to the historical events and scientific research we were reading about, and they were easy to approach, almost to the point where they felt like high-school-level STEM questions.\r\nLots of freedom and preparation for the final paper, which made it more manageable.\r\nEun-Joo is always available for students and encourages us to meet and talk with her outside of class.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses:\r\nThe execution wasn't entirely as advertised; it felt more like 80% history and 20% science in contrast to the 50/50 it aimed for, though I think that was to be expected, as it's a hard balance to achieve.\r\nDiscussions sometimes felt lackluster and awkward; I think requiring responses to and questions about the readings before class instead of afterwards would have made for more engaging discussions, as it forces each student to think critically about the source before class.
