Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540924\n 1540924\n \n 1540924\n The main strength of the course is the final project - we had the freedom to create a cool interactive ML-based project of our choice, and we learned a lot and had fun in building it. The weaknesses of the course stem from the lack of instruction, both in lectures and on the homework. Half of each lecture was spent on paper presentations which hardly anybody paid attention to, and there was no accountability for anyone, including the instructor, to pay attention. The other half of each lecture was spent on interesting topics, but we hardly went into depth into any of them, and there was no accountability to pay attention, as the assignments were completely unrelated to the lectures, and there were no exams. This class could be improved by making the assignments directly related to the lectures rather than individual frustrating forays into ROS, and by holding people accountable to pay attention and learn something from the lectures and paper presentations.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541015\n 1541015\n \n 1541015\n I thought that this course gave both a great dive into the theoretical and applied aspects of each new concept, that was a huge strength. I also thought that there could've been more TA assistance or guidance on some of the problem sets as they were a bit daunting.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541108\n 1541108\n \n 1541108\n I thought the course did a good job covering relevant papers, though a lot of the papers were somewhat outdated and did not really show the full power of the techniques. In general, I thought the support provided for the course was quite good, and my questions were always answered very quickly. I loved that the class had a slack.\r\n\r\nI wish that the assignments hadn't been so much about how to use ROS. The class wasn't really a robotics class, and so it felt a bit out of place. On top of that, ROS required a lot of setting up the system. It felt like the problem sets should have been devoted more to learning concepts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546129\n 1546129\n \n 1546129\n Strengths: the course only had 12 students in the class and it had a professor and TA so there was a 6:1 student to teacher ratio. The problem sets and material was well-presented and very interesting.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: no discussion section, as is the case with all CS courses at Yale.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1548207\n 1548207\n \n 1548207\n Strengths: Very interesting and exciting topics. Offers high levels of engagement between students of the class. Gives students an opportunity to work on something very cool for their project.\r\nWeaknesses: Workload is quite high especially during the middle of the semester where we have back to back homework assignments. It was quite challenging to do well in all these assignments with the limited time we had with them. However the workload became much more manageable after homework 3 or 4. Either some parts of the homework can be trimmed down a bit or more time could be given to do them (perhaps moving homework 1 earlier could help).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550101\n 1550101\n \n 1550101\n The psets in this class are extremely well organized! They really enable you to spend time on conceptual understanding instead of getting bogged down in the nitty gritty of programming details / environment setup. Questions on the written portions are challenging in a good way. In general, much better and more in depth than the the AI (470) homeworks. Marynel is an awesome lecturer.\r\n\r\nThe final project was a fun and a productive application of things we learned in class. We were well supported through the proposal and checkpoint, and expectations were clear. Lots of deliverables but totally manageable.
