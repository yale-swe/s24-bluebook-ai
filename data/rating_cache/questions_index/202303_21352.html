\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1713083\n 1713083\n \n 1713083\n This course is branded as a course on consolation literature as a genre, but that theme seems rather forced to me. Prof. Stoner's passion for Boethius was palpable, so I think this course could be made stronger by embracing that and focusing solely on Boethius. Seneca was a nice introduction to the genre, but I felt like Boethius was the true core of the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714509\n 1714509\n \n 1714509\n Strengths: Dr. Stoner is great at teaching the course as the bridge course intended to bring students Latin skills to college level. I wish that we spent more time doing close readings, tracing larger developments, and learning about the context. The large size of the class (since it was designated as the bridge course this year) may have restricted the seminar.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1718555\n 1718555\n \n 1718555\n Class discussions, lessons, and translation sessions were helpful, and the overall pacing of the class was good. This was helpful to brush up on our language skills at the beginning, but it would have been to do more writing and analysis as the course went on.
