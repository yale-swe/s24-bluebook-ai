Around 70-80% of your grade is writing, so you only need to participate minimally in order to get an A. However, take my words with a grain of salt - I discovered halfway through the semester I despised philosophy and thought experiments and other extremely theoretical humanities subjects, so if you know you're into that kind of thing then you could enjoy this course a lot (as I know many of my friends did). \r\n\r\nAbout the labs: they were not good. Not well organized, it felt like high school in terms of doing useless exercises without feedback. I honestly only improved writing through this class through the final research paper, but that was me pushing myself, not the lab helping me. Lab certainly needs to be more involved and (I hate to say this, but) contain more writing exercises that are graded with feedback.
