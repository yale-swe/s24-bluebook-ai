\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1607568\n 1607568\n \n 1607568\n The biggest strength of this course is the clinical rotation portion. Having the opportunity to connect with direct clinical work is incredibly valuable for students hoping to take a patient-oriented path in psychology rather than a research-oriented one. \r\nPerhaps I could structure my weakness as a strength; this class teaches you everything wrong with the present state of clinical psychological care. We have many lecturers come in who are well-versed in their respective practices, but they have been doing these practices for a very long time. That results in outdated perspectives on clinical care that are dehumanizing, eugenicist, ableist, and ignorant regarding race, ethnicity, gender, and orientation. I felt sick to my stomach multiple times throughout this semester as a disabled student of color because of the ways that lecturers and even our own professors spoke about conditions that I have lived with as well as the fact that I don't think that people of color were ever even considered in these conversations. The information is from such a purely clinical perspective that does not have any self-awareness regarding how the subjects of these conversations are experiencing this care. There is absolutely no input from the perspectives of individuals who have the discussed conditions. It's incredibly unclear at times how this is supposed to be a developmental psychopathology course because it often just feels like a poorly articulated abnormal psychology class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616446\n 1616446\n \n 1616446\n The strengths of this course were the professors and guest speakers who had a wealth of information to share with each and every student. No weaknesses.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1617291\n 1617291\n \n 1617291\n I loved the placements in this course. I got way more out of my placement than the actual class. The course was very very open and there wasn't any structure that kept people engaging with the content. For example, there was a required textbook and readings posted. But, there was not a single assignment, quiz, paper or test that interacted with any textbook content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1619171\n 1619171\n \n 1619171\n Strengths: super chill, I loved that the final paper was on a topic of your interest, interesting guest speakers, the best part was the clinical placement opportunity \r\nNo weaknesses

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627490\n 1627490\n \n 1627490\n The strengths of the course were the professors and guest lectures. I felt that I was able to learn a lot more about the various fields in child psychology and was exposed to various fields that I had not known much about before. I also felt that the professors were very understanding of the students' circumstances, especially for observations and assignments, which relieved stress and helped us to stay more engaged with the course material. One weakness of the course is that we are not exactly sure of our grade throughout the semester. There is no concrete way to determine if we need to improve, which is slightly worrying. My hope is that if we complete the assignments with clear effort, we will receive full marks, but once again, this is unknown to us. As such, I think a great way to improve the course is to provide students with grades and feedback after they turn in assignments just so they know where they stand gradewise.

I don't feel like I was held accountable for any of the information in the course and I feel like any of the information in the course could have been found through an abnormal psychology class.
