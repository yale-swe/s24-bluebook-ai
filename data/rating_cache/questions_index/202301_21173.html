Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1647085\n 1647085\n \n 1647085\n This course is very good at teaching you how to actually conduct an ethnography, and it is set up well to keep you on track for writing the longer paper as he has smaller deadlines throughout the course. The only thing that could be better is if we started doing the practical work earlier in the semester. Sometimes, the reading discussions would drag on, and I feel like it would have been better to just switch to talking about practical work like we did in the end of the semester. This would also have allowed us to do a more in depth project, which would have been cool and good practice for future ethnography work.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1671773\n 1671773\n \n 1671773\n Strengths:\r\n - The syllabus is perfectly crafted, a great balance between ethnographies and theory\r\n - The sophomore seminar format creates a great sense of community\r\nWeaknesses:\r\n - Especially at the beginning of the course, it was difficult to understand why we chose to study capitalism from an ethnographic lens\r\n - I wish class activities were more varied. I really appreciated the second class, where we simulated a Polish shoe factory, and the class where we talked about Prof Roger's child's money circulation worksheet. More hands-on ways of connecting with the course material.\r\n - Assessment structure: I would have liked more writing assignments throughout the semester graded for completion, for instance pre or post-class discussion posts or creative/personal writing assignments like "the shoes on your feet." So many thoughts floated after each class and wished for a space to recollect, organize, and apply to my own life. I also wish that I could've gotten feedback on my writing before the bigger midterm essay. I also wish that the midterm could ask for less number of connections to the course content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1678126\n 1678126\n \n 1678126\n Strengths- interesting content, great final project\r\n\r\nWeaknesses- discussion could be guided a bit more
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1678396\n 1678396\n \n 1678396\n The course does a good job of exposing you to various ways of studying capitalism and different ways capitalism is experienced across people. Just because of my personal interests, the most interesting part was our discussion of medical anthropology and people's experiences navigating capitalist structures for their healthcare. I think that class could be a bit better if it focused more on these kinds of things, looking at how capitalism influences very intimate parts of people's lives. I would have loved to learn more about how sexuality is formed in capitalist systems, how health is formed, how disability is formed, etc. The course was very heavy on things like marketing and formation of personhood but I feel like that link could have been made stronger. The readings were good. I enjoyed Dunn, Cattelino, and Ho as our larger ethnographies (Fattal fell a little flat for me) and I think the articles discussing racial capitalism were incredible. I think the seminar could use a little more instructor direction to make many of the connections to everyday life clearer and more relevant.\r\n\r\ni also think that many of the classes started to feel like they were dragging on and like we were rushed for our final project. I think it would have been better to integrate the project much earlier in the class, allotting some time at the end of each class for the project rather than lumping it all at the end.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1680417\n 1680417\n \n 1680417\n The strength of this course is the seminar discussions themselves. It is great that there exist sophomore seminars. The readings themselves were manageable and engaging. The structure of the class was also helpful and conducive to learning complex ideas, while also being fun. It was quite a change of pace from the usual hot and academically steamy nature of seminar courses.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1684320\n 1684320\n \n 1684320\n I loved how small the course is, and I felt like it really made it so everyone was engaged. I also liked how only sophomores were in the class, and we were able to create community that way. The readings were also quite engaging, and I felt like I was able to gain a holistic view of capitalism.
