\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697224\n 1697224\n \n 1697224\n The course, illuminated by Trevor's exceptional teaching prowess and fervent passion for his subject, transcends all expectations of engagement and enjoyment. Trevor's ability to captivate the class for hours on end is nothing short of remarkable; his lectures are so enthralling that boredom becomes an alien concept. His unique talent lies in his capacity to hold the entire class in rapt attention, united in their intrigue, as they absorb the eerie, high-pitched screams and unsettling scraping sounds that are the foundation of the course. This is no ordinary class at Yale - it's a singular experience, a testament to Trevor's unparalleled skill as an educator. The least engaging part of the class would have to be the student presentations, but they are also invaluable as they serve as the culmination of the skills we have been studying throughout the entire course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697787\n 1697787\n \n 1697787\n Loved that there was no readings, it really allowed us to dive deep into the music. I think Trevor has good taste, and even if I didn't like a piece we studied, I could still gain something from it. I might've been the only one who didn't really enjoy having a large chunk of student presentations at the end; it's important for students to gain experience teaching but I still would've liked to have some more professional analysis of pieces during that period. I know that Trevor was thinking of adding in more time for discussion for the next iteration of this course, and I'd like to caution against that. People generally made good observations about the music but they were mostly things I think we were all already aware of, so there wasn't actually the generation of new knowledge through our discussions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1698650\n 1698650\n \n 1698650\n The course was perfect. Keep it!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1699149\n 1699149\n \n 1699149\n The course is structured around the growth of students, not around punitive grading. That allowed for real opportunities to take risks, ask for help, and go along for the journey without fear that our grades would suffer. Trevor is an incredibly engaging lecturer; I could listen to him talk about music for hours and not get bored.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1701127\n 1701127\n \n 1701127\n Overall, a great course for anyone interesting in exploring new territories of music and creation.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1701264\n 1701264\n \n 1701264\n This course is amazing! The course benefits most highly from its professor, who made the class incredibly engaging while providing a safe space for people of all musical or non-musical backgrounds to learn and grow within it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702305\n 1702305\n \n 1702305\n The only weakness I can think of would be that there is not enough time to cover more material. While the sparsity of content did make the class easier to manage on top of my other classes, I think I would have managed fine and enjoyed this class even more if we got to hear and analyze even more pieces. Strengths: interesting music, listening quizzes!, Baa.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710361\n 1710361\n \n 1710361\n The format of the course where Professor Baa would lecture on the context and nuance of the piece on Monday and then we would start Wednesday with a listening quiz was fantastic. There was the perfect balance of challenge and curiosity in delving into each piece and I felt extremely motivated to take the required time to digest the pieces.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1717472\n 1717472\n \n 1717472\n strengths: collaborative, structured in a way to maximize learning/engagement without putting pressure on a letter grade
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724321\n 1724321\n \n 1724321\n This is the kind of class you look forward to and feels welcoming. Done a different way, with a different professor, this class could have easily felt uninviting or stressful, but not in the slightest with Trevor. The listening quizzes were a great non-stressful motivational tool to engage with the pieces.\r\n\r\nI will note: it is at least worth considering that adding assigned readings and papers to the syllabus could be beneficial. Yes, maybe the reading and paper format is not the ideal one for this class, but considering it is the one used by 95% of humanities classes, its absence makes me wonder if I didnt learn as much as I could have. Yes, this made the class much more manageable (that is, easy) and allowed us to focus on the most important part of the coursethe music itself. But Im sure theres something I could have gained from reading some texts about the histor(ies) of the last 50 years of Western art music and from completing analyses of aspects of the pieces and/or their historical contexts in prose form. I thoroughly enjoyed this class and did learn a lot from it, but Im not sure Id say Im walking away feeling confident in my knowledge of the history of this art form or in my ability to critically discuss it in a broad sense.
