\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530429\n 1530429\n \n 1530429\n I learned how to cultivate and go through the writing process to produce modern nonfiction essays. I learned about the importance of details and how to effectively get my point across.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530833\n 1530833\n \n 1530833\n I learned how to read essays for craft in order to determine the techniques a variety of writers used in order to compose compelling nonfiction. I learned to write such essays as well: this course is designed to give you an introduction to five genres of creative nonfiction: personal essays, humor essays, place/travel essays, cultural criticism essays, and profile essays. It's split up into five units: you read a hand of essays in each unit's genre, discuss them in class, and then write your own. Then there are workshop sessions (everyone has two essays workshopped), so fellow students can provide feedback on your work and you have the opportunity to improve your writing. A lot of the material we read was really interesting and honestly quite excellent, and I feel like reading works by such greats as James Baldwin, Joan Didion, and David Sedaris (among numerous others) really enabled to pinpoint what was working in my writing and make it a great deal better.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530879\n 1530879\n \n 1530879\n I helped develop my personal writing voice, figuring out my style by experimenting through a variety of techniques we learned from other essays and our discussions about them.

I learned how different types of personal essays differ in form and techniques that can make each type successful.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530833\n 1530833\n \n 1530833\n Strengths: the curriculum. Tons and tons of really great essays. I do wish that the selection of essays had been a bit more diverse, though. But the format of the course is really great: I like how we got to try out five different types of essays, and I feel like I was really able to challenge my skills as a writer. Weaknesses: I felt like the professor didn't have a super great grasp of how best to teach the material. Reading for craft is difficult, because I feel like it can be challenging to identify exactly what a writer is doing in their piece, but I really think that I learned more from reading the essays than from the discussions we had in class. In part, I feel like that was because the professor kind of had very set opinions about her pieces and got a little bit passive aggressive when students voiced perspectives on pieces that differed from her reading of a writer's intention. That made group discussions pretty tense.

This class doesn't have any notable weaknesses.

I feel like it would be better to add more on the syllabus than is anticipated to be taught and then take things off as you go.

It will stretch and challenge you as a writer but it's really quite worth it! Just take it with a different professor. As far as I've heard, the other professors are all really great.
