\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549207\n 1549207\n \n 1549207\n A major strength of the course was that the material engaged was on the cutting edge of science. Almost everything covered was developed in the last decade. A weakness of the course was its lack of organization and lack of space to brainstorm or explore new solutions. It often felt that the class sacrificed its creativity to remain within the bounds of the assignments. I wish a significant portion of class time was devoted to creating potential solutions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549259\n 1549259\n \n 1549259\n Some major strengths of the course include the selection of topics that we covered, Karla and Saras engagement and enthusiasm for the course, and the Thursday small group journal club. All of these contributed to my furthering of scientific learning significantly! In terms of weaknesses, I wish that we had learned some of the mechanisms and biochemical reactions/cycles in a more systematic and thorough way. I felt like most of these topics, like covering the Shikimate pathway or nitrogen fixation, was covered in a more topical and cursory way, and I left class often wanting to know more.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549998\n 1549998\n \n 1549998\n One of the greatest strengths of this course was Professor Neugebauer's passion and excitement. I could tell that she truly cared about the topics and how climate change is threatening our world, so her lectures were always very engaging and pertinent. The field trips to the Marsh gardens and the architecture firm were also great additions to the lectures since they helped us visualize and see lecture topics in practice. I also really enjoyed the discussion aspect of the course, since rarely do I have the opportunity to discuss research methods and experimentation with my peers, and it was exciting to see my own experiences in the lab transfer over to helping me better understand the papers we read. Another huge strength of the course was the classroom community. I felt as if I really did become closer to many of my classmates in the same discussion section as me, and the atmosphere was always so welcoming and encouraging. In terms of the workload, I thought it was very manageable and reasonable, and I actually really enjoyed both the mini-review and the proposal. \r\n\r\nOne minor weakness of the course was the confusion surrounding the mini-review. Through talking to my classmates, I think most of us had never written a mini-review before, so we were a little confused as to what was expected, especially since there was no clear rubric. However, after talking to Professor Neugebauer and addressing my questions, everything became very easy to understand, so I think providing clearer guidelines in advance will be very helpful for future students. I know that Professor Neugebauer noticed this as well since we were given very clear instructions and expectations for our final proposal, which I really appreciated. Another small weakness also associated with the mini-review was that feedback on topic selection was a little delayed. This didn't end up posing a problem for me since mine was approved, but I know that some of my friends had to rush to find a new topic and we were all hesitant to start writing before we received feedback. \r\n\r\nOverall, I thought this course was taught wonderfully despite being new! There was always very clear communication between Professor Neugebauer and the students, everything felt very organized, and it is easily my favorite MB&B class I've taken so far!

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549998\n 1549998\n \n 1549998\n I would definitely recommend this course to any student with a sufficient biochemistry background! The material is genuinely engaging and so pertinent with regard to the current state of the environment, and Professor Neugebauer is delightful. There is a good mix of instructional styles, including lectures, discussion sections, and field trips, so I think this course will appeal to a wide variety of students. The atmosphere of the class is also very encouraging, and it felt like my classmates and I were on a learning journey together since most of us had very little exposure to the biochemistry behind various climate topics. I also quite enjoyed both of the major assignments and felt like they did push me to grow as a scientist. Finally, Professor Neugebauer's mission of trying to promote an interdisciplinary approach to addressing climate change is extremely relevant, and I would love to see more biochemistry students get involved with climate research in the future.
