\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1561918\n 1561918\n \n 1561918\n Yes. Going into this course, I hadn't taken a Spanish class for around 2 years and needed to complete an L5 credit for my language distributional requirement. I had heard that it was a gut, and I was deciding between this and Advanced Spanish Grammar (SPAN 243). I am very happy with my decision. While I initially thought that Advanced Spanish Grammar would be more up my wheelhouse as I had studied a lot of grammar in high school, I have a friend in the class who has significantly more work (that is harder) every week. If you need an easy L5 for Spanish, this is definitely the course. There are no in class exams; your grade is dependent on an end-of-semester presentation, final essay, homework, and participation. Do keep in mind though that it is difficult going in if you have not taken Spanish in a long time. The first few weeks I could barely understand what she was saying (my high school teachers were all Latin American and she is from Spain) and the readings/homework assignments took me quite a few hours. However, after about a month I got used to it and started to remember a lot of the vocab I had forgotten. She also assigned a lot of homework at the beginning (4-5 2 page essays/week) and then slowly decreased it in frequency to about (1-2 2 page essays/week) which was very manageable but overwhelming at first. Also, if you haven't taken Spanish in a while, I highly recommend getting a free tutor through Yale's program. It's super easy to request one; I met with mine for an hour weekly the entire semester, and she was super helpful to get up to speed with a lot of the Spanish I had forgotten and make sure I was comprehending the material properly. Overall, Carreras is a wonderful prof who really cares about her students and is a very sweet lady. I would definitely recommend this course.
