\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1678011\n 1678011\n \n 1678011\n Everyone (from day one) knew that this course was being workshopped and developed as it was being taught, and so the feeling of having a bunch of disjointed components that only tenuously fit together was fairly pervasive (and acknowledged by Prof. Quinn as well.) Even so, the course was fantastic. Prof. Quinn is the consummate theory teacher. He cares so much about both music and his students, devotes so much effort to creating beautiful graphics and visuals to aid memorization and analysis, and overall is just a wonderful pedagogue who made every class an event to look forward to. As someone who dabbled in theory beforehand and never really "got it", Prof. Quinn's usage of the line of fifths (and insistence on focusing on chroma, sharpness [ti] and flatness [fa] in the scale itself) was truly unique and an incredibly effective way to learn theory. One strong suggestion I would have is to split the course into two tracks, sort of what exists in the Physics department (and what DSAC is trying to implement in the Computer Science dep't), namely - just because students come into the track with no background at all doesn't mean that some of them (e.g. myself) don't want to cover a ton of ground and get a lot of practice, far more than is generally associated with MUSI 110 (known as a "gut"). Making an intensive introduction to music theory track in parallel would be something I'd love to see and that I would have loved to have taken myself. In terms of the actual course materials themselves, I think Prof. Quinn is quite aware of what could be better, what needs to be scrapped, etc. (but just in case / some other thoughts: we never did anything with the melakartas after the two days we spent on them; I would have liked to see more discussion of functional analysis and a bit more roman numeral analysis, since it was mentioned altogether; it would be cool to introduce some maqamat [albeit very complicated with the course as is]; homework was nonexistent for the second half of the course and was sorely missed; the musical capabilities of the group were such that we probably could have used Sibelius/Noteflight/Musescore for the last assignment, which would have given us experience with an important set of tools for writing music; a lecture/section model would make good use of the TAs and would have been very helpful for me)
