RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562809\n 1562809\n \n 1562809\n Perhaps a bit more direct explanation as to how some of the more complicated frameworks we've read actually function.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1578505\n 1578505\n \n 1578505\n The syllabus for this course was great; all of the readings were fascinating, and we clearly followed the trajectory of the phenomenological tradition as it has evolved through the years. Discussion posts on Canvas nicely complimented seminar, as they let us get some ideas on the table before we even walked into the classroom (which was critical as there were MANY ideas to discuss). Everyone in the seminar contributed meaningfully to my experience, but I wonder how discussions might have changed if the class size were slightly smaller.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1583280\n 1583280\n \n 1583280\n For those interested in phenomenology (or continental philosophy in general), this course is nearly perfect. There is a lot of reading, and it is tough, but if you are interested (and it is super interesting!) it really pays off. Professor Khawaja does a great job of anchoring the course in the 'traditional' phenomenologists, but really centering more recent, diverse critical theorists for the bulk of the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1590635\n 1590635\n \n 1590635\n strengths: Prof. Khawaja is wonderful, and very knowledgeable. Her office hours were super helpful, and she was very understanding and conscious of the intimidating nature of phenomenology, which she accounted for in her style of seminar (e.g., not picking on people)\r\nweaknesses: i felt that the discussions did not always have a clear direction, and were quite dominated by grad students at times. I'm not sure exactly how, but i feel that students would have benefited from more lecturing perhaps, and ways of making sure everyone contributed so that the material could feel a bit more accessible
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1590804\n 1590804\n \n 1590804\n The syllabus set up was not the best, however, overtime students adapted to the classroom.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1597302\n 1597302\n \n 1597302\n That this course was a mix of graduate and undergraduate students means that seminar discussions were often dominated by the voices that have engaged with this material for a substantially longer period of time. Though this was incredibly informative as someone who is very new to phenomenology, this environment made extensive verbal engagement rather daunting. The undergrad section facilitated by the TF was a helpful way to allow those students that were hesitant to participate in the main seminar setting to ask whatever happened to be on their mind while working through a week's readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1599228\n 1599228\n \n 1599228\n I thought this was an excellent selection of readings. I enjoyed reading from Husserl forward (very thankful we skipped the idealists in retrospect), and I appreciated reading feminist and queer phenomenologists. I also appreciated the subtle grouping of postcolonial philosophers within the broader context of phenomenological thought.
