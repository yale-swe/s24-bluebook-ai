\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1607749\n 1607749\n \n 1607749\n The biotic and abiotic aspects of the ecology of wetlands, including properties of soils, plant communities et cetera. The challenges in managing wetlands from a policy/regulation perspective as well as the technical management perspective.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1612074\n 1612074\n \n 1612074\n Everything from wetlands ecology and soil structures to how they are classified, regulated or mitigated. We learned about many examples and heard from professionals in the field.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614152\n 1614152\n \n 1614152\n I learned a lot about wetlands ecology conservation and management.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1615742\n 1615742\n \n 1615742\n Defining different varieties of wetlands, understanding basic management ideas
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1617015\n 1617015\n \n 1617015\n I learned about wetland ecosystem function and restoration
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1631602\n 1631602\n \n 1631602\n I learned a lot about wetlands, specifically the scientific, regulatory, and policy perspectives! i appreciate the way that this course broadened my perspective on what a wetland is and what it can provide
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1636854\n 1636854\n \n 1636854\n I learned how to identify different types of wetlands, the ecological benefits they provide, and the natural and anthropogenic threats they are under. I learned to identify wetland plants and animals, as well as identify in the field what category of wetlands were present. We thought critically in class about mitigation plans for preserving, replacing, and restoring wetlands as well as the pros and cons of each one. Wetland protection policy was another main focus of the class, and I felt like I now have a solid foundational understanding of what that includes.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1636919\n 1636919\n \n 1636919\n We learned about different types of wetlands, how to classify/delineate wetlands, the governmental agencies that control the management of wetlands (such as EPA), and recent regulations. We went through several case studies of people/entities/companies who wanted to destroy wetlands for different construction projects, and how wetland scientists (including hydrologists and soil scientists) would determine if it's a good idea. We also learned about impacts of climate change on wetlands (including how bogs degrade and release carbon), how wetlands sequester (or surprisingly release) carbon. We also learned about wetland carbon banks and mitigation banks, and the policy behind them. We learned about wetland flora and fauna such as obligate plants, animals (such as beavers and their importance as ecosystem engineers), and invasive species. We also read a LOT of primary literature (5-6+ papers per week).
