\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545921\n 1545921\n \n 1545921\n This course dives deeply into feminist perspectives on philosophy. Maybe they are a little out there but this class gave me to tools to articulate my issues with how philosophy is often done, especially at older, more traditional institutions like Yale.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545928\n 1545928\n \n 1545928\n I learned a great deal about different approaches to feminist moral theorizing. We tackled such questions as: What does it mean to philosophize in theory versus in practice? Is "objectivity" possible or even desirable? Is an ethics of care "feminine," feminist, or anti-feminist? Is emotion useful or harmful when you're making moral judgments?\r\n\r\nAs for skills, I learned how to write. How to REALLY write. I've taken dozens of WR classes and none of them taught me how to write clear, concise, and engaging prose like this class has. Other WR say vague things about topic sentences and passive voice; this class really gets into the nitty-gritty of word choice and sentence/paragraph/paper structure. We even learned how to psychologically approach writing so that papers didn't feel like pulling teeth. The things I learned about writing in this class are things every Yale student should learn, whether they're in the humanities or in STEM.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546001\n 1546001\n \n 1546001\n I learned how to critically think and analyze the readings. I genuinely learned so much over the course of this semester and have been thinking about each text I've read in light of new experiences and other classes, especially about anger, virtue ethics, and care ethics. Moya's class gave us the space to develop our writing and be a little more experimental with our writing style, their willingness to let us critique the work and work through texts and problems we had with each of them, and their ability to make the class feel like such a safe space for personal and academic thoughts made it one of the best courses I've ever taken at Yale.

I also improved my writing SO MUCH. Moya had us read many readings on how to write, and we did lots of little editing exercises, and ultimately I came away a MUCH better writer. This was literally the best class and I learned a huge amount!!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549443\n 1549443\n \n 1549443\n This course taught two main things extremely well.
