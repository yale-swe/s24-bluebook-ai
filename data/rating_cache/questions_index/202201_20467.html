Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575175\n 1575175\n \n 1575175\n The main weakness of this course is its lack of organization. Every week Prof. Anderson assigns a *lot* of readings, and we don't actually end up discussing most of them, so it feels like a waste of time to try to finish all the readings when only 1 or 2 are the actual focus of class discussions. I would have preferred to do those few readings more in-depth and be able to devote more time to them. There's not really a clear progression or structure to the course, and I think it would've helped to even just give each week a clear theme/topic. The seminars themselves were not really organized either; it was mostly Prof. Anderson rambling (although it was super interesting rambles!) without any discussion questions or real topic. So really it was just pretty disorganized. We also didn't find out about deadlines until a week or two in advance, so it was difficult to plan around those assignments. We ended up with 3 essays - one reflection paper in February, a midterm paper in March, and a short essay final exam due at the end of reading period. So workload-wise it wasn't too bad, I just had no idea in advance when those deadlines would be. \r\n\r\nAlso, the scope of the course is pretty limited. It mostly covers the deviance of Black men, which is obviously a very important topic, and I think the class as a whole would've benefited from broadening that scope to other forms of deviance - women, LGBTQ+ people, disabled people, how forms of deviance and marginalization intersect, etc. Prof. Anderson had said he wasn't very familiar with those topics, which I suppose is fair, but it would've been nice to have a broader scope, especially when a lot of the same frameworks that we did discuss could be applied to tons of different marginalized groups. I think every single reading he assigned was written by a man, which was pretty annoying because there's a lot more scholarship out there that was neglected in this class. \r\n\r\nBut overall this class was great and I learned a lot in spite of those weaknesses. He was pretty good about giving feedback on our essays and trying to engage students in discussions. I think the main strength was just how knowledgeable and experienced Prof. Anderson is; he's very good at explaining these concepts and making it clear how applicable they are to everyday life.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575182\n 1575182\n \n 1575182\n The readings are amazing and Prof. Anderson is kind and knowledgeable. I wish class discussions had a bit more structure to them
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575223\n 1575223\n \n 1575223\n Strengths:\r\nThe Professor was extremely knowledgeable, especially in regards to race, and was open to examples from different fields. \r\nThe readings were insanely interesting.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses:\r\nI feel the class would benefit from more open discussion -- with students responding to each other, rather than the Professor's points. Some guiding questions could help in this regard. \r\nSometimes, the prompts given to us for essays were slightly vague. They would have benefitted from some extra information on guidelines, so that we could know exactly what we had to do.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1580995\n 1580995\n \n 1580995\n This course benefited from an excellent selection of readings, covering a range of approaches within the sociology of deviance. Course discussions were engaging and always connected to real-world events and experiences. Despite the variety of topics covered, recurring themes helped to connect them all in fascinating ways. That said, I think the course could also be improved by a more formal laying-out of those themes, and a more explicit roadmap of how the readings discussed are to be viewed relative to each other.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1598763\n 1598763\n \n 1598763\n Some may say that it was slightly unorganized during the lectures but I think that helped our discussions get as deep as we went.
