\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541154\n 1541154\n \n 1541154\n Strengths: Very interesting/relevant topic; balanced (though somewhat heavy) workload with weekly brief structure; occasional guest speakers via Zoom; writing weekly briefs (500-700 words each) helped me to digest readings and think about my reactions to them. Writing one every week forced you to stay very engaged with the class, and I appreciated that weekly routine to the class, even though they got tiring at the end. In my opinion, smaller assessments on consistent schedules help you learn better and are a lot less stressful than large, one-off exams; course schedule is very well organized with a build up to the main SRM/SAI discussion during the first half and an unraveling of it during the second half. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1551176\n 1551176\n \n 1551176\n The organization of the course timeline and materials are a huge strength. The focus on technology, followed by ethics and public perception of geoengineering technology also allows for very meaningful and intriguing discussions. \r\n\r\nAnother strength relates to the weekly briefs. Although I would say the briefs result in more work than a course with just a couple of papers or exams, these assignments really prepare students to engage in discussion each week. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses with the course relate to a significant lack of emphasis on diversity, equity, and inclusion in geoengineering spaces. I would have liked to see more discussion on the implications such an imbalanced sector in terms of how the geoengineering technology is perceived globally. Perhaps that would make an argument for a push for broader representation in the field.
