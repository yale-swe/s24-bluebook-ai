\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1573270\n 1573270\n \n 1573270\n At its core, this is a literature class, so it is important to keep that in mind. You are reading and analyzing literature, and the assignments (other than the final) are structured in that way. I loved the field trips and the dedication and attention of prof. cadieu. Also, the field trips at the end of the class to the YUAG and Beinecke were great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1583354\n 1583354\n \n 1583354\n This is a great class. It is interesting, engaging, and fun. If you like trains, this might be the best class you take at Yale.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1590817\n 1590817\n \n 1590817\n Strengths: fascinating, interdisciplinary. Well organized and received some of the best feedback on my writing from any instructor in college. \r\nImprovements: Zola (the beginning) and the CEID/YUAG part is really fun, but I felt we spent too long (for my taste) on the cultural anthropology of the subways. Also, close reading was fine but it would be nice to focus more on the writers big picture philosophy.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1591431\n 1591431\n \n 1591431\n Strengths: The class discussions were really well-guided and the topics of discussion were super interesting. Fun examinations of texts beyond the classroom.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Some text discussion felt abrupt and out of place, especially those we spent just a day on.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593442\n 1593442\n \n 1593442\n Professor Cadieu did an incredible job structuring this course. She didn't make the reading assignments too intense, encouraged each member of the class to contribute, and helped us form a community around nothing other than French trains. These seminars never felt like they were 75 minutes, and the questions she asked us to discuss were always super insightful and encouraged us not just to discuss the literature but our own experiences interacting with the various subjects. I never felt that we rushed through the material, which was the first time I had ever felt like that in a Yale class. Each essay we received was marked up in its entirety and with a paragraph of comments at the end, which was terrifying at first, but it was so clear how much she loved this subject and wanted to develop students' writing skills. The only weakness that I could think of is that the reading assignments became a lot longer towards the second half of the class, which didn't give us as much time to dive into the text as the first half, but still great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1595006\n 1595006\n \n 1595006\n Strengths: Most of the readings were interesting, Professor Cadieu is kind and very knowledgeable, and the final project where we make a train was fun! For the final paper, I appreciated that there were many prompts to choose from. I needed this flexibility as having a final paper in addition to the final project was a lot, especially amid my other finals. I also liked Professor Cadieus system of using Calendly to schedule office hours. \r\n2 Minor Weaknesses: The syllabus states that all students should get the same edition for the required course texts. However, I would have appreciated more flexibility in this rule as this would have allowed me, as a low-income student, to get the books for free through Borrow Direct. Borrow Direct had all the required books, but in different editions, so I ended up renting them from the bookstore. I did notice that some students ended up not getting the required editions, and they did not seem to encounter any issues as a result. In the future, it would be great if the rule about getting the same editions could be relaxed to a recommendation. I also thought it was unrealistic that we had readings assigned on the days we were presenting our final projects. We wanted to spend the whole class presenting the hard work we had put into our projects, and all of our time for homework that week was devoted to finishing the projects. We ended up not discussing the readings, so I think it would have been better to distribute these readings to earlier weeks.
