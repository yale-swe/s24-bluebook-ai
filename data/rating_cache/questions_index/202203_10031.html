\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618679\n 1618679\n \n 1618679\n Strengths: Professor Quarshie. He is a genius and makes everyone feel comfortable in his class. He pushed us to interrogate our understandings and to challenge preconceived notions. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: we only wrote 11 total pages for this course, which is supposed to be a writing credit. I felt like one more essay would have made the grade distribution less stressful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618713\n 1618713\n \n 1618713\n The readings could be shortened for concision, as it would make them easier to discuss
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618732\n 1618732\n \n 1618732\n The strengths of this class are that students are exposed to new perspectives and are forced to think hard about their own world view in order to understand the material. Additionally, having challenging paper topics with relatively low page limits pushes students to say what they really mean faster and more clearly. The only weakness is that the class had an extremely heavy reading load, this could be improved by swapping out some of the readings for podcasts or videos that discuss the same topic.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625964\n 1625964\n \n 1625964\n The seminar-style discussions were very engaging and a major strength of the course. I found that sometimes the readings were a little long and it seemed many students werent able to get through them, so that may have affected our ability to discuss those readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1632098\n 1632098\n \n 1632098\n Strength organization. The most important thing for a course to have is a defined structure, set deadlines, and clear expectations. Nothing else really matters because if you have those things from the start, anyone who chooses to take the course is agreeing to whatever expectations were set. This course had a lot of reading but I don't feel it was excessive, even coming from a background where many of my classes actually have zero reading.\r\nWeakness n/a? If you don't like reading or want a course where you can get away with not doing the reading, don't take this class probably, but I wouldn't call that a weakness of the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1635181\n 1635181\n \n 1635181\n Strengths: 1 meeting per week for a longer time, plenty of time in between meetings for completing the required readings; each student has an opportunity to lead the discussion week of their choice, allowing them to bring personal enthusiasm to the topic for that week\r\n\r\nWeakness: allowing students to lead discussions sometimes leads to long stretches of silence at the beginning of class which might be avoided/shortened by asking discussion leaders to pick out specific sections of the text that broadly interest them, not just specific (sometimes leading and complex) questions.
