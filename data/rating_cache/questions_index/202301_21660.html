\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1647163\n 1647163\n \n 1647163\n Though I know part of the point of the course is to cover lots of material with little depth, this was a bit too far. Sometimes Dr. Booth would speed-talk his way through every type of screw head in 1 minute and then move on, with that never to be seen again. Or he'd spend 2 minutes talking about how molds should be properly designed for lost wax casting. Cool I guess, but kind of a waste of time. Another difficulty is that because there's so much breadth, we hardly do any practice work in class. So, when you sit down with the pset, you realize you have no idea what to do, and the only examples Dr. Booth works through in class are extremely complicated and under-explained. The course should build up this base of knowledge better, starting with simple examples and fully explaining equations, then working up to complex examples. Also, we received negligible feedback on problem sets. For example, no psets were graded until 3 days before the first midterm, at which point Dr. Booth graded all the psets but provided no comments on what went wrong, and no answer key to figure out how you could have done it right!\r\n\r\nThe main lab project is pretty cool though!
