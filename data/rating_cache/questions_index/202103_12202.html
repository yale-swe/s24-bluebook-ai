\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1551710\n 1551710\n \n 1551710\n I think that this course was amazing, and there really is no way that it could be improved. We covered a lot of short stories from a variety of time periods of Russian history. Professor Bojanowska always began each class discussion with an overview of the history of the time period and a biography of the author, which allowed us to contextualize our discussion more. Each story was also situated within a larger unit - the structure of the genre and critical tools, intertextuality, and narrative complication - which really helped with focusing the discussion as well and allowing us to learn more about each of these ideas. I also think that the course breakdown really made a lot of sense and helped with cementing everything that we have learned. I think that the midterm quiz is helpful as a review of all of the stories we have read. The only comment that I would make is that I think the smaller group discussions were at times more helpful and I would have enjoyed a few more of those because it provided a bit more structure to the discussion and allowed us to really explore the big ideas of the course through the questions or excerpts that Professor Bojanowska provided. The larger group discussion was still thoroughly engaging, but I think that sometimes discussions jumped very quickly from one theme to the next.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554501\n 1554501\n \n 1554501\n I thought the amount of reading for this class was Perfect because it allowed for an in-depth exploration into the text without worrying about completing the texts in general.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554724\n 1554724\n \n 1554724\n The workload is manageable and spread evenly across the semester and we got a heads up when a text was longer than 50 pages. \r\n\r\nProf. Bojanowska is the largest strength of this class. Her passion for the stories we read was simply infectious. She had the unique ability to make century-old stories accessible and meaningful to students with varying degrees of exposure to Russian literature. She also created a learning environment in which I felt challenged but equally comfortable to make mistakes and test out ideas.\r\n\r\nEven though the class centers on the themes of love and death, the selection of stories was vast and addressed other pertinent issues like Russias imperial campaign in the Caucus, Anti-Semitism, and the afterlives of communism in Eastern Europe.\r\n\r\nThis class is truly accessible to all students of all writing abilities. It assumes no background in Russian, literature, or the humanities. I appreciated that the essays tested ideas and not ones ability to compose beautiful, ornate prose.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555985\n 1555985\n \n 1555985\n The course consisted of a pretty good array of Russian short stories, some of which were great reads, and others of which were okay but by no means special. My biggest qualm with this course is that it was an enormous waste of an hour and fifteen minutes twice a week. You would struggle to find another course in which you could meet for such a long time and yet gain only a surface-level interpretation of each story. Professor Bojanowska had little to offer herself, though she had notes, and I would have loved to see her steer discussion in a more coherent manner. Seminars would consist of some context from her, then individual students making isolated points for the majority of the class, with a final firing off of points from the professor at the end.\r\n\r\nThese isolated points were sometimes very good, and in those cases Professor Bojanowska should have stopped the discussion there and dived deeper, but pretty much every time she failed to do so, and simply nodded and pointed at someone else to say their point which was completely off topic. In many cases students came up with, frankly, bad interpretations, and instead of challenging these interpretations, or challenging the student to make a better one or refine their point, the professor again simply nodded and pointed to the next person.\r\n\r\nThis made for generally intellectually weak discussion, and I probably would have learned the same amount if I had not turned up to any classes.\r\n\r\nOne other thing I noticed, having looked up many of the stories on the internet, is that many of Professor Bojanowskas notes were taken straight from Wikipedia. One time she rattled off word for word what I had on my screen in the lesson. This was pretty shocking, especially as there is a lot of rich literary criticism of the stories we read, criticism which Professor Bojanowska may have quoted a couple of times, but never really engaged us with.\r\n\r\nOverall I think this class was really let down by a lack of creativity from the professor. Discussions were stilted and boring, and apart from a simplistic structuring of the course into three modules, the stories were rather homogeneous in meaning and subject matter. I dont think this class should be taught again in its current format.
