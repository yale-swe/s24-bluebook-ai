RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1584685\n 1584685\n \n 1584685\n This is a great course for those who want to continue to engage with Japanese.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1584707\n 1584707\n \n 1584707\n The strengths of the course is that we were able to have a lot of conversation practice in class, and that we learned a lot of grammar, read a lot, and engaged with various types of Japanese media(movies, videos, conversation audio). \r\n\r\nThe weakness of the course is really dependent on the student, but I would say it's kanji and vocab memorization. I liked that we didn't have in class kanji quizzes, or vocab quizzes, but I think that as a result it's easy to neglect practicing and memorizing them. I would recommend students study and review kanji and vocab steadily throughout the semester, or the midterm and final writing sections will be difficult. \r\n\r\nIn general, a lot of the work of learning new Japanese will be practicing and reviewing outside of what is expected in class/ for homework, so I think you have to be willing to do that to see exceptional improvement in your Japanese- especially in your writing.
