I will carry that mindset with me throughout my time in college and the rest of my life reading.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539561\n 1539561\n \n 1539561\n I think DS is the best Yale can offer for first-year students interested in the humanities. Although the pace of the program doesnt allow you to go too deep into the material, I found many lectures highly inspiring and most discussion sections extremely fruitful. Overall, DS provides a solid foundation for whatever you hope to pursue later. \r\nSometimes DS sections have different pace so that the timing for lectures is not always perfect. For examples, once the lecture came after we already finished discussing the book in seminars. I wish the program overall could coordinate this better. Most of the time, I have to do it on my own in addition to the already tremendous reading workload.

It also made the task of reading everything in time for class, for me personally, almost impossible, forcing me to prioritize and not always finish the readings which held me back from appreciating some of the texts the way I wish I could have.
