\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714876\n 1714876\n \n 1714876\n Directed Studies Philosophy introduced me to philosophical reconstruction, analysis, and objection. Professor Buturovic fostered an environment that encouraged nuanced analysis and critical thinking, in a way that no other class at Yale has provided me. In particular, we deconstructed philosophical arguments at such a granular level that we were able to (1) fully understand highly complex philosophical axioms, and (2) reconstruct the argument better than the author made it -- identifying the flaws and deficiencies in the author's original argument. Moreover, this class gave me confidence in philosophical writing. Professor Buturovic's comments are exhaustive, thorough, critical, fair, salient, and nuanced; she provides perhaps the most detailed feedback I've received to date -- providing margin comments AND a 300-400 word executive summary of strengths/weaknesses of the essay. This granularity of feedback allowed me to understand the strengths AND deficiencies of my argument and objection, enabling me to understand multiple facets of improvement: argumentative clarity, textual precision, philosophical analysis, and craftsmanship of writing. Above all, I feel that DRST Philosophy Fall taught me a far greater understanding of the Western cannon than I otherwise would have had. I believe Professor Buturovic is responsible for this lucidity. In seminar, Professor Buturovic would frequently connect readings to the cannon they built on (e.g. connecting Parmenides and Anaxagoras as Pre-Socratics to the evolution of Platonic and Aristotelian thought, relating the arguments of Empedocles and Alexander of Aphrodisias to later Islamic Philosophers, linking Aristotelian boulesis and Stoic prohairesis to Augustine's On Free Choice of the Will). These intra-textual connections drew a clear line in the evolution of thought, which I think is unique to our section of DS Philosophy.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714876\n 1714876\n \n 1714876\n Strengths:\r\n* THE PROFESSOR. This 4000 character text-box cannot do Professor Buturovic justice. Her seminar was brilliant. I will attempt to explain the strengths of this course through an explication of Professor Buturovic's seminar: (1) ANALYTIC RIGOR. Professor Buturovic explained the nuance behind philosophical arguments in a way that is unique amongst DS professors. We delved into ideas like Agent Dependency in the Euthyphro, the Compresence of Opposites as they related to Plato's Phaedo, the Existential, Veridical, and Predicative Definitions of Knowledge vs. Belief in Plato's Republic, Stoic Prohairesis as they related to Augustine's Free Will, etc. The unparalleled nature of Professor Buturovic's analysis made the class discussion especially sharp, as our section grasped deeper nuance and more rigorous connections in the texts. (2) FREE MARKET OF IDEAS. We built our understanding of the text with rigorous seminar discussion. However, Professor Buturovic rejected a dogmatic interpretation of text. Instead, we debated all ideas. We debated Platonism vs. Aristotelianism. We had calm, conversational, critical discussion of highly nuanced ideas -- debating Professor Buturovic herself as she played the devil's advocate to stress-test our interpretations, and debating our peers as we tried to figure out the meaning behind our texts. This free and open academic discourse made our seminar especially special. (3) STRUCTURED FLOW OF CONTENT. In Philosophy, it is very easy to get 'lost in the sauce.' Professor Buturovic maintained organization and order in class by providing structured outlines of our discussion at the beginning of each class; these were documents for us to take notes on, which also provided an implicit structure for our discussion. In that way, our seminar could explore ideas, while we followed a structure of pre-established content, allowing us to stay on track and get through high quantities of content, while exploring the quality of discussions. (4) META-TEXTUAL COMMENTARY. Professor Buturovic provided us contextual explanations with CODAs, explaining how each text 'fit into' the larger cannon, explicating how ideas grew and evolved, and providing us a broader understanding of the text beyond simply words on a page. In this way, Professor Buturovic gave us a higher-level understanding of texts, by allowing us to figure out how texts evolved from each other, influenced each other, connect with each other, and respond to each other. We truly understand the 'cannon' as opposed to a series of independent texts. \r\n\r\nWEAKNESSES:\r\n* none. fantastic class. best experience at Yale to date. lectures are incredible. seminars are brilliant. MUST TAKE FOR STUDENTS.
