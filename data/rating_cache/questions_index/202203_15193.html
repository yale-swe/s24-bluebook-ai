\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1608737\n 1608737\n \n 1608737\n The strengths of this course were Professor Soles and Jon, who both cared so much about their students and their wellbeing. The discussions we all had in class were also very beneficial and productive, and always helped me better understand the texts we read. A large weakness of this course, though, was the writing labs on Fridays. When we went to the Beinecke or YUAG to explore ancient texts or artworks, these labs felt useful and were very cool actually. However, sitting in the Poorvu for three hours on a Friday afternoon, talking about structures of our papers and approaches to close reading, was very, very unproductive, and felt like a waste of my time. On those occasions, I felt that we should have only needed to listen to an hour lecture. Of course, this lab component is still being developed, and I felt our students did a great job giving feedback on how to improve the lab, but from my experience it was still a great weakness of this course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614969\n 1614969\n \n 1614969\n Strengths- the syllabus had a lot of texts that kept me engaged. I think I was shown a variety of literature that is good foundational knowledge of the remainder of my Yale experience. \r\nWeaknesses- Class discussion time was very unstructured. Many times it felt like a mini lecture. I did not really understand the "goal" of the discussion or that week's reading. Because there was so much reading with such dense texts, I think it would have been helpful for there to be at least some guiding questions for that week. Or specific points to talk about. A lot of times the discussion was just flow of thought. The Friday SPGX sessions in the Poorvu Center were frankly a mess and a waste of time. I learned the same amount of writing material in a semester of Friday lab as I did in one week of English 114. Do not take this class if you are looking to learn to write. I think a common weekness, for both the lab and the class, was that there was not a "point" or a shared understanding of why we were doing what we were doing.

Instead, they largely discussed writing in a theoretical sense and focused on a thesis formula for nearly the entire semester, even when we had been submitting assignments to our seminar professor. The only good and educational part of the lab was our field trips to the Beinecke and the YUAG.

The only weakness (and this has nothing to do with this class specifically, more the 6PGX suite as a whole) was the 3 hour writing lab every Friday. It sounded nice in theory but in practice it was never all that helpful, I think it's just too long to stay in one place Especially when most of the time no actual writing was being accomplished, only lecturing on writing and even this moved at a very slow pace.
