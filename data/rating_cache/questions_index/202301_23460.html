\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1660442\n 1660442\n \n 1660442\n China in Six Keys (EALL 256) part of the 2023 DeVane Lectures program was a powerful, important and topical class taught by Yales Dr. Jing Tsu. For those not familiar, the DeVane Lectures program opens up Yale classrooms to non-Yale affiliated New Haven residents at no cost and as a result, our class was often filled with as many adult and continuing education learners as there were Yale students. That said, the courses discussion sections are exclusively for credit-earning students. \r\n\r\nChina in Six Keys ambitiously tackles six contemporary topics (ranging from Chinas ambitions in the Arctic to an examination of the artistic work and life journey of artist Ai Weiwei) about modern China and examines them all contemporaneously as well as in deeper historical context. Against the backdrop of those topics, Dr. Tsu focuses on how China has historically adapted, assimilated, rejected and integrated with a world system that has been largely shaped by the West. Each of the individual topics offer opportunities for students to think deeply about and engage with original source material. Dr. Tsu makes clear that her goal is to draw lessons from the past to help students better understand the drivers behind the choices China and the West will make in the present and future what those choices might mean. The course easily achieves and surpasses her goal.\r\n\r\nClass mechanics are straight-forward; weekly lectures and discussion sections. Grading is entirely based on class attendance, discussion section participation and six (relatively short, approximately 600-to-750-word essays graded on a check/check plus basis) on each of the topics the course covers. While there are suggested prompts for each of the papers, they are by design open-ended and invite students to voice their own ideas, interpretations and connections with the material/subjects. \r\n\r\nWhile I really valued and enjoyed the lectures, I ***loved*** the smaller, more intimate discussion sections. I was incredibly fortunate to have Melodie Liu TF our section - and she was phenomenal. Melodie brought her deep domain expertise, backed by thoughtful and strong classroom moderator skills to each session - always facilitating meaningful discussions; always with a positive, encouraging approach. Beyond the actual sessions, the feedback she provided on each and every paper I submitted was reflective of the extraordinary time and care she invested reviewing our work. Her comments and questions about my essays were insightful and helpful. Reading her feedback on each assignment inspired me to think even more deeply about the themes and topics we were covering; and often motivated me to go back and spend more time with the original source material. \r\n\r\nBecause it was part of the DeVane Lecture series, Im not sure if/when this course might be offered again. That said, if you have an interest in better understanding China, its role in the world as well as the historical drivers behind current economic, social and political trends within Modern China and how those might effect the global world order, China in Six Keys is a course you cant afford to miss.
