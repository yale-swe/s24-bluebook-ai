Prof Kinder is gifted at encouraging and lifting all voices; she was fully and enthusiastically engaged w the material at all times, and her devotion was contagious. She managed our zoom mtgs w conviviality and assurance; the seriousness of attention to writing was supported by goodwill and exquisitely apt, challenging instruction. Our readings reflected a diversity of identities, chronological eras, stylistic methods, and ethical concerns. Our writing assignments were likewise varied and rigorous. Assignments were short, long, and in-between; we had to write spontaneously in class as well as to craft and edit longer pieces for submission. The peer editor aspect was helpful and stimulating. My writing improved in gratifying, noticeable increments because of her insightful editing and personalized challenges. In class, I appreciated her seminar-directed "card system" which kept the zoom mtg lively, respectful, intellectually stimulating. The course neatly balanced teacher-directed expertise w opportunities for student exploration. I can't think of enough great things to say about this demanding and deeply profitable experience.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1515147\n 1515147\n \n 1515147\n The course was one of my favorites in that it pushed me both as a writer and a reader. Highlights include the three 30 minute sessions we had one-on-one with Colleen where we talked about our writing and the class as a whole, the texts she selected (satire was both engaging and difficult to understand the workings of), the group of people taking the seminar (many very interesting and fun to get to know through their contributions and writing). I also really enjoyed the micro-essay prompts, which required creative response and guided our writing. There werent downsides per se, but the class was a lot of work. With three micro-essays and a macro-essay due per week, on top of the 6h30 of class, reviewing your writing partners essays, reading all the texts, and possibly a meeting with Colleen, the class consumed a lot of time. You get what you put in in most classes and here its similar you put in a lot of time but develop your writing unlike in any class Ive taken so far.\r\n\r\nThe one thing I will note that could possibly be refined was the requirement to submit a piece to a publication. I appreciate the requirement (as we either get a piece published or learn to deal with rejection), but I think it was left as somewhat of a side-note till the end. A little more guidance from Colleen on what she felt was publishable and perhaps to what publication would have been helpful. The micro-essays felt a bit short to be published and I would have put more thought into the topic of my macro-essays had I known more about how publication worked earlier on.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1515218\n 1515218\n \n 1515218\n This course was the best course I've taken at Yale and I think will be the best course I will ever take! Colleen Kinder is an amazing professor, and I'm so sad she doesn't teach at Yale during the academic year! I think taking it in-person will make it so much better, and I think it could be a really interesting course to take on Yale's campus instead of in France. Yale is a place we know really well, and I think a lot of interesting pieces could come out of the class when students focus on writing about the campus instead of a place they've never been. That's the only suggestion I would have!

I found myself wanting to return to some of my essays, but it was already time to write my next. I really found reading and improving the sentences we wrote in our essays useful. Not only could we learn from each other's writing styles, but it gave us practice revising.
