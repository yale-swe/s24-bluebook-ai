I think that was really encouraging of growing as a writer and refining your work. She is does prefer more open intros instead of the nailed down specific thesis so that can be different. She was always very kind on deadlines and generally nice to talk to in office hours. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: our table was very long and we had masks on and margaret homans voice is very gentle. she also i think talked longer than necessary and had trouble understanding the ideas we communicated. i think while we had active discussions, their values were fairly low to me. i think talking more in small groups which we did sometimes would be very fruitful and i think also having books feel like they're being chosen for the nuanced work their doing and not mainly as a survey of different ethnicities would be nice. i think that homans is thinking about taking off moby dick and i think that's a good idea since while there can be powerful messsages in moby dick, i think it's just not the best structure for that.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1581109\n 1581109\n \n 1581109\n The course had a good list of readings, though I agree with others in the course that the treatment we gave white vs non-white authors was not equal. I enjoyed discussion, but, because many did not complete the readings for class (especially when it came to Moby Dick), sometimes these were lacking. I also think Professor Homans did, at times, not take up or fully allow us to work our way to the end of a discussion thread brought up by a student. And that this sometimes resulted in missed opportunities for a more fruitful discussion. I think allowing students to answer to each other for the discussion to be more round-table than a back-and-forth between professor and student, professor and student would help with this.\r\nAll this said, I think Professor Homans brought up some incredibly important themes for us to consider, and I felt, as we approached the different readings and topics, the amount of thought she'd devoted to her syllabus and discussion plans. \r\nA major, major strength of the class was that Professor Homans showed a willingness to work with us on each of our essays like none I've ever seen in another professor. One reason for this was the draft/final system for papers (which I had not had in a class before, but which I now firmly believe is the absolute best way to structure a writing class. There's no doubt i got so much more out of every essay assignment this way than i would have if we'd only been able to submit one draft per assignment). But Professor Homans offered incredibly detailed, clear, and HELPFUL critiques on each of my essay. I could tell she had truly taken the time to step inside and think about my essays from the inside. I know from speaking to others in the class that they were also thoroughly touched and impressed by this attention of hers.

The array of texts we read introduced many different conceptions not only of America, but of the role of writing and how we think about writing as an act of protection, survivance, healing, and more. Office hours with Professor Homans were incredibly helpful in developing my writing, the way I organize my work, and my essays. \r\nWeaknesses: At times class discussions felt slow, but this was likely the result not of the readings and more so to do with the roller coaster of spring semester being on zoom then in person.
