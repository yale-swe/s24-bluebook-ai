\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528461\n 1528461\n \n 1528461\n Fun and interesting experiments (at least, the ones we chose; there's a few that are notorious for being on the more difficult side). There are a few experiments that require a LOT of data analysis which really isn't too fun to do. The first few weeks are a bit rough, but you kinda get into the hang of writing a 9-15 page lab report (with pictures) each week, honestly. It sounds bad, but I never really paid attention to page count, they just end up being that long after a night of writing. Overall I think it's a better time than 205L (from how hard I heard it is from other students who took it this semester) because you already have all the data analysis / error propagation skills.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531996\n 1531996\n \n 1531996\n I like that students get to choose the experiments theyre interested in from a big set of possible experiments. This allowed me to choose labs I genuinely found interesting, and made the course a lot of fun. I also think the lab manuals are generally helpful and a good guide to students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536108\n 1536108\n \n 1536108\n Experiments are very interesting, lab reports get a bit tedious.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537572\n 1537572\n \n 1537572\n This was a very strong course. The biggest strength were the premade lab experiments. There were enough of these to have ample choice between them, and the lab sheets were very well-researched and informative. The principal weakness of this course was the lack of introduction to the equipment. This was due in part to the circumstances surrounding covid, as I had taken the preceding course, 205L, remotely.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543630\n 1543630\n \n 1543630\n The strengths of the course mostly lie in the lab investigations, rather than writing the reports. However, there are several labs with poor equipment that is very fallible, which can be frustrating. Writing the lab reports is also quite difficult, as there are a lot of skills that not everybody has that are not taught. Also, there are no office hours that I knew about.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546163\n 1546163\n \n 1546163\n Strengths is that in order to do well you have to know what you are doing but that can also be a downside.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546344\n 1546344\n \n 1546344\n Very good labs. Some were a bit too long to complete during the class period though
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550196\n 1550196\n \n 1550196\n Most of the labs are set up very well. A few of them have outdated experiment guides that need updating. I enjoyed the rigor of the weekly experiments and reports, HOWEVER, given the amount of time I devoted to this course, it should have counted for a full credit instead of a half credit. In-lab time was 3 hrs per week most weeks (the same as my full credit classes) and the lab reports took anywhere from 5 to 15 hours, depending on the level of data analysis required. Something I loved about this course was our ability to choose our own experiments; it really allowed me to do experiments that corresponded to my interests!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550477\n 1550477\n \n 1550477\n This course was really well-designed. The lab guides are great; the feeling that the homework is all relevant and meaningful and not contrived whatsoever (as was the case on 205L) is great; my TA, Kirsty Scott, was awesome; and the resources for the experiments seemed top-notch to me. The length of the period to carry out experiments and the pace and the flexibility to choose which ones seem interesting -- all great. The only weakness I can think of (and it's really a weakness in my work ethic) is that there's nothing to guarantee that the students have to engage with the lab guide before the lab. So maybe a tiny pre-lab assignment, just some basic questions about the lab guide, a little quiz like that or something, would encourage students to read through the guide before class, and that would help them get more out of the experiment, and make the experiment process more efficient.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550497\n 1550497\n \n 1550497\n It was great to be able to choose certain experiments from a large selection, so you could focus on what you thought was most interesting. The structure and organization was also great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554550\n 1554550\n \n 1554550\n A wide variety of available labs makes the class enjoyable for anyone. \r\n\r\nThe setup for the some labs can be quite difficult, and I wish there was a bit more support. The workload outside of the class is massive, as most of the labs require their data to be analyzed outside of lab. A little support on the data analysis or a common lab at the beginning of the semester to teach programming and data analysis would help greatly.

Especially if you get a chill TA and good lab partner. There's really no lectures or material to be learnt besides the background info and theory for the experiments if you took 205L already.
