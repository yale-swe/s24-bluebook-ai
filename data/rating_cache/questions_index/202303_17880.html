\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702259\n 1702259\n \n 1702259\n We were exposed to a large variety of philosophies of mathematics; we began with Kant, then Frege, intuitionists (e.g. Brouwer), structuralists, etc. Given this survey-style approach, we were able to understand and compare a number of vastly different philosophies, evaluate them, discuss them, and then decide which one (if any) we found most persuasive. The course content is especially helpful for Math + Philosophy majors, since you get a really broad understanding of the major schools of the philosophy of mathematics since Kant that can serve as an ample starting point for further research.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702259\n 1702259\n \n 1702259\n Strengths: Sun-Joo!!!! Professor Shin is so fun and engaging. I've taken a few classes with her, and every time she makes the class so enjoyable. Cannot recommend Professor Shin highly enough. Professor Shin read each of our assignments very quickly and gave thorough notes/comments for each. I also enjoyed the structure of the class (presentation each week from a group + Sun-Joo's support, weekly reading reports, abstract presentations, and final paper). The structure facilitated discussion participation from everyone in the class, whose students came from a wide mix of math/philosophy/CS/physics and tangential majors. \r\nWeaknesses: none--wish I could continue it into next semester!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1704389\n 1704389\n \n 1704389\n I think the readings are quite good and the weekly reports really motivated me to engage substantively with the material. I think we should have done some more ground work before jumping into the different theories, though. For instance, we spent a huge amount of our time discussing whether mathematical objects exist, but from what I remember we never really talked about what it would mean for a mathematical object to exist. Things like that made me feel like I couldn't follow the conversation at times.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1712541\n 1712541\n \n 1712541\n The material of the course is interesting and the readings were very carefully selected to be short but substantial. I think that this facilitated active discussion in class, but I think I would have learned more if I heard more from Professor Shin, who was an excellent and passionate teacher.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722122\n 1722122\n \n 1722122\n Professor Shin runs the class exceptionally well, while managing to be rather hands off. When needed, Professor Shin will interject to ensure the views are treated fairly.

Weaknesses: Because of the flipped classroom, sometimes seminar would drag along slowly if it felt more lecture-like than discussion-like, but Prof Shin did a good job of trying to ensure that each class had a balance of both.
