\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697017\n 1697017\n \n 1697017\n This course allowed me to gain a perspective nuanced in social, political, and cultural factors regarding the performance of music and the persistence of musical trends in the African-American tradition. A specific instance of this was a very insightful conversation about the connection between the technological advancements of the Space Race and the global militarization in relation to the theme of transcendent modes of being and the use of psychedelics by musicians during that time period. Our frequent and often intellectually dense conversations made this course very stimulating. The intense integration of observations about the music of both John Coltrane and Jimi Hendrix with observations about elements of their music also made this course very palatable.

RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1696419\n 1696419\n \n 1696419\n Strengths included the interesting topics of discussion and the use of readings as background knowledge to develop more complex ideas during class time.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697017\n 1697017\n \n 1697017\n A major strength to this course is that it prepares to you think critically about social context of music. Furthermore, the workload is (for the most part) very manageable as the bulk of the semester revolves on writing just 5 reasonably sized papers. A weakness though is that the timing of these papers can be a little rough, with sometimes just a week to write, so it may be hard to devote time just to that. Another weakness, in my opinion, is that sometimes the required readings for the seminar are a bit lengthy and sometimes vague; they contain a lot of biographical information and it can be confusing to discern exactly what information is going to be useful for discussions in class if a specific reading is discussed. Another strength is that the professor, Michael Veal, is very easygoing, understanding, and easy to communicate, at least in the classroom setting. He's very approachable and is not difficult to a request an extension from. Lastly, another weakness is that it can be confusing to determine what exactly professor Veal is looking for in papers, but he is usually quite open to answering questions about writing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1698840\n 1698840\n \n 1698840\n It had engaging reading and subject material. It could be improved by more equal participation amongst students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1704887\n 1704887\n \n 1704887\n Veal is one of the most well-spoken, talented professors I've ever had. He's knowledgeable and humorous, and his lectures/seminars were always engaging. There was a lot of writing, but it is a writing credit so it made sense. Sometimes feedback on the papers was a little vague, and I couldn't quite figure out what was expected of me in that context.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1705836\n 1705836\n \n 1705836\n I think that the professor was very engaging but the course could have had a bit more of an outward look at the social and political factors impacting the 1960s and how these musicians were a part of that. The grading was overly harsh. The professor held an obvious bias within in the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1705927\n 1705927\n \n 1705927\n Strengths: First and foremost: Veal. Absolutely amazing prof. Every time he opens his mouth I get the sense that I'm talking to a world-class expert on the subject. He will guide you through breaking down some seriously complex ideas in a way that feels so natural, and he never takes himself too seriously. Definitely kept the class lively. What's really neat about this class is the different levels of depth you can get based on your proficiency in music. Some students with a high theory background contributed heavily to technical components of discussion, while those who didn't focused on historical and contextual lenses. No lens is better than the other, but it just added some organic variance in opinion and what we discuss. Weakness: honestly the only weakness I can give this class is that we had not 1, but 2 essays for the final. Not great, but fine if you plan around it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1711285\n 1711285\n \n 1711285\n Strengths: Professor Veal is the biggest strength; he's very knowledgeable about music and an overall very chill guy to talk to. Weaknesses: some of the readings were very lengthy, but you dont really have to read all the excess and can just focus on the important stuff for class discussions and essays. Also, Professor veal was a bit disorganized and had to assign the last two essays to be due on the same day (though we did have like 3-4 weeks to finish both, so it wasn't too bad. However, it was during the final weeks of the semester so we had to study for other classes too.)
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721565\n 1721565\n \n 1721565\n Strengths: Professor Veal is very clearly passionate and an expert about what he teaches. He creates a comfortable and engaging environment where we all would laugh before class and in between when he would put on YouTube videos. Discussion was open to all and he facilitated it so everyone can participate, and the content of the course organically made it so discussion was abstract and unique to each of us which I really enjoyed. Most people in the class ranked the seminar as their first choice so naturally everyone was passionate and had a genuine sense of curiosity in the course itself which would lead to us going a bit off the proposed outline of the day but nonetheless still remain on topic. The listenings in class, although sort of a given for a music course, were a great aspect as it allowed for a more sonic experience and also new song recommendations to add to your playlist. Weaknesses: Honestly none, Professor does cold call during class but honestly you'll be fine, he does so in an engaging manner rather than intimidating. Readings are quite lengthy at times but they're actually really interesting so it's worth the time and especially useful for the unit papers.
