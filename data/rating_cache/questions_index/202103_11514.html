\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538229\n 1538229\n \n 1538229\n This class NEEDS content warnings. I have been personally impacted by suicide--my grandfather died by suicide with a firearm in my sophomore year. One week, every reading mentioned suicide. One was an incredibly graphic description, and another included an ILLUSTRATION of a man's body in a morgue with a bag over his head. I have PTSD from this event and it sent me into a tailspin that impacted my other classes and my overall quality of life at Yale. I want to note that I still would have been able to engage with EVERY reading that week if I had been told what to expect. I also did not feel heard when I told the professor about this, and she continued to use language like "committed suicide" in class, which is upsetting when it is so easy to google how to carefully speak about suicide. She also said that "a lot of us" had dealt with suicide, which is true in a capacity, but it is not true that everyone developed PTSD after their grandfather's violent death by suicide. I still enjoyed this class, but this occurring early in the semester made it truly difficult for me to engage with the rest of the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550622\n 1550622\n \n 1550622\n strengths: ability to try out and read different forms of creative writing\r\nweaknesses: workshops felt a bit robotic, but I'm not sure how that can be fixed. also, the second assignment was to turn our first personal essay into a fictional story, and even though we read fiction in class I didn't feel adequately prepared to do that. More of the latter would have been helpful.
