\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622080\n 1622080\n \n 1622080\n Prof. Lindskog did an amazing job guiding us through the seminar. I left class feeling like I understood the text at a depth I certainly would not have been able to reach on my own. The essay feedback we received was incredible as well. I definitely feel like I have become a more intentional writer. To improve, I wish we were given a little more autonomy during the seminars; there were times where it felt like the seminars were being dominated too much by the professor.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622106\n 1622106\n \n 1622106\n Weakness: I realize that this obviously a point of the program, but there are SO MANY books. Sometimes I felt like I had not gotten enough out of a book because I had so little time to read it and we spent so little time talking about it in seminar. But if perhaps we just had a few less books to read (three or four maybe) it might enable just enough more time to be devoted to each. \r\n\r\nStrengths: I just love being able to discuss a text with a small group of students and a teacher. And Directed Studies is exactly that. I think that the dedication from the professor was excellent and the lectures were engaging and informative.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622123\n 1622123\n \n 1622123\n This course was amazing in its breadth of readings and the quality of discussions. It has been a joy to be a part of, and I can't think of something to improve it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622142\n 1622142\n \n 1622142\n I am sure the strengths and weaknesses of the program have become trite at this point; the invaluable strength of DS Lit is to show broad patterns in the discipline of literature and develop the way students approach the text I believe this course epitomized this strength. For weaknesses, the course naturally does not allow for very deep analysis of any particular text, which also is already known by the instructors.\r\n\r\nFor our particular class, I found that a real strength was the ability to draw connections between texts and cultures say, how the Homeric poems reflected one cultural era as opposed to Dante's "Inferno". This ability to connect was particularly gratifying; I feel that I am starting to understand the broader literary discipline. Additionally, the course was conducted in a sense that brought out the best possible remarks in discussion; I felt that I was always supposed to articulate a deeper point and was given the platform to, which was certainly a strength. At the same time, I do feel that the weight attributed to each point in discussion did not always allow for actual "discussion" in the Socratic sense, but rather very articulate students taking their turn at the lectern. There are strengths and weaknesses to this, and, while we each individually were able to find deeper meaning of the text in discussion, I did not always feel that we were able to do so as interlocutors, challenging and poking at each other's remarks. I think the perfect discussion has both of these virtues deep individual thought within a group conversation. Perhaps the mechanism of calling on individual students felt too formal for Socratic banter. Either way, the discussions were very good, and I hope my critique only serves to improve them.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622199\n 1622199\n \n 1622199\n The strength was the amount and breadth of books we read and became familiar with, but that also leads to the major drawback that we dont spend enough time with each text.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622692\n 1622692\n \n 1622692\n More time for reading the texts/less texts and more time to analyze.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1623016\n 1623016\n \n 1623016\n I think we spent too little time on certain texts, but that might just be the nature of Directed Studies.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624570\n 1624570\n \n 1624570\n DS is a fast-paced course, and this not only makes it difficult but also can leave you feeling like youre moving onto another material before youre able to engage with a text or author in depth. Since all this process of reading and discussing texts is accompanied by paper assignments, it might also feel overwhelming. On the flip side, this fast-paced structure and the demanding reading load compels one to become a faster reader and writer, as well as making it possible to see patterns and draw connections between a broad body of work since one goes over so many texts and different authors.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1639680\n 1639680\n \n 1639680\n The main strengths I noticed in this class are the thoroughness of the feedback on papers as well as the context or additional materials provided for each text. I think something very useful that happens in other DS sections is that the professor will point out specific parts of the assigned reading that are particularly important ahead of the discussion which help guide our reading. Of course, this would make it easier for students not to read all of the material, but in DS it is also important to be realistic, and part of that is making sure that everyone knows what is being talked about at least on the more significant aspects of the material at hand.
