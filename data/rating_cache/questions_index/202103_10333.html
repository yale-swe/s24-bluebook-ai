\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535992\n 1535992\n \n 1535992\n The strength of this course lies in the structured progression of assignments, both in terms of the actual assignments and the development of these assignments. That being said, all assignments were graded with about the same general scale, so poor performance in the first assignment could put you in a hole that you'd have to dig yourself out of. Aside from that, we had several workshops and discussions within small groups in the class for 0-15 minutes on various different parts of assignments such as ideation, argumentation, and structure to help us out with approaching completion. \r\n\r\nWhile the content of the class was interesting on a surface level, I found the reading assignments to be a little disconnected from what preceded occasionally. The content was, however, organized exceptionally well from a classroom discussion standpoint.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537821\n 1537821\n \n 1537821\n Strengths - the readings were very engaging and well-chosen and the themes of the class were personally very engaging to me. Maybe this was by nature of the course being an English seminar and not an Art/Fashion/Music History class, but I felt that a lot of our arguments were grounded in our opinons/the opinions of academics fairly removed from the fashion industry itself; gaining more material literacy and learning more about fashion history really would have helped improve the level of rigor in our arguments.
