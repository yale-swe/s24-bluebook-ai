\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536262\n 1536262\n \n 1536262\n The environment of class felt a little tense, and it felt like your thoughts had to be fully developed and incredibly intellectual before speaking up. Thus, at times it was definitely difficult to feel comfortable speaking. However, the professors really did try to emphasize that was not the case, and they made sure to try and lighten the mood every once and a while.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536484\n 1536484\n \n 1536484\n The readings and discussions were really great. Professor Oskian and Vatsal were great at moderating the discussion; they did not talk a lot, and they really gave the students space to share ideas and respond to each other's thoughts. They were also very kind and much more invested in their students than other professors that I've had at Yale. They provided detailed feedback on all of our assignments, which really helped me improve my writing and challenged me to sharpen my thinking.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546332\n 1546332\n \n 1546332\n The course was strong in that the readings and topic areas provoked a lot of discussion among students, and everyone was always comfortable participating. I feel like the other students in my class also made this a very comfortable learning environment because people were courteous and willing to build off one another's idea. Sometimes, in political science seminars, there tends to be some students who just talk to hear themselves speak even if they don't have anything meaningful to contribute with their perspective and positionality -- however, this wasn't the vibe of this class at all, and it was great! \r\n\r\nI would say one weakness of this class is that there is a very high volume of readings and not all of them feel necessary, but I guess sometimes that can't be helped. To combat this issue, something more helpful might just be for the professors to introduce the next week's readings the week before and help crystallize the connections between them. If I'm being honest, I'm still not sure how Ambedkar's reading on Buddhism related to Tocqueville, Du Bois, or the other readings that week, and it would have been great to get a heads up about why the professors chose that reading in advance.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1548630\n 1548630\n \n 1548630\n Strengths: The readings and the professors. The readings delve deeply into race, slavery, the genocide of Native Americans, capitalism, white supremacy and democracy. The professors were very helpful in guiding the class discussions towards examining the connections between all these themes and allowing us to form our own profound opinions. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: there are multiple long and dense readings each week. This could make it difficult to finish all of them sometimes. Sometimes the shorter readings (outside of Tocqueville and Dubois) were also hard to find because some were posted on slack and others on canvas
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550441\n 1550441\n \n 1550441\n This class was fantastic. Discussions were rich and engaging, and there is not much I would change. This problem was probably a function of the specific personalities in the class rather than the actual topics or readings discussed though.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553612\n 1553612\n \n 1553612\n Discussion could be really hard some time and it was hard to participate when it felt more abstract or secluded to a couple students
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553740\n 1553740\n \n 1553740\n Almost everything about this course was a strength: brilliant and caring instructors, amazing texts rarely read together, probing and creative discussion questions.
