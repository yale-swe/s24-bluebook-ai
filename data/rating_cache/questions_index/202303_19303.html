\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1699637\n 1699637\n \n 1699637\n Yes!! Just be prepared to need to be really committed.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1701270\n 1701270\n \n 1701270\n Definitely! As someone who isn't Jewish and had no prior knowledge of the language, I didn't know what to expect coming in but it was the highlight of my semester. You will be shocked with how much of the language you are able to learn in just a couple of months. The small class size makes participation very easy and Prof Dina fosters such a fun, inclusive community for people of any background. But be warned, Modern Hebrew is not for the faint of heart! The daily homework and weekly quizes were definitely overwhelming at times but you just need to trust the process and do what you can to keep up. The work is hard but it is incredibly rewarding! And have I mentioned how great Dina is! TAKE HEBREW!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1703701\n 1703701\n \n 1703701\n Absolutely. In a short time, I gained elementary proficiency and can't wait to learn more next semester!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1705408\n 1705408\n \n 1705408\n Yes! It's a well-organized class and a great introduction to the language. The workload was pretty manageable, and all of the quizzes were online. If you need a language credit or are at all interested in Hebrew, I recommend it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1712221\n 1712221\n \n 1712221\n Yes, this is the perfect course for anyone who wants to learn Hebrew.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1712400\n 1712400\n \n 1712400\n Absolutely, yes. I have taken several languages at Yale, and I have never been so invested in learning a language at Yale until I started taking Hebrew. In most other language courses, a lot of people are taking the class just to get their requirement out of the way, which is totally fine until it's the entire class doing that. Then, it just leads to you feeling like you're just wasting your time every day because no one participates and you spend most of the 50 minutes being cold-called. The way I see it, if I have to be in a class 4-5 times a week, I want to at least take something away from the course. Hebrew offered exactly that. Every person was taking the class for a different reason, and we all came from extremely different backgrounds. I totally anticipated it just being Jewish students, but there was a decent portion of us that weren't Jewish (and also notably a decent number of grad students and international students). I really got to know a lot of people in the class, including Dina, which was truly a treat. All in all, this Hebrew class will go down as one of my favorite classes I have taken at Yale.
