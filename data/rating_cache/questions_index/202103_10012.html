\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532077\n 1532077\n \n 1532077\n The concepts that this course touched on were all pretty interesting, and by the end of the semester, I felt like I had learned quite a lot about consent. Professor Fischel is also very passionate about the class and about the subject matter, so that's a big plus. \r\n\r\nMy biggest critique would be that the reading load is fairly heavy, especially for a class that teaches theory (which tends to be a bit dense). We sometimes didn't get through all of the readings during our class discussions. A slightly lighter reading load would probably allow for deeper engagement with the texts. \r\n\r\nAll in all, however, this is a good class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534697\n 1534697\n \n 1534697\n The course is perfectly organized. Its best strength is the refreshing variety of sources not only in terms of representation/the diversity represented in the authors, but in the type of media. Ive never had a class that combined theory, articles, legal cases, podcasts, TV episodes, and more. It made it so the class accommodated something for every learning style and also showcased how the concepts we were learning about manifest in actuality rather than restricting our discussions to the theoretical. Additionally, the alternating weeks with groups made it so that it was okay to get by successfully if you had an off-week and put the ball more in the students court in terms of time management, which felt amazing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543758\n 1543758\n \n 1543758\n Strength: Very engaging material, Prof. Fischel is very knowledgable and well connected to the authors we read which adds a great layer to class discussion, Prof. Fischel was also very open to class feedback which was great\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: A couple readings were a bit dense, midterm and final essay project have questions that are a bit complex and long (leave less room for student creativity) but with support from Prof Fischel and our TF they were answerable
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543759\n 1543759\n \n 1543759\n This course is incredibly strong. The readings are all fascinating, and often, not super long too... Some of the legal theory gets a little heavy, but it is well worth the effort.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543777\n 1543777\n \n 1543777\n The workload was backloaded in a way that made the second half far more intense than the second, and the final assignments were overwhelming. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544307\n 1544307\n \n 1544307\n STRENGTHS: I looked forward to each week's readings because the syllabus was super well-crafted. It pulled from feminist theory, creative nonfiction, podcasts, news articles, and case law. Professor Fischel and Isabel's feedback on assignments was thorough, which helped me develop careful arguments about dense texts. The biweekly discussion posts were never burdensome. They were a helpful opportunity to clarifying my thoughts on the week's material and consider questions that my peers were raising. \r\n\r\nWEAKNESSES: We could have received more guidance about expectations for the response papers. Some guiding questions or even some examples would have been helpful. Professor Fischel tried to give each student a chance to speak, but I noticed that some students were called on far more than others. Sometimes students were clearly not listening to their peers, either. Perhaps future iterations of the course could establish classroom "norms" about genuinely listening to classmates, which would make for a more supportive learning environment.

The arc of the class is also great and I remember being blown away at the degree of complexity we were engaging in by the end having conversations we were TOTALLY unprepared for, collectively, at the beginning. Insane. I also read the discussion posts every week and was always touched by the thoughtfulness and creativity of my fellow students.
