\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1649999\n 1649999\n \n 1649999\n The course was fairly unorganized and had quite a bit of stress attached to it. It was difficult to know when things were due and instructions about what exactly we needed to do were unclear. The classes in this course seemed to mostly be filled with busy work. In L1, classes were usually spent practicing conversation and going over material and new concepts, but this class spent most time going over drills that we had already completed for homework, instead of talking about actual content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1650058\n 1650058\n \n 1650058\n The structure of the class was a little confusing at times but overall it was great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1650071\n 1650071\n \n 1650071\n In terms of weaknesses, the course is very disorganized. Lectures are very disorganized and hard to follow; they also aren't directly synchronized with the book, so we just start learning random grammar topics and are expected to internalize it without feedback in real time. Asynchronous assignments were graded too harshly and didn't help me use the relevant vocabulary until late in the semester. We are tested on the Shami dialect but we never use it in class or in assignments. Most of the time when I ask questions about concepts I was more confused or dissatisfied with the answers. Communication about which assignments were due and when was lackluster, and we never talked about them in class or received real feedback on homework, so basically all of our learning was decided based upon confusing lectures and reading the book on our own (or going to office hours if we have time outside of the lengthy homework and studying for exams/quizzes).\r\n\r\nIn terms of strengths, grading was rather lenient and we have opportunities for extra credit. Moreover, the instructor and TA are both really kind and willing to help if you ask for it outside of class. Though explanations/lectures didn't always help.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1650441\n 1650441\n \n 1650441\n This course is strong on grammar and vocabulary, and if you study you gain a lot. I think this course's weakness is spontaneous conversation because it is not something we did often.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1650463\n 1650463\n \n 1650463\n I think this course's main area for improvement is in its organization. While most students (including myself) adored Dr. Aziz as a lighthearted and passionate person, his class structure allowed for miscommunication and disengagement. I consider myself to be a dedicated student of Arabic, but even I found myself skipping on the textbook's readings in order to complete the high number of drills assigned. I think the class would be greatly improved by a slight alteration to its structure. If Dr. Aziz assigned daily textbook readings along with ~1 relevant drill per day (without students needing to complete all drills in the units), the students would be more likely to stay on track and value their learning over a completion checkmark on the companion website. I also believe that spending more of the class time on reviewing errors students frequently make on tests and less time on translating from English to Arabic would cultivate a more intellectually challenging but rewarding class environment. \r\n\r\nStill, Dr. Aziz is a kind and approachable professor who is always understanding of his students' external circumstances (and sometimes probably a bit too forgiving).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1650903\n 1650903\n \n 1650903\n Strengths: The class incorporates lots of different types of activities, the al-Kitaab book is well structured and if you follow along with it closely you'll learn a lot, Professor Aziz is super understanding and flexible, he helps make the classroom environment fun.\r\nWeaknesses: Professor Aziz is not always the best at explaining grammatical concepts. We theoretically covered a lot of different ones (verbal sentences, the subjunctive vs. the nominative, the future and past tense, etc...) but a lot of the students never grasped them because of unclear explanations.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1656276\n 1656276\n \n 1656276\n Weaknesses: This course lacked the necessary regular engagement needed for students so early in their Arabic journey. The only students who I saw succeed were ones who were very independent in their Arabic learning and taught themselves from the book on their own.\r\n\r\nStrengths: Doctor Aziz is extremely kind. I have the feeling that he wanted us to succeed and did the best he could. This created a fun and happy environment in each class which included fun elements like Youtube videos, Kahoot, and Interactive communication. However, I often felt like I was smiling through the pain of my language learnings atrophy.
