\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575456\n 1575456\n \n 1575456\n We covered so much in this course! The difference between allophones and phonemes, how to identify alternations with zero, positional neutralization, and free variation, articulatory phonetics and phonotactic constraints. We looked at autosegmental theory, including covering how to describe natural classes of sounds using features, and describe variation with feature-based rule notation. And then we looked at constraint-based phonology and optimality theory, alongside suprasegmental features like stress and tone. And then we finished up with syllable structure phonology. And! Throughout all this, we were working on a research project, in which we had to analyze and describe the phonological system of a language we did not speak, by working with a consultant who spoke that language.

The class is intellectually challenging, especially for someone without a linguistics background, and when a month or more goes by without you getting any grades or comments back on your homework and final project updates, it is impossible to know how you are doing and if you are only further entrenching existing issues with every new assignment you submit. The Psets were quite fun, and despite being challenging, I really enjoyed trying to analyze puzzly data. Weaknesses: the quizzes were HARD, and since we had to take the quizzes before we'd discussed the chapter material in class, I often came to class very confused. This confused me a lot, because I thought that that's what the purpose of the project had been! And since I received feedback so late, I had only three days to fix the entire thing before turning it in. Additionally, Professor Weber's grading system is really wonky.
