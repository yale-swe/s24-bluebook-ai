\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710635\n 1710635\n \n 1710635\n I did learn a lot and improve my Spanish writing the very long and challenging term paper. Unfortunately, that was the most I got out of the class. \r\n\r\nThis course was taught mostly as a lecture, and the lectures mostly covered historical context rather than analyzing the stories. There was very little discussion or analysis. I did not get to practice and improve my spoken Spanish or close reading and analytical skills. Moreover Borges is one of the most interesting writers of the last few centuries, and his works reward extensive conversation and exploration of precedent texts, but we spent most of the time discussing the politics of the different decades when Borges wrote each story. The class covered many of the 'greatest hits' of his obra, but it did not dive into any of the interesting obscure work of his, including his essays, film reviews, lectures, and more than two of his poems. Even if we had read the same stories we did and had spent all of our time analyzing these stories as a class (or even listening to a thorough analytical lecture), that would have been great, but unfortunately I learned very little about Borges, literature, or Spanish. \r\n\r\nThis class could have opened the minds of all of the students, changed how they understood the world. And the students read every story and came to class prepared to engage, with questions, ideas to discuss. Usually students just coast through such easy classes, and I have never before felt the class actually wanting more but being unable to engage because of the lecture format (in a seminar class).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714122\n 1714122\n \n 1714122\n The strengths of this course were the material and Professor Gonzlez-Prez engaging teaching style. While I do not think that this course had any obvious weaknesses, my only critique would be that I wish we had a few more opportunities to engage in critical discussion amongst ourselves rather than merely having call and response questions during the lectures.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721279\n 1721279\n \n 1721279\n The strengths of this class are the readings. The stories are interesting and it feels like an accomplishment to know so much about one author. The professor is knowledgeable about the subject. The weaknesses are that the lectures don't have often have slides or a notes equivalent. This makes it really difficult to pay attention as I found myself stopping to look up a word and then losing track of the lecture. I wish there were more discussion in the class. It is a small class, but really more like a lecture. Because it is entirely taught in Spanish and I have weaker listening comprehension in Spanish, I found it really difficult to absorb all the information I needed for this class. More detailed notes or slides and discussion should help. The TA did make very detailed and helpful study guides that definitely saved my grade in this course. It seemed like the students who had studied Comparative Literature or knew some about Borges and Philosophy already were ahead. Some of that was never explained and I had never studied a lot of the works Borges was influenced by so I felt at a disadvantage. The quizzes in this class are memorization based and don't check your understanding of the story on an interpretive level. There is a midterm and final that allows for deeper analysis, but memorizing niche characters, places, and even quotes for a quiz seemed pointless as they didn't change my understanding of the work at all.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724863\n 1724863\n \n 1724863\n This course is nearly all strengths, I thoroughly enjoyed it! Completely understanding this writing requires careful analysis, so I'm thankful that the class allowed for this. The professor is also fantastic: kind, knowledgeable, and an engaging lecturer. I always enjoyed interacting with him, whether inside or outside of classes. The in-class quizzes and midterm were manageable. The final paper was a significant time investment but well worth it to truly analyze a Borges story in-depth; however, I wish that the expectations for the writing were clearer, as this was the only paper assignment of the semester.

This class is a lot of work, but if you put time in to read the stories fully and make sure you understand it entirely you will do well. The stories are interesting, but very hard to read in English and Spanish. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724863\n 1724863\n \n 1724863\n I would definitely recommend this course to those at all interested in literature, particularly Latin American literature! Borges's work is absolutely brilliant, and it's an unparalleled opportunity to be able to dedicate an entire semester to reading it. in addition, conducting the analysis and background learning done in this class truly helps to understand his writing in new, profound ways. The class is engaging and the workload is light, with the exception of the final paper. However, I would recommend this class to students who are confident in their Spanish skills or are ready to be challenged with this writing. Borges's writing can be difficult to understand even in English, and I often had to turn to a dictionary and/or translation while reading; thanks to this class, I've greatly improved my reading abilities in Spanish and expanded my vocabulary to include some of Borges's favorite obscure words!
