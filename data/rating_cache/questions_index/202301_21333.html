RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1654319\n 1654319\n \n 1654319\n One huge strengths of the course is Professor Aslanidis. He comes to class and prompts thoughtful discussion, stimulating conversation effortlessly about topics that can be quite difficult to discuss. The material we engage with coming to class prepares us for an enriching discussion, but the supplemental materials and videos Professor brings to class elevate the class's understanding of the subject matter in an important way. The assignments do not feel like busy work and allow students freedom to explore the fields they so please.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657298\n 1657298\n \n 1657298\n Paris is a great professor and there was a lot of healthy and good discussion in class that really enriched my experience.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1660509\n 1660509\n \n 1660509\n Professor Aslanidis is definitely one of the biggest strengths of the class. He does a phenomenal job of leading seminar by balancing student participation with his own prepared slides/lectures. He also makes sure to incorporate the work that we do outside of class in the classroom in a way that makes the work feel more meaningful. For example, he would share excerpts from everyone's reading responses and have the two people sitting on either side of that person play devil's advocate in response to what that person had written. His encouragement of debate in the classroom leads to a ton of learning and growth. One potential weakness is that the class focuses predominantly on the US/Europe (partially reflective of the existing literature). It would've been nice to cover other parts of the world in more depth, though.
