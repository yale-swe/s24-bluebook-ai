\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616429\n 1616429\n \n 1616429\n Strengths: Everything! This is the best class I have ever taken at Yale by far. Professor De is an inspiring lecturer and he always asks for input from the class. The assignments allow you to be creative, and the readings do a great job of capturing varied viewpoints.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses:\r\nThe final is quite a time crunch!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618608\n 1618608\n \n 1618608\n The course is fantastic! It really is not super difficult honestly, and the teaching staff does a good job of 1. telling you which readings to prioritize and 2. summarizing the important points of the readings in section. I will say that this course is probably way more difficult for someone who is not of a South Asian background, as there is a lot of contextual knowledge that Rohit/the TFs kind of gloss over. It's definitely still doable -- the non-South Asian students in the class were often the most prepared and most engaged students in lecture and section, and I'm sure they got some of the highest grades in the class -- but they definitely needed to put in more work than the rest of us. That being said, if you are of South Asian descent, and have even a really small amount of background knowledge on the region from your parents, it's super interesting to take this class and fill in a lot of the gaps in knowledge that you may have. Additionally, Rohit approaches the history that he teaches from a holistic standpoint, weaving together a lot of themes, so it's cool to trace those throughout history. I think the workload of the course is perfect! You definitely need to come to class and section, and do at least the main reading for each lecture. Aside from that and the occasional, short writing assignments, the course workload is very manageable. The two exams are very approachable, and I think that Rohit/the TFs are very understanding people!!

It is by no means impossible to do well in the class if you're not South Asian, but it is easier to do well in the class while doing a lot less prep for the lectures/sections/exams if you have a bit of that cultural context. I would say that the pro of the course kind of leaning toward having a bit of that cultural context is that it makes it even more interesting and engaging for South Asian students. There is an extremely limited number of South Asian Studies courses at Yale, which is such a shame, so it's wonderful to have the opportunity to finally walk into a lecture and really understand the full context behind the content of the course.
