In other words, perhaps more time on concepts rather than content. Perhaps more use could be made of videos presented by the investigators that discovered the basic concept. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513339\n 1513339\n \n 1513339\n Strengths: Informative, collaborative, offers many real-world applications that make the information relatable in multiple contexts, the opportunity to work through problems with other students makes the class a lot more manageable \r\nWeaknesses: Despite what the description says, this course definitely has prerequisites. Anybody who has not taken a basic chemistry, biology, or physics course prior to this class will struggle, because the foundational concepts in these areas that you would expect to touch on in an Intro. course so you can then understand the actual material are not explained. I would not characterize this as a course for somebody who has had 0 exposure to any of those three categories. I think it would have been almost impossible to take a second course alongside this one because of the volume of work for this course alone, so I think it might be valuable to restructure some of the lessons so we could apply some of our other outside class time to alternative things, such as a second class or outside work.
