\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1569039\n 1569039\n \n 1569039\n At times, the direct relevance of the course readings to our in-class discussions was somewhat unclear. I was sometimes left with the feeling that I pretty much had to interpret them myself without a lot of input from the professor or other students. However, this course did help me with interpreting texts in translation, and working with philosophical texts in general. I think having more discussion of the readings, like in an online canvas forum between students, would improve the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1578324\n 1578324\n \n 1578324\n This course was at its strongest in the early units about historical Islamic philosophy, particularly the free and fascinating discussions we had about Neoplatonism and its consequences for Islam, and the works of Al-Kindi as a synthesis of Islamic and Classical Greek Thought. I would have liked to spend more time on Al-Ghazali, who felt like the centerpiece of the course, and really dealt with him as the center of controversy and debate in Islamic thought (incl. reading more of the philosophers he refutes and who attempt to refute him) because of how fascinating he was to everyone in the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1588608\n 1588608\n \n 1588608\n The class covered a broad amount of Islamic thought & law. Over all, I cant think of any weaknesses. I guess we could have spent less time on Ancient thought and more time discussing the Quran itself.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589680\n 1589680\n \n 1589680\n Strengths: (1) Range of topics. We cover a lot of ground in a short time and the overall plan for the course is pretty broad. Makes for a good ground-level introduction that can lead to more exploration later. (2) Great assignment structure. Really, really liked the alternating weeks of response papers. They were a great way for me to really do a deep-dive on the readings for the week! Would recommend adding another week of the response papers, actually. But I'd like for them to be less formal. The final paper can be an opportunity to work on citations etc, but I think the response papers should be pretty low-key, just aiming to get us to think more deeply about a particular question. (3) Guest speakers were very cool! Think that their topics could maybe have been included in the syllabus. But also maybe ask them to shorten their presentations and pose a couple of questions to the group so that we can discuss and try to tie in themes from the class as a whole.\r\n\r\nImprovements: (1) More focused/intentional in discussion and lecture. At times discussion and lecture felt a little scattered and it was difficult to suss out the main points/takeaways. Would have appreciated more explicit emphasis on the key takeaways and the main themes that we needed to focus on for discussion or pull from the readings. (2) Make clearer the links between different weeks. These links were definitely there and we mentioned them eventually in class, but it would have been good to really emphasize how each week bleeds into another and how all of the topics that we're learning interconnect and build on each other. Again, we got this to some extent, but I would make that a priority moving forward. (3) Recommend some kind of packet or document on key terms or baseline knowledge about Islam that we can all read prior to coming to class. Would help to even out the differences in background between each of the students; those who know a lot about philosophy, but not much about Islam and vice versa.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589747\n 1589747\n \n 1589747\n The course was great as an introduction to Islamic Studies, one of the best philosophy classes Ive taken at Yale.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589767\n 1589767\n \n 1589767\n The strenght is small class size and very interesting readings. The weakness I would say it that there is not a lot of discussions about the readings during seminars. While the Professor gave a general overview, he did not cover each reading in detail.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589839\n 1589839\n \n 1589839\n The strengths of this course was the professor's openness to student engagement, and the diversity of thinkers that we explored. The weaknesses were the lectures were sometimes unstructured.
