RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1662559\n 1662559\n \n 1662559\n Strengths: Prof. van den Bosch is a great lecturer. There were some days that I had difficulty focusing because the class was at 9am and often there was just a lot of content to get through, but he is great at explaining things in an intuitive way. I have never understood physics and astronomy concepts as well as I have in this class. Weaknesses: The out-of-class support and the exams. Office hours were only available if we emailed to schedule it each weekit would have been much better if there was a standard office hours time. A TA would also be incredible for this class. The professor didn't want us working together too much, but without a TA or regular office hours, working together was our only option. And when we would all get the same things wrong on the problem sets, he wouldn't address those concepts in class, so it was difficult to learn from his feedback. And the exams really confused me. Let me reiterate that I loved the lectures and I learned SO MUCH in this class. But the exams didn't test that. About 70% of each exam was just writing down formulas from memory (the other 30% was explaining things conceptually, which makes sense). I conceptually understand so much after this class, but I am terrible at rote-learning formulas, which is what we needed to do for the midterm and final exam. The exams could be exponentially improved if we were instead given the formulas on the exam and had to explain what they meant. I see no point in memorizing over 100 formulas (that's how many boxed formulas there are in the textbook, we counted) when in day-to-day research, we can just refer back to them when we need them. The more important part is knowing what these formulas mean. I think the professor also forgets that we have less baseline knowledge than him, so he might not realize that he is asking us to memorize so many formulas. After the midterm exam, I felt demoralized for the rest of the semester, because I felt that there was little point to the class if the exam was mostly going to assess my memorization skills.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1664661\n 1664661\n \n 1664661\n The lectures in this class were amazing and very helpful to my understanding of the concepts. I wish they weren't at 9 am, because waking up and immediately trying to understand vorticity is a little bit challenging. When we would ask him in class if he could have office hours this week, he would often tell us that he was too busy, and to try again next week; usually, we would be asking for help with the problem set, and he would schedule office hours for after the problem set was due. Second, it would be very helpful to have a TA for this class. It would mean that even if Professor van den Bosch couldn't hold office hours, then at least we could get some help, and it would also help us get a different explanation for certain concepts. Third, the exams in this class are not at all structured in a helpful way. I understand that making the exams like the problem sets would be too math-heavy and would definitely be too hard, but I think there is some middle ground between that and just forcing us to memorize all 200 pages of Professor van den Bosch's lecture notes (which admittedly are very well written and helpful). It doesn't seem important for us to memorize 100+ equations that we will forget a few days after the exam rather than being tested on our understanding of the concepts.
