\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536032\n 1536032\n \n 1536032\n Teacher is incredible and is by far the thing that got me through this class. I wish there were more opportunities for student-led activities, instead of listening to the teacher talk or only calling on one or two students per activity/question.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536035\n 1536035\n \n 1536035\n Strengths- You will learn so much. The workload is very manageable, but does not come with a tradeoff in learning quality. The pacing of each chapter of the textbook also works incredibly well\r\nWeaknesses- As a student, I think I could have benefitted from more practice in reading/ reading comprehension, of which we had some but not many. Also, I think some of the contents of the textbook tackled very important issues, but they were approached in a way that was perhaps not the most tactful. For example, the chapter on gender inequality and its history in chinese culture was very important, but treated with an it-is-what-it-is approach that I think could have been done better. The same applies to normalizing tiger-parenting methods that surprised me a little bit.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536124\n 1536124\n \n 1536124\n Really good class, especially if you want to improve quickly at your command at the language.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536190\n 1536190\n \n 1536190\n Strengths: The nature of the course is definitely a little slower paced than other Chinese courses I've taken here, which definitely allowed me to focus on improving my spoken and written Chinese, as opposed to only forcing myself to complete the homework assignments. The discussions that took place in class were also very useful and helped me improve my listening skills.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The amount of vocabulary we were expected to learn in a week felt rather far-fetched, especially since we were expected to know how to write the characters as well. What this ended up achieving was a sort of , as I felt like I would only remember the words/characters for each quiz and then immediately forget them after. I think it would have been a lot more useful if we were allowed to stagger out the character learning or if we were made to incorporate character learning in other ways (For example: weakly small assignments/essays)
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1551186\n 1551186\n \n 1551186\n Strengths: Awesome instructor (Fu Laoshi)! Very engaging and enjoyable class times. The small group setting and general energy level of the class made it one of my favorite classes, although this may have been particular to the specific group of people. The structures we learned were generally useful and I believed helped me to speak more fluently. Nightly homework and weekly dictation quizzes were reasonable and manageable.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Somewhat inconsistent pacing; some weeks were quite easy, but other weeks felt very rushed, especially after the midterm.
