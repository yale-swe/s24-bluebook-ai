RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1649480\n 1649480\n \n 1649480\n Strengths: The papers each week were fantastic to read and digest. Dr. Mooseker's engagement during class time was simply remarkable. We went through each figure together, and he drew names from a hat so that everyone was 1) held accountable for doing the readings and 2) had a chance to explain the paper to the group. By the end of class, everyone had contributed to the discussion. Although the cold call could be scary because you never knew which figure you would have to present in front of the class, it was great for holding me accountable for reading the papers in order to fully understand the science. Also, Dr. Mooseker actually emailed the author of the paper each week and shared their insight with the class. We got to hear how each paper impacted their lives and what they have thought since then!\r\n\r\nWeakness: I think the group project at the end could have been a bit better organized. I would have liked to have everyone assigned groups instead of having to form their own, and it would have been nice to have every group present their paper in front of the class. However, this was the first time that Dr. Mooseker did this, and he has already expressed some of the changes for the future. This is a 10/10 class, plain and simple.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1650104\n 1650104\n \n 1650104\n Strengths: Straightforward format, allowed for discussion amongst peers, furthered understanding of cell biology principles, and delved into materials\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: the sheer amount of papers and knowledge we had to read was sometimes overwhelming. I wish we only chose one paper or there was a "pre-class lecture for it". I also struggled with the methods a lot, and since there were so many methods, it was hard to understand which ones we should know and which ones should be emphasized. I wish we had pre-class videos on the methods too.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1651348\n 1651348\n \n 1651348\n strengths - professor mooseker is so kind, engaged, and understanding and you learn so much from him
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1651351\n 1651351\n \n 1651351\n A big strength was that prof Mooseker is extremely knowledgeable and will be able to answer any questions you may have about papers. A weakness may have been that the course focused on the cytoskeleton a lot towards the end, which is not my favorite topic in biology.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1651604\n 1651604\n \n 1651604\n Excellent course, only small suggestion is it would have been nice to have access to Mooseker's slides that contained background information to aid us in understanding the paper before class
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1651676\n 1651676\n \n 1651676\n I loved having the space to talk and learn about science in a way that is fun. The cold-calling was rough at first but you get used to it after a while.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1652494\n 1652494\n \n 1652494\n Strengths: read many papers-good overall look at cell bio and techniques used, a chill class with low workload, engaging-participation \r\nWeakness: cold calling however this did force us to read the papers and therefore was good in that sense also made the class more engaging and the conversations were deeper due to everyone coming prepared
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1654951\n 1654951\n \n 1654951\n Strengths: Professor Mooseker is an incredible professor with an amazing wealth of knowledge in the field of cell biology. His ability to break down complicated concepts and methods is next to none. However, what makes this class special is his connections with the authors of the landmark papers we read. The insight and context they provide enrich our reading of their papers. Weaknesses: No glaring weaknesses. Everyone has a chance to participate in class and there is no pressure to be correct. In the future, we talked about having everyone get a chance to present a landmark paper of their own. I think this would be a beneficial learning experience.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1669198\n 1669198\n \n 1669198\n This course is very well organized, and the papers were well-curated by Professor Mooseker. I really like how Professor Mooseker structured each class session with his PowerPoint sessions and guiding questions as we moved through the papers. The students were engaged, and the reading load was more than manageable (1-2 papers per week--sometimes 3 but they would all be shorter in that case). In terms of improvements, I think the size of the class made it a little bit more difficult, at least in the beginning, to develop an organic sort of banter and discussion. Eventually, we were able to get to this point, especially in our final few class sessions where students would chime in with their thoughts. In that way, it felt more engaging and like a "journal club." The last few classes were especially fun. I wonder if maybe either using a different seminar room (a smaller one where students would be actually around a table and closer together) would help with this. But overall, no major weaknesses. The class was really fun and interesting, and Professor Mooseker was always so insightful.
