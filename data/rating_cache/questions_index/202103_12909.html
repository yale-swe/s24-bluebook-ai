\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521054\n 1521054\n \n 1521054\n Over the course of this semester, I got to engage with race and different forms of media in ways that I haven't experienced before at Yale. There should be more media studies classes that intersect with the ER&M banner. We started off the course with a foundational understanding of why comedy is effective, which informed the rest of our semester as we studied everything from stand-up comedy to satiric novels to movies and sitcoms. I developed a language of how to talk about comedy and its implications, a skill that is ever-relevant in today's time.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525253\n 1525253\n \n 1525253\n Great introduction to cultural theories of comedy, media analysis, racial/comic history. Writing and research skills for cultural analysis.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525523\n 1525523\n \n 1525523\n I learned how to think critically about comic cultural productions and how to ask questions such as: who is the audience? how is the performer employing humor? for what purpose? what's the role of race and identity? what strategies are used? I also learned that there is a thin line between racism and humor that often gets blurred.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531841\n 1531841\n \n 1531841\n Unique opportunity to look at comedy from a critical lens, engaged w/ lots of material with strong theoretical and historical perspective set up in the first weeks. Laguna always tries to bring in cool and interesting guest lecturers which is a plus. Course is true to its description and does not separate comedy into "good (PC) comedy" and "bad comedy," rather allows for really nuanced discussions of the way race is working throughout different comedic pieces. Analysis of sitcoms/stand up/literature/plays and performances/movies/memes etc.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532698\n 1532698\n \n 1532698\n The history of comedy and the nuances in the media we consume today and how it relates to race relations
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532831\n 1532831\n \n 1532831\n I learned about comedy but I'm not exactly sure *what* I learned about comedy. I loved watching movies about racial identity reflected through comedic lenses, but I'm not entirely sure what I'm taking away from the class as a whole.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1533056\n 1533056\n \n 1533056\n I learned about the history of racialized comedy and racial humor in the US. I also felt I gained some critical tools to dissect and digest jokes and humorous mediums (like sitcoms, standup, etc.). I feel like I can now close read comedies along with dramas in general as well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1533175\n 1533175\n \n 1533175\n I feel much more comfortable analyzing comedic works as they handle and relate to racial issues
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539168\n 1539168\n \n 1539168\n I learned to be more critical about the humor that I encounter every day. Is there anything wrong with laughing at these kinds of jokes? We explore all of these questions in the class. The readings and shows that we were assigned to watch were all super engaging!
