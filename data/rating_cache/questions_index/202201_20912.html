\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1561930\n 1561930\n \n 1561930\n Yes and no. This course is great. It is one of the better CS courses offered at Yale and shows you the practical applications of the theoretical CS skills we gain at Yale. Anurag is clear and very knowledgeable in his field (he is leaving Yale tho and not sure if he will be back to teach again). He makes great slides. Also, Seung-Seob (the coinstructor/TA) is probably one of the most responsive people on this planet and was up until 2 am answering questions on one of the programming assignments.... SHOUT OUT TO HIM! I do wish the programming assignments were clearer (maybe a walkthrough session from TAs/ULAs or even the professor himself???). Additionally, the homework assignments were kinda hard. They covered analytical applications of the theoretical concepts we learned. The analytical side of things weren't really covered in lecture and you would be left searching online for concepts required to answer many of the things being asked. The internet became my go to resources over the course of the semester because there were minimal opportunities to ask the ULA, which I often prefer over TAs. The grading in this course is exceptionally harsh and the exams were stacked against you. You can know all of the content and still fail the exam. They are really hard and make up for 50% of your grade which leads to a highly anxiety inducing experience. This anxiety is amplified by the fact that the professor is extremely vague with how he will end up curving the course. He releases raw grade information as well as statics and histogram of grade distributions, but this is of little help when almost everyone is scoring around a 60-70. I wish he curved scores on an assignment by assignment basis. This would make everyone much more confident with their grades and standing in the course. For future students: although I am unsure if the course will remain the same without Anurag, the first programming assignment is the hardest, but must work at a basic level because you will build onto of it in assignment 3. The second assignment was by far the easiest once you got through the learning curve of understanding what is happening (the spec is kinda unclear). The third assignment was the second easiest. Use that information to plan accordingly.
