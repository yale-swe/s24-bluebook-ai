\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512848\n 1512848\n \n 1512848\n This course had many strengths. The syllabus was innovative and engaging. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512923\n 1512923\n \n 1512923\n Class time was very bad questions were rarely answered by the professor
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512928\n 1512928\n \n 1512928\n It was super fast paced and the reading was tough with a full schedule
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1514883\n 1514883\n \n 1514883\n This course did for me what no other statistics course (I have tried two before!) have been able to in the past: I understand statistics. Its focus on conceptual understanding is one of its biggest strengths in my opinion. The professor did not try to teach us how to conduct major statistical analysis or which formulas to use on advanced statistical softwares, which is what most professors do in introductory classes; he made it so we could have a full understanding of what statistical analysis actually is, what it can and cannot do. This is important for all persons to understand; I think this particular strength benefitted students who work with and study statistics/students from STEM fields, students from social sciences fields and humanities students, such as myself, in equal amounts. I wish all introductory statistics courses reserved a portion of their syllabi to address the conceptual part of statistics and the current crises in statistical analysis as applied to the sciences; it would make learning much more interesting, easy and relevant. The seminar-style was an intriguing concept for a science class, or at least in my experience I had never encountered a science class with this structure before, and it was amazing! Getting to engage in discussions about the material was just as valuable for learning as any lecture-style moment was. This course was EXTREMELY organized. The most organized course I have ever taken at Yale. Each class had a detailed description, with well explained learning objectives and directions and context for each reading. This was honestly just AWESOME. It made it so much easier to keep track of the content and of the learning experience in general. Another strength was the professor's use of the TA's skills in the class. Our TA was essential in my learning experience, and I believe this not only to have stemmed from her competence and experience, but also from the professor's purposeful class dynamic when it came to her involvement in our experience. The only weakness that comes to mind at this point is the fact that most scientific studies and articles we got to practice our skills on were from the professor's field of behavioral biology/anthropology. This was a bit daunting and alienating at first, though along the course of the class I got used to it and was able to engage with these materials more quickly than before. I would suggest adding studies/articles from other fields for practice purposes.
