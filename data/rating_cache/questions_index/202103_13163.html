\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521368\n 1521368\n \n 1521368\n Amazing course with an amazing, talented, incredibly caring, intimidatingly intelligent (in a good way!) and kind professor\r\n\r\nStrengths: 1) Professor Hayes' amazing teaching. The style of the course, which was kind of a lecture-seminar combination. For the first several weeks, we would all do the reading, then come to class, where Professor Hayes would lecture in tandem with questions asked of the class, which would often become incredibly interesting conversations about the content. Professor Hayes has a gift for stimulating enthusiasm and conversations about relatively abstruse material and debates, and really brings color to every single philosopher and thinker being discussed. The second part of the course involves each person being assigned a particular question for a particular reading (say, a certain concept Aquinas is expositing) and describing it to the course. Both parts of the course were absolutely electric in terms of the classroom's intellectual energy. Professor Hayes really did well to cultivate this excitement. I just remember leaving the classroom every single day excited to return the next, perplexed and amazed by the ideas being discussed, being on the edge of my seat every class meeting. 2) Classroom discussion was some of the highest quality I've seen so far at Yale. Just amazing, and Professor Hayes deserves full credit for fostering such a space.\r\n\r\n3) Perfect workload in my opinion. The readings were hard at timesvery hard at times, especially towards the end with thinkers like Dworkin and H.L.A. Hartbut always accessible with the knowledge you had built up from previous sections of the course. The "study guides" offered for later readings were super helpful. I estimate the most a reading took me was 4 hours. A previous review said some readings should be "cut" and that there was "never enough time for us to fully digest each piece." I disagree with this contention strongly, and think that the number of readings was perfect. The workload was if anything, relatively light!\r\n\r\n4) The structure of the course, as mentioned in another part of this eval, was spectacularly done and the perfect format for a first-year seminar.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: 1) through no fault of Professor Hayes, some students had varying levels of participation, or had clearly not really done the readings in-depth, leading to Professor Hayes having to fill the cracks a little more than usual. That said, no real way to remedy this. 2) More time for the final paper would have been nice. I am currently composing mine and while I feel confident, I wish we would have received the prompt earlier, especially since my grade hinges on the essay. Maybe the process could have began slightly earlier, with meetings to discuss topics occurring a couple of weeks earlier? Don't let this sole weakness discourage you from taking the course, which is otherwise flawless. :)
