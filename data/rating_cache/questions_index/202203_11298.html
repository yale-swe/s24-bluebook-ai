\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1611857\n 1611857\n \n 1611857\n The organization of the course was quite frankly terrible. I took this class in conjunction with Econ 121 (intermediate micro) to ensure I had the mathematical skills needed for intermediate economics, which is how the course was advertised. By the time we got to the concepts relevant to Econ, we were already at the end of the semester, and I had to learn the mathematical concepts of econ individually. Moreover, this course leans heavily towards linear algebra and only touched calculus for 1-2 weeks at the end of the semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616450\n 1616450\n \n 1616450\n Very disorganized, hard to know what we were supposed to be learning. The content isn't that hard, but you really cannot rely on class to actually teach the material. You do have to go to class to learn what you're supposed to be learning, though, because there are never slides and no comprehensive list of the material you're supposed to know. It's kind of nice that there are weekly quizzes instead of psets, but still have to do a lot of homework to be prepared for the quizzes. The review sessions are helpful, but basically a necessary third weekly class, and if you can't make it to that you're not in a great position.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616452\n 1616452\n \n 1616452\n I think the class is perfect for learning the math needed for higher level econ classes. Not needing to submitting psets was incredible. \r\n\r\nThe professor struggled to explain abstract ideas, and the later ideas in the linear stuff was very over and under explained. Also, these things would have been much easier if some kind of visual explanation was paired with the mathematical one.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616453\n 1616453\n \n 1616453\n I thought the curriculum of this course was very straightforward and enjoyable! It was well-paced, and having weekly homework quizzes (instead of turning in graded psets) forced me to focus on understanding concepts rather than merely regurgitating homework answers, which I personally REALLY enjoyed. Prof. Rosenthal was very accessible, and his optional weekly homework review sessions were incredibly helpful (although lectures were occasionally a bit convoluted/ difficult to follow). Also, there weren't many connections to economics in this class. Although the specific skills we learned are applicable to intermediate micro and beyond, none of the problems we specifically worked on in class were focused on economic topics.
