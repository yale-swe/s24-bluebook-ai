\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562098\n 1562098\n \n 1562098\n Strengths: the professor gives good topics for the papers and the mock committee meeting on the last day is a fun way to apply what you've learned throughout the course. Lectures are a bit dry, but professor English does teach well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1570794\n 1570794\n \n 1570794\n The strength of the course is definitely professor English. he is super knowledgeable, energetic, and engaged, and does a great job conveying what he knows to the students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1580415\n 1580415\n \n 1580415\n This course covers a wide array of topics from the theoretical formulation of the DSGE model to evaluation of the real-life implementation of monetary policies. Many insights are offered through lectures and readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1581187\n 1581187\n \n 1581187\n This is an INCREDIBLE course. I absolutely loved it. Professor English is a legend. \r\n\r\nOnly caveat is high workload, but if you put in the work I promise you will not regret it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1582741\n 1582741\n \n 1582741\n Strengths: Professor English brings so much of his expertise and knowledge from working at the Fed into this course, and it's a fantastic opportunity to learn from someone so knowledgeable. The class and casual class coffees were also great opportunities to learn about monetary policy happening in real time. Papers were stressful to write but proved to be rewarding. I also liked that although the course focused on the Fed, we did have time to discuss monetary policies in other countries. Professor English's slides were also always super clear.\r\nWeakness: I wish the expectations for the final had been clearer (e.g. to what extent we should know about specific papers / specific math exercises), because the course had so much content and studying for the final & trying to remember everything from the beginning of the semester gave me a lot of anxiety. But this is somewhat a minor point because overall the course was very rewarding. I also wish the papers had been graded slightly faster to offer more time to get feedback before starting the next writing assignment (but this is a minor point because I really do appreciate the level of detail in the comments, and I wouldn't trade that for faster grading).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1583905\n 1583905\n \n 1583905\n Strengths: great lectures (at least, if you're interested in the subject) including some very cool guest lectures, Professor English is also super knowledgeable about this topic and always happy to share his views based on 25 years of working at the Fed, and the papers are a great way to dive deeper into the material from class. You also get to cover a lot of material and there are plenty of ways to engage with the material outside of class. Weaknesses: if you're not interested in monetary policy, the class will be a lot of work for not that much payoff. Some things (terminology, etc) can also be confusing at times if you don't have a background in finance. My only suggestion for improvment would be to ensure that terms like "risk spread" are defined as soon as Professor English starts using them (it took a few weeks for me to really understand what he was talking about).
