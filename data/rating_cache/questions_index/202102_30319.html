\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512704\n 1512704\n \n 1512704\n The class was discussion based which led to a lot of interesting conversations about the readings and our interpretations of them. The guest speakers and the variety in the mediums we used to learn were a strength. From readings, tv shows, movies, books, art, everything was super interesting. A weakness would definitely be that we werent able to do our outside cultural events in person. We had to watch recordings of them, which is understandable considering that the class was taught online.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512796\n 1512796\n \n 1512796\n Strengths -- all of the syllabus content was so interesting, I found myself recommending almost everything we did to friends/family. We had great guest speakers, all of them were incredibly interesting. I enjoyed class discussions and I enjoyed when Shiri would lecture! \r\n\r\nAreas for improvement -- the class was such a positive experience, so it's difficult to think of any. I did enjoy all of our class discussions, but, like we talked about in class, 3 hours is a long time to be on Zoom. Maybe switching up the style of discussion (ie, more breakout rooms) would help? However, I did appreciate how most classes were discussion-based (rather than activity-based) and especially when we would all go around and share our thoughts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513190\n 1513190\n \n 1513190\n This course provided an in-depth approach to understanding Israel and the story of the nation of Israeli in both historical and present times which was a strength. Also, it provided many opportunities to learn about Israel in different ways such as short films, graphic novels, or traditional books. Also, there was opportunity to interact with classmates and discuss the information in the course. One weakness in the course was that sometimes the readings were long and very time consuming, especially during weeks when the midterm or final was due. This can be improved by decreasing the workload when those larger assignments are due.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513331\n 1513331\n \n 1513331\n The "Israeli Narratives" course approaches its main theme through a variety of works by authors, directors, and photographers from multiple backgrounds. This allowed us to understand how the Israeli condition is complex and made up of many perspectives and points of view. Students were encouraged to read and watch most of the materials outside of synchronous meetings, so that the time we spent together was dedicated to group discussions. This allowed us to interact more, which made the course similar to an in person one. Furthermore, a few guest speakers were invited to classes, and this guided our discussions and brought more specific insights about the works analyzed. Additionally, students were given a fair amount of time to complete each assignment, and evaluations were accompanied by productive suggestions by professor Shiri Goren. I didn't notice any significant weaknesses in the course. The only idea I had was to make the second outside event report optional, because completing it on the last week of the course left less time to do the final project.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513839\n 1513839\n \n 1513839\n Overall I really liked this course. It was well organized and the professor was considerate of the student's needs and challenges during COVID and online learning. For example, the professor always inserted a couple short breaks in the class (3hrs long) so that the students would not burn out on zoom, which I found really helpful. The environment was also positive and engaging, which made it easy to participate.\r\n\r\nI cannot think of any weaknesses other than those caused by the online format (zoom fatigue, online distractions, etc.).
