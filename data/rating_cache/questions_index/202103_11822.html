Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1552852\n 1552852\n \n 1552852\n Prof Fadiman has made pedagogy into an art. Every minute of the class is carefully planned out. It's really rewarding to know that someone pays such close attention to your writing and is so invested in your development as a writer. Prof Fadiman's strength surely comes from how much time she devotes to each student individually. She conferences with students after each essay and breaks down your pieces into the word level. Though I enjoy these conferences a lot, I did sometimes wish there was written feedback on our essays. I also think that the rigorous nature of the class limits spontaneity and I wish there was a bit more freedom in what we did.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553468\n 1553468\n \n 1553468\n This course is excellent and completely transformed my ability and love for writing. (Marginally, I think it could be improved by being shortened by 20 minutes, as we often spend time just reading the syllabus at the end of the class, which feels redundant.) Otherwise, it's the best course I've taken at Yale.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553551\n 1553551\n \n 1553551\n Strengths: one-on-one feedback, Anne's seemingly preternatural ability to select a workshop cohort of 12 that have really good chemistry, opportunity to report long form, author visits, social culture of the course, Anne as a professor caring about both you as a person as well as a writer. Excellent in general.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: only two that come to mind are really 1) having the Irene/Mara visit about a week or two earlier would've helped inform the initial site visits, first calls, and interviews at the start of the Yellow Pages, and 2) sometimes working the first paragraph during conferences wasn't always the most helpful application of the conference (some essays needed more macro feedback than sentence by sentence edits, but the sentence by sentence edits were still very helpful).\r\n\r\nAlso loved the little 1/3 class segment we did on applications/cover letters/interest statements/etc. Maybe a little more of that added in one class could help.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553872\n 1553872\n \n 1553872\n I think the readings are very well-selected, the writing assignments are challenging in a good way, and Anne really tries to push you out of your comfort zone.\r\nThe weakness, I would say, is that sometimes privacy is not so well protected in the class. Ive heard from multiple students in my class (as well as learned from my own experience) that private things discussed in conferences were disclosed to other students in the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554645\n 1554645\n \n 1554645\n Strengths: the atmosphere among classmates is very supportive, Anne devotes tons of careful attention to the class and to all her students, the assignments are incredibly well-paced, the Yellow Pages assignment is fun, readings are generally good, and many interesting professional writers visit the class for Q&As. Also, an underrated part of the class is that you get a fair amount of in-depth editing practice (a rare quality in other writing courses).\r\nWeaknesses: discussions of the readings and workshops of student pieces can feel scripted and hurried along, there isn't any written feedback from Anne on pieces (although she does meet with each student for an hour after each submission and give verbal feedback).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1556001\n 1556001\n \n 1556001\n Strengths - You will have a lot of people reading and commenting on your work, as well as helping you improve it. Every piece gets posted to Canvas, everyone has one in-class workshop, and there's a peer critique of every piece (three written, one verbal). Also, Anne herself is very invested in her students' writing, and she offers a lot of verbal feedback in mandatory, hour-long, one-on-one conferences. Weaknesses - I respond best to written feedback, so I really wish we'd gotten written feedback on our work from the instructor. (There was none.) I also would've liked for the class to lean more toward the seminar side than the lecture side - it definitely was pretty interactive, but I found that there were often times when I would've liked for more open discussion on the (fantastic) readings, as it sometimes felt too structured or rushed. And honestly...I don't think this really needs to be a three-hour class. A lot of time at the end of class was dedicated toward just explaining whatever was on the syllabus for next week.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1556016\n 1556016\n \n 1556016\n ENGL 469 is a phenomenal course that teaches you so much about writing. It brings together really smart, humble, and supportive peers and an incredibly dedicated professor in Anne. \r\n\r\nMy only suggestion for the course would be that the seminar was somewhat long, and perhaps unnecessarily so. A quarter to a third of it could probably be trimmed down and communicated in an email or handout (in which form Anne likely has it already). I definitely felt that I learned so much outside class (during workshops, while doing assignments and critiques, etc) but sometimes found class dragging a bit.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554645\n 1554645\n \n 1554645\n This course can be amazing if you decide to buy into it. It's designed incredibly well -- Anne provides you with lots of structure and lots of encouragement, and the course is paced so that you produce four drafts plus two polished pieces by the end of the semester.
