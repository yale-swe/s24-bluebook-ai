\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1574078\n 1574078\n \n 1574078\n The strongest part of this class is that Prof. Hawkns clearly cares a lot about his students and he's very passionate about teaching macroeconomics. That said, this class has a lot of room for improvement. For one thing, the class moved incredibly slowly, largely because we got incredibly bogged down by questions - it's great that people are able to ask questions, but there was never a sense of urgency in the class and we quickly fell behind, resulting in us just not doing various topics that were covered in ECON 116. I also think the format of the class could be improved: the post-class practice worksheets are really excellent, but the pre-class prep assignments didn't really contribute anything to the class. The problem sets are fine, but I actually think they're less valuable because they're infrequent - there's no consistent practice of these topics. Finally, the group project at the end of the class was just awful: no one felt like it helped solidify topics, and it just felt like a lot of work with no benefit. Everyone I talked to said they would just prefer an extra problem set or two at the end of the class, especially given that the final project was in addition to a final exam.
