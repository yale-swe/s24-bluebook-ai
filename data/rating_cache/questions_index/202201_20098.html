\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1565283\n 1565283\n \n 1565283\n It is nice that the course placed an emphasis on group work and collaboration and I think that the subject matter is really interesting even to people who don't study astro-physics. However, the course is entirely inaccessible to entry-level, non-stem majors. The recorded lectures are available to review but the concepts are often not deconstructed enough for understanding. Further, the math component of the course completely went over my head and I think that made success on the exams and problem sets feel unobtainable. In the future, I think explaining the concepts in class would help as we would be able to ask questions to facilitate understanding. Most importantly, I think it would be helpful if math problems were perhaps not as wordy or longwinded- sometimes its hard to understand what it is you need to do. Additionally, teaching the methodology of solving problems would be tremendously helpful. I know it is emphasized that students should understand the concepts, not memorize equations, but for non-math people learning an equation or methodology to solving a problem can contextualize the concepts a lot better.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1572300\n 1572300\n \n 1572300\n Doctor Faison has built a brilliant curriculum within this course for Yale students. He teaches with passion and great enthusiasm. The TFs are also amazing, and they really go out of their way in helping to ensure students can easily grasp the concepts in the course. Students really enjoy this class! Doctor Faison has structured the course to support students of all types (even the math phobic ones like me). Students have all of the tools available to succeed. Meaning: come to class, watch the weekly discussion videos, do the assignments, come to a few of the optional discussion sections (or office hours) when you have questions, and you will pass with flying colors. The course is laid out so you just absorb all of the information as you move through the course. \r\n\r\nOther massive strengths: The focus is NOT on being a QR / STEM prodigy. The weekly assignments (problem sets) place an emphasis on comprehension of the material and understanding the methods behind the concepts relating to astronomy and the 3 major themes, not on computational perfection 100% of the time. A student who shows the methods they used and demonstrates that they grasp the concepts behind the material, even if they sometimes reach the wrong answer, will ALWAYS get a decent grade on the problem sets. In addition, for the exams you are not expected to memorize the various formulas you encounter throughout the course. Instead, on exams you will be provided a sheet with formulas relating to astronomy. From the beginning students are told that the concepts behind the course are the most important, and this is true. Come to class and discussion sections, watch the discussion videos, and you will literally absorb everything you need to know. It is likely that this may even become one of your favorite classes.\r\n\r\nAnother important strength: The problem sets are always harder than the two exams (midterm and final), to ensure that if a student understands the problem sets, then they will excel on the exams. There are not a lot of problem sets, only 11 total if I remember correctly. There are only two tests / exams which are the midterm and final.
