\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618548\n 1618548\n \n 1618548\n So much! While this was my lowest-workload class this semester, it felt like I learned more from it than any of my other classes. We learned about phonetics (how humans make the vast array of sounds and signs from the world's many languages), syntax and semantics (how languages construct sentences from words and how different sentences are related to each other through their truth conditions), morphology (how meaningful chunks of words are combined to make full words), ambiguity that is built into even grammatical sentences, how learning language is very different than learning any other skill like piano or math, some of the traits that vary across languages and what is universal in all languages, a bit of how language is processed in the brain, and more!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1620317\n 1620317\n \n 1620317\n I received a broad introduction to the study of language. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1620412\n 1620412\n \n 1620412\n This course was a broad survey on Linguistics, which taught us cross-language analysis and invited us to think deeper about the social implications of language and any prejudices that may arise from that.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1620496\n 1620496\n \n 1620496\n We learned a good deal about most major areas of linguistics, especially phonetics and phonology, morphology, and syntax. Among the most valuable skills we learned was understanding the International Phonetic Alphabet (a VERY useful skill, especially if you're learning a new language), and understanding what makes a particular construction ungrammatical in a dialect, and how it might be grammatical in another dialectin other words, how grammars may differ across dialects, as well as across languages. We learned to analyze the structure of sentences and words and sounds in any language, and to describe the grammars of different languages as they actually occur out in the world (rather than as someone believes they *should* occur).
