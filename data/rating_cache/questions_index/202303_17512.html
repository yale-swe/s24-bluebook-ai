Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702918\n 1702918\n \n 1702918\n Strengths: Covers a wide range of intellectual history across 300+ years and weaves together different writers from across time and space very well. The discussion section is fantastic and vibrant. The exams are just three papers (~1,500-2,000 words) plus section participation. Plus very short weekly assignments for sections (like response paragraphs).\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The lecture is mostly random professorial commentary on the topics. After a few weeks, you realize that the lecture is, for all intents and purposes, just for fun. You don't need to go to any lectures to participate fully in the discussion section and write all the papers.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1703709\n 1703709\n \n 1703709\n The real strength of this course was my TF Charlie Troup. And its weakness, if any, was its lack of structure in lecture.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706003\n 1706003\n \n 1706003\n The biggest weakness is a lack of student engagement. Most of the lectures were listening to the panel and a few mins of questions after. Some more engagement with students would have been better
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1707262\n 1707262\n \n 1707262\n The lecture format with discussion among the three instructors was great. I enjoyed hearing them respond directly to one another (and jab at each other from time to time). It made for lots of interesting and funny moments. I think one thing that could be improved is bridging the gap between theory and current events. I'm partial to theory, so I would have liked to see more of it in the latter parts of the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1708352\n 1708352\n \n 1708352\n Content is interesting and professors are entertaining. But it's not a traditional class. Lectures and essays aren't really connected. Lectures and sections won't cover all the readings. Take the class if you're interested in the readings and will find the Douthat/Moyn disagreements in lecture entertaining.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710218\n 1710218\n \n 1710218\n Lots of variation in the readings and very thoughtfully curated syllabus. The lectures are less helpful and the sound was muffled for a lot of it. 3 professors lecturing sort of made topic unclear for each day it went on.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710380\n 1710380\n \n 1710380\n The strength of this course is the unconventional format. The three professors essentially doing an IRL podcast was interesting, with their banter or answer to a question often being more insightful than the mini-lecture at the beginning. All of them are really brilliant and great to listen to. While their analysis and teaching were great, I think they were too ambitious when it came to the syllabus. They often never touched all of the readings for one particular class during their discussion, preferring to go deeper on one small point. This made the overall narrative of the class very unclear, as each meeting felt like its own dive into one particular idea on one particular reading. The course would be much better if the syllabus and scope of the class were smaller, tackling the micro-issues the professors would be talking about anyways.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723953\n 1723953\n \n 1723953\n The strength was the section for this course truly excellent and helped me to improve as a writer.\r\n\r\nThe lectures were interesting, and each professor is an interesting thinker, but the lectures were redundant and the readings were not always inspiring. The class should be more about putting the three views in conversation with ancient political theory; instead it often felt like the three professors (Douthat and Moyn in particular) were just repeatedly saying their views and never conceding.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724187\n 1724187\n \n 1724187\n Syllabus was great at the beginning (the theory seemed apt), but really lost its way over the course of the semester.
