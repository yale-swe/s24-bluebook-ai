\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1565603\n 1565603\n \n 1565603\n Tons! This is a course that invites students to fully immerse themselves in artistic practices and thinking. You lead with the materials and interact with the environment, learning how to translate what you see into lines, shapes, and shades. I learned how to approach drawing and how to experiment with materials. You learn by doing in this course, which means from class to class, you get better at drawing simply by engaging with the project of that week. I learned how to think artistically and critique others' work, as well as how to decide what I want my own work to look like (and how to make it look that way).

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1565742\n 1565742\n \n 1565742\n Sorta a bit of everything honestly! Obviously, I learned how to draw a lot better (drawing is about observing and isn't hard so much as it requires practice); but also, I feel like I learned a lot about how I view the world. The process of art and drawing helps you understand how you see the world, and how you approximate it. I feel like I learned as much metaphysics as three philosophy classes just by understanding how I experience the world and decide what lines to draw through art. This class is epic because it combines physical skills with personal growth, and I would HIGHLY recommend anyone thinking about this course to apply and or take it jeez it's that good.

This is an experiential learning course that excels purely because Justin is an insanely talented and insightful instructor. The focus on the materials, the thinking, and the process relinquishes students from the burdens of "making something look good" or "getting a good grade". I never once thought about my grade in this course--we were never given any nor did it ever cross my mind. I showed up and engaged with the course the best way I could, and I was rewarded with insight and fun (really!). This class is truly a gem, and you should not hesitate to take it should it ever pop up again.

I think some of the best moments in the class were when he analyzed my/our artwork: I feel like, through looking at my art, he understood me in a way that few people did, because he could tell from my drawings.
