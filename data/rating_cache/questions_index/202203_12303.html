\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618833\n 1618833\n \n 1618833\n This course created a community of artists. Both the class as a whole and our individual sections established creative spaces where individuals were heard, encouraged, and excitedly included. I always felt thrilled both to see the processes of my peers as well as to develop it on my own.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622758\n 1622758\n \n 1622758\n I loved this class. I would have liked to work with people outside my discussion section though sometimes.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622871\n 1622871\n \n 1622871\n I appreciated how the focus of this course was thinking, discussing, and creating rather than attempting to achieve a grade - it was liberating and made me feel comfortable letting my creativity flow. In addition, everyone says you can only get good at a thing by doing it, and this class was a continuous stream of doing, which I really appreciated. The only things I would say as improvements would be the corpus of readings and viewings; I felt like they were often more in the experimental realm, but I would have liked to read and viewed a more even survey of works from different genres.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1623183\n 1623183\n \n 1623183\n I think the strengths lie in what, for me, was initially frustrating: the lack of specificity in the assignments. In the beginning of the course, I was often confused and unsure how to proceed. As the course continued, I grew to be immensely grateful over this lack of form, because it allowed me and my peers to pursue whatever ideas we wanted. No idea was too big or too small; we could explore whatever we wanted. The ability to just do, and to grow and collaborate freely alongside my classmates, was a huge strength.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624615\n 1624615\n \n 1624615\n Reading and discussing the pieces of work that were assigned was helpful, as we started thinking like interpreters and adapters. I also benefitted from learning a method of providing feedback for performances. \r\n\r\nI didn't feel like the length of the class was necessary to access the core material. There was time used in class for warm-ups that felt slightly unrelated, and during our section, which wasn't listed on the syllabus, there were some materials assigned that felt unrelated and unnecessary for the projects at hand.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625815\n 1625815\n \n 1625815\n This course did a great job of bringing students together to create new and exciting pieces of theater that emphasize and uplift the collaborative process. However, I think the amount of time that students were expected to dedicate to this course was one of its biggest weaknesses. An hour-and-a-half section meeting on top of a course that already met for three hours often felt like too much and at some points it made me feel tired of and less engaged in the class. I think this course could be improved by dedicating more class time to the creation of group projects instead of requiring students to meet for an additional 90 minutes (and oftentimes more) outside of class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1626469\n 1626469\n \n 1626469\n There were too many strengths to count. In terms of improvements, I would love if the groups were mixed up after eacb project so we werent working with the same people all semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1626842\n 1626842\n \n 1626842\n A strength of the course is that it familiarizes you with the creation of performance very quickly. It also introduces you to several theater concepts through making pieces to embody those concepts, making the engagement with the course necessarily high. Additionally, you meet many other students that are also interested in theater and the arts and begin to collaborate with them in this course. Weaknesses of the course are that it is only once a week and for 3 hours, which can be a bit fatiguing at times. Also, you often have to find time outside of class and section to work on theater pieces, which can be difficult to coordinate sometimes.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1631428\n 1631428\n \n 1631428\n One disadvantage is that I couldn't work with anyone outside of my discussion section for group projects. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1634343\n 1634343\n \n 1634343\n Strengths: meeting once a week together and then for section allowed for ample, spaced out time. Weekly discussion posts helped accountability.\r\nWeaknesses: there were times where an outside of section / class rehearsal was added, which sometimes could be difficult to schedule. It was beneficial to the class, but be prepared to devote a lot of time outside of class too. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625815\n 1625815\n \n 1625815\n I would recommend this course to any student who is interested in the theater studies major. Not only is it already required as one of the prerequisites for the major, but it's also a great way to learn more about the major and a fun way to meet other students interested in theater. Furthermore, the lessons in theater-making that this class teaches are important for any burgeoning theater-maker to know. That being said, I would not recommend this course to anyone who isn't planning on majoring in theater studies because this course feels like it was designed for theater people. A lot of the projects are really abstract and feel more like experiments in theater than actual theater pieces, and while this is an important foundation for anyone who is interested in further pursuing theater more seriously, there are better (and perhaps more fun) courses for non-theater majors who have an interest in exploring theater.
