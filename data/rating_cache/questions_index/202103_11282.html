Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528556\n 1528556\n \n 1528556\n Professor Shanahan crafted a perfect introduction to historical research at Yale. I really appreciated the workshops he organized as opportunities for us to work with librarians at Sterling and become more familiar with the research process. He always left time at the end of class to work through challenges in our research, and the in-progress assignments (e.g., an annotated bibliography, outline, etc.) kept me on track throughout the semester so that I was able to turn in a draft by early December, for which Professor Shanahan then gave detailed, informative line edits.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532015\n 1532015\n \n 1532015\n Really enjoyed the course and am confident that I improved as a writer from the helpful and detailed feedback of Dr. Shanahan. I felt that on some weeks where we had additional assignments, there was too much reading
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536507\n 1536507\n \n 1536507\n Overall, I thought the readings and discussions were great. Professor Shanahan is so knowledgeable, kind, and clearly invested in the students' success. I also really appreciated how the course was structured to guide us along the process of creating a final paper. However, I felt like we were asked to pick a topic a bit too early in the semester-- at that point, we had covered maybe one third of the class material, so I did not yet have a good idea of what I was most interested in writing a research paper on. If we had picked topics later in the semester, I would have had a better sense of what I was most interested in, and so I would have picked a different topic that I probably would have enjoyed more.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543892\n 1543892\n \n 1543892\n The course's biggest strength is Professor Shanahan, who is one of the most supportive professors I've ever had. He truly cares about students' wellbeing and mental health and is great at helping students to follow their interests and develop their own original research papers. He also leaves space for students to spend time examining the parts of the readings that they found most interesting in class while also ensuring that key information is still covered. Most of the seminars end in small group discussions about how each student is doing with their research project, which I found helpful. My only area of improvement would be cutting some of the readings as some weeks it felt like they were too much. I would potentially suggest cutting the ones on political intrigue as I found them difficult to follow and a lot of pages to read for a short takeaway.
