\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1576781\n 1576781\n \n 1576781\n A strength of this course was that the format of it allowed for it to be recorded and the lectures be made available online afterwards, so that any student could access ALL available course materials. However, this turned into a major weakness for me when these lectures were made unavailable unless a certain percentage of students attended class in person. As a student with ADHD, any courses that have materials (especially) lectures available outside of class are a major assistance, as oftentimes, even if I am present in class, I am almost certain to miss some material. On the other hand, what bothered me more is that there can be any number of reasons that someone may have to prefer an online course to one that is in person, and thus the whole class would be punished over someone's preference and decision on how they as an individual prefer to learn, and how they believe that they might learn best. This ruling was put into place after the midterm in the hopes of improving class performance (which it did not, based on midterm and final averages both settling around 70). I am frustrated with the fact that this course had the ability to give every student even more tools for success than the average class, but chose not to. In fairness, we were given the opportunity to "unlock" them for the entire class by participating in creating review questions. We did not meet the threshold, yet everyone was able to benefit from the few who took the time to write review questions. After a semester of amazing lectures and material (I truly mean this, everything was presented and explained exceptionally well), as well as fair homework and exam assessment, it upsets me to feel how I do about this. I understand that recorded lectures are not the norm, and there very well are likely reasons for the decisions made that are unknown to me and beyond what I've acknowledged, but I very much am struggling to understand why any course that takes the time and resources to record lectures for the benefit of student learning would at the end of the day choose to keep those unavailable - keeping an already created, valuable learning tool, unaccessible to any student who would greatly benefit from it. I am leaving the answer to this course being well organized to facilitate student learning as agree, because at the end of the day more was available from this course than some of my other ones. However, I do still believe that if the tools are already available to help students succeed, they may as well be accessed and used by students instead of sitting in storage. \r\n\r\nA final weakness that I would point out is with autograders being set for homework, yet not available before the submission deadline. I believe this was only the case with one assignment, but I was able to complete the assignment clearly and coherently enough as to get a near perfect score on the human-evaluated portion - yet did extremely poorly on the autograder portion due to one parameter being off before submission (which I documented). Yes, this was my fault as I should have double checked that value before submission, however, had I been able to use the autograder before submitting I would have recognized immediately and fixed that one parameter. Again, my fault, but mistakes like this can be easily avoided.\r\n\r\nAgain, I would just like to point out how much I enjoyed this class overall and I would recommend it to anyone interested in AI. Lectures were extremely interesting and presented in an excited and engaged manner. Homework and test assessment were both fair and transparent. Aside from the above weaknesses I describe in this evaluation, I have absolutely no complaints or regrets taking this course - overall, extremely glad to have been able to take this course.
