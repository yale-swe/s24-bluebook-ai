\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1700084\n 1700084\n \n 1700084\n Strengths- very engaging discussions and interesting ethnographies. Really amazing professor and TF.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses- none
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702377\n 1702377\n \n 1702377\n The course is set up very well, with each few weeks focusing on a different theme (i.e., "gender and medicine," "LGBTQ identities," "women in war," etc.). I liked that we got to choose which weeks they wrote a book review---this allowed everybody to focus on the topics they found most interesting. If you're interested in taking this course, know that there is a lot of reading involved! Class discussions are so much more interesting/fulfilling if you've thought pretty deeply about the book before class. Because it was a large seminar, sometimes the discussion tended more towards 'everyone is trying to speak a couple times' than genuine conversation; that being said, a lot of the time the discussions were really interesting and thoughtful. Everybody was clearly invested and wanted to be there.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1703875\n 1703875\n \n 1703875\n Strength: Marcia is awesome. She is so knowledgeable and intelligent, and there were times I truthfully would've rather her just lecture for a period of the class than have it be a whole class discussion. Each ethnography is very specific (i.e. trans people in Iran, beauty standards in Turkey, schools in Jordan), so I felt like we really did get a good sense of each of these niches. Weakness: the work load is pretty high with the expectation being that you read a 300 page ethnography each week. It's pretty difficult to meaningfully contribute if you don't actually read the book, so during midterm weeks the conversations definitely felt more forced.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1704986\n 1704986\n \n 1704986\n This course had so many strengths. One highlight was the syllabus. I loved that it was entirely based in ethnographies. I think this anthropological approach to the topic of Middle East Gender Studies is part of what made this class so impactful for me. Class discussions were also engaging and interesting. Another obvious strength of the class is Marcia! She is so incredibly knowledgable and experienced about this field, and it is such a privilege to learn from her. She is very kind and genuinely cares about the course and about her students, and provides great feedback on assignments. If I had to change one thing, I would make some of the assignments less structured. While I appreciated learning how to write an academic book review (and writing them definitely deepened my understanding of the texts I chose), I wish there had been some sort of creative component to the assignments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1713173\n 1713173\n \n 1713173\n Strengths: the breadth of this class's syllabus is so wide that any student is bound to find at least 80% of what we read interesting. I also really liked the format of seminar, being led mostly by students rather than by instructors. The class also just has a very friendly vibe, which is of course nice in a seminar! Many of the students (myself included) were not in anthropology or Middle East studies, so there was a very diverse array of perspectives, and I am sure this is the case in every iteration of the course. And I cannot stress enough how much I learned in this course. Another positive: for all of the book review assignments, Marcia has uploaded templates and several examples on the Canvas page. This was immensely helpful for someone like me who had no experience with anthropology before this class.\r\nWeaknesses: the only weakness of this class is its homework assignments. You read a book every week, which ranges from 180-300 pages. I am totally fine with this! But the WRITING assignments for the class are just book reviews. I was a bit frustrated with the book reviews because, while learning how to write anthropological book reviews is a valuable skill, I feel that writing book reviews is not a great way to critically engage with the IDEAS in the books we read as opposed to just engaging with the books themselves. That being said, I did enjoy the comparative book review final paper, since the thematic comparison of two books offers a lot of room for creative analysis.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1720800\n 1720800\n \n 1720800\n Strengths: engaging, relevant material, inclusive learning environment
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724771\n 1724771\n \n 1724771\n Strengths: Marcia is great and passionate about what she teaches. Every week, conversation is robust and interesting. Comprehensive insight into the study of the Middle East.\r\n\r\nWeakness: Course felt rushed at times with a focus on a book per week. Really heavy reading load that impacted student engagement. Financial accessibilitybooks were pricey and added up!
