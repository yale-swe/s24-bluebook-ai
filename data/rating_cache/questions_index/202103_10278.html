\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537369\n 1537369\n \n 1537369\n This course was fantastic. The collecting trips for insects were a great strength, and I wish we could have done more of them. Time spent in lab pinning and organizing insects was also really nice and relaxing. The only weak part was the weekly quizzes, which frequently caused us to memorize frantically the names of families in Latin. I understand why they were necessary but it sometimes felt as if we were learning things that we would just forget again because the volume of taxonomic information was so high.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544866\n 1544866\n \n 1544866\n A strength is that Haysun, Petra, and Dr. Wells are all very supportive and enthusiasticit was a joy to spend time with them. Another strength was that a good community was built among the students. \r\nPerhaps a weakness of the course would be that much of the work was in the final 2 weeks. If there were intermediate deadlines with the collections, or even guidelines (ex. have _____ specimens ID'd by ____ date), that would be helpful.\r\nAlso, I wonder if there could be an option to take pictures of the specimens and ID those so that we didn't have to kill them? That is something I meant to ask but had forgotten (that is my bad).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545696\n 1545696\n \n 1545696\n This is a wonderful course all around. The field trips at the beginning of the semester were amazing for providing a sense of community within the class which carried forward for the rest of the semester. Some of the people I met in this class have become some of my best friends over just one semester. Dr. Wells and the TFs were amazing and very passionate, and they made it clear that they cared about your learning, answering any question with respect and care. The class was a lot of work for half a credit, but I think that thats more of a problem with Yales lab policy than it is with the course itself, because I honestly wouldnt change anything about the course. While the first of the weekly quizzes caught me off guard, after that I felt that they were a good way to keep me in practice, and helped me learn to study more effectively. I wouldnt change much of anything about the lab- it was easily my favorite class this semester, and perhaps my favorite class at Yale so far.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545786\n 1545786\n \n 1545786\n It would be more ideal to know something about what you need to catch before having to catch them (all the collecting is at the beginning of the semester before you know anything), but I realize that's a limitation of seasonality. Otherwise, helping us to spread the workload through the semester could be helpful. It kinda felt like we collected, then there was a pause in work intensity before we ground out the end of the semester. Just helping guide workload could be useful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546011\n 1546011\n \n 1546011\n Strengths: wonderful professor and TAs, wonderful lab community, wonderful field trips across CT (and hopefully Florida next year), wonderful project (bug collection, identified and labeled). Not a weakness, but do note that you 100% should take stock of the classes' requirements: demanding criteria of collection, weekly memorization (ID) quizzes (not that bad if you study), frequent field trips.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546482\n 1546482\n \n 1546482\n Strengths: Professor Wells and the TA's, particularly Petra. They were heavily involved in the course at all times, so we worked very closely with them! The camping trips/bug-catching trips were also incredible-- it was my first time going to such places so this was an amazing experience. \r\nWeaknesses: The workload was heavily concentrated towards the end of the semester-- my friends and I would oftentimes stay in the lab into very late hours to finish up our bug collection, even though we had also put extra hours in the weekends throughout the semester.
