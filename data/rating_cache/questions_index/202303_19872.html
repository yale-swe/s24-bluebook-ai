\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697422\n 1697422\n \n 1697422\n I think the format of the course was great. My class was very participative and collaborative, which made the seminar feel very comfortable by the end of the semester. I also liked the flow from topic to topic, and the way in which we were constantly practicing writing, reading, speaking, and listening in Spanish; it was almost immersive, and I think that's the best way for me to learn a language.\r\nOne improvement could be the way in which assignments were assigned on Canvas. Sometimes it was difficult to see what work was due for the next class, and it might have happened once or twice that the syllabus wasn't updated with links until a few days after class. If those links were provided sooner that could be one improvement.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1700270\n 1700270\n \n 1700270\n Strengths: Incredibly kind and engaging professor. She truly wants you to succeed and help you improve your Spanish. Frequent YUAG visits might tough if you have a class right after at another location on campus. \r\n.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721252\n 1721252\n \n 1721252\n I think this course is awesome! Wouldn't change anything.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721295\n 1721295\n \n 1721295\n The small discussion-style classroom forced me to get out of my comfort zone and converse in ways I am not used to, allowing me to pinpoint my weaknesses in my conversational Spanish with the intent to improve. The first-hand experience of going to the Yale University Art Gallery to discuss paintings in the presence of the actual paintings is also quite a unique experience that only enhanced my engagement, entertainment and retention in the course. One weakness in the course is the ineffective application of technology. Unlike stem courses that can just formulate problem sets, a conversational art course is more nuanced to come up with productive out-of-classroom assignments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721328\n 1721328\n \n 1721328\n This course spent a lot of time on each unit. I really liked and found very useful the grammar review. I think that we might have been able to fit in a few more grammar topics, and I would have enjoyed that and found it helpful. I loved that we were able to talk to students from Per; this was one of my favorite parts of the course! It was a fun and valuable experience.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721359\n 1721359\n \n 1721359\n The small class size helped make our discussions and presentations not too stressful! (Also, the weekly Zoom conversation with students in Peru was really helpful in improving my conversation/listening skills, as well as simply fun.) Overall, I didn't find any notable weaknesses with the class; just important pay careful attention to the instructions given in class for projects/presentations.
