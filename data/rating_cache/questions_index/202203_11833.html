\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1620916\n 1620916\n \n 1620916\n Strengths-Selections of the readings, classroom community, essay feedback\r\nWeaknesses- Our class was not exactly the most diverse group of people.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625917\n 1625917\n \n 1625917\n Strengths: Great reads! Every one of the books we read was worthwhile, even in the busy-ness of the school year. Prof. Peters' intellectual enthusiasm really helped energize the class. \r\nWeaknesses: I'm not exactly sure how to accomplish this, but I think we could've been a little more focused and grounded in the text during our discussions. I'm a fan of tangents but sometimes it felt like people were holding their point in mind and waiting for their turn instead of responding to things people said before them. It was mostly fine because people had good points to make, but it would've been nice to structure our discussions to work through one thing at a time. Otherwise, I think this class is extremely well-structured.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627740\n 1627740\n \n 1627740\n One part of the class that was both a strength and a weakness was the somewhat freeform structure of our seminar discussions. Professor Peters definitely came to the class with ideas about elements of the texts that he wanted us to discuss, but the majority of the seminar was guided by the students and passages that we brought to class. In general, this free flowing format was generative of really fascinating conversation. On the other hand, discussions sometimes got off track, fell into a bit of a lull, and we sometimes drifted a bit far from the text or were all over the place. However, this was pretty rare. All in all, Professor Peters always shaped the classes in a meaningful way without being overbearing and telling us what to think which allowed for engaging and fun classes.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1635265\n 1635265\n \n 1635265\n I really loved the structure of class. We engaged in really natural, organic discussions. I also loved the selection of literature. I would have liked to spend more time on each book, however.

In general, having heard from friends in other sections, I do think there is some extreme disparity in quality of the class based on section.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1636352\n 1636352\n \n 1636352\n Many strengths -- great readings, discussions were organised, well-led, and let us come up with our own direction of conversation, fun writing assignments, good workload. \r\nWeaknesses -- Though I was really engaged in the curriculum, and loved all the texts we read, I would really like to read some more classic novels we only read Moby Dick. I get that there's just not that much time in one semester, but I would almost have preferred to not cover as much from each text in order to get more exposure. Moreover, I understand in this era, there is a push to read more diverse texts, and I don't want to detract from that. Rather, I would like to put the texts in conversation with each other. As someone experiencing American Literature for the first time, I would like to Twain or Hemmingway as well.
