RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525571\n 1525571\n \n 1525571\n The strengths of this course were Prof. Arkolakis and his careful explanations of the content. I also enjoyed being able to experiment in QGIS. I would say the only weaknesses of the course are the fact that problem sets and exams can be very difficult and there was not much review material provided in advance of the final exam.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532029\n 1532029\n \n 1532029\n The variety in topics covering history, theory and analysis makes it one of the most well-rounded economics courses at Yale.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532030\n 1532030\n \n 1532030\n The course covers a lot of different topics, and it's interesting to go from historical examples to the development of economic models.\r\n\r\nI did not like the fact that each problem set was 10% of the overall grade. Getting a single part of a single problem wrong resulted in a 10 point deduction, equivalent to one whole percentage point overall. More (smaller) problem sets would have been preferred, rather than only 3 problem sets.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532034\n 1532034\n \n 1532034\n A strength is that the lectures are very organized.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532061\n 1532061\n \n 1532061\n Strengths: Applied economic models to a variety of interesting questions and although the models were simple I did feel like they were worth considering as models of what was going on in the real world; Arkolakis is clearly an expert in the area and does a great job of simplifying what could be complex ideas into easily understandable pieces; PSETS do a good job of helping you better understand the models by solving key components of them; Exams were fair and studying for the exams really did help me learn the material in a deeper way, which I think is a big plus. Weaknesses: Sometimes I lost the thread of the course in that I would get lost in the details of a particular lecture/problem without recognizing how it fit into the overall picture of which model we were using and exactly how we arrived at that point. I think this is an area which could be readily improved upon by just mentioning what model is being used to at each stage and how the problems relate to issues we have previously considered; Felt like PSETS, though helpful, sometimes (once or twice) had problems with just annoying algebra which didn't provide any very deep understanding. My guess would be that some other problems would still be equally challenging but provide a more general understanding of how those models were functioning.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532068\n 1532068\n \n 1532068\n Weaknesses: though the models we started learning were relatively simple and seemed intuitive to understand and think about how different shocks change the result, the models quickly became more and more complex and unintelligible. It was extremely hard to see how the models connected to the real world, and when we did talk about real world examples they seemed very disconnected/unrelated to the models themselves. Much of the class is just various algebraic manipulations/derivations of various of the model equations, which made it feel more like algebra/calculus (with literally never any actual numbers, just variables) than an econ class seeking to model actual human behavior. Both the class slides and hw solutions skipped numerous steps and explanations for these derivations, so we were often left guessing/checking/rearranging to try to figure out how the equations were achieved, and even then having no idea why. HW and exam feedback was also extremely minimal and hard to understand. In the end the class was extremely theoretical and for that reason I found it extremely hard to follow and pay attention.\r\n\r\nStrengths: it wasn't that much work I guess, hw grading was pretty lenient, when we looked at real urbanization and space data that actually seemed relevant to the world, the small bit of GIS we did was interesting (would have been nice if we were actually taught how to use the software rather than just answer a couple specific pset questions), guest lecturers were an interesting connection to other research.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532283\n 1532283\n \n 1532283\n Strength: I like the material covered in this course. Prof Arkolakis explains the ideas in a really clear way. By the end of this semester, I think I get some insights on not only the mathematical modeling (which is the core of the course), but also general concepts that affects the economic performance from the spatial perspective. Moreover, the guest lectures are really insightful. I was excited to see the applications of our models in active research. Also the workload is relatively low (a huge plus during course selection).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532331\n 1532331\n \n 1532331\n Strength: Good professor, enough content for the semester; Weakness: Difficult
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532400\n 1532400\n \n 1532400\n I didn't go into this course thinking it would be as theory heavy as it was, but perhaps that is my own fault. I have data science background, so I really enjoyed the discussions on say the Fogel paper or the interstate highway counterfactual. I really liked the GIS portions as well, I wish they were more than a sideshow and we did something substantive with it. I think all the theory portion before midterm was great but past then I became lost and bored with it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535358\n 1535358\n \n 1535358\n Strengths: workload is manageable and the problem sets really help me to understand the course material and different models.\r\nWeakness: lectures could be more engaging. Also, this course involves a decent amount of math, but I feel that some math part is made to be that complex (more complex than it could be).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541441\n 1541441\n \n 1541441\n A strength of this course is the content; it is very engaging and interesting to learn about. A weakness of the course is the memorization necessary; the exams are really just how much you can regurgitate, which doesn't seem to facilitate learning very well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1556507\n 1556507\n \n 1556507\n The workload of the course is very fair--3 psets over the semester, one midterm, and one final. The material tested on all is also very fair. I appreciated that the exam covered only material that we explicitly covered in lectures. Overall, an excellent class.
