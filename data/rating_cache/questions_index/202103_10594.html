\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1526654\n 1526654\n \n 1526654\n I really enjoyed the beginning of the course, and how it was structured as a "singing school." I thought that was fun, and I felt like I was gradually learning more about rhythm and sight-reading. I liked how we weren't focusing on Western standard notation, because I'd never seen that before. The system Professor Quinn created was also interesting. We weren't learning traditional music theory, but based on what the instructors were telling me, I believed it would all come together at the end of the course, and we would learn standard music notation. However, it never really came together. I felt like we spent the majority of the time sight-singing, and only really touched standard music notation during the last 4-ish classes. It felt like a total 180 from what we had been doing, and I found it really hard to keep up with the music jargon. I felt I came into this course wanting to learn standard music notation, and just be able to understand more about music, and I don't think this class helped me fully achieve that. I also felt that even though this class is meant for those with little to no experience with music, many people in the class actually had a lot of experience, which made discussion sections hard to follow, since people were using advanced terms I had not heard before. Overall, I think this course could be improved by having more emphasis on music notation and integrating standard music notation more with the system Professor Quinn created.
