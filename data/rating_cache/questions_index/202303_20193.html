RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1704301\n 1704301\n \n 1704301\n This course was structured really well. I liked the way we would read a real Supreme Court opinion each week along with a CRT rewrite. The pairing was really helpful. I also really liked our circle discussions every class. It not only made sure I spoke but because it was something I knew about, it helped me engage more deeply with the readings to be prepared. I also liked the way Professor Vinson would frame our discussions at the start of each class, asking which ones were the most insightful and which ones raised the most questions. All of this structure was really helpful to my ability to grasp the material. I cannot think of any weaknesses.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706817\n 1706817\n \n 1706817\n I loved this class. Professor Vinson's teaching was absolutely a strength of the course. Her teaching style was engaging, and her background as both a Yale alumna and someone who worked at the Equal Justice Initiative brought so much to the course. She provided opportunities to engage with and "participate," in the course beyond just speaking up in class, whether through discussion posts, discussions with classmates. The format of the course was another strength; every class began with a short lecture on some of the historical background or relevant themes from the readings from that week. We would then have a circle discussion where each student would share a takeaway from the readings or lecture. Every class included a ten-minute break, and it kept class engagement high throughout each session. The content was so relevant to our current political world, and I couldn't have taken this class at a better time.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710547\n 1710547\n \n 1710547\n Strengths: Very organized. Intellectually stimulating. The readings are always perfect compliments of each other. The cases we read are so important, and we each are provided the skills and context to analyze each of them in depth. The lectures are riveting. Discussion flows naturally. The assignments we are given are intentional and fresh.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I wish it was listed under the African American Studies department, and it should probably be a required course for the major too. It is actually the perfect class
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723533\n 1723533\n \n 1723533\n A huge strength of the course was the syllabus and the content if you plan to go to law school, these are concepts that are not only super engaging but that are really important to engage with (that often arent typically centered)CRT, Constitutional Abolitionism, etc. Professor Vinson really does make her class feel like a safe space for students. The course was also really well structured. Its apparent that Professor Vinson is super intentional about how she plans the course which really helps students. Assignments are paced so you dont get too overwhelmed and they build upon skills as you learn them, so you get plenty of exposure and practice before writing each assignment. Its definitely a solid amount of writing but Professor Vinson provides a lot of support and wants you to succeed in her class. \r\nThe class is also a good balance of lecture and discussion. The lecture is multimedia at times which is great and where Professor Vinson breaks down the readings and provides a basis of information and background to allow for richer discussion. The discussion is prompted by Professor Vinson and by student questions and is a great way to hear and learn from students while getting to voice thoughts you have. Its a really good balance especially in a course where many students dont have a lot of exposure to legal classes (there arent that many at Yale). So students arent thrown into the deep end, yet you still get to have really interesting and informed discussion.
