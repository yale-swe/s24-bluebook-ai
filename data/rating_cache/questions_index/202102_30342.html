RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512459\n 1512459\n \n 1512459\n Good overview of all the different topics in heat transfer, but could be helped with a little more depth in practical applications e.g. an assignment or class activity to design a fin or cooling system for a real application. Definitely helped me understand the principles behind thermal engineering design.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512562\n 1512562\n \n 1512562\n Strengths: good balance of introduction to theory and working out of examples. The lessons prepare you very well for the homework and give you the knowledge you'll need to fully explore the textbook in your own time (it has useful stuff!). \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: With Doc A, weaknesses are hard to come by. The distribution of work was a little wonky, but primarily because of the summer format. With the Tu/Th schedule, any homework due Th was crammed compared to the excess of time for Tu homework, so possibly releasing assignments a bit ahead of time so the Th HW could be worked on during the long weekend as well. The class flowed differently than the initial syllabus, but honestly this is a strength because Doc A recognized the needs of the class and adjusted pacing to work with us where appropriate. If the class weren't as small and communicative amongst each other and with the professor I can see where a lack of stricter organization could maybe cause issues - or just more emails than needed into Doc A's inbox.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513007\n 1513007\n \n 1513007\n The course flowed in a logical order and built upon itself well. I have no critiques and don't have any suggested improvements.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513113\n 1513113\n \n 1513113\n I really enjoyed how this class was taught. I really tried to think of ways to improve this class but couldnt think of any.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1513318\n 1513318\n \n 1513318\n Strengths: The concepts were made very clear and the applications felt relevant to potential careers in industry \r\nWeakness: The speed of the course (by nature of being a summer class) made some of the derivations unclear
