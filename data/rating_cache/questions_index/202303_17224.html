\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1694136\n 1694136\n \n 1694136\n No, even though Professor Rushmeier is a very kind and helpful person, and I respect her greatly, I cannot in good conscience recommend this class. To be honest, I came into this class expecting it to be a gut. It isn't, so if you are looking for a gut, this is wrong place to go to. Each visualization assignment takes a long time to do, and the two midterms require fact-based recall of at least 100 entries of facts each, which is another time sink. It would be justified if these concepts were actually relevant. They are not. They are very abstract, academic theories about data visualization that might be useful if you are interested in doing meta-research about visualization, but are not useful for any kind of practical applications. To give a specific instance (to illustrate the absurdity of some of the facts we were asked to remember), we were asked to recall the name of a person (who is not even particularly famous) who created a specific visualization at a specific event in the twentieth century. I don't understand why remembering facts like these is conducive to anyone's learning. As another example, we were asked to recall what three guest lecturers each specifically talked about in their presentations. It almost feels like the class is structured around taking attendance, such that the purpose of the exam is also to check attendance. Indeed, on each lecture attendance is taken, and you cannot even miss more than 4 lectures even if many of them you will find convey little meaningful information. Data visualization is great, but you should learn it elsewhere. Please do not take this class.
