\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1520997\n 1520997\n \n 1520997\n I strongly disliked the class format. We had to watch 1-3 hours of lecture per week, and we had 2.5 hours of in class "group work" time. I felt that the lectures were often confusing, and it was hard not being able to ask questions (and it was also less engaging). The in-class time felt pretty useless -- we would have 30 second discussions about questions about topics that had been barely touched on in the lecture videos, and the TFs would end up having to explain most of it to us. We would do problems at whiteboards, but then we wouldn't have those problems in our notes, and when problems like those appeared on Problem Sets or exams and people were confused about them, Professor Faison would just say "we did one like that on the whiteboard." Overall, class felt like a waste of time, and I think the class would be much improved if in-class time was reserved for lectures, especially if the lecture included maybe a couple opportunities for group discussion or questions. The main strength of this class, in my opinion, is the TFs. All of our TFs were super helpful and very patient in explaining the concepts -- in class, in section, and over email. I wouldn't have made it through the class without them!
