Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1548311\n 1548311\n \n 1548311\n The readings of the course, particularly in Diversit, seemed to repeat themselves; although immigration is one of the most important themes in understanding French culture, I wouldve loved to see a wider variety of texts, or even movies or other productions, portraying the phenomenon. Un sac de billes was by far the most interesting reading we had, and the Second World War presentations were a great addition.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550588\n 1550588\n \n 1550588\n The course definitely compiled as much reading, grammar, and conversational practice into one semester as possible, which I think helped me improve in all ways, but sometimes the curriculum felt jumpy and disjointed as topics did not really relate to each other. Some of the course material also felt heavily outdated which we discussed in class. While I enjoyed La Symphonie Pastorale and some of the films we watched, I cannot help but think there is much more current, engaging, and enriching reading material to replace it with.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1552922\n 1552922\n \n 1552922\n Strengths was our absolutely amazing reading and the interesting discussions that could be brought from it. Weaknessvocabulary expansion was not emphasized much in class. There was a lot of self-study expected of you.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553202\n 1553202\n \n 1553202\n We often ran out of time to correct or discuss assignments, and I think this was due to the fact that the homework workload was too high. I don't think that certain activities were necessary but we did them anyways because they were in the course packet. I think it may be better with less adherence to the course packet and more room to talk about other topics. This course could also benefit from a more diverse range of novels.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553553\n 1553553\n \n 1553553\n I enjoyed the layout of the course. I thought the workload allowed me to keep my engagement with the language at a high level. I think the course could have incorporated more opportunity for students to discuss between one another.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554548\n 1554548\n \n 1554548\n The group activities we performed in class worked very well for practicing the grammar concepts and vocabulary (provided that everyone arrived to class prepared having done the homework as a preview). The written grammar exercises were in general appropriate, but were at times repetitive, to where it sometimes felt like you were practicing the same sentence over and over again with only a few words changed in between. I think in general the array of difficulties (in terms of syntax and vocabulary) provided by our course readings was appropriate, but at times their subject matter were concerning. The composition prompts about the course readings were thought provoking, and it was usually possible to find at least one prompt that you would want to write about.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555655\n 1555655\n \n 1555655\n The literature in the course was somewhat troubling. In past semesters, it can be concluded that dark or more difficult readings would be chosen as the course difficulty increased, however, some of the literature chosen did not seem the most appropriate as it did not make students want to participate. Also, there is a film that students must watch that does not contain a trigger warning. With the growing interest in mental health, the literature and film choices should be reviewed, and trigger warnings should be announced to accommodate.
