RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1699476\n 1699476\n \n 1699476\n The strength is Shan. Shan pushed me to become a better student every week. From her facilitation, I have grown so much as a student. Specifically, I have applied the knowledge and statistical skills acquired throughout the GLBL major requirements and integrated them into the project's research process. I have also developed essential career skills like project management, teamwork, and effective communication. Lastly, it has allowed me to think more critically about the global health sphere, specifically TB, and how to creatively develop solutions for problems surrounding the disease, primarily for people in low-to-middle-income countries. Very very meaningful work! The only weakness I can think of is the teamwork nature of the class. Since it is a group capstone, some people will put more effort into it than others, which can sometimes be frustrating, especially around crunch time.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1701039\n 1701039\n \n 1701039\n The professor provided strong feedback and kept us all engaged from early on, I just wish grading schematics would have been made clear from the beginning, also given that the time required to be put into this capstone was greater than that of all other capstones in global affairs.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1718429\n 1718429\n \n 1718429\n Strengths: Dr. Soe-Lin by a mile. She is so dedicated to making this capstone a meaningful learning experience for all of her students. The guidance, structure, and discipline she brings to the semester-long process are so invaluable. She also brings us lovely pastries for class, huge bonus. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: It will be difficult if you're not a morning person; sometimes, we would have very early group meetings outside of class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722168\n 1722168\n \n 1722168\n The biggest strength of this course is Professor Soe-Lin, who is entirely focused on building your skills and building you up as a potential leader. She did a fantastic job of setting us up for success from the beginning, providing space to check in and solicit bi-directional feedback, and leveraging her connections and talents to obtain access to leaders in the field whom we could learn from. She created a great environment for our team to work together productively and bond. Know that this project will take an immense amount of time if you are doing it right. There may be multiple decks and publications a week due, multiple meetings outside of class, and an overall aggressive timeline to getting the bulk of the work done. There were a few bumps along the way, including a learning curve on mutual expectations regarding what was expected at each meeting, and figuring out how to best present our work as both individuals and a team. But, it was an extremely rewarding experience that I would recommend to anyone even remotely interested in public policy, global health, and the non-profit/IGO space.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1699476\n 1699476\n \n 1699476\n YES YES YES. As a senior, I could not have asked for a better academic experience to encapsulate my Global Affairs major. From writing papers for publication, having the opportunity to interview global health scholars from all over the world, and briefing our clients and classmates on policy recommendations and data findings, I have improved so much as a communicator, a writer, and, most importantly, a student. Ultimately, compared to other capstone groups, our group has the most well-thought-out research with concrete quantitative data models to defend our tangible policy recommendations. I'm walking away having learned not just about the topics that we touched on within our capstone research, but about public policy, consulting, and leadership as a whole. Make sure that you are able to give the project the time that it deserves, as it is truly a special experience.
