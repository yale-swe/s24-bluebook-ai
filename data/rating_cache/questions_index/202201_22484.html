We began the course with electrostatics and electrodynamics, discussing coulomb's and Gauss's law, as well as how to understand the concept of potential, since the electric field is conservative in this case. We then progressed to magnetostatics and learned about the formulas/laws that govern how an infinitesimal piece of wire creates a magnetic field, as well as how moving charges experiences forces due to magnetic fields. We then introduced time dependence and learned about the relationship between current, magnetic field, flux, and induced emf, finally arriving at the complete list of Maxwell's (corrected) equations. \r\nIn the last few weeks of the course, these equations were used in lecture to derive the wave equations, and to understand light as a self-propagating EM wave. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1596864\n 1596864\n \n 1596864\n Through taking this course I learned about the fundamentals of electrostatics such as Coulomb's Law, dipole interactions, Electric fields, electric potential, Gauss's Law and its applications, Magnetic fields, and Ampre's Law and its applications. We discovered the effect different combinations of resistors, capacitors, and inductors have on the behavior of current, voltage, and charge over time in DC and AC circuits. In this unit I learned how to use complex exponentials to model sinusoidal functions and solve difficult differential equations. I also learned how to use phasor domain to understand the relationship between the frequencies and phase shifts of current, voltage, and charge functions in both the complex plane and real space. We derived in class Maxwell's equations in integral and differential form. From these equations, we determined the wave equations for electromagnetism describing the wave-like behavior of light. We then studied the particle nature of light through the quantization of properties such as energy and momentum. From this, we then dove into quantum mechanics, studying wave functions of definite state of momentum using the complex analysis techniques learned in our unit on circuits. I'd say my biggest insights from this course were a deeper understanding of the fundamental connection between electricity, magnetism, and light, and how the properties of these fields influence the foundations of quantum mechanics. The mathematical tools I learned and developed further in this class techniques to solve ordinary linear differential equations, surface and line integrals, and phasor domain were incredibly interesting and will definitely be useful in future physics, engineering, and math classes.
