I usually prefer this course format to one where there are no clearly defined lesson topics or units. A weakness is that the course sections are all taught by different professors, which can mean that the teaching is not fully standardized. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536752\n 1536752\n \n 1536752\n The problem sets were helpful and the extensive time that peer tutors were available was also great. Another strength were the concept checks and practice exams before each exam. A weakness in the course was that the problem sets were always due on Wednesday while some classes meet on a Monday Wednesday schedule and some on Tuesday Thursday. This meant some classes were teaching information on Thursday after the relevant problem set was due. This was not an effective learning strategy because students had to teach themselves or rely on the peer tutors to teach new information when that should be the instructors job. This would be improved by making the problem sets due on Thursday nights. Also, another weakness would be the fact that there are many different sections with different instructors but all students take the same tests. Then, the exams dont seem to test whether or not we learned the material, but whether or not our teachers taught the right material. Maybe a solution would be to provide clearer information in the umbrella course tab about the expected knowledge going into each test.
