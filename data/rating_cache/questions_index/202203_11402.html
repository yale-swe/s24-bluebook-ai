\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625361\n 1625361\n \n 1625361\n I liked how most of the work we did for the class was done during class time. I think in the beginning some small tasks to more expand the types of drawing we did could have been nice.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1628451\n 1628451\n \n 1628451\n Fantastic engagement, great comments from the instructor and TF. You really improve and it's wonderful to get to see 19 other people improving alongside you.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1630315\n 1630315\n \n 1630315\n The weaknesses truly depend on what you wanted to get out of this course. If you wanted to learn basic drawing skills from scratch, this is not the class for you. The target demographic for this course is someone who is artistically inclined, which is not to mean they are good at art. But at least someone that would be open to learning about art and is receptive to the values the professor is trying to teach. This class truly teaches you how to mold your existing skills into something more, something you probably wouldn't have even thought of. You get to develop some of those skills, but that isn't the point. The point is to incorporate the feedback and to change your way of thinking about art. And that is exactly what it did for me. I am grateful that it was like this because you can learn to draw anywhere, but there are only a few places when you can learn to think like an artist and how to incorporate it in your life. Another strength is that you have the opportunity to try various media and are given the space to truly let your creativity run wild. I would find myself sitting in my room just thinking what I wanted to do for my next project, planning it out in my head, and that was honestly my favorite part, giving myself the dedicated time to be creative.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1631760\n 1631760\n \n 1631760\n The first two units (still life and figure drawing) were really well organized to help facilitate our learning and also help us experiment with different materials. The feedback we received was clear and consistent, helping us discover our styles and explore different approaches. The last two units (collage and our final project) were interesting and good but I wish we had a little more structure (perhaps smaller portions of each class to do more in-class drawing to keep up the routines).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1632099\n 1632099\n \n 1632099\n Strengths: very flexible and open, able to develop projects in any direction; supportive teaching; loved Alex, our TA, as well as the field trips to the YUAG and the Yale British Art Gallery\r\nWeaknesses: I would have appreciated a bit more technical instruction at the beginning (students came from different levels of experience, so at times, it felt like some techniques/ concepts were assumed knowledge)
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1638218\n 1638218\n \n 1638218\n I really liked the class but felt a bit confused why we did collage instead of drawing with ink like other classes. Also I wouldve loved more discussion on anatomy and figure drawing focused more on the body than the clothes

Of course, some of the other students in the class will be crazy talented, but I think Matt does a good job of leveling the playing field by encouraging experienced artists out of their comfort zone, so that the class is engaging for everyone. In terms of workload, the class is pretty chill, and you usually get time in class to work on projects so you don't need to spend a ton of time outside of class if you're making good use of that time.
