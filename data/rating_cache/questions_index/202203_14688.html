RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614132\n 1614132\n \n 1614132\n Strengths: teaches you to write well and quickly, emphasizing values like the economy of language and getting to analyze fundamental texts throughout the Western canon. This propagated a greater interest and understanding in such topics for me.\r\nWeaknesses: Not much thoroughness with each of the texts, since there is such little time to cover each book. A lot of reading for each class as well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614168\n 1614168\n \n 1614168\n I really enjoyed this class, it was probably my favorite one. I think the way the professor instructed the seminars was nearly perfect. She allowed us a lot of time to discuss and bounce ideas off each other while still taking time to explain the stories and move us along.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614189\n 1614189\n \n 1614189\n A key strength of the course I appreciated was that there was a clear logic to why certain texts were chosen; following the development of the 'Western canon' chronologically allowed me to see the connections between texts and traditions, as well as how successive texts built on one another. A weakness is that there might have been too much time spent on particular texts, such as the Aeneid or the Gospel of Matthew, that could have been used instead to cover other texts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614242\n 1614242\n \n 1614242\n I think that the strengths and weakness are entwined. Each text in the enormous breadth of literature covered this semester was fascinating, and as a whole, the course structure gave me an intimate look at a certain, but certainly not the only, literary tradition. On the other hand, I felt like I would equally enjoy a course in which we read and analyze any one of these works in greater depth.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614383\n 1614383\n \n 1614383\n I really loved the discussion sections and how Professor Valdivieso facilitated those, as well as her writing feedback. I just wish we didn't have to move quite so fast, only taking 2 days for Dante's Inferno was really tough for example.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616700\n 1616700\n \n 1616700\n Overall I was extremely happy with this course. Sometimes I wished we would have had the literature lecture before we started reading the texts, or at least earlier in the reading process, since they enriched the reading experience.\r\n\r\nOur class discussions were extremely interesting and well-moderated by professor Valdivieso, in a way that we felt free to talk about what had interested us the most, but at the same time were guided through the reading process and through how to interact meaningfully with the texts. Professor Valdivieso made me feel well-prepared for writing my papers as well as my final exam. Her regular weekly assignments ensured that at least most of the class stayed on top of the reading material (since we had to submit something related to its content for every class), which I am convinced made our discussions more lively and interesting. \r\n\r\nSomething I thought would be a bigger part of DS, however, is an ongoing discussion on the legacy of the text we read, within the literary tradition but also regarding how the narratives may have inspired or been used to justify actions and practices, for example within the context of colonialism or environmental destruction. We did address this in class from time to time, but if there would be a way to incorporate more contemporary readings of the texts into the program in a more systematic way I am convinced a lot of students would be very positive towards that.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627350\n 1627350\n \n 1627350\n Strengths:\r\nEngaging, easy to discussion sections that included a variety of activities. Fascinating lectures that effectively framed the texts we read. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses:\r\nSometimes felt like we had a few too many time consuming discussion posts so maybe class could be improved by cutting down on the number of required discussion posts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629692\n 1629692\n \n 1629692\n It really does expose you to a breadth of knowledge that is useful for understanding other things later on; for example, when I was reading someone's thesis on rage in classical texts, so many of the characters and referenced books made sense because I had read them before. However, the course went by way too fast and I had a difficult time processing everything and I feel like I will have to go back and reread them in order to truly understand what was happening.
