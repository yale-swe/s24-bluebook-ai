\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1564159\n 1564159\n \n 1564159\n Strengths: Excellent textbook, clear and well-prepared-for lectures, plenty of office hours, challenging but not tedious problem sets, fair exams.\r\nWeakness 1: There were zerp demos during lecture. A physics class, especially one covering electrodynamics, should always have demos, which help with the problem-solving intuition immensely. This isn't unique to this class, however, but Yale Physics as a whole - the departmental attitude seems to be that demos are reserved for Physics Lab, and lecture should purely be about derivations. I disagree with this apparent mentality.\r\nWeakness 2: Griffiths himself writes in his textbook that a one-semester course has its hands full with the first seven chapters of the textbook; we try to cover all of it, and made it through chapter 10 and into 11. This meant a more rushed treatment of chapters 4 and 6, and not enough time to get used to the sudden ramp-up in algebra starting with chapter 9.
