\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1699656\n 1699656\n \n 1699656\n A keystone to the mechanical engineering curriculum, Strength & Deformation to Mechanical Elements builds off of one's understanding of classical physics to real engineering problems. The beginning of the course feels like any physics course, but once you begin analyzing trusses, you build off these concepts to discover stresses on an object, deformation due to axial and torsion loads, and beam selection/deformation. The course covers 16-17 chapters of R.C. Hibbeler's textbook over the course of a semester, which are key to future Mechanical Engineering courses such as Manufacturing, which expects one to know these concepts on the first day. The foundation is set well by the problem sets and exams.
