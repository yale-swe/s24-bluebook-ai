\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1512388\n 1512388\n \n 1512388\n The strengths were definitely the clarity and organization of the course. It was all very thoughtful and easy to follow which enabled us to focus more on the truly difficult and confusing ethical issues being raised instead of wasting time on unclear logistics or course expectations. Additionally, the instructors made themselves available for discussion outside of class with office hours and gave very thoughtful feedback on nearly every assignment, no matter how "small." Of course, this is made easier and is more common in a seminar/discussion based class; but I felt that more so than other similar classes I've taken, the instructors were generous, constructive, and attentive with their feedback. All of this gave me the feeling that the instructors were invested in my learning and understanding of the material which motivated me, and I believe the other participants, to be even more engaged during class discussions. Another thing that I found helpful was how dynamic the course was. On Zoom, it is easy for it to feel endless and tiring but switching between lectures, group discussions, and smaller breakout rooms made it easier to stay focused for the rather long 3 hour summer session class period.
