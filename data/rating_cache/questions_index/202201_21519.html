RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1571492\n 1571492\n \n 1571492\n Strength - good people & feedback\r\nWeakness - repetitive structure and feedback
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1571668\n 1571668\n \n 1571668\n Strengths: Professor Cunningham is a very good workshop facilitator. He also offers in depth comments both in workshop, office hours, and in writing. You can tell how much he cares about each student's story; he devotes a lot of time to reviewing our work.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: We didn't really learn much about how to structure a story, develop characters, write dialogue, etc.; we were treated as if we already knew that (which is maybe part of the "Advanced" nature of the course?) But more guidance on this at the beginning of the semester, before we started crafting our own stories, would have been helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1571861\n 1571861\n \n 1571861\n Both a strength and weakness of the course is that you spend the whole semester revising one story over three revisions. Three revisions was really useful in terms of coming out of the class with a story that I was proud of. Your classmates and prof also really know your story well and will give great feedback. The downside is that you might feel stuck if your initial idea that you came up with in the beginning of the semester ends up not being the story you want to write. That being said, this class encourages drastic revisions. Another downside is that it gets to feel a little repetitive as you have to read the same stories by your classmates over and over. But inspiring to see how they develop!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575674\n 1575674\n \n 1575674\n I like how we need to write three drafts; it is very unique for a writing class, and it feels very satisfying to polish a story to its perfection. I do feel like the pace towards the end of the term can be made faster, and I wish we can have clearer guidelines on what to write when we line-edit and comment on each others' works. Because some of the comments are very detailed and useful, while some of the other comments are just check marks near underlined sentences (and makes me wonder if the two are graded the same way as long as they turn them in).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575751\n 1575751\n \n 1575751\n The strength of this course is the professor and the quality of other students in the class -- their writing and their discussions in workshop. Every discussion was considerate and structured to help each writer improve.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1587600\n 1587600\n \n 1587600\n No weaknesses. Great course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1590253\n 1590253\n \n 1590253\n Strengths: LOVED the classroom environment, all the students were so great and being in small groups from the start made us really close with one another and the classes really comfortable. It was super supportive, everyone was so great and offered respectful, kind feedback.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Much higher workload than I expected- line edits and a page-long letter to three workshoppers every single week (must be completed between monday night and thursday morning) for 20 pg long stories. Reading the same story three times from everyone got a bit repetitive.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593594\n 1593594\n \n 1593594\n Strengths: The workshop setting was immensely helpful, and Professor Cunningham is a superb asset.\r\nWeaknesses: Running through three drafts of the same twelve people's stories was quite exhausting, I felt at times like I couldn't think of much to say after the first two drafts.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1599419\n 1599419\n \n 1599419\n It is very engaging and supportive.
