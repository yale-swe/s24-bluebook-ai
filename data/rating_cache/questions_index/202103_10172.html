RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1520920\n 1520920\n \n 1520920\n Sekhar is the strength, he is very friendly, approachable, and a great lecturer. I appreciate that he was always willing to ask us whether we wanted to see a particular proof.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527310\n 1527310\n \n 1527310\n Strenghts: Sekhar is a very good lecturer. He proves everything rigorously. He is very kind as well and clearly cares about his students. Also, for a math class, psets were relatively short. Weaknesses: the material was generally boring until martingales, which I feel like we could have done a lot more of. For example, the proof of the Kolmogorov SLLN took like 2 whole classes and was a big yawn. It would be much more effective if we could get into more stochastic things. Another thing to cut might be some of the CLT proofs at the end of class. We spent some time doing a bunch of proofs, but again, it would have been more fun to learn about more martingale stuff. There were other things we proved that took like most of a class but were just kind of pointless. Finally, I wish the professor would relate this measure-theoretic stuff to basic probability more. For example, conditioning was really hard to understand at first (especially Kolmogorov conditional expectation: very scary), but after I reread my probability notes from last year, I started to put the pieces together for myself to see how everything connected.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1529257\n 1529257\n \n 1529257\n Strengths - a lot of material is covered, and it's the kind of stuff you need to see if you're going to do advanced work. Sekhar is incredibly knowledgeable and explains the material very well. Weaknesses - there's just so much material, and it's almost impossible to follow everything.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530657\n 1530657\n \n 1530657\n Strengths: First, the problem sets were very well-thought-out and the final project was a great opportunity to dig deeper and acutely understand the course material. Also, Sekhar was clearly very passionate about the subject and was always willing to meet with and help students. \r\nWeaknesses: I wish this course could have been split into two. I understand that there is a lot of cover in the very short span of a semester, but it really compromises students' understanding. This is not Sekhar's fault but rather the fault of the MATH and S&DS departments. Key concepts such as the Radon-Nikodym theorem are covered in a single lecture and barely mentioned ever again. This is the only thing I disliked about the course: it felt like you were running a marathon but could never slow down for water.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1533739\n 1533739\n \n 1533739\n Course is extremely well taught and interesting. Final project guidelines could be clearer.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1537248\n 1537248\n \n 1537248\n While this course covers very important material, the lectures tend to be a bit dry, since a lot of the proofs are very long and hard to follow all the way through.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543320\n 1543320\n \n 1543320\n Strengths: The format of problem sets and a final paper with no exams is a nice change of pace from many other math courses. Breaking down proofs from an actual published paper in a field of your own interest is a very important skill and I am a bit surprised that it is not emphasized as much in other courses.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Course moves way too fast. Students are a mix of people who have taken 300 (which doesn't exist anymore) and 305. Those who have taken 305 feel like the first half of the course is too much review, while the second half goes too quickly and could be expanded to fill the whole semester. I had only taken 300 and felt pretty lost for the first few weeks, but then I got used to the pace. I still feel that I learned more about measure theory (which is very useful but not really the focus of the course) than probability theory.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546356\n 1546356\n \n 1546356\n This course is hard and fast, and Sekhar did a great job of squeezing so much content into so little time. I don't really have any advice to offer on how this course could be improved.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550254\n 1550254\n \n 1550254\n Professor was good at explaining concepts, problem sets could have corresponded better to the pace of what we were covering in class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1551762\n 1551762\n \n 1551762\n Strengths. \r\nProblem sets. These were really informative and a great way to let the material sink in.\r\nSekhar. Hes awesome.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses.\r\nClass pace. This was way too fast. I would have learned so much more if the pace of the class had been about 20% slower, with more examples of how the results we studied apply to the real world of MATH 241. \r\n\r\nLack of exams. It pains me to say this, because math exams are often really stressful, but this course would be better with a midterm or two. Otherwise there is no incentive to review past material, making it very easy to forget results weve already proved. This makes lectures even harder to follow.\r\n\r\nDisconnect between pset and class material.\r\nPsets were way easier than lectures.
