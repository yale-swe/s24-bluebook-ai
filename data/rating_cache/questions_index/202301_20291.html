RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657072\n 1657072\n \n 1657072\n Strengths--Marta is one of the best and most dedicated professors at Yale. Weaknesses--basically everything else. This is a course that was passed down to Marta, and you can kind of tell that this is fundamentally a pretty flawed class. "World cinema" as a concept is one that can really only be critiqued, which we did do in the class, but not nearly enough. I wish we had continued to come back to the concept as one to consider and think about. One of the reasons "world cinema" is a flawed concept is because how can one teach the entire world? This is the largest problem with the course--because nobody in the class, including the professor, is actually an expert in say, Indonesian documentary, the class basically became each student (of which there were 21 in a seminar) latching on to other concepts they were familiar with, which of course other students were not equally familiar with.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657280\n 1657280\n \n 1657280\n Selection of films is wonderful, seminar style facilitates good discussion. Because it covers such a broad range of films, it can sometimes feel like it lacks depth in certain topics, but the breadth and diversity of films makes up for that
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1658834\n 1658834\n \n 1658834\n At the beginning of the course, more information could be given for how to approach film analysis, especially if it's from an unfamiliar context.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1665572\n 1665572\n \n 1665572\n Strengths: Interesting films, good class analysis and discussion, great instruction. Loved the talks\r\n\r\nOnly weakness I would say is that it felt like this class was more suited to people who had prior knowledge in film analysis, world politics/history, and/or theory.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1666971\n 1666971\n \n 1666971\n I do think this is a super difficult course to teach because of how many regional cinemas are discussed, but I think there could have been more of a focus on the concept of world cinema generally and then more of a deep dive into a couple regions.
