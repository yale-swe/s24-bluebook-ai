\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521112\n 1521112\n \n 1521112\n I loved this course! Professor Wilford was extremely knowledgable and prepared for every class. She did a great job facilitating discussion. My favorite thing about this course was that Prof Wilford would also deeply consider students' questions and opinions and give them a well-thought-out response.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541627\n 1541627\n \n 1541627\n This course naturally requires a balance between historical and political thought, along with lecture and class discussion, and this balance was struck excellently throughout the semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1546643\n 1546643\n \n 1546643\n The reading list in this course is, for the most part, exhilarating. These historians are remarkably skillful writers and much of the reading is entertaining. However, the focus of the course is historical and political "thought." It often felt that the history itself was being glossed over, and we were instead going straight to the conclusions we glean from it. This meant that only a tiny fraction of the assigned reading would be focused on in section. In DS, it's impossible to read everything, and because only some of the reading actually felt "required" for this course, I would often skip/skim much of the H&P reading. I probably got as much out of the texts operating this way as I would have reading every word, but a lot of the joy of reading these works was lost. The skimming is almost unavoidable with such a massive reading list, but pointing us to an hour's worth (or so) of crucial reading within the assigned reading for each discussion would be helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1552373\n 1552373\n \n 1552373\n This course was great overall. The material was super interesting, Professor Wilfordss mini lectures at the beginning of each class were very helpful, and our class discussions were always very engaging. Shes a bit of a harsher grader than my other DS professors but shes lovely to talk to during office hours and will always offer helpful feedback.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555290\n 1555290\n \n 1555290\n Though I enjoyed this course overall, I felt that it really should be split into two courses: one for political science and one for history. It was sometimes difficult to figure out how to connect the political science texts to the history texts, and much of the content of the histories was overlooked in favor of discussing the political science texts. That is probably inevitable given how quickly we had to move through the texts, but it is my only major criticism of the class. Otherwise, it was wonderful, and I got a lot out of the discussions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555913\n 1555913\n \n 1555913\n I feel as though it hasn't quite found its footing yet, it can't tell if it's a history class or a literature class or something else entirely.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555924\n 1555924\n \n 1555924\n The books were really interesting and certainly all relevant and engaging. However, I only really understood the big picture in studying for the final. It would be nice if more time could be built into the reading schedule for consolidation and overarching reflection at the end, so as a class we could spend time synthesizing ideas. Also, the first half of class sometimes felt more like a lecture, and when we did have a discussion, we'd get off on some irrelevant tangent that not everyone would want to speak on. It would have been better if class time had been divided into sections where we decided from the class certain things would get discussed in each section so we wouldn't leave class feeling like we missed half the important content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1556222\n 1556222\n \n 1556222\n Strengths - It was very easy to contribute to the conversation. The professor spent part of the beginning of class setting the context for the works we were discussing, which made the class a lot less daunting\r\nWeakness - I felt like a lot of the H&P readings we did revolved around the same topics, which made discussions a little boring at times since some of the writers agree with each other on certain points.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1556524\n 1556524\n \n 1556524\n The curriculum of this course did feel a bit all over the place at times, as if we were trying to cram political science into things written before the field of political science existed. For example, reading Exodus with a focus on the system of government developed in the desert and the significance of the law codes was a bit odd to me. However I still really appreciated all the individual pieces of literature we read. I think for this course, even more than the other two DS courses, professor really mattered. I was so lucky to get Professor Wilford who helped guide our class and gave us common threads and themes to focus on throughout the semester, continually referring back to one or more themes and connecting works across the course to create a more focused and cohesive experience. \r\nI do wish the readings assigned for this class were a bit shorter. The length made some of the assignments feel somewhat impossible, causing me to lose motivation, and the vast majority of what we read was never even discussed in class. I think this class could be improved by shortening some of the assignments.
