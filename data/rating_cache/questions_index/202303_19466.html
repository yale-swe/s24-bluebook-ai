\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723315\n 1723315\n \n 1723315\n Independent project and generally good lecturer were a big plus. Really interesting readings. A weakness would be that Kortum could sometimes lose track of his own work on the board.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723877\n 1723877\n \n 1723877\n Strength: the final project. Our TA was amazing and so we got some really good guidance on how to adjust our project as we went along. I would have loved to have taken a course with a final project like this before starting my thesis, as preparation. The p sets are graded super easily and are good practice in drawing up and interpreting models a little. The class is very workload besides the final project.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Lectures were not engaging at all in my opinion. It was very dry material and the focus was on the details of the models we were looking at, and the notation and technicalities, rather than the big ideas. I didn't feel like we focused on the important concepts at all, or on almost any contemporary or real-world debates or problems. The closest thing was when we were learned about how a carbon tax works. Readings were also technical economic papers that did not focus on what the big environmental real world problems that I went into this class wanting to learn. The class was also basically designed in a way that made most lectures and readings completely detached from anything you would actually be graded on, so there was no incentive to push through because they were largely not interesting and not very important. So engagement was low for me.\r\n\r\nAnother weird thing about the class was the grading scheme. Most people got 100's on every assignment leading up to the final project, and then the final projects were graded on a curve that could leave people who got 100's on everything else with an A-, for example. Which I just think is an unfair and non-sensical way to grade a course. There could have been more differentiation earlier on if you are going to grade on a curve, so that you don't end up virtually curving people down.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723922\n 1723922\n \n 1723922\n Strengths: paper assignment built into the schedule for the semester with checkpoints and milestones throughout. Extremely well supported by an amazing TA. Professor Kortum teaches at a good pace and really explains everything out clearly and is patient with questions. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Course deadlines were still being experimented with as this was the first semester with built in paper milestones, but overall the teachers were flexible and understanding. I'm sure that the schedule for deadlines would be better next time as there is more feedback to work with now.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724588\n 1724588\n \n 1724588\n Strengths: Very interesting material, Prof Kortum is very supportive and accessible, and you get to research topics that you care about. Weaknesses: It was sometimes hard to figure out what the problem sets were looking for, and for this reason, they could feel inaccessible without attending office hours.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1725089\n 1725089\n \n 1725089\n I have a big problem the way this class was graded. Throughout the entire semester everyone I talked to in the course had all 100s on every assignment. This included the final paper check points and the problem sets. Then we learned the class was going to be curved (so only a certain percentage of students could get an A) which meant basically the final grades relied on the final paper grade and the last problem sets thats grades were never released. Having this type of grading and then adding a curve is ridiculous and unfair. Either grade without giving 100s across the board at the beginning or dont give a curve like that. This also made it quite impossible to have any sense of how you were doing throughout the semester, or to have much power over your grade in the end. This was one of the more ridiculously graded classes Ive seen. I also think the class should focus more on the bigger picture. I felt like most of the time was covering models from papers and graphical representations of different carbon taxes but that felt not as engaging when there was not as much to feel like we were going beyond theory. I felt like I mostly came out from the classes with micro economic theory. \r\n\r\nThe strength of this course was the final project. I thought the checkpoints were helpful to keep my team on track and we really learned all the steps involved in approaching a question and answering it through data and econometrics. Like I said above it would be a good exercise for someone before they write a thesis.
