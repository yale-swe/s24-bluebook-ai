\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1651508\n 1651508\n \n 1651508\n A huge strength of this class is Professor Soles. He brings a great energy, wealth of knowledge, and genuine excitement to class. He picked classic texts, some of which I had read before, and instilled them with totally new life. He also gives incredible feedback on essays! No weaknesses beyond the fact that it's just one semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1655045\n 1655045\n \n 1655045\n The topics we discussed and poets were read were terrific. On account of the thick syllabus and the class sometimes falling into long but fascinating digressions, however, it often felt rushed and like we weren't able to spend as much time as the poems required. Maybe next time there could be fewer poems or a 2x1.15-hour schedule would be better.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1660132\n 1660132\n \n 1660132\n This course was not for me. I like class discussions that naturally evolve and go in unexpected directions. Professor Soles knows exactly what he wants to tell the class before he has entered the classroom (and which Shyamalan-esque twist he will end with), which means that student comments are often only interruptions in the flow of what is basically a lecture. There is little room for students to disagree with any of Soles' views.\r\n\r\nThe poetry itself was interesting, although Blake's Milton was a misguided selection; it took Professor Soles two classes worth to even explain the poem and we were all still confused. This was unfortunate as we remained about two weeks behind for the remainder of the course, which meant our weekly discussion posts (which were not two weeks behind in subject matter) had no bearing on that week's class discussion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1666626\n 1666626\n \n 1666626\n Strengths: The course covers interesting material from a fantastic range of poets. The syllabus is great. The reading load for each week is very manageable. The discussion posts from each week are pretty laid back and allow you to experiment with your writing. I always looked forward to doing the readings each week because of the great selection of poets!\r\n\r\nWeakness: Professor Soles is great, but the class often felt like a high school English class. People were often participating for the sake of participating and not at all listening to each other or Professor Soles's questions. I would have loved it if Professor Soles controlled the conversation a bit more and prevented the class from going on too many non-related discussion points. This class may also be better suited as a more advanced seminar. In addition, we had a great syllabus, but it often felt like we never went into enough detail/depth on each piece. The whole class felt rushed and the analysis sometimes felt very superficial or over-extended. I would have loved to spend more time on each piece. Another issue with the class is that it has a large Bloom influence. Professor Soles talks about Harold Bloom almost every class. While Bloom was a major figure in literary criticism, the class felt very biased toward the "Bloom" perspective, and as a result, all the analysis we did in class felt a bit narrow and not very encompassing of the broader literary debate. It felt too much like regurgitating the western "canon." I think in the future, it would be great to include more secondary voices in the conversation about these poets (especially more about the pushback against Bloom's perspectives). Finally, a major problem with the class was that Professor Soles did not have enough time to devote to the class. Due to his busy teaching schedule, he had to delay two out of our three papers so that by the last week of class, we only had one graded assignment with feedback! I understand that his schedule was busy, but without feedback on more writing assignments, we could not improve our writing over the semester. I think I am not the only student to feel a bit frustrated with the pacing and organization of the course. While the idea for the class was great (and I love the syllabus!), I think there is still a bit to be done before students can truly engage with the course and its material. This class has so much potential, and hopefully it will get better in the future!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1669572\n 1669572\n \n 1669572\n The course was very well thought our discussions were quite entertaining and thorough. Unfortunately, we were able to get to maybe half of the poems that we read, but this wasnt really a weakness as we use those poems to help us understand how to read the other works By the poet. This class also gives you a good chance to refine your close reading and writing skills.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1682954\n 1682954\n \n 1682954\n Strengths: class discussion, readings, workload, professor engagement \r\nWeaknesses: organization (lack of clear syllabus)

It felt REALLY weird.)\r\n\r\nPeople idolize Soles, especially DS first-years and post-DS sophomores and this worries me. Professor Soles knows what he is going to say from the moment he enters the classroom. At times I felt like he didn't want people to raise their hands because he wanted to make his predetermined points. There was no room for disagreement with anything he said. If you like lectures disguised as seminars, maybe this is for you.\r\n\r\nWe were behind by two weeks for almost the entire course, and each week we would read poetry that we sometimes didn't discuss until two weeks afterward. Because of this, motivation to read the poems and complete the discussion posts was low. We ended up having to work on our final paper and final project at the same time because of how behind we were. It was a frustrating situation.
