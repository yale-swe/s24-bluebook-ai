\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1646238\n 1646238\n \n 1646238\n A great survey of the history of Europe during the High Middle Ages. Professor Fancy asks his students to consider what it means to label something as "medieval" and to consider what separates the peoples of the 14th century from those of today. In terms of specific movements and events, we learned about political tensions between the Catholic Church and medieval monarchs, the investiture controversy, the Renaissance of the 12th century, the rise of Gothic Art, the Black Death, the Papal Schism, the Hundred Years War, and finally, the movement towards the Renaissance of the 15th-16th centuries. In section, we were expected to read many primary sources, major medieval works like the Lais of Marie de France, the Letters of Peter Abelard and Helose, etc.

It really depends on your preferences: I know that a lot of people like this class, but it was honestly my least favorite history class I've taken at Yale. The biggest issue is that the professor prioritizes entertainment ahead of literally everything else, including historical accuracy and nuance. Instead of relating the actual complexities of the past, he dumbs everything down to fit into lazy narratives that he can tell with dramatic flair. For example, I once heard him tell a student after class that he purposely left out information about St. Francis in order to fit his narrative betterbut his job shouldn't be to sell us on one particular narrative, it should be to tell us all the possible narratives and give us the tools we need to sort through them and make sense of how they relate to one another. You can also tell that he has a bit of an overinflated ego, which makes his lectures even more grating. That's not to say that there's nothing good about the course: the lectures were certainly entertaining (although cringy), the workload of the course was reasonable, and the grading seemed mostly fair, although the feedback on essays and exams was not very good. So it really depends on what you're looking for in a class. If you want to actually learn history, this class might not be for you, but if you want a relatively easy and sorta fun class and you're willing to look past the professor's cringe-inducing style, then go for it.
