\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540926\n 1540926\n \n 1540926\n The main strength is that the course is very well-designed to give you a thorough introduction to archaeology, especially if you don't plan on becoming an archaeologist. Part of the course was lab assignments where we'd examine actual remains from sites like bones, tools, and pottery and try to interpret them as an archaeologist would. We also had two short essays where we applied our newly learned archaeological knowledge to everyday situations where archaeology might be relevant: evaluating a museum display of an archaeological object and a story about archaeology in the news. This helped me understand how I might see everyday things in a new light using my archaeological knowledge after taking this class. The main weakness I think is that the labs can tend towards busywork. Even though they were great for getting exposure to archaeological objects, the write-ups were often tedious and I had to repeat myself many times. For example, we had to analyze around 8 different spear points in one lab in pretty fine detail. I feel like doing half the number of specimens would impart the same knowledge without so much of the busywork. It made me less enthusiastic about labs over the course of the semester.
