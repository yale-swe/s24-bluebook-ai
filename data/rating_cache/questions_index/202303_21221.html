Despite PIs stating that knowledge of XYZ was unnecessary, the TAs overloaded evening sessions with precisely those XYZ details. Furthermore, although the last exam was declared non-cumulative, some questions were distinctly focused on details from the initial module, which were not revisited in the third module. More transparency about this discrepancy would have been appreciated, and additional time to review modules 1 and 2 for the last exam (held just two days after the final lecture) would have been beneficial. Perhaps scheduling the third exam after the reading period could have provided more preparation time.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1710571\n 1710571\n \n 1710571\n I liked the way the modules were set up to teach the material. I really enjoyed the final module that focused more on conceptual learning.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1718214\n 1718214\n \n 1718214\n A huge strength of this course was that it was able to cover so much information but be able to connect the topics and lectures to a wider concept and to each other. I feel this course could be improved by having the PIs giving the lecture have a bit more guidance on what needs to be covered. There were a few occasions where the lecturer didn't cover the material that would be on the exam and the TA session would have to cover it instead. This was fine but I feel it would be much more efficient and interesting if the topic had been covered in the lecture and the TA session could just be a review. Also, the amount of material covered in the exams felt very inconsistent. For instance, the first exam was pretty light but then the second one covered all of B and T cell development, signaling, effector functions, activation, etc. I feel that this could be improved by having maybe an extra exam added (break up the second exam material into two exams) or add material from the second exam into the first or third exam.
