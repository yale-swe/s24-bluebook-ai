\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562720\n 1562720\n \n 1562720\n Strengths: The course is taught largely by Prof. Harris walking through powerpoints during each lecture, and then those powerpoints are posted to canvas. This means that you have access to all of the contents of the course at all times, which definitely comes in handy when studying for tests. Weaknesses: The PSETs for this class can be really challenging. They largely get easier as the course continues but some of the advanced electricity and magnetism in the beginning of the semester makes the work really difficult.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1576135\n 1576135\n \n 1576135\n Strengths: Organization. The lecture slides were incredibly detailed, so I would print them out before class, and then I could focus on the content instead of trying to frantically write everything down. The problem sets and exams were very reasonable extensions of the course material, and we followed the textbook enough that it was helpful for studying for exams. In lectures, Prof. Harris did a good job of stopping for questions and answering them.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I wish we'd done more quantum mechanics. The course was taught with the assumption that we would all eventually learn quantum mechanics in more depth in our 400-level physics courses, but as someone who isn't likely to take more physics courses, I was disappointed not to have learned more here. On the other hand, I don't think there was any way to do the rest of the course faster, so I don't know how this would be fixed.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1581317\n 1581317\n \n 1581317\n Strengths: We got to see cool topics beyond typical EM, such as how electric and magnetic fields work in special relativity and the basics of quantum mechanics. For example, I know of no one who could answer either of the first 2 (out of 8) questions on the final exam. I believe this was mostly due the professor's teaching style.
