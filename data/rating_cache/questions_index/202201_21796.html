A lot of time was wasted touching on things like the AD-AS model. If students came in with this knowledge, macroeconomic parts of the course (particularly the new deal) could be treated better. If the course does insist upon reading academic papers so closely, students should be required to have the econometrics background to actually understand the methodologies (particularly regression and diff-in-diff). I happened to come in with this background, so I found the material completely unchallenging. \r\nThis should be a more specialised, less survey course with a completely different approach. As an economics and history double major, this should have been a course right up my alley, but turned out to be one of the weakest courses I've taken at Yale.
