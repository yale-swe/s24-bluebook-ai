\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1567912\n 1567912\n \n 1567912\n Strengths: Frequent projects that give very open direction for our interests, interesting reading topics, seminar/studio style instruction\r\nWeaknesses: Seminar discussions were often dry and could have been facilitated better. The occasional cold calling to talk about our reading responses I didn't mind, but it didn't feel like a smooth way to incorporate the entire group into a cohesive discussion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1567921\n 1567921\n \n 1567921\n i was not a big fan of how discussions were formatted. would have loved to do more hands-on things in class and have discussions while being engaged in that way.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1569998\n 1569998\n \n 1569998\n Strengths: Introduction to the material listed above. An interest in tying art-historical and theoretical considerations to the aesthetic and technological possibilities of artificial intelligence. It wasn't hobbled in by association to status-quo or functionality-oriented ideas of contemporary practice, but rather looked at its relevance to culture at a greater scale.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The biggest weaknesses are those that I associate with the seminar structure at YSoA in general: namely, the class length and the limited depth of inquiry allowable due to that sort of structural limitation. Two hours a week isn't enough for any computationally-oriented course, especially one in which there must be both reading discussion and presentation as well. Another general issue I have with Yale is an over-reliance on student-led discussion and presentation, which tends to yield uneven results of questionable value. With this course, as with most of the others that I have attended here, I would prefer to see more lecture content from the professor than from students. The only real course-specific weaknesses are the final project (perhaps a bit too proscriptive and too tenuously tied to the course themes for that reason) and the fact that the computational element was entirely provided by a student Teaching Fellow. He did a great job considering, but his primary responsibilities as a student led to a delay of the release of tutorial content, often far past when its incorporation into the assignment at hand was functionally possible for people like me who with a full course schedule who needed the weekend between classes to meaningfully explore the tutorial content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1580590\n 1580590\n \n 1580590\n I enjoyed the studio portion of the class a lot more than the discussion portion. I don't think 2 hrs/week of class was enough for both reviews/desk crit style discussion about our progress on projects and for discussing the readings. The readings were interesting, but I think the discussion could have been a lot better and more natural. It felt like our discussions were essentially recapping the reading responses that we wrote, so there wasn't a lot of new information/new ideas being discussed. We would have had a lot more time for discussion if we didn't have reading presentations, which could take at least half of the class. I think it would have been better to simply discuss the reading as a group rather than listen to a summary of the reading. The class is also much more enjoyable if you take it with people you know/make friends quickly because all of the projects are group projects.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1588318\n 1588318\n \n 1588318\n Strengths: Assignments, projects, and reviews. The assignment reviews were insightful and the feedback and discussions were very constructive and helpful. The readings were also quite interesting, but often were a bit dense. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Each student was required to do in-class presentations on one reading throughout the semester--these were often uninteresting and repetitive in format but I guess were a good way to do a deep dive into one of the readings, and were a good way to learn about a reading in class if you hadn't done it. I feel like that time in class could have been put to use in better ways though. The discussions on the readings were often dry. I also felt like sometimes the assignments were a little too open-ended and might have been better if they were more structured or if there were more guidelines.\r\n\r\nI think that an additional discussion section / working period would be extremely beneficial to this class to allow students to work through their projects and discuss 1 on 1 with the professor/TAs in more depth the work and process.
