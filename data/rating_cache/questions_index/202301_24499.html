\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1655279\n 1655279\n \n 1655279\n Midterm felt extra/unnecessary even though it wasn't too bad when I actually took it, maybe something could better replace that so people don't worry about it so much. The project is enough to worry about later. The class is very well structured otherwise. I really enjoyed the build up of the class to prepare to actually make our own instruments, since we learn a lot of basics foundations first then go off on our own. The profs are super significant strengths of the class being very available, communicative, and helpful
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1661351\n 1661351\n \n 1661351\n Strengths: manageable workload (ca. a few hours/week for hw outside of lab, though the final project takes more time and setting sizeable weekly goals is a must), accessible office hours, hands-on labs, fair midterm\r\nWeaknesses: lecture could be more interactive, don't let brainstorming for the final project idea sneak up on you!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1663499\n 1663499\n \n 1663499\n There are labs/weeks that barely take any time at all, and some that take much longer. The final project in particular is extremely time consuming. That said, Larry and Konrad are always willing to guide you through all of the assignments, and do an excellent job of getting you excited enough about the course that it rarely ever feels like work.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1666721\n 1666721\n \n 1666721\n Strengths: It's really really fun, especially the final project, where you get to design and create your own instrument. \r\nWeaknesses: I wish we had more time to work on said final project; I would definitely prefer if it were started alongside the labs, rather than after all of them are over, since we ended up only having about 4 weeks to design and create the instrument, a lot less time than it seems. Also, 9am lectures aren't great, and I would have definitely preferred later lectures.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1669422\n 1669422\n \n 1669422\n The strengths are you get to work on a project of your choosing within the realm of musical instruments, so long as it had acoustic and electronic components. The professors monitor you very very closely through this project, helping you through most of the aspects. The projects turn out cool. \r\nThe weakness is that this class IS SO MUCH TIME. The profs will tell you that workload ramps up in the last 1/3 of the semester once the final projects start, but I found the workload (6+ hours outside of class in lab homework and midterm prep) and class time (3 hour lab plus 2 weekly lectures) to be steady and tedious from the very start. I had all of my prerequisites, too. \r\nThe other weakness of this class is that the lectures end once you start your project, so you basically stop learning 2/3 of the way through the semester, and you start building and iterating, alone. There was zero collaborative element to this course, and I think this is a huge fault, especially as compared to the other CEID project-based courses I've taken. We could have done so much more in teams, and felt less siloed and frustrated while in the process. \r\nAlso, this course is marketed as a senior course, but I highly discourage you from taking it senior spring. None of the seniors in the course wanted to be spending 20+ hours a week in the CEID during their last month at Yale, when the weather was glorious outside and everyone else was winding down their coursework. \r\nFinally, the professors, while very very generous with their time and ideas, pretty much expect you to follow their ideas and frameworks to a tee, and I saw them get frustrated with other students who had diverging processes or ideas. I didn't mind their ideas because I was pretty lost on my own, though it was frustrating also to have to talk to the professors for every step in the process, and I would have done much better on a team where different people brought different skill sets. \r\nIt is so hard to build anything, and the expectations for this course are really above and beyond any course that I've taken at Yale, including other project and building classes taught in the CEID.
