Specifically, I learned about the way that intersecting identities have played a role in movements since the civil rights movement, and about the challenges in creating a movement that encompasses multiple marginalized identities. The skills I developed in the course were analyzing the methodologies of authors, utilizing the index and bibliographies of books written about history, and researching and writing a paper about history and social theory. The main insight I drew from this course is that the civil rights movement, segregation, and even slavery are not a part of the remote past, and have direct impacts on the lives of people in the present. Furthermore, I gained insight into the extreme difficulty of challenging a social hierarchy, and I learned that while movements in the past have certainly made gains, none have achieved the structural change that is tru7ly necessary to challenge the roots of racism in society.

Not a hard class to do well in if you complete the assignments on time and in line with what we are discussing, which is always something I appreciate. Stress about the grade did not hinder my engagement and interest in the class. I also loved reading all books written by Black women.
