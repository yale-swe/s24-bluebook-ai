\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538448\n 1538448\n \n 1538448\n Strengths: Consistency in lectures, covered a lot of ground, helpful feedback on the homework, useful balance of theory in the lectures mixed up with application problems both worked through together as in-class exercises, as well as on the homework. Weaknesses: The course topic seems like it has a lot of potential for being presented as an exciting search for lost knowledge, an "archaeology with words". Occasionally I got glimpses of this in lectures, but largely it felt more like a very functional primer in historical linguistics specifics and methods, with less attention paid to addressing the fundamental question of any intro class: "why should you find this cool?". This is great for students already interested in (historical) linguistics -- they learn exactly the necessary grounding in the subfield that they'd have wanted to have learned as the next step in their linguistics education. It might not be as great for students who are more unsure. I think part of the result was that class sometime seemed a bit dead -- not as many people as I would've hoped engaged with questions, even when we were doing in-class exercises.
