\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1608625\n 1608625\n \n 1608625\n Gained a really broad understanding of robotics and intelligence. I wish the last few lectures had included a little more present-day examples. We talked about autonomous vehicles and trucks, but not the new Tesla electric semi-trucks that were being presented the very same week. We learned about integrated systems, but not about the intelligent ChatGPT that could someday be thrown into ATLAS.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1609339\n 1609339\n \n 1609339\n Each lecture was really engaging and concise, so it was easy to pay attention to the whole thing. I think the problem sets and exams were incredibly fair if you kept up with lecture material and readings. The project seemed to come at a bad time of the year -- split up with Thanksgiving break, and it was difficult to gauge whether we were approaching it in an appropriate manner.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1611501\n 1611501\n \n 1611501\n I think that there could be a bit more coding work in the beginning of the class that could help with the final project
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1611644\n 1611644\n \n 1611644\n Overall, this is an incredible course. The content covered in it is fascinating, and Scaz may be one of the greatest lecturers I have ever heard. That being said, this course stumbled when it came to pacing. The course is extremely front-loaded, meaning almost all the meaningful content is delivered in the first half of the course. This results in a midterm that is much, much more challenging than the final. Also, the lectures were notably more organized in the first half, having a very clear structure and progression that was easy to take notes on. In contrast, lectures after the midterm essentially took the form of a series of stories loosely tied together by a central idea, which made note-taking more difficult. Finally, it is worth noting that while the content is frontloaded, the actual work is backloaded to an almost unmanageable degree. This semester, there were 3 problem sets spread out over the first few months, and I never needed to spend more than five hours each week when we had a problem set due. However, there is also a final project that is assigned in the last month of the course. This project is both extremely ambitious in scope and a group project, which meant that the large work was rarely distributed in a fair manner. Also, because of a lack of clear information and the specific timing of the assignment, it was very hard to put in much work until the week before it was due. These facts combined meant that the penultimate week of the course was a 60+ hour slog, while most other weeks had little to no work.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627028\n 1627028\n \n 1627028\n Strength: Scaz's lectures. He has clearly taught the subject many times over, and the way he speaks is very engaging. \r\nWeakness: final project. I enjoyed programming a robot on our own, but it didn't feel like the best reflection of what we'd learned in the course because we spent most of our time debugging and figuring out how to make the sensors work rather than implementing the architectures and design considerations we'd learned. In fact, they encouraged us not to implement any one architecture we'd learned. I think the final project could be re-worked and made to both better test students' learning while making the project less difficult/stressful for students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629004\n 1629004\n \n 1629004\n Strengths: Scaz is a great orator. Lectures were fun and interesting. The specs were reasonable, but some of the tasks we were required to do had not been talked about much in class, but we were expected to know how to execute it anyway (a decent amount of self-study was necessary). Additionally, the webots documentation is not very descriptive and the software is a bit buggy, so a lot of effort went into trying to understand the platform and avoid resulting issues.
