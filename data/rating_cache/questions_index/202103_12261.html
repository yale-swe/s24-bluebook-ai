\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527869\n 1527869\n \n 1527869\n Latin grammar, etymology, and literature analysis skills were all built upon this semester. We read selections of poetry and prose spanning several centuries of Latin literature, all centered around the theme of Roman dining, an activity that often involved complex etiquette, elaborate displays of wealth, and vying for attention of social elites. We were introduced to many different authors (Horace, Catullus, Pliny, Petronius, Juvenal, Seneca) and became accustomed to the telltale signs of each author's individual style. We wrote two interpretive essays (one on poetry and one on prose), and also composed two short polished translations (again, one on poetry and one on prose), so the assignments in this course were quite balanced and built a nice breadth of skills. I was initially worried that a course under this theme would be too restrictive or too focused on a historical reconstruction of the details of a Roman meal, however neither of these worries came true whatsoever. We explored a diverse set of themes: wealth and social status, friendship, and philosophy, and our aims were mostly literary.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532809\n 1532809\n \n 1532809\n Strengths: Professor Solodow had a great working knowledge of the texts we read and a deep appreciation for Latin etymology. The midterm, I thought, was fair. Weaknesses: The pop quizzes kept me on edge and sometimes tested tiny details in the commentary. In addition, during most classes, students were randomly called onto translate lines, and though this was a bridge course, I felt that there could be a more engaging way to spend class time. Especially near the end of the semester, we rushed through texts without thinking too deeply about them, and the workload got significantly heavier later on, which I was not prepared for (Juvenal and Horace are difficult to translate, and we were not given much warning or guidance). For the first essay, we were not given a lot of detailed instructions on how to write a paper that incorporated Latin texts; we were just told that it would be like writing an English essay. Giving the class a sample essay on a different topic would have been helpful.
