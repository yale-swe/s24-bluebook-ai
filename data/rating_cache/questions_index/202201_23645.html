\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1566743\n 1566743\n \n 1566743\n Strengths: Professor Armstrong is incredibly knowledgable and I really enjoyed the selection of artwork she chose for the course. I am a STEM major who has never taken an art history class and the course was still very accessible. I enjoyed learning about both the paintings themselves and the relevant history and external context.\r\nWeaknesses: some of the readings were far too long / convoluted, it would be nice to only need to know decades for the less important paintings because it does amount to a lot of studying to have to memorize the artist, title of the work, and date for ~250-300 important paintings in the class. Also, I wish the prompt for the first paper was more related to the actual content we were learning because we were asked to write about Manet but didn't study him until a month or two later. And one more thing - I think my understanding of the readings would have been greatly enhanced if the entire reading schedule was pushed back a week so I could see the paintings in class and learn a little bit about the painter/movement and THEN take a deeper dive into the readings...I think that would have really helped with my understanding and appreciation of the readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1574079\n 1574079\n \n 1574079\n The lectures and sections were really great and informative in helping me understand the material. The readings were good, though a bit overwhelming in terms of the density of what was assigned - in that sense, I don't think that facilitated me fully appreciating the art historical readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1576896\n 1576896\n \n 1576896\n Strengths: the sheer VOLUME of content. Covering more than a century of a particular topic is always hefty, and there were a lot of artworks introduced (~400). Lectures had so much information in eachas an extremely extra notetaker, my notes at the end of the class ended up being 119 pages (as bullet points) on a Google Doc. The Professor and the TAs were so, so knowledgeable.\r\n\r\nThe class also always felt very structured, with a guiding theme for each lecture and section. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I really wish that the professor was able to asterisk the most important works. Even though she says that the exam IDs would concentrate on the major works and that is pretty much true, it is often hard to sus out what "major" exactly means. The ~200 in the first half and ~250 works in the second half to memorize for IDs were tough and sometimes felt very pointless.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1591519\n 1591519\n \n 1591519\n The latter part of the course felt rushed compared to the beginning, it would have been great to spend more time on the impressionists and the post impressionists and discuss underlying causes for the change

I felt like I gained a LOT of tangible knowledge from this course. Because it's such a niche subsection of art, you kind of have to do the readings (which is definitely part of the reason I felt so engaged in this course). Also, the second half of 19th century French art is objectively cool (I'm talking impressionism/neo-impressionism onward). \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: My biggest complaint with this course is that sections did not align with lectures. This course could have been even more enriching and fulfilling if section was used as a time to really explore the time period/artists discussed in lecture that week, rather the sections felt disjointed in terms of the course content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1592304\n 1592304\n \n 1592304\n Lectures were a good combination of historical context and discussion of artworks. Fantastic TFs; fair assignments/exams, although there were too many IDs. Weaknesses real disconnect between lecture and section, which were often out of sync material-wise. Sections covered much more difficult primary source / secondary readings material not covered in lecture that was interesting but not relevant to assignments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1593611\n 1593611\n \n 1593611\n The lectures are not always organized well, so timelines can sometimes be confusing. Other than that, Professor Armstrong is a fantastic lecturer and describes artistic themes and paintings in a way that opens my eyes to new ways of describing art. Lectures always feel rushed because there is always more information to learn. Section gives us a good opportunity to practice analysis and look at different perspectives of art criticism throughout the 19th century.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594866\n 1594866\n \n 1594866\n Strengths: great lecture courseorganized, clear, and interesting content. If you're interested in French art at all it is a great overview, and it will also deepen your understanding of more general movements like Romanticism and Impressionism. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: hard to study for as the midterm and exam have no list of key images
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1597213\n 1597213\n \n 1597213\n The strengths of this course were its content and its enthusiastic teaching team. I greatly enjoyed coming to the lectures and discussion sections each week, as I found the content and the conversations we had around it extremely engaging. It was especially cool to trace the larger themes and developments of the art we studied while preparing for the final. However, the weakness of this course was that I sometimes felt that we had too much content. Sometimes, we could only spend a minute or less on a painting during lecture. Our weekly readings were also incredibly long, meaning that we could not discuss everything to its fullest extent during discussion section. Although this high level of content gave me a fantastic idea of the overarching themes and movements of the course, I occasionally wished that we spent more time in the details in order to gain a deeper understanding of the pieces and artists that we studied. However, this weakness is common to Yale courses in our short semester schedule, and I also appreciate how Professor Armstrong tried to expose us to as much art from the period as possible.
