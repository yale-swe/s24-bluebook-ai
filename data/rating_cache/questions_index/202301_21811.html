RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1646243\n 1646243\n \n 1646243\n I enjoyed this course and its format. The writing assignments are well structured and the readings are as well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1652292\n 1652292\n \n 1652292\n It would be nice to have some more structured writing exercises, maybe some in class. The assignments were very broad (childhood, route, travel) and in a workshop-based class devoted to writing as a craft I would have appreciated more specific instructions on subject and style and form (literary nonfiction or journalism or memoir or architectural criticism).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1653947\n 1653947\n \n 1653947\n The course is well structured -- you quickly get into the flow (each week there is typically a draft or a final version of an essay due). I felt like I was never completely overwhelmed, but that I always had something to do for the class. The readings were good, although I might have enjoyed more attention spent on the readings Prof. Zarin posted and less on the anthology.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1653950\n 1653950\n \n 1653950\n I enjoyed how we were able to write pieces of many different lengths.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1653953\n 1653953\n \n 1653953\n strengths:\r\n- lots of practice writing was nice\r\n- readings chosen by Professor Zarin were quite good (exempting the ones in the Best American Traveling Writing book)\r\n- fun seminar environment where you get to know your peers\r\n\r\nweaknesses:\r\n- "place writing" often felt like quite a broad term and expectations sometimes felt unclear about what that means\r\n- larger assignments toward the end of the semester felt quite high-stakes and made it difficult to experiment with different types of writing\r\n- workshops in class often did not feel very productive; it was clear that most people had not read the essays beforehand. maybe it would be worth telling people to bring comments written down before, or even submit them on canvas
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1668176\n 1668176\n \n 1668176\n I appreciated that ENGL 478 was cross-listed with Architecture, and I wonder if a broad overview of architectural vocabulary could be a helpful inclusion in an early class meetingboth for when it appears in assigned/workshop readings and for our own writing. Class discussions were energetic and thoughtful, as were workshops, and the reading list was varied in a way that allowed for consideration of what a "place"and therefore place writingis or could be.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1671572\n 1671572\n \n 1671572\n Strengths: well-structured in a way that allows skills to be progressively developed with each assignment, good readings, fantastic instructor, fosters a rich and supportive learning community\r\nWeaknesses: noneI thoroughly enjoyed this class\r\nImprovements: I really enjoyed the explicitly instructional parts of the class (when we talked about structuring devices, words to avoid, etc.), so more of that would make this class even better, in my opinion
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1684193\n 1684193\n \n 1684193\n I thought the best part of this class was the in-class workshopping and the one-on-one peer editing. The peer editing especially was incredibly helpful in producing more effective and meaningful pieces. I might have appreciated the workshop essays being posted slightly earlier so that we had more time to look over our peers' work before class the next morning.
