Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1573716\n 1573716\n \n 1573716\n The strengths of this course are primarily that it does not ask too much from its students. It has never been a source of stress for me. It is also cool to be exposed to all the amazing resources and collections Yale has. I also liked that the instructor gave the students some choice and flexibility with what collections we choose to visit, and the broadness of the final project allowed the creation process of my own art to be a great introspective experience. However the weaknesses lie in its lack of substance and academic stimulation. I don't feel that I learned anything through the instruction of this course that I could not have learnt by visiting these exhibitions on my own. There is a constant pressure to participate but often students will say anything to fulfill their participation points. Additionally the three hours straight of instruction with no breaks in between on Wednesday afternoons are extremely draining. Quite simply the class was really boring, even for people interested in learning more about art. A lot of the themes from early classes are continuously repeated with not much gain between them, and I'd continuously step out of class feeling like I had gained the amount of knowledge that can be learned in 15 minutes in 3 hours. The course could be improved by having it be divided into two shorter sessions rather than one 3 hours session a week, and by having instructional material with an overarching theme or goal for learning in the class rather than it just feeling like looking at random art collections every week, perhaps by truly tying the artwork back to history of its time rather than just having students form speculative connections based on knowledge they already possess.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1573855\n 1573855\n \n 1573855\n The course really challenged me to think deeply about art and the way that I think and process information. Visiting different locations around Yale's campus was fun, as was the exposure to all of the interesting art and artists. One think that I think brought the course down a little bit was meeting only once a week, and for such a long time. Personally, it's hard for me to pay attention to anything for 3 straight hours, and I think breaking class up into multiple days a week (or just having a break halfway through class) would go a long way towards making the class more enjoyable.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1583343\n 1583343\n \n 1583343\n A strength of this course is that we went to a variety of art galleries and archives around campus that I otherwise probably would have never gone to before. It was also approachable for someone who had little to no experience with art before, which I greatly appreciated, and even though it had a low workload, I do feel that I learned quite a bit. I think it could be improved by breaking up the three hour class time in some way, either through a break or by having two activities for each class period since sometimes the class felt very long.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1583842\n 1583842\n \n 1583842\n Strengths: getting to see many interesting objects and discuss their artistic and historical/societal meaning. I think that the grading is fair and the system of class note-taking is interesting!\r\nWeaknesses: I wish we had more time/assignments for students to choose objects from archives that they found most interesting and present them in class.
