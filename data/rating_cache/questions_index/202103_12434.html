\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1547359\n 1547359\n \n 1547359\n I would definitely take this course. If you are an Econ/Math major, you are likely going to take this anyway but you do have an option -- I would advise taking this class. You will learn a fair amount of general equilibrium theory, and John G does a great job of tracing the development of many of the key ideas (he was the student of the founders of the area). He is also a very entertaining storyteller, which adds to the course, especially as some of the more math-y material could be viewed as more slightly more dry. You will also learn about some really interesting ideas he has introduced, such as the leverage cycle, which has become fairly well-known/accepted in both the academic and financial community. \r\n\r\nOne caveat is that you should be relatively comfortable with math. I took this concurrently with analysis, and I think having the familiarity with analysis-style argumentation helped me understand the material more fully. You'd probably be fine in taking the course without proof-based math under your belt, but you might get a bit less out of the more theoretical lectures (as Psets don't really ask for proofs of theorems).
