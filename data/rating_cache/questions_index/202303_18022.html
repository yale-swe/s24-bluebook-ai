Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1701768\n 1701768\n \n 1701768\n Dr. Schenker's teaching is one of the big strengths; she knows how to get the class involved in learning (ex: we all had to present on one topic from DDR history, so we all researched at least one thing in depth) and uses fun activities to keep everyone engaged.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1702231\n 1702231\n \n 1702231\n Strengths: Dr. Schenker is incredibly kind and the topic is clearly near and dear to her heart, and our classroom was always a fun social space. I also enjoyed the email exchange program much more than I had expected to. The workload of the class is well spread-out across the entire semester. \r\nWeaknesses: Above all, this class is easier than an advanced L5 class should be. The highest level of German course on offer should not have students who struggle to hold a conversation about an analytical topic they belong in lower-level courses. This is a shame, because the course was oversubscribed and so the level could definitely have been raised a little to benefit the stronger students. I mostly took this class because I wanted more German writing practice, but there were very few times writing assignments that received substantive feedback. Much of the class involves discussing the most recent reading assignments in small groups or listening to student presentations, both of which are not super engaging or helpful in cultivating language skills. \r\nOne suggestion for improvement is for the instructor to send out periodic updates on what assignments are due when there were lots of minor assignments that could slip through the cracks if you weren't paying close attention to Canvas.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706356\n 1706356\n \n 1706356\n Strengths: interesting subject material, awesome and knowledgeable professor, cool exchange with former East Germans\r\nWeaknesses: a lot of small assignments that were sometimes listed in different places
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1715088\n 1715088\n \n 1715088\n More general focus on the history of the East German state.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1720517\n 1720517\n \n 1720517\n There was always so much to learn! Frau Schenker put an immense amount of effort into making the course engaging for us, and I knew that I had to bring my best to the class every day to match this energy. One strength of the class was that although everyone was on completely different levels, Frau Schenker made sure that everyone could get through the material and get something out of each activity. One weakness was that, because there were so many readings, it felt sometimes as though there wasn't always a through line.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721772\n 1721772\n \n 1721772\n Strengths: email exchange with someone who lived in East Germany. Diverse learning materials\r\nWeaknesses: level/quantity of reading material was sometimes too difficult and grammar lessons were breezed through
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724680\n 1724680\n \n 1724680\n I enjoyed the different formats of learning in this coursefor example, learning language through movies, interviews, exchange partners, worksheets, games, etc. I think I wouldve also enjoyed more activities where we could practicing more niche structures of German language (like how we learned to transform verbs into adjectives in German. An example in English is the verb run to say the running child).
