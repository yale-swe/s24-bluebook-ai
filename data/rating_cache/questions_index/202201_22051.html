\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1581682\n 1581682\n \n 1581682\n Strengths: the course goes very in-depth, so you come out of the course with a large understanding of many of the mechanisms and pathways that underpin much of transcription, translation, DNA repair, and other processes that are central to the maintenance and reading of the genetic code. Both Dr. Pyle and Dr. Isaacs are great teachers who can communicate information very well in a manner that breaks down complex topics in molecular biology and makes these concepts understandable.\r\nWeaknesses: the length of the exams is one of the weaknesses of this course; the exams feel like they are designed so that students cannot finish on time. The questions are almost entirely free response, and since the class covers a lot of detail, the exams are likewise very dense in the material they cover. As a result, the exams will take a lot of time to study for and you should not expect to know the answer to every question tested. The good news is that the class is heavily curved, so even if you get a 67 or 72 on the exams (which is a common occurrence in this class), as long as you are around or above the median, you will probably end up getting an A- or A.
