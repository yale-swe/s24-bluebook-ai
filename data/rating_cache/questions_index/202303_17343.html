\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1699950\n 1699950\n \n 1699950\n Strengths: lots of opportunities for feedback (professors and peers), writing process was a slow buildup so nothing felt too overwhelming\r\nWeaknesses: lots of small day-to-day assignments - some of which seemed a little bit tedious
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1700063\n 1700063\n \n 1700063\n Strengths: Good checkpoints between assignments (so it doesn't clump up/feel like too much work at once), in class discussion opportunities, readings not too long but also quite insightful (both for writing and for the topics covered in the course), feedback very applicable, Dr. Klemann is very understanding good at working with students, opportunities to meet with her outside of class if necessary\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Nothing in particular, to be honest
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1709192\n 1709192\n \n 1709192\n No particular weaknesses.\r\nStrengths in course structure and materials and quality of instruction.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714549\n 1714549\n \n 1714549\n Firstly, the course has multifarious and extensive opportunities for students to succeed. Because we are required to write 2-3 "drafts" or "versions" of every academic paper that eventually receives a letter grade, we are able to incorporate feedback we receive on every "draft" or "version" in all pieces of writing that have "high stakes" and play a role in our final grade. This is incredible because it removes a lot of stress and prevents us from procrastinatingthe drafts are spaced out pretty well, forcing you to consistently work on your papers. The course isn't fundamentally "weak" in any way, but to make the course more exciting, I would suggest inviting more "guest speakers" or "guest lecturers" who produce work that pertains to the theme of the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1716510\n 1716510\n \n 1716510\n I particularly enjoyed the course blogs and hands-on experience that this course gives the students. There are few assignments where students do not have some choice on what they would like to explore. My favorite assignment was the P3 since it both connected my interests as well as the course material that we had been working on all semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1716581\n 1716581\n \n 1716581\n This class was very well organized and a nice mix between learning how to write and learning about the subject about which we were writing. The teaching was engaging and I never felt that we were doing "busy work".
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1723683\n 1723683\n \n 1723683\n One of the strengths of this course is that it's writing intensive, and that alone helped me improve my writing. Another strength of this course is that since it has a small class size, each student receives individual, personalized feedback on their writing assignments. This feedback was extremely helpful to me for improving my writing. I think that having individual, personalized feedback also caters to each student's level of writing when they begin the course, and this makes the course focused first and foremost on each student's improvement. I liked that in my English 114 section, we were required to meet with our professor at least 3 times throughout the semester. If this isn't a requirement across all the English 114 sections, I think that it should be. Similarly, I think that each 114 section should have a course blog if that's not the case. Other than that, I don't really have any other suggestions for how the course could be improved. I personally had a great experience in the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724464\n 1724464\n \n 1724464\n This course offers a wide variety of material (from movies to research papers), it teaches many different types of writing, the discussions allow for a deeper understanding, and the assignments are very useful in learning to become a better writer. I received amazing, in-depth feedback from Professor Klemann, and she really helped me think creatively, try new things, and experiment with my writing rather than just write mechanically. Although I loved so many parts of this course, some of the assignments seemed either needlessly long (such as the idea mapping assignment). Therefore, it may be better to do simpler, less intense day-to-day assignments.
