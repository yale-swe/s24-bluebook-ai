\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534522\n 1534522\n \n 1534522\n A big strength of this course is its seminar format, and how the discussions we had each class were engaging and fun. I would also say that all the readings were interesting, although sometimes the amount of paged we had to read for the next class (which was in 2 days) was too much. Id also say this course could be improved by shifting its focus a bit: it was only Unit 1 that I thought had a philosophy focus with Jim Holts book (the assigned reading for that unit). Every other Unit of the course felt like it was focused on literature with sprinkles of physics and philosophy on the side, rather than having all 3 balanced. I just wish more of the primary readings were philosophical or physics analyses rather than literary works.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534563\n 1534563\n \n 1534563\n Maybe one too many books as it was a lot of required reading but overall the pace and information was very interesting.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534638\n 1534638\n \n 1534638\n Strengths: really interesting connections and points made during engaging class discussions; intellectual freedom within essays; great feedback on essays\r\nWeaknesses: heavy reading; some of the science/quantum mechanics readings were difficult to understand and did not always feel relevant to the rest of the class content
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535001\n 1535001\n \n 1535001\n Honestly, I think the biggest weakness of the course is the fact that we don't get more of Prof. B's personal insights into the material. At times, the texts are incredibly difficult and complex (Heidegger, Woolf, and even Auster's could be difficult to parse through), and yet we were left to figure it out through lukewarm class discussions which often didn't do the text justice and left me often confused, despite asking questions in the discussion that attempted to get at the heart of my points of confusion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535368\n 1535368\n \n 1535368\n For the most part, the course is well-structured, engaging, and manageable. Although the amount of reading is fairly intense (five relatively challenging and somewhat technical books/novels interspersed with regular short readings on physics and philosophy), the rigor of written work assigned is fair, especially for a course that fulfills a writing credit. Each essay assignment becomes progressively more demanding; both the length of essays and the sophistication of the prompts step up in difficulty throughout the course. I felt like this structure enabled me to experiment more with ideas as I gained confidence in my writing. The writing feedback is incredibly clear, detailed, and specific, and I never left a conference with Prof. B feeling confused about my performance on an essay or without a clear plan to improve my next piece. It's incredible that Prof. B meets individually with each one of her students to provide feedback; she was also willing to meet with me outside of the official conferences to discuss ideas on a regular basis. \r\n\r\nThe content of the course itself feels like the somewhat weaker component of the class. I took this class because I've always gravitated toward literature, had taken a fair amount of physics in high school, and hardly had any technical exposure to real philosophy. While I thoroughly enjoyed the readings and discussions, I'm not sure my knowledge of any of the three disciplines truly coalesced in a revolutionary way. There's not a ton of emphasis on philosophy, and the discussions of physics felt somewhat superficial, but I did feel like these issues were counterbalanced by reading some thought-provoking literature. For this reason, the second half of the class was more enjoyable to me, as I was able to delve into literature in a way that felt more authentic.

To improve the class, I think having some more multimedia sources would be interesting. For example, Holt mentions lectures given by certain people, I think it could be interesting to watch one of those for homework and make that a source as it would be a primary source and a good look into the actual thoughts of one of the people we talk about.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536112\n 1536112\n \n 1536112\n I think this is a very good course to encourage you to think deeply about the intersections between different topics, though sometimes I felt like philosophical ideas could have been more explicitly explored and related to the other topics being discussed. Sometimes the syllabus also felt a little rushed, but with so many things to cover that is to be expected.
