\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1597594\n 1597594\n \n 1597594\n I learned the basics of performing solkattu, and learned about rhythmic forms found in South Indian classical music; maybe that doesn't sound like a lot but it definitely was, and the fact that we learned through performing ourselves was amazing. We also learned a lot about the history and politics of Carnatic music, which I enjoyed a lot and was incredibly well-taught; usually, history taught by non-historians tends to be boring and/or clumsy and reductive, but Fugan really took the material seriously and made us understand why it was relevant and important.

Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1584117\n 1584117\n \n 1584117\n A strength is that it is so interdisciplinary, that it encompasses both performance and historical context. There should be more of these kinds of courses in the Music Department. I think the final composition was a little overwhelming at the end, but it was never unmanageable.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1584161\n 1584161\n \n 1584161\n The strength of this course was how Fugan incorporated history, theory, and practice together so I felt like I had a well-rounded perspective on the material. \r\n\r\nTo improve this course, I would consolidate the assignments. For example: HW week #1: 50% annotating article, 50% video performance. This would be easier to keep track of than separate assignments in many different "categories" like video performances, essays, etc.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1585283\n 1585283\n \n 1585283\n The malleable nature of the course was great, and it facilitated learning and growth. I only felt that the class time was particularly long, especially when we would go into slides.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1589658\n 1589658\n \n 1589658\n The course's only weirdness was the long meeting times and the occasional lack of clarity on recorded exercises. Everything else was perfect
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1591877\n 1591877\n \n 1591877\n A lot of the music practice in the course is taught orally/aurally and in a group. This different way of learning music is definitely one of the strengths of the course, requiring you to participate and engage actively in class. It can be daunting at times, however, especially when there are such spectacular musicians as your classmates. Also, a huge weakness and frustration was the fact that a lot of the things done in class were done aurally/orally but there often wasn't an online recording of any kind to practice along at home. This was a problem esp. for the exercises that didn't come from the book. It would've been really helpful to me personally to have these. In general, there was quite a bit of confusion and lack of clarity about expectations of assignments, homework, and projects.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1597594\n 1597594\n \n 1597594\n I have to mention how amazing Fugan is, he's incredibly knowledgeable and supportive and I'm so honored to have been taught by him. I was really scared of taking this course because it seemed like a lot of pressure, but it absolutely wasn't. We were still expected to know the material but if we didn't, Fugan was there to help us without ever seeming like he was judging us. He's also the most organized professor I've ever had, I love the modules setup and the videos he made at the beginning to help us.\r\nI think the balance of the class sessions in the first half of the semester was really good, having both performances/learning sessions and historical discussions was a good way to break up the 3 hours. I also enjoyed the full performance classes near the end of the semester, but they were pretty exhausting in good and bad ways. \r\nMy only real strong criticism would be to not have the final group performance as a video, I know it already wasn't the plan but if it can't be person, at least let us split up the sections to record. I can perform all the sections, but trying to go 14 minutes with minimal mistakes on your own is not fun (everything else in the course was fun, so this was a notable departure).
