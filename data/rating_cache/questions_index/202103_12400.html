\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539474\n 1539474\n \n 1539474\n I really feel that there were many possible strengths that I could take from the class. However, the professor made it really hard for any strengths to really shine. I can see how people can learn about economics from the class as a potential strength, however, Maria made it very hard to learn. Professor Saez is honestly mean at times to students, I remember that in some of her office hours, many responses to other students were uncalled for. Another weakness is that it is very hard to hear Maria during lectures, very often, she would just mumble a lot of her words, and it was content that was not on her slides. I found myself struggling a lot because this was my very first econ class. Many kids took AP Micro in high school and were doing fine on the psets as they had learned the material, but if you've never taken anything econ-related, you will have a hard time as the psets are not from the content taught in class. Basically, the syllabus says you don't need econ experience, but this only works in theory. Maria will also choose the worst possible times for office hours. She would often hold these Wednesdays, in the middle of the day, when most people have classes. Other classes give problem tests before a midterm or a final, Maria would just tell you that she did enough work on the slides and the psets and she wasn't doing any extra work. For the final as well, she made it impossible for people traveling. Most professors decided to waive the final and give students the choice to keep the grade they had. Maria decided to host the final, the day after all students were encouraged to go home because of the pandemic. I think that the course could be greatly improved by structuring it in a more friendly way for people who have never taken econ and take students' petition for test prep into consideration.
