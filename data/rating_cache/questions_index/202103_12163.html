\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544774\n 1544774\n \n 1544774\n Strengths: This course exposes sophomore (and surrounding classes) undergraduates to specialized material that was otherwise not available to undergraduates until senior year. I believe this is extremely beneficial for a number of reasons. First, taking this course has the potential to change the course of multiple students' undergraduate education, as it did mine. The course goes in-depth into biophysical methods and theory in the first half of the semester, whereas the latter end of the course is more of a survey of spectroscopic techniques and basics of molecular dynamics simulations. While this latter end definitely brushes over a lot of things and is a bare-bones introduction to this side of the major, it is still beneficial for students to be exposed to these topics. After being exposed, they can then choose to explore these topics more in specialized classes found in the MB&B or Chemistry Departments. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Even though I did thoroughly enjoy the course, I did find it to have a fair bit of weaknesses, all said and done. First, I'd argue that the amount of statistical knowledge expected from us greatly exceeded the scope of the course, especially on the last problem set/lab report. I understand that part of the function of problem sets in this class was for continued learning, especially for Mathematica, but I do think asking us to determine a p-value just from bootstrapping is unreasonable when the conceptual interpretation of p-values was never really addressed in class (and in reality, we got one bare-bones lecture on statistics). My suggestion to address this is to either spend more class time going over statistical concepts or to make introductory statistics a prerequisite for the course. Additionally, I found the Mathematica instruction to be highly inaccessible at first, although this feeling subsided as the semester progressed. As someone who had not taken a computer science course since junior year of high school, a formal Mathematica instruction section, for those who felt underprepared when it came to Mathematica/coding generally, would have been extremely helpful (something more formal than the 7pm study halls on Tuesday's). Lastly, this course could perhaps benefit from a peer tutor or two so that all of the onus to answer questions about the problem set does not fall on the instructors or the teaching assistants. Generally, more office hours at accessible hours would also have been helpful throughout the semester.
