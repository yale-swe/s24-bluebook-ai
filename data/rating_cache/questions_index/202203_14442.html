Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614135\n 1614135\n \n 1614135\n This class challenged my critical thinking skills and pushed me to write even better, more so than any other class I have ever taken at Yale (and I'm a senior). The strengths lie in the extensive knowledge Professor Brancati has on the subject of peacebuilding, and hearing her stories about her work with government officials to inform policy has been fascinating. She also centers the class around students learning, which deepened my learning in the class. One thing to keep in mind with this class is that it is a lot of work. There is a lot of reading and the reading takes time to digest and understand.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618297\n 1618297\n \n 1618297\n The assignments of the course were very well structured to teach students about the fundamentals of writing a political science paper. Sometimes there were too many readings to really hit all of them in class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624601\n 1624601\n \n 1624601\n Strengths: readings were very interesting, extremely lively discussions in class/ encouragement to participate, Prof Brancati creates an engaging environment for everyone/ provides detailed feedback on all assignments so you know how youre doing throughout the semester, well-organized so that you get a comprehensive and clear look at quite a complicated topic \r\nWeaknesses: none
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624676\n 1624676\n \n 1624676\n S: Very well structured. Each reading and assignment is intentional, and I got way more out of class discussions in PB compared to other polisci seminars. You will also end the semester with a significant piece of work in the form of your final paper.\r\nW: The expectations of the course do not seem adjusted to the undergraduate level. Students are expected to produce graduate-level work, which can be challenging for those with less experience.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625158\n 1625158\n \n 1625158\n The main strength of the class is the material. The readings really cover all of the major topics related to peace building in current political science discourse. They relate but don't overlap which makes the content really interesting and refreshing. The class was also heavily reliant on class participation which was really valuable both because other students made interesting contributions and because I felt like I improved my own ability to critique political science papers.\r\n\r\nI think a major weakness is Professor Brancati's approach to giving feedback. While she is highly responsive and gives extensive comments (both strengths!), she very much approaches giving feedback to students as if she were peer reviewing the work of other professionals in the field. She quite often shut down very good points that people made in class (especially when they fell outside of a narrow political science framework), could be extremely rude when making comments about the quality of people's work (outright saying that people's final posters were "ugly"), and just generally expected us to produce publishable political science work in every assignment we turned in (which meant scrutinizing every single sentence we wrote and trying to poke holes in our thinking to the extent that it was essentially impossible to meet her standards). While it ultimately is possible to be successful in the class, the learning environment was made to be quite stressful in a way that was honestly totally unnecessary. I would really only take this class if you're a senior in the major who's looking for guidance on writing a senior thesis or a grad student, and you have very clear expectations.
