\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575727\n 1575727\n \n 1575727\n My writing really improved this semester -- Professor Spillane is one of the few teachers that has given me feedback on the small-scale minutiae of my writing in a way that has made me much better at describing things. One of our guest speakers said that all writing is just describing things, so even though the performances you're describing in this class may have very little to do with anything you write about elsewhere, when you become better at describing these performances you become a better writer.\r\n\r\nBeyond that, I've learned a lot about different performing arts. Professor Spillane has a whole life of experience in all of the arts she teaches about and does a good job of giving you a working knowledge of everything you're learning about. I have a much better sense of how to craft a review that feels like, as Prof. Spillane says, a "performance on the page"!

Perhaps my biggest takeaway was that I was able to strengthen a writing muscle that I'd never worked before - journalist or creative writing. I feel am able to better communicate my thoughts on art in a non-academic way, and will continue to work on and strengthen these skills going forward.

Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575557\n 1575557\n \n 1575557\n Im sure many will complain about the weekly Saturday at 3 PM deadline, which I really struggled to meet. However, this forced me to produce a piece of work that I was proud of in a crunch, which made me a faster and better writer. The class is demanding, but dont let that hinder you if you want to see your writing completely change trust me, its worth it. Also it allows Professor Spillane the time to give you the INSANELY DETAILED feedback she does. Ive never had a professor pick apart every single word I wrote like she did, and it was really intimidating at first, but my second draft of each piece was always so much better because of it. I grew so much as a writer because of it! Another strength is that I loved doing the readings, which I dont normally, and they honestly werent as heavy as I thought theyd be. Also having a professor like Spillane is just incredible. She is so wise and accomplished and she was a highlight of my semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575584\n 1575584\n \n 1575584\n The biggest strength of this course is the many, many comments Professor Spillane leaves on every essay. While at first they were a bit disheartening, I soon realized that they were helpful when I stopped being so proud.\r\n\r\nThe biggest weakness of this course is the number of readings per week. For many weeks, I struggled to complete the readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575651\n 1575651\n \n 1575651\n The professor is amazing and so involved. You get so much feedback and it is so engaging. The material is wonderful and intriguing. It is just a lot of work and sometimes overwhelming to read and enjoy everything.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575727\n 1575727\n \n 1575727\n Strengths: The seminar discussions are almost always interesting, even in the first couple weeks of the semester when they were over Zoom. Professor Spillane knows how to keep a class moving and engaging, and I learned a lot about the performing arts world in our discussions. Our writing also improved a ton over the course of the semester -- you get your drafts back covered in feedback every Tuesday like clockwork.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Don't let the relative length of this section make you think I disliked the class! All the feedback you get can be demoralizing, since negative feedback is weighed more highly than positive feedback. The first draft you'll get will make you think you are a terrible writer -- it took me until I talked to other people in the class who's writing I read for workshops and knew was good and yet still got the same demoralizing feedback from Professor Spillane. You also don't get a grade on each assignment, which probably works in your favor, but it doesn't give you a great sense of what your writing is like. The only thing I could pick up from the writing is that it is imperfect, which no duh, this is the second or third time I've ever done anything like this, of course it will be imperfect. On rare occasions, something similar in class will happen: you'll say something Professor Spillane disagrees with and she'll explain at length why you are wrong.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575760\n 1575760\n \n 1575760\n Professor Spillane's structure of working through drafts is rare at Yale, where we often are rushing to finish a paper at the last minute. I appreciated the opportunity to fine tune a shorter piece of writing using her advice, and that of my peers!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1583055\n 1583055\n \n 1583055\n I loved how we had more and shorter writing assignments. Writing two truly polished pages is a challenge, and so few classes at Yale encourage you to learn the art of writing concisely. I thought the 2-page assignments were fun and a wonderfully fresh way to do a writing class. The syllabus is also a huge strength of the course. It's the most diverse syllabus in any English or performance class I've taken, featuring lots of non-male artists and artists of color that I'd never been exposed to before but definitely deserve to be studied. That said, I often wished we had discussed the readings more in class, as they were fascinating--there was plenty of stuff that was assigned that we didn't touch much, if at all. All of it was very interesting and enjoyable to read/watch, but it was just hard to tell what the priority was for the class day. As well, though I found the class blog fun to read and write, I felt like the online platform was difficult to navigate and outdated.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1599005\n 1599005\n \n 1599005\n It's genuinely difficult for me to think of weaknesses in this course. Perhaps it would have been nice to get a little more time talking to other class members one on one, for example having paired review sessions, or small-group film viewings. One other thing I'll mention is that I really appreciated being able to chose what I wanted to write about - specifically in terms of genre. It would have been nice to trade one week of writing about an unfamiliar art form for another week of being able to write about a form in which I have some level of expertise. In terms of strengths, I really appreciated the depth and precision of Professor Spillane's feedback on my writing. I also thought that the in-person and virtual meetings with writers in the field was incredibly helpful and fascinating.
