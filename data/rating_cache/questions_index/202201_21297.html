\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562355\n 1562355\n \n 1562355\n The strength of this course is that so many topics are being covered and we learn so many aspects of data mining and modeling. However, most of the course load, I believe, was brought on the students at the last two weeks. We had one quiz and one homework in the entire semester before the two weeks. However, in the last two weeks, we were given a second quiz with a short notice, another homework, and a final project with presentation. I think this problem can be improved by assigning the homework and quizzes earlier or at least with a longer notice period so that the students are ready for it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1577389\n 1577389\n \n 1577389\n Strengths - pretty broad overview of biomedical data science if you're into that? However, it didn't feel like there was that much new material - almost everything we learned was something that another class had covered in greater depth.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses - Honestly, I rarely write course reviews anymore and it takes a lot for me to dislike a course. I'll unfortunately make an exception for this course. I wanted to like it, but it was really poorly organized - too many different lecturers, way too much content, wacky distribution of assignments throughout the semester. We either zoomed through the material too quickly or, in a couple of cases, spent a ton of time diving into the details of algorithms. This made it difficult to figure out how to study for the quiz or even what would be on the quiz, despite the study guides provided by the TAs. (Tip if you do decide to take this class, though: the quiz archive is actually really helpful.) Moreover, the class was ridiculously backloaded - we had only one thing before spring break (the first quiz), and then everything else hit after the break: the first homework, the second quiz, the final presentation and report, and the second homework. I took particular issue with the second homework because it was assigned the day after class ended and due on the last day of finals. I contacted my dean about this because it was a violation of Yale's academic regulations (regular course work can't be due after the last day of reading period! know your rights!) and he reached out to the instructors but received no response, so I ended up working on the final report and the homework simultaneously. I've also only gotten two grades back at this point (the quiz grades) - no feedback on the first homework, my section participation/presentation, or the final presentation. I'm honestly kind of astonished that the course is still organized like this in its second decade of being offered at Yale, but maybe I shouldn't be given the sorry state of STEM education at this university.
