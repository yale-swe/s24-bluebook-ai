\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1669913\n 1669913\n \n 1669913\n Strengths: amazing Professor and TF (some of the best Ive ever had at Yale), incredibly interesting subject matter, great class discussions, detailed and personal writing feedback \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: sometimes I just wish we had more time to discuss everything! \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1676882\n 1676882\n \n 1676882\n Strengths: Marijeta is amazing, she is one of my three favourite professors at Yale and this is one of my 3 favourite courses after two years at Yale. Marijeta is super interesting and unapologetic in her opinions but also very balanced in expressing them. She is very eloquent and a careful reader and writer. She created mini writing workshops in class where we criticised "bad" writing pieces and commented on what worked and what didn't, which helped us become more aware as writers. Also, she always met with us after each paper to make sure we understood the feedback well which made it a lot easier to learn and incorporate her constructive feedback. She is a very demanding critic when it comes to writing and analysis which I really appreciated. Most writing credits don't actually make you a better writer but this class certainly does if you are willing to put in the work. She also met with me like 3 or 4 times before the final paper to help me brainstorm and was literally always enthusiastic to chat and share her knowledge and opinions. \r\nAnother strength is John, the TA. he is the best TA I've ever had. He has very interesting opinions and is unapologetic in expressing them, but also he, like Marijeta, always lets you be free to express your own opinions even if they differ from theirs. We mostly talked about pretty delicate topics so I appreciated feeling free to say what I thought, especially considering that I definitely didn't have their knowledge and so my opinions were a work-in-progress, which they definitely welcomed. John was incredibly available to chat on Slack about papers and always answered super quickly which really helped me with my papers, and I have never seen a TA put that much effort into helping students. \r\nWeaknesses: sometimes the course was a bit disorganised, which I definitely felt at the beginning and other times when the prompts weren't published on canvas and we had to ask for them multiple times or they hadn't downloaded the movies for a while so we had to watch them more last minute. I think that can easily be improved
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1676887\n 1676887\n \n 1676887\n The course was perfect in terms of class discussion management, topics, organization, and assignments. I wouldn't change anything.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1677099\n 1677099\n \n 1677099\n This course had a a great selection of works and a rather balanced workload.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1677262\n 1677262\n \n 1677262\n Strengths: Engaging and approachable professor, interesting material, well-structured classes\r\nWeaknesses: Discussions were sometimes hard to follow and I felt like the same people always spoke
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1681870\n 1681870\n \n 1681870\n The course looks at a larger variety of films and novels, some comedic and others gruesome, that kept me engaged with the course material. I loved Marijeta's style of teaching and how she interacted with students. The one critique I would give is that I would have liked to focus a bit more on the historical context for the actual fall of the Soviet Union and the rise of Putin because it would have helped level the playing field on knowledge between students in the class; we did focus on the history aspect a bit, but I would have liked to go more in-depth.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1682045\n 1682045\n \n 1682045\n This course has a good mix of literature and film, and is conscious of how students' workloads fluctuate throughout the semester (less workload during midterm season, etc.) It also covered fascinating works and inspired engaging discussions in class that encouraged us to look at the source material through a critical lens: who funded it? what does it represent beyond the obvious message? how does it mix genres? However, the course felt somewhat disorganized. We used a mix of Canvas and Slack, so it was at times difficult to keep track of the files we needed for the course. At times, Marijeta was also quite slow responding to emails, which was difficult when I was reaching out for support as I brainstormed an essay but only received a response after it was almost complete. I also had a hard time understanding exactly what the expectations were for some of the essays. Some of the feedback I received felt overly critical, and even when I improved it in the following essay it did not seem to be reflected in my grade. It was discouraging to see that an essay the professor said I did better on was graded lower than the prior one. Hence, the benchmarks I was trying to reach felt unclear, and it was stressful to work on my final project not knowing what the expectations were and not feeling like I could get all the support I needed.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1684442\n 1684442\n \n 1684442\n Overall the course offers a wide survey of post-Soviet Russian culture, but is somewhat thin on post-Soviet Russian history. The works of literature and film covered in the course need more contextualization. At times it was unclear why a certain work would be classified as "protest culture" and what social and political developments in Russia these works were influenced by or responding to.
