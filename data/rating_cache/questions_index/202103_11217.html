\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521401\n 1521401\n \n 1521401\n Mathematics of language, as the title suggests. Set theory, first-order logic, automata theory, regular languages, context-free grammars, proof-based math.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525643\n 1525643\n \n 1525643\n I had never studied set theory before, but now I know a lot about set theory!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528669\n 1528669\n \n 1528669\n I learned a TON in this course. A whole new way of approaching math, how to write proofs, introductions to set theory, automata and formal languages, etc. Even insights into what goes on in the mind/brain during language processing. This class is full of fun puzzles and neat concepts, most of which will change the way you think about language and the mind, and all of which feel like simply formalized versions of interesting games. Note: this class is mostly a proof-based math class, and applications to linguistics normally appear as a problem or two in the homework. Although the applications become more obvious and frequent in the second half of the course, don't be fooled by the LING listing--this class felt primarily like a math class (it should be double listed, IMO).

RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521401\n 1521401\n \n 1521401\n Strengths: fast-paced, addresses every concept precisely as opposed to handwavy arguments. weaknesses: turn around between submitting problem sets and receiving feedback is way too high. hard to know how you're doing in the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525643\n 1525643\n \n 1525643\n The course was really fast-paced and exciting. Sometimes the pace was too fast, especially at the beginning of the course (when we were first learning about set theory) and at the end of the course (when we were learning about languages). Not all of the set theory material seemed relevant to the end goal of explaining languages, so it might help to lighten the pace by cutting out some of the less relevant material (lattices, posets, algebras). Professor Frank was a great instructor. I'm not a math person, but he made the course very enjoyable! I would definitely take another course with him.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528669\n 1528669\n \n 1528669\n Strengths: this course does nearly everything right: lots of interesting learning that changes not only what you know but how you think; PSets are fun, cool, and interesting; grading is generous; intellectually challenging but never insurmountably difficult; dynamic, friendly, charismatic, funny, and engaging professor who always goes at the pace of the class (doesn't follow a strict schedule for the course--simply does as much as the class can handle and goes at our pace, moving on when we understand). The exams are all take-home and are basically just extended PSets.\r\nWeaknesses: the only weakness was that assignments were often sent out late and grades were sometimes slow to come back. The former of those things was normally a strength, however, as we were always given at least a week to do everything (Prof. Frank would often consult the class to decide jointly on a due date), so a late release of an assignment meant more of a rest-period between assignments and more time to do them. Only annoying if you care about having a fixed regular homework schedule.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536099\n 1536099\n \n 1536099\n Wonderful course all around, first few weeks were a bit boring for those already familiar with basic proof based mathematics, but I don't see any way around that.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549800\n 1549800\n \n 1549800\n This class was very enjoyable if you were engaged. The teaching style certainly can leave some people behind, meaning it's extremely important to ask questions in class; missing a couple minutes of lecture staring into space potentially means not understanding any of the rest of the lecture and struggling on the pset for sure. However, if you manage to stay with it, the class is very rewarding, especially for people like me who have no background in either linguistics or any of the mathematics covered.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555292\n 1555292\n \n 1555292\n Overall the course was very strong and interesting to me. It was pretty challenging at times, but ultimately very satisfying to come to some profound conclusions about the structure of language.\r\n\r\nI think the course could have been improved if the connections to natural language were a bit more immediate. In the beginning of the semester, I think a lot of us were wondering how set theory related to natural language.
