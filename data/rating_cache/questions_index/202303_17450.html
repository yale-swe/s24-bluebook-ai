\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1705623\n 1705623\n \n 1705623\n This course is a time sink. Be prepared for 2 50 min lectures + 50 min discussion section + 3 hour film screenings every week. It gets a lot. I felt as if I was spending more time attending this class than any other class, perhaps combined. The lectures are fine, but would help if the slides contained a bit more information as opposed to just Professor MacKay mentioning about crucial concepts once and never having the opportunity to revisit it (lectures are not recorded + slides have limited information, just film clips).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706014\n 1706014\n \n 1706014\n No weaknesses, except that I personally did not feel particularly engaged with certain readings/films.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706051\n 1706051\n \n 1706051\n Strengths: there is a lot of meat in the course; the close analysis assignments were genuinely helpful in improving the skills; most of the films screened were at least interesting to think about. Weaknesses: not a lot of variety in terms of accessibility / country / decade in the films, I wish there are more contemporary films or asian / african / south american films; the lectures are helpful usually but sometimes go on tangents that could've been spent teaching us the important materials; the theoretical readings are quite dense and difficult at times, some of them I feel like are too abstract to be good intro materials for students
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706785\n 1706785\n \n 1706785\n Strengths: Love Professor MacKay. Really interesting films. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1709560\n 1709560\n \n 1709560\n The syllabus is very well planned, covering a wide range of films across different time periods, themes, and concepts. The film screenings in Alice Cinema are so cool, some of the films are displayed in 35mm (not digital) so there is insane picture quality. No major weaknesses, except that there are no recordings and missing a class means you might have to figure out a lot of the content, and a lot of the things that the professor talks about are not elaborated on the slides.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1713422\n 1713422\n \n 1713422\n MacKay is a wonderful professor whose lectures are well-planned out and easy to follow. The screenings are around 3 hours long, which can seem daunting, but the great films you'll watch make up for it. Assignments are spaced out with enough time to craft a paper, and there is a lot of freedom around the topics you can write about. I think that is what makes this course so wonderful - the guidance it allows in developing sharp analysis skills while also giving students the freedom to bend those skills to the topics that most interest them in their analysis.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1713773\n 1713773\n \n 1713773\n The films studied in the course are generally very good, surprisingly global, and from a wide range of genres. Additionally, some screenings were very long, lasting more than 3 hours. Finally, the guidance regarding what to study for the final was very vague. Although, the exam itself was not unreasonably difficult.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714841\n 1714841\n \n 1714841\n A strength is the selection of the films. Theres a wide variety and most are really incredible. Prof MacKay is great but lectures can ramble and be a bit difficult to follow.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1720754\n 1720754\n \n 1720754\n Strengths: Professor MacKay was very interesting and had a lot of insight into the films and the theories we were learning.
