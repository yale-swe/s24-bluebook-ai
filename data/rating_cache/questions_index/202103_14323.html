\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1520940\n 1520940\n \n 1520940\n Strength - intellectual stimulation and rigor\r\nWeakness - Professor Handler Spitz is terrible at managing a classroom! She won't let students use the restroom (if you do, she sends you a menacing email!) and gave someone who had to miss a class due to a family emergency a very hard time about it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1532492\n 1532492\n \n 1532492\n I think the fall DS literature curriculum is largely superb - I really enjoyed the vast majority of the texts we read, and even for those that I didn't enjoy as much, I completely understood why we were reading them. It's the expected nature of DS that we're always rushing through these texts, so I wouldn't necessarily call it a weakness, but I will say that the end of the course in particular was very rushed. We might have been better off with 1 or 2 fewer texts at the end. Finally, the lectures were sometimes hit-or-miss: we sometimes had unbelievably amazing lectures, but we also had lectures that didn't feel worth their time. That said, even the bad lectures are more than made up for by section.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534970\n 1534970\n \n 1534970\n Due to the nature of DS, a lot of content is covered in a relatively short amount of time. This unfortunately leads to topics of interest being cut off in favor of moving forward in the curriculum. Professor Handler Spitz is a lovely individual who cares deeply about her students and the content she presents. Her passion for art and literature made everything more interesting and engaging.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534996\n 1534996\n \n 1534996\n The strongest aspect of this course was certainly the breadth of works covered in just a semester. But two corresponding weaknesses directly caused by the breadth was the fast pacing and sometimes disappointing depth. Some of the greatest works of Western literature were given a single week of exploration, and while I understand that moving quickly is essential to getting through the course material, it can feel disappointing when there is a work you especially feel connected with and wish to explore further. Unfortunately, there is no easy fix to this issue without cutting texts from the syllabus, which I feel is a bad idea. If anything, I wish more books could be added while having more time for each of them, which I acknowledge is am impossibility. It is a fast paced, rigorous course in which it easy to fall behind in. But if you commit to staying with the class and engaging with every text, this course is incredibly rewarding.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535045\n 1535045\n \n 1535045\n I adored this course. I think the emphasis on art, psychology, and personal experience was particularly meaningful and will make this class stick with me for a long time.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536944\n 1536944\n \n 1536944\n I loved the opportunity to read so intensely and widely. Just be aware that you'll be spending one class period on books that deserve to be studied for an entire semester!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538270\n 1538270\n \n 1538270\n Professor Handler Spitz was incredibly engaging and devoted to her students, and she helped me grew tremendously in the way that I approach reading, writing, discussion, and engaging with literature. There were times when the pacing of the course felt a bit rushed and I would have liked to spend more time on some of the texts, but that reflects more on the pacing of DS at large.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538798\n 1538798\n \n 1538798\n Strengths- As mentioned, the way the works of literature were intertwined with artwork (paintings, operas, etc.) made it a really engaging course. The professor was amazing and her passion for literature shown through every class, making it exciting for all. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses- Having students lead the discussions sometimes felt a bit unproductive, but at the same time it did allow students to share with the class a different passage/section that may of not been covered otherwise. Overall, I would urge these to just be more concise in the future is all.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539558\n 1539558\n \n 1539558\n This emphasis on learning about the seminal western texts through reading and discussion is a strength of this course. Being able to read a text, and have a discussion about the many different interpretation everyone has on it, helped enrich the conversation we had.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1543515\n 1543515\n \n 1543515\n The strengths of the course were myriad. For me, this DS Literature section had enormous strength in its seminar discussions (a truly wonderful hermeneutic circle, with many differing ideas) and in its assignments (three papers and optional passage responses allowed any particular student to really enter into, and figure out, a particular text for themselves). The nature of any DS section is an extremely fast pace, and one would have liked to have read Dante over the course of a semester; yet this pacing is also a great strength of the course---excitement never waned.
