\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618725\n 1618725\n \n 1618725\n Some introductory results in measure theory, and their development in probability theory. Measures, probability measures, and applications to prove basic laws like the LLN, and at the end the CLT. Probability on product spaces, culminating in conditional probability which is intuitive if you think about it enough but is MUCH more complex than one would think from elementary probability. Most importantly, you get a sense of how probability operates, so that you understand what proofs mean and can start to read some probability theory papers. Numbers seem exact while probability is random, so knowing what is actually happening under the surface is the key insight from this class. Theres also some application to Margingales near the end.

However, the course felt extremely fast-paced due to having to review all of measure theory in the first few weeks, and it also could have used a lot more analogues and examples from regular discrete probability to help with intuition

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618725\n 1618725\n \n 1618725\n I loved the class. Clear strengths were the lectures and associated notes, which were extremely intuitive, and final project/problem sets, and the professor. It is the best taught class Ive taken in the department. Sekhar is REALLY good at understanding what is intuitive in his way of presentation. If you study his lectures enough, you feel like you understand what is happening; his proofs dont skip steps and everything is motivated (albeit not always concretely, but much more than common in math). The chosen textbook by Pollard is similarly excellent, although the proofs skip a lot of steps that Sekhar fills in, motivates, and extends. Maybe a weakness is that he could give out treats at lectures to reinforce attendance cuz otherwise its hard to make the trek to science hill but thats a stretch. The final project required more work than most finals but less grade stress and gave much more motivation if you found a good paper (find a good paper!). I thought it was much better exercise than making you memorize unmotivated details from the class to forget later. However, to be clear, this class is hard. Sure you could get an A tightly collaborating/not-quite-copying your psets and picking an easy final project paper, but the class is set up to help you actually learn and if you do that its a very high but not crazy high workload.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1624482\n 1624482\n \n 1624482\n By the nature of the course, there was a need to find a middle ground between those who have taken measure theory before this course and those that have not. Thus, some may find that the course is either too slow or too fast depending on whether one belongs to the former or latter. I personally found that the course was a bit slow in the beginning, but went a bit fast nearing the end of the semester (once we started conditioning).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1630291\n 1630291\n \n 1630291\n The course is taught well, and the problem sets really helped me process and master the material.
