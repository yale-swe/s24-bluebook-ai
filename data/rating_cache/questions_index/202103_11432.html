We learn about listening, developing a shared pool of meaning, asking power questions, intellectual and emotional humility, and gratitude. I learned how to be more conscious of how I communicate. I learned how to listen to understand, rather than reply. I also learned how to SHOW that I'm listening. I learned how to ask good questions that draw out the best in other people. I learned how to navigate the thorniest of conversations while remaining both completely honest AND respectful. I learned how to be a better public speaker. I learned how to be a better writer through adhering to Peter's writing rules. All of these skills don't just help you be a more smooth communicator, but also help you learn from others and be a more empathetic and supportive partner, teammate, friend, and family member. In addition to these communication skills, I also learned just important life skills like how to experience and express gratitude through keeping a gratitude journal and writing a gratitude letter to someone and giving it to them. Through class readings but also through Peter conducting the class, I learned how to create spaces for people to open up and be honest and vulnerable. Peter even taught us very practical skills like how to do a handshake, how to conduct yourself at a networking event, and how to hold a microphone. The best part was that we all got to practice these in class. It's hard to believe that there's even more that this class taught me that I haven't written down. But in sum, this class has not only taught me how to be a better teammate and leader, but a better friend, family member, partner, and human being. It has made me less cynical. I learned that almost everyone has good reasons for doing things and incredible stories to share, if only we're willing to listen.

Since we had 20 students in the class, he held at least 60 office hours over the course of the semester! And this isn't even his day job! I've never seen a professor invest so much in a course. After all of the one-on-one time we spent together, Peter was able to teach me things about myself (like my strengths) that I had never even known. He organized class so thoughtfully and not a single minute in class or doing work for the class felt wasted. He assigned us seats randomly for every class so that we wouldn't feel anxious about where we were going to sit and to get to know each other better. While it seems like every class syllabus touts inclusivity, Peter is the only instructor I've ever had to make class feel like a truly safe space. Throughout the semester, I felt like I could be my whole, vulnerable self in front of my classmates and be accepted and celebrated for who I am, even when I made mistakes. I can go on and on; the point is that Peter is the best teacher I've ever had.
