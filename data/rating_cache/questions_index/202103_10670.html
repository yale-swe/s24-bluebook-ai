\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1526710\n 1526710\n \n 1526710\n Professor Mayhew is obviously incredibly knowledgeable about the subject, and I found that his insights were valuable, as well as his clear passion for Congress. That was a big strength of the course, as was the assignment structure, which I really enjoyed-- it allowed me to write about topics that I was particularly interested in. I think a weakness of the course is that in-class discussion is not very structured; at times, I found discussion to be incredibly productive and engaging (such as the final class, when we talked about Watergate), but at other times, it was just the professor lecturing to us. While Mayhew's knowledge is valuable, that can be tedious in a 2-hour seminar.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1526738\n 1526738\n \n 1526738\n Some strengths of this course include the pace and the Professor. Professor Mayhew is really knowledgeable in this field so it made discussions with him all the more rewarding and insightful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1526767\n 1526767\n \n 1526767\n The greatest strength about this course, by far, is the instructor - Professor David Mayhew. Professor Mayhew's contributions to the course, and the field of political science in general, cannot be understated. The professor provides insightful feedback on coursework, and makes excellent contributions during the seminar - allowing students to the lead the conversation, while facilitating it to ensure students have all the necessary context. Additionally, the reading selections were excellent and all very interesting in their own right. As for improvements, the only two are that the reading load can be a bit much, especially towards the end of the semester. Professor Mayhew warns students about this, so it's no surprise, however, there is quite a bit of reading to do. Also, since it is a seminar setting, the class should be held in a space more conducive for seminars. This semester, the classroom did not have enough tables or space to accommodate all students, which made it difficult at times to achieve full participation. This, however, is more of an operational or administrative suggestion, not necessarily reflective of the content of the course itself.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528360\n 1528360\n \n 1528360\n I appreciated that the workload for this class was customizable for each student. All you need to do is complete weekly readings (on average it was 150 pages per week), participate in seminar discussion, and choose 5 weeks to write a 3-5 page comment essay on the assigned readings. I rarely felt overwhelmed by this class because I could plan to work on these essays during my less busy weeks. It would take me around 4-6 hours per week to complete the reading, and another 3-5 to write an essay. Professor Mayhew always provides feedback on essays within 2-3 days of submission.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528880\n 1528880\n \n 1528880\n Mayhew is the strength of this course. He knows every fact there is to know about Congress, but more importantly he has wonderful insights about all the facts he knows. He leads wonderful discussions every week and assigns great readings. The two narrative readings at the end of the semester, although very long, are very fun and teach a lot about the forces at work in Congress.\r\n\r\nThe only downside of the class is that as the semester ramps up, many students do not do all the readings. Furthermore, as the semester went on, students started to pay less attention to the conversation and instead did other work on their laptops or merely played games. I think Mayhew should ban all screens in his seminar. This cannot force students to do the reading but it does force them to pay attention.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545100\n 1545100\n \n 1545100\n Strengths: amazing professor, interesting content, reasonable workload, flexibility in when we wanted to write our papers.\r\nWeaknesses: sometimes class discussion would feel a bit all over the place. More structure would've been good.
