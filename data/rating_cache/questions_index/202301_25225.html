\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1665685\n 1665685\n \n 1665685\n Strengths: A fascinating selection of texts are read and a wide variety of concepts are covered; I felt extremely intellectually stimulated but didnt feel at all like we covered too much content for us to adequately discuss it. A perfect balance! Moreover, I felt that the in-class discussions were uniformly excellent. Although this could simply be attributed to the assortment of students in the class, I felt that professor Brass had an excellent understanding of how to properly moderate discussion; every day I left class feeling that Id said everything I wanted to say and also discovered many new things that I hadnt thought of before!\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The course is highly comparative, so some knowledge of German philosophy and aesthetic history could be useful in getting the most out of the course, but certainly not necessary, as my knowledge is limited but I still had a wonderful time and felt extremely satisfied.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1665899\n 1665899\n \n 1665899\n This is a fantastic course which explored this multi faceted topic in an engaging, informative and fun way. The professor was very accommodating and encouraged our conversations which often went very deep and we all learnt a lot in the process.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1665951\n 1665951\n \n 1665951\n What troubled me most about the course had nothing really to do with the syllabus or the structure, which I think are both very strong, especially with the mindset of anticipating divergences, but with this particular student constituency. I found that one of my classmates saw it fit to take up a disproportionate amount of the seminar airtime, so to speak, and it hindered my ability to participate and really invest myself. If they hadn't occupied so much space, I think we would have collectively gotten more out of the class, but maybe that's just me.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1671672\n 1671672\n \n 1671672\n Strengths:\r\n- Varied and interesting syllabus\r\n- Exciting ways of student engagement--not just a typical seminar discussion, but the presentation component was super well-received and was my favorite aspect.\r\n- We always found a way to make our readings relevant to our life and other intellectual interests, so everything felt salient and inter-related.\r\n\r\nNot so much weaknesses as 'considerations':\r\n- Not much theory, but for good reason\r\n- The amount of reading varied quite a bit, though with good forewarning. Some weeks we'd have pretty long, whole books, and other weeks short stories. (But we'd always know in advance when a reading-heavy week would be approaching)\r\n- There could be more structure to our class discussions, but the open-ness also contributed to in-the-moment revelations and meandering and highly productive comments.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1665685\n 1665685\n \n 1665685\n This was the best course I took this semester. Even though I had little knowledge of German literature or philosophy, I felt that the texts we read were so exemplary of the concepts we covered (which were expertly discussed) that I felt completely comfortable wading into an area which I wasnt previously familiar with. I think one of the drawbacks with explicitly-titled introductory or survey courses is that they truly just scratch the surface of the topics which they cover; this class, by contrast, took the students deep into a relatively wide range of German (and world) literature that left me feeling very satisfied!
