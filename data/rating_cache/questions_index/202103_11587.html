\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1529738\n 1529738\n \n 1529738\n Strengths: Professor Lee-Smith is absolutely the best. This class has every potential to be a very stressful language class with all grammar and vocab thrown to a beginner, yet Professor Lee-Smith made everything so accessible, engaging and just so fun to learn. When I talked to my other friends about the grammar structure we are learning in class, they said they were only learning similar things in L3, but we never felt such structure was challenging at all. We would just practice some grammatical structures or vocab one day and somehow we ended up with the ability to write an entire story. Professor Lee-Smith's teaching methodology is truly legendary, and I remember her saying not just once that she wants to assess us based on what we can do, not what we can't. That was just such a comforting and uplifting thing to hear. Even as a beginner, I never felt judged or embarrassed when I make a mistake, perhaps that's why my level of comfort with Korean has improved a lot just over the course of 4 months.\r\nI really can't think of any weakness ... If I have to pick one, perhaps for someone very used to the "punitive" type of language learning, sometimes you don't really feel you are learning a lot because Professor Lee-Smith very seldom gave negative comments, especially in speaking. But then again, no one has perfect pronunciations from the start and being overly conscious of how you pronounce a certain consonant might not help in improving your general fluency in the language. So I wouldn't really consider this a weakness - unless you are really obsessed with getting everything absolutely right from the beginning.
