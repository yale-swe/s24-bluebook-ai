\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1652229\n 1652229\n \n 1652229\n The second semester was better than the first because we were allowed to focus entirely on our project instead of having to complete random assignments that added no value to our project and simply wasted our time (like building a mock chicken coop). However, there were still a couple of lectures which again were irrelevant as they did not communicate useful engineering concepts. We also had to complete irrelevant assignments every two weeks or so which had no relation to our project and simply wasted our time.\r\n\r\nOur primary advisor did not get any input with regard to our grades, and in the first semester of this course, our grades were determined based on 'Peer Evaluations", "Instructor Judgement" and 10 minute meetings with TAs rather than by the quality of our work. The TAs and instructor felt they knew exactly who was doing what without ever once seeing us work as a team together. The end result was that for MENG 487 (fall 2022) we all received different grades and the grades were in no way correlated to our performance or the quality of our work. To say I am dissatisfied with the way MENG 487/8 was run would be a gross understatement!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1658116\n 1658116\n \n 1658116\n Strengths: it's a great, hands-off approach to allow the students to work with a company and their peers to produce and present a year-long mechanical design project. For much of the semester, the only required meeting times are for the presentations and the biweekly 10-20 minute meetings with the instructors to check in with your project. You as a team must decide by yourselves when to meet, what to do, and who does what. When you do need help, there is ample support to give you a nudge in the right direction. \r\nWeaknesses: your project, teammates, company, and advisors honestly do really affect your experience in this course, it's very very important to have great teammates, great communication, and a project you at the very least are somewhat interested in. Question 9 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1650677\n 1650677\n \n 1650677\n a word of advice: do NOT let your senior year become about this course. If you let it ruin your life, it will. No amount of tiredness, sleeplessness, exhaustion, etc is worth it. This course will test you, not because the engineering is hard, but because group projects are the worst possible form of education. Your teammates will likely be stupid, lazy, incompetent, or all of the above. Putting in anything more than the bare minimum amount of effort will only teach your useless teammates how to freeload. You will receive limited feedback from your client because they are actual adults who have actual projects that actually matter unlike this one.
