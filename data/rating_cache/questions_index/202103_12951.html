Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521277\n 1521277\n \n 1521277\n Strengths: this is a hands-on class. If you want practical knowledge more than knowledge of history or theory, this is the class for you. The professor is also extremely helpful and willing to spend one-on-one time outside of class to help students. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: the class could be a bit more rigorous. I think clearer and firmer due dates would motivate people to come by the press on their own time, which would allow us to complete more projects in a semester. It might also help to mandate some number of hours for students to spend in the press outside of class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544079\n 1544079\n \n 1544079\n Strengths were that it was a very fun class to be in, and wasn't a stressful commitment.\r\nWeaknesses were that the teacher was new and so the class was a little unorganized, meaning we didn't complete many projects.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544084\n 1544084\n \n 1544084\n The best part about this course was the chance to receive such personalized instruction from Professor Marsolais. He strongly emphasized a hands-on experience with the printing press, which I think was the best way for me to make tangible progress on creative projects. Professor Marsolais also included exciting lectures and demonstrations that complemented the more menial and mechanical steps required in the printing press. There were sometimes a few issues with class timing and organization, but I think that this was largely due to the short time block of the class period, and overall it did not hinder the class experience.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544287\n 1544287\n \n 1544287\n Typesetting is not accessible, so I'm very grateful for being able to take a class on it this semester! Jesse is a professional in the field, so he knew a lot of tricks and tips that really helped move things along for me (such as the carbon paper impression). However, because the syllabus required us to move at a faster pace, some of us ultimately did not have time to finish our projects...
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544455\n 1544455\n \n 1544455\n Strengths - engaging with an actual printing press and getting to see the Yale Center for British Art's collection of old books definitely informed my aesthetic as a designer, as I've always been drawn to vintage-inspired pieces. The assignments can be really fun and fulfilling if you typeset something that's meaningful to you. Professor Marsolais is also very passionate about his work, which is great. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses - the course wasn't that well organized so it was hard to be motivated until the very end, especially because there's such a steep learning curve. At times, the course feels less creative than an actual graphic design class and more like repetitive labor, which can be nice for de-stressing during finals but also frustrating during the rest of the semester. It would have been nice to learn more about graphic design history etc or even to have more specific assignments to help inform our creative process.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1551080\n 1551080\n \n 1551080\n Strengths: Prof is incredibly passionate about both the subject and the class and he is willing to devote a ton of time outside of class to students who put in the effort. It is interesting learning about the history of printing. You will never have the chance to take a class like this again! \r\nWeaknesses: The pacing of the class could be improved. Many of the students felt rushed to complete the major benchmarks of the class (there are three main projects throughout the semester). Additionally, a clearer introduction at the beginning of the term of each step of the printing process (maybe accompanied by a handout for future reference) would have been helpful. I didn't feel like I really understand how to operate the press until the last few weeks of the semester.
