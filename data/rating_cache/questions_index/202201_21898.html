\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1564938\n 1564938\n \n 1564938\n I believe a strength is that the course offers entry-level material that is easy to understand and learn. If you already are interested in clean energy chances are you already know a lot of the things talked about in the class (although it's always good to review), but if you don't know much this is a great place to get started! Another strength was the guest lectures Professor Prober brought in at the end. Also, the workload is not high and very manageable, especially with the help of the TAs. The final group project is not stressful (as long as you pace yourself and find a group you work well with). Midterm was also fairly based on lectures and slides, no trick questions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1564961\n 1564961\n \n 1564961\n Strength is that Professor Prober definitely cares about his students, is generous with his time, and clearly very passionate about the topics. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: So much of this course was taught on Zoom because of guest speakers, and I don't think the disengagement with virtual lectures was worth it. Would've much rather had 1 Zoom guest speaker max. I would've appreciated more variety for different learning styles. Lastly, and most importantly for me, there was no connection to systems of oppression, which is deeply related to all of the course content. There was no mention of the displacement of and/or violent harm towards BIPOC communities when discussing pipelines, thinking about global conflicts over oil, and so much more. It is imperative that we ingrain these acknowledgements (at the very least!) in all courses, ESPECIALLY stem courses with legacies of scientific racism and false claims of objectivity/neutrality.
