\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594893\n 1594893\n \n 1594893\n The wide range of required reading means that this course is very comprehensive. We read Thoreau's proses, poems, biographies, journals, and articles written by other scholars about him. All these served to give me all the necessary information to truly understand, or at least begin to understand, the significance of Henry Thoreau. The weakness of this course might be that we sometimes cannot possibly cover all the materials listed in the syllabus because we didn't have enough time. This is a shame for if all the readings were properly discussed and evaluated in the seminar, my experience would be much better. One great thing about this course is that we needed to post comments on Canvas every Monday in preparation for our in-class discussions on Tuesday. This helped us exchange thoughts and opinions on usually elusive writings of Thoreau and without it, I would find it difficult to analyse these readings. I hope this measure could be extended to seminars on Thursday as well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594916\n 1594916\n \n 1594916\n Strengths: Professor Warner leads the course with the perfect balance between his own expertise and student contribution. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1597418\n 1597418\n \n 1597418\n Strengths: discussion prompts were provided by students. Field trip!\r\nWeaknesses: I wish Professor Warner had been a little clearer in advance about what we would discuss on any given day. I had trouble narrowing down what I wanted to write my reading responses on.
