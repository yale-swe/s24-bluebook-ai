\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1571066\n 1571066\n \n 1571066\n Incredibly well connected Professor, thoroughly organized, prompt feedback. Readings could be toned down through editing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1575655\n 1575655\n \n 1575655\n I enjoyed the diversity of topics that were presented each week, as well as the guest speakers who were experts in their respective fields. Prof. Simons brings so much of his knowledge and experience to the table. Simulating the G20 negotiations during the final class was also engaging. I do think spending more time discussing specific readings during class time (since the readings were generally quite interesting) would make the class even better.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1576305\n 1576305\n \n 1576305\n Strengths: Professor Simons is a gem of a professor and a mentor, and his enthusiasm for teaching and the subject matter shines through everything you learn in the class. The Fireside Chat guests help bring the course material to life and are very interesting. The assignments are super cool and really engage you with the class material. Very well-organized, and great feedback on the assignments (especially at the midterm). Good office hours accessibility. I liked the group work in class.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I wish some of the Fireside Chat guests could visit in person, and I also wish there was more diversity/representation among them (especially more women!). I would also argue that energy justice and equity are key themes that should be through-lines of the entire course; sometimes it felt like we glossed over those important lenses when evaluating approaches to the clean energy transition. More emphasis on that (or a few more readings) would be great if an entire class can't be dedicated to it. Lastly, I think the discussion questions were hard to answer sometimes--I often felt like I had to answer "THIS" or "THAT" as the most promising solution for the future, and so many times I was confused why it couldn't be both this AND that. So opening the questions more broadly would be helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1580635\n 1580635\n \n 1580635\n The strengths are the literature shared by the Professor. The Professor himself was always well prepared and forthcoming. \r\n\r\nI think that may be the course could have touched on the developing and emerging countries perspective and how their reservations and constraints can be addressed as the developed countries the question is primarily political whereas in emerging countries it is social, political and economic as well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1582403\n 1582403\n \n 1582403\n The course was amazing. Very well structured I learned so much.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1599783\n 1599783\n \n 1599783\n The course material was quite interesting and Ambassador Simons gave a ton of personal insight and expertise into the matter. We were given examples of previous submissions of all of the other assignments, and it was very difficult to work on the final without that same baseline and not very much guidance.
