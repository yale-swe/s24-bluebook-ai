RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622316\n 1622316\n \n 1622316\n This course has broadened my perspective on the definition of genius and if there truly is a definition for genius. You come into the course with a pretty solid opinion regarding the definition of genius, and that opinion won't survive much longer. Musical Genius emphasizes the nuances of this terminology while allowing me to learn about culture, music, perceptions on disabilities, and much more. I've learned that we are wrong when we talk about "society" because we easily overlook how many societies exist in the world that have different judgements and perspectives on what genius truly is. The mixture of music and philosophy, along with other academic subjects, has intrigued me in so many ways and has formed opinions and thought processes that I see myself using outside this course as well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622323\n 1622323\n \n 1622323\n I learned how to engage with many different types of academics texts, including writing about them and discussing them in class. We read everything between very old scientific papers to contemporary critical essays, and doing so helped me understand different methods of analyzing similar topics.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622467\n 1622467\n \n 1622467\n This course probes one to question the assumptions they make about the concept of musical genius as well as talent and success in general. I was shocked by how several of the readings contradicted my initial beliefs about genius. The course also discusses the societal implications of genius and its perceptions of it, which I believe was the most meaningful part of the course. Prof Wright was also very helpful in providing advice for writing in general as well as reading and summarizing. Above all, I think this first-year seminar helped me formulate nuanced ideas and thoughts in a discussion setting.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622474\n 1622474\n \n 1622474\n I gained a nuanced understanding of various musicological perspectives on the perception of musical genius. I gained practice with reading professional level publications, and learned how to communicate my thoughts concisely through in class discussion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622497\n 1622497\n \n 1622497\n Through readings and discussions on numerous debates and contradictions on musical genius and genius in general, I learned not to discretize debates on not just genius, but issues in general. There are so many nuances to account for in any debate, with information almost never totally supporting one side when there is no concrete, empirical answer.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1626024\n 1626024\n \n 1626024\n The biggest insight I developed was a refined ability to look beyond the surface level of a subject. Reality TV competitions, for example, appear to be fun and games, but Ive learned to analyze the deeper meaning behind the choices production teams make. There are also deeper meanings behind words depending on the context and time period. I developed a strong ability to read text from a variety of different perspectives. These varying perspectives have strengthened my ability to argue from conflicting POVs. \r\nMy writing skills were also developed thanks to Professor Wright and her constructive advice.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627617\n 1627617\n \n 1627617\n I learned how to engage with difficult academic scholarly writing. There is a lot of technical musical elements that you learn to navigate in the class (if you aren't a musician) so from that perspective it was definitely doable. I learned how to better my argumentative essay writing, how to quickly read lengthier materials, and how to summarize complex texts. Professor Wright challenged us to write essays taking two sides of an argument, in which we could not favor either side but present them equally well. Another one of our assignments asked for us to write a satire using the different themes of each unit, and our presentation was one of the highlights of the semester. I liked how I was able to engage creatively with this class and learn a lot in the process.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1628703\n 1628703\n \n 1628703\n I developed my essay writing skills and my knowledge of how genius works.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629895\n 1629895\n \n 1629895\n I learned to write and read effectively. The most important skill I developed in this class is recognizing and understanding the nuances of topics.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1631798\n 1631798\n \n 1631798\n I developed my writing and reading analysis skills as well as my critical thinking. I learned to question concepts I had previously accepted without thought.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1637089\n 1637089\n \n 1637089\n This class was very helpful in evaluating nuanced issues from multiple point of views. Analyzing the concept of musical genius from contradicting sides allowed me to consider things I would not have considered otherwise and to read articles that I likely would not have sought out. Even though this is not a writing class, the feedback from this class helped my writing significantly, maybe even more so than my English class.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622467\n 1622467\n \n 1622467\n I think the format of the course, in which students must argue for both sides of a debate on genius, is a very insightful one. One thing that could be improved was the class structure as we often ran out of time during discussions, but this was pointed out in the mid-semester evaluations and was better by the end of the semester. Obviously, there being almost too many things to talk about is fantastic for the course as there is no shortage of materials to study inside and outside of class. Furthermore, discussions were thoroughly engaging thanks to the plethora of engaging content to discuss.
