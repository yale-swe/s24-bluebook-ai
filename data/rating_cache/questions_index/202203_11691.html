\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1610464\n 1610464\n \n 1610464\n Interesting material, very low workload -- just problem sets, one presentation, and an in-class final exam. Professor Chalioti is amazing and really cares about her students! No improvements!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1611896\n 1611896\n \n 1611896\n Professor Chaliotis teaching was the main strength of this course. She was passionate about the material and kept it interesting through less exciting topics. The course was a good chance to practice problem solving skills and offered insight into the future of AI. The main downside was that AI was not present for all the material in the class and it sometimes felt like discussions about AI were a very small part of class with the rest being focused on econ topics and problem solving. I wish the two could have been integrated further.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1613457\n 1613457\n \n 1613457\n Strengths: the material is super interesting and the class itself is very chill (meets once a week with straightforward psets for which Professor Chalioti provides office hours). If you were one of the unlucky students who took ECON 121 without Chalioti, she is an absolutely amazing professor and teacher. Many other economics faculty lack the ability to effectively communicate ideas and concepts with students in the classroom -- this has never been the case with Professor Chalioti. It is so clear how much time and effort she puts into making her course material accessible and useful. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I can't really think of many, although maybe a smaller class size. In the beginning of the course, there was never really time for discussion and the course felt similar to a lecture. That changed as the semester progressed, but it would have been even better with a more intimate class setting in which people could ask student presenters questions and there could be more connection to outside readings, videos, podcasts, and other instructional materials.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625857\n 1625857\n \n 1625857\n Strengths: Professor Chalioti and the course content. I was super engaged throughout the semester, and Professor Chalioti explained everything very thoroughly.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Professor Chalioti did not grade the final presentation or memo before the semester ended. So every single student basically went into the final with a 100% from psets and participation. I wish she had graded these other huge assignments earlier! Its very stressful to not know these grades (theyre still not even in Canvas).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1628028\n 1628028\n \n 1628028\n A major strength was Prof. Chalioti, and the material that the course was focused around. Professor Chalioti had many interesting insights on innovation through personal experience and from interesting colleagues, and also presented our material in a very digestible manner. I found lectures very interesting, and never too fast-paced. The qualitative material covered about how firms innovate and protect their innovations was also really cool to learn about since they have many applications to the real world. A bit of a downside were that some of the math concepts didn't seem to wholly line up with what the rest of our weekly lecture covered - it almost seemed like two back-to-back lectures about entirely different concepts in some weeks. Quickly switching gears between these topics was a little difficult at times, but it by far was not a deal breaker.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1638594\n 1638594\n \n 1638594\n The course is relatively evenly split between economics concepts and the mathematical foundations of those concepts. Professor Chalioti knows both sides of the coin well.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1639222\n 1639222\n \n 1639222\n This course was very informative.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1640660\n 1640660\n \n 1640660\n The strengths of this course are both the content and the Professor. From day 1, where we discussed current trends in artificial intelligence and the digitization of many economic systems, the course felt extremely relevant to today and to the future of a functioning economy. Professor Chalioti did a wonderful job mixing economic theory and math with the real-life applications of our exercises, which made for a very interesting class both mathematically and conceptually. Professor Chalioti is also a wonderful professor who is a strength in any course she teaches.
