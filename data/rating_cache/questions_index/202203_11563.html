\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1611905\n 1611905\n \n 1611905\n Professor Elbousty is definitely the course's greatest strength. He makes sure to bring everyone into the conversation and is so passionate about the material. Above all, he supports and cares about his students like few other professors I've had at Yale. The class became a community really quickly and the environment was very welcoming and warm. \r\n\r\nI think any improvements to the course would involve changing the texts on the syllabus. Most of the works we read weren't originally written in Arabic, which didn't feel fitting for an Arabic course -- I think it would be good to either go back to teaching this course in English as in the past, or teach the class in Arabic with a heavier focus on Arabic literature (rather than English novels or complex theory translated into Arabic). My favorite book of the semester by far was Season of Migration to the North -- the literary quality of the book was very high as compared to the others, and it was a great experience reading an Arabic classic in its original language and improving my Arabic through that. I would love if the whole semester was books like Season of Migration to the North. The other thing I would have benefitted from is more feedback on my work and language skills throughout the semester -- perhaps having a paper during the semester that we get detailed comments on, rather than just the final paper.
