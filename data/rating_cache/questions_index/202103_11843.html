\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1554861\n 1554861\n \n 1554861\n This course was strong in that it taught students a useful method for solving physics problems, and I found the units on kinematics, forces, work, and energy to be the most useful.\r\n\r\nOn the other hand, there are many improvements that must be made:\r\n\r\n- Weekly recitation problems were meant to enhance our knowledge of concepts learned during lecture, but the selected problems were often so specific that they did not prove very useful outside of recitation. Weekly sections would likely be more useful if they were held in a format more similar to office hours, allowing students to ask questions and proceed through worked examples as a group.\r\n\r\n- There must be greater communication between the instructor and the TAs. Many times, the TAs were not able to provide the same solution to homework problems or recitation problems; this made it difficult to study for quizzes and exams. Furthermore, differences in grading policies between TAs and the instructor caused various grading issues that had to be rectified.\r\n\r\n- I often found that we did not go over enough example problems in class to adequately prepare us for the homework assignments, which often took an inordinate amount of time to complete without the help of a TA in office hours. In the future, problems akin to those found on the PSET should be covered in class such that students are actively learning and practicing important concepts.\r\n\r\n- In future years/semesters, it would be beneficial to use a different, published textbook. This semester's textbook was often difficult to navigate due to an overabundance of lengthy equations with little indications as to which were most important. Furthermore, almost all of the example problems in the textbook are rather complex; students require less complex problems at the beginning of a chapter such that a grasp of basic principles can be achieved before moving on to more complex ones. Lastly, the textbook used during this semester focused on a variety of topics that did not seem entirely appropriate for a physics course---even with a life sciences application (e.g., probability). Many students who enroll in this course are looking for a curriculum that prepares them to take the MCAT; as a premedical student, I found that some of the topics covered in this course had little overlap with MCAT material---something I found surprising given the name of the course.\r\n\r\n- The course's weekly schedule should be reevaluated. More specifically, a more appropriate quiz day should be chosen. By holding recitation on Thursday, the quiz on Monday, and setting the PSET deadline for Sunday/Monday, students must review, apply, and study each week's material largely over the course of about three days. To make the material more manageable, the amount of material covered must be decreased, or the schedule must be rearranged such that students have more time to work through problems and ask questions.\r\n\r\n- I found that the release of grades and uploading of course documents (e.g., quiz and recitation files) was often inconsistent or delayed; in future semesters, it would be beneficial for students to receive feedback on a more consistent and efficient basis.
