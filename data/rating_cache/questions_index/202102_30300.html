\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1514935\n 1514935\n \n 1514935\n I believe the strengths of this course are that the heavy homework load and the inquisitive questioning really provokes the student to engage in deep thought and personal reflection with their own personal principles. It engages the students' personal biases and attempts to shed light on potential different routes of thinking. I also think that another strength with the course was its transparency on what was expected on assignments, and the guiding discussions that were shared prior to class starting. One thing that I did consider a weakness of the course was how heavily regulated the class discussion was at times. Being in a virtual format made it difficult without doubt to engage in seamless conversation, but the discussion style really revolved around the instructor calling on people with their hands raised. I would have thought that if the virtual format was going to be continued, that it would adopt something similar to a round-table or harkness table discussion, where any student that wished to contribute would just unmute and speak up, instead of having to stick to a long order of speakers. I recounted that at times, the order/line for speakers became too long to the extent where even the students and the instructor would forget who was supposed to speak next. And at other times, the thought or contributions that a student might have had to input may have lost its relevance to the conversation.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1515081\n 1515081\n \n 1515081\n I liked about the course that the texts we had to read were interesting to discuss. The mid-term and final questions were also well-chosen and I liked especially the question for our final exam because the case of Charlie Hebdo which we wrote about is a case that was in the news all around the world. Even though the course was focused on the First Amendment of the American constitution, I really liked that the final exam introduced an international insight on the issue of free speech. The course overall was multifaceted and the content we learned was well-organized into the five weeks we had. \r\nThe only thing that I could think about to improve would be to minimize the assigned material within the weeks in which we write our final and mid term exam. For example, I had one week in which I completed the material we had assigned relatively fast and the week of our final exam had a lot of assigned material. However, I am not sure if it is possible to actually distribute the material differently because it was with regard to the content well organized. If distributing the amount of assigned work before class differently would mess up the overall organization of the course, I would not like the different distribution and would leave the course as it is without suggesting anything that could be improved.
