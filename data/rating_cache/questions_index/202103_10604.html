\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530446\n 1530446\n \n 1530446\n Composing a cohesive larger work and preserving motivic unity throughout, modes/non-diatonic scales, different instruments
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531141\n 1531141\n \n 1531141\n We worked on music composition, specifically use of pandiatonicism and pitched percussion instruments. I developed my skills in Sibelius and Logic Pro, as well as knowledge of many different orchestral instruments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535127\n 1535127\n \n 1535127\n I learned a lot about music theory, and classical music composition.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536522\n 1536522\n \n 1536522\n in class you'll learn about the "ingredients" (like scales, ranges for instruments, playing techniques) of composition and final products. in the lessons, is where you'll really learn about the process w/ the school of music graduate students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540695\n 1540695\n \n 1540695\n Before taking this class, I had a lot of experience with topics in composition including chromatic harmony, motivic development, musical structures, etc. However, this class did challenge me and make me learn things I wouldn't have otherwise:\r\n1) Expanding my harmonic palette through pandiatonicism \r\n2) Expanding my rhythmic vocabulary through an emphasis on mixed meter through the course\r\n3) Careful formatting - as a composer, I've always been focused on solely the compositional process, and I never paid attention to the formatting and legibility of my scores. However, this class did force me to work on those skills, and they are tools I will need to use extensively in the future.

RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525716\n 1525716\n \n 1525716\n Strengths: Individual projects.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Limitations on individual projects. Requirements often felt like a hassle and a hamper on creativity.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1526733\n 1526733\n \n 1526733\n Strengths include the very warm and helpful faculty and students, the integration with the grad program during the guest lectures, the access to New Music New Haven performances, and the opportunity to devote the semester to an open-ended composition project with helpful tech resources, lessons, and incremental deadlines. The one way I think the course could be improved would be to provide a bit more of a nudge earlier on in the semester to meet our classmates and colleagues -- maybe by moving the visual media group project to earlier in the semester or offering an event with all the students.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530362\n 1530362\n \n 1530362\n I thought the course was extremely well designed because there was a good balance of theoretical lectures and actual composing. We also got to meet a lot of really cool modern composers, which was incredibly inspiring. The only thing I would consider a weakness is that the lecturing can sometimes feel long since the class is almost 2 hours long, but we only met once per week and these lectures were only towards the beginning of the semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530446\n 1530446\n \n 1530446\n Strengths: the semester-long assignment is super open-ended and you can basically compose whatever you want, as long as you fulfill the requirements (which are not super hard to fulfill)! Also, working on the visual media project (scoring a short film) in a group is super fun + educational, as you can learn from your group members. The lessons with grad students are also incredibly helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530843\n 1530843\n \n 1530843\n Strengths: Detailed work on compositions, lessons on expanding compositional palettes are very useful for growth. The TAs are wonderful for helping with all sorts of things.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Much of the course consists of general listening to the music of other composers, which is not a weakness in itself but felt excessively dominant as a focus of sessions (maybe 75% of total class time?). I didn't feel that I was able to learn too much from these sessions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531141\n 1531141\n \n 1531141\n The course structure, with a long term project and several parallel smaller assignments, makes it both valuable and rewarding, as a student ends up being able to work on what they want to while still applying what they learn in class in smaller pieces. The guest lectures were also a great resource.\r\n\r\nThe assignment of course TFs who do private lessons could better be tailored to the interests and workflow of the student. The lecture slides were only posted as Apple Keynote slides which made them difficult to review after the lecture.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1535127\n 1535127\n \n 1535127\n Many strengths and a good support system.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536522\n 1536522\n \n 1536522\n the one on one instruction is the most helpful part of class. i find that the requirements for composition projects can sometimes be a hindrance rather than an aid to developing your composition skills. the teaching style is not very engaging (reading from slides).\r\n\r\nthe joint seminars with school of music were great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540695\n 1540695\n \n 1540695\n Strengths:\r\n- Dr. Alexander - she's really nice!\r\n- The TF's - they're great!\r\n- The weekly seminar itself, as well as the presentations of guest composers\r\n- The very fact that this seminar incentivizes you to compose!\r\n- The rules you have to follow in your composition assignments (it forces you to be more creative than before!)\r\n\r\nWeaknesses:\r\n- The weekly seminar itself doesn't meet very often; guest composers will often come and present instead, and we won't meet just as a class for weeks\r\n- The at-times confusing assignment requirements and occasional difficulty of the grading\r\n- Lastly, the group project. Every year composers get a group project where they score 2-3 minutes of film, and our year was no exception. If you're not tech-savvy like me or don't know what logic, dorico, or a DAW file are, you're in for a rough time. There's so much technical stuff, and you might have to find a way to collaborate with group members who use different softwares... which isn't easy. Also, I've found that composers have very specific ideas about what music should sound like and it's sometimes hard for them to work together. Despite all of these challenges, I think ours turned out okay, but it was very stressful and time consuming.
