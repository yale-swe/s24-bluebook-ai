\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1607910\n 1607910\n \n 1607910\n Strengths: Loved the professor, Aaron Gerow is truly an icon and is one of the most engaging lecturers I know. He's awesome, and for the most part, his readings and analysis of the films we viewed were also great. Weaknesses: The screenings felt a little inconvenient at times. Also, the papers were a lot more time-intensive than expected. Also wish we watched less older, historical films like jidaigeki or footage that felt less engaging.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1615593\n 1615593\n \n 1615593\n The course does provide a decent range of films from Japan, China, and later from America, but the Korean film feels like an after thought halfway through the semester. It also felt like engagement in the course declined rapidly as the semester went on, and this could be in part due to the sometimes excessive amounts of reading, and in part due to the quality of lectures. While it's a film class, we were also expected to read two novels in their entirety while also committing several hours a week to watching films on top of other often dense readings. The books just feel unnecessary since you can easily grasp the cross-media influences of the genre without reading two entire novels. The lecture format of the class also is really unfortunate since the course content feels fairly subjective at times and would be better approached in a seminar-style class. There is a weekly section, but it didn't feel super substantial and meaningful in any way. Finally, it also seemed like a lot of people were not satisfied with the way the essays were graded, and some of the TF's comments felt really out of left field, especially some insubstantial nit-picky comments on word choice.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1617795\n 1617795\n \n 1617795\n I really enjoyed how the discussion sections and lectures were structured, where both allowed a good combination of "lecturing learning" and "discussion learning". I enjoyed all of the movies we watched in class but by the end of the class, it was difficult to remember all of them come time for the final paper. It might be nice to have one class at the end of the semester that just reviews all of the films seen throughout the course!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1621938\n 1621938\n \n 1621938\n Strengths: I really enjoyed the content of the classI think there was a good balance of watching film, analyzing it, and broader theory about film and genre studies. The movies themselves were great, and the Tuesday night screening was always a nice way to enjoy a movie on the big screen and set the world aside for a moment. The teaching staff was also greatboth the professor and the TAs were all extremely knowledgeable about the subject. Weaknesses: Nothing glaring. There were times that wed watch a movie on Tuesday or over the weekend and then do an analysis in class that didnt spend much time on the actual film itself.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1623076\n 1623076\n \n 1623076\n I really liked the film screenings for this class, and the ways that we would review clips from these films during lectures and discussion sections. I also felt like everyone was excited about what we were learning in class, which made for very fun discussions, including which of two martial artists would win in a battle. One weakness of the course was that the organization of the syllabus was sometimes confusing. I also wish we explored more East Asian films outside of Hong Kong and Japan. At least with the latter, I wish we didn't mainly focus on jidaigeki films.
