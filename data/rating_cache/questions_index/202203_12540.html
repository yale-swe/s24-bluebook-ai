RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1608187\n 1608187\n \n 1608187\n Strengths: I loved the films we watched and as someone learning Italian I thought it was a great way to increase my exposure to the language\r\nWeaknesses: I'd never taken a film class before and I don't feel like I'm walking away with an understanding of how to analyze films. There was a good deal of instruction on specific aspects of Italian/Sicilian history and culture but not on film analysis itself. Somehow though, during in-class discussions it often felt like there was a "right" answer which I found off-putting. I didn't think the papers were fruitful assignments as I was unable to analyze in any meaningful way three films in conversation with each other in 5 pages. I also don't know why a course with 3 papers needs a final exam too it feels excessive and I imagine a longer final paper would provide a better medium to apply and show what we've learned. Also, the class session itself would often run overtime which was very frustrating.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1611354\n 1611354\n \n 1611354\n Strengths: great films, interesting seminars / lectures, clear feedback on essays, and Professor Marcus and Federica were both very approachable\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: I wish the class was structured to be more of a seminar especially for weeks where we had two class periods for one film; I would have liked to hear more about my classmates' own opinions and readings of the scenes / movies than go over plot points. I really enjoyed hearing what everyone truly thought about a film or the perspective of the filmmaker in a certain scene and thought it made me look at the movie through different lenses and made me a better viewer / consumer of film. I also think that a clearer understanding of grades in this class would be great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1623501\n 1623501\n \n 1623501\n The strengths of this course were the discussions we had about each film. In class, after watching the film altogether, we would talk about different aspects of the film and our interpretations of them. This ended up leading to a lot of interesting discussions on society, gender roles, and racial issues here and abroad. The weaknesses, however, included our lack of studying actual film techniques that convey certain messages. Honestly, this class felt more like a history class than a film class in the sense that we didn't really study film techniques. We focused heavily on the content of the films rather than the mechanisms of the films themselves.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627072\n 1627072\n \n 1627072\n I wish there had been some more conventional film theory as readings
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627097\n 1627097\n \n 1627097\n Strengths: very engaging films, seminar is often fun, paper topics are given well in advance of due date\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: none
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627104\n 1627104\n \n 1627104\n Professor Marcus was a strength of the course. I wish that she had lectured a little more instead of having class discussion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627890\n 1627890\n \n 1627890\n The course's strengths are its emphasis on open discussion and its structuring by Professor Marcus. Its only real weakness for me was a couple films that seemed redundant. Sometimes we didn't get enough time to discuss aspects of the films and books on the syllabus.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1628160\n 1628160\n \n 1628160\n Strengths - the small size of the classroom really encouraged me to participate. I feel that I really got an in depth knowledge of Sicilian film as well as the films that we viewed were truly fascinating. Professor Marcus and Federica were both extremely vivacious and I always looked forward to going to class.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses - there was very little structure given to the coursework especially in regards to grading. I was extremely confused on how to prepare for the final exam. The proportion of the grade allotted to the papers was a bit high in my opinion and made writing each assignment extremely stressful as they were each worth 25% of our grade. I do wish we had the option of writing more papers of a smaller length analysing fewer number of films.
