RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1608085\n 1608085\n \n 1608085\n I really liked the road-trip structure of this course. The support for our final papers library workshops, presentations on structure/footnotes were super helpful. Sometimes our class would end up mostly discussing the short primary sources by famous politicians, rather than spending ample time on our secondary works, which I found somewhat disappointing because I generally felt I learned more from the in-depth secondary readings.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614513\n 1614513\n \n 1614513\n Some of the reading was lengthy and dense. Would have been nice to have slightly smaller and more meaningful extracts selected rather than just half of a not super interesting book.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1615559\n 1615559\n \n 1615559\n Strengths: Professor Gage is truly incredible! She is a master at leading an interesting discussion and chose fantastic readings. I was sad when the semester was over because I loved the content so much. Weaknesses: None!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1620723\n 1620723\n \n 1620723\n Strengths: the readings were fascinating and varied. I think each of us walked away with a different favorite week -- anyone can find something they find interesting in the seminar. I was also impressed by the other students in the class, who came from a range of perspectives and departments, including history, political science, and philosophy. Professor Gage was very accessible and gave great advice for approaching our term papers (this was especially impressive because her book, G-Man, came out in the middle of our term). I also really appreciated the workshop at the library and the inclusion of workshop time for our papers during seminar. Weaknesses: Because of the road-trip format (which I liked overall), sometimes the chronology got a bit confusing. I would've liked an extra week on the 1990s and the Clinton presidency. I also might have appreciated one more writing assignment, say a short paper on one of the week's readings. I enjoyed the political autobiography, but it would have been good to have more historical writing practice ahead of the term paper.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1630723\n 1630723\n \n 1630723\n Strengths: Quite literally a *brilliant* syllabus. The place-based and chronological structure results in connections and comparisons being made between political trends, events, and phenomena from week to week, which means that knowledge is not lost, but is developed and synthesized throughout the semester. The readings were engaging, and there was not busy work aside from the traditional weekly discussion posts (which I actually found valuable for once, given how committed my peers were to meaningful posts). The political autobiography assignment rocks-- it is something I will keep for the rest of my life, and was an opportunity for political self-reflection that I never would have pushed myself to complete were it not for this class. Professor Gage is probably the best seminar leader I have had at Yale-- she deftly balances guiding the class and contextualizing the readings with her own knowledge with letting her students share their thoughts and reactions to the material. \r\nWeaknesses: I think that the pre-Thanksgiving paper draft opportunity great in theory, but I (along with others I spoke with in class) found this to be a little overly ambitious/early. I would have appreciated the opportunity to turn in an outline or a partial draft. That said, I understand why Professor Gage had the expectation of a full draft and it's probably my own incompetence that made it feel overly ambitious.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1631947\n 1631947\n \n 1631947\n The readings Prof. Gage selects really dive deep into certain regions of the United States and shine a light on the way regional politics develops. The road trip format was really helpful for me because I haven't been exposed to most parts of the US and it's often difficult to resonate with politics without understanding the history and community behind it. This format also helped narrow down the focus of liberalism and conservatism and avoid broad assumptions about national politics--of course, we covered that lens too. I think the course was extensive and informative, but we often failed to discuss the supplementary readings each week so it was harder to connect them to the central topic/region. I also think that minority voices were crammed into the week in Northern California, but that may have just been a by-product of the lack of diversity during this period.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1632259\n 1632259\n \n 1632259\n Weaknesses: None. It was quite a bit of work, but completely worth it. Strengths: Excellent readings, interesting discussion, and well-timed writing assignments
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1638066\n 1638066\n \n 1638066\n Strengths: very engaging readings with a great mix of primary and secondary sources; lively class discussions; opportunity to do a major research paper with archival materials.\r\nSuggestion: I think it would've been helpful to workshop our research papers in small groups
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1638162\n 1638162\n \n 1638162\n Strength: Course is structured to ensure students do the readings and can meaningfully engage with material.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1639533\n 1639533\n \n 1639533\n Strengths: excellent mix of professor's explanation of the political trends observed in that week's reading and student conversation. Never felt like a lecture (as some seminars do), but provided the necessary professor-led explanations and moderation.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1640483\n 1640483\n \n 1640483\n I think the course was really well-designed, and I very much enjoyed its structure. Im not sure the weekly reading response model is the most valuable, but Im not sure what an alternative would be.
