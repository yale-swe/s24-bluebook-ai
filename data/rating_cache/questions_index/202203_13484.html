\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1619111\n 1619111\n \n 1619111\n This course is definitely demanding but I felt it was really really strong. The syllabus and course format are very well organized and provided a detailed overview of the Harlem Renaissance and modern art movements. I think some of our discussions were more fruitful than others, so maybe mid-class breaks into small groups or just general exercises that get everyone talking would be helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1619385\n 1619385\n \n 1619385\n Strengths: Professor Berry's fantastic syllabus, which is chock-full of great old texts, carefully chosen complementary new ones, and a robust selection of artistic works from sculpture and music to movies and plays. Often professors will include one or two multimedia elements, but Berry incorporates many, and all of them are useful. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: This was Professor Berry's first year teaching this course. One of the only places where this showed was that she was a little shy to lead discussion. She could have guided it more. I'd recommend asking students not to be on their laptops, and maybe having a little section at the end of class to summarize the day's discussion and emphasize some takeaways. The other weakness is that while the syllabus was very creative, the assignments were relatively introductory English-level assignments. This works for an intro English class, but it might have more widespread appeal to vary the assignments more.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625941\n 1625941\n \n 1625941\n Strengths: Readings were all interesting, papers were well structured and clear, in class discussion was for the most part really rich and engaging, class environment was friendly and not stressful, Prof Berry did a great job of giving us takeaways that apply to life now which made the content feel super relevant and important; another strength was the class presentation assignment, kept students engaged and was super interesting to both present and watch\r\n\r\nweaknesses: some of the longer readings got a little dense and were hard to get through, discussion sometimes took a while to pick up\r\n\r\nhow it could be improved: If a text is pretty dense, having a lower page count is helpful because you can spend more time understanding and unpacking; specific discussion questions or small group exercises could help the discussion get going faster
