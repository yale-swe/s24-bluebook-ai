\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1614216\n 1614216\n \n 1614216\n Professor Fadiman was incredibly well-organized, the course was seamlessly taught and executed, down to the last minute. All of us were in a state of constant awe at her organization. Professor Fadiman also exhibited a deep devotion to her students and approached the coarse as something potentially life-changing that could push students towards (or away from) a career in journalism. The camaraderie that Professor Fadiman encouraged among us was amazing, as were the lengths to which Professor Fadiman went to help us get in contact with and meet real-life writers. \r\n\r\nThe course held us to a very high standard, which I loved. Because of the renown of the course and Professor Fadiman's half-ironic, half-genuine references to the ritual and tradition baked into the course, sometimes it felt like the stakes were higher than I was comfortable with or think stakes should be for a college class. There were rumors circulating that meant that Professor Fadiman would ask students not to pass/fail the course if she particularly admired their work, and I think these put everyone ill at ease, half-expecting and half-hoping that she would approach them thus.
