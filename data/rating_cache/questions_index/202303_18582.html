\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697920\n 1697920\n \n 1697920\n This introductory architecture course was excellent for students just starting to learn about the field. The conceptual syllabus was structured to help build an understanding of the overarching frameworks and theoretical underpinnings of architectural design. The out-of-class exercises and studio assignments helped connect theory to practice. The TAs provided extremely helpful support, taking the time to ensure all students comprehended topics and giving responsive feedback on our hand-ins, which was valuable for improving. Adding more wording descriptions to the lecture slides, rather than just pictures, could help with following the progression of concepts throughout the term. Overall, the material was paced well for newcomers to grasp principles. This was a very solid foundation for green hands looking to pick up architectural design, and I learned a great deal. The TAs were instrumental in that learning through their dedicated support.

I dont think one round of mid-semester feedback and then one final grade at the end is nearly enough.\r\n\r\nI also think perhaps there could be more engagement during lectures.
