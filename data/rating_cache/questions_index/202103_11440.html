RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521077\n 1521077\n \n 1521077\n The readings were heavy and a bit unmanageable at the beginning of the course but Professor Hacker was extremely gracious and responsive to feedback so the readings mellowed out after that. Other than that I thought that the course flowed well and each week we had interesting discussion of the topic at hand. I found the setup of the course, which allowed for time at the end of the semester to focus on the final research project, extremely helpful. I also appreciated the reading responses as they were set up to allow some wiggle room as other class assignments made necessary, and I felt like they were a good way to form cohesive thoughts about the readings both individually and in conversation with each other.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1530707\n 1530707\n \n 1530707\n Strengths: interesting topic, readings covered different nuances and perspectives, group capstone project at the end was a really exciting way to bring our knowledge together at the end of the class, also having Sam as a TF was excellent!!!!\r\nWeaknesses: would've benefitted from more class discussion about the topics and content of the readings, not just the methods and tiny details of the experiments in the readings. Class could be pretty dry at times because of that.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1534021\n 1534021\n \n 1534021\n The syllabus covered a wide variety of pertinent topics and different scholarly approaches to studying the issues of interest. \r\n\r\nMany people in class did not participate during discussions, which often ended up being relatively small discussions between the Professor and the few students who would engage.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540994\n 1540994\n \n 1540994\n Strengths: the content matter is incredibly important for students interested in politics to know, and Professor Hacker assigns excellent readings. Additionally, there are lots of opportunities to succeed outside of the final project, but the final project is definitely the heart of this course, and it's clear that Professor Hacker takes the class and the research projects really seriously. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Two hours of Professor Hacker's lecturing can be difficult to stick with. Also, there wasn't a ton of support for students to learn Rin the future, the skill sessions should probably focus more on that particular skill and start looking at it earlier in the term so that the TF isn't overwhelmed with teaching everyone in the class how to use the program right before the final project is due.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553773\n 1553773\n \n 1553773\n Strengths: cool format, clear feedback, accessible Professor and TA, nice subject matter, group project\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: too much reading assigned at the start, students talked way too quietly and I often couldnt hear their responses, we got into groups for the final project a bit too late in the semester
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553852\n 1553852\n \n 1553852\n strengths: great training in political science research, very rewarding final project, labs with the TF were really useful, really fast and helpful feedback on our papers\r\n\r\nweaknesses: there was a lot of reading, though about halfway through the class it became much more manageable when some of the readings were made optional. I think the discussions could have been organized a bit better to really dive into the meaning of the results and to synthesize a picture of how money affected American politics, which sometimes got lost in the focus on research findings and research methods
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1555467\n 1555467\n \n 1555467\n The primary strength of the course is indubitably Jacob Hacker and Sam Zacher. Professor Hacker did a fantastic job of making class engaging, and Sam did a remarkable job of leading skills sessions with relevant and useful material. \r\n\r\nThe secondary strength of the course is the capstone project, which prepares you for (1) a senior thesis and/or (2) a career as a political scientist. Professor Hacker and Sam offer tremendous support throughout the entire process from ideation to tracking down data to developing analytical methods. If you are interested in conducting quantitative research in political science and don't know where to start, then this course is for you!\r\n\r\nThe only "weakness" of this course is the sheer amount of reading assigned each week. With that said, the readings are masterfully curated and each paper assigned is worth reading. You will leave this course with a strong understanding of the multiplicity of ways in which money can and does influence American politics.
