RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1525641\n 1525641\n \n 1525641\n The course material is excellent, and the pace felt just right. The weekly listening quizzes were quite stressful (even though they constituted a small portion of the grade), and it would have been nice to have a smaller or more consistent number of eligible songs per quiz. Sometimes, it felt like we were studying for the sake of memorizing the material for quizzes instead of for learning the material for learning's sake. Otherwise, the course was really well-designed, and the instructors were very interested in making good pedagogical choices and encouraging us to think outside of the box on assignments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1533104\n 1533104\n \n 1533104\n There were very few weaknesses in this course -- I liked the general structure, the listening quizzes, singing in class, and the projects. \r\n\r\nI do wish I had gotten more feedback on the four projects we did (two of them as of now haven't been graded, so I'm basing this off of the first two grades). The feedback I got was great, but I would've loved more specific comments on my work since I put a lot of time into these projects.\r\n\r\nAnother thing is that I wish there were a list of terms (genres, composers, etc.) we need to know posted somewhere. For people who had to miss a class, it was hard to know which terms were very important and which terms were just throw-away interesting tidbits from class. This was especially a problem because the powerpoints were already pretty sparse. So if on canvas there were a list of terms somewhere that would be helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538831\n 1538831\n \n 1538831\n Prof. Z is an excellent lecturer and pedagogue who helps students engage critically with this material! The projects are all creative and make you feel like a medieval music scholar yourself: we did a transcription of medieval notation, a collaborative composition of a jeu parti (debate song), and an edition, all with written reflection components, and then a short research paper on a self-chosen topic that was not represented in the survey. The quizzes and exams require a lot of memorization; I don't know if that is a strength or a weakness necessarily, just worth noting that a lot of your time studying for this course will be dominated by listening and memorizing. Sometimes it was unclear what we were going to be quizzed on (ex. quizzes asked us to identify quotes that did not come up in readings/assignments, but that were mentioned in lecture). Another strength is that class and section are both highly interactive and incorporate many different modes of learning: group work, discussion, activities, singing, and so forth.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541221\n 1541221\n \n 1541221\n The class is really enjoyable, and the material was unique. One weakness of the class is the over-emphasis on memorizing the listening material, which I think is less important than being able to understand the styles in a holistic view.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1544675\n 1544675\n \n 1544675\n I like the check-in quizzes and assignments in that they spread out the work over the semester and thereby reduce stress towards the end. If the final remains open-note (a format that I personally like because it is more applicable to the real world), having the last assignment due during reading period is more reasonable. I liked the opportunity to go deeper on something from the course period that I was interested in. The debate song project was also really fun. The neume transcription assignment and "De toutes bien plaine" assignment were both a little bit tedious but also instructive. Sections should be devoted to discussion rather than covering additional material.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549341\n 1549341\n \n 1549341\n Strengths: so many things! excellent instructor and TFs, logical and organized progression of material, reasonable assignments with clear grading and feedback; a real sense of support and community\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: few! I would say the transcription project was a bit time-consuming. Perhaps splitting it in to two parts so that one could receive some feedback before turning in the entire thing might help.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549461\n 1549461\n \n 1549461\n I think the strength was the in-class lectures, particularly with singing and other group activities. \r\nThe weakness was that I hated that it was at 9 am! Also the assignments were a little bit of overkill. I thought the jeu parti was fun, but the 1st and 3rd assignments could have been slimmed out. There should have been only 4 versions of Deo Nostro. And we should have just done half of the song for the editing project. And the last project was assigned last minute and I couldn't make time for it. At a certain point it, these assignments became tedious rather than educational.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1549652\n 1549652\n \n 1549652\n Professor Z knows how to balance breadth and depth in a survey course. All concepts are explained clearly and thoroughly. Her model of asking why study Medieval music is followed by a detailed an convincing answer to that question in each lecture.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550024\n 1550024\n \n 1550024\n Given the amount of assessments we take in the course, sometimes all the projects on top of them felt like a little too much and could be overwhelming.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1550892\n 1550892\n \n 1550892\n Despite the seemingly dry or irrelevant subject matter, it is incredibly engaging and interesting, especially with hands-on assignments and weekly listening quizzes.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1553104\n 1553104\n \n 1553104\n Lectures by Prof. Z were really great! Sometimes, it felt like a few classes were not as fleshed out as others, and there was just less material on some periods. This could have been remedied, I think, by assigning more readings probably an unpopular opinion, but having read more Taruskin or other academic sources would have helped round out the picture sometimes. I also found some of the assignments a bit busywork-y. I felt like I learned the most outside of class about the material through the readings and studying for quizzes. I did like the first assignment a lot, though. The third felt like a bit of a repeat of the first, and as such didn't really grip me as much.
