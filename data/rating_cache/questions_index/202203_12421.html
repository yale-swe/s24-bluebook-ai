\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1608063\n 1608063\n \n 1608063\n I learned A LOT about stat mech. The class is extremely difficult but rewarding if you enjoy the content.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1616458\n 1616458\n \n 1616458\n I learned basic methods in statistical mechanics, such as dealing with microcanonical, canonical, and grand canonical distributions. I learned (yet was unable to apply) how to deal with quantum ideal gases.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1619744\n 1619744\n \n 1619744\n You first start by counting things, applying central limit theorem, and deriving amazing things about the world that are non-mechanistic, such as temperature and pressure of a classical ideal gas. Then you move on to thermodynamics and just assume basically that everything microscopically is working as expected, and you do some calculations and things that are easier than the statistical mechanics you had first seen (micro canonical). Then the rest of the course is pure black magic: you unleash the canonical partition and grand canonical partition functions to basically do all of thermodynamics with very little work: everything is formal and less approximations are necessary. You move on to the UV catastrophe, quantum ideal gases, Bose Einstein condensate, and a little bit of phase transitions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625846\n 1625846\n \n 1625846\n In this course, I learned the basic results of thermodynamics and statistical mechanics. In thermodynamics, I learned how to work with equations of state to obtain thermodynamic quantities, analyze stability conditions, and use thermodynamic potentials. We covered exact and inexact differentials, and particularly how to describe and compute quantities in quasi-static processes. For statistical mechanics, I gained insight into into the description of large systems of weakly interacting particles remaining close to equilibrium. I learned how to derive the microcanonical, canonical, and grand canonical distributions through the postulate of equal a-priori probabilities, and use these ensembles to arrive at the thermodynamic relations. I also learned how to construct and work with the partition functions of various classical and quantum systems, including Bose and Fermi gasses and black body radiation.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1628351\n 1628351\n \n 1628351\n Learned about the foundations of statistical mechanics and some of their applications in thermodynamics.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629717\n 1629717\n \n 1629717\n Broad understanding of statistical mechanics and thermodynamics.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1632314\n 1632314\n \n 1632314\n good survey of stat mech
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1634580\n 1634580\n \n 1634580\n Insights that I learned decently were the canonical and grand canonical ensembles, as well as basic thermodynamics. However, radiation and solids were covered very briefly but with great depth expected towards the end and I did not learn those well in this course at all.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1636083\n 1636083\n \n 1636083\n Fundamentals of thermodynamics, statistical treatment of thermo and analysis of systems of large particles, microcanonical, canonical, and grand canonical ensembles, classical and quantum treatment of gases.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1619744\n 1619744\n \n 1619744\n This course is amazing; I mean, its really hard, because even though you know oh, I need to use the canonical distribution, it is still hard to come up with the right physical formulation.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1625846\n 1625846\n \n 1625846\n This course was one of the first times I felt that physics has given me direct insight into the world around me. Statistical mechanics enabled me to contemplate phenomena like vapor pressure, elasticity, and radiation in a way I had never understood it before. I felt that the strength of this course was the applicability of the material to describe these everyday phenomena. The place I think this course could be improved is the structure. It was not clear to me until midway through the second half of the semester that thermodynamics and statistical mechanics are considered to be independent topics. The textbook reflected this confusion, as it seemed to jump back and forth between statistical and thermal arguments in a way that made it confusing at times to follow. Personally, I think that this course would be easier to follow if it began with the canonical distribution and partition function, and then worked forward from there, presenting a unified framework.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627345\n 1627345\n \n 1627345\n Lectures were very good and gave nuanced descriptions of the material. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629717\n 1629717\n \n 1629717\n Lectures moved very fast but were interesting. Having more posted notes or recordings would have been hugely helpful. I have taken several graduate physics courses, including Advanced Classical Mechanics (PHYS 500) and Quantum Mechanics I (PHYS 508), and I found this course to be far more challenging than either of them. The beginning of the course was a bit rocky because the instructor was out sick for several weeks, and so I did not have a good indicator of whether I was keeping up or not. I felt confident on the homework assignments, but I was completely lost on the midterm. After this, I began coming to office hours regularly every week, and diligently studying my notes and reading through the textbook on my own in addition to class. Even so, I found it difficult to catch up, and I felt lost in lecture throughout most of the course. I really appreciate a challenging physics course, but I think there were structural reasons that this course was much more challenging than it had to be, to the point that it detracted from my ability to learn the material. For this reason, I would hesitate to recommend this course to a student who did not already have to take it for their major.
