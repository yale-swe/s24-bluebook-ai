\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1582988\n 1582988\n \n 1582988\n This is a tough question. \r\n\r\nI really did enjoy the class this semester, but it was such a drastic change from last semester and it was all dependent on the instructor. I can't help but think of how much further along I'd be in my French learning journey if I had Prof. Ngame last semester in addition to this one. \r\n\r\nLast semester was honestly such a traumatic experience for me. Prof. Angeli did not care at all about our success in the class. People have mixed reviews, but the students who struggled did not feel like they were welcome in the class; it is something I have discussed with others many times throughout this semester because we were so SHOCKED by the difference in quality and culture in Prof Ngame's class. Not all of us had the privilege of going to schools with rigorous language coursework, so language classes are really out of left field for us and we really had to fight to stay afloat. Nicola seemed apathetic to the different factors that could play into a students success or struggle in the class. To him, either you had it or you didn't and if you didn't get it, well, too bad. We honestly thought the brutality we got during the fall term was just the culture of the language department of a whole, and that we should switch to a different language.
