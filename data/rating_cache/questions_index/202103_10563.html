\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1522037\n 1522037\n \n 1522037\n This course needs extreme improvement should it provide any value to student learning. Every class, we would sit and watch the instructor perform an experiment for the first 1-2 hours and wait until he could help us through the dissection or set-up as nearly every week, we required instructor help. This was made worse by the TAs who often had no idea how to help and would just wait until the instructor could help. Labs should be pre-set and ready to go when students arrive instead of them having to wait during their lab time. The lab was not a good use of my time. Furthermore, there were no objectives or learning goals of the lab. It often was an exploratory lab to see what results you could find, and then TAs would harshly grade any and all data you could get, often saying 'not enough data' for labs where we literally could not get the equipment working to get even a single source of data. The inconsistent grading had all students extremely worried. The course needs to have explicit learning goals and procedure every lab, the dissections and materials should be prepared and ready to go when students arrive, and the TAs need to be trained and be able to actually assist students and taught how to grade. Taking this course concurrently with the lecture, was one of the worst courses I have had to take at Yale.
