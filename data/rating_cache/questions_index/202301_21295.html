Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1647026\n 1647026\n \n 1647026\n The workload and pacing of this course were excellent. The work was substantive and meaningful and also manageable. Our class discussions were rich. The only downside was that we often did not cover much of what we read for class. That being said, our discussions being so focused allowed us to dig deeply into a smaller number of poems which may have been more valuable than a broader, more surface-level survey of all of the poems.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1673988\n 1673988\n \n 1673988\n strengths: we cover a lot of poetry and many different poets, so you really get a sense for what's out there and different kinds of poetry across time. The class discussions are often super fruitful and revealing. We usually close-read a passage of poetry and then discussed it in depth, meaning that there was less homework pressure to do the reading carefully before class. Because we were reading poetry, the reading load was quite light. Professor Eccles is absolutely brilliant and an incredible professor. I have never gotten such amazing feedback from a professor on my work: her comments are always spot-on and extremely productive. I learned so much from her as a professor and person.\r\n\r\nweaknesses: there's never enough time to talk about most of what we've read, so you end up reading more poetry than you can discuss, and that can be frustrating. I sometimes felt like the small homework assignments weren't fully explained or clear, probably because they're often somewhat open-ended and creative which is probably a good thing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1680475\n 1680475\n \n 1680475\n The big strength is the amount of reading: light enough that everyone always has time to do all the readings, but dense enough that conversation quality never wanes.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1680609\n 1680609\n \n 1680609\n this course is amazing, especially with eccles. she is super responsive to feedback about how to improve the course
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1680907\n 1680907\n \n 1680907\n I really enjoyed the readings given in the course as well as the homework assignments, which were the most enjoyable and the most effective homework I have had in an English class at Yale. They were thorough enough to get me thinking about the texts, but not too much to make them hard to complete. Professor Eccles is a great rhetorician, and I learned a lot about writing in an academic style just by listening to her speak and pose questions about the text. The essay prompts were expansive, and I always felt like I had access to Professor Eccles via office hours. \r\n\r\nIt is difficult to think of specific weaknesses. I think maybe one thing I would like to see different about the course is more supplemental criticism being incorporated. Towards the end of the class, we were told to perform more external research about the historical context of the texts we were writing upon. I recognize that this is likely placed at the end so we can make use of skills developed earlier in the semester, but I wish we had more time to go to the archives in the Stacks or Beinecke, and do more extensive external research.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1681291\n 1681291\n \n 1681291\n Great selection for the syllabus. Professor Eccles is a great facilitator of seminars, providing mini-lectures for each class to give everyone a grasp on the readings. Professor Eccles' evaluation of my essays was always incredibly thoughtful, and she provided very helpful feedback fro improvements. For an intro class for the English major, Eccles is amazing!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1682556\n 1682556\n \n 1682556\n strengths: discussion always revealed very interesting insights into a poem, helped me conceive of it in a different way; i liked how we paid attention to the reading of a poem and had people read them aloud\r\n\r\nweaknesses: sometimes discussion felt repetitive or we would often run out of time as we were digging into something complex; wish we were guided in a more specific direction sometimes with how to think about a poem instead of more broad thoughts because it felt like everyone just shared their opinions on the poem rather than considers its project
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1683064\n 1683064\n \n 1683064\n I appreciated how we delved into specific moments or lines within our larger class reading assignments I really feel that it taught me to analyze poetry in a way that I couldnt before the class. We had the perfect balance of getting an overview of what we were learning, to delving in deeper and analyzing the text.
