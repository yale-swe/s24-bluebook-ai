\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562867\n 1562867\n \n 1562867\n Strengths: The course material is fascinating and Prof. Njera has designed the course well to promote student-driven learning and discussion.\r\nWeaknesses: It would be nice to not do all the assignments twicetwo debriefings, two multimodal projects (one group and one solo)
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1565091\n 1565091\n \n 1565091\n Such a disorganized course with huge miscommunications and SO MUCH work.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1566427\n 1566427\n \n 1566427\n The course only has several components - class discussion, two "debriefings" (basically short powerpoints but could be videos, podcasts, any kind of presentation format), weekly reflections, a practice final project and a real final project (the real one is worth 3x the practice). This means it's ok to mess up in one category and still get an A. The projects are also split up into small steps with staggered deadlines, which is very helpful.\r\n\r\nOne weakness is that busy students may be tempted not to do the reading before every class, since the reading often has nothing to do with the debriefings or projects (unless you want to make your project an extension of the class discussion). Take that as you will.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1567093\n 1567093\n \n 1567093\n Strengths is that overall the workload is manageable however there Are definitely waves where there is a lot of work. Professor Njera was accommodating which was helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1567096\n 1567096\n \n 1567096\n The homework texts were also interesting, and the discussions felt well-structured and engaging. Prof. Njera takes a very multimodal approach to the learning. This means watching videos, documentaries, looking at paintings, etc., instead of just analyzing traditional written texts. This made the class more engaging. Prof. Njera also expected us to take a multimodal approach to our projects and assignments (i.e. using graphics, videos, musics, etc., to produce meaning). This was difficult, but fun, because it required thinking in a different way. The workload is reasonable for most of the semester, but very high at the end, because the final project is a fair bit of work and worth 50% of the final grade.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1567307\n 1567307\n \n 1567307\n Strengths: incredibly well organized, the professor cares a ton, interesting content, more than just papers, engaging assignments\r\nWeaknesses: instructions were vague in the beginning, but after midterm feedback the professor switched and it became a lot better!
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1567399\n 1567399\n \n 1567399\n I cried so much during this course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1569162\n 1569162\n \n 1569162\n Strengths: bring you closer to topics of globalization of the Americas in nuanced ways; Weakness; too big of an emphasis on theory, assignments and deadlines can be a little too convoluted
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1577293\n 1577293\n \n 1577293\n This course is amazing, and I feel grateful to have taken it. The strengths of this course include the interesting concepts taught with concrete historical information. I find the projects helpful as a meaningful way to practice and show our understanding of course material and provide us an opportunity to create something interesting that could be continued and shared beyond this class. The weekly journal entries are a helpful part of the class because we write questions and ideas, providing the professor with an indication of the depth of understanding; Professor Njera spends time thoughtfully responding to and engaging with these entries. My one suggestion is to reduce workload, as more work does not mean greater understanding, and having additional time for fewer projects would have made them more meaningful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1584040\n 1584040\n \n 1584040\n I think the strengths of the course have to do with the rigour of discussion -- there's a substantial focus on developing the theoretical frameworks for understanding globalization and its impact, and that has translated into some really wonderful seminar discussions. I also appreciate the course's focus on the experiences of indigenous communities in understanding globalization -- the experiences of indigenous communities were at the center of almost all of our class discussions, which is of course by design. Overall, I really loved the class.\r\n\r\nAs for weaknesses, I would have to say that sometimes the guidelines for our submitted work weren't quite clear, and this sometimes translated into students doing far more work than they had to or should have. I know professors sometimes have aversion to giving word limits or word count guidelines, but they really are helpful -- both because they force students to be concise and because they prevent students from working themselves to death. I also think the course grade weighting could have been done better; our final project was 50% of our grade, and our midterm project (which was the same type of project and was described as a "practice run" for the final) was less than half of that. The effect was that after a very labour-intensive midterm project, our performance on it didn't matter as much as it should have in grading. I think that may have demoralized some people going into the final.\r\n\r\nThose are quibbles that can be addressed by changing some numbers on a piece of paper, though!

Just note that going into the course, and be sure to balance your work on an expansive and broad project for this class with your work in other classes.
