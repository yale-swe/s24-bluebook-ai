This was partly because other students would take the conversation in different directions than I personally wanted to go (which is obviously going to happen in any seminar, especially one of this size), but I felt that discussions could have been more productive in general if we went in with more of a prompt than "Let's discuss this poem today."

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1629077\n 1629077\n \n 1629077\n Strengths: Professor Soles is fantastic. If you want a Dead Poets Society English teacher, he's it. Animated, incisive, inspiring and inspired. He's great at giving everyone a chance to speak while still directing the class toward more fruitful discussion. The class itself is set up in a way where you only discuss one or two poems a week. As someone who usually blazes through whole books during seminars, this was an awesome change of pace, and it allowed us to truly relish every line. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Reading every Emily Dickinson poem is not only a tall order but frustratingfor a poet who is so cryptic, you can't really get much out of skimming. Soles does, however, give the class 15-20 poems to focus on each week. I wound up abandoning the directive to "read everything" and focused on those poems, which was both more enjoyable and manageable.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1635280\n 1635280\n \n 1635280\n Strengths: compelling course content (and compelling presentation to pair!), excellent student engagement, and opportunities to independently reach exciting and novel conclusions.\r\nWeaknesses: a few too many students (not everyone was always engaged, and often you could only speak a few times throughout the 2 hour duration of class)
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1635827\n 1635827\n \n 1635827\n This class was phenomenal. While I loved diving deep into the work, I do wish that we had prioritized getting through a few more of the poems each class meeting, since it was only once a week. Also, while I appreciated the emphasis on Dickinsons work, I would have liked a more readings to understand the context of her life. Also, a further emphasis on the mechanics of her poetry would have been appreciated, such as a detailed understanding of the poetic strategies that she was employing. Overall, this class expanded the way that I think about poetry and the world, and I look forward to the next time I get to take a class with Professor Soles.
