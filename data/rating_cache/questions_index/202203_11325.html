\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1639150\n 1639150\n \n 1639150\n This course was a fairly comprehensive introduction to the theoretical concepts of vector analysis and manifolds, composed of three main parts: differential calculus in R^n, integral calculus in R^n, and manifolds. The first part of the course focused on rigorously extending single variable analysis concepts, namely rigorously defining mutli-dimensional derivatives, along with the inverse and implicit function theorems. Similarly, the second part extended the idea of Riemann integration to multiple dimensions, with key concepts including Fubinis theorem and the change of variables formula. Finally, the manifolds section (by far the most substantial), included definition and intuition regarding manifolds with and without boundary, along with discussions of tangent spaces, integration over manifolds, exterior algebra, differential forms, and Generalized Stokes Theorem. More generally, this course was a combination of a proof-based multivariable calculus course and a general introduction to manifolds; it gives you a good overall and theoretical understanding of all these concepts, with a mix of proof and application-based practice problems. Additionally, you develop good general mathematical problem solving skills and timed exam skills, as the homeworks and exams in particular are fairly rigorous.
