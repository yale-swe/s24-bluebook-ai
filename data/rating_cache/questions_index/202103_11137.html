Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1523041\n 1523041\n \n 1523041\n The strengths of this course was that it was a small enough class where I felt like there was an environment where it was safe to ask questions, make mistakes, and have positive reinforcement from my peers and instructor. The class also had a lot of opportunities for small group work, which meant that I got to collaborate with others in class and we could all work towards learning this new language together.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527420\n 1527420\n \n 1527420\n This course is excellent and very well-organized, and it teaches you to learn Spanish well. I think a little more in-class focus on grammar (which we learned mostly through homework) would be helpful.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531456\n 1531456\n \n 1531456\n All around very good course, occasional disconnect between daily Supersite lessons and class lessons, was often tedious and sometimes a cause for confusion. The way Supersite grades certain activities is also a bit unfair sometimes, so maybe a closer review of those correction procedures in activities would be helpful because it is a category of our grade that is based on accuracy. I also think that the pace of the course made vocabulary retention really hard sometimes. There were days where we would be introduced to 5-10 new verbs and be expected to recognize them in passages the next day. Those were just quick turnarounds, so a day in between that we re-emphasize the most important verbs vocabulary words would be helpful. That said, I loved that this class was more than just learning a language. It was learning the history of the language, how it came to be, its variations that resulted from its diaspora, the reason things are the way they are in the language, and the nuanced aspects of the language that make knowledge of it so important. Really like the seminar-based, interactive nature because that communication is truly crucial to language learning.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531716\n 1531716\n \n 1531716\n I liked the professor, and I think the small discussions between 2-3 people in class was helpful. The biggest issue for me was that I didn't feel comfortable enough with the language to fully engage in participation. That could definitely be a me-thing, but I think the speed of the class and the way each section was structured hindered me from improving my communication skills.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540640\n 1540640\n \n 1540640\n Strengths of this course were keeping all the students engaged. Professor Russell did a great job of this. Weakness would be maybe incorporating more culture.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540657\n 1540657\n \n 1540657\n The small class size is a strenght and having a good instructor makes it a lot easier. There is definitely a bit too much homework, it is very difficult to keep up with it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541172\n 1541172\n \n 1541172\n I think the biggest strength of this course is that the daily work never felt like fundamentally busy work. I always understood why I was doing an assignment (for the most part), and there was just enough variety to keep me not feeling super drained. Also, I loved reading el escabe cubano and how culturally focused all the big assignments were. \r\n\r\nThe weakness I think was the group project. I did not get anything out of this project frankly, and don't know how much could have been gained from it. In general I think group projects are not suitable for language learning at all, and this one did nothing to change that perspective.
