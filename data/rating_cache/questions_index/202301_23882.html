\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1676256\n 1676256\n \n 1676256\n I would say that this is an extremely strong course. The texts are incredible a truly fine and solitary intellectual experience. Prof. Schillinger's introduction to the text are also very helpful to contextualize and should remain. I will focus on debate.\r\n\r\nAs outlined in "On Liberty", debate and discussion are the forms in which we improve our ideas. This particular classroom was more honest and willing to debate than most I encounter at Yale; however, there is always room for improvement. It should be outlined in the beginning of the year that students are not only allowed but encouraged to share honest opinions about what they believe in a text. There were a variety of times in which a student would strongly agree with or strongly defend a textual point, or disagree or agree with their classmates or Prof. Schillinger, and these were genuinely the richest times in the classroom. We should make sure that these moments are harnessed and fostered even more so next year, and the texts and professors should be geared not only at driving the correct points of the texts but at evincing the ideas of the student.
