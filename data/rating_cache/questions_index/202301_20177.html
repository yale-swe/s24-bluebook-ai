\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1646435\n 1646435\n \n 1646435\n Strengths: Not all of the readings are great but there are some that are wonderful, and many of the visuals that we had to look at in the second half of the semester were supremely fun to look at and generative for thinking about data visualization. The projects were useful and interesting and I think the visual essay as the final assignment is definitely the right choice for the course. Prof. Rankin provides insanely useful feedback on our own ideas about the readings in class and on projects, and he is extremely structured in a way that is not rigid or overbearing. The people in the class were also really interesting and gave me tons of new insights.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: There was simply not enough in-class emphasis on mapping software or Adobe Illustrator. Many students including myself often felt at a loss for working with ARCGIS and we would've hoped that more time be devoted in class to working with the software; for example, having us download all the same shape files, datasets, etc and making a map step-by-step in class. The readings were, at times, very long.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1647092\n 1647092\n \n 1647092\n This course was very well designed for it being the first time this course was run. I think the readings were very good, aligned with the practical skills we were learning, and built on each other very well. I didn't read anything that I thought was irrelevant, which is a rarity in these seminar courses. There could have been a little bit more time devoted to tutorials for the various softwares, but I also understand the learning by doing method. I also really appreciated the classes where we looked at our peers work and got feedback because it both gave me ideas for my future visualization and helped me to see the amazing things my peers were doing.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1653606\n 1653606\n \n 1653606\n Strengths: you can produce visualizations that you're really proud of and improve significantly over the course of the term\r\nWeaknesses: Professor Rankin really throws you into the fire on the visualization assignments. They take a long time, and although I appreciate that approach to learning the software, I wish Professor Rankin had a little more appreciation for how time intensive it can be
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657726\n 1657726\n \n 1657726\n Strengths: I learned so much in this class and am coming away with actual visualizations that I made (and am proud of!) and can now show to others. The readings were some of the most interesting I have done at Yale. Professor Rankin gave excellent feedback, and the studio-style critiques were a fun opportunity to learn from my classmates' work. Weaknesses: Seminar discussion sometimes felt too unstructured, and occasionally the assignment critiques felt a little too intimidating.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1659991\n 1659991\n \n 1659991\n Prof. Rankin is really cool and has a lot to offer both as a historian of science and a data visualization practitioner. I really connected with the ethos behind this class, but I don't think it was executed as well as it could've been. I think the biggest thing I'd change would be the structure of the syllabus; the first half of the course is currently 100% devoted to theory and philosophy, while the second half is focused on history. This doesn't really make sense to me, because theory and history have the greatest value in conversation with each other. We never referenced any of the philosophy we read once we got to the history part of the course, which was disappointing because we read some thought-provoking works. Pairing theory and history works in the same week would definitely improve the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1663945\n 1663945\n \n 1663945\n Strengths: great introduction to data visualization theory and the wide variety of approaches plus the rich history that it has. Weakness: discussion was not always fruitful and it was hard to understand the relevance of certain readings or discussion topics to what we were supposed to learn. Rankin was also not the most responsive to emails, and while not completely a weakness, you should be prepared to learn a lot about the data visualization programs yourself through experimentation rather than learning it in class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1668304\n 1668304\n \n 1668304\n I felt like this course was walking the line between being a seminar and being a workshop, and therefore wasn't really effective in either regard. I entered this course hoping it would be a workshop and that I could gain some expertise in Illustrator, Excel, ArcGIS, etc. However, Prof. Rankin only gave us very fast tutorials at the end of our first few classes of the semester, and rather than walk us through a demo, he just said "this is the so-and-so function, and it does this." I talked with others in the course and this frustration about the lack of being taught how to use the programs was common. When we brought it up to him he said that he didn't know of any tutorials that existed, which put all of the legwork on the students to teach ourselves the programs. As a result, a discrepancy emerged in terms of who had more technical know-how with the different programs when we had to present our work to the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1674179\n 1674179\n \n 1674179\n I really enjoyed the data processing and data visualization assignments we were given. I found the practice helpful to apply some of the theories we read and discussed in class. The in-class discussions helped a little, but the practice assignments were the best. I think if did more in-class workshopping of each other's projects I would have enjoyed the discussions more. When we focused on just the readings, I sometimes felt directionless.\r\n\r\nOn a similar note, I think this class could be improved by being more practice-oriented. Different kinds of assignments (illustrations, group projects, mapping, visualizations relating directly to the class readings, tracking our own personal data) would have helped me feel more engaged.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1676466\n 1676466\n \n 1676466\n I think the structure of the class could be significantly improved. We were shown the tools to produce graphics, but were offered little to no guidance at all on how to implement it.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1677644\n 1677644\n \n 1677644\n Strengths: Prof. Rankin is the best discussion moderator I've ever had. It was clear that everyone was doing the readings (probably because of the discussion post requirement) and most people participated consistently throughout the semester. Rankin did a great job encouraging people to speak and offering his own (always valuable) insights. Readings were usually interesting and covered a broad range of relevant subjects; certain themes reappeared throughout the semester but the discussion was rarely stale. \r\nWeaknesses: This class definitely throws you in the deep end -- most of us didn't have much design background, and Rankin expected us to produce high-quality graphics for all three assignments (and is not the easiest grader). The final paper is ~10 pages of writing in addition to 5-10 graphics, each of which takes a couple hours, so it requires a lot of time and effort. This is probably a more effective way to teach design than to walk us through the software step by step, and Rankin was always very helpful in office hours, but be aware that this course demands a lot (and gives you a lot in return).
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1679005\n 1679005\n \n 1679005\n Strengths: Getting to create our own data visualizations on any topic of our choosing Weaknesses: limited/weak guidance on how to use certain softwares like ArcGIS and Adobe Illustrator. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1683621\n 1683621\n \n 1683621\n The readings in this class were very diverse and interesting (although at times difficult to grasp individually). I would say one improvement that could be made to this class would be to have more hands-on demonstrations of graphics/graphic making during class--- Professor Rankin has such a cool website and I wish he had explored it more with us in class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1684227\n 1684227\n \n 1684227\n One of the greatest strengths of the course was its approach to class discussions, which were always robust and insightful. Readings were wide-ranging and helped to provide a nuanced view of the history, practice, and theory of data visuals. I think that one area of improvement for the course could be in the teaching of visualization tools and techniques. Limited time meant that many skills had to be learned independently, without clear guidance or support. Workshops could be organized around shared exercises that all students could complete together (this could be in real time, or given as guides to be followed later), and a greater emphasis could be placed on making sure all students have access to the same tools (i.e. software) and information sources for creating visualizations.
