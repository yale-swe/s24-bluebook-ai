\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1698963\n 1698963\n \n 1698963\n Strengths: I like how each week was focused on a specific city and that was the focus of that entire class. \r\nWeaknesses: I wish we had the city presentation at midterm. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1700175\n 1700175\n \n 1700175\n seminar style is great for this class; very interesting readings and topics; Professor is great at leading the class; no obvious weaknesses
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1704308\n 1704308\n \n 1704308\n Strengths: Nari is an enthusiastic lecturer who cares about the subject matter and wants the class to take away the more abstract and subtle ideas that come from the readings and presentations.\r\nWeaknesses: I felt that the seminar was hamstrung by its size. I get the impression that in other iterations of this course, there was a smaller class size which meant people were more engaged with the material and learning with the professor. As happens with many seminars, the first iteration of students rates the course highly, which means that many other students will sign up next time; ostensibly, this is a good thing, but I felt that engagement with the readings was very low in general which worsened discussions. Furthering that point, I think readings should be shortened in general. I think some weeks, the expectation of around ~200 pages was a little excessive.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706030\n 1706030\n \n 1706030\n I really liked the overall structure of this course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1706071\n 1706071\n \n 1706071\n I believe that more multi-media content could enliven the readings for some cities and help students to get a better sense of their genius loci.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1712846\n 1712846\n \n 1712846\n The class is very interesting and unique among classes that I have taken at Yale. I loved learning about the different cities, and I felt that we had strong in-class discussions. I did think that the order of the cities could be revised so that it does not jump from topic to topic so much. As it is, you never really know what you're going to get in class. Also we received little feedback over the semester, so I did not feel that I had improved as a writer very much.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1717744\n 1717744\n \n 1717744\n The course is great at bringing together a wide variety of academic fields under the umbrella of different cities, thus providing a concrete example to learn from. At times, however, I felt I was lacking the theoretical lexicon to discuss the specificities of urban planning and architecture.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1722365\n 1722365\n \n 1722365\n There are only strengths. The curriculum is incredible and approaches each city from diverse perspectives, so for each city there is something sure to suit every student's interests (there are topics relevant to literally every major here!). Something I think most will appreciate is that there is so much freedom for students in this course to focus on what exactly interests them in relation to these cities (at the beginning of the course, the professor makes this clear by encouraging students to focus their attention on those topics coinciding with their own academic interests). Additionally, and probably most importantly, this is a professor that cares a lot about his students. The professor is very responsive to emails and to meeting in and outside of office hours. The professor prioritizes his students, a rarity in the university setting!
