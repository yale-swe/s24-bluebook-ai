\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697258\n 1697258\n \n 1697258\n This class is targeted at students that want to learn to write essays that are meant to be read. Our professor told us this on the first day in class, and I don't think I fully absorbed what it meant. In this class, we learned to think critically about some truly exemplary essays. We broke the authors' works down sentence by sentence, and while I wouldn't consider myself the most meticulous reader, the insights my peers offered allowed me to engage more deeply with the author's purpose. \r\nHaving completed the course, I now understand what the professor meant about writing to be read. Words exist for more than conveying ideasor rather, if an author truly believes they have a message worth sharing, then the author will take the time to refine their text into its most engaging, impactful form. \r\nIn this class, we learned to write well, which is one of the few skills that will influence every class I take hereafter.

Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1697258\n 1697258\n \n 1697258\n The biggest strength of the course is the feedback we receive on all of our papers. Professor Shirkhani puts an EXTRAORDINARY amount of time into helping her students develop as writers. This is a definite strength. \r\nAlso, you will disagree with the feedback that she gives you from time to time. But to steal a line from Stephen King's On Writing, "The editor is always right." Going into the class with an open mind will really help you get the most out of it. \r\nAnother strength of the class is how interesting the model essays are. I had grown very accustomed to reading extraordinarily boring texts. In this class, however, 95% of the selection for the readings was perfect. The texts for the class are also a major class. \r\nLastly, the environment is very low-stakes. The professor is not there to fail you. The most important part of that class was learning rhetorical strategies amongst our peers while working together to improve our own craft. I feel like every student is incentivized to play an active role in shaping the class, and the grade will come pretty naturally to those that are willing to engage with the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1707030\n 1707030\n \n 1707030\n The course is very organized. It is very clear in advance when the essays and the drafts are due. I liked the readings we had to do for seminars. The only thing I would do is to let students know in advance that it is not necessary to buy the course packet if they don't mind printing and (minimally) organizing it in advance. The packet costs a lot more than student printing and you would have to carry hundreds of pages with you to every class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1720720\n 1720720\n \n 1720720\n This course honestly made me a worse writer, I was encouraged to write less concisely, less clearly, and less directly
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724355\n 1724355\n \n 1724355\n The strength of this course was Prof. Shirkhani. While all ENGL 120 classes tend to have rave reviews, I cannot recommend ENGL 120 - Shirkhanis version enough. She has a spark within her that engages every single person in the room. When she picks up that piece of chalk to write down a technique, we are all waiting for that special moment when it makes contact with the board. I loved almost all of the readings and wished our discussions would never end. She was incredible at Office Hours, helping me turn my pieces from bad to good to great to phenomenal. I also loved reading Klinkenborg. The best part of this course was the amount of feedback you get on each and every piece of writing. As a STEM major with no background in writing, I was truly terrified about this course. Now, I know that I am a writer. And I will continue to be a writer even after ENGL 120. I am deeply grateful for her and this class. One major weakness was that there was a ton of confusion regarding the course packet at the beginning of the semester. While I dont believe that was Prof. Shirkhanis fault, it was a major headache. Another weakness of the course would be that Prof. Shirkhani has a soft spot for David Foster Wallace and Joan Didion. I would have preferred to read fewer pieces by those authors in order to have greater diversity in the selections read. One final weakness is that she has very unique tastes. Make sure to check with her about whether she likes your draft BEFORE you take it to someone else (e.g., Poorvu Writing Center, Residential College Writing Tutors) as you will be wasting your time otherwise.
