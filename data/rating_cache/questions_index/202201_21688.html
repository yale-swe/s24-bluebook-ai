\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1596915\n 1596915\n \n 1596915\n Strengths:\r\n - Assessment: Assessments are fair and spaced out well. Grading is fair. \r\n - Resources: Prof. Hawkins prepares ample and relevant resources for exam preparation, including lists of relevant topics (basically your fourth grade science teacher's study guide) and practice worksheets. After reviewing these resources, I gained confidence and clarity. The lecture slides are also filled with in-depth explanations relevant for problem sets.\r\n - Extra help: the TFs and Prof. Hawkins are incredibly generous with their time. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses:\r\n - Class format: a quarter of the class is spent listening to Prof. Hawkins' long-winded response to a classmate's far-too-advanced question. While I recognize that the purpose of the seminar class is to provide students the opportunity to ask questions, several questions are irrelevant and not only fail to further, but may at times hobble, collective learning. I would encourage Prof. Hawkins to be more selective with the questions he answers, and limit his responses when the information is not directly relevant to the course material.\r\n - Timeline: this class does operate at a very last-minute timeline. Incredibly helpful resources may not be provided until days before exams. Assignments are usually not graded before the next assignment is due.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1597283\n 1597283\n \n 1597283\n This course covered a wide range of topics, so it was consistently engaging as we worked with new material each class. I also greatly appreciated the opportunities we had to discuss and come up with our own answers in this class, instead of it just being entirely lecture-based.\r\nThe only weakness of this course is that it was sometimes difficult to tell which information being presented was crucial for the final exam, and which was simply supplementary knowledge. Therefore, I would have appreciated if a bigger emphasis was placed on the more important topics beforehand, so we knew what to focus our studying on in advance and could rule out topics that our professor deemed less important. Essentially, I wish that the review sheet (what to know/not to know) was circulated in advance, or that a similar version of that was present throughout the semester, not only at the end.
