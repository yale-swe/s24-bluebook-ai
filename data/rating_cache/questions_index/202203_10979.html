\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1621905\n 1621905\n \n 1621905\n Strengths: TFs are AMAZING. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622036\n 1622036\n \n 1622036\n The class had really cool content overall and I found myself really intrigued by each new topic. I think the format of the class was generally really nice.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1622971\n 1622971\n \n 1622971\n A major strength of this class was the format: reasonably-spaced problem sets of an appropriate challenge, low-point exercises to ensure understanding of the relevant material, and a take-home final exam all served to reduce stress while still making sure students understand the material. The professors were also a strong point, as their enthusiasm and familiarity with the material were both very helpful. One downside was the lecture notes/slides-- while these were posted, they were often incomplete or hard to parse without additional information, and in general could not be used to "reconstruct" the lectures for the purpose of studying. While I understand the hesitance to post recordings as this may reduce attendance, if recordings are not posted then improving the slides/notes would be very helpful. Another (somewhat minor) downside was the pacing-- while I realize that everyone's mathematical background may not be the same, I felt that the course moved a bit slowly in class. Even if the core content stays the same, spending less time on algebra (while still including the full derivations in an improved version of the slides/notes) and potentially replacing that with additional examples or MatLab content would be more engaging.
