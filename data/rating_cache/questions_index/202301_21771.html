\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1652992\n 1652992\n \n 1652992\n I really enjoyed this course! One thing I greatly appreciated was Professor North's no-technology policy -- especially in an English seminar, it's so great to be able to interact with the text in an authentic print form without people getting sucked into their laptops. I think this was a huge factor in how much I was able to put in and therefore get out of the course. Another strength was the discussion-based format of the class. I think this is one of the more effective seminars I've been in, as we were guided sufficiently by the instructor but also allowed to reach our own conclusions and come to them organically.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657810\n 1657810\n \n 1657810\n Strengths: During each class meeting, we deeply explore a few poems from the previous night's homework for content and style. The poems themselves are curated based on writer and theme, and these intense explorations of topics and poets builds a fascination for the course. And Professor North creates a class community that is so tight-knit. At the beginning of each class, we share names and "threshold thoughts" so that we build friendships with our classmates so that we are more comfortable rigorously analyzing wonderful poems together. The essays facilitate student discovery through the encouragement to tour poetry for its rich meanings. Professor North also makes himself available for insightful office hours conversations to brainstorm and review essays and explore the course content more deeply. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: The turnaround on essay feedback can be slow at times, but only because Professor North provides such articulate and thoughtful feedback that this, of course, takes time.

He starts every class going around asking whats on everyones mind which brought us closer together as a class and focused everyone for the rest of class. I learned a lot and especially as a non-English major this course really made me love reading and writing about poetry. The only weakness is that the papers started so late since they were delayed so there was almost no work at the beginning of the semester but more toward the end. But I think overall it didnt interfere with my enjoyment of the course at all. I really liked that he gave us the option for the final paper to write and analyze our own poem based on the formal features of one of the poets we read in class.
