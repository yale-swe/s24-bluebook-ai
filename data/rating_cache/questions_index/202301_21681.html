\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1654457\n 1654457\n \n 1654457\n The strength of this course is that it is a seminar (nominally). By this I mean the professor lectures at you, but around a small table. This means that you have the opportunity to ask questions, although the professor moves really fast. Another strength was that the material was presented in a way that made sense sequentially.\r\n\r\nThere are a lot of weaknesses in this course. Firstly, the uneven workload. Having weeks where we have an exam every other week, including heavy homework and huge readings of a novel about cancer. The other weakness of this course is that it attempts to cover more material than there is a reasonable time for, which led to the professor keeping us late every class and having giant "quizzes". Finally, the grading system was super unclear and the professor was not able to give me a clear and definite answer on what my midterm grade was. The professor often did not elaborate on how the "curve" would impact our scores.

Much of the content felt rushed through especially as concepts got more and more difficult. I think this could've been better as a lecture course where there are sectioned off times for discussion.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1655596\n 1655596\n \n 1655596\n Deeply valued and ***enjoyed*** every aspect of Dr. Belperrons MCDB 103 Cancer course an introductory biology class designed primarily for freshmen and sophomores including those who have yet to take or complete college level biology classes. Whats great about this class is that while Dr. Belperron thoughtfully starts from the premise that students only possess a high school level exposure to biology, the course methodically helps students construct a deep foundational understanding of all aspects of the biology of cancer which includes deep dives on genetics, biochemistry, immunity, infection agents, as well as cancer prevention and treatment modalities and challenges. \r\n\r\nBelperrons lectures are super insightful and straight-forward helping students parse through complex concepts and gain a working knowledge of key topics. Assigned readings have been carefully curated for accessibility providing pathways toward both understanding as well as jumping off points for additional investigation. Class assignments all make sense and are again, straight-forward; these include having students draft and submit weekly discussion questions, exams, participation in a group project/presentation as well as an individual end of term research presentation and paper. \r\n\r\nThroughout all these assignments and exams, Dr. Belperron invests significant time and energy to consistently provide objective and actionable feedback that contribute to the learning process. Throughout the course, Dr. Belperron was readily accessible via office hours and email and is always positively responsive; happy to make time to help guide and mentor students. She also invests considerable amounts of her time beyond office hours to conduct optional review sessions and has hosted her classes for working lunches. I was fortunate to take Dr. Belperrons MCDB 106: Biology of Malaria, Lyme and Other Vector-Borne Disease this past Fall and had such a positive experience that I enrolled in MCDB 103 during the current Spring 2023 term.\r\n\r\nFor any student interested in a subject (cancer) that has global health importance and touches so many of us whether on policy, research or granular clinical levels I could not recommend Dr. Belperron and this class (along with her other class, MCDB 106 - Biology of Malaria, Lyme and Other Vector-Borne Diseases) more highly.
