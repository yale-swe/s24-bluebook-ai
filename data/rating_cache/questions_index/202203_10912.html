\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1639490\n 1639490\n \n 1639490\n I think this course could benefit from more in class work. It would be cool to work with teammates in class while having the opportunity to check out what other teams are working on.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1639531\n 1639531\n \n 1639531\n I liked the assignments given to help encourage planning early on and the timeline for MVP and our final presentations, but I think experimenting with a flipped classroom format / using some class time for groups to meet and work on their projects would help with accountability issues within groups, and maybe also encourage more attendance during lecture.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1640450\n 1640450\n \n 1640450\n I think the idea behind the course is quite sound -- learn software engineering concepts in lecture, work on an actual piece of software in a team outside of lecture. However, these two parts of the course are pretty disjoint, as you don't see much of a practical reason to use many of the ideas from lecture beyond the notion that it's supposedly good practice to do so. Also, being in a team where everyone including yourself is inexperienced can be very difficult at times, and I didn't really feel that there was enough support built into the structure of the course to help with this. There were, of course, TAs, but they, obviously, lacked specific knowledge about your team's project, and so, in my experience, although they did try their best to help, the objective amount of help they were able to give was quite little (through no real fault of their own, of course).\r\n\r\nPersonally, I think the course could benefit from a redesign. Being able to choose your own project is a neat idea in theory, but I think I would have preferred it if we were given a set of possibilities to choose from, in which case the TAs and professor would have been able to provide more specific help for any problems you ran into. Additionally, the project could then be broken down into assignments, where you have to complete a particular component of the project by the assignment's due date. The entire team would then work on each component, allowing for everyone to gain more general experience, rather than specific experience with the role they had within the team. I also think it would be good for teams to be somewhat forced to abide by the software engineering best practices discussed in the course, as this kind of strikes me as the whole point of the course, and yet our team mostly just brute-forced our way through the project. There's not really a clear penalty for doing otherwise, so it's difficult to convince your team that it's worth trying pair programming or scrums, for example. \r\n\r\nThese are just my ideas and should all be taken with a grain of salt, as they reflect my own personal experience with the course, which will obviously not be the same for everyone.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1640450\n 1640450\n \n 1640450\n To be honest, no. I think the idea behind the class is good, but the execution is not. In its current state, I think the class is more stress and effort than it's really worth. Even though the workload of this course is technically fairly low, another important factor to consider when assessing workload is how it feels to do the work for the course. For me, the feeling was usually pretty miserable, as I had little idea what I was doing and ran into tons of minor issues that, while minor, still blocked all progress until I figured out the solution, either through trial and error, looking through StackOverflow, or a bit of both. As the deadline to have the project done by closed in, my stress levels became greatly heightened, as I still had no idea what I was doing but still needed to do my part for the team. \r\n\r\nIf you have some idea of what you're doing, the course might not be as stressful, but then it's also difficult to say whether you would really get anything out of it. My recommendation, if you're looking to gain software engineering experience, is to read a book (such as the textbook used for the course) to learn about the concepts, and then get an internship. I just don't think this course is particularly necessary for anyone to take.
