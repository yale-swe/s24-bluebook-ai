\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1618130\n 1618130\n \n 1618130\n I would hesitate to recommend this class to other students. I took this class because I wanted to practice my Spanish, I wanted to learn a bit about Latin American legal systems, and I wanted a lighter workload class. The classes were not designed to get students to practice speaking in Spanish, so I don't feel like I got to practice Spanish in class as much as I would have wanted. That being said, I think my reading and listening skills did improve. We learned a bit of content about the legal system, but about half of the classes were spent talking about random podcasts or news articles about something that happened in the Spanish-speaking world, and it was unclear how these discussions of current events were supposed to relate to the legal concepts we were learning about. I also would caution that this class is not as light of a workload as its reputation would suggest-- you have weekly assignments that take on average 3-4 hours to complete. One major reason that I would probably not recommend this class is that the professor was quite disorganized and did not respond to student emails. A syllabus was posted, but she told us early in the semester to ignore the syllabus, and she would post our homework on Canvas. But she sometimes did not post the homework until a day before it was due, which is quite inconvenient when it takes 3-4 hours to complete. When I emailed her with a question about the homework, she would never respond to my emails. She also once cancelled our class and then, an hour before it would have met, sent a canvas announcement un-cancelling our class, which was also quite inconvenient.
