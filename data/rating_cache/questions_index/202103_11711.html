\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521563\n 1521563\n \n 1521563\n I wanted to love this course, and there were many great things about it. The field trips were well thought out, as were the topics covered on the syllabus. I feel like I left the course with a greater understanding of the natural systems around me. However, Dr. Freidenburg really struggled to get organized all semester. This had a few notable impacts on the course: 1) Tuesday sections were completely led by students and thus were often ungrounded and unfocused. Dr. Freidenburg should really be giving a mini-lecture before every Tuesday section-- one that sets up the topics of discussion in a clear and logical manner. Despite promising to do this after mid-semester feedback, it never happened, which was very disappointing. 2) The lab report assignments were very stressful, especially because the objective of the final two field trips wasn't elucidated before we went to collect the data. If we were given the opportunity to discuss potential topics before the field trips, it would be much easier to collect the necessary data and thus tell a compelling narrative. Furthermore, the first and second lab reports should not be due within two weeks of each other, especially when feedback was so scant. 3) Although not formally required, you really should have some statistical or coding experience before you take the class. The lab reports would have been overwhelming if I didn't know R, and I feel really bad for those who didn't. 4) Lastly, the final month of classes/field trips felt thrown together last minute, or not planned at all. Although we certainly appreciated the sentiment of "you all are busy at the end of the semester so we'll just end class early", it felt like we weren't having class because Dr. Freidenburg forgot to prepare something. On a few occasions, this was literally the case, which was awkward. All in all, I learned a lot in the course, but it could certainly be improved with a little more organization on the instructor's part.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527352\n 1527352\n \n 1527352\n This course was great. I liked the weekly field trips, which start the first week, because they are highly engaging and also allow the class to get to know one another quickly. This makes for a better learning and discussion environment because we were all comfortable working together and sharing thoughts. The field experiments were great because they gave us an idea of different types of field science that can be conducted. In addition, the final iNaturalist project was a great way for everyone in the class to look at ecology of New Haven through a lens that they found was interesting, leading to a diverse array of presentations. The TA and professor were both very helpful and clearly wanted to help us learn. One weakness was the timing of the lab reports, which sometimes came very close together and felt far from the time we actually conducted the labs.
