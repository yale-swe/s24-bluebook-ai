\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1584648\n 1584648\n \n 1584648\n This class was phenomenal. I recommend it to all. \r\nDiscussion about characters like Falstaff and Barnardine was especially memorable.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594825\n 1594825\n \n 1594825\n I really enjoyed the class discussion
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594836\n 1594836\n \n 1594836\n Strengths: Bromwich has been teaching this class for years and is super knowledgeable on the topic. Shakespeare is all about his extraordinary language and his ability to write from the perspective and in the style of such diverse characters. In this class, you gain a lot of exposure to his use of language and should be able to trace the evolution of his writing from the start to end of his career, which was very interesting.\r\n"Weaknesses": This is not a class that chiefly does close reading. At the beginning of the semester, he said something along the lines of: there are many valid interpretations of Shakespeare, but there are also wrong interpretations. So don't be too surprised if he straight-up rejects an interpretation you propose in class--as he sometime does (nicely). But I think this frames the discussions on the plays in a more structured way that illuminates the interesting "Shakespearean questions" and you get to know Shakespeare as a writer/thinker/intellectual rather than as "Shakespeare," the works.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594869\n 1594869\n \n 1594869\n Strengths: Professor Bromwich is very intelligent and holds us to very high standards. I appreciated that he wanted us to be highly specific in our comments in class and would work with us until he understood just what we meant. I also appreciated that he always made himself available for meetings and office hours.\r\n\r\nWeakness: This course was billed as a seminar but was basically taught as a lecture. Our course was moved from a seminar room to a lecture hall. The first hour of class was simply a lecture; the second hour was class participation, but we all directed our comments to Bromwich as opposed to each other. He would respond to our comments individually and then allow the next question, meaning we hopped from topic to topic and did not have a naturally flowing discussion.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1595249\n 1595249\n \n 1595249\n This was a really interesting course, and strongly taught by Prof. Bromwich, who is an excellent lecturer and professor. Bromwich does an excellent job serving as you guide through Shakespeare's plays, highlighting the interesting questions/language. Shakespeare explores so many questions in every play and has such diverse characters. So, you definitely need to come prepared to class (i.e., finished the plays and carefully thought about them on your own before class). Otherwise, you will be lost during class when Bromwich starts lecturing about all these different scenes and insights.
