Professor Shayesteh is the only avenue you can practice and learn Persian, but she expects you to do outside work too. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1609495\n 1609495\n \n 1609495\n I really enjoyed getting a deep understanding of Persian culture through weekly discussions about prominent Persian literary and arts figures as well as aspects of Persian culture such as food, music, dancing, aesthetics, gardening, architecture, decoration, and more. It was great to go on a class visit to the Beinecke and explore collections that included historic Persian works. I enjoyed watching a movie in Persian one day and discussing it, along with the occasional music video. I'm glad our professor offered us outside-of-class immersion opportunities such as Persian Students' Association and women's rights events. I also felt that the textbooks we worked with were really good at teaching us to practice Persian, and going over our homework sometimes in class was helpful. We had an amazing TA who ran our language table once a week, and that was a great place for practicing the language. Another strength of the course was that we learned Persian grammar rules thoroughly. I enjoyed doing roleplay projects and presentations with my peers, which were often humorous but also a great practice opportunity.\r\n\r\nI wish we'd been able to read some of the literary works that we explored, but I don't know if that's feasible considering how much actual language curriculum we have to cover. I also think that language table should have been used exclusively for Persian conversation as opposed to for learning new Persian words.
