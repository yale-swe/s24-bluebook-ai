\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1646957\n 1646957\n \n 1646957\n STRENGTHS:\r\n- Profs. Bojanowska and Roosien are both fantastic. Both are experts in \r\n- The format works quite well for what the course is going for. It's a lecture with discussion section, and the lectures had significant student interaction to add our own interpretations. However, I do feel like the lectures could've been longer. We'd often read an entire short novel and then only spend one lecture (and however much time in section) discussing it. 75 minute lectures may encourage deeper analysis of the material. \r\n- The variety of sources was refreshing. This isn't your standard history class focused solely on readings. Film screenings and art helped to diversify the material of the course and my favorite part was analyzing artwork. \r\n\r\nWEAKNESSES:\r\n- The structure of the course can be somewhat disorienting. It's structured in a way such that we focus on particular regions or groups of people at a time instead of chronological order like you may expect from a typical history course. More context would've been helpful from one week to the next. Additionally, it felt like we would revisit topics we already learned about. For example, we discussed the Soviet Union in the first or second week of lecture and then came back to it in the final few weeks of lecture. \r\n- Some of the readings were more interesting than others. I loved Tolstoy's Hadji Murat, but I slogged through other readings like Ulitskaya's Medea and Her Children. It seems like I wasn't alone in this, but Profs. Bojanowska and Roosien are committed to improving the course in the future and took a poll of what readings we most and least enjoyed, so I expect this to be addressed.
