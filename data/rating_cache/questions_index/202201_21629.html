\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1569751\n 1569751\n \n 1569751\n The strengths of this course lie in the material itself, which is imperative to any prospective math majors. The main weakness of the course was the poor design. The problem sets became worth progressively more and more as the semester went on, which doesn't make sense since the problem sets should probably be the same workload and value through the whole semester. The material on the tests was also rarely similar to what was on the problem sets or what we learned in class. As a result, the ultimate curve was odd as well. If this class was better designed with guided lecture notes for the lectures, problem sets which were equal over the whole semester, and midterms and finals where we knew what to expect, the students would have had a more enjoyable time in the class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1574420\n 1574420\n \n 1574420\n Strength: Very useful and wide introduction to mathematical proofs and proof-writing. Her lectures were a good mix of explication of the basic ideas and applying those ideas to produce larger, important results, as well as to answer smaller questions. Professor Toprak responded clearly and concisely to all student questions during lecture. Homework was well-written to encourage deep engagement with the ideas of the course, useful feedback was given on both homework and tests, and tests did not contain any surprising material not covered in class or homework.\r\nWeakness: I'm not sure that we covered all the material we were supposed to have; the spectral theorem was listed in the course description but never mentioned in class, for example. This may have been Professor Toprak's response to some students struggling with earlier material, but it was frustrating.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1599377\n 1599377\n \n 1599377\n Please, please, please offer Math 225 AND 226 in the same year! Sometimes students did not utilize their help. It is hard to assess in this course if you are totally lost or not so I wish there were more indicators of that early on. You had to rigorously seek help and feedback to pass, it wasn't just given to you.
