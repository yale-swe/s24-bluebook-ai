\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1521882\n 1521882\n \n 1521882\n I learned how to evaluate empires from history, identifying what strategies the imperial force created to justify their colonization efforts. I want to emphasize that this was not what I expected the course to cover. My expectation was that the class would look at modern examples of imperialism, and dismantle narratives we ourselves may be convinced of. Instead, we looked at historic examples of empires, which was at times dull, and the "narratives" we searched for were often fairly obvious and somewhat repetitive. It was still interesting, but different than my expectation.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527465\n 1527465\n \n 1527465\n I developed new historical knowledge as well as an in-depth understanding of the impact that empires and their strategies/practices have had on shaping many aspects of the world today. While I was familiar with some of the topics we covered from high school history courses, material was presented in a new way and I felt that I learned something new each class meeting. \r\n\r\nI also think I developed my discussion and synthesis skills in this class. The content we covered has definitely impacted my worldview -- there are imperialistic structures present at all levels of society.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528378\n 1528378\n \n 1528378\n I learned a lot about the ways in which imperial powers have generated false narratives in an attempt to justify colonization, especially through the themes of "civilizing missions," commercialization of empire, and paternalism. The most interesting insights that I developed by taking this course were from the counter-narrative literature we read, especially A Small Place by Jamaica Kincaid. The novels were absolutely incredible, and the discussions about them were always extremely enlightening.

I also think that the discussion form of the class allowed me to sharpen my critical thinking skills ad to be a more active member in discussions.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538447\n 1538447\n \n 1538447\n I developed skills in careful reading and analyzation. I learned that there are always more perspectives to understand than what is immediately presented. I gained better insight into understanding the significance of the viewpoint I learned history from.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540462\n 1540462\n \n 1540462\n In this class, I learned how to identify and deconstruct imperialistic narratives in a multitude of forms (i.e. historical documents, movies, advertisements, videos). I learned how to apply a critical lens to the stories that have been created about the past and build my own response to such stories through analysis of both primary and secondary sources.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1548599\n 1548599\n \n 1548599\n I learned a lot about the narratives empires built about their colonies. By the mid of the course, you get to a point when you start recognizing interesting patterns that appear very frequently in those narratives.
