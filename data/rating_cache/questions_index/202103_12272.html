\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527589\n 1527589\n \n 1527589\n Reading Poe and Kafka together was something very few people outside of our class have done before. They have little in common, most likely never read each other's work, if they even knew the other existed. So starting there, the class fascinated me at the opportunity it provided. Literary comparison was something I was largely unfamiliar with, and definitely gained from this class. I had never really read any poe or any Kafka, so not only was the comparison new, but the material itself as well! Lastly, the writing assignments in this class were remarkably creative, forcing us to think about literature analysis in new, fun ways.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1527589\n 1527589\n \n 1527589\n The strengths of this course is the material--there is so much more to Poe and Kafka than there really is any right to be. The weakness of this course is how the material is conveyed--very little did we actually draw comparisons between the two, spending more of our time looking at individual texts and how they should be analyzed. I wanted more cross-author comparison, broken down by theme or subject matter, and got very little of it. Because of how good the material was, I got to do a lot of it on my own, but that's not the point of a 70,000 dollar education now, is it?
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1528129\n 1528129\n \n 1528129\n Discussions in ENGL 257 were always fascinating, but there were times when they felt intentionally dense and hyper-philosophical to the point of being inaccessible or unproductive.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1529946\n 1529946\n \n 1529946\n Strengths: good combo of lecture and seminar, big class so the instructors talked the most and had super interesting things to say. Reading materials were well organized and thematic (there was a Halloween-themed week with creepy stories, I don't know if it was on purpose) and class was genuinely fun.\r\nWeaknesses: The criticisms we read towards the end of the semester were really dense and hard to discuss. Also, I wished we had gotten a primer on poe and Kafka's lives and the history surrounding them so we had more context.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1531204\n 1531204\n \n 1531204\n The co-teaching model by this class is a double sword. I was able to learn from two different professors and two literary traditions. I also saw how Prof. North's and Prof. Smith's ideas interacted. But sometimes the class ran more like a Podcast, and the profs chatted with each other more than with us. The professors did do a good job facilitating group discussions, though--these discussions allowed me to know my classmates and see different readings of the same texts. The assignments tended to be more creative and academic. They were really thought-provoking and fun to write, but if you want a course to improve your academic writing, maybe this course is not it. If you're a creative writer, though, the assignments should feel like a breeze.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1538797\n 1538797\n \n 1538797\n The biggest strength of the course was the teaching team. The professors knew not just the material but also the theory and philosophy to reach a level of conversation beyond any other English or humanities class I have ever taken. The way I know this is that they spoke about 70% of the time, at least in the beginning. This is a HUGE strength. We go to Yale to learn from the best of the best, not to hear 19 year olds flex about something they may or may not know from a fancy high school class they've taken. Also, the assignments were incredibly creative and experimental and actually engaged in work far beyond the classic response paper. AND, I never feared getting punished for taking a risk. They made it very clear that ideas and thought were praised far beyond polish. \r\n\r\nA weakness if I had to give one would be that sometimes there were inconsistencies in the syllabus. They are both SO chill, meaning that things would get changed and decided in class, submission portals would not be made, etc. but this is a very small grievance.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1541731\n 1541731\n \n 1541731\n Strengths: Fascinating course with engaging readings! I loved seeing the two professors bounce thoughts off each other and think out loud. Discussion questions were also very interesting.\r\n\r\nImprovement: Group discussions really helped people who struggle to participate usually and I wish there were more of them! There were so many different types of assignments which could be a strength but it is hard with each assignment to know what the professors expect.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545309\n 1545309\n \n 1545309\n Weaknesses is that the class discussion felt very disorganized; I felt like I did not have much guidance on how to engage with philosophical discussion for the first time. I would have liked more guiding questions to help prepare me for unexpectedly connecting a work to the abstract question it supposedly engaged with. At least the written assignments were often unrelated to the more abstract/philosophical side of discussion, so if I had a hard keeping up in discussion at least it was not held against me, but then again I wished I had had a chance to be forced to engage in that type of thinking to become more comfortable with it.\r\n\r\nStrengths is that it was pleasant to come to class with an open mind and see where discussion lead with little stakes. The professors were very kind, clearly great friends with each other, and it was fun to hear them talk to each other so comfortably about the work and draw from their respective expertise on the authors. I also appreciated how they would often respond to student points and rephrase them/connect them to larger points, which made me feel appreciated and reassured that my sometimes desperate contributions had value. I wished I had gone to office hours more often because the one time I did was very helpful, but they were not super prompt in responding to email.
