\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1520902\n 1520902\n \n 1520902\n It could be improved by either lengthening lectures or by diving into more specific events in section.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1522237\n 1522237\n \n 1522237\n strengths\r\n- lectures extremely engaging and easy to follow\r\n- very interesting readings \r\n- papers that gave a lot of freedom \r\n- content was excellent \r\n\r\nweaknesses\r\n- need more of a reason to go to lecture (the main reason I went was because Professor Westad is extremely engaging and the content is interesting - but when the course gets bigger and does not have as many history majors, there should probably be more of an incentive to attend lecture) \r\n- structure of course with large participation grade but not enough guidance about how to succeed in section
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1523886\n 1523886\n \n 1523886\n Strengths: Professor Westad's lectures are dynamic and engaging. He is a fantastic lecturer with a gift for making complex concepts, such as the remaking of the financial system in the 1970s and 1980s, really approachable. At the same time, he delivers his points concisely while allowing for questions at regular intervals during and after lecture. Overall, really strong lectures which will keep you coming back. \r\n\r\nThe structure of the course assignments, four 1,200-word papers, gives you license to really digest the readings without worrying as much about a midterm or final. It's a really well-paced, intuitive structure which does allow for improvement across the four papers.\r\n\r\nThe course covers a wide variety of topics; it is truly a global history course. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: There is a significant reading load for this course, especially at the beginning. But the reading will even out, especially as you adjust to the reading load, and the four assignments are spaced out to give you time to complete any readings if you are behind. \r\n\r\nThis course does not delve very much into specific historical facts like details about WWI itself (it's not necessarily a weakness, since this is a global course). It could be frustrating not to have additional details within lectures and readings. Fortunately, the readings and texts have good references and help you to find other additional readings you may wish to include in the four written assignments.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1533212\n 1533212\n \n 1533212\n The greatest strength of the course was the passion and knowledge professor Westad brought to lecture. I believe the greatest weakness, however, is that there are no examinations in the class. The lack of any examinations means that there is very little incentive for students to attend lecture or learn the course's materials, outside of lectures and readings which pertain to their chosen essay topics.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1536217\n 1536217\n \n 1536217\n Lectures were great, the readings were pretty good and section was actually the most helpful and enjoyable I've had so far at Yale. Prof. Westad was very engaging and incredibly knowledgeable, and Nikolaus cared more about us than any other TA I've had so far. The assignments were quite short, which was great, but the one downside was the grading was sort of weird. I felt like by the end I had improved in my ability to write to Nikolaus' wishes, but not necessarily improved as a writer. I learned to keep everything very simple, but I was still frustrated by some of the stuff he took issue with in papers. Overall though, a very good class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1539626\n 1539626\n \n 1539626\n Section was incredibly time consuming, but the way the course was structured was a huge strength (4 papers over the course of the term). Lecture was also great.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1540996\n 1540996\n \n 1540996\n The lectures were well produced and the section discussions were interesting. While the reading load was heavy, the grading structure allowed for flexibility in prioritizing the topics that most interested you. You get to pick what you write your essays about.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1545016\n 1545016\n \n 1545016\n Strengths: Professor Westad and our TF Nikolaus made a great teaching team. Lectures were engaging and the workload wasn't too bad. At the beginning the reading load felt very daunting but eventually I got used to it (and learned to skim). I loved how much freedom and flexibility we had in our papers, both in terms of topics and timing. We just had to turn in four papers before their respective deadlines, so you could pretty much write them whenever you'd like. \r\nWeaknesses: I often found myself craving for something more in-depth, but because we had so much ground to cover, it wasn't always possible to really delve into any particular topic.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1547562\n 1547562\n \n 1547562\n I thought the course was a great overview of the main events of the twentieth century. However, I thought more focus on the Middle East could have been a good addition to the course.
