Question 3 Answers

\n \n \n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
RespondentAnswer
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1562655\n 1562655\n \n 1562655\n It's hard to say how this course could be improved. The two biggest problems I noticed in the course were student attendance and full group engagement (though small group engagement was very good). The inconsistent student attendance was confusing to me considering that each day the lessons we coveredbe them literature or grammarled directly into the next days' lessons. The few times I had to miss class across both semesters, it took a work on my part to catch up to the next days lesson.\r\n\r\nThe class is almost entirely a positive experience in my opinion. Prof. Alexandrov's direct approach, array of subject matter and examples, and interactive format created an environment where the teaching was very effective. We bounced between grammar lessons, news articles, films, and a book. All of these were rounded out with regular writing assignments, some formal and some informal. The result was a very effective method of learning Spanish, and one that is both lasting and sets a foundation for continued growth.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1577145\n 1577145\n \n 1577145\n Biggest strength students being comfortable to converse with each other. I appreciated the non judgmental environment. I would say the only weakness was that the workload could get overwhelming.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1584962\n 1584962\n \n 1584962\n Very tight knit class with engaging conversations and the environment always felt safe and welcoming. I honestly can't think of any improvements. There was however a ton of homework for this course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1587486\n 1587486\n \n 1587486\n Such a pleasant experience for all students, only improvement could be to have more conversations face to face.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1587566\n 1587566\n \n 1587566\n Range of topics, class activities, and projects
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1597452\n 1597452\n \n 1597452\n Having never taken a heritage class before and having a group of classmates that did not make me feel embarrassed about my Spanish, this class was more than just a place for learningit was a safe space for me. One strength of the course is the incredible warmth of Professor Alexandrov, who pushes us to better ourselves in Spanish while covering important topics such as racism, environmentalism, and Covid19. After taking this course, I feel that I have improved in my Spanish ability in addition to my knowledge about Latin America, which I learned from first-hand experiences from my peers themselves. \r\nFor this class to function, all students must be comfortable speaking to their peers in Spanish without embarrassment or fearthis is one thing that is especially difficult for many still-learning heritage speakers. Nevertheless, it is much easier to speak to someone who you may see as a friend instead of a classmate. One weakness of the course is that if the natural dynamic of the class is not already extroverted enough to get students talking freely with each other, conversations are stale and do not bode well to learning. I would recommend spending a large amount of time at the beginning of the semester doing randomized partner to partner icebreaker activities/prompting conversations where the students need to learn things about each other, because it will facilitate closeness and greatly improve the functionality of the class.
