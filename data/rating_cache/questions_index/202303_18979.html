\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1716423\n 1716423\n \n 1716423\n Strengths: \r\n-the information is in depth and you will definitely leave the course knowing more than when you came in\r\n-the discussion sections allow you to review what you learned in lecture and reflect more on the readings\r\nWeaknesses:\r\n- the lecture style is not effective and does not promote learning. You can only write it down and hope to read it later. The lectures are also not recorded so if you miss something you have no way to hear that information again. \r\n- there are way too many readings for this course which makes it difficult to read anything in depth because you always have so much to read. You should be allowed to write all 3 papers then only be graded for your best 2 but only 2 should be required. This would encourage students to do more readings and know all the material because they would write a paper each month incorporating the lecture information and readings. It would also allow them to get a better grade as only the best 2 would be in the gradebook which would show their improvement rather than only having 2 opportunities. \r\n-the final exam also is not a productive tool for learning as students are given 7 prompts to prepare so that they can respond to 2 of them on the final plus answer key terms. I think the final should just be an essay or multiple essays that students submit via Canvas which would allow us to put more time into it, review the readings and lectures to incorporate in the essay, and intrinsically learn that way. Then if the professor would like us to take an in-person exam it should only be on the key terms. It was frustrating for me to have to practically write 7 essays and then memorize each one only to write 2 on the exam. It felt like my other work was not being accounted for and I was not able to write as eloquently as I wanted to on the exam because I had less time and was under more pressure and had to write by hand.
