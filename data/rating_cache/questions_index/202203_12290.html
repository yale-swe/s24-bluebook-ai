Something super especial about this class is that they bring many guests to do the week's lecture, and since all of them are passionate about what they talk about, the passion for the topics is very contagious. Something to improve is the structure for the class: instructions for the journals were not clear at all until pretty late in the semester. \n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1626854\n 1626854\n \n 1626854\n Strengths: a lot of the course was structured around our weekly observations--I definitely think that seeing child development in practice was the primary part of my learning, and is the primary strength of this course. I think that it was also a strength that the professors had been in the field for so long, and that they would bring in guest speakers to speak directly on the fields they worked in--e.g. they brought in teachers to lecture on curriculum, a psychologist to speak about psychology, etc. All in all--I think the course is pretty in touch with the practice of child development, which really helped me engage with it. The workload also never felt overwhelming. \r\n\r\nWeaknesses: the lecture portions of the class were a tad long. I wish we'd spent more time discussing the material in the class. In terms of workload, keep in mind that you will have to spend time each week out of class doing observations, which I didn't mind (and, as above, found a good experience), but which can be a lot depending on how busy you are.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627537\n 1627537\n \n 1627537\n The strengths of the course are the professors and their guest lectures. I was exposed to various facets of the field and I felt that I retained much of the material presented to us. The context was incredibly interesting and useful, especially for those pursuing futures in child care policies. I gained a newfound appreciation of child care and education, as it is far more complex than I initially presumed. Another strength was the observations of the daycare and individual children. I felt like I could finally apply what I learned from the lectures and readings to real-life scenarios. I was able to apply my knowledge and write journals that reflected these connections that I made between observations and course material. As such, I retained the material and understood the implications of what I learned in the course, which will be useful in my future medical career. I don't think that the course can really be improved because the professors were understanding of the students' situations, even when there was confusion with the journal writing instructions. The professors are clearly passionate about the material and care about the wellbeing of the students, which leads me to the conclusion that there are really no weaknesses in the course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1627999\n 1627999\n \n 1627999\n STRENGTHS: Getting to observe real teachers who are experts in the childcare field. Also talking to guest speakers who are deeply involved in the work we were discussing. WEAKNESSES: Sometimes, it felt like the instructors and daycare center supervisors were saying that a parenting technique or idea was "CLEARLY the best way" with tone of judgement, especially towards people/cultures who parent different/have different values than white-American-middle-class. In fact, very few students had white-American-middle-class upbringings, and it felt like all those parenting styles were discounted and othered as "less than." Phrases like "that's why I don't understand parents who..." or "that's why THOSE kids aren't successful" felt like it was targeting BIPOC and immigrant parenting. I encourage the entire teaching team to really think about what conclusions are supported by large bodies of data, which are supported by case studies, which are supported by personal experiences, and what voices and experiences are left out of data and conclusions. I also noticed that there were quite a few Asian American women in the class, and it seemed that the teaching team could not tell them apart, even after 13 weeks of instructing a small seminar class. They would mix up their names and specifically call them out for attendance. This also added to the air of judgement towards immigrant/BIPOC parenting styles that I was picking up from the teaching team.
