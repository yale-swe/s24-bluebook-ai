\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1708899\n 1708899\n \n 1708899\n Many people taking this course already know quite a lot of Maths and Discrete Maths; they are familiar with the material, which created a very unequal distribution of grades for the midterms, with many people scoring a 100 while many others getting below 50 and dropping the course. This also created a false sense that the pace at which the professor might choose to proceed accommodated the students' needs. I felt very unsupported throughout the course. We did not go over proofs at all, which is integral to discrete maths since one is expected to prove everything. Although the lectures were okay, the supplemental materials we received were very insufficient -- they were almost non-existent, to begin with (we only had the psets and a set of practice questions for each midterm, none of which had answer keys...). This was a very unsatisfactory course for me overall, and I would not recommend it, at least not with Abinand Gopal.

\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1714444\n 1714444\n \n 1714444\n This course was fascinating and very engaging. The homework was relevant and the lectures were very well done.\r\n\r\nHowever, this course is a little slow and rarely challenging. The truly interesting questions required creative "aha!" moments, rather than thoughtful application of the course material. In the future, it might be better to have two versions of this class one for CS majors (as it's required for the major), and one for math majors interested in the topic. As a math major, I often found the material to be trivial and the pacing too slow.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1715346\n 1715346\n \n 1715346\n The lectures followed the textbook pretty well. However, the professor never posted lecture notes or recordings, which could have come in handy during review.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1721145\n 1721145\n \n 1721145\n Strengths: Abi! Warm-ups, PSETS, little jokes throughout lectures.\r\n\r\nWeaknesses: Nothing really.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724158\n 1724158\n \n 1724158\n In terms of the course structure itself, I think the topics covered had a decent order and I found the material relatively interesting. A lot of the issues I had with this course stemmed from how poorly it was managed. Problem sets took insane amounts of time to be graded, often with almost no meaningful feedback. Moreover, there were absolutely no sample solutions released for anything in the entire class, including exam review. I found myself doing near perfect on problem sets only to walk into the exam and be woefully unprepared because I had gotten no feedback to indicate I was doing anything wrong. The majority of the math classes I've had at Yale at least gave good feedback when it came, often with sample solutions, but without that fundamental feedback it felt very difficult to succeed in this course. I simply don't understand why giving us examples of problem sets or exam practice questions was deemed impossible. The worst feedback for certain was on exams; the professor often just said a question was wrong without elaborating why, which is especially bad when the midterms were all three questions split into parts. But with all that being said, a lot of that does probably have to do with the professor and how the course is managed by said professor; the material itself is I think pretty interesting and the textbook we followed was decent. If more consistent feedback was given, I think this could be a much much better course.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1724789\n 1724789\n \n 1724789\n This was a good introduction to discrete mathematics for students like me with proof-based math experience. There's nothing I would change, and I have loudly recommended this class to as many people as I can.
