\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1654228\n 1654228\n \n 1654228\n The course is very unstructured, which made me like it less than other art courses at Yale. If you like freedom to pick your own projects, it's a good class.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1657926\n 1657926\n \n 1657926\n It is a fantastic course - it gives sufficient amount of independence and flexibility and allows for exploration to develop own unique style. Sometimes I wish though the course provided a slightly more prescriptive guidance on the techniques to explore. That being said, when I requested such guidance during my 1:1 interactions with the Professor and the TA, I always received very helpful tips and feedback - it was just more in an unstructured format and highly personalized.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1658486\n 1658486\n \n 1658486\n The strengths of this course are its professors Prof. Maria and TA Daniela were truly amazing. They were so supportive and gave incredibly beneficial feedback, answering questions and allowing students to explore their artistic identities while also developing their technical skills through practice. However, direct instruction on those technical skills (beyond direct observation) would have been incredibly helpful. I'd also love to have learned a bit more about different media and how to safely manipulate the texture of oil paints.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1661470\n 1661470\n \n 1661470\n It is a lot of projects for not very much time but the instructors are aware of it and encourage students to present and critique even unfinished work. Because of this system sometimes its difficult to know when one project ends and one begins. Projects are also self driven and the prompts are incredibly open ended which can be both bad and good.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1663791\n 1663791\n \n 1663791\n Strengths: diversity of assignments, freedom in making work, structure of course (time spent painting vs. doing demos and talks vs. doing crits), and teaching staff.\r\nWeaknesses: few and far between, though the one that stood out to me the most at the end of the course was how we seemed to have a far shorter period of time to complete the final project than we had to complete the other assignments. This made it pretty challenging to complete as many layers of paint as my final required since I was using oil.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1666144\n 1666144\n \n 1666144\n The assignments were structured but open enough to allow for complete artistic freedom and expression. Additionally, each critique was kind yet helpful. I believe that in this phase of students' lives, when we are still learning whether we want to center our lives around art, encouragement is much more fruitful than criticism, so I am grateful that critiques led each student to feel more proud of the work that they had created. In terms of improvement, I think if future professors encouraged students to meet with them in office hours (for longer periods of time), students might feel even more supported throughout the semester.
\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1680221\n 1680221\n \n 1680221\n There was not much time to do each assignment, which lead to many rushed paintings that I wished I couldve worked more on
