\n \n Navigate to all answers for Respondent Id 1594311\n 1594311\n \n 1594311\n STRENGTHS: THE PROFESSOR: Professor Alvarez for sure. This has been one of the few, if not the only seminar, where I have been engaged each class. This has been because there have been engaging activities in each class period, the activities were also varied, and there was a LOT of mixed levels of discussion, from partner work to small group work to entire group discussion. He set up the class incredibly well and set up an environment that was very conducive to learning. THE READINGS: having a primary text for the class has its limitations of course, but it was so helpful to keep a consistent reading schedule and to keep the historical perspective consistent and really build up to content knowledge. I really enjoyed the beginning of class, when we had one theoretical work next to one historical content work to read and they complemented one another very well, that was an incredible reading experience. CLASS STRUCTURE: The way class was structured was such that a small group of students presented each week, but this wasn't just a presentation, it was basically like teaching a class. The presenters had to set up a warm up activity, a presentation with context, and scatter different discussion activities throughout the class. I feel like this structure led to the class being very engaging and motivating, and the varying activities that the presenters brought to the table made for very rich discussion in groups of different sizes as well as a variety of sources that we got exposed to. WRITING ASSIGNMENTS: The writing assignments were structured in such a way that by the end of the semester when the time came to write our final papers, we already had a historiography and primary source analysis written. It was really scaffolded well. Also all the assignments came with a very detailed description of what was expected, not only for this paper, but in historical writing as a whole in general. I gained a LOT of knowledge about how to write historical papers and also gained a LOT of resources for Mexican history research that will be useful for me in the future. \r\n\r\nNo weaknesses.
