 Yes, I would recommend this course to another student but only if you are in the Economics major and really want a strong theoretical and empirical foundation for future research. This course is difficult conceptually, but the resources are there for you to succeed and while I did struggle many times, by the end I am thrilled by how much I learned. Prof. Meghir was excellent at providing support and explaining econometrics in an intuitive fashion something that previous econometrics courses and professors failed to do. If you are hesitating to take this course, know that while it is a lot of hard work, it will also be very rewarding to complete the work, but only take it if you really want to delve into the mathematical and theoretical aspects of econometrics and learn about research methods and techniques.

 I was not expecting it (because dude, it's econometrics), but I loved this class. Loved it. Listen, econometrics is challenging and not always the most riveting subject in the world, but Professor Meghir made it so concrete and interesting. We had the opportunity to replicate a study of his using our own code, we got to really think critically about how to set up RCTs and other intervention techniques, and Prof Meghir was very intentional in going deep into derivations and the core, core elements behind econ-based stats. I feel so much more confident in my analytical and technical skills having taken this class. I would recommend this course to any student who realized (like me) that we would be kidding ourselves if we thought SDS 230 or Intro Econometrics was enough to even remotely (and responsibly!) engage with stats in the real world. Fantastic, fantastic course.
