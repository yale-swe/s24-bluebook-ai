 This course is extremely effective in making students, who may not be from a statistics/math background, move rapidly from a superficial to thorough understanding of probability. The strengths lie in the excellent organization of the course, in addition to Professor Zhou's wonderful lectures and attitude. The slides provide a condensed version of the textbook for efficient review of material. The problem sets address key concepts without necessitating extra experience with mathematical reasoning beyond the prerequisites and what is taught in lecture. Professor Zhou's lectures are engaging and instrumental to the success of the course. A large portion of the grade was left to the two exams, and I may have expected the weekly assignments to comprise a larger portion of the grade. However, I understand that this is how it's done in many S&DS and math courses at Yale, and the exams were definitely reasonable.
 The course was excellent except that it required much more of a knowledge of calculus than I was prepared for. So students should be warned of that.
