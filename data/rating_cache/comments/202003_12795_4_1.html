2020-Fall PLSC 028 01 American Constitutionalism

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409 CRN: 12795 Term: 202003

Back12345
 This course is a social science credit for a reason. There's a lot more thinking than there is history, though your thoughts and claims are based on writings and court cases. If you are taking this course because you are truly interested in the topic, go for it. Those who put in the effort to do all the readings and stay engaged are well rewarded. If you are taking this course because you are looking for a So credit or because the ratings seem really high, think again. The class is only as interesting as you are engaged. The same goes for workload; if you struggle with being engaged with the topic, readings and papers become much more difficult. That being said, this class was very intellectually stimulating and made me think a lot. I would recommend this course to students truly interested in the topic.
 Yes! I could not recommend this class enough, even if you are not interested in constitutional law more broadly. Professor Silverstein creates an unforgettable atmosphere in his class and is super approachable. The class allows you to question your assumptions about certain topics in law, and provides and incredible background in some foundations topics. This is a must take while at Yale.
 I would say shop the class to see if you want this class because it isn't for everyone. There is a lot of reading and you have to get ready to really contribute to feel fulfilled. That being said, the professor is great and really nice.
 I would definitely recommend this to someone interested in US government. In some ways it surveyed a lot of different topics about the United States, the constitution in particular but it was not limited to just the constitution. I think I came out of it with a greater understanding of the forces impacting American society and the role that the constitution plays.
 I personally did not enjoy this class. I think if you think are sincerely interested in studying the Constitution, then this is the class for you. This class taught me that I am not that interested in conlaw and I suppose that is one benefit of this course. I did not hate it, but I did not get as much enjoyment out of it as I thought I would.
 YES GORDON IS GOD
 If you enjoy reading difficult and sometimes older political writings, this course is perfect for you. It also helps to go above and beyond and do some extra research--we spent time on some intriguing and difficult topics.
 I would recommend this class to other students with a strong interest in politics and/or American history. The material was interesting and engaging and I think it makes a great foundation for any further study of politics.
 Yes, yes, yes. I was delighted to be able to take this course, and it was the absolute highlight of my first-semester experience at Yale. There's so much to take away from this course, whether you're interested in history, politics, law or just in American society. You'll be exposed to a fascinating set of readings, and get to interact with a tight-knit and likeminded group of classmates. The small, discussion-based nature of the class also means that it is better suited to Zoom than most.
 This course is very much student-led with much emphasis on discussion and analysis. I would recommend this course to another student that really wants to ask the questions about American politics they feel they have not had the chance to. Professor Silverstein is open to discussion after class and I would argue those that engage with the readings in this class more will better enjoy this course. Overtime, the reading does become more demanding, but I think it is a glimpse of the academic standards of a Yale College student, and thus, many will benefit from taking this course their freshmen year.
 If you are interested in law and American political theory and don't have a super rigorous schedule for the semester than yes. If you have a lot of high workload classes and are taking this as an extra elective then no. In order to actually enjoy this class it is necessary to read everything and be very engaged. If you cannot devote time to this class it is such a huge burden and not enjoyable. The readings pile up and they are not easy. If you haven't completed the readings you won't be able to participate in class and it will be very hard to pay attention. The few weeks I was able to fully process the readings I loved the class and the discussions and learned so much so I highly recommend taking it if you have time.
 Yes, very engaging and exciting class
 Yes, I would recommend this course to another student. The course is super interesting, and Professor Silverstein is incredibly knowledgeable and very interesting to learn from. The material is worthwhile to spend time on.
