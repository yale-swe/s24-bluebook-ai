Â The strength of this course was that it was only 5 weeks because it was the summer. The weaknesses abound. Biochemistry should NEVER have been a flipped classroom. It makes sense for problem based classes like math or physics, but for something as memorization-based as biochemistry, it did not make sense. The slides that Nicole Clay made were garbage - they were full of errors, terribly organized, and included audio clips of Nicole Clay's voice. These clips often were mostly "ums" and "ers" rather than actually information, which when you're sitting and listening to them is ridiculous. Additionally, the problem sets were not helpful because we didn't even have time to do them fully in class. Getting into groups was unproductive and resulted in me only learning the problems that my group completed but not the problems that other groups completed. The quizzes during each class were similarly ridiculous, since they were testing the material that we had listened to the slides, not material that we had gone over together in the problem sets. They were often badly written, cryptic, and very detailed. Nicole Clay constantly said not to study too much or not to know the "minutia" but then exactly test the minutia on both quizzes and exams. In fact one time, she specifically told us not to study the initiation factors of transcription, but then there was an entire problem on the exam asking us to describe initiation factors. The exams were fine but very harshly graded. Nicole Clay did this super cute thing where she gave nearly the entire class an extra 5 minutes of time on a test AFTER she had lectured for over an hour, and everyone CHEATED by looking through their notes while she was lecturing. Several students went up to her before the tests were handed back out and asked her not to give them back since everyone had cheated, but she completely invalidated the students concerns, dismissing them by saying that the students who were looking at notes during the lecture were "conscious of the honor system" and wouldn't change anything. She even gave answers to the some of the exam questions while she was lecturing. Additionally, Nicole Clay was extremely offensive and mean in class. During the interesting facts introduction, someone gave their home town as their fact, to which she responded, "that's the most interesting thing about you?" She laughed in people's faces when they got answers wrong to questions. Once only one person raised their hand to a certain multiple choice answer, and she scoffed "You really thought that was the answer? Good for you! It's wrong!" She also once was looking for someone to answer a question, saying "Let's call on [name] because he's annoying." Nicole Clay also made several homophobic comments during pride month. When referring to transketolase, she said that you needed a ketose and an aldose, because you "can't have two girls together or two boys together." She also constantly medicalized race, by speculating that the differences in "Eskimos' metabolism" had a biochemical origin, and similarly with "American Indians." When talking about "Asian women, and I guess this applies to Africans too" and the practice of skin whitening using arsenic, her only comment was "Arsenic is the worst way to die! What a way to go!"
