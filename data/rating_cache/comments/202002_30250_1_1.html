I think that more thought could have also gone towards how the class time would be broken down (I found discussion section to be some of the more valuable class time, but it always felt like we had too much content to get through and were just skating the surface of most topics rather than truly engaging with the difficult questions that the readings presented). Also, feedback was not timely. I know that it's hard in a class with such a condensed timeline, and I really feel for the professor and TFs having to grade so many assignments quickly! But the students were held to a very high, unwavering standard as to the timeliness of our assignments, so I would have appreciated a similar standard of timeliness for grading. It was difficult to approach assignments having not gotten any feedback on our previous work. I think it's maybe worth considering that if there are too many assignments in too quick succession for the teaching team to turn around grading in a timely manner, maybe there were also just too many assignments in general.
