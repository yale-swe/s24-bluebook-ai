2018-Summer ENGL S247 01 Travel Writing

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

ID: SU100 CRN: 30125 Term: 201802

123Forward
 My writing significantly improved over the 4 weeks. Having that isolated, dedicated time to work on craft alongside such talented students and such an invested professor made improvement really inevitable. The daily writing assignments pushed us to continue churning out work even when we felt squeezed dry of ideas, a skill necessary for anybody pursuing a career in writing. But above all the environment was so unique and conducive to an immersive creative experience. Wouldn't change anything about it.
 I significantly improved my writing in the following ways: More adept at structuring essays (specifically, essay conclusions) Better at editing More efficient at writing (ie: can churn out high quality work faster) Improved critical reading abilities I have a better understanding of my strengths and weaknesses as a writer. I also feel more confident in my abilities. I have a much greater appreciation for all of the work that goes into travel writing and other nonfiction pieces. Most importantly, none of these improvements felt like 'grunt work.' They happened seamlessly/gradually over the course of the month.
 One of the greatest skills I developed was finding my own voice in my writing. While we read and emulated more authors than I have ever read in such a short period of time, the key focus of the class was discovering what worked for me and the writing styles that flowed naturally to show my personality in my essays. I not only honed in on grammar rules, I also learned the leeway authors can have in the creative and travel writing genres. I truly learned to write under pressure and produce quality work under very strict time constraints; work that still held personal meaning and a connection to bigger ideas instead of solely focusing on my own experiences. I discovered for the first time that when writing about place, or any personal narrative, telling a specific story conveys a greater message than generalizing an idea.
 Through this course, I developed holistically as a writer. I learned to center my thoughts, build structure into my creative process and developed productive ways to edit others' work as well as productive ways to respond to criticism. I also developed my technical attention to writing. Together in class we'd analyze essays more deeply than I've experienced with other courses as well as more comfortably. I never felt that we were bullshitting through our two hours and found myself itching to get to work writing by the end of each of our discussions: eager to try my hand at the tools we'd just discovered how to use. I think examples are very important in learning how to write. Mimicking and then growing from that experience into your own voice is very important and I found our course material and discussion very very helpful in whittling down exemplar essays to their core of what makes them so powerful. Discovering what makes good writing good is oftentimes a mystery and I feel this course taught me reliable techniques for identifying the power of a piece so that the technique can be co-opted for my own work.
 Thinking about writing consciously. Describing with originality and intent. Making my writing do more than one job. Concluding with subtlety and nuance.
 My writing skills developed immensely, and I learned how to edit and read with a critical eye.
 I have never written so much in my life, never received such clear and dedicated instruction and feedback on my writing, never grown so much as a person, a student, and a writer. This course requires stellar time management and internal motivation. Most importantly, I feel like I took away from this summer a clear direction of how I want to proceed with the rest of my Yale career.
 I learned how to write on an extreme deadline, forcing me to do away with my perfectionism and embrace raw ideas and run with them. I became much more comfortable experimenting with my writing, as this course helped me see writing as a form of expression and a process of development rather than output for a grade. I also became a much better editor which has helped me look at my own writing through more constructive eyes.
 I don't know how I could possibly do justice to this course (Grand Canyon tactic?), but I'll try. The first is obvious: I learned how to write, and write fast, and write personal. It seems kind of silly to say that everyone who took this course grew dramatically as a writer (because how obvious is that?!) but I guess it merits mentioning. I learned so much about myselfabout how I perceive the world around me, about how I experience myself and the places in which I've existed. I'm really at a loss for words at this point because I know I'm not articulating how this course changes you, and because I feel like surely because of this course I should be able to, but it just shows how wildly changing it was. This is a class that will make you a better writer (by leaps and bounds), but it will also change how you perceive yourself and your relationships with others and your world at home. This is a good thing.
 We analyzed texts and discussed ways to integrate these techniques in our own writing. We also explored the politics of the travel writing genre and the responsibilities we have as both readers and writers.
