2021-Spring EP&E 215 01 ClassicsOfEthicsPolitics&Econ

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 21697 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 The strengths of this course are twofold, 1) Professor Kapustin is a really engaging professor. He is good at explaining really hard to read texts in easier terms, and even if you were really bored by the readings, he has an enthusiasm that gets you motivated and super engaged with the material, and 2) the readings are really, really interesting if you take the time to do them. I did not do all the readings, but when I did I found them incredibly intellectually stimulating and enjoyed them. The readings and discussions I had in this class are possibly the most interesting and stimulating that I have had at Yale. Personally, I felt the course had one main weakness, it doesn't have enough time. It can be quite difficult to leap from authors who have such different approaches so quickly, and because they are often hard to compare because of how radically different their approaches are, it doesn't always lend itself to comparative study either. Additionally, you will spend almost all your time in the class working towards understanding what the authors are writing, and why their ideas are robust, thus the focus is not evaluative (whether you believe or are convinced by their claims). So, while all the authors are interesting, it may not feel like a really cohesive set of ideas, and you will not have time to evaluate many of their arguments.
 Strengths - Professor Kapustins insights, the way he runs the seminar makes people want to participate and learn the material Weaknesses - none
 The course's biggest strength is in its breadth of content covered. Yet, I think lightening the reading load could be helpful to allow students to more deeply engage in each week's material.
 I would say that the biggest strength of this class is the material and Prof. Kapustin's discussions on it. It forces you to look at the reading in many different ways and really understand the text at its core.
 Strengths: Professor Kapustin is absolutely wonderful. I have never had a Prof. with so much genuine passion for philosophy in and of itself Weaknesses: I think the online format definitely took away from our discussions. Other than that, no obvious weaknesses in the course
