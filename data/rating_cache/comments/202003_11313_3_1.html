2020-Fall HSHM 321 01/HIST 244 01 Cultures of Western Medicine

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 11313/11314 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 open note 24hr exams were helpful (unsure if this is typical or an adjustment) In section the whiteboard function was used and sometimes we would collab on a google doc - that facilitated online learning well
 Time allotted for exams was helpful.
 This course really took advantage of the virtual format to keep us engaged. I appreciated the special courses, especially when Barbara gave the tour of the dissection theater in Bologna, Italy. I still learned a lot through the lectures and discussion sections even though they were virtual.
 Professor Warner tried really hard to make the most of the virtual setting and I am super appreciative of it. I think that the adapting of the midterm and final exams to be written essays was a great way to bridge the challenges of the semester.
 I think this class adapted to the COVID-19 pandemic pretty well, as the lectures and discussion sections happened as it normally would have. In fact, we talked about how COVID-19 is similar to many other historical events, which was interesting to consider!
 The professor held live lectures that were also recorded, which I appreciated. But, the professor rarely stopped to ask if there were any questions, and no students wrote anything in the Zoom chat because they had not been addressed in Week 1/2. Thus, there was no room for dialogue with the professor and no time to ask clarifying questions, which could have been a major asset to the course especially given the lack of interpersonal interaction during the pandemic.
 I thought Professor Warner did a great job making the class compatible with pandemic virtual reality that we are living in.
 Breakout rooms during discussion section were helpful for smaller discussions and in-depth conversations.
 Lectures and section were interactive, smooth, and clear.
 Because Professor Warner encouraged us to attend lecture if we were able to and asked us (though didn't require us) to keep cameras on, it actually made lecture feel more "real" and close to the in-person experience.
 Every live lecture for this course was posted on Canvas afterwards, which was very helpful when preparing for the midterm and final examinations. All of the readings for the course were posted on Canvas as well, and there was no need to purchase textbooks or other course materials.
 Professor Warner and his TAs were incredibly understanding of the pandemic. All professors should model their efforts.
 I think the course was geared well towards learning during the pandemic.
 They were really good about being accommodating with the 24 hr open notebook exams and section was a great place to synthesize learning and topics and connect to modern times.
 Since this was a live virtual lecture, the course went very smoothly despite COVID-19 restrictions!
 This course was generally well-adapted to COVID restrictions. Professor Warner's lectures were sometimes delayed by technological difficulties, but on the whole this was not a huge problem.
 The format of the class was great for virtual learning and virtual section was helpful as well.
