 This course is amazing and solidified my interest in economics. I think that this is the best course at Yale in terms of its organization and structure, which can be attributed to Dr. Hawkins.  - Lectures are run by one professor but all the important stuff (psets, midterm, final) is run by hawkins. therefore material you need to know and material that is interesting is hard to tell apart, you have to sift through all of the nonessential stuff. - Midterms and finals are structured so that you can guess the answers correctly, but students who study hard are very likely to confuse themselves on the exam. Therefore all of the grades are clustered in the center, with the Econ curve and luck essentially determining your final grade.

 Strengths: the lectures incorporated funny asides and stories that made me want to go to them live. This was an incredibly organized course (go Dr. Hawkins) and I felt like the overall pacing of material was perfect. The one-page lecture summaries and Ed discussion threads on what to study for exams were definitely my favorite parts. I wish I had those in every class. The course emphasized applications of earlier concepts with more recent ones at times, and although it was hard for me to do that at first, I've definitely gotten a better understanding of the macroeconomy. Also, I really liked the PSet structure! I liked the questions where we had to go to a site/article and then synthesize the lecture content with the new info. Another small plus was how I never had to assign questions to pages on Gradescope; I loved that. My discussion section TF was great and super patient. There were so many resources in this course that even when I was struggling, it was relieving to know that I could go to an office hours and have it figured out within a day or so. Even if I didn't go to an office hours, the TFs and Dr. Hawkins were extremely on top of the Ed discussion so I felt super supported 24/7. Weaknesses: It was really hard to keep up with notetaking during the lectures. I'm not sure if the slides were text-heavy, I was a slow writer, or if the speed was just a little too fast for me, but I often had to go back and fill in parts of the notes after lecture. Maybe this was a ploy to get me to review the content again though because it certainly reinforced my understanding.
