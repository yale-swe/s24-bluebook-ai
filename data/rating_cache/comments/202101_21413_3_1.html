Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 This was a great course to think about war in a totally different way, and the assignments were creative and low-stress. I'd recommend this course to someone coming from any department!
 I strongly recommend this course! It is very interesting. There is a lot of reading but the class is top-tier!
 Yes. This course is rare in the sense that it's fun while also very informative and engaging over the material. It's also uncommon for three professors of different areas within anthropology to co-teach a course, which is a great experience - especially if you're new to the anthropology department (like an underclassman) and want to get a feel for what sub-fields you might be interested in.
 Yes definitely if you want to learn about anthropology and archaeology and are at all interested in the history and practice of war.
 I think it is a really nice anthropology course (and So credit) for anyone who is: 1) generally interested in finding out more about anthropology, no previous experience necessary; 2) want to engage with a body of material from at least three different perspectives and ready to critically engage with them through discussions both written and oral; 3) interested in understanding warfare, genocide, peace-building (could be out of academic, but also completely valid if out of personal interest/background/experience); but it is not a history course and not a rigorous course on methodology in the field
 Yes, if they are interested in war.
 I want to be able to recommend this course, and I want to like it more than I did, but unfortunately, I had some issues that were hard to get past. The main one being that too much reading was assigned, leaving me less engaged with the material than I would've been had there been fewer, more targeted readings. At the beginning of the semester, this was not as big of an issue, but especially towards the end, 100+ pages could be assigned for one class. This was particularly frustrating as we never talked about all of it, but we were not told to prioritize some readings over others, and this left me not motivated to do any of the reading because the chances were slim that I'd do the "right" one for what we were talking about. The discussion posts did require that you at least do some of the readings, but I felt like I was largely skating by and not getting as much out of the class as I would've if less reading has been assigned because then I would've actually done it all. Instead, I was constantly fearful of speaking because I didn't want to say something that would expose me for not having done all the reading. Additionally, I didn't appreciate the comments on our essays when they were to the effect of "I wish you had talked more about xyz" because that's not really helpful feedback for a 2-page paper; I didn't have the space to talk about it more. This probably sounds more negative than I mean it to, but it is something I would like to see improved in the future/is something one should take into account when considering this course, making it hard for me to say whether or not I recommend it.
