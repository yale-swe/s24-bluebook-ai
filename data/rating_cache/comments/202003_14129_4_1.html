2020-Fall HIST 112J 01 Reconstruction

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409-YCWR CRN: 14129 Term: 202003

 Yes! This is a fantastic seminar that really lets you dive into Reconstruction and its origins, how it played out, its effects, etc. There are a lot of readings and to be honest I feel slightly behind on them in the later half of the semester, but they are all really interesting and you end up wanting to do the work. The three papers you write are really engaging (especially the argumentative paper on whether Reconstruction was radical and the research paper/final) and you get great feedback. Professor Shinn is really engaged with the class!
 Most definitely! James really cares about the content he teaches, and cares even more about students getting a good experience out of the class. He holds frequent office hours (more in weeks where papers are due) and always makes himself available outside of those hours as well. He splits up the course between lectures on Tuesday, where he contextualizes the readings for the week, and Thursday discussion of the week's readings.
 Absolutely
 YES! This course is great. Shinn does a great job of really explaining the material and helping you with your writing giving you clear and concise feedback to really improve your writing skills. He also does a great job of really breaking down the historical material at hand explaining why it is relevant in the grand scheme of things. I will say the readings may be a lot some weak, but it all came together in the end. Also, the discussions were great, I saw the different ways in which other students broke down the material which has definitely reshaped my overall understanding of the period. This is one of the most engaging seminars I've taken at yale.
 Yes. I am more interested in political science and philosophy, so I found this history seminar to be a change of pace -- a bit slower, sipping tea around the dinner table discussing our observations -- which had its strengths and weaknesses. The course material, though, is without a doubt fantastic and opened me to so many diverse primary sources. Shinn was very knowledgeable, and this class just touched on so many aspects of a time period that is so rarely covered in US history. Overall, while it was not the most invigorating or challenging, I think it was worth it and a nice exposure to what history seminars are like.
 This course was extraordinarily good. I thought the balance between lecture- and seminar-format was great. The lectures were always engaging and informative, pretty much all of the readings were interesting, and the writing assignments were genuinely enjoyable to work on. Even if you aren't 100% interested in studying history, this class is really worth taking. Shinn is all around incredible -- you can tell that he puts immense effort into choosing the readings, etc. Do not miss an opportunity to take one of his classes.
 Yes. Reconstruction fell through the cracks of my high school U.S. history curriculum, and after learning in some depth about it, I think it's a time period that really helps contextualize later history and current events. The discussions explored interesting themes, and the class was well-taught. The workload isn't anything crazy, and I definitely got something out of it.
 Yes, without a doubt. A valuable opportunity to learn more about a fascinating period most Americans know little about, while engaging with carefully selected primary sources and historical literature in a small seminar environment. Recommended leminar for those not yet fully accustomed to seminars or already enrolled in other seminars. Note that, like most history classes, and despite the lectures, this course heavily relies on the assigned readingsso be sure you can get them done (theres a fair amount, but its manageable and Professor Shinn is sensitive to your needs). If you're new to history classes, you may consider taking a non-seminar lecture first; on the other hand, this course introduces students to fundamental historical skills. If you are coming in new to intensive History courses (or if you're experienced) be sure to attend office hours frequently, as Professor Shinn is always happy to discuss material, essays, or just get to know you.
 I would absolutely recommend this class to anyone interested in American history. This class reveals a number of connections between America's past and present injustices and political conflicts.
