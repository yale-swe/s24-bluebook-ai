2020-Fall HUMS 005 01/NELC 005 01 Egyptian New Kingdom Empire

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 14019/14018 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Strengths of this course include the fantastic teaching, the generally engaging readings, the extra resources/podcasts, and the in class lectures. The only weaknesses would be the generally unhelpful breakout rooms and occasional irrelevant/drab readings (very rare).
 Strengths: The professor had the right mix of presentation time and asking questions in my opinion. She often asked questions to the students while going through the presentation slides, but the questions were relatively easy to answer, so I never felt like I had constantly rack my brain in the class or that I was tiring myself out from thinking too much (which I often feel like I do in English classes, which are more discussion-intensive). The professor is very knowledgeable and often added a bit onto the students' responses too, so we learned a lot in class and my experience attending lectures is very positive. There also isn't that much work in the class and we had a tiny break in the middle of class to stretch our legs or go to the bathroom so that was nice too! Overall, the professor is very excited to teach the class, and I could feel that energy so that made my class experience very enjoyable :). This was my favorite class first semester. Weaknesses: we didn't have a lot of timed assessments, so when the final rolled around, I felt a bit unprepared for how an exam in this class would feel like. However, the professor provided us with a study guide and tips for how to study for the final, so everything went fine :).
 strengths: interesting material, engaging discussions/lectures, instructor is very knowledgeable and accommodating to students' needs weaknesses: readings were sometimes hard to read and fully comprehend but gets better throughout the semester, review of material covered throughout the semester was limited improvements: more opportunities (ungraded quizzes and refreshers during class) to review material covered throughout the semester would make studying for the final easier
 Strengths include Prof. Moeller's lectures and enthusiasm for the subject, and the subject content itself. If you are interested in ancient Egyptian history and/or archeological investigations, this class will be super interesting! The online format was definitely a drawback for discussions, but overall these were also well facilitated and interesting. Course grading and assignment expectations were very clear throughout.
 Professor Moeller was lovely. She was always very accommodating and her lectures were engaging. I liked the discussions we had during class, especially when we were asked to look at things from the perspective of an archaeologist. The class was sometimes a little disorganized, as we were prone to get a little behind schedule in lectures.
 I really liked the break in class, it was nice to be able to stand up and stretch in the middle of class without seeming rude. It would've been nice to go to a museum in person, but that would be unreasonable for this year.
