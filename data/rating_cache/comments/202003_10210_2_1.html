2020-Fall ENGL 123 03 Intro to Creative Writing

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 Strengths: It's engaging and chill, not overwhelming amount of work like some other classes do, very reasonable work load. But really build that strong foundation of creative writing. Weaknesses: nothing.
 I enjoyed this course a lot. I think that more writing is better-- I liked the short writing exercises that we did in class the most because they really gave us the chance to hone our skills. I didn't like as much when we were just discussing various pieces of literature for a long time-- I didn't find that to be as engaging as the solo/group writing exercises.
 I cannot think of any notable weaknesses of this course. I thought the three-unit format worked really well and that the assignments were spaced out with enough time to allow everyone to think about what we wanted to write. I also liked the way workshops were structured because it was a low-pressure way to receive feedback from classmates.
 Wonderful course. Great feedback and source material for inspiration but also enough independence to explore my own abilities and achieve my writing aspirations.
 I truly don't think this class has any weaknesses. I enjoyed coming to class every day and found it thoroughly engaging every time. I got such helpful feedback on my work and felt there was a great balance in our assignments between being granted creative license but also having structure and guidance.
 strengths: not too time consuming, engaging, a safe and supportive outlet to explore your creativity in writing weaknesses: none are glaring out at me at the moment! this is a great course!
 Strengths - Professor Skillings could not provide for a warmer or more low-stakes environment, while at the same time expecting excellence and pulling it out of us. I was constantly blown away by the creativity she elicited from each person in the class, no matter the writing background we were coming from. I don't see why anyone wouldn't take this class. It is so purely about learning and improving -- whatever barrier some people feel at the title ("Creative" Writing) does not exist. We get to try out whatever we want, basically, whether that is imitating a specific writer, going according to her guiding prompts, etc., and get feedback on that draft, and then we can choose to continue with that draft or change it up for the graded assessment. The professor was always available to meet about our work and our writing aspirations in general. I felt that poetry was the best unit, which makes sense, because the professor is a poet, but I definitely gained a greater appreciation for writing and reading in fiction, poetry, and theater. Professor Skillings is so nice that I had to seek reassurance that my work was actually good when she would tell me it was; I thought she might not know how to say anything mean. But, once I learned how to read in-line comments, I saw that her niceness was always accompanied by really useful and practical suggestions for improvement. Additionally, the wide range of writing that she exposed us to was another sort of commentary on all of the many kinds of writing that people find "good" -- it seems like there is an audience for almost any kind of style (and she really helped us pursue our own styles!), and that it's just about getting a feel for what sounds good. Weaknesses - While this is a great starting point for anyone looking to dip their toes into creative writing and certainly anyone who is just looking for a spark to pursue personal projects, the breadth of the course means we give short-shrift to some of the material (this obviously makes sense for an introductory class, but I was hoping to have a bit more of a "method," rather than just exposure and a little bit of practice). For example, I didn't walk away knowing that I could write fiction well, but I definitely have the appreciation for it and the confidence to take another class in it - which is not necessarily a weakness, but just something I'd like to explain.
 Strengths: Being able to write creatively for a class is amazing, and the way the class is structured makes sure you get a chance to dip your feet into a lot of different kinds of writing. The works chosen to read are really interesting and varied, and there are three or four guest lectures, which are super informative and fun. Weaknesses: In some of the units, I wished there were a couple more (ungraded) smaller prompts given outside of the large assignments; this was done really well in the fiction unit, but I felt like there could have been more in the poetry and especially the drama unit.
 I think this course is very strong, I have no suggestions.
