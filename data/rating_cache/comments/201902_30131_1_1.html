2019-Summer SOCY S216 01 Social Movements

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30131 Term: 201902

Back123Forward
 The strengths of this course include the selection of different sources for each week's case studies, which combined with theoretical knowledge about movements in general, gave me a deeper understanding of certain aspects of each movement. The weekly short essays gave me a chance to better organize my thoughts and gain a deeper understanding of the course materials we've learned each week. Because of the large class size, this course could not be a seminar, but consequently, this gave me more opportunity to meet and discuss with people from diverse backgrounds and different experiences through group discussions. In addition, the talks from local activists have really opened my eyes to the issues people and activists face in their real-life and for me, made the topic of social movements (including issues such as police brutality, institutional discrimination, and lack of living wage) real. I'm really thankful for the opportunity to listen to and meet activists. The weaknesses of this course would be how lectures, although short and compact as they were, does not involve much participation from the students, and could sometimes make me drowsy. In addition, it was a bit difficult to get the many books to read for this course - I originally ordered from the Yale bookstore but turned to Borrow Direct and online pdfs when the books hadn't arrived in time. Nonetheless, the classes were enjoyable and successful in the task of introducing social movements to students and providing them with the tools to understand them.
 Strengths: there are more students in the course and so there were more insights in our group discussion. Also, the course combined teaching format like lecturing and playing videos with students' autonomous study, like group discussion and presentation, which is effective for learning. Weakness: the class hour is too long and sometimes made students feel sleepy. and the lecture can only provided some basic knowledge about the history, evolvement of certain movement, but a big picture of it. Sometimes I feel I can only grab some details or a vague figure of certain movement instead of having a deep understanding into its nature. So it seems students can learn the course themselves by reading and searching for some materials online.
 Strengths: (1) the movies and guests' lecture are good ways to broaden our horizons, and help us know what happens in real social movements. (2) the feedback of the assignments is really helpful in improving our argument and writing. Weaknesses: (1) the lecture part, sometimes without very detailed slides, is a little complicating and hard for me to catch up with. I think some more key words and pictures can be added to slides to help students understand the lectures better. (2) The course, including the textbook "Protest", seems to concentrate more on detailed specific facts and lack some broader and more abstract theoretical perspectives. For example, I think it will be better to give an introduction to some theories on social movements' role in the society, or some reflective ideas of what it means that the movements' identities are more fragmented than before.
 Strength: (1) clear narrative: compared with the other course, our teachers lecture and PowerPoints were both very clear, which was really friendly for international students. (2) ample participation: we had a lot of opportunities to discuss our own perspective and ask questions in our class, making sure that everyone have a change to talk and listen to others opinion. (3) comprehensive materials: our teacher chose a lot of movies and historical documentaries for us, providing a deeper insight of the events. Weaknesses: (1) Superficial discussion: sometimes our discussions were not so deep. For example, we were asked to search materials while discussing about One Job is Enough Campaign, instead of preparing the material in advance and discussing about their reliability or how to use them. Personally, I think the peer-revising of essays was the best one among all the groups works.
 One of the strengths is properly placing steps for us to go into the world of social movements, instead of simply and directly going to a high level. I also love the style of it which Roger comprehended lecture, group activity, and guest presentation into a whole system, making the class not fixed to a certain mode. To me, personally, I went through struggle reading one book per week, so difficult to accomplish. So I probably suggest to leave selected text instead of complete books for us to read. In addition, in the lecture section, since PowerPoint was always in a small number and hardly any words, I felt hard to understand and easily distracted for a few times. (Maybe because I am a foreign student)
 Although the number of students in the class is higher than I expected, the teacher really did a good job in allowing the student to discuss among themselves and set good guidelines to lead the discussions, ensuring that the discussions are efficient and productive. However, I think it will be even more productive if the amount of time spent on student discussions are longer since some parts of the timeline of the movements are not necessarily important to go through in class. Students can easily go through them by themselves and the information are easily accessible online. However, I do think that some parts that are main subjects of discussions need to be stressed in class.
 The teaching style is great.
 I thought this course could have been improved if the seating arrangement had been modified to be a U-shape so that all the students could see each other. The strengths of this course included its structure/organization and its professor-TA combo.
 maybe could invite more social movements participants (although current is great).
 One of the most obvious strengths is the content was abundant. It covered almost all the important and typical social movements in the American history from civil rights movement to LGBTQ movements. The content brought me to think of democracy, freedom and social movement itself to a whole new level. I used to believe social movements were just like some peaceful marches, but it turned out to be a hard progress towards the minority's deserved but long lost rights. One weakness maybe that the class only studied the movements in America. There were many international students in the class, it may be a better idea to study some cases in Europe and Asia. Besides, just because the class tried to cover all the important movements, it seldom went deep into any movement. Thus the sociology course felt a little like history class to me for it just introduced the background, reasons and outcomes of the movements.
