2020-Summer ENGL S256 01 Writing about Family

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

ID: SU109 CRN: 30061 Term: 202002

Back123
 I would highly recommend, even if that student is not experienced or as familiar with creative writing or creative non fiction, as there is something they can learn and benefit from in how they can use their own studies and experiences to channel and create a unique and impressive piece (you certainly won't feel alone!).
 Absolutely. There is an overwhelming sense of community, bonding, and care that is developed within this course, all under the guidance of a very empathetic, thoughtful instructor.
 Absolutely. Everyone should take at least one creative writing course in their life and this class is it. It was so emotionally validating to share our family experiences and writing in this class and receiving amazing feedback to even further our writing abilities.
 This is the best academic experience, perhaps one of the best overall experiences, I have ever had. I implore you to take this course.
 YES! It is a safe space, which is very important for the subject matter of the course. I felt comfortable sharing my own personal experiences and felt connected to many of the writers (some of which came to speak with us!) on the syllabus. I also felt a deep sense of connection to my classmates, who were comforting and kind.
 "Writing about Family" should be a required course at Yale! No other course has provided me with the opportunity to develop not only my writing craft but also such a profound sense of empathy. I believe that empathy is the most important quality we can have as humans, and the world would be a better place if everyone in it took this class. Understanding our families is central to understanding ourselves; even if one does not intend to pursue creative nonfiction writing beyond this class, this style of writing is has tremendous benefits, including skillful self-reflection, the cultivation of empathy, and an awareness of social issues, that transfer to many other areas. I was not considering being a writer before this class, even though writing is something that I have always enjoyed (albeit less so in other settings other than this class). Now I feel that writing is something I have to do for myself and others. I feel privileged to have been in community with such talented, kind, and brilliant writers through this class. While I do not believe that the community Dr. Hopper fostered through this class can be replicated, I will certainly attempt to recreate the magic of this course in the future.
 Short answer, yes. If you are looking for a humanities credit to take during YSS, this is a good class to take. The workload is not demanding, the professor is understanding, and you will probably learn something about yourself through taking this class, whether it's about your family, how you feel about writing, or how you feel about reading other people's writing about their families. It was a not-too-exhausting class to taking during this pandemic. Longer answer: I would only take this class if you have a goal that needs to be met. Do you want a humanities credit? Do you want something to kick start you into writing for yourself? (again or for the first time?) Do you have extreme writing anxiety and want a constructive and support space to practice writing (even if creative non-fiction is the type of writing you want to do, knowing that the writing process, including feedback, does not have to be a harshly judgmental process can be uplifting to experience and build confidence)? Because if I didn't have a goal/reason to take this class, I can honestly say that I wouldn't really have liked it/bordering on hating it lol. I wanted a humanities credit and I was intrigued by the writing assignments and readings, and being able to focus on those two things was sufficient motivation for me to continue to somewhat actively participate in the class. The biggest drawback in the class for me was mostly my other classmates. Which would be the only other thing that I would suggest taking into consideration: who else is in the class. So many cis, white women to talk to for 4 hours, twice a week, was a lot. I don't ever need that energy in my life again. Especially as someone who's family and life story is "complicated" I felt like I had to do a lot of explaining (culturally) for my writing to make sense to others.
 Yes. Especially during the pandemic, the course was not only engaging in terms of what I was learning, but became a real community. I loved it; would recommend to anyone else looking to take a writing course.
 100%!!! TAKE ITTTT. This is seriously a must-take course if you're looking to improve your writing and have meaningful conversations in an open and honest setting. The group taking the class is pretty self-selecting, which is what makes it great - you are going to take more out of this course if you're prepared to share things you would not normally share in a Yale class, but this shouldn't be scary at all. Dr Hopper is fantastic and I could seriously not recommend this class more if you have the chance to take it.
