2021-Spring AFAM 455 01/ER&M 438 01/EDST 340 01 Anti-Racist Curriculum

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 24979/24978/24980 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: Guest lecturers who are in-service teachers, teacher educators, youth/community/labor activists provided a critical, outside of Yale perspective of the teaching profession. More importantly, they painted a picture for students of the type of teaching and learning community that we might aspire to join or create for ourselves post-graduation. A community that supports anti-racist pedagogy both within and outside of the classroom, for all members of the classroom, students, teachers, parents and local people. Another strength was the practice of "3-questions" which I will elaborate on more in the virtual teaching section. Weakness: Frankly, none. This class was rigorous and dynamic and Professor HoSang was flexible enough to accommodate both.
 Strengths- Professor HoSang is great, The content is great, TAP projects were genuinely transformational (if you are remotely interested in teaching or going into the education field, this is a great opportunity to work with New Haven teachers), the class time was always packed with speakers who shared so so much wisdom with us. This course could not have exceeded my expectations more than it did. Weaknesses- I wish we had more time to sit in the things we were reading and hearing about from speakers/ HoSang. Obviously, this class meets once a week but more discussions would have been nice.
 I loved this course so much! I got to develop a lesson plan and actually teach it in a New Haven high school! Every single guest lecture was sensational and the teach ins for teachers were unforgettable. It was a lot of work but it was worth it ten times over.
 Strengths: bridging theory into practice, being real about the world, and talking to people doing real work.. I feel like in ethnic studies we can get really caught up in just the readings and envisioning different futures/possibilities without actually thinking about what conditions we are working in. We did theory in this class, definitely, but it was all in preparation for accompanying teachers in our Teaching Accompaniment Project by providing them with resources for their classroom based on their needs (example, for a social studies classroom my partner and i mainly looked for primary and secondary sources that highlighted Indigenous voices during the colonization period). Also we had a LOT of speakers, most of them teachers, some of them professors, some of them organizers. I think this class did an amazing job at grounding all of us and really bridging all of these academic texts to what the possibilities are in the classroom. I loved this class because of that, probably my favorite humanities course because it connected to the real world and to talking to people who are actually doing the work. I wish more professors were like Professor HoSang and integrated class and the outside, developed partnerships with outside organizations and communities, like HoSang did with this class and the ARTLC (anti racist teaching and learning collective). If you want this, please take this class. If you're an ERM or EDST major, please take this class. I think we all need a class that is not just about doing readings. workload is manageable, but i think this class requires more attention than other classes do. Weaknesses: there was a lot to keep track of, it was easy to forget assignments, so just watch out for that. if that doesnt change, make sure to have a good organization system. between the canvas posts, the readings, the spreadsheets, the work with New Haven teachers, and sporadic other canvas pages, and additional roles in the class like helping with the ethnic studies events planning it can be really easy to forget to do something or dedicate less time to something than something else.
 This class was packed with way more content than could be covered completely in just one semester, and I mean that in the best way. There was not a single moment wasted in this class because everything we learned was incredibly important and relevant. I found myself wishing we could spend more time on each week's topic because I felt like I needed more than just an hour and fifty minute session once a week to really fully understand and think about what we learned.
 Strengths - Prof HoSang is fantastic and works hard to adapt the class to students needs. Guest speakers made the class fantastic! Hopefully this will continue even after Covid. Getting to work with real teachers is pretty great as well Weaknesses - while working with teachers is fantastic, it also creates another layer of coordination to manage. So keep this in mind as you think about what workload is realistic for you!
 Strengths: all the guest speakers! the class community created! prof. hosang! the real world interactions working with teachers! Weaknesses: the tuesday night two hour slot kinda brutal tho... also, this could be a strength or weakness but all the students in the class shared very similar interests of er&m / teaching so there weren't necessarily a lot of diverse perspectives
 Definitely the highlight of the course was working with a local teacher to compile resources to supplement their teaching! I learned so much from the teacher I was paired with, and looking for resources and activities for him to implement into his classroom was both fun and challenging. One way the course could be improved is by having reading responses/discussion posts to be due at a consistent time each week.
