 Yes! Professor Miller is a good instructor who has reasonable expectations for his students. It's pretty clear that he knows that this class is aimed for students who are just trying to fulfill the science distributional requirement, but he still tries to give students an understanding of key technologies--to a conversational degree at least. That being said, there are probably easier classes for non-STEM students to fulfill their science requirement. This one will require you to do some work, especially on the quantitative side. I had some STEM background from high school and am in QR-heavy majors, which definitely helped. TLDR: If you took high school physics (especially APs) and were half decent at it, this shouldn't be too difficult of a class for you. If you are numbers-adverse, take a different science class (bio the world and us or something along those lines).
