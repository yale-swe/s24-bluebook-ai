2021-Spring HIST 277J 01 Memory&History inModern Europe

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

Â This course is meticulously structured. Each reading is assigned for very specific purposes and it was gratifying to see how all of the readings weaved together and interacted with one another by the end of the semester. The assignments are carefully thought out with a good mix of formal and creative assignments. Seminar discussions were engaging and energizing. At times I felt as though the course was overloaded with assignments such that it took away from the readings/seminar discussions/content of the course. It was rare that I could just read the readings and enjoy them and think critically about them without having to turn that reading into a writing assignment. During the first six weeks students had to choose two weeks in which to write a short paper on that week's readings. I usually wasn't able to finish the readings before Saturday or Sunday (our seminar met on Tuesdays) which gave me not much time to digest the readings, decide whether or not I wanted to write a paper on them, and then actually write the paper. Thus, each week it felt as if I was preparing to write a paper, even if I didn't end up writing one. This added an additional layer of stress to the reading and took away some of the enjoyment from the reading process. I wonder if the instructor might consider adjusting this format and specifically assigning deadlines for the two papers (maybe week 3 and 6). Alternatively, the instructor might combine the two short papers into a larger assignment and have one medium-length paper due on week 6. I also think the instructor might consider changing some of the assignment deadlines. Our final research paper was due on April 25th, a few weeks before the end of the semester and before reading period. It is difficult to write such an intellectually demanding paper while also doing other reading and attending class, which is why I enjoy writing/finishing these assignments during reading period when I don't have to attend class. I appreciate that the instructor wanted us to finish the assignment early so we wouldn't have to juggle it with other similar assignments during reading period, but I still would have appreciated finishing it up in a slower-paced environment so I could really slow down and invest more energy into it. I think one reason that the instructor decided to have the paper due so early was so that we wouldn't procrastinate and leave it for reading period. If this is the case, maybe a detailed rough draft could be due on April 25th (or at the equivalent time during the fall semester) and then the final wouldn't be due until after reading period. On the topic of the research paper, I wonder if the writing/researching process could be started even earlier in the semester. I find it very difficult to think of a good topic, find sources, and then write a good paper in a short amount of time during the middle/end of the term. I wonder if it would be better if students were told to choose their topics even in the first four weeks. This way they could have a lot more time to research and think. I understand the difficulties of this, though, because students perhaps might not know what they want to write about (or have the proper tools to consider it) so early in the semester.
Â This course is phenomenal. Although it is challenging (and I mean challenging), it is rewarding and interesting at all points. The readings themselves are fascinating and engaging, and although there are a lot of pages assigned each week, they are enjoyable ones. Secondly, the essays are structured to be thought provoking and challenging but not long. In this class, you don't write for the sake of writing an essay, but to prove a specific argument and use the allotted space to support your thesis. The methods of writing are challenging, but improvement is necessary. Also, I appreciated how engaging our discussions were. Professor Allen allows the class to flow but interjects ideas here and there to keep us on track.
Â Strengths: Professor Allen is incredible. She cares very deeply for her students and will devote tons of one-on-one time to help them develop ideas or talk through problem spots. Importantly for a seminar, she frames each discussion very well. You're never listening to her lecture for too long, and she also never fishes for an answer. The readings in the course are great. The load is never too overwhelming, and you're encouraged to skim. I don't have any real weaknesses. Maybe the moment proposal should be weighted a little less - it felt weird to spend a ton of time on the research paper and have that be worth the same. But Professor Allen is very fair with grading, and the huge participation portion balances it all out.
