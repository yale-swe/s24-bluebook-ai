2021-Spring MCDB 109 01 Immunity and Contagion

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409 CRN: 21602 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 Yes! If you want to better understand your body and the decisions you make for it, it's an excellent class. It is not an easy class if STEM is not your strong suit, but I would recommend taking it your first or second year as a science credits with actually beneficial knowledge. Especially during the pandemic, I had a much greater understanding of vaccines and herd immunity. Not to mention the history we went over, which made me realize that we are going through is nothing new, and that humanity has always recovered from pandemics. It was a great class and I definitely recommend it.
 Please take this course! Great for non-science majors and science majors alike.
 Yes -- especially fro non-science majors who need a science credit. Material isn't too challenging, but sometimes you may eel a little lost with all the terminology .
 This is a great science class for someone interested in biology but not a stem major/hasnt taken biology since high school. Professor Kavathas was hands down the sweetest and professor Ive had in a stem course at Yale. She is passionate about the material, wants her students to succeed, and takes the time to get to know students as best as she can. I definitely recommend taking a course with her, specifically this one if you would like to learn about the relevant topics of pandemics and interesting information about how the human immune system works (its lowkey really cool).
 I would! It feels very relevant and Professor Kavathas is so kind and caring. There is a good amount of Biology involved, however, so I would not recommend it if you have 0 experience with Biology. Further, there is a good amount of homework so be prepared to put in some effort throughout the semester!
 100% would recommend this class to all students both non-stem and stem!
 I would definitely recommend this course to anyone who does not know about the immune system and how it works. Even if you are a science major in another science, I would recommend this course. It helped me to understand how and why vaccines work, how the immune system coordinates its response, and what are causes/effects/solutions for a number of illnesses (e.g. viral/bacterial infection, autoimmune disorders, cancer).
 Absolutely! Professor Kavathas is delightful and this class genuinely taught me a lot about immunology (not just the science, but also the social impacts of certain diseases)
 Certainly! Though you will find a mix of students in this course (STEM and non-STEM alike), this is not an advanced-level course. That said, you can gain a rudimentary understanding of a variety of topics vital to developing biological literacyProfessor Kavathas ultimate stated goal of the course.
 Yes! Great for all majors - Gained a better understanding of my own body, the pandemic, diseases, etc. Looked at art and history related to immunity and epidemics Helped me unpack readings about science - helpful as a humanities major Teaching assistants were amazing - appreciated after class tutoring events
 Yes! Good to learn about the body and how it fights against pathogens/diseases, especially for non-science majors. You don't need that much science background if at all, and if you're engaged and doing the work, you'll be learning a lot about the different mechanisms that body has, ranging from T and B cells to macrophages to dendritic cells and the places they are located and the many different ways they interact with each other and detect pathogens (and sometimes self, which is cause for pathology) in order to create the right response. I didn't really know anything going in, but the course material and lectures really help!
 Yes. I personally do not like biology that much, but I still think it was an interesting class. The professor also kept the course engaging and fun. The assignments were not bad and there was a lot of opportunity to succeed since there were many different types of assignments (problems sets, essay, presentation, etc).
 Yes, I would recommend this class. I hadn't taken biology since my freshmen year of high school, but I still felt this class was accessible to me. At the sime time, I do feel that I learned a ton about the immune system and pathogens. It's even helped me be able to understand one of my friend's (a bio major) thesis research better. Professor Kavathas really cares about students and is always willing to answer questions. I liked a lot of the reading assignments as well.
