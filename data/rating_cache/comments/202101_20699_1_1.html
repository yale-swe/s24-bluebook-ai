Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 This class was full of fun facts and interesting theories. The lectures were, at times, dense and challenging, but the material covered was generally interesting and engaging. Professor Frank is a very good lecturer, and office hours/review sessions were always helpful. I believe this class could be improved by focusing more on theories of linguistics as opposed to specific phonetics/tableaus. At times, I felt we did not take the time to delve into these topics but were still expected to know the ins and outs of them. I also think this course could be improved if Professor Frank delivered all of the lectures.
 Sometimes I felt the course went a little too deep into the material for a course that should be introductory. The 4 homeworks were also somewhat long, which was not an issue for me but could clash with your schedule. The tests were fine, though.
 No particular weaknesses come to mind.
 The course was very very broad yet still very deep, which made it rather difficult at times.
 Strengths - very useful overlap between lectures and readings. Good preparation for exams. Weaknesses - sometimes homework instructions weren't clear.
 Strengths: - extremely well organized material - lectures made easy to understand and follow by instructors and TA's Weaknesses: - pacing of content could be improved - having a final exam during the exam period as well as a final project seemed a bit much in terms of workload
 Overall, the course is great. Less scientific articles or reading studies might be good, but finding material that synthesizes findings might not always be possible.
 Strengths - Professor Frank is very good at explaining material -- you can tell he's supposed to be a professor. The readings were long and dense and had lots of technical jargon, but you didn't even have to do the readings (I stopped after a few weeks of trying) because Professor Frank explained them so well in class. The PowerPoints were helpful for review, and the exams were pretty fair. Weaknesses - The assignments. They weren't on the syllabus, so we were given a week's heads up (not enough time for planning when all other classes has their assignments posted on the syllabus). They kind of just dropped out of thin air, and towards the middle/end of the semester, we'd have assignment after assignment with no breaks in between. It would have been better if they were more spread out. They were also SUPER hard to understand and EXTREMELY time consuming.
 The course was well-taught and there were many opportunities to ask questions in class and in office hours.  I feel that the content of the course is misrepresented. As a cognitive science major, I often found myself missing cognitive science related content. I think the teaching team spent a lot of time building a foundation in the general linguistics topics like phonetics and structure and grammar, but not a lot of time on bigger cognitive science topics like acquisition and nativism. I think the class would have been more enjoyable had it centered more typically cognitive science related topics.
 Strengths: really interesting concepts, and I liked the casual but fun nature of the final essay project as a blog post!
