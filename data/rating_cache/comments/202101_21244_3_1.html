Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 Yes, I would, especially for someone who is interested in feminist philosophy but doesn't know where to start.
 YES! Take it if you want to learn about feminist philosophy and want to get to know of the coolest and youngest professors at Yale. Definitely not the stuffy white philsophers you think of when you think of philosophy.
 I would definitely recommend this course to another student! I had never taken a philosophy course before I took Phil of Gender, but Robin and Moya made philosophy so approachable and engaging that I was able to actively participate in discussion despite my lack of philosophy experience. In fact, many students in the class had never taken philosophy before, so if that's you as well, don't hesitate to take this class. It will be so worth it and you'll learn so much! I think everyone should take feminist philosophy. It is so important to examine how gender and sex function in our society and to understand how systemic patriarchal oppression pervades all aspects of society. You will let go of so many misconceptions you had (and even ones you didn't think you had) and become a more conscious person. Definitely take this class with Robin if it is offered again!
 I would recommend this class to anyone wanting to learn about feminist philosophy
 YES! This has definitely been one of my favorite courses that I've taken. You learn so much and your perspective is both challenged and expanded. The readings are, for the most part, incredibly written (or purposefully aggravating) and often come in manageable chunks.
 I cannot recommend this course enough. While I especially recommend it to people starting out in the philosophy major, I would really encourage anyone who is remotely interested in the subject (or not!) to take this class. The readings and the subject matter were so engaging and made me rethink, almost every class, the conceptions I held about our world. The assignments are clear, manageable and open (which is honestly not a combination I have found in almost any other class). The discussion section I was in was well-facilitated, and I appreciated the fact that the class left space for student opinion while not being afraid to point out inaccuracies or discrepancies in contributions.

 I would. Especially if you are interesting in taking a philosophy course but are looking for something more relevant to present day discourse, this class is a great opening into the world of philosophy.
 Yes!
 Yes, absolutely. Take this course. You should take any course with Robin Dembroff. I came into this course being kind of confused about many terms and concepts regarding gender, patriarchy, feminism, etc. In some Yale courses, I feel like these concepts are kind of taken for granted. That is, had I taken a sociology of gender class (for example), I would expect to be told outright more or less that certain things are a certain way. In this course, you get a rigorous treatment of these concepts and go through the reasoning of how they get built up. Even though these topics are complicated and the material can be intellectually difficult to get through, you not only feel like you can ask hard questions, but you actively want to. Definitely one of the most useful and interesting courses I've taken at Yale.
