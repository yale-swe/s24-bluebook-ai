2020-Fall MATH 115 01 CalcOfFunctionsOfOneVariableII

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 11689 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 I think that the online lectures went so well. And I love how the notes are posted up for students who can't make it or need a refresher
 notes being done on electronic tablet of sort, breakout rooms, office hours were good but jamboard is annoying.
 The lectures themselves were well done technologically. I never felt like Zoom was an issue. I wish that the lectures were recorded, though, as being able to go back and watch things through a second time would be great, as Zoom classes definitely don't allow the material to stick as well the first time. Reliance on breakout rooms was a great idea in concept, but I always got put in the same room with two students who refused to talk or turn their cameras on, so I often just found myself working alone.
 The frequent office hours were very helpful.
 The exams were proctored over Zoom, which was a strange setup. Having to keep your computer tilted down at your paper while you looked at the questions on the same screen was not super enjoyable, and having to keep your mic on but volume down meant that occasionally it felt like you had to check the questions many times. I know some of the other courses did timed psets for exams, and this course may have benefitted from that sort of re-imagining.
 Jamboards + Breakout rooms were good.
 I think uploading the live lectures would have eased the course somewhat. But overall it wasnt a big issue.
 The virtual lectures were somewhat engaging, and the breakout rooms definitely helped, though it is not an ideal method of learning. The tests via zoom actually worked out rather well, and the grade scope problem sets worked out.
 For the resources available, the course practices applied during this semester worked very well. I do not have any particular recommendations expect to make the feedback on the exams more explicit.
 Although most of the breakout rooms were productive, some were definitely awkward and silent, with people working independently. I do think that the use of Google's Jamboards was effective in having everyone participate on one platform.
 Explorations succeeded
 Strength: canvas modules, preset Desmos graphs with sliders, balance of class structure to maintain attention, mid class group practice problems, jam board for group sharing Weakness: because most of the class was freshman, I think many of them werent used to the different platforms let alone using them in a pandemic. I think a more comprehensive overview with suggestions at the beginning of the semester would be helpful and I realize some things were also said many times. Just going over things like: jam board link is the same add it to bookmarks, the notes are posted after class if you miss something, the lectures are here on canvas.
 More of the lectures could be recorded so students can be more flexible especially after we went home.
 I think that video shouldn't be required, but it should be highly encouraged. By the end of the semester, everyone had their cameras off and I was the only person with mine on. Eventually, I caved and I turned off my camera in class. After I started having my camera off, I found myself coming into class less prepared. As it was my first class in the morning on Mondays and Wednesdays, I would sometimes start the class in bed until I would have to turn my camera on to answer questions. Looking at a bunch of blank screens with names on them ruined my productivity and engagement during class. I know that I could have had my camera on, but it was so depressing being the only person with it on.
 I think that the online course option was a success especially considering certain circumstances. Although it was not the best for my learning style, it was helpful.
