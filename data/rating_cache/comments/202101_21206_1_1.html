2021-Spring ENGL 114 11 Magic, Shamanism, & Medicine

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 21206 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: The professor. Larissa Tsukamoto was an amazing person to learn from and she recognized when we needed breaks or extensions and was incredibly generous with them. She made each class period engaging and fascinating even if the material was neither of these things. She also gave us ample opportunities for extra credit which unfortunately is the first time I have had a professor do this in my past two years at Yale. I believe she did this intentionally, so that we could focus on learning the material rather than stress out about grades. Since she was incredibly accommodating and personable, I honestly can't think of any notable weaknesses.
 This course was phenomenal. Professor Tsukamoto did an excellent job of creating an environment where everyone could get engaged in interesting discussions. The space was positive and collaborative, and I particularly enjoyed our daily Writing Exercises at the beginning of class. There was also a great balance of readings and videos for homework. I also appreciated the emphasis that Professor Tsukamoto placed on self-care, and how she implemented two optional mental health days to allow students to focus on taking care of themselves. I honestly cannot think of improvements for this class; it is amazing.
 I think this class focused on how everyone could become a better writer, not on determining who the best writer was, which I really appreciated. I think any issues I had with the course were only due to the online nature, because Zoom is not the easiest discussion platform, which is obviously out of anyone's control.
 Strengths: Professor Tsukamoto is amazing, the course contents are SUPER interesting, and Professor Tsukamoto is really knowledgeable about different CAM modalities. Good balance of different types of readings and had really engaging discussions with other students. Weaknesses: that it wasn't in person, but that's because of COVID :(
 The strengths of this course were the unique material and topic that was covered because I learned something new about alternative medicine every day. I appreciated the complexities of the conversations held in class and the social engagement required. I believe that this course could be improved if we were able to more deeply engage with the types of CAM covered rather than a great overview of a few, but I am unsure how this depth would be achieved.
 Strengths: - Wide diverse set of topics but with a common focus - Variety of writing styles introduced - Relaxed learning environment which improves learning effectiveness - Clear, flexible deadlines - Discussion is very much encouraged, student-led presentations of readings Weaknesses: - Wish more interaction with students outside of class (some minor facilitation to potentially match students with each other would be nice, this is not something I would say if we were in non-COVID times, but because of COVID and everything being online, would have been nice, at the same time, I could have done more of this myself) - Sometimes not as sure what the takeaways from readings were (I know the instructor didn't want to impose her worldview which may have played a role in this, but perhaps a summary of bullets would be nice in a document form after class)
 One strength of this course was that the discussions were really driven by students and allowed for lots of learning from eachother. Another strength was that when the teacher did involve herself in the discussion then it really helped to prod more ideas that led to more rich and complete discussions. I think the course could have been improved towards the end because at the end of the course when the discussions mainly involved watching videos, it definitely took away from time that could have gone to having fruitful conversations.
 The strengths of this class consisted of allowing extensions for assignments, especially during the online format, encouraging students to take care of their mental health before anything else, allowing students to guide the class discussions and content, teaching grammatical, tonal, and thematic concepts in relation to our individual writing assignments, as well as being open towards any requests or desires of the students throughout the semester. The only weakness was the online format.
