Â Strengths: Prof Yuichi is EXCELLENT! He explains things so well, even boring econometrics concepts can be made simple, easy to understand, and even interesting by him. He uses mental shortcuts and abbreviations to make things intuitive and second-nature for students. Although the concepts can seem challenging at first, everything made sense as the semester progressed and all the concepts fit in so nicely with one another! He also focuses on the conceptual aspect of things rather than the quantitative stuff. Thus, problem sets and exams ask more conceptual/qualitative questions that test your understanding, rather than mathematical questions that one will forget shortly after taking the course. Problem sets are quick to do and usually quite straightforward. Exams are also easy if you study and follow the lectures. Sufficient practices are given and solutions are uploaded in a timely manner. This course also teaches you programming with R and Stata without throwing you into the deep end. The required commands are given so you don't have to waste time searching. Rather, you learn and naturally remember them over time. The only weakness would be the curve. Everyone gets perfect scores (mid-90s and above) on all exams and problem sets, which is so stressful. And the final is 50%, so one mistake can really affect your entire grade, even a simple minor error by accident. I really hope the professor looks into ways of addressing this so that the curve is not strictly imposed, especially when everyone is getting such high grades. If he wants to impose a curve, exams should be harder so that students can be differentiated. If not, students who get low grades aren't the ones that don't understand, but rather those that make accidental mistakes.
