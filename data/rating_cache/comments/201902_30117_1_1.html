2019-Summer GLBL S287 01/PLSC S126 01 Balance of Power

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30117/30116 Term: 201902

Back123Forward
 This was a fantastic course. There was less discussion than perhaps might be expected in a seminar, but that was certainly not a bad thing.
 This class did a really good job applying some basic principles to actual events. However, it spends a considerable amount of time on explaining World War I and WWII. I feel the most influential period that shapes current situation is post Cold War era so I would really like to spend more time on discussing how current international affairs can be analyzed based on the basic principles.
 strengths is that it is very informative and you learn. lot in a short period of time weakness is that sometimes the readings were not mentioned in class
 Strengths: Firstly, the professor uses the method of lecture+seminar. The lectures could help us gain a general idea of the topic and the seminar could let us apply what we have learned and share ideas from different aspects. Secondly, the professor and Tutor are really glad to help the students. We could reserve office hour to have a deeper discussion and thinking about the issues. And the feedback from the professor and the tutor is always specific and helpful. Thirdly, the assignments are great. The request of the professor cultivates the capability of critical thinking which would help us in future research and study.
 I thought the course was very strong and I learned a lot from it, even in the condensed summer form. A bit more structure around the first paper would be helpful for students who are new to political science.
 Most of the course is taught from a western perspective and it could be fruitful to look at how the balance of power impacts and influences non western states
 Great lectures, but the questions asked and discussions had were often centered on very simple facts and other things that could be answered by a quick Google search. I would have appreciated discussions focused a bit more on the readings assigned. The professor is very engaging.
 This course was amazing! Professor Monteiro is an amazing lecturer and is very knowledgeable about the topics covered. The lectures were interesting and engaging, and both him and our TF Carlos were always available outside of class to ask questions.
 My only regret is that I wish we had more time so that we could go more in depth with each week's topics, but overall the course was incredibly well balanced and covered fairly far ground in the time we did have. Course discussions were always engaging, in addition to the excellent selection of readings that made you think critically.
 Strengths of the course include the very engaging/ inspiring professor, who had tremendous knowledge about the course topic and was always willing to engage deeply with students' questions. Weaknesses of the course include that there wasn't enough emphasis on the assigned readings in terms of classroom discussion (we wrote essays about them, but only briefly discussed them in class). The course could have been improved by greater synthesis of the assigned readings with the lecture material. Another way to facilitate improvement would to place less emphasis on past "balance of power" examples and more emphasis on modern ones (not just the US/ China, but also perhaps in the Middle East, in Europe, in Latin America); much of the course was focused on the past rather than the present.
