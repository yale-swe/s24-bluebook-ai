2020-Fall FILM 150 01 Introduction to Film Studies

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 10316 Term: 202003

 Online format resulted in a compounding of work- the recorded lectures on top of required films, on top of required readings, on top of live lecture was too much
 Professor MacKay is a clear lecturer, but at times he is not very engaging. The subject matter is interesting, though, so it balances out. Additionally, the online format of watching the movies made it harder for people to focus on the film at hand, but that isn't anyone's fault really.
 No weaknesses
 The lectures were extremely clear, and the slides were helpful.
 This course goes very deep into film theory (at least from my perspective as someone who has never studied film before), and it never feels boring.
 Strengths: Professor MacKay is an awesome lecturer with an incredible wealth of of insights and knowledge. I learned so much from his analysis of the films, as well as his thoughts on film genre and history. Honestly, I would recommend any class with him. I think another strength of the class is that we watched so many cool films. The syllabus gives such a nice variety of popular movies you may have heard of (Citizen Kane, a Hitchcock movie, a Star Trek movie) and less conventional movies. One weakness of the class is that it's a lot of work to keep up with. 2 weekly lectures on Zoom, one additional recorded lecture on Panopto, watching the films themselves, readings, canvas discussion board, and section. I think it's well worth it though!!!!
 Prof. MacKay and the TFs were great and made the lectures and section incredibly interesting. The written assignments and readings for the class were a lot drier and hard to get through, but they're manageable.
 strength: a thorough overview of the film history; great choice of films and reading material weakness: wish the class could be more engaging. wish more guidance were provided in terms of midterm paper and projects.
 Strengths: Professor MacKay is very kind and passionate about the subject matter. To me, the lectures were actually a nice and relaxing break from constant work that allowed me to always learn something interesting on Mondays and Wednesdays. Weaknesses: None
 Strengths: Professor Mackay is great along with the TA's that are selected. Weaknesses: Four papers was a bit too much for this course but they were all fun to write.
 I really enjoyed all texts and films I engaged with in the course. One suggestion would be to explore more on critical race theory and diversity in the industry, as it seems in the news that there is a new wave or approach to diversity in film production (thinking about ARRAY).
 Well-structured course, with clear deadlines and a fun final project. Professor MacKay explains topics clearly and concisely, and offers additional learning through smaller, pre-recorded lectures on a given topic for that week. No weaknesses really, it's a great course.
 I thought that a strength would be to allow for movie recommendations from the class, as I feel many kids in the class would give recommendations of movies that were similar to the content we were learning, and by taking their suggestions we could have an even broader selection of films to learn from.
 strengths: nice and thoughtful lectures weaknesses: panopto lectures, 2 part midterm and 2-3 part final. double features could've been trimmed down in light of pandemic......
 I did end up making my own copy of the syllabus including times for how long the films would last -- a minor thing, but I think including durations/page counts on the syllabus would help students' time management
 Runs through the basics very well. Was always going to be tough in terms of watching the movies required for class -- just not as fun virtually as it would have been as group screenings on campus.

 This course does a good job of having students consider films not just as forms of entertainment, but also as art forms shaped by distinct voices. It caused me to begin to see films as messages conveyed within a certain context, whether the filmmaker is responding to world events or simply reflecting on their own inner thoughts.
