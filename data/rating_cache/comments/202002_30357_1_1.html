2020-Summer ARBC S130 02 Intermediate Mod Stnd Arabic l

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30357 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 The condensed summer time-frame forced me to spend a lot of time studying daily, which really helped me learned the language. As expected, I have progressed a good deal more quickly than in the school year. The unavoidable Zoom component, however, is less optimal. It's harder to read facial expressions and conduct an effective class with so many people, and students (count me guilty sometimes) are more reticent to volunteer answers then in in-person class. I'm glad we spoke more Arabic in class than last year, but I think I would have been happier had we tried to move to an Arabic-only classroom (or close).
 It was sometimes hard to stay focused/ engaged during the two hour Zoom sessions. Maybe a slightly greater variety of activities (fewer drills from Al-Kitaab and more Kahoot and that sort of thing) or perhaps a 5-minute break in the middle of each session would be helpful.
 The online nature of the course worked well and I felt workload was pretty evenly distributed throughout the 5 weeks. I also liked that we got to do our own projects on Friday with the video analysis and recordings + it was nice to not have the burden of a two hour mandatory zoom every day of the week. The only weakness of the class was that I felt we sometimes dragged out lessons and they could have been shorter/we could have done more alternative activities since it followed the same patten every week by following the book.
 Strengths: Dr. Aziz is a great intstructor! Despite having to use Zoom, he implemented as many resources he could to the best of his ability to improve the quality of our learning. I also tremendously enjoyed listening to Tsunami by Ahmed Chawki. Weaknesses: Al-Kitaab is a very disorganized book. The vocab has no real structure, it's all over the place. Also, I wish we practiced more Shaami.
 Strengths: Lots of drills in the textbook, plenty of assignments to reinforce class work Weaknesses: Too much vocab in short period of time
 The workload was very intense, with many assignments per day, which is both a strength and a weakness. On one hand, the level of exposure helped us grow as Arabic readers, speakers, and writers. On the other hand, sometimes there was so much homework that I had no choice but to move through the assignments as quickly as I could, putting each word into a translator tool without pausing to remember the vocabulary, or making guesses on dictations instead of meticulously thinking through each exercise. The amount of homework also meant that we spent most of our shared class time reviewing the homework; the rest of the class time was usually spent in breakout rooms. I understand that a heavy workload is necessary, but it might be more helpful if paired with class instruction that engages students, like asking students to explain grammar concepts, having conversations that involve the vocabulary, or having students write on the blackboard (in this case, the virtual "whiteboard" offered on Zoom). That being said, the time spent in breakout rooms was very helpful and my classmates and I learned a lot by sharing our knowledge! And I also appreciate the many forms of technology used to make this class interesting: VoiceThread, FlipGrid, Kahoot, and more.

 The strength is that the work is outlined very well in the schedule so that you can stay on track and even get ahead. The weakness is that there was not a huge focus on the dialect in comparison to MSA.
 I think the class was well organized, and I really liked our use of multiple books. However, I thought the in-person class time was a bit long, and it often felt as if we were doing random drills instead of learning. I think class could've been shortened, and we could've focused more on actually learning the concepts in class, as opposed to just doing more homework-like drills. The other thing I would add is that there should definitely be more of an emphasis on speaking. Though my reading and writing levels have improved a ton, I can still barely carry a conversation because we never practiced.
 This course was challenging because it was so condensed. It was very heavy on the homework and at times it felt that this was at the expense of ensuring a strong understanding of what was being taught. In a regular semester, it might have been a better course as there would have been more time to review concepts and go over things. However since it was condensed, it felt rushed. The main strength for the course is that it follows one textbook exactly so you always know where you are up to and can keep track of what is being taught. The main weakness was the lack of instruction. The course employed a flipped classroom style which personally wasn't very engaging. Most of the in-person/online classes were spent going over textbook drills and homework type exercises, and there were only a few classes that involved explicit instruction.
