Â I absolutely and unequivocally recommend this class. The energy that Professor Lofton and Professor Peters bring to this course is really unparalleled, and you wont walk away seeing Mormonism (or the world, for that matter) in the same way. When one of my friends asked me why I liked Mormonism so much, I said that one reason was that it was clear that Professor Lofton and Professor Peters brought out the best in each other. I felt like their strengths as instructors complemented each other, leading to pedagogy that was organized yet adaptable, fast-moving yet allowing for moments of pondering in small groups, engaging in awe and admiration and yet totally willing to be irreverent. This class was also incredibly well-taught, even over Zoom. Having a break an hour into the session? Gamechanger. Consistently having the chance to discuss readings in small breakout rooms, each led by a professor or TF? Groundbreaking. In the first half, beginning most classes with a short lecture? Genuinely added something new and useful to the experience every time, while also varying our mode of learning. I was also impressed with the level of interaction with the professors: Professor Lofton and Professor Peters made sure to meet with every student one-on-one, and then we had additional meetings to talk about our object analysis outlines, and then another meeting to share progress and check in. On the last day of class, we all shared something that we would take with us from this class, and everyone had such beautiful and important takeaways, I genuinely started tearing up during that last twenty minutes. Mormonism cultivated a really collaborative class community, and I cant wait to hear about how it evolves in future terms!
