 I think there's great exposure to many interesting short stories and novels from famous authors. For the novels, it is more difficult to discuss them in their entirety in the lecture slides so we tended to only cover certain passages from our reading assignment.
 Strengths: Professor and TA are very kind, and extremely passionate about the subject material. Great reading list as well. Weaknesses: Im not sure how well the combined lecture-seminar format worked. I think the course would have functioned much better as either a lecture, or a limited enrollment seminar - 35 students is a bit too large for a discussion based course.
 For anyone who loves reading and literature, this is an amazing class that really taught me to appreciate literature. The class was a little big so it was sometimes hard to participate, but I loved being able to read the text out loud in class and see the beauty of the words. I also enjoyed the collaborative work, since it allowed me to talk to my classmates and see all of their wonderful creativity!
 The class size made it a bit difficult to fully express oneself because it was a bit intimidating in a class where skill level varied greatly. In addition, it often meant that it could sometimes be difficult to find a time to jump into the conversation. I loved the fact that Professor Adorno and Esteban (our TF) were always available to discuss any questions we had about the course or just to simply discuss the reading materials.
 We were helped to ease us in by structuring the course with lighter readings at the beginning. The escenficaciones are a lot of fun and divided well throughout the semester.
 The strengths of this course are the diverse course material and wonderful teaching of Dr. Adorno. Her style is wonderful and easy to follow along. This course can be improved with small group discussions in the form of break-out rooms or small group discussions if in person. This would give students more ability to analyze and engage with the texts together.
 I really enjoyed how Professor Adorno sent out PDFs of the texts/class notes so that we could engage in very focused readings. However, I felt the class was too big to provide all students enough time to really engage with the material verbally. It was less of a conversation and more of a lecture, which is not necessarily a weakness, just an element of the class and the demand.
 The professor was so engaged in the lectures and she really made sure to make the presentations in a way that could help us understand the literature better because they were sometimes difficult to understand. She was also open to all ideas.
 Strengths: Professor Adorno and Esteban are wonderful, caring instructors. Weaknesses: the course runs like a seminar, but there were 40+ students, so not everyone could participate each class.
 Srengths: - The professor's personality is amazing. - The work was quite low outside of class besides the reading (no tests, so that really took the stress off the shoulders and encouraged deeper engagement and reading - Great TA - She would always be helpful during her office hours if we had any concerns or questions. (Tbh, they were quite infrequent, once every two weeks, but she would be willing to have hold private meetings if one desired) Weaknesses: It was online, so ya (to be expected).  I think it was weird because of COVID, but there were very very few assignments for the entire semester, which was great. The reading list was excellent, but I think I would have enjoyed this class much more if the English works were read in English instead of translated into Spanish.

 One strength of the course is how in-depth the novels and stories are analyzed, as well as the context that is given before each class to not only explain the author's perspective and background, but also to help us better understand the setting and time period in which the story or novel takes place. One weakness is that since there were so many people in the course, it was sometimes hard to participate despite it being designed to be a seminar class. As such, I didn't get a chance to develop my own ideas about the stories we read as much as I would have liked.
 The strength of this course is in the syllabus. All the readings are wonderful, and the course ends in "Cien anos de soledad" which is absolutely breathtaking. It is now one of my favorite books ever. I also really liked the 'escenificaciones' that we had to put on. It was awesome to extend the readings in a creative way. I think the weakness of this class was in the format. Going to class and just re-reading the pages that we had already read for homework was often boring. It got better as time went on, but definitely at the beginning, I was disappointed with how much we actually got to comment on and discuss things. I think things got better by the time we got to Garcia Marquez.
