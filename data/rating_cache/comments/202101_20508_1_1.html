2021-Spring RUSS 260 01/LITR 202 01 Nabokov & World Literature TR

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

 A lot of reading! Not a good or bad thing, but people should definitely come ready
 This was a fantastic course. I think one thing I would change is that it felt like we didn't get enough time to talk about any one book in particular because there were so many different novels to read. I think I would've preferred to do the same books except maybe remove Ada or Ardor, and one or two others and then have more in depth discussions in class about the novels. Another great aspect of the class was that professor Bozovic took a lot of time to look closely at various parts of the reading and give beautiful analyses of the passages in the novels providing for a really rich understanding of the text. I think something that could complement this really well would be to turn over a passage like this to the readers and ask students to summarize it and or what we had read in the session before. Even this very simple technique of asking students to summarize what we had read and then look at various significant passages from the students perspectives really helps.
 This course had an amazing reading list and wonderful writing feedback!
 Weaknesses: None! Strengths: Bozovic is a wonderful professor. Her lectures are interesting, engaging, and rich with insight. She also makes it a point to meet with each student individually to talk about their writing. The TAs were both great and led engaging sections.
 Strengths: - Marijeta's availability and required meetings after papers - Slack
 Strengths: Professor Bozovic is awesome. She is brilliant, kind, and looking to help students improve. Her lectures were engaging and really cool. Weaknesses: The virtual aspect made the course somewhat less engaging, but that was inevitable. Other than that, really no weaknesses of the course!
 Absolutely fantastic course. Professor Bozovic is amazing. The lectures are short and engaging, regular breakout rooms kept them lively, and the reading was obviously amazing. One of the highlights was the feedback on papers. Everyone gets a meeting with her, and she gives you two or three key things to look at rather than a barrage of tiny comments. The grading was generous and there was a very kind recognition of when you had put in effort. You were encouraged to have crazy ideas and write about them: at least fail spectacularly.
 This is a really great and engaging course which both offers an in-depth view of a fascinating author and provides a perspective on that authors impact across the rest of literature.
 fantastic prof
 Strengths: incredibly personal attention and genuine care from both professor and TF; very clear syllabus and organization; not too much work despite the heavy reading load (but I got away with not reading all the books and adjusting reading to fit what I could handle); only the 2 papers, weekly writing responses (very chill), and final paper Weaknesses: zoom and easy to opt out of live & watch recordings, which made engaging with the novels a bit more difficult; would have gotten more out of it if I came in with some background information on modernism/post-modernism culture and literature; a lot of reading
 It can be easy to fall behind on readings because there's just so much to get through, but the excitement of the Prof. Bozovic and the TFs about all of the course materials will make you want to stay on top of things! The strength of this course is that it makes reading and discussions about literature fun, and I have the instructors to thank for that. There's a lot of flexibility around choosing what to write on, which makes the work pleasurable and engaging all around. I can't think of any weaknesses, this course only offers strengths. By the end of it, I felt much more confident in my analytical reading and writing.
 Professor Bozovic is brilliant and very, very encouraging. One of the major strengths in this course was her passion and knowledge. I would say this class has no weakness. Sometimes it felt like there wasnt enough close reading analysis in section, but this may have been a product of the students and not the course itself, which highly encouraged deep analysis of Nabokovs language.
