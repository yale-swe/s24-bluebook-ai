2020-Fall FILM 150 01 Introduction to Film Studies

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602-YCWR CRN: 10316 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 The online format of the film watching was a bit odd, which was my main issue with the class in regards to differences caused by the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the Professor MacKay and the TAs were very accommodating of various situations.
 This was a great class presented virtually, no comments
 Having the lectures recorded was extremely helpful, since it let me return to specific information after class. Additionally, when I had to miss class, I was able to catch up by watching the recordings. However, the amount of class content was excessive. In any given week, we would have to attend two lectures, watch additional pre-recorded lectures that could be up to 50 minutes, attend a 50 minute discussion section, watch and analyze up to two movies, and read from both the textbook and additional readings. There was not a single week in which I could keep up with this workload. This is part of a larger trend of professors assigning undue amounts of work during a pandemic, something which I do not understand the motivation behind, as this adjustment is hard enough.
 I wish that we had been able to watch films in a theater, on a projector rather than alone in our rooms. I felt that there was really something lost by not having screenings. When I was watching the films, I found it very helpful to pause, rewind, or take breaks, although we were discouraged from doing so. Because of the nature of Zoom classes and screen fatigue, I would recommend that viewing habits like taking breaks and pausing be less discouraged so that people feel better about doing what they need to for their mental health. It just feels incredibly draining to watch Battleship Potemkin and The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari alone in a dark room during a pandemic. However, the class itself translated very well into Zoom and our section was lively and helpful.
 I thought Zoom lectures were great. I think the panopto lectures were a bit long- and they made the work pile up.
 strength: lectures and sections were generally fun and easy to follow weakness: the class might feel a bit dry in virtual setting. For most of the time, I feel like I am watching & analyzing the films all by myself.
 Really enjoyed the ability to watch lectures posted after set lecture time. For me, the closed captions enhanced my ability to synthesize topics we went over.
 The extra Panopto lectures helped immensely in filling in some gaps that may have been created in lectures.
 The ability to watch the required films on our own time as opposed to during a mandatory screening day was a huge help.
 Panopto lectures were far too numerous. I don't think the students signed up to view extra hours of material every week.
 All of my other courses had groupmes -- I don't know if I just wasn't added to this one or if there wasn't one, but having one for the whole class would be helpful for students to communicate
 Definitely reduce the number of movies required to watch. Limited engagement with that.
 Slideshows were well done. I would have loved to have seen the professor as they gave the lecture - would have been more engaging but Im not sure if that is possible with zoom. As stated above, use of the media library and breakout rooms during lecture would do well in the course.
 Virtual all year. I thought the discussion sections went well through Zoom.
 I think that it would have ben nice to feel more unified and present in the class especially considering that we were so separated due to the pandemic. However there isn't much that can be done about that. I think the class did a good job of making the materials accessible for the students.
