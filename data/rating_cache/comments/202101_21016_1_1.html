 Strengths -Professor Ryan is an excellent lecturer, and his lecture slides are perfectly structured for both the lecture itself and for doing content review afterwards -The TAs are the best- hats off to them. The piazza page is very well administered with prompt and helpful responses to questions. -The TAs create invaluable section recap slides that are short summaries of that week's content -The lectures and psets do an excellent job connecting statistical concepts to the work being done by economists, and real-world issues -Psets are very well designed, helping to internalise the course material. Weaknesses -While the quizzes are a good way to recap content, I don't think they are as low-stakes as intended. I spent far, far more time working on psets than quizzes, but each pset was not weighted that much more heavily (5% of total grade per pset vs 3.33% per quiz). I recommend that the course either reduce the weightage of quizzes, or increase their number.

 The strengths of the course are the lecture slides - the definitions of the terms and the explanations of those were clear. Also, the discussion sections were very helpful to understand the material from the week. The weaknesses are that the examples in class, done through polls, were not explained very clearly and were hard to follow at times.
