2020-Summer ECON S159 01 Game Theory

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30041 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 The course content is inaccessible. The professor acts like were all game theorists even though we know basically nothing
 With 3 hour lectures, we covered so much material that it was hard to stay focused and not get overhwlemed. Also, the first half of the class seemed to cover a much more reasonable amount of imoformation compared to the second half. I liked that we had all the notes in one massive file that we could go back to.
 strength: the notes are really extensive. weakness: i personally felt like the level of engagement throughout the course was very low, perhaps in part due to the online setup. there is a really wide range when it comes to the level of conceptual sophistication and mathematical rigor of examples discussed in class and the problems encountered in the homework, to the point where it was really difficult to gauge whether the level of the course was appropriate for me in the beginning and whether i was meeting the expectation of the course as it progressed. the homework problems (graded for completion) were also quite different from the exam problems and felt frustrating at times when it was something way above my head. the curve of the course was generous but i dont feel like i have a good command of the material :(
 It was interesting but the notes over complicated the concepts
 The TAs were both very good at explaining concepts and remaining accessible to students. The professor seemed somewhat disorganized and juggling competing priorities. I also didn't think the format of lecturing from a long pdf was super helpful and the long pdf used as reference for the course was much less user friendly that sets of slides. The problem sets were also harder than the exams and hard to use as a starting point for engaging with the material, and the solutions were released like a day before the midterm and final, basically encouraging cramming.
 Strengths of the course were that there were lots of resources available to help (e.g. TAs), the lectures were recorded so we could rewatch them, and the professor shared lecture notes and practice midterms/finals, which were useful. The course could have been improved by having 3 slightly shorter sessions a week instead of 2 long ones as it was very hard to keep concentration the entire time, although the 15 minute breaks in the middle were helpful. The course also could have been a little bit more interactive at times, but the TA sessions were useful for this.
 The strengths of this course are the problem sets; they are challenging and include good exercises that prepared me for the midterm and final exam. Weaknesses: amount of time for office hours (not enough)

 The format of the course was not very conducive to learning in my opinion. The concept of a 3 hour lecture is already daunting, but forgivable considering it is a summer class. The lengthy, often unnecessary examples made the class difficult to focus on, especially when these examples contained knowledge outside of the class's scope, and when there haven't been more clear, simply examples beforehand. The saving grace of the course were the 2 TF's who had weekly office hours to help with homework and concepts. They often explained the material in an easier to understand manner than Prof Neeman.

The two TAs were amazing, but there should have been more TAs to better facilitate student learning.
