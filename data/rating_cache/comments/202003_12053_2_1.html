Â Strengths: Great professors. Dr. Samuels and Dr. Marcus are amazing people. They are so approachable and interesting, and do a great job of leading an engaging seminar. My peers were also really intelligent and thoughtful during class which added to the experience. The texts we read and the movies we watched were often phenomenal. The ones we read/watched that weren't were chosen intentionally. The professors did a great job of making sure we got a wide diversity of the literature and film in the Holocaust genre. The TAs were great and really really helpful for writing our papers. The class is two papers and a final exam (which is two 1200 word essays). This was nice because It felt like we were doing work but not an overwhelming amount. Weaknesses: TOOO many books and movies. The class is a book and a movie every week which is really hard to keep up with. Some of the books were really long and it was clear that by the end of the semester, few people were able to keep up (less people spoke during seminar discussions). Although I loved the class format for the most part, I would have appreciated some breaks in structure. Maybe an opportunity to talk to my peers in a breakout room.

Â The workload of this course was heavy. I felt that we hardly got to talk about the intricacies of some of the works and that it would have been nice to devote an entire week to some of the pieces we looked at. I also found that the expectation to do two papers and a final exam was a lot during this semester in particular in which we were dealing with a worsening pandemic, a national election, and being sent back to our homes at Thanksgiving.
