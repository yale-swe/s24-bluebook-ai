Â Unfortunately this was one course that despite amazing course content and lectures, did not provide a sufficient support given the ongoing COVID pandemic. Professor Snyder overloaded the reading amount. This is supposed to be an intro survey lecture course but I have never read more than I have for this class-- sometimes over 500 pages a week or 200 pages per lecture (we had two a week). And despite the material being interesting, the most difficult part was Professor Snyder's requirement that we take handwritten notes on all the material and submit them to our TFs by section. While the purpose of this note-taking exercise is acknowledged by students, we all suffered from physical and mental health due to this task. As Yale students we are meticulous notetakers, especially since this was relatively new material to everyone. It was stressful to submit so much work on time when the readings were by no means structured for introductory lecture. Had this course been a seminar, and all of us hoping to become Eastern European historians/specialists, this density of work would have been understandable. But the pandemic continues and it just unfortunately seemed that Professor Snyder increased the workload rather than adjust it to the pandemic at hand. Of all courses that ever needed a midterm feedback form, this was one that REALLY needed one so that students could communicate with Professor Snyder that this approach was not working; he himself was never very available besides minimal office hours that were at difficult times.
