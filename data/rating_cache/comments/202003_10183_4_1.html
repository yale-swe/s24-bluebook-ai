 Take it if you need the science credit. It's not a complete gut, but it's not super difficult either. The class is honestly kind of boring. But you'll be hard-pressed to find a science credit (for non-science majors) with actually interesting and engaging course content. Most difficult part of the class for me was sitting through discussion section each week, listening to some rote variation of "humans are bad for the environment and here's why!" or "we should all be vegans." Exams were fair and tested concepts covered in lecture. The non-exam assignment grading was honestly ridiculous at times, and grades on these assignments seemed to depend significantly on the whims and personality of your assigned TF. The professor clearly knows what she's talking about, her lectures were clear, and she was very nice and understanding throughout the semester. As science credits go, this isn't a bad one to take if you have few other options. I mean, what else is there to take? Plants and People?
