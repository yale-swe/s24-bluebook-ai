Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

 This class was very easy to follow even as someone with little experience in the Classics.
 Strengths: professor, weaknesses: section, organization
 A lot of regions/topics are covered throughout the semester, and the workload itself is fairly manageable. However, grading was pretty unclear. You have to write three concise response papers, and you are given a recommended structure/format, but then your TA grades off a rubric that you had no knowledge of (so even if you write in the concise format, you still get points taken off). Feedback is clear, as your TA will leave comments, but it is just frustrating that they use a rubric that they do not share with students beforehand, or make clear what it is that they are looking for. The same goes for the written response for the midterm exam--there isn't a rubric and you can get points taken off without knowing why.
 Some strengths include that the professor was very kind and that there were three one page response papers that teach you to write concisely. Course content was also extremely interesting. I might not have read so many Hippocratic treatises and ancient texts had it not been for this course. I particularly enjoyed this course since we ourselves are going through a pandemic - learning about plagues of the past helped contextualize our situation now. A weakness of the course was that feedback for response papers were delayed, so it was hard to see what I needed to improve in my writing.
 Strengths- interesting course material Weaknesses- lectures were sometimes confusing, powerpoints were often ineffective and did not portray a lot of info
 Strengths: The course really provided a deeper understanding of antiquity medicine practices and how they are relevant to medicine today. Prof. Lamont was a very enthusiastic, engaging lecturer, and the course was very enjoyable. Weakness: The feedback was very slow. 40% of our grade was made up of three different writing assignments. For the third/final writing assignment (a month after the second writing assignment), we had still not gotten back feedback for the second writing assignment. As such, there really wasn't much room to grow here and really do any better on the last writing assignment over the second one. This is a personal preference, but it was at times confusing where different topics fit relative to each other as the course was taught by topic and not chronologically. Once the course had been finished, it all became clearer, but it was at times confusing during the semester.
 The course content was very interesting, and I liked that the professor encouraged us to make connections to the present day. I found the one page response papers to be a bit stressful, as I felt the expectations were a bit unclear. However, the professor was extremely engaging in both her live lectures and her recordings, and I really enjoyed the class!
 Strengths: Really engaging professor who supported her students' learning. There were lots of opportunities to ask questions and learn from the professor and TAs. Weaknesses: I suppose it's my fault for not reading the syllabus more carefully, but I could have loved even a lecture on medicine in other regions of the world. There's a lot of emphasis on Hippocratic medicine too, and it would have been interesting to learn more about temple medicine in the Mediterranean world.
 A strength of this course is that everything within one unit related to each other. There was never a time when it felt like the readings were purposeless to the overall topic at hand. Office hours were extremely useful for talking about the readings and bonding with other students and the professor even though it was through zoom. The papers were short and sweet and we were given enough time to write them. The end of the year project was chill and fun to present.
 Strength: Professor Lamont is fantastic! Fascinating lecture, such a nice person and lecturer, and you really feel like you get to know her and that she cares about her students, even in a lecture. Interesting material, especially if you like Greek history or are a medicine person. Biggest strength is how organized her lectures are and how she really tells you what exactly you need to know from the readings by covering it all in lecture. Weakness: non. I couldn't recommend this class more
 Strengths: Professor Lamont and the content itself! I thought a lot of it was really interesting, and the professor was really passionate about the topic and was really accommodating this semester. Weaknesses: Virtual format made it harder to stay engaged.

 Very engaged professor
 One of the primary strengths of this class was that it was very well organized. A great deal of thought clearly went into arranging the order of the units and the readings. The readings for a given class might all touch on similar themes, for example, or might ask different questions about a given historical event. I also appreciate that the professor took time to explicitly go over the readings in class. Oftentimes, there were ideas or rhetorical strategies that I missed in my own readings of primary sources, and so it was helpful to have those points out. This added to the depth of my understanding of the sources. On a related note, I found that the lectures that made use of lecture slides, images, bullet points, etc, were more helpful for me than those lectures that did not make use of these elements. The images were engaging, and the bullet points helped me to take more structured notes.
 The material is fascinating, and the course is laid out so that students can draw connections between societies and cultures across geographical regions and across time. Because the lectures are highly correlative with the readings, it is always quite clear what students should be focused on and responsible for in terms of assessments, which is very helpful and straightforward. Professor Lamont's lectures were fantastic, but I do think that they would come to life even more in person, so I hope that future classes will have that opportunity.
 A strength is that all of the lectures were recorded. A weakness the course wasn't that intellectually stimulating (i went over all of the material in the semester for the midterm and final in a combined total of around 24 hours) and discussion sections were boring
 I think a major strength of the course was going through all the readings together as a class, allowing us to truly understand the material. Dr. Lamont was also very kind and accommodating of us, allowing us to really enjoy the course.
