However, a weakness for this class was the way in which the exams were set up. The expectations for what we could complete in limited time frames felt unreasonable at times and when many students voiced their concerns it felt as though their concerns disregarded. Especially with how hard this year has been, I just felt that our concerns were not acknowledged and the structures of the exams ended up creating more stress than necessary. I think we could have gone through more policies that have worked in regards to combatting these issues rather than just describing the problems. I think the exams were unnecessarily difficult, meaning it involved rote memorization of statistics and lecture slide facts that don't test your understanding, but rather your memorization abilities. Because the class final was scheduled to be on the last slot of exams, Professor Fallon wanted to change the format to be more accommodating. In sections, we voted on a new final exam format and I know that a lot if not the majority of students voted to have the exam be take home, but the format didn't change. Instead we just got a new exam slot, which ended up being fine. I just thought it was weird to have students vote on a new exam format, and then disregard that a little bit.

 Strengths: Professor Fallon is incredibly passionate about the material and does a great job at making the course engaging. She's also very accomodating and cares about her students' well-being. The paper assignments really striked the right balance between length and depth, and I found them rewarding to write. I do believe the exam can be restructured to involve more interpretation/writing.

I could connect different topics, and I could apply topics from one case study to others. I appreciated the discussion section activities that enhanced our understanding of certain concepts.
