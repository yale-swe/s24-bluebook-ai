 I think this is a good course for someone looking for a broad overview of sociological concepts as they relate to public health. I'm a STEM major and had never taken a sociology course, so I did feel like I learned some new concepts and it was nice to learn about a different perspective towards health. It also doesn't hurt that this class is very low workload (a few discussion board posts, 3 open-note quizzes, and 1 paper). For someone more familiar with these topics, though, I think the class would likely be redundant. Discussions seemed to be pretty one-sided, and there didn't seem to be much room for nuance or more rigorous treatment of the studies presented (there seemed to be a lot of steps skipped between presenting a paper/study and drawing a conclusion from it).
