2021-Spring MATH 225 02 Linear Algebra & Matrix Theory

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 21519 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: Proofs make the material more intuitive, and they helped expand my mathematical thinking. Weaknesses: I wish we could have spent more time with calculations applying the things that we proved. Also, lectures were sometimes confusing, and I found myself turning to the internet more often than not to explain a concept.
 Prof. Landesberg is an excellent lecturer; his explanations are clear and unambiguous, and he makes sure to thoroughly answer students' questions. The peer tutors were extremely helpful for problem sets (especially near the beginning of the semester when we were all adjusting to proof-writing). One weakness would be the TA sessions; the TA's explanations were unclear and not helpful for me personally, so I stopped attending mid-way through the semester. I would also like to have seen a bit of a greater emphasis on applications of linear algebra. Also, while the pace allowed for a thorough and rigorous curriculum, if we had moved through the earlier topics a bit more quickly, we might have been able to cover more of the material that Or was hoping to. Overall, though, this is a very solid introduction to proof-based math and to linear algebra.
 Strengths: learned how to write and approach proofs, Or is nice Weaknesses: the TA sessions that were optional covered material on the exams so it felt forced to be attending multiple zooms outside of the learning class period
 One strength is that you do learn a lot from this course if you put in the effort and it will be beneficial for you for future math/science classes. One weakness is that learning the material is extremely difficult and takes a lot of time. Since this was my first proof-based math class, I felt like I was thrown into a pool and had to figure out everything on the spot with little guidance. While I did eventually figure things out, it was a bit too late for the midterm.
 Strengths: The class was very flexible and it was clear that the professor wanted people in the class to succeed. The psets and the peer tutors were also good. Weaknesses: The lectures were sometimes a bit dry and grading was sometimes slow
 Strengths: PSETs were very helpful
 Strengths: professor seemed really engaged and cared about the material, first half/three quarters of the course was thorough and in-depth. Weaknesses: We ran out of time so the last bit of the course was not proved as rigorously as the first part. I found it to be harder to understand because it we went through the material quicker than at the beginning of the class. I think spending a little more time on the connection between the material taught in a more applications based lin alg class and the material we were taught in the first part of class would be appreciated. The last bit of class moved a bit fast and I think this could help slow it down. I think the stuff at the very beginning could be moved through a little quicker.
 Strengths: material is difficult but problem set grading is very generous, which is nice in that it allows you to collaborate with classmates and learn difficult concepts, but the course is still manageable because grading is so forgiving. Weaknesses: I wish there had been more visual intuition building and examples given for the concepts we learned. The final exam structure was quite miserable in that we had to memorize all the content from class (the whole exam was closed note), which to my understanding is not usually how proof-based math classes hold exams.
 This course was very comprehensive overall, and it was enlightening at times with respect to subjects I've learned about previously in math. All in all it was very interesting. At the same time, I sometimes felt unmotivated in what we were learning, as if I didn't really see any practical purpose to what we were doing. Perhaps a few more concrete examples or further discussion of applications would have been helpful.
 Strengths: The ULAs and TA were all very nice and helpful, and the overall material itself was interesting; Weakness: The instructor.
 I think the course was structured pretty well. The pace was very slow at first, which was extremely helpful for students new to proof writing, and picked up in the latter half of the semester as we became more comfortable with proof writing.
 It was what it needed to be. My only issue was that the midterm (32 hour take-home) and final (3.5 hour proctored closed book) were not in the same format, which created a lot of stress. Additionally, it was pretty easy to make it in this course by just doing the psets and learning the material there without paying much attention in lecture or TA sessions.
 I thought the psets were really well designed as the problems were fun to solve while also requiring me to fully understand that week's material. There is of course a tension present in this course given the large amount of possible material and the level of rigor of the from first principles approach. After the midterm, we shifted more towards covering more material at a lower level of rigor, which at the time felt like the course was rushing, but could have also been a planned progression.

The were very hard to complete on your own or even with classmates a lot of the time, making it absolutely necessary to show up to peer mentoring.
 I think the course content was very well taught and everything was highly connected throughout which made ideas much easier to understand and build upon. I think one thing that could have helped is more activity-style/interactive content during lectures.
