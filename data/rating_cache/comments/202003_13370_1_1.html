2020-Fall PLSC 186 01/GLBL 203 01 Globalization & Politics

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401 CRN: 13369/13370 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 I gained a basic understanding of multiple topics pertaining to globalization and its effects.
 I learned a lot from this course. It covers everything that economics touches in the global realm. I learned a lot of economic insights that I ended up using in one of my final papers for another class.
 I learned about how globalization affects trade patterns within a country and between countries. This course was very informative and I learned about how various models predict trade patterns as well as various socio-economic factors for trade.
 To analyze and discuss how preferences are aggregated, and how these preferences are reflected in governmental decisions and implementation of public policies.
 Learned how to read a data table
 I developed a knowledge of basic international political economy and game theory.
 I learned about modern theories of political economy. I learned about trade politics, international finance, aid and loan disbursement, and global climate change efforts. The course also covered comparative/absolute advantage, game theory (prisoner's dilemma), exchange rate and other elements of macroeconomics. I developed the ability to identify and compare economic and cultural theories on trade policy.
 This course instilled the framework of International Political Economy in its students. The class covers a variety of seminal papers/concepts in IPE and then moves to applications of these concepts (climate change, foreign aid, etc)
 This class taught me a lot about trade, international finances, cooperation games, and other incentives/strategies that motivate international behaviors and negotiations.
 Theoretical underpinnings of various political economy phenomena, understanding of trade-offs of globalization and its distributional consequences.
 I learned how to apply many different political science models to the real world through examples and pressing modern issues.
 I learned the basics of the IPE approach to understanding global and domestic issues. Additionally, I was introduced to a variety of (very relevant) topics, such as international trade, how trade affects situations "at home", monetary policy, migration, foreign investment, foreign aid, and international cooperation on climate change.
