2020-Fall ENAS 558 01/PHYS 353 01/BENG 353 01 Introduction to Biomechanics

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 10199/10129/10118 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 The strength of this course was having a final project instead of a final exam. Fewer exams, especially over the virtual format, made the course more about learning and applying the concepts instead of only trying to score well on an exam. The professor was very engaging and made the overall experience very positive.
 I think the course is strong. One large weakness was the availability of office hours however I understand that this particular semester there was a very low TA allowance so they did the best they could. I personally think this is a course that would really Benidorm from a weekly discussion section.
 Strengths of this course include the homework problems sets, which were organized in such a way as to emphasize significant models and well complemented the material covered in class. In addition, the highly interactive aspect of lectures (particularly with lectures being online due to the pandemic), encouraged all students to remain engaged and immersed in the new material. The real-world applications and medical scenarios covered in the class were also very helpful in improving students' comprehension of the material and its importance. After lecture, the professor would spend a few minutes reviewing over the material covered that day in a holistic way, which was IMMENSELY helpful in improving the student's understanding of the material. The lectures focused on review were also very beneficial in a similar way. One weakness was the scheduling of office hours for the professor and teaching fellow, which were both on the same day of the week, within less than two hours of each other. Due to this proximity in office hours, students who couldn't make it to one office hour, likely couldn't make it to the other either.
 Strengths: new course format with a final project rather than a final exam Weaknesses: compared to previous years, there was no MatLab involved at all (I thought we were going to engage more with the software); class could be structured a little better with more in-class problems (I felt like some of the psets were a lot of manipulation of equations that we didn't go in depth in class)
 I loved Prof. Murrell's energy throughout the course, and it felt like all the material was well-taught. However, some of the quiz questions were based on lectures we hadn't yet gone over, and the homework tended to be a little more quantitatively-based, despite us not always going over the math portion of biomechanics. In addition, I wish we had been able to use ImageJ more, since we used it only for I think one problem set and the final project.
 Strengths: Professor Murrell was very engaging and passionate during virtual lecture. He always does a review of the previous lecture at the start of every class, which is super super helpful. Additionally (hot take), I was a fan of the quizzes before class. They were decently easy and were helpful ways to keep up with the material while also getting extra points. This year, we had a final project, which was really enjoyable and a great way to research and learn the material. Weaknesses: Office hours were all on the same day - the day after the PSET was posted. It would have been helpful to have office hours at different times. Also, the last couple of PSETs are extremely math heavy and unnecessarily long.
 My favorite class days were when we learned about applications of biomechanics because it put it more into context for us biomedical engineering majors. The daily quizzes were a bit of a pain, but I understand their purpose. Engagement in class was high, but no one put on their video camera. Maybe because the class was taught at 9am, and we all looked sleepy.
 The only real weakness I found in this course it that the daily quiz scores were not released until long after the next quiz so it was hard to see how well/poorly I was doing. I think I could have done better overall if I knew how many mistakes I was making and would have been more motivated to make sure I understand everything from last class before the next class. The strength of this course is Professor Murrell-- he's a really great prof and I felt supported throughout the course.
 I thought overall the course was structured very well. Some of the homework assignments were a little tedious though and were hard to get through.
 The problem sets vary largely in difficulty. Some of the PSETs are extremely hard and take a long time to complete while others tend to be easier. I hope that problem sets for students in the future could be made easier. A strength of this class were the ties to application. I thought Professor Murrell did a great job of emphasizing how biomechanics can be applied to technologies we see today. I also enjoyed the last lecture, where some of our students presented the research they were doing. A weakness of this course is that there are really few office hours available. It would be helpful to place office hours available closer to the due date for PSETs instead of on the same day that the PSET is released. Additionally, it would be helpful if quiz scores could be released sooner so that we can study and learn from past mistakes before taking the next quiz.
 Strengths: engaging professor and great TA support Weaknesses: none really! I feel like the semester went well!
 There was only one TA for the course in addition to the professor. This made office hours crowded.
