Â WEAK: I'm sorry but I detest the grading system. We all went into the first of three quizzes being like, pfft we'll all do fine. Nope. The averages on the quizzes were 85, 70, and 80, and the midterm average was a 77. There were 7 of us and yet all of our grades were all over the place; at least 4 of us were consistently disappointed with our performances despite hours and hours and hours of studying. We tried to study the big picture, but sometimes the details matter and vice versa. I always personally felt confident turning in my quiz only to find out that I completely missed the point of the question. This comes down to a few things. 1) there are now guidelines on what to study. The "big picture" is meaningless. If our understanding of the most basic of the science is incomplete, our comparisons are obsolete, and that happened countless times. 2) the questions were excessively wordy and the exam was via share screen with a word document, and you could either handwrite or type. Not sure why anyone would handwrite, b/c shifting through windows was enough stress, especially when you had to email the quiz right after as Belperron began the lecture. 3) there was never enough time to finish anything and it always felt rushed. Looking at any of the quiz questions I was befuddled to the point where it was a struggle to pick a question to begin with b/c I was afraid my answer would be utter garbage (and lo and behold, oftentimes it was because evidently I didn't get the question). And then I consistently felt anxious, rushed, and then stupid when I got my score back, not knowing why some questions I did okay on and on other I COMPLETELY MISSED THE MARK. It boggles my mind. Teaching philosophy = if you regurgitate everything, how have you actually critically thought and learned anything? Fair enough. But when the averages are that low and we all feel horrible after the assessments, well.. that's where I draw the line. This is a class intended for non-STEM majors. IT IS NOT A GUT AT ALL and after 37 college classes, this class I spent the most time on I have ever in preparing for quizzes, exams, or presentations (it took 8 hours of independent research, combing through impossible to understand studies, not knowing if your understanding is right, or what she's looking for, and then grading seemed incredibly arbitrary). The required readings were a waste of $80 too, b/c not once did I ever open the physical book and never read any of the journal articles unless we had to write questions. AND FOR THE LOVE OF GOD please get rid of the "read this article and then write some questions about it" requirement. That is the single dumbest assignment I have ever had. One article was 2 pages long. Well, I don't have any questions. So what am I supposed to ask?? Well it can't be simple. It can't be googleable. It can't be too short. It can't be anything but absolutely MINDBLOWING unless you want to get a subpar grade on the stupidest assignment. It didn't encourage critical thinking, and if anything just made me try to come up with the haughtiest question possible in order to be delivered not the grade I wanted (if you're looking for a 10/10, she only gives those out every few years). The panel discussion also felt pointless. Only allowed 4mins max. to talk about your intervention even tho there's so much more to say, and then no one actually has any questions, so it became a lot of awkward silence. And even then, grading felt unnecessarily arbitrary. STRONG: Belperron is brilliant. She's down to earth, kind, considerate, willing to laugh, an awesome conversationalist, excellent lecturer. She literally does research on this stuff, so she definitely knows what she's talking about. The paper was really well designed, and I have to admit that it was the only fun assignment. She held a review session from 8-10:30pm before the midterm, answered qs in the morn, and then held office hours before the exam. Understanding. Content is super interesting.
