Â The main strength of the course in my opinion was the overall focus on getting skilled at making arguments and expressing ourselves in both written and spoken Japanese. I think another strength of the course is that it balanced a good mixture of both formal and casual Japanese practice. The course focused on teaching us both formal expression techniques (e.g. like when giving a presentation on a set of data) and casual expression techniques (e.g. going over specific contractions or particles that get dropped in casual speech). Another BIG strength of the course is the amount of detailed feedback opportunities you get. Nishimura is great about giving feedback quickly, and her comments show she really cares. Any of the weakness I can think of are primarily a result of the online format. I think there were times where we had too much time in the breakout rooms. I also think it would have been useful at times to spend a little more time after a breakout room session to go over the grammar we just practice once more (so having a format of grammar overview --> breakout room practice --> more extensive grammar overview. Or even just doing more grammar review together as a class where sensei could correct us right there on the spot. Of course, this would limit speaking practice, so it might not be ideal.
