2021-Spring MCDB 205 01 Cell Biology

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 21449 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: very organized teaching team! The amount of resources available was also great Weaknesses: THis class felt like it would never end. The weekly quizzes were relentless -- being able to drop 3 quiz grades was so helpful
 I was incredibly grateful that this course provided study guides/review sheets for each lecture which helped me review key topics. These guides also had review questions that were very useful in making sure I understood the concepts at hand. One thing that could be improved would be that sometimes for quizzes, I would lose a point on something very technical (such as not stating explicitly that ATP hydolysis was the source of energy even though the question may have already suggested that it was so).
 This class goes at a very fast pace and is not designed for students who may be dealing with other things or courses. There is not much accommodation for students and there is far too much material and too much detail you must pay attention, as they never specify what information is important for quizzes or tests.
 Lecturing was high quality. Sometimes quizzes were stressful. Having weekly quizzes were important to keep you on top of material. I do kind of wish there was maybe some sort of homework or some other assignment we could do
 I thought this class was extremely interesting and we learned a lot but I was overwhelmed at times. I wish we spent more time connecting the different topics and identifying which components of a complex system were actually important to memorize. Trying to synthesize all of the information for the final was very difficult and I would have found it almost impossible to do had the quizzes and exams been closed note.
 The strength is definitely the weekly quizzes. This is the only way to manageably test you on the incredible amount of content you are faced with in the course. Unfortunately, the grading for the quizzes was very unpredictable, and took a sharp downward turn later in the semester. On the last couple of quizzes, it got very challenging to determine what, exactly, the graders were looking for and how much detail to go into in the limited time available. Also, the final exam at the end of the course completely undid the awesome quiz format. It is completely impractical to be tested on all of the course content at once and it was ridiculous that we couldn't go back to revise our answers, despite that being a Yale final exam requirement.
 The course is fast paced, but it is clear the professors want to provide an engaging learning environment. They are very good about posting slides and their study guides in advance. The fact that lectures were recorded this semester was also incredibly helpful; I was able to rewatch several before the final exam. The weekly quizzes are also very fair, and I appreciated how they ensured I stayed engaged with the course. One of my best experiences in a STEM course as Yale. The final exam was well-written, and I appreciated how the professors made it challenging in spite of it being open note.
 The lecturers were all passionate and the course material was relatively interesting. Sometimes it was quite fast paced and a lot of reading was requires. The weekly quiz format was a great way to understand the material and familiarise oneself with the exam layout.
 It was extremely well organized and structured to facilitate learning. The material was very fascinating and engaging, with many applications in the real world.
 Strengths: Extremely comprehensive Weaknesses: Quick paced
 The grading for quizzes was slightly nitpicky, but they really helped when it comes to staying on top of the material. The teachers are amazing and the textbook is very straightforward and helpful.
 Great professors and TFs. The handouts were very helpful. Weekly quizzes were also helpful for keeping me on top of material. A weakness would be that the material was a bit uneven some weeks were harder and more packed than others. Reallocating time a bit would be helpful, even if this meant some class periods we went over two units while for others we went through less than one.
 This course is the best course I have ever taken at Yale. The organization is outstanding. You can really tell that the professors prioritized their students. Grades are entered extremely quick and professors put recording the lectures and uploading them immediately as a priority. There are fair opportunities to improve your grade. You learn so much about the cell and the emphasis on applying the knowledge as opposed to only memorization is very successful. TAKE THIS CLASS! Do not be dissuaded at all by how much the workload appears to be, this class will change your entire understanding of cell (and frankly, mol) biology. There are no weaknesses.
 Strengths: I liked the quiz format Weakness: the pace was hard at times but I understand why it was necessary with the amount of information we had to cover
 I believe that in person lectures will be better than online learning, but this lecture format still did well despite this weakness.
 This course was such a great foundation to biology. The course was very organized, and I learned a lot of the details I needed to in order to apply them in the future to research. We talked about experimental design, which is going to help in the application of the material. The professors and TFs are very receptive to questions, and they make sure you have all the resources you need to succeed in the course, such as the study guides and the many practice questions. The study guides enhance understanding and help organization, and the discussion sections help to understand the questions since the material is very dense. My only suggestion for improvement of the course is to maybe have psets in addition to only the quizzes and the final. Exams were stressful, although the questions were always interesting and enhanced learning.
 I think having the peer tutors record their review sessions for each quiz, or have it earlier in the week to accommodate other sections or scheduling conflicts would be nice.
