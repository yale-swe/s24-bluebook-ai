 Yes, 100% recommend! Before I talk about the class in particular I think it's important to point out that 231 won't be offered again (they're restructuring the intro classes) so here's my rambling attempt at explaining the changes to anyone curious: the old intensive classes (230 and 231) which each combined proof-based vector calculus (aka real analysis) and linear algebra are being split into linear algebra only (226) and real analysis only (256). Since the real analysis class is covering a stuff in a different order, 250 and 300/301 are being phased out in favor of 255/256 (single-variable analysis, 256 intensive version of 255) and 302 (multivariable analysis, manifolds and stuff) that is no longer a prerequisite for 305 (the way 301 was). Due to COVID (and perhaps also the impending change) our 230 in the fall was almost entirely analysis and 231 almost entirely linear algebra, so 230 from fall 2020 was basically a dry run for 256 and 231 this term was a dry run for 226. I hope this helps people compare course reviews while the transition is taking place. But anyway on to the class itself, Pat is AMAZING (deservedly given a teaching award this year!) and really supports everyone in the class. Linear algebra was fun, probably not my favorite course of the semester content-wise, but definitely the most fun class to go to because of how accessible and interesting Pat made the lectures. You know what you're getting into with the psets having done 230, lots of intellectually challenging proofs - the difference is with linear algebra it's more computational than theoretical (as it is with analysis) which I personally found to be more enjoyable. The workload is more or less the same as it is in 230, some people say higher and others (a majority I think) say a bit lower. But definitely use the opportunity Pat offers to really get to know your classmates, because the 231 community was really fantastic and supportive. Again you probably know all this stuff coming from 230 (or now 226 going into 256 or whatever), but it's a lot of work, intellectually challenging, but the experience is great and it's very possible to do well if you put in the time.
