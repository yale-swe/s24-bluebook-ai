Â Strengths--Fantastic Instructor (Hugo) who is a very interesting, intelligent and understanding human being! He makes the course rather interesting with the questions he raises and class organization he plans. He is not rigid with plans (which some might consider a weakness since syllabus was frequently changed)--this allowed us to engage in several tasks during one meeting session--with a 5 minute break! Another strength is that the class essentially meets JUST on Thursday, with Tuesday being a brief 30 min session with Hugo and two of your group members. Speaking of which, the Tuesday PSET feedbacks were very helpful in guiding us through our papers and our ideas--Hugo is very amazing with giving practical feedback. Finally, the fact that we were able to choose whatever topic we wanted for Paper 3 and Paper 4 really made the whole research/writing/editing process worthwhile since we all, of course, chose topics we were super interested in! Weakness--Thursday class times can be boring at times. The issue is not with Hugo, but sometimes the students. While it's supposed to be a discussion session, it's really not usual for most of the class to volunteer and raise a topic for discussion (or follow along a started discussion). Had we been able to do this, I think interesting debates/conversations would have transpired--an interesting experience. Second weakness is that we got very little feedback for Paper 4 (even though it was similar to Paper 3, which we received a tremendous deal of feedback for). Although this was because we had to submit P4 after Thanksgiving break and so had little time for feedback, it still meant that key mistakes in our paper did not get the chance to be corrected, hurting our grades somehow. Finally, I feel like Hugo did not give us much feedback regarding our writing style (word usage, emphasis, sentence structure). It's probably because he felt that we were all fine with that as Yalies, but I hope more specific comments regarding writing will be offered for future students.
