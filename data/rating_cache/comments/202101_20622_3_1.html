 Yes! Great course for learning various biochemistry topics
 Please only take this if you are an MB&B major. It's not worth taking, otherwise.
 This is a brutal class, and is too demanding in my opinion. If STEM courses can be divided into quantitative (physics, math) and qualitative (organic chemistry, biology, etc.) this was by far the hardest "qualitative" STEM course I have taken at Yale. I imagine that most, if not all, of the students taking this class are MBB majors, so they don't need to be persuaded to take the course. If you must take it, you ABSOLUTELY should not take it with a heavy schedule. Overall, the number of hours I have spent memorizing E. coli protein subunits and the method, year, and name of various experiments has somewhat dampened my enthusiasm for biochemistry. If you want a taste of this course, go try to make it through a page of Voet & Voet's Biochemistry and you will know what you're in for.
 MBB 301 provides a thorough understanding of cellular systems and includes many applications that go beyond textbook understandings, including a particularly interesting lecture on the specific viruses and genes involved in cancer. I was really satisfied with the content learned in this class, and I feel that much of the abstraction used in the introductory biology sequence was elucidated. I would recommend taking this course as an elective for the chemistry major as well. The choice not to use a curve proved to be really beneficial in fostering collaboration.
 Please take it. You will learn more than you ever wanted to.
 This course is definitely difficult; the amount of content in each lecture is probably the highest of any course I've taken. It is definitely much, much more demanding than the fall semester. I did learn a lot though, and I would overall recommend it.
 I would recommend this course to a student with true passion for biochemistry and the willingness to put in a LOT of time studying. This is the hardest class that I have encountered at Yale, yet also one of the most satisfying.
 Yes, I would definitely recommend MB&B 301 to students who are interested in nucleic acid biology. MB&B 301 is a requirement for MB&B but can be taken as an elective for various majors. It is an excellent course with a ton of information and very gratifying to complete.
 Yes, I would recommend this class. Although it is challenging, it is very worthwhile and interesting.
 Yes, but know that you are getting into quite a lot of work!! This class is much harder than the first semester (MBB 300)
 No. Do not take this course. The instructors rush through as much content as they possibly can in as little time as they possibly can, and then test you on it after only saying 5 words on the topic. The problem sets are unnecessarily difficult. The instructors claim that this course is designed to teach you to think like a scientist, but asking unnecessarily hard questions without once showing students the process of answering such a question, does not sound like teaching to me. Many test questions are simply unfair; they ask about a minute detail that was mentioned on a lecture slide that was rushed through and which the instructors never even spoke about. The instructors will often ask questions during lecture, but few if any students will have a response; the instructors fail to understand that, if no students can answer your questions, it is because they didn't understand anything the instructors said, because the instructors went through the content too quickly. This course felt like one of those stereotypical competitions where hypercompetitive high school nerds are presented a lot of information in a short time and then tested on it. This resulted in a hefty amount of stress and anxiety for students, poorly impacting their mental health. Given that the instructors rush through all of their content without actually teaching the students anything, and then ask impossibly difficult questions about the content that they never explained to us, which destroys students' mental health, it is impossible to come out of this course viewing the instructors as anything other than our enemies. The course can be improved if the instructors take notes on better teaching practices from Enrique De La Cruz, Matt Simon, Candie Paulsen, and Michael Koelle.
 Yes take this course but it will be very difficult.
 i highly recommend this course to MB&B majors and those who are not! It is challenging and you are asked to analyze a lot of primary experimental data rather than just being fed facts about how all the systems work, but it is worth it and so satisfying when you figure out a process from the data - it gives you a great understanding of how biology/biochemistry got to where it is today, and the perspective of the instructors, particularly Dr. Steitz, is so incredibly cool.
 Yes, if you're up for it! This class is probably the most time consuming class I've taken at Yale, just because of the amount of studying needed for the two midterms and the final, but it was SUCH A GOOD CLASS and I feel like I got so so much out of it. If you enjoyed MB&B 300, this class is like that but on steroids. I've learned so much biochemistry, but I've also become very comfortable with interpreting data and thinking about biochemistry experimentally. The professors really care about making the class a good experience, the course is really well organized, so it has probably been the best STEM class I've taken so far as a junior Chemistry major. I remember reading a review last year that said each lecture has enough material for an entire midterm, and that's definitely true. The first 7 lectures or so weren't too bad, but after that, each lecture probably took at least 3 hours to review, which is a lot of studying to put into a midterm and final. Overall, if you like biochemistry and are willing to put in the work, I HIGHLY recommend this class!!
 Yes, if you really want to learn biochemistry.
 Yes, I'd recommend it. While you won't remember every detail, I think it's important for students to be exposed to the fundamentals of nucleic acids and their processes in greater detail than you'll find in most other undergraduate courses. The class is also great for getting experience in analyzing biochemical data and applying basic facts to novel situations. Professor Steitz was wonderful, of course, but Schlieker and Bleichert were also very able instructors who I'm sure run the class well in the near-future. Lastly, the tests are difficult but the grading is generous, if you're worried about that.

 I would definitely recommend this course to anyone who is interested in biochemistry as well as learning the type of thinking necessary to interpret data. The class covered a lot of material, but in the end, it was rewarding to see how much we had learned throughout the semester.
 I would recommend this course only to students who need it as a requirement. As an MB&B major, I found this course rewarding despite the difficulty, and it really delved so much deeper than any previous biochemistry course I have taken. That being said, unless you're already familiar with the material, it takes an insane amount of work outside of class to understand the content, and the tests are pretty challenging.
 Yes! This was by far my favorite class this semester. You learn so much from some of the best instructors in the MBB department.
 It's required for most people. Otherwise I think there are classes with better resources for students, although the professors clearly do try hard and I appreciate that.
 I would if you are interested in Biochemistry or any cellular functions
 Most of the students are MB&B majors, though the class also counts as an Elective for the Chemistry major. For anyone considering the course as an elective, I would consider a few things 1) are you really interested in molecular biology as this is not really a biochemistry course (it's mostly about transcription and translation, with few other broader topics; but the focus is not on enzyme catalysis rate or mechanism, at least not nearly as much as in 300 or for example CHEM 421: Chemical Biology; as a Chem major it felt like a straight up biology course and memorization heavy, and most of the class including Profs. are often not looking at the materials or answering questions from the chemical basis); 2) who are the instructors (I would definitely say some Profs make this class a lot more "worth it"); 3) do you have other friends in the course or will work to build a strong study group (you need it!); 4) how busy is your schedule otherwise (this is a big time commitment even though I did feel like I learned a lot, I would especially look at your midterm and exam schedules); 5) what are the possible alternatives (there is CHEM 421/522 for Chemical Biology, there are also other course in MB&B and MCDB that focuses on specific topics covered here, mostly that are smaller class sizes)
 I would recommend it for the sake of learning cool stuff about biochem. For getting a good grade? Skip.
