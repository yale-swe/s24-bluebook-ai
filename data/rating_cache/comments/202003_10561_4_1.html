Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 If you are interested in Latin American/Spanish-language literature, absolutely. You'll read a LOT, but the texts are interesting, and you can always ask others for help or clarification. I don't know if I'd recommend this for first-years in their first semester, since the advanced level of the course was a little overwhelming in terms of prior knowledge and expectations, but this is a great course overall.
 I would recommend this to a student who is really interested in Latin American literature, wants to challenge their Spanish reading/speaking abilities, or is pursuing the Spanish-language certificate. Also take this course for the professor - he is excellent!
 Yesas far as Spanish courses here go, this was the course I've been the most interested in, content-wise. The reading load is fairly standard and very manageable, as are the two comentarios. The final paper is doable, though a little of a doozy if you are not a native speaker/very comfortable writing in academic Spanish. Prof Gonzlez-Prez is really good at this stuff, and is very kind. I will say that as an online course, a twice a week seminar that meets for over an hour will probably be very tough regardless of the class itself.
 YES!! This class was so fun and informative.
 I would recommend this course to another student. The workload is not excessive, but one should be prepared to spend quite a bit of time writing the final paper. The readings were usually quite engaging and very manageable as well. Professor Gonzlez-Prez does a great job of discussing the readings in class and drawing students' attention to details they might otherwise miss. One thing to note is that while Professor Gonzlez-Prez does ask questions throughout the class, it does feel a bit like a lecture as he does most of the talking.
 Yes! This course is great.
 If youre interested in Spanish literature and culture, this is the perfect intersection of the two. I really enjoyed it.
 Yes.
 If you are looking for an enjoyable level 300 Spanish course - take this class!!! Anibal is an excellent professor and has a wealth of knowledge to provide. The reading load is much much lighter than other Yale classes so you can actually focus on the weeks reading and engage in detailed discussion during class. The readings are also super interesting and do not all fall under exile literature so the class discussion doesnt ever feel stagnant or boring. The final grade is also only based on three essays assigned throughout the semester, so you can feel free to practice your Spanish in class without any pressure.
 Sure, as long as you are willing to put in the work and want to spend time reading and thinking in Spanish.
