Â If you are considering taking this course, keep in mind that this is primarily a PROOF based class. You will spend a large portion of the course learning how to write proofs and how to think logically and concisely. We didn't get to "actual" linear algebra concepts (i.e. matrix operations) until about halfway through the semester. For this reason, we definitely did not end up learning as many computational linear algebra techniques as people who took math 222. In fact, many key computational topics were introduced in somewhat of a rushed manner at the end of the semester, forcing me to self-learn and practice them on my own. So, if you want to learn linear algebra for APPLICATION purposes, 100% take math 222. You might not understand the theory behind what you're doing, but you will become much more capable at solving practical problems. On the other hand, if you want an in-depth survey of matrix THEORY and a robust introduction to proof writing, coupled with some basic linear algebra, then by all means take math 225. It will be a steep learning curve (after the midterm I considered dropping the course), but it all worked out in the end and I am actually amazing by how much I have grown in terms of proof writing and mathematical thinking.
