 Yes. Very interesting and applies to many different fields of study.
 Definitely!!!! This class was so great and has made me interested in pursuing grad school in urban planning, which I had really never thought about before. Elihu is amazing- he is so passionate and thoughtful about what he is teaching and puts a lot of time into considering how best to include a variety of voices and narratives in the curriculum. Please take this class so that your understanding of the world and the dynamics of cities in our everyday life is expanded, like mine has been. I also loved the ongoing scrapbook project throughout the semester, which allowed for so much creativity and flexibility, so that students could dive deeper into their specific interests, but also push students to explore a variety of methods of urban planning critique. It was also really cool that Elihu paired groups of students with point people from New Haven organizations for our final projects. It was really awesome to be able to engage directly with New Haven in that way and feel like the time we invested in our final projects actually helped to make a difference in New Haven. It was very cool to see everyone else's presentations too! And I liked that Elihu brought a lot of people together from New Haven to come to our final presentations. It made our work feel real and appreciated.
 I would absolutely recommend this course to another student. The subject material is fascinating, and the teaching team are among the nicest and most enthusiastic professors I have encountered at Yale. They strive to make each lesson engaging, and their feedback on each scrapbook assignment was so thoughtful and helpful. The events outside of class, including on-site lectures and various workshops, made the class a vibrant community that always seemed to have something new to attend or explore. The scrapbook assignments themselves were always fun and rarely stressful, thanks to the teaching team. I would especially recommend this course to someone who has never encountered Urban Studies or even thought to take it. Each week, I entered class not knowing what to expect, and I always learned something completely new and fascinating. The class completely expanded my worldview and my way of thinking. I cannot recommend it enough.

 It can be a great, interesting fourth or fifth class or an immersive and intense one depending on how much work you want to put in.
 This course is an absolute gem. Professor Rubin, MC, and Katie were a fantastic teaching team who will guide you through an incredibly ambitious course and syllabus. Professor Rubin is brilliant, and his insights on longstanding issues in urban planning and the unique challenges we face in the aftermath of the pandemic were amazing to hear. Do be advised, however, that if you are taking this course after Professor Rubin's longstanding course on American architecture, that it will be a bit less organized, as the topics are more broad and it can feel a bit confused. Also, the guest speakers were a bit hit or miss often times, the discussions struggled to go past the buzzwords and lingo of urban planning and really delve deeper. The assignments, while manageable, were very open-ended, which was kind of frustrating, but the upside is this yielded an awesome array of contributions from the class. Still, these are minor critiques overall, you'll get to take a course that is highly relevant and necessary in a supportive environment with a great instructor and classmates who by and large were very engaged.
