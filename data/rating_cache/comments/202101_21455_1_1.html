 The professors do not care about helping the students succeed and limit interactions with the students like the stereotypical STEM professor. I was extremely disappointed in this approach to learning given COVID-19 and the difficulty many students are facing and was hoping for a more humane approach. I think that material as interesting as this would be a pleasure to learn if taught by professors that were interested in the students in their course.
 I wish the instructors would have spent more time on the more complex concepts and less time on the simple ones. The problem sets were also out of six questions which were each worth one point, so a mistake on one question would bring the overall grade of the problem set down considerably. That being said, this course is somewhat outside my major, so the material may have been more difficult for me than it would be for the average student interested in taking the course.

 Strengths: Professor Hatzios is an extremely clear lecturer and also super clear when answering questions through email or during office hours. Live lectures were helpful in understanding some of the applications of what we learned from the recorded lectures and how we might be tested on the content. Weaknesses: The TAs were extremely incompetent. I honestly wish this was like many other STEM courses where there are peer tutors or undergraduate learning assistants since I feel like someone who had taken the class before would've known the content way better. I never felt like the TAs knew the material that well and many times they would either not know how to answer questions or provide answers that were incorrect. There was also very little coordination between them so when answering clarification questions on the problem sets, they would often give varying answers, which can be very confusing. The grading on exams and problem sets were also extremely nit picky. They would take off points for the littlest things. The questions were never specific enough to clarify exactly what they were looking for but if you didn't mention specific terms or phrases, you would immediately get points off. Professor Yan was alright as a lecturer but honestly very confusing and went a bit fast. I wish he went a bit slower and explained concepts more clearly. Also, the grading system is so unreasonable. They tell you that they can't determine letter grades until the end of the semester because they have to look at the total points, but that sounds so senseless to me. There is no transparency on how any student is doing, which makes it difficult for someone who might be considering dropping the course or credit/d/f the course. I also didn't like how they didn't disclose how letter grades would be determined. They say it's based on "class performance" but that's extremely vague and I feel like any course that grades on a curve just fosters a competitive rather than collaborative environment. Lastly, I wish they would've released the answer keys to problem sets and exams. The feedback was often unclear and I don't understand what the issue is with releasing the answer keys.
 Strengths: I appreciated the format of the class. Watching pre-recorded lectures and using the live lecture session to go over confusing concepts and apply our understanding was a good format. Also, Professor Hatzios was an excellent professor. She explains concepts very clearly and she was good at responding to emails and follow-up questions. Furthermore, I appreciated the fact that the teaching staff held office hours and review sessions multiple times a week. Weaknesses: I don't think the TAs were competent enough to teach this course. When I went to office hours, the TAs were very restricted in what they could and could not say, they provided vague and confusing answers, and sometimes they guided us in the wrong direction. For example, I would go to one TAs office hours and she would tell me something completely different than when I went to another TAs office hours the following day. Also, I believe the TAs took off unnecessary and unjustified points on the Psets and exams. Often times I had to email the TAs who graded my assignments and argue for points back, but the TAs were nonresponsive and unreasonable.

 I learned a lot from this class, and overall, I would recommend this course. Friends who took the class the years before told me the content material was enjoyable, but I couldn't believe it at the time it turns out that was true! A large part of that had to do with the professors themselves: Yan and Hatzios were very thorough and clear in their explanations, and were always responsive to emails if you asked them a question. I found them incredibly helpful I will say that I found Professor Yan's portion of the class to be significantly harder than Professor Hatzios' half, just because the first half focused more on the finer details of molecular mechanisms and bacterial cell processes. My one complaint about the class, and this is a big one, is about the TAs. Oftentimes I'd go to office hours ahead of a pset to get some guidance on a question, and two different TAs would guide me along two different answers. If you asked any one of them a question during one of the review sessions, 75% of the time they wouldn't be able to answer it and sometimes, when they did answer it, it was wrong... Additionally, their grading of psets and exams was terribly inconsistent. The answer they look needs to be worded a specific way in order for it to be graded fully correct if you had a reworded version that still had the same idea, they would dock you. This has happened multiple times to me and some peers who also took this class and this indicates that they didn't understand the material enough to see an answer rewritten and still perceive it as correct. Oh, another thing: we'd have psets due the week of an exam, for every exam we took. We never got those psets graded back in time before we took the test, and there was no answer key posted for those psets. That was a pretty annoying inconvenience. Normally for any other class, I'd be shy to go to the professors, instead turning to the TAs for my questions. But these TAs were so incompetent that I found myself turning to Hatzios and Yan unexpectedly and this was not a problem because the professors were so helpful and kind anyway. Please, for next year, I urge you to change the TAs. Two out of the four of them worked in a microbio lab and still gave horrible, muddled explanations, with no clear understanding of the course material.
