Â This might sound extremely redundant, but I would recommend this course for anyone interested in learning Old English -- Old English being a new language with its own grammatical rules, syntax, and specific canon texts. That said, I think it is important to note that actually learning how to read Old English texts comprises a bit more than half of the course, so be prepared to do a lot of translation work, to wrestle with specific cases (dative, genitive, accusative, etc.), and to feel a bit intimidated by the sheer amount of words you don't initially recognize when picking up a new OE work. However, Prof. Thornbury is very understanding of each person's comfort level with a text, and she has a wealth of resources and pointers if you reach out to her. Though the class shifted towards interpretation towards the latter half of the course, translation remained the point of focus and difficulty. If you enjoy that kind of work, you will enjoy this class - especially if you want to take an English class that's not too focused on generating ideas surrounding texts. There's no real expectations for you to understand the "point" of the text, and you don't even have to get every translation right. This class just gives you exposure to texts written in a language that a small circle of scholars are very passionate about.
