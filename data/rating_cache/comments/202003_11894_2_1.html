2020-Fall CHEM 174 01 Organic Chem for First Years I

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 I liked how study halls were set up (3 per week). It would be nice to be able to talk with the professor outside of office hours
 Dr. Newhouse is a great teacher because he is very nice and remains after class everyday to answer questions. I always felt like I could send him an email if I had a question or felt like I wasn't performing well. The study halls the night before class are usually quite helpful as well. My main complaint with the class is the large discrepancy in student abilities based on past chemistry experiences and the advantages given to students based on friend groups and residential colleges due to the flipped classroom format. To do well in this course, you must (emphasizing must) have a group of friends who understand the content. If you don't, you're bound to underperform. I wish this weren't the case, but the flipped classroom style and Sapling homework assignments essentially require you to have friends who've taken chemistry before, which I think is quite unfair. The class would be better taught if Sapling were not used for homework assignments and if the teaching method were not flipped classroom. The current method gives great privilege to kids who took organic chemistry in the past, which I did not.
 The flipped classroom makes it more difficult to learn the material, since Tim expects you know all the material and does practice during class.
 This course definitely has a ton of work. This is not necessarily a strength or a weakness, just something that has to be advertised appropriately to incoming students. Do not take this course if you have never seen orgo before and you already have a rigorous courseload. I had never done any orgo before this class and was surprised to see a ton of kids who had already taken a whole year (some less, some more) of organic chemistry. It goes fast - very fast. The lectures do not stop and wait for questions. But, after rewatching and taking the time to study previous material/lectures, it is definitely doable. There is also a ton of homework every week (really, every day). Some of it is tedious and unnecessary. Other parts of it are helpful. The TAs are amazing - I am speaking only on behalf of my TA, but he was extremely helpful and very approachable.
