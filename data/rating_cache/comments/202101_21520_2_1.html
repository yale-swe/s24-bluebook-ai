2021-Spring MATH 231 01 VectorCalculus&LinearAlgebraII

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 21520 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 The course was excellently adapted to the COVID situation with the mixture of online lectures and a plethora of office hours.
 Well taught online lectures at a perfect pace using an extended whiteboard so students could still see previous notes if the instructor moved on (avoiding the problem of switching to a new slide too fast)
 I think the sense of community building succeeded even in the pandemic. I have many friends that are in MATH 231 with me and they are awesome. I think having office hours not on Zoom but on a different software called Gathertown also made it better.
 Pat is great with the iPad!
 I like watching recorded lectures because of my time zone. It works well.
 I felt like the whole set up for the class was geared toward COVID-19 learning. All the problem sets were done independently anyway, and the teacher always made each online lecture helpful and meaningful. Not to mention, all materials from class (and from outside the class that the teacher thought would be helpful) were provided at all times. There were lots of office hour sessions too. On top of this, in the era of COVID-19 learning, deadlines did become more shaky for everything (and in some other classes apparently, the workload increased). The professor took this into consideration by having a reasonably flexible deadline schedule for all assignments that allowed the students to submit their assignments at times that did not put them behind in other classes.
 Virtual course notes worked well and everything ran smoothly.
 This course worked well in a virtual format. Pat was able to make the lectures enjoyable and engaging despite them being online this semester.
 There is a threshold limiting how high "virtual engagement" can be, and I think this class just about reached that.
 The course meeting times were inconveniently placed during lunchtime, so was not able to attend live lectures.
 The Gather Town office hours were really nice, since we could collaborate in different groups depending on where we are in the Psets. All lecture notes and recordings are uploaded, so it is pretty easy to review classes or catch up to a class you missed.
 Having access to video recordings of class and screen captures of lecture notes was super useful! I also found that the online office hours via Gathertown were convenient and helped me find friends to work on PSETs together. The friendships I made here go beyond the class. Breakout rooms during class for discussions increased immersion although they sometimes ran quiet (keeping the same group over each day was better for this purpose).
 Recorded lectures and course notes were super helpful. I was able to come back to them anytime I was confused with the pset. We were also able to collaborate with friends on zoom, which was nice. However, I wish that we were able to take classes in person just so that we learn about each other a little more.
 Gathertown for office hours worked very well, and even better than Zoom office hours.
 The office hours held using gather.town and an online whiteboard were very sucessful. The YUMS speakers and Pat's weekly 'Pat's cats' power hour' were also very engaging, and an important part of the creation of the rich undergraduate math culture at Yale. Of all of my classes, this one was the most successful in the transition to the online format.
 The office hours were great!
 I think Gather Town was a very creative way of doing office hours. Overall, the main problem was finding a group to do psets with as an off-campus student.
 Pat's live lectures were great, he handled the transition well.
 There was quite a bit of engagement in the class; Pat does a warm-up in every lecture and will frequently ask students to answer the problems. He also broke us out into breakout rooms; I personally didn't like that part as much, but I'm sure many others liked it. For office hours, we just gather.town and that was GREAT--I love the platform, it's super engaging and fitting for those us working in groups without going through the hassle of Zoom breakout rooms (not to mention aesthetics)!
 Pat's live handwritten lectures were great for the Zoom setting because it takes more time to convey information when there's such a disconnect between us and him. He frequently asked in polls how well we were understanding the material and adjusted accordingly, which was also extremely helpful. The office hours website (gather.town) I found to be a bit glitchy at times - there were certainly ways that made it better than Zoom rooms but I guess I just never acclimated to it so it always felt weird for me.
 The live virtual lectures as well as the recorded lectures we could watch on our own time if we couldn't make it or if we needed to go back and understand something better, using gathertown as a space for office hours
