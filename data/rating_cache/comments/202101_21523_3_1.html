 Yes, absolutely take this class.
 I would recommend this course to another student depending on their background in math and current professor. To my knowledge, this course is usually a lot less rigorous. However, with Cosmin this semester, the problem sets often felt like biweekly olympiads. This is not ideal, as Math 244 is a foundational course in multiple majors (including but not limited to S&DS, Math, Applied Math, and Computer Science), and its only prerequisite is Math 115. I have friends who did well in Math 115 but dropped this course by the end of the first week, as the step up in difficulty was unreasonably drastic. If a student has a strong background in proof-based mathematics and competition math, I would surely recommend this course to them -- it would probably be fun. Otherwise, if Cosmin is teaching the course, I would advise them to stay away unless they wanted a semester of intellectual suffering and perpetual feelings of ire and inadequacy.
 I would only recommend this course if you consider yourself to be very talented at proof-based advanced mathematics. It is very challenging and the tests will leave your tearing your hair out for hours. There are lots of very smart freshmen that take this class and ruin the curve. Be warned.
