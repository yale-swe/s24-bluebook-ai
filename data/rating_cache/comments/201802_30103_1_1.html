Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 Strengths: -The instructor explains the lesson and goes over in a manner that is easy to understand -The instructor always asks to see him if you have any questions regarding the Chapter that you may need clarification. The course is good as it is.
 The course was well-taught, but I wasn't a huge fan of the curriculum structure. The main focus of the course was experimental design and interpretation. That's important stuff, of course, but I feel like it should have been confined to the first week. I would have liked to have learned more actual psychological theories that I could apply to my own life.
 The course can give students a better understanding of the basic priciples of psychology through the use of specific examples.
 The main strength of this course is the sheer amount of information we were able to cover in the allotted time frame. I greatly enjoyed learning about a topic about which I had very little prior knowledge. The weekly readings and writing responses were also incredible interesting and engaging a great way to learn relevant class information as practiced in real-world situations. A major weakness of this course was the lack of feedback we received from either the professor or the TA. The comments on our reading responses were minimal, and we did not receive feedback from weeks 2-5 until the 6th week. Despite this, both the professor and TA did an excellent job of answering our questions in class, and provided guidance to students who were in need of additional help.
