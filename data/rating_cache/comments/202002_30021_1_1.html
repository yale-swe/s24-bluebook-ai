 Two strengths of this course were (1) the group quizzes and (2) a step-by-step breakdown of research projects. The group quizzes fostered peer communication, and this student engagement was immensely helpful under the given circumstances, given the isolation that often accompanies remote learning. I also found that the group quizzes allowed for better understanding of the material, as students had to go beyond their own understanding and convince others of their logic. Moreover, I appreciated that each of the two research projects were broken down into smaller assignments throughout the semester, rather than just having one final paper due. This layout allowed me to respond to feedback, which was especially helpful when writing the research proposal because I had not formatted a college-level research proposal prior to this course. However, the online formatting and accelerated pace of this summer course came with weaknesses that may not necessarily be present during an in-person fall/spring semester. Whereas a 10-minute break per class was offered in the first half of the course, the second half of the course only offered either a 5-minute break or no break at all. In addition, the second half of the course did not offer as many breakout discussions as the first half of the course. In my opinion, one 10-minute break and several brief peer discussions are needed each day in order to keep students engaged in what is otherwise a long lecture, especially when students are isolated within a remote environment.
