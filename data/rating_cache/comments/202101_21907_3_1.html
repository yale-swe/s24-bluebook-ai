Â Listen, you will hear raving reviews about Adriane and how great of a lecturer she is and that this class is really great. And it's partially true. Adriane is a very intelligent person and goes above and beyond to make her class have many resources to get help. However this class is pure masochism. Adriane brags that she is a not a "by-the-book" professor, which in its own regard is nice to know since she dedicates herself to making her own class materials, but this comes at the expense of orderly, concise material. There are lecture notes and many review materials on canvas, which again are nice, but this class feels wholeheartedly like a word salad of Adriane's physics knowledge. Some of the topics covered are so incredibly esoteric and quite frankly needlessly complicated as to diminish the students' experience. There is perhaps no better example than the psets to justify what I am saying. I came into this class with the ambition of perhaps studying physics or EE and am leaving a regret-less social science major. The problem sets in this class are SO long, filled with ridiculously long algebraic expressions that do not contribute at all to learning. I wasted so many hours doing tedious algebraic simplications after doing a conceptually interesting integral/derivation from first principles. Also, everyone just goes to the study halls to get the answers and I know of many people that would just divide and conquer, each person getting an answer to a pset question and then sharing all of the pset together. The workload in this class is so so so needlessly over the top. By the end of the semester, I ended up loathing this class and having to spend countless hours every weekend either going to the study halls or asking peers for help just to finish the 10+ hour pset every. damn. week. I honestly felt like I learned very little from doing these psets and overall from this class. I had taken a physics class at a local state college in high school and it was so much better, which is embarrassing considering I attend an institution with seemingly infinite financial resources and thus power to implement a successful physics program. At this state school, we followed concepts from a book which were expanded upon in lecture with reasonable, practical examples to do as homework. In PHYS 181, you attend a lecture where Adriane haphazardly slaps a few new concepts into your head, she does an example or two on her board, and then you are rewarded with a 20-hour death sentence in office hours to apply the concepts that were shakily taught to you on a problem set that everyone just copies from each other for an easy 100. You have no time to throughly play with the class material in your mind as you are too busy drowning in tedious, mind-numbing psets. This class truly drained any joy that I had in the sciences from me. And also, the exams this year were basically just copy and paste from her lecture notes, save for the final which was death. The only saving grace in this class is that it is graded so leniently such that if you bomb the exams and just do everything else (psets and participation) the worse you will get is a B. I am begging you for your own sanity, unless you HAVE to take intro physics, save yourself the stress and take classes that Yale actually dedicates its resources to in the humanities and social sciences departments.
