2020-Fall HUMS 027 01/LITR 027 01 Six Pretty Good Selves

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401-YCWR CRN: 12528/12529 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 A LOT
 I improved at close-reading and feel much more prepared for college writing.
 This course helped me significantly improve my writing abilities and confidence. The readings and discussions also opened my mind to new ways of thinking about formation and manifestations of the self in different cultural and temporal contexts. I also improved my ability to participate in active discussion of readings and films through this class.
 I learned so much more than I could have bargained for. From learning how to write emails, form theses, structure academic papers, explore Yales vast resources, connect with tutors, and build relationships with professors, TFs + peers in a virtual setting, this class did this AND more. I was able to read the classics, explore texts close to my identity, all while feeling confident and supported.
 I learned a great deal about Yale's collections, the idea of a collection, close reading, comparing texts, and technical and stylistic aspects of writing. This course also expanded my approach to texts from just looking at what they say (or what someone says they say) to what they do/what they are/where they come from/why they matter.
 Close reading/analysis of a text, improving my writing process, and learning about Yale resources.
 I improved my college writing immensely, honed discussion skills, learned how to utilize some of Yale's incredible resources including the library and collections.
 I learned a good amount about the writing process and new ways to think about writing. I also developed an appreciation for literary traditions I had never been exposed to.
 I learned how to read texts critically, write an essay and grow comfortable with my writing process, and think about abstract concepts like selfhood.
 I was able to develop my writing skills as a whole. I learned how to get more out of the text that I was presented, and then with that extra information I was able to apply it to my writing more efficiently.
 This class really improved my writing process and introduced me to works of literature and film from all around the world. It also made me feel a lot more comfortable with using Yale resources, especially in terms of research.
 Through this course, I developed college-level writing skills. I also became a better reader, and more able to understand difficult or dense texts. The course material itself was fascinating, and I gained a lot of insight into how I view myself and how that view is influenced by my environment. I was exposed to texts from all different locations, cultures, and time periods, so I felt I developed a good understanding of how views on the self vary across the world and throughout time.
 i learned everything about developing a proper writing process to thinking differently and critically about complex readings. i pushed myself as a writer in ways i had been afraid to before this class. the positive impact of this course on me has been immeasurable.
 I learned how to close read, to write, and to analyze text. My writing and reading skills improved. More importantly, I think, I learned about perspective. This class really opened up my thoughts to other perspectives and opinions by hearing my classmates and their insights about the texts and films featured in the syllabus. I also learned about writing as a process and not simply as a one-time, one-sitting task. Writing, as a process, was challenging for me, but I know now what I can do to help myself become a better writer. I also learned more about myself as a communicator and an individual with my own thoughts, morals, and values.
