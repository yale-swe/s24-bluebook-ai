It's honestly not the most fruitful class and you will probably take away very little but that can be fine senior spring. If there was an alternative or I was able to substitute the class for something (even if it was more challenging), I probably would have. Overall, the "student presentation" format of the class isn't really productive to learning. Truthfully, this class sometimes felt like a waste of time. "The present three times over the span of the semester" format led to the following: no engagement for 12 weeks and a TON of work for the 3 weeks one presented (don't underestimate the amount of time it takes to put together a class-length presentation). I genuinely had no idea what was happening the weeks I didn't present. Further, all my peers are really smart, but it was tough for us to present on material we were neither particularly passionate about (this isn't our thesis) nor particularly knowledgeable about. We ended up basically regurgitating what the textbook said. Lastly, the discussion posts every class were unmaintainable. Most courses have a discussion post once a week which I think is much more maintainable. Because we were supposed to do one after every class, no one did them in general. It's tough to put effort into writing one (and read an outside paper) for every class session. At the same tine, the course is low workload -- three class length presentations and discussion posts every class for the whole semester is not bad at all (especially because people really stopped doing the discussion posts). The professors are also very generous with final grades and I think most of the class received A's. I appreciated this because it showed how considerate the professors were about our virtual energy levels at this point in our Yale careers. It's also VERY uncommon for econ and math department professors who tend to ignore the outside world/state of the world when setting expectations. The professors themselves are really dope and are just a great energy to be around senior year. I wish they would have been more involved in presenting material in class as I know they were both incredibly knowledgeable. Dr. Bergemann in particular was super approachable and kind and seemed genuinely engrossed in pour presentations even when no one else was. He also brought us brownies on the last day of class. All to say, this class won't change your life but it is not a bad way to round out a really challenging econ and math major. I will say: there should be an alternative to this class. This class fell at the same time as two other classes I have desperately wanted to take since my first year at Yale but had never gotten into previously. When I asked the DUS, there was literally nothing I could substitute for it. It's not a productive enough course for that to be true. I could understand if this was a more core theory requirement.
