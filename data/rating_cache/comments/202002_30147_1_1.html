2020-Summer MUSI S212E 01/HUMS S212E 01 Brains of Genius

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30146/30147 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 The content is a strength, as are the essay topics (I've really enjoyed writing them). However, I found the essay grading to be unnecessarily harsh. I got a low grade on an essay because it was too broad in scope and then another low grade on an essay that was supposedly too narrow; be careful to do exactly what the task is asking of you. For a class that emphasizes creativity and thinking out of the box, this is a bit of a paradox. Overall, the course was very interesting and I'm glad I took it.
 Stengths: - Clear feedback on the papers to improve writing - having a writing tutor was a big advantage - the questions accompanying the reading enabled better comprehension of the texts and were useful to index specific information in a text when I had to find quotes - the discussion sections, more so than the powerpoints perhaps, helped cement understanding of key terms and expand my views on certain topics Weaknesses: - the powerpoints were perhaps not as interactive as they could have been - the hand raising option in-built in zoom might be a neater way to divide screen time among students who wish to speak; the physical hand raising is often missed via Zoom - the prompt on the third essay was perhaps too scattered. The motivating questions are useful but they need to be accompanied by a succinct and direct prompt.
 The readings assigned in this course were relevant and captivating. The fact that the questions assigned with the readings had page numbers that corresponded with the text made completing the assignments a lot more efficient. Another helpful aspect of the course was the resources provided to help with writing the papers. On top of that, the instructors made themselves available to meet and answered all questions very quickly. One thing that would improve this course is if there was a way to view and access all of the readings at once; this would make writing papers easier.
 I enjoyed the class discussion along with the readings about various geniuses. Another strength of the course was its structure. It was kind of like a high school class where you had regular homework and engagement with the material. So you worked with the material almost every day and I think that helps you get more out of the class.
 kind of unorganized, loved the opportunity to resubmit essays
 Strengths were some of the discussions. Interesting to read about various people who are revered as geniuses. The central weakness of the course was that it sometimes seemed to lack rigor. I don't think we ever got to the bottom of whether 'genius' is a social construct or not. For papers, we were told to assume that 'genius' was a real thing, but we were also told that it was just a social idea. I also don't think that the course touched on the role of luck enough. If you have to change the world to be a genius, you need to be lucky, too. We seemed to gloss over that point. We also could have talked about whether the concept of genius was at all valuable, or if it was completely superficial. Some of the readings were simplistic. People like Malcolm Gladwell are just pop psychology--should we really be reading them? Given that the course was being taught by two people with strictly humanities backgrounds, I think it would have been better to steer clear of psychology all together. Lastly, I thought many of the paper prompts were unclear or overly complicated relative to the paper lengths. In no other course at Yale have I been asked to write so many papers under 1000 words. I don't really think these short papers help improve writing that much.
 Very strong course. Enjoyed it very much. Well rounded course that teaches humanities, writing, and culture.
 The class length was ideal, because longer sections in summer classes can become hard to focus on. I thought the reading quizzes were helpful, as were the reading assignments, but many of the video assignments seemed redundant. Because the readings served as the source material for most of the papers, I think the videos and video questions could be eliminated.
 The strengths of the course include the multiple avenues that facilitated student learning. Through in-person seminars, video lectures, readings, and research essays, we were able to engage with the topic in a variety of different ways. The instructor feedback could have been more extensive or in-depth than it was. I would like to have seen scheduled office hour times.
 The biggest strength of the course was definitely prof. Wright and our TA Jakub. They were both very insightful in the subject area and you can tell that they truly care about students me success. I feel that while this class isnt necessarily easy, it is a low-stress environment that allows you to focus on learning rather than grades. The only weakness I can think of would be that the videos we had to watch and their subsequent questions were at times a bit tedious to answerI think that the questions should either be shortened or the some of the videos be a bit shorter.
 I think the structure of the class with the video responses, readings, and daily quizzes were really good for a summer course, as they kept me engaged and not slacking at all throughout the process.
