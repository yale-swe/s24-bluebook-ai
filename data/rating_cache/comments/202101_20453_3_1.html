Â FOR PHIL MAJORS: I would 100% recommend this course to other philosophy majors. The content is different from some other philosophy classes in that it's very creative, and it feels immediately relevant (at least to me, who is interested in figuring out whether God exists). Lots of modal proofs and analytic stuff, but also some continental philosophy towards the end. Professor Pittard also includes a diversity of perspectives (i.e., some non-Western content) but not in a way that feels forced. Overall I would say this is one of the less-difficult PHIL courses out there (from my sampling), and it's not too hard to do well if you're as fascinated by the content as I was. FOR NON-PHIL MAJORS: Yes, take this course, but you should know what you're getting into. The readings are pretty dense, and you're expected to have a high level of rigor and precision on your essays. You should definitely talk to your TA about their expectations for the essay to get a sense of how you should be writing. However, of all the philosophy courses you can take, I think this one is one of the most interesting ones as it's on a wonderfully important and complex subject, and Professor Pittard is just excellent. OVERALL: Take this course! With Steph as your TA if you can..
