2020-Summer G&G S130 01 Global Climate Change

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30264 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 Strengths: Discussion section with the TAs and the Ruddiman textbook. Weakness: the Archer textbook. I would have appreciated not reading the Archer textbook so more time could be devoted to the Ruddiman readings.
 Strengths: Interesting material, a good textbook Weaknesses: Professor Bolton, lack of organization
 The breadth of knowledge covered in the course was a big strength. I felt like we covered a lot of material, all of which were relevant to the theme of the course and the modern climate crisis. Prof Bolton and the TAs were also extremely helpful and accommodating.
 The course was online, which made engagement very difficult. Technology issues severely hindered the flow of the class. However, the content was pretty interesting.
 The strengths of this class were that the TAs were very accessible. It was also nice going over the textbook readings in class as opposed to just moving on to new material. The weaknesses of this class were the discussion groups, which become very awkward when people do not want to talk. Also, though it is the nature of the beast, reading two full textbooks in five weeks and being expected to understand the material is a very heavy load. 40 pages of reading a night certainly took its toll and studying 28 chapters for the final can be overwhelming.
 This course was a great introduction to geosciences for anyone who is interested in the area. It is also a great class for those who are interested in climate change and want to better understand the science and history of it.
 Strengths: Ruddiman textbook is fantastic, like so so useful. Archer textbook is harder but also pretty good. The material is really interesting, both the modern-day stuff but really looking at Earth's history is really cool. Please keep this and don't make it more about global warming and stuff, I think the focus is good and gives students a better understanding of climate as a whole, which helps enormously in understanding the present. The P-Sets are also good, they force me to really understand topics, and go back and learn stuff I missed or zoned out during. I like that they're written. Also I love how the professor says "I don't know this" or "science hasn't figured out this problem yet". Makes the whole thing feel very human and like we're all in this together. Weaknesses: Lecture wasn't super strong. I found that lecture too often looked to discuss details of Delta O18 instead of the big picture. I had to read a lot of the textbook to do the P-Sets. As a student, the most frustrating thing can be that you never feel like you fully understand anything as the climate is just too complicated and there's always a level deeper. But, I don't think that this is anything wrong with the class, and just how climate science is.
 Strengths: The professor, the TAs were very helpful in answering questions during class as well as going beyond the bare minimum (e.g my TA, Jo, created mini lectures that supplemented class which were very insightful), the clear structure of matching the material with the textbooks. Weaknesses: None
 The class format was easy to follow and lectures were not too long.
 Strengths: Straightforward homework assignments and exams, fair grading. Weaknesses: Could have done some minor things like provide an answer key to the homework assigments once completed.
 Professor Bolton's lectures felt a bit scattered, but the readings encompass the necessary material well.
 The course was generally well organized, however, the Archer reading was a bit confusing given that this was an introductory course. Assigning Ruddiman alone would have been enough.
 Good overall breadth of geologic sciences. Very fast paced and lots of reading. Lectures were not as beneficial because they simply seemed to repeat what had been said in the textbook without adding insight or further information or application.
 The class was interesting and the psets helped me learn more. The two hours during each lecture felt like a lot and I often zoned out. Although the breakout sessions were meant to keep us engaged and have discussions, I think it would have been helpful to have more discussions during the lecture.
 I felt like a strength of this course was the way information was presented for students with different levels of experience with the material, and that it offered opportunities for both broad strokes and in-depth analyses of the material. I thought that a weakness of the course was that the Zoom breakout rooms didn't have super clear direction.

 The course mainly consists of problem sets that require you to explain climate change concepts discussed in the textbook. The exams were similarly formatted. I personally found this to be a great way to learn the material and retain information for the future. I do think the lecture could stand to be more interactive.
