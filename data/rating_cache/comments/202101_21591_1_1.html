2021-Spring CHEM 330L 01 Lab for Physical Chemistry I

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

Â Despite the fact I still think this course was a 5 for workload, I understood why we did almost everything we had to do. I thought the experiments took a reasonable amount of time, the teaching team was responsive and clear, and the requirements of the lab reports made sense. One of the only weaknesses that I noted was that I am not sure how productive it is for the students to tabulate all the measurements for each experiment, as I have rarely seen this done in an actual scientific paper. Also, we sometimes did not get feedback from a previous report before turning in the next one, which definitely hindered our ability to improve on our reports.
Â I felt very supported throughout this course, and there was always someone I could reach out to with questions. Professor Cooper and the TAs were super accessible through email and lecture time. I also enjoyed learning about the physical-chemical concepts from an experimental perspective- the course material was often engaging. I think this course naturally has a high workload as there is a lot of material to cover. The main weakness I noticed was that we often received feedback on previous reports after the next ones were submitted.
Â The strengths of this course were its instructional staff. The weaknesses were its incredibly high work load and the how long it took to receive feedback on assignments.
Â This course enabled a lot of flexibility with assignment completion and allowed students to fit assignments around their schedules. The progressive buildup to composing full length lab reports was helpful. The course TAs were always accessible by email, and having time during Friday lectures to discuss data analysis was really helpful. I really enjoyed the final presentation as opposed to a final exam. I felt like the labs themselves were very repetitive across the weeks. We did not gain exposure to many different tools or measurement types. I feel like I just turned a gas knob for hours week after week. I would have appreciated exposure to more tools and applications.
Â I think the streamlining of this course from The Old Days has been very effective at the expense of quite a small amount of depth. That said, it was still *very* time intensive, almost entirely because of the data analysis, even as someone with a knack for automating routine tasks with real programming languages. I would certainly recommend that students in the future come up with a single framework to calculate 95% CIs/make I think close to 80 plots that are 8-12 points with error bars and a trend line/plot residuals. Even so, lab reports take ~10 hours. The last big change I think needs to be made to bring the class down to the "normalized at every other institution" level of workload is to do away with the requirement to include tables of all of your raw data in the reports. Just let us submit our excel analyses. All those tables can take 20+ minutes to format, especially with having to manually go into each data point and type the little confidence intervals in parentheses. It's all in the name of complying with "journal requirements," though for all the time I've spent reading physical-chemistry-adjacent journals, I have seen more Excel files with raw data in SIs than non-copy-pastable pdf tables. It's 2021.
Â The strengths of this course were the teaching staff, including Dr. Cooper. The data analysis lecture replacing the pre-experiment lecture (which was recorded and you watch on your own time) was much appreciated, and helped in analysis of data for the report due the following week. Another strength is that we were able to do final presentations, rather than the traditional final. This final presentation was, I think, a better way to understand the material more thoroughly, and provided great experience in scientific symposium talks. Weaknesses in the course, although improved from previous semester, still permeate through this semester. The amount of data analysis and attention to detail needed to complete the lab reports is something I (and everyone else) was not used to. I cannot imagine how to improve the lab report writing, since the purpose of the lab reports is to be technical reports, and therefore are inherently long and tedious. I think Dr. Cooper and the teaching team did their best in mitigating the weaknesses, especially with the data analysis lecture. The only thing that could be improved is being more explicit in what was expected in the lab report, since it was sometimes a guessing game. Possibly doing consistent recaps at the end of the pre-recorded lectures would be quite helpful (it happened often, but it was not consistent).
