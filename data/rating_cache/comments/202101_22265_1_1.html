2021-Spring BIOL 104 01 Ecology & Evolutionary Biology

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 22265 Term: 202101

 Professor Near was a great lecturer and included many interesting examples in his lectures to help really cement the key concepts. The Ed discussions were also very helpful and the professors / peer tutors responded pretty quickly to our questions on that forum. I also appreciated that the pset questions prepared us for the exams which is a lesson that a lot of other Yale STEM courses could learn from.
 Strengths: The discussion section papers were fairly interesting. It was nice to pick your own topic for the writing assignment. Weakness: There is very little ecology. Learning the phylogenetic relationships seemed kind of pointless at times, as it wasn't very well explained what we were supposed to be taking away from all of the phylogeny. I think focusing more heavily on the evolution of traits would have helped, as the brief descriptions of trait evolution we did get were some of the best parts of the lecture.
 I found the problem sets and writing assignments very useful; the problem sets helped to solidify foundational concepts, and the writing assignments offered a level of personalization that allowed me to apply course content in a way that was enjoyable. The papers discussed in our weekly discussion sections were also interesting and entertaining. The course could be improved by reducing by providing lecture outlines to emphasize the key concepts in each lecture; it was difficult at times to distinguish between primary and secondary course material. It would also be helpful to provide more practice exams.
 I thought the lectures were very clear and well-done. I also thought the papers chosen for the course were helpful in clarifying and expanding on topics brought up in class. No suggestions for improvement
