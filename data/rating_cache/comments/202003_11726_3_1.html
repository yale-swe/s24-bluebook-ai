2020-Fall AMTH 222 02/MATH 222 02 LinearAlgebraWithApplications

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 11727/11726 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 I liked that we could watch recorded lectures at our own pace, since we are all in different time zones.
 There was a bit too much recorded material and not enough live in my opinion. The polls that were used during the live sessions helped with engagement, more of those would have been nice.
 The asynchronous lectures were sometimes hard to follow, and I found it difficult to wait until the live sessions to clear up my confusion. I did find the ample office hours with the professor and TA helpful.
 I think that having recorded lectures and then live sessions for questions was a great way to get into the material. I do think that it would've been nice to have longer live sessions.
 Succeeded: Pre-recorded lectures to give students flexibility and 30 min live sessions. Could be improved: Record the live sessions and post in Media Library.
 The lectures NEED to be edited or rehearsed more. Nothing against the professors personally, but I felt they were pretty unprofessional in how they went about lecturing and it rarely felt like there was a greater point or direction to the videos. Also, they need to hire more peer tutors/TA's, as one guy literally carrying the entire class on his back is completely unfair to both him and all of the students.
 I thought the combination of pre-recorded lectures and live sessions was really helpful. However, I wish the professors could be more accommodating, especially considering the fact that we are living through such an incredibly stressful time. We could not turn in psets late for credit, and the justification for this was that the lowest pset grade would be dropped. This seems really counterintuitive to learning as there is no incentive to make up late/missing work. It also seems kind of absurd to only allow students leniency on one week. I really wish the profs could have been more understanding and accommodating, and I think the cutthroat nature of the math department in general is why so many people steer clear of it.
 I thought that the recorded lectures worked well. I liked that each week had 5-6 shorter videos instead of 1-2 longer videos. This made the work feel more manageable, and it was easier to go back and find specific topics in the videos. I felt that we could have made better use of the synchronous time during class. Especially towards the beginning of the semester, the live sessions were often reviews of the material, which felt repetitive. The professor usually didn't stop for questions, so it wasn't much different than watching a prerecorded lecture, but with content we'd already covered. The live sessions where we did example problems were more useful than the times when we did recap.
 I think that the recorded lecture/live lecture format was honestly very difficult for students. With my experience of math classes near the size of our sections (~30 people), there can be a dialogue between the professor and students. 100% recorded lectures fully stop this dialogue, that in my opinion, actually facilitates learning and understanding. The 30 minute live lectures were rushed and difficult to follow. They often jumped between topics, and we'd usually run out of time to cover everything. I don't think I can ever take another virtual math class unless it's much better than this. It was extremely difficult to learn in this format, and I often had to rely on the internet to actually understand what was happening. Very few examples were shown, which I think is critical to understanding- and almost no examples similar to pset or test questions were ever shown.
 I liked the system of recorded lectures and then a short in-person meeting. I think Prof. Aizenbud (my prof) was always very welcoming about questions so even though we didn't have a lot of time with him, I thought the level of engagement was totally fine for me.
 I think one of the shortcomings was the half-hour length of the class. A longer length (1:15) would have helped for furthering students understandings of concepts.
 I really liked that the instructors agreed to have midterms open for 24 hours. I think that having live lectures (and recording them, of course) would allow for better engagement and understanding.
 Recorded lectures were widely accessible, but without any supplemental material, became more difficult to understand. The ability to take the exam at any point within a day was well-implemented. I think that this course would have benefitted more from additional sessions with the professor.
 I found having the lectures prerecorded saved people a lot of time. We could go back to them whenever we wanted, fast forward, etc
 In general, I found the prerecorded lectures to be helpful, as I was able to take notes at my own pace, and even rewind to better understand a concept. However, I felt that the live sessions added very little to the learning experience, as most of what was noted came directly from the prerecorded lectures without elaboration or example.
 Making the exams open note and allowing students to pick an hour long block in a 24 hours period worked really well
 I liked the idea of doing recorded lectures and then shorter in-person live classes, but I feel like there could have been more focus on solving example problems during the live classes rather than restating what was in the videos.
 I believe that the short live virtual lectures were inefficient as they were only 30 minutes long each and did not cover much material. In addition, I believe that the organization of the class could be better. Homework solutions were never posted and homework grades did not come out until after the exams were taken. In addition, many of the practice exams did not give out solutions until the very last minute, which was very stressful.
 The use of pre-recorded lectures helped guide the week's discussion and helped to clear up confusions throughout the week.
