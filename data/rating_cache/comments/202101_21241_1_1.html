2021-Spring HIST 158J 01/AMST 398 01/ER&M 308 01 American Indian Law and Policy

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 21240/21241/21242 Term: 202101

 I loved this class!
 Strengths - It seems many more Indian law cases enter the legal radar as the US reckons with legal and racial history. Therefore, the instructor has the ability to incorporate different legal cases and legislative procedures into course materials. Weakness - The instructors does not understand how to fully operate all the necessary technologies to ensure a good virtual classroom.
 This class teaches so much about a specific part of Native American history that is fascinating and seldom taught in any other class
 Weakness of the class: the instructor grades too harshly off the bat without adequately preparing students with information about his standards. The course's reading load is also poorly distributed--there's a lot more reading in the beginning and very little in the end. Strengths: the course material is fascinating, the assignments are well-designed and interesting to complete, and the instructor is really invested in student learning.
 I really learned a lot in this class, and I think everyone should learn more about American Indian history. The assignments were not too demanding, and Professor Blackhawk was very accommodating. I do think it might have been better off as a longer seminar once a week than meeting for a shorter time twice a week, because it sometimes felt like we didn't get to have a full conversation about the readings, but other than that, I can't think of any weaknesses!
 Strengths: Engaging readings. Grading that dramatically improves writing style. Engaging discussions in class. Incredible guest speakers. Class does not feel hard to keep up with. Good for people with an interest in Indian Law and structure of American government. Relevant to modern issues. Weaknesses: Some readings came directly from law journals, so their length and verbage were hard to engage with. Some readings were too long, so seminar discussions rarely incorporated close analysis of texts. Class discussions often deviated from plans on syllabus, so it was hard to remain motivated to do all of the readings. Grammar and sentence structure are really addressed in the class, so it feels like it impacts one's essay grade to an extent larger than feels necessary.
