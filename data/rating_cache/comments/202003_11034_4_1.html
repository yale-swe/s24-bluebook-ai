 I would. I think that overall it is a fair assessment of my engineering knowledge, the test content was very justified, and the professor seemed to care whether we knew the content or not.
 Yes! Great intro class to electrical engineering, I learned a lot of new concepts that I wasn't really exposed to in high school. The professor is really great and patient when it comes to explaining confusing concepts. All of the material covered are also summarized concisely in the presentation, with step by step solutions to problems. I found that the textbook was a great supplement to the course, highly recommend purchasing a physical copy. The concepts can be a little challenging at first, but it gets SO much easier once you understand the concepts, because a lot of the analysis that is done is very logical and sequential. Once you get a grasp for the process, the analysis does get easier, for example, Professor Reed teaches you how to step by step solve for the Thevenin Equivalent first by zeroing the sources to find the Thevenin impedance, then you try to find the open circuit voltage etc. Plus you also just get a huge boost of self esteem and satisfaction when you solve a question right. My one tip for this class is go to the professor's office hours, go to TA office hours, go to peer tutor office hours. They are so helpful with explaining a lot of the new concepts. Don't be embarrassed to ask questions! Overall I strongly recommend this course, doesn't matter if you are majoring in electrical engineering or not, very useful.
 If you're interested in EE or want to major in EE, you should take this course. However, if you're just looking for a science course (or a science course that carries a writing credit), I wouldn't recommend this course. The problem sets, even though they're confusing, don't take very long to do if you do them at peer tutoring. However, the five labs throughout the semester were time-consuming and each involved a >8 page lab report. The course isn't taught in an engaging way. In terms of grading, this course is fine, as the professor curves grades to students' advantage rather than on a true bell curve.
