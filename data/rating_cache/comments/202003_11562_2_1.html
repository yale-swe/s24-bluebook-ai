2020-Fall ENGL 120 02 Reading & Writing Modern Essay

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 11562 Term: 202003

 Strengths: Professor Shirkhani! She encourages students to guide the discussion with their insights/reflection while nudging the discussion toward certain strategies/topics if need be. I really loved this seminar style; I never felt pressured to speak, but I always wanted to! Also, it's amazing to hear your classmates' thoughts (everybody seems so insightful and intelligent). This class is very much shaped by the class dynamic. I'm thankful that Professor Shirkhani fosters an environment where people are loving, supportive, and hilarious while all feeling comfortable enough to critique and workshop each other's pieces. Workshopping each other's pieces is SO fun and it's such a good feeling to get multiple sets of eyes on your paper. The support from your classmates, Professor Shirkhani, and potentially other sources (i.e. writing tutors) won't ever let you fall through the cracks if you reach out when you're struggling. ---- Weaknesses: some units have consistently longer pieces that aren't quite as spaced out. This really isn't a big issue (the longest pieces are typically by David Foster Wallace and are ~20 pages) but the satire unit really blows every other unit out of the water. To all future students: the satire pieces are so much shorter and it is so refreshing! Almost wish that the satire unit was earlier in the semester to serve as a sort of break. Grading transparency is also a bit of a problem (this is a problem across all my classes this semester though) since we received letter grades (ex: B+/A-) and it's hard to average without numbers. Even if I didn't really know what grade I had in the class though, this was by FAR my favorite course this semester. Strengths outweigh weaknesses by a landslide. Take this course if you have the chance!!
 The grading was in a way a weakness. It was not very clear how grades worked with the essays, it could be clarified because at times I found it difficult to see what specifically was making my essay worse, and what I could do to make my essay better. Strengths were the content of the essays, insightful discussions and workshops, and I also found the pace to be fair.
 This was such an incredible class. All of the readings were so well chosen, I was always excited to do the readings for class which is pretty rare. Discussions were intentionally and effectively lead, and I felt like we really talked about a broad range of writing tools that will help me a lot inn the future. If anything, I would maybe appreciate a 15 minute brainstorming session before the first draft of each essay is due so we can help get started with our ideas, but other than that, this class is just incredible and I can't really think of any weaknesses.
 Strengths: great energy, very detailed and helpful comments on writings, wonderful selection of reading material Potential improvements: more classtime discussing writing techniques may be beneficial
 Weaknesses: Sometimes we would not be able to discuss all the essays assigned for a given week due to lack of time. I can't think of any major weaknesses. Strengths: The discussion based format of the course was really helpful and facilitated interesting conversations on what makes good writing.
 Strengths: interesting readings and good questions asked by professor to keep discussion moving along. Weaknesses: I had trouble keeping my attention on class in the virtual seminar format. Class WAY too political. Sometimes I had trouble following along with the writing lessons of the day.
 Strengths: strong sense of community, lots of feedback from the professor, a good number of assignments with adequate time for revision, good selection of works, not too much reading or writing (a balanced workload) Weaknesses: We often read pieces that we did not have time to discuss in class. I feel this is sort of a necessary evil because spending a good amount of time on each essay is what allowed us to delve deep into that piece, but I would perhaps recommend planning beforehand which pieces we really want to go over that day and budgeting a specific amount of time for that piece.
 One great strength of this course was its focus on student discussion. The seminar style, with the teacher stepping back and allowing students to work through their ideas, helped keep the class active and engaged. The prioritization of learning through reading and analysis rather than rules, guidelines, or lecture-type information was also very helpful. This made students absorb knowledge and use it creatively in their writing, instead of just memorizing or repeating lessons. A potential weakness of the course is the limited amount of writing activities; time is mostly divided between reading and talking about example essays and writing essays of one's own. More activities involving student writing could be helpful in practicing and applying skills learned at a smaller scale.
