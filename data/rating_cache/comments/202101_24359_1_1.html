2021-Spring ENGL 275 01 Emerson, Dickinson, & Melville

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

Â The biggest strengths of this course were Professor Deming himself and the syllabus. During our last day of class, many of my classmates expressed how these readings and the course itself changed their lives, and I could not agree more, particularly with Dickinson. There are not many courses out there with such a large emphasis on Dickinson as ENGL 275, so taking this course just for Dickinson alone would be worth it. However, Emerson and Melville are both incredible writers, and all the readings were fascinating and engaged with each other so well. Professor Deming was also a fantastic seminar leader and really pushed us to take our ideas further.
Â Overall, I think this is a very strong course. I think there could have been a better background on transcendentalism/how these authors contrasted or conformed with convention of the time. Over time, the through-line of these three authors definitely becomes obvious, but I do think we studied each in a silo and putting them in conversation could be enriching. Going back to how I improved, this course heavily emphasized using the text to justify any argument you are making, where I felt in previous classes, it was easier to rely on feelings alone. A weakness would be the amount of material (I'm thinking specifically of reading, or trying to read, Moby-Dick in 2 weeks). We definitely ran into that classic problem of not being able to discuss a large amount of what we readeven on Dickinson, which was definitely the lightest part of the course, we often spent one class discussing one poem. This isn't a totally bad thing, as I think the central virtue of this course is how it pushes you to think, less the content it forces you to read, but I think it's something to maybe be interrogated.
