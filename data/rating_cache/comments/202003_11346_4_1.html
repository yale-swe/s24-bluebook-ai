 Yes, I would absolutely recommend this course to another student looking to fulfill the senior econ requirement. Take it for Eva if nothing else!! The workload and intellectual challenge of the course was similar to that of many other econ courses. It is math heavy and very similar to intermediate micro, just with different applications. It is an extremely fair course, and Eva is a delight.
 I would highly recommend this course to a senior econ student as their seminar. Eva is the best econ teacher who gives very fair assignments and exams. All the psets can be found in her slides and she also gives review exercises that are almost identical to her exams. She really wants you to do well and to learn. It was pretty stress free. Just a few nights of grinding before exams and you're good. Very chill econ seminar with the best teacher. Take it if you just trying to get through econ with an easy seminar.
 Yes this is a great senior seminar for Econ majors!! I'm someone who is really interested in both technology and economics, so this course perfectly covered the intersection between those 2 topics. You'll learn a lot about patents, copyright, licensing, vertical mergers, price competition, the effect of technological innovations on various industries, how you can analyze all of these topics with mathematical models, etc. These are all relevant topics, especially since the rise of AI and antitrust cases are frequently discussed in the news these days. Professor Chalioti is extremely kind and helpful, the workload is manageable (1 pset per week, 2 exams, 1 group assignment), the exams are very very fair (there are no surprises at all on the exams). It's definitely one of the easier 400-level Econ classes, but the material is still interesting. Actually, if you've taken ECON 409 (Firms, Markets, and Competition) before, then there is some overlap between the 2 classes when it comes to the mathematical models, but you're learning much simpler versions of the models in this class (ECON 414), and you're applying them to scenarios related to IP and innovation. I didn't find all of the models super interesting, maybe because I recently took ECON 409 and Game Theory, so there was some overlap/repetition for me, but I think the other cool topics of this class (patents, copyrights, licensing, technological trends, etc.), the fact that the class was low-stress, and the fact that the professor was so great, outweighed any of the cons. I would definitely recommend this class!
