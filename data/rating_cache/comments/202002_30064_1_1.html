2020-Summer AMST S372 01/ER&M S297 01 Food,Race&MigrationinUSSociety

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30071/30064 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 I found the class to be a bit lengthy, and thought the presentations at the beginning of class ran a bit long. However, I really appreciated section as my TA and my peers were very engaging.
 Although taught through Zoom, this class was structured in a way that facilitated easy learning. Classes started with engaging student presentation on various food topics, followed by a break, then a lecture portion with slides, and then a breakout room session with our TAs. The assigned readings as well discussion section activities and discussion questions kept us focused on the week's topics and important concepts. It was easy to ask for help and share thoughts.
 I enjoyed how the class was split into a lecture and discussion section. I also really enjoyed the presentations by students throughout the course.
 I really loved almost everything about this course! The TFs and professor were amazing and facilitated great discussions. The topics discussed were very interesting and needed as well, but I feel like the content of the course could have been diversified just a little more, but this is probably due to the fact that summer session is so short.
 The student presentations were sometimes not as engaging, but our presentations were a great way to discuss mini topics related to the broader course themes. The format of the class was engaging, with section held at the end of class. Personally, some of the readings (e.g. the pineapple one, cultivating food justice) were not as engaging as others (Holmes, Jayaraman, Pachirat, and Twitty) but all of the assigned readings were useful in helping me deepen my understanding of the class themes. The professor and TAs were wonderful and I truly learned so much! Using Zoom comments was also a helpful part of discussion.
 The strengths were the readings, which were very interesting overall. The weakness was the amount of time devoted to long-winded student presentations.
 Well, I wish the course hadn't been online, but it is what it is. I think that overall, the structure of having lectures and smaller discussions was great. It felt rushed and there was a lot of reading, but that's what a summer course entails.
 This course was thoughtfully organized to encourage student participation and well paced. It is challenging to keep the attention of the class for three hours via zoom, but I liked how each class period was split up among lecture, student presentations, and section discussion. That said, I think giving students the freedom to do things like watching films or conducting activities outside of class as a way to shorten the meeting time would have been helpful stamina-wise.
 the class was very interesting and insightful overall and I do not think the class had any real weaknesses. I think the class would have been better as a small seminar just because there tended to be a lot of people who did not speak at all throughout the short semester and I felt this would sometimes lead to awkward pauses which would make discussion a bit difficult at times.
 I loved the large lectures and the smaller breakout rooms. I think if there were more activities in the large discussion so there was more participation that would be good.
 Strengths: The classes were very interactive and I was engaged throughout them. I also found the assignments to be very fulfilling and fun to complete. Weaknesses: Sometimes sections felt rushed as there was so much to discuss in the time allotted for them.
 Strengths: The readings were very well chosen and thought-provoking (and very manageable!). Participation-based reading responses let me organize my thoughts about the material in a productive way. Section discussions were very robust, and were the highlight of class for me. Weaknesses: the lecture material felt a little basic, and I don't think it added much to our critical discussions. Because this ended up being more of a lecture-style class, we spent an hour or more each class period listening to presentations. While I do think the material offered in those presentations was important and interesting, I don't feel like that was an effective use of our time. I feel like we could have been better served by making the presentation groups larger (~4 a group perhaps) to cut down on the presentation time in order to make more time for class participation activities like discussion section.
 This course was particularly enjoyable since it gave students the opportunity to engage with content through a diversity of mediums such as films as well as more academic research. I think it could be improved by spending more time investigating some of the solutions to the problems we investigated. For instance, it would have been great to get insights into sustainable food production.
 Seriously so good. For a summer class too?! No complaints. Great to have class broken into several smaller sections that help made 3 hours on zoom much more manageable.
 This class is very engaging and asks for students to think about the food they eat, how they cook, how they eat out, how they shop, and how they have access to food. It allows the students to discuss, to think critically about a basic part of our life, and I learned so much. It was an amazing class.
 The course included a good balance of lectures, student participation, and discussion sections. I liked that the the lectures included a variety of media that made them more interactive, like videos and polls.
 The course was conducted very well. The lectures were very engaging and I enjoyed how professor Tran created an interactive format over zoom even in such a large class. The only weakness was that the expectations for graded work was kind of unclear.
 The class's subject was very interesting and the professors and the TFs were all very good. The class was 3 hours long, which I think is too long because it is simply not possible to focus for that long in one sitting.
