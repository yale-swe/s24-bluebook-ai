 Definitely!
 I would absolutely recommend this course in its current iteration. That being said, I feel that a large part of what made this course so fantastic is Winston, the professor. As the course currently stands, I think it is unique among the offerings in the S&DS department in that the main focus is understanding techniques of causal inference and their assumptions, then critiquing and discussing the limitations of their application, often by reading journal articles/newspaper articles that rely on them. I feel WAY more comfortable and equipped reading these types of articles after this class. Winston is incredibly well-read and extremely thoughtful about what he assigns and the questions he asks. He also gave great feedback on homework assignments and class discussions, even recommending readings based on your individual interests. Winston is such a gem in the S&DS department, and so is this course as taught by him. Winston is moving to another institution next year, and I honestly have no idea what the next iteration of the class will look like. I hope it continues to be offered, because it is one of my highlights of the major.
