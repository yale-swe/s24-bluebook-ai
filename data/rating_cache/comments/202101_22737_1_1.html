2021-Spring RLST 117 01 What Didn't Make It into Bible

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

 Strengths: well-organized, diverse content, excellent and flexible and kind teaching team. Weaknesses: Because it was a survey course, sometimes the transition into different topics felt a little disjointed/sudden, although I don't think this was the fault of the teaching team.
 The biggest strength is the reading list and how interesting the material is. The infrequent but interesting lecture are also a strength. One major weakness is that the syllabus is absolutely stuffed, and some classes could have really used more time to allow for fuller discussions.
 Professor Doerfler deeply cares for her students, engages them in conversation while making timely interventions and mini-lectures to further the class' knowledge. One of the best professors I've had at Yale.
 Keeping students involved was definitely a strength, as conversation was a constant in the class.
 A strength of this course is that there are lecture components, but the core of the course is a seminar. The teaching staff were incredibly helpful and gave great feedback. A weakness of the course might be how quickly we move from readings, which get difficult to keep straight after halfway through the course.
 Professor Doerfler was an amazing teacher. It is clear that she cares a lot about her students, their education, and the material of the course. She puts a lot of work into the class and is very available to talk to about papers (and to talk in general). I think she does a great job of pulling out the interesting questions and ideas from the class (I'm saying this as a STEM major with no background or specific interest in religious studies). I think there is also a good balance between lecture and discussion (the class is definitely mostly discussion), which makes the class welcoming to people without a lot of background in the subject. Probably the one thing I didn't like about the class was Perusall, which was a website through which we can post comments and annotations about the readings. I don't feel like I gained a lot from the comments, and it seemed like the main goal was to make sure that we did the reading. For me, a way to make sure we did the reading that would be more useful/that I would gain more from would be to have a question to respond in 4 or 5 sentences to before each class.
 Strengths: A lot! Professor Doerfler was extremely helpful and just a really amazing professor overall. Despite the constraints of online learning, I always looked forward to going to class. I also found the readings to be really interesting and fun, so I thoroughly enjoyed doing the readings for this class. Weaknesses: I was intimidated by the papers since I am not a humanities major, so the stress of writing those made the course slightly less enjoyable. Maybe more instruction on how to write a paper would have been helpful. Also, I would have loved to talk about the Old Testament more, though I know that would not be possible given how much is covered in this class.
 The primary strength of this course is Professor Doerfler. She is INCREDIBLE and truly one of the best professors I've had at Yale. She did a fantastic job of making each class engaging and worthwhile, and even in this weird virtual semester, I looked forward to every class.
 The readings are all very interesting, as is the subject matter of the course. Professor Doerfler, Christopher, and Julia all gave extremely detailed feedback on the papers, which was very helpful in writing future papers. Professor Doerfler is also a very warm and welcoming human being, and taking this class with her was a delight. She was always available to meet and give advice.
 Strength! Definitely Engagement level! Online is a very difficult place to pay attention but this class constantly had me engaged. While a strength for me it could be a weakness for others but this class is very discussion-heavy. I do feel like this helped me learn from my other classmates and just really love the class.
 Strengths: The Professor and TF's really made this course shine. Their dedication to the course, expertise, and ability to make all the course content so interesting and relevant created an amazing learning environment. The other students in the seminar also contributed to the strengths of this course-- because there were people from such a wide variety of backgrounds, conversations were always extremely interesting, and I really felt that I learned a lot. Weaknesses: none. Really.
 The diverse range of readings was really helpful. I did not like that we had to make comments on the readings before every class. I enjoyed the breakout rooms, especially because they were kept the same throughout the entire semester.
