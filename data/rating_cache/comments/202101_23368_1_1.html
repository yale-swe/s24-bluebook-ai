2021-Spring CPSC 584 01/CPSC 484 01 IntroHumanComputerInteraction/Human-Computer Interaction

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 23271/23368 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 The course is extremely well-structured. Expectations for all assignments are clearly laid out from the start on the course webpage, along with detailed rubrics on Canvas. Students have opportunities to check in with teammates on project progress through breakout rooms on Zoom at the end of class. Instructors provide lightning-fast responses to student questions on Slack. Team projects are split into a series of milestone assignments, taking the pressure off that is usually there in a big project. Grading is extremely quick, and instructors provide detailed feedback.
 strengths: the teaching staff gave students a lot of opportunities to interact with other students weaknesses: lectures were a little dry
 I literally loved this course. The only improvement is that I wish it were in-person, but thats a function of the public health situation and not the professor or class.
 Overall the course does a great job introducing to students an under-covered important element of most computer science work.
 A lot of the tests were redundant (and very detached from real-world design processes) and grading was very arbitrary/unclear (random points docked off for poor reasons). HCI lessons and methods were dated and couldn't be executed in remote settings while we were discouraged to use software that would ease this process at certain parts. Projects were good, but detached from one another. HCI toolkit taught seemed inapplicable to majority of the projects.
 strengths: it's an easy easy gut. Take it with 323 or like 2 other CS electives weaknesses: boring af. There's barely any coding, and whatever coding there is is very specific frameworks that are shoved to the end of the semester. The groups are a toss up: my first group had a pretty annoying person and my second group was ok. This is not a serious class; take it if you'd like to pay attention with half an ear
 It was a great class and I loved how project-oriented it was, fostering teamwork
 I wish that the course went a bit deeper into the latest trends and developments in HCI. I had some previous experience in user experience design and didn't feel like the lectures taught me a lot. The projects were interesting, and working in assigned groups made for an interesting challenge that is otherwise rare at Yale.
 Lecture material is interesting. However, I wish there were more ways to interact with the material. This would probably be easier with in person teaching, as a large part of this course is about groupwork--working with both your team of designers and users.
 Strengths: Prof. Vazquez runs super engaging lectures, you are never bored and always learn something. Overall, I also thought the group projects were designed and spaced perfectly, so that we weren't rushing or cramming work but were able to complete them and learn something in the process. Weaknesses: There was a very long midterm that ended up taking me 6-8 hours, and was more of another project or problem set, but with limited time. That was a bit unfortunate, mostly because it was all creative design work that is difficult to rush.
 This course is probably one of the better CPSC electives I have taken at Yale. The strengths are numerous. Firstly, Marynel is an amazing professor. The course content is fascinating and the lectures are not just boring monologues but rather discussions, interactive videos and games. The guest lectures in the class were truly instructive and the amount of structure in the class is immaculate. Marynel makes sure everything is clear, from the course websites to weekly reminders of what is due and tips to complete assignments. The projects are really hands-on and cement understanding. The only weakness of the class is perhaps the complexity of the second project which requires people to either know some amount of web development or to learn the basics quickly.
 I enjoyed how we had the second project added to the course. However, it did feel a bit rushed. I wish we had more time to implement it from the start. If we had more time, we could have put more effort into it and flushed our implementation more. I also wish we could have been able to upload our code ourselves to the CEID display to help test more instead of going through the TFs. However, the TFs were very helpful and were willing to do it when asked. Also, since we weren't really tested much on the lecture material (we used it occasionally for assignments), I felt less motivated to pay attention as the semester went on.
 The course was great at building on itself throughout the semester. We went through all the material by talking about it and engaging in discussion, putting it into practice, and receiving feedback.
 A definite strength of this course is the two group projects. They reinforce the key concepts of design process while still allowing room for creativity. The deadlines were well-spaced to give enough time to make unsuccessful attempts without too much stress. There was also ample opportunity during regularly-scheduled class time to work on the projects. A weakness of the class was the fact that everything had to be remote. I would have enjoyed the coursework far more with in person demos - but this flaw was unavoidable.
