2021-Spring ENGL 450 01 Daily Themes

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 20191 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 One of the strengths of this course is that students get meetings with one of the amazing tutors to receive feedback and discuss the themes produced each week. This is the only class I've taken at Yale so far that incorporates 1-on-1 meetings for feedback, which has been extremely helpful and provided deeper insight on my writing than could the normal mode of feedback for writing classes. Another great thing about Daily Themes is that it's taught by Mark Oppenheimer, who is not only an excellent lecturer but is also energetic and enthusiastic. His teaching never disappoints, and is one of the classes that I actually look forward to attending (and I took this class virtually, so that's quite impressive!).
 I really liked the podcast format, since it makes virtual learning a lot more interesting. In fact, I found the podcast format easier to pay attention to than any in-person English lecture I've had at Yale.
 Strengths: tutor relationship, podcast lectures (a great break from staring at screens), interesting conversation. Weaknesses: Wish that in-person lectures were a little more interactive, and, though most of the lectures felt relevant to the craft of writing, a few left me scratching my head about what I could take away from them.
 This was the most worthwhile class I took at Yale. I would definitely take during your junior or senior year - when you have more life experience to write a theme a day. The only suggestion I have for improvement would be for more themes to be revisions of prior themes
 The highlight of this course are the tutors. It is so special to be able to meet with your tutor and discuss your writing with them in-depth, every week, and to get to know them. There are so few classes at Yale that give you this level of individualized attention to help you improve. The tutors are all such interesting, engaging, and passionate people who genuinely wish to see you improve and offer such practical feedback to make you improve. The other strength of this course is the fact that the themes are not graded and the work is graded as a collective. This really reduces the academic pressure of this class and allows for experimentation and risk-taking that a strict grading regimen often prohibits. I really wish that all classes at Yale were graded and assessed this way, rather than putting the weight on particular assignments. And finally, the last strength of this course is the fact that it is daily themes - making us write daily, while can be stressful alongside other classes' work, really lets you get into the habit of not romanticizing writing as well as making sure you are practicing writing consistently. I really appreciated the opportunity to take our Daily Themes work and then translate that into a longer, two-week piece at the end of the semester as well. This was an experience that really made me ponder and reflect on the stories I wanted to write and craft. I would say the only weakness of this course is that sometimes, the prompts can be a bit repetitive or rather cliche, which makes them a bit boring to write about.
 The main strength of the course is simply writing once a day. It also benefits from great podcasts and tutors. The main weakness was the revamped lecture, which were mostly engaging and fun, but occasionally (especially towards the end of the semester) became professor Oppenheimer and the tutors discussing books that I think most students had never read/heard of.
 Great course! Would totally recommend if you like creative writing. I got slightly burned out at the end from churning out something everday, but I never stopped enjoying it.
 I thought the pre-recorded lectures were great, but the live virtual lectures were a bit dull. Working one on one with the tutors is a fantastic experience though because they really get to know your writing. I loved my tutor, and she helped me make significant improvements in my writing.
 The strength of the course is undoubtedly the weekly meetings with a tutor. This was an excellent way to reflect on my writing.
 Strengths: The weekly meetings with the writing tutor were so helpful. As someone with no workshop background, having a set time each week where someone gives feedback on your pieces gave me the opportunity to work on my writing style, without being overshadowed by others in a group setting. Also, the daily assignments keep you accountable; some days are worse than others, but everyday I tried to use the feedback from last week in my new pieces. I also thought the podcast lectures were a good way to split up the lectures online. Weaknesses: Some of the weekly lectures had less actionable advice than I would have liked; workshopping a specific piece (maybe with the Word Review tool) would have been nice.
 A strength was that the work was very flexible and could be as personal or impersonal as the writer saw fit; you made the class what you wanted it to be. A weakness is that we didn't get to spend as much time on the different aspects of writing each week than I think they deserved, but it was a nice introduction into them anyway.
 Strengths: the premise of writing every day, Mark, podcast lectures, final short story assignment (voluntary) Weaknesses: tutor meetings are kinda useless because they address super micro aspects of your work without granting any advice on how to grow as a writer, live lectures offered little, prompts sometimes felt restrictive, I found the length of the pieces frustrating (would have preferred to work on longer pieces every day than create individual 300-word blurbs), workload
 I really struggled with the format of this coursewhich I recognize may be more a me problem than anything to do with the course. Basically, I found the lectures to be super thematic, but with little practical writing advice. My favorite lecture was from a guest lecturer who spoke about metaphor; I found it helpful because in addition to giving examples, which the normal podcast lectures were chock full of, it gave tips and tricks for implementing metaphor into your writing. I think the tutor idea was good in theory, but I felt that 30 minutes was too short for how much we wrote a week. I also often felt like I wasn't sure what I was supposed to say to my tutor after they gave their feedback, since there was a lack of continuity from theme to theme.

But as much as I'd like to be free of my GPA, I'm not. Would have really appreciated a bit more clarity about where I stood at the mid-semester mark rather than just 'doing a pretty solid job'.
