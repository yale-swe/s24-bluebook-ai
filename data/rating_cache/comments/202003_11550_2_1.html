2020-Fall SCIE 010 01 BiologicalResearchPerspectives

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 11550 Term: 202003

 Strengths: The groups were very helpful to have as they made the assignments less daunting. The course was very relevant and engaging. Dean Chang is a wonderful professor who is always willing to help and offer guidance. Weaknesses: It was sometimes hard to grasp the harder concepts in certain research papers we read.
 I think that one big strength for this course was the fact that much of it was guided by the students, and especially the discussions that arose when students asked questions. There was so much to be curious about in this course, and those questions definitely helped the entire class tackle challenges that were fascinating. Another big strength was that each individual group presentation solidified understanding of the paper and helped answer burning questions we may have had. One weakness of the course is that because p-sets were due before the presentations, sometimes, it was challenging to come up with an answer when we didn't know exactly what it was before having seen the specific group/expert presentation.
 Strengths: Amazing class where I learned so much about the research surrounding the pandemic! It is so, so pertinent - you will be discussing about vaccines one week and seeing vaccines released another week! Dean Chang is such an exciting lecturer and makes you excited about science. Weaknesses: A lot of work for a first-year seminar! The p-sets are challenging (but manageable) and Dean Chang challenges you by giving real, cutting-edge research papers rather than watered-down material. Presentations and writing the grant proposal are rewarding but stressful and work-intensive.
 What I loved about this course is being engaged in the latest COVID-19 literature and other latest biotechnology research. It helps me discover what I would want to research in the future. I would say that the only weakness about the course is the lack of a five minute break to go drink water or use the bathroom. Often times, I think that my lack of focus in the class stems from the two hour period without an interim period to process the information in the presentations or to get a snack or water.
 A weakness is that the course should provide more guidance on where to research procedures and methods for scientific techniques or tests run in labs. It seemed as if the professor expected students to be familiar with certain obscure procedures at times.
 The class is very engaging and Dean Chang is an amazing person! You will learn a lot about molecular biology concepts and techniques (and Covid). The class is organized so that everyone will get the opportunity to talk and share which is really nice for enriching discussions. Note that this class will most likely be filled with premeds and future grad students which can be intimidating though this makes the class only much more enriching as you will definitely learn a lot from your peers and be amazed at their breadth of knowledge.
 One of the strengths is that a lot of the learning you do about scientific papers is solidified in class because a group presents a paper every week. One of the weaknesses is that there isn't a lot of room for discussion outside of commenting on presentations because the majority of the classes are centered around a group presenting.
 Strengths: creates an excellent foundation for experience of what it's like to be a scientist. Very welcoming environment to facilitate discussion and exchange of ideas, especially on challenging and technical topics. No weaknesses!
 Amazing course! Would highly recommend
 The strength of this course is how engaged it is. There were only 21 of us, and everyone was very knowledgeable about biological sciences. This made our conversations very stimulating and we were able to discuss coronavirus and its pathology at a very high level. The weakness of this course was that it was kind of uncertain what Dean Chang wanted from us at the beginning, but as we went further in the semester it became clearer. Whenever we did an assignment for the first time, everyone in the class was slightly confused and asked each other questions. We also strayed from the syllabus and that caused confusion about deadlines and the work we were doing.
 I think the work for the class is highly valuable and gives essential skills to students regarding research and growth as scientists. I think the 2 hour lectures can at times be tedious but I think it is exceptionally valuable to be taken to grow as a student and researcher.
 One strength was the way we delved deep into the subject of COVID-19 research while learning many important biological techniques on the periphery along the way. One weakness, which may also be labeled as a strength in some ways, was the overly presentation-focused nature of the class. A potential improvement would be to allow more open discussion on topics.
 I think that the course was very well suited for the circumstances.
