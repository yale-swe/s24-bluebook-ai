2020-Fall ENGL 474 01 The Genre of the Sentence

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 10248 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: -the availability of the professor to meet in office hours -the detailed comments on our pieces -the sharing of pieces during class Possible areas of improvement: -It is nice listening to Verlyn read, but I think it would also be nice to hear students read their own pieces out loud.
 This class was a bright spot in my weeks. The structure of the class is great - I liked being able to choose what to write every week, and 2 pages is just enough to dive into a topic. Even over Zoom, the class maintained a supportive atmosphere. I liked listening to pieces being read aloud, especially those written by my peers (though it may be neat for the author to read their pieces too!). I also really liked revising the "worst" sentence in each person's piece. Since we break down every piece so much, though, each person only ends up having their piece workshopped by the class once or twice - you have to go to office hours to get the most out of this class.
 Strengths of this class include the small class size and the class format, which consisted of readings of both outside authors and classmates work. My only regret is that we did not meet often enough.
 I liked its chillness- you just write and talk about writing. It did sometimes feel a bit unstructured but thats not necessarily a bad thing.
 Verlyn is a lovely man and an amazing teacher. I really appreciated the routine of the course we wrote around two pages every week, read our classmates and a sample essay, and after 12 weeks, we were simply done. No final paper worth 40% of our grade that changed our experience of the course into a solitary one. I loved the rhythm of thinking and writing and coming together, and it helped me learn a lot while not even feeling I was working too hard Id sit down at the end of the week with my journal-like brainstorming google docs and realize Id been thinking about the class all week. Very few classes at yale achieve that.
 Had this class been in person, I feel that we wouldve been able to cultivate an even better class dynamic. The strengths of this class were in the workshop every week where we were able to apply what weve learned in the class on a technical level.
 No weaknesses. Strengths were a thriving and kind community, lots of personalized feedback from the instructor, great readings, small, intimate class setting, clear learning goals.
 Strengths: Pretty much everything. The biggest strength is Professor Klinkenborg, who is an absolutely wonderful professor, a skilled writer, and an even better person. I also think the course is structured really well and the weekly writing pieces and weekly reading for class both worked quite effectively. The feedback was really effective in moving my writing forward, and I felt that focusing on writing new pieces rather than emphasizing revising was a smart move for the course and for writer development. Weaknesses: None (sorry!)
 The greatest strengths of the course is the weekly feedback on our writing assignments and the discussion in class. I was able to expose myself to different ways of writing through class what was good and what wasn't good. In addition, Professor Klinkenborg's line by line edits of our weekly work and openness to discussion during office hours was very helpful for understanding my own writing practices. There are no weaknesses!
 The strengths: you're writing the same amount every week. Verlyn very much leaves the seminar in the hands of the students in it.
 strengths -class zoom drive where we all shared our work each week (very fulfilling to read everyone's pieces each week even if they weren't discussed) -class scrapbook drive to build unity -very available for office hours each week -anonymous bad sentences discussion each week to think about mechanics of sentences -weekly chances to write short pieces and receive regular and clear feedback weaknesses - i feel that the course size (bigger than 16 so we didn't all fit on one page) made the class feel a little more anonymous and less seminar-like. some faces blended together and I would've appreciated a slightly smaller class and maybe more time in the beginning to get to know each other so that the faces have people for other classmates
 The Professor was very fair and gave wonderful feedback. He was always present for office hours and really encouraged line editing.
 The only weakness is that you don't get to have your piece workshopped in class very often, but it's fun to talk through the prose of your classmates.
 Strengths- the format of writing 2 pages each week was a struggle but a great one. You write even when you think you have nothing to say. You learn to read and reread and edit. The careful line-edits from Verlyn taught me so much about tiny nuances in rhythm to big structural things that I did fairly badly at the start of the term. I learned so much from this combo of big-picture as well as granular thinking/editing. I loved the pieces we read in class; they were diverse, provoking, and showed us what was possible for the format. Office hours were insanely helpful you can talk to verlyn about anything, and it really helps you get past brick walls! Weaknesses- I struggled a lot in this course, but it was a good thing. There were many weekends where I hated what I wrote and started from scratch 2-3x. But the more and more you learn to lean into the process, the more you learn. Dont get too stuck in your head; just try things. Verlyn is incredibly kind and will help guide you through the troughs of sorrow. One thing I actually would have liked to read more poetry in the class. The scrapbooking idea Verlyn came up with was cute. It was kind of hard to stay on the bandwagon but I really appreciated seeing these little visual glimpses into peoples lives (and pics of their pets!)
 This course is incredible. I loved the standing assignment and the freedom to write whatever we want, I loved the readings that accompanied it, and I loved the community built by this class. I loved the attention Prof Klinkenborg gave to each of us, and I really felt cared for. I loved how much discussion there was between everyone in the class, and I loved reading out the bad sentences as well. No weaknesses about this class at all :)
 Strengths: Freedom!!! So much freedom to let our creativity and TRUE written voices come out. Also, Professor Klinkenborg is exceptional. Weaknesses: Also freedom!!! It can be a bit overwhelming, having no guidelines for our two pages each week. But as I mention above, it's probably worth it. I think this year it was hard because my inner voice was often influenced by the sadness and negativity in the world... sometimes (in my opinion) having a prompt or guideline would have provided me a necessary nudge AWAY from what was hijacking my mind when I was trying to think of new paper topics. I also wish that everyone got at least one chance for their piece to be peer reviewed (some got pieces reviewed multiple times, some, not at all). I think it's a really special experience to have a piece discussed by the class, and all of us deserve that chance!
