Â Strengths: Professor Valis is a strength in the sense that she imbues the class with her appreciation of Lorca's works. The syllabus includes works by Lorca in several mediums, including poetry and plays (as the title would suggest), but also an essay. There are also secondary commentaries, as well as other works relating to Lorca's works or commenting on Lorca himself (including short films like Buuel's Un perro andaluz). These works provide additional perspective on what we read and I think were carefully-chosen. Professor Valis also makes herself available to students and is happy to meet and discuss the works. The major assignments included three short (2-3 page) and a final paper. Weaknesses: Participation was uneven (with some students speaking often each class and others almost not at all the whole semester). I'm not sure if this was because the class was large (though I'm really, really glad the class wasn't capped; everyone should get the opportunity to study these works if they'd like) or if it was a function of being on Zoom. There were some instances that enabled participation, including assigning stanzas to students and having each student speak, as well as breakout rooms. Additionally, I'd add that there weren't many moments that involved critique of Lorca (for example, Romancero gitano centers partly on the Romani people, but as an aesthetic and thematic element. We briefly touched on this, as a student brought it up in discussion, but otherwise, I don't think we would have commented on it). The syllabus was strong, but sometimes we weren't able to discuss some of the works we read.
