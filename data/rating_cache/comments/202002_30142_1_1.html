2020-Summer GLBL S343E 01/PLSC S270E 01 Disinformation and Democracy

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30141/30142 Term: 202002

 Strengths - very well organized, interesting, draws on real-world and modern examples, very easy to contact the professor and TF with questions or concerns, facilitated strong discussions Weaknesses - besides readings and lecture videos, there were not a lot of notes provided that organized all of the information that would be discussed in each class meeting.
 The strengths are that the content is very relevant to the world we are living in and it is very interesting. The conversations held in class are also extremely thought-provoking. The only thing that I think some people would consider is a weakness is that some of the content about Russia and the 2016 election was repetitive.
 Strengths: Engaging. Clear. Easy-to-follow. Not difficult if you organize your readings. Plus, she is a former FBI agent focused on counterintelligence! Weaknesses: Sometimes readings are long, but the content definitely makes up for it.
 Strengths - Professor and TF were awesome. The media bubble analysis assignments were great. Weakness - the "seminar" had 26 people. the syllabus was pretty dense for a 5 week course.
 I feel that being online course greatly affected the course, but it was very informative and I looked forward to every class.
 Strengths: the media bubbles were very insightful, helped me be aware of trends and vulnerabilities that Russian disinformation tactics often exploit, and practice learned media literacy skills. I appreciated all the additional documentaries and videos we watched!! Weaknesses: Just a lot of readings! While some were very interesting (esp. Mistakes were Made but Not By Me) but others felt really repetitive. However, this might've felt like a lot just because of the short time frame.
 This course was extremely well organized. The readings were all great and very interesting. The pre-lecture videos were a good way to prepare for class, and the live discussions themselves were generally very engaging. I thought the structure of the assignments were good as well - the media bubbles allowed for "easier" weeks in which to engage further with the readings and also think about the big paper at the end. Though the policy paper in the last week felt like a bit of a crunch, it was actually pretty easy to write given all that I had learned and read.
 The course was good in its entirety. Be it the content, the assignment or peoples contributions. However, I feel that the discussion could have flown better if there were break rooms and activities on zoom since it was a large class of 25 people.
 This was a very strong course with great guest speakers, useful Zoom conversations and a thoughtfully curated reading list. It was well designed and focused, and I really enjoyed it. This was the best online course I've taken, and Prof. Rangappa's guidance was a large part of that - Yale should consult with her on how to run an effective online seminar now that online courses are the norm. If I had to identify a weakness it would be the global critique I have of Yale courses that have a final paper - while we had very detailed constructive feedback throughout the course, there was no feedback on the final paper. I know this calls for extra work on the instructor's part, but I always find feedback extremely helpful and would have appreciated it on the final paper.
 Strengths: great professor and TF, brought in guests, did their best with online format Weakness: online format doesn't really work
 Strengths - Highly relevant topic, so so crucial to understanding the changing landscape of politics of the world. The TA and students were thoughtful. Weakness - This course should be called "Russian disinformation & American democracy" since it was obsessed with Russian active measures in the US and did not spend time acknowledging or discussing threats to democracy from other sources. Moreover Professor Rangappa did not encourage disagreement with the material or with her own beliefs. It was a very singular way of analyzing a global phenomenon.
 I thought that the class and the professor had mostly strengths, with very few weaknesses. Professor Rangappa was extremely engaged with the class, she always did her best to answer questions and facilitate discussions. I think that like the other classes I have taken, there are certain limitations imposed by the Zoom platform, and the learning curve thereof, but overall it was a highly informative course, and I very much enjoyed learning from Professor Rangappa. The TA, Juan Carlos Salamanca, was also very engaged with the class, and frequently contributed with his own knowledge, and was very helpful visa vis questions about assignments and general course information.
 The strengths are discussing these issues with an expert who is passionate in the topic.
