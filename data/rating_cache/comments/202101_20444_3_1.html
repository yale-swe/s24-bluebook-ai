 I would recommend it. Definitely be deliberate about taking it: while the readings are relatively short, and there are only three essays (no exams or anything), it takes a significant amount of time to write a good essay in this class, both because the content is so abstract and also because the grading is very rigorous. And speaking of the grading, because I know it gets a very ~interesting~ reputation: I would say that it is more difficult than most other classes, but it's not apocalyptic. At the beginning of the semester, Shelly told us that, in an average year, 20-30% of students get an A or A-, 50-60% get a B+, B, or B-, 10-20% get a C+, C, or C-, and a small percentage of students get a D or F. So the grading is not ideal, but it is not terrible. The class content, though, is absolutely fascinating. Not only did I learn about ethics, but I feel like I learned a whole new way of thinking, one that has definitely spilled over to help me in other classes, and one that has enriched the way I experience life in general. So understand that the class might be a bit stressful, but don't let the grading scare you away, because it was definitely worth it. One other thing that I wished I knew before I took the class: don't worry too much about the readings. Definitely do them if you can, but don't bother taking notes, or anything like that. Shelly does a really good job of explaining everything in class, way better than the original authors. I knew that the grading in this class was difficult, so I put a ton of time and effort into the readings early in the semester, but I eventually realized that that really wasn't necessary at all, so don't fall into the same trap that I did!
