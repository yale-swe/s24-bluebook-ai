2021-Spring WGSS 032 01 History of Sexuality

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 20903 Term: 202101

 I enjoyed the discussion format, especially sharing our emotions before class.
 This course only has strengths! I could go on and on about how amazing this course was, even in a remote learning environment. I think by far the biggest strength is Professor Trumpler herself. She is so wise and caring. Every discussion with her was incredible, and she has a way of drawing out the best in each and everyone one of us. She was always providing some bit of historical context or a new perspective on a reading that would just completely change my entire view. She was also so so SO accommodating and understanding. Before every class, we would have a little check-in to say what we felt about the readings, and having a class where what I felt mattered was so important for me, especially as a remote student. She was also so understanding of what was going on, and she was always willing to give an extension or offer pass/fail in order to make things easier for us. She is such an amazing human being and I wish I could've met her and had class with her in person. The other strength was just the class environment itself. Everyone was so insightful and engaged; every discussion I felt I was learning so much from my classmates. It was great to be able to form that little community even through a remote setting. Also the content is AMAZING! We read texts that traced the beginning of sexology like Krafft-Ebing and Malinowksi. We also read Freud and Foucault, which prompted one of the most incredible discussions ever. Towards the end of the class, we got to more modern works, particularly ones that dealt with science and sexuality, and reading Undoing Monogamy is incredible! That book will just blow your mind! Also the workload was never overwhelming. Again, Prof T is so understanding and really wants to make the class a space for learning and sharing. She also helps you so much when it comes to writing your papers, and I have become a better writer because of it. NO WEAKNESSES!
 I think this course is amazing. The professor is amazing. The readings are well chosen, and the discussion is extremely engaging.
 The frequently and lengthy readings for each class made it difficult to remain engaged in the information because of short amount of time offered to properly digest information.

 Absolutely love Professor Trumpler. The readings could be a bit dense and overwhelming in the beginning, but the seminars make everything worthwhile because prof trumpler and your peers will help dissect the text during class.
 strengths: well-organized, readings were extremely engaging and interesting, discussions were thought-provoking and illuminating, workload was not too laborious and felt useful and fulfilling how it could be improved: only one comment- the fact that the class met Tuesday and Thursday meant that it was very manageable to complete the readings due Tuesday, but readings due Thursday were more difficult to complete because there are only two days between Tuesday and Thursday. I'm not sure if this has already been considered, but maybe it would be possible to assign shorter readings due Thursday compared to the ones due Tuesday, especially if there are readings assigned for both Tuesday and Thursday.
