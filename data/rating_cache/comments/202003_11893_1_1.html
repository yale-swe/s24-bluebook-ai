2020-Fall ER&M 231 01/HIST 232 01 Hitler, Stalin, and Us

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401 CRN: 15567/11893 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 This course introduces you to the writings of Marx and Lenin to begin study of communism. Then the course examines Stalin's Soviet Union and communism in practice. The course closes the study of communism with selected readings from former communists describing their journey to disenchantment. Similarly, the course began the study of fascism with academic papers attempting to do just that. You will find that it is difficult to define fascism. From there, you will study Hitler's personal ideology which shaped his brand of fascism. The key insight this course provides is an extension to modern day via discussion of the legacy of Hitler and Stalin in the 2010s.
 Historical analysis of some of the overlooked features of Eastern Europe, ranging from the 1800s to the present
 Learned an amazing amount about world war two, eastern Europe, and colonialism. Also learned about the Fortunoff Archive at Yale and how to use it -- it is truly remarkable.
 I learned how to write better. Simply because of the amount of writing as well as TA feedback.
 This course will teach you a lot of information on the subject matters of Hitler, Stalin and their contemporary legacies. It will also offer important and immensely interesting insights into the nature of historical practice, methodology and writing.
 I've learnt about 20th century history before, but Snyder's take on things is totally different to what you might have learnt in high school. You learn to absorb a lot of material quickly and think critically about what you're learning constantly because of the intensive writing component.
 The weekly assignments were helpful to strengthen analytical/writing skills. The topics and historical concepts discussed in lecture and in the readings were new and at times challenging. In addition to material about Hitler and Stalin, this course focused on broader approaches to and applications of history.
 I learned about the ideology behind Nazism and Stalinism and how they apply both in history and today.
 Writing/Editing skills through the 12 essays and 6 revised essays assignments; understanding of the legacies of empire and colonialism in Europe, Russia, and America; historical perspective Strengths: extremely engaging course; the readings usually felt relevant to the course; plenty of opportunities for feedback
 I think I became better at handling heavy writing tasks, as well as at editing my own work. I learned so much about the connections between Stalin and Hitler, in addition to many universal themes in history that are applicable to other historical periods.
 I learned to synthesize primary and secondary sources to make weekly historical arguments. I learned extensively about the regimes of Hitler and Stalin, and why those regimes matter for today. I learned what it means to think and write like a historian.
 In this course I gained an understanding of both fascism as defined by Hitler, and communism defined by Stalin, how the ideologies were related, and how each played out in practice across the Eurasian continent in the early to mid-20th century.
 I learned a great deal about the history and rise of the Soviet Union. In particular, this included learning about the continuity and change from Marxism to Leninism, and eventually Stalinism. A key theme of the course, the rise and fall of empire, was a fascinating way to confront the rise of Hitler and Stalin.
 In this course, we were required to produce weekly essays based on primary and secondary sources, which was a challenging but rewarding exercise in itself. I learned to synthesize ideas from different sources and produce nuanced historical analysis.
 I learned an enormous amount about the experience of eastern Europe in WWII. My writing improved dramatically. I was given a very powerful insight into how history is essential to us in the modern day as a tool to counteract memory politics employed by populist movements.
 This class was wonderful. I learned how to become a better writer and critical thinker. I learned how to address difficult subject matter in a objective way. I dealt with very difficult subject matter but was able to handle it pretty effectively because of the teacher and the class itself. I was provided with clear feedback and was very in-tune with the topic. I learned about the true impact of Hitler and Stalin as well.
