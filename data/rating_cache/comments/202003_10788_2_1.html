Â More often than not, especially with the homework prompts, I felt that this was more of an art course than an architecture course. I wish it was more analytical than abstract.
Â Strengths: you can get by just fine without doing any of the readings and the assignments were fun to do. Weaknesses: there is no grading system. I knew this going in but thought that they would at least give us clues about our standing. They did no such thing (even after the final project, they had nothing to say about where we stood in the class). This means that your TA's will have complete power of your grade and if you are not on their good side then there's no system that you can rely on to ensure that you are being judged based off of your work. Additionally, how this class is organized is that there are lectures and section meetings once a week. Each week, you do an assignment that's discussed in the section. This section is just a time where the TA's openly (and sometimes in a very disrespectful manner), criticize your work. I think this class can be improved by ensuring that these TA's have the appropriate training on giving feedback. Another aspect of this class that does not make sense is that the weekly assignments are completely unrelated to the material covered in lectures and to previous assignments. This means that each week, you start from scratch and figure out what you have to do, then get criticized for it in sections. This made no sense to me at all. They also say that they put improvement into consideration when grading the class and god forbid if you do well early on, you could possibly be doomed for the rest of the semester because you have set the standard too high. In terms of the lectures, they were mostly pictures of different beautiful architectural figures that the professor liked and talked about. We had zoom classes where they played a prerecorded video to us. Because of this, there was NO engagement in the class and their proposed solution to this lack of engagement was to force us all to have our videos on (and we would be marked as absent if we did not have them on throughout). I don't believe that policing us so closely (especially in these times where not everyone has the same access to wifi) was inappropriate. Now, onto the biggest weakness of the class, it was extremely American-centric, which is fine. In fact, we sometimes had references from places in South America and Europe as well which is great. However, each time these places were mentioned, the Professor would go deep into where each place is located whereas anytime we had pictures from places in Africa, they were mostly referred to as "somewhere in Africa." I thought this was extremely wrong and hope that it can be corrected for the future versions of this class.
