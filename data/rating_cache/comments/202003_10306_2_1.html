Â Professor HoSang and the teaching assistants tried to be thoughtful about the way this class was carried out. They did make an effort to ensure that the workload was not excessive. Reading was kept to around one hundred pages per week, and our weekly assignments usually consisted of a reading response or being responsible for a section of the class. The class largely revolved around two assignments: one to design a curriculum unit and the other being a somewhat open-ended final paper. These were probably the highlights of the class, as we were given the freedom to focus on a topic of our choice. The teaching staff were quite thoughtful about pairing us with other students with similar interests, and did offer quite a bit of support for both of these projects. I will admit that this course fell short of my expectations. I was quite excited to take another class with Professor HoSang, having previously taken Race, Politics, and the Law. Knowing that Professor HoSang is a stellar instructor, many of the shortcomings of the class can probably be attributed to it being conducted via Zoom. Nevertheless, the class itself was structured quite strangely. For one, there were, in my opinion, too many students in the class to allow for any type of substantive discussion. While the teaching assistants did put in quite a bit of effort into the class, it often felt like they ran much of the discussion, making a bit harder to engage with Professor HoSang directly. The workload was also distributed quite poorly, and piled onto the last few weeks of classes. This proved to be slightly stressful, as we essentially spent most of the semester having little to no feedback on our progress. Class discussions were also arranged ahead of time (i.e. who was speaking). While this was helpful in allowing us to focus on a specific part of the reading, sometimes we would be asked to speak at length on a reading two days before class. This was a bit frustrating, and I feel like it would have been better to have free-flowing discussions instead of imposing an excessive amount of structure. While it was not downright terrible, the organization of the class could have been much better. Thematically, it often felt like our takeaways from each week did not vary much, and the insights I took away from this class pale in comparison to those from Race, Politics, and the Law.
