2021-Spring PLSC 465 01/GLBL 288 01 Civil-Military Relations

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 23585/23230 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 This was one of the best courses I've taken at Yale. One major strength was the breadth of the material we covered. I also appreciated the number of diverse case studies, which helped us to see CMR theories in action. I also found the structure of the writing assignments (in terms of the final paper drafts that build on one another) to be quite helpful. I did think that the number of weekly reading responses could be cut down. Also, I was very interested in other students' final papers after they had been presented. I think one additional assignment could have been to review another student's paper draft.
 The course was fantastically organized. Professor Lotito did a great job of making you understand exactly what to focus on as you were learning and doing the readings (through reading response questions that actually helped guide you). The syllabus and order of topics also does a really good job of bringing you into the topic of Civ-Mil Relations, helps you understand the field and background, and then has you dive into specifics for different regions. While the final assignment is long, it is broken up into component parts over the semester that are very accomplishable and was not a stressful exercise. There are honestly very few weaknesses in the course, though perhaps it would be nice to have slightly more chances to discuss historical civil-military relations (pre-WW2) which was never really brought up in the course. Then again, that might just be a better topic for a different class (that doesn't yet exist at Yale, sadly).
 I think one of the main strengths of the course is Professor Lotito. He is an expert on the topic, and does a really great job of guiding discussion and supporting students. The course is also structured really well and has nice thematic organization, so it was always very clear what the readings were going to be about, and what specific topics we would be discussing in class. I appreciated that work on the term paper was spread out throughout the semester, which allowed us to get regular feedback on our progress, and also alleviated some of the work towards the end of the semester. I think one potential weakness is that the course topic is very niche, and sometimes I felt like material got redundant at times, especially in the middle of the semester before we started case studies. I think the first half of the course could potentially be shortened so that we could have more time for case studies, which I found to be some of the most interesting parts of the course.
 The class was really well structured and and Professor Lotito was also great -- he was very accommodating (modified readings to make it easier on us, dropping the lowest reading responses, was very flexible with extensions). I also liked how the final was structured -- although it was 15 pages, there were smaller deadlines scattered throughout the semester so by the time the final approached I already had practically half the paper written, had thought about sources, etc. which made it a lot less stressful. The only weakness I can think of is that there weren't a lot of opportunities to get feedback on how we were doing -- only the first reading response or two had comments, and so if we ever didn't get full marks after that point it was unclear why or how to improve. We did get feedback on the paper assignments which was helpful, though.
 Would like it if office hours could also be on a different day from class. Sometimes something would be mentioned in class and I wouldnt have a question until a day later, but I would have to wait until next Wednesday for office hours (Id rather discuss things in person than try to explain over email). The professor did a great job facilitating discussion and was helpful when approached.
 I like that we worked on doing comparison tables together as a class. given the nature of the case studies, creating summary tables made sense, and also allowed me to interact with other students
 Strengths: The material is very interesting, and Professor Lotito is a great teacher -- our class discussions were always very engaging. Weaknesses: I would have appreciated a bit more flexibility on the weekly reading responses. They were sometimes difficult to fit in during the weeks when other writing assignments for the course were also due.
