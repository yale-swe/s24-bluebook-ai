Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

ID: SU100 CRN: 30105 Term: 201802

123Forward
 How to read scientific literature and how to write a scientific paper as well as good presentation skills.
 I learned how to discuss others scientific findings, present my own findings, and develop new hypothesis. Most importantly, I learned how to write a paper and present it for 15 minutes.
 I gained the ability to read primary scientific literature with understanding and conceptualization. I also learned how to better present research findings in a clear and concise manner.
 I learned how to read and present scientific journals.
 I learned the process of writing a scientific paper from beginning to end. This includes the first round of writing, and then how to edit the manuscript. I also learned in depth how to present my research to an audience that doesn't necessarily understand science.
 By taking this course, I received valuable skills regarding the presentation and writing aspects of scientific research. I learned how to properly write and format a scientific paper. I also learned how to formally present my work to a scientific community in a conference setting. Along with those skills, I also gained a better knowledge of how to read and analyze scientific articles.
 how to write a scientific paper - all parts of it - the abstract, background, intro, etc. I wasn't too familiar with how to properly do this beforehand. I also developed my public speaking skills - I'd been used to presenting memorized speeches, but this class helped me practice my delivery of content that I was familiar with yet didn't have memorized. I also learned of the process of research in general and what exactly it means to do research
 I learned how to perform, write about, and present scientific research. I learned how to manage my nerves while standing in front of small and large groups of people. The class also went into how to read primary scientific papers; I learned how to write my own scientific paper in IMRaD format.
 I gained valuable skills in presenting scientific research. I learned how to make effective and informative presentation slides. I also learned how to better make a scientific explanation using appropriate and professional diction specific to the field.
 I learned how to read scientific papers more in-depth and with a more critical eye, how to write a research paper with proper organization and wording, and how to present scientific data professionally. More specifically, we learned how to question others' presentation of data, how to organize data into results that are comprehensible to the public through statistical analysis and creation of figures, and how to create presentations that would lure in the audience through certain visual design elements and manner of speech.
 I learned how to write proficiently about what I was researching and how to visually present it.
 I learned how to communicate scientific research, both in oral and written form.
 This course taught me how to present my research findings in a way that was clear and concise. The class also taught us how to digest scientific literature.
 Writing and presenting my research, reading research papers
