Â Strengths: Overall, Professor Costas is very good at explaining the econometric concepts and his slides are pretty comprehensive. I also think the general flow of lecture was very understandable. The exams are fair (maybe bordering easy) and straightforward and grading is pretty generous. Weaknesses: First, there's a lot of work in this class -- imo there's too much to the point where it feels like I'm not learning anything and I'm just grinding away at the weekly homework or just trying to come up with some answer so that I can submit and just be done with it rather than having the time to really try and think through the questions. Tbh I think most of my learning from the psets came after the answer keys were posted, as opposed to while I was doing the psets which is suboptimal. Second, pacing of work in the class is terrible. There was a week that initially had a pset, project, and midterm all due (i.e. due dates Sun, Mon, Wed) and that's just way too much. However, the BIGGEST weakness by far in my opinion is the motivation of students for taking this course. This course follows Econ 117, and since only one econometrics class is required for the Econ major, the majority (probably close to all) of students taking this course are trying to write a senior thesis. Furthermore, most of the students are also seniors in the fall trying to take this course concurrently with writing their thesis. And so you have a significant (if not majority) chunk of students who legitimately don't care about this course at all and are just trying to get the requirement out of the way, and I think it makes for a terrible learning experience. Furthermore, the methods covered in the class aren't even relevant to a lot of thesis topics or are too basic to be of use for thesis analysis (and even if there was a particular method that was relevant to your thesis, the class will only spend 15 minutes on it anyways). As someone who was only 50% interested in the material, I felt like I was already one of the "most" interested students in the class and that's downright depressing, but I also can't really blame the students / seniors who have other things they're (rightly) more worried about. It's pretty sad when Professor Costas asks a really simple question (e.g. "Does anyone have any thoughts why we might have gotten this result?") and it's just silence for 5 minutes, punctuated by Professor Costas's occasional pleading for someone / anyone to just say anything until someone answers. Imho, the Econ department should just remove Econ 123 as a requirement for writing the thesis since it doesn't contribute any knowledge to the thesis writing experience and the differing incentives of students taking the class just overall make for an extremely subpar learning experience. That way, only people who are actually interested in the methods (which can be interesting!) will take the course instead of this hodge podge of students who don't want to be there / are just trying to complete their requirement.
