Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 Yes, the professor is great and the readings are great as well. Not too heavy reading load and the papers are very manageable.
 For any student looking for a writing credit who has no prior experience with poetry may feel a little bit daunted at times like I did but overall it was a great experience. I learnt a lot and grew as a writer. I also got to practice my French in section which is nice as a native speaker.
 Yes, for sure!! Prof Connolly is fantastic and a very engaging lecturer. The mad poets covered in this class completely break free from conventional poetry, so it's very refreshing and exciting to analyze their works. By reading these "mad" poems, I was able to think deeper about elements of poetry (like ambiguities and poetic structures), which I have never considered before. I came out of the class as a much stronger writer. Overall, the class has been one of my favorites since coming to Yale, and it's a must-take for any comparative literature or English major!
 I would absolutely recommend this course to any student who is interested in the given course topic. Despite having no prior experience in working with French poetry, I believe that this course was designed in a way that still allowed me to contribute to the group discussions.
 I would recommend this course if a student is looking to learn about poetry or how to write about poetry. I don't know if I would recommend the course to a student particularly interested in MADNESS, however, because the topic was not touched on as much.
 If you have an interest in poetry or study French and choose to be in the French section then I think there is a lot of vital information and knowledge to be found in a course like this and it will be very enjoyable, also many of the texts we read are fundamental to these fields and they should be on your reading list. On the other hand if you are on the fence and don't have any real interest in poetry or think that poetry is "easy," then I would not recommend this course to you, simply because there is a great deal of reading, though not that much more than most other Yale courses.
 Definitely! For those in the French Major: what are you doing if you have not yet read Baudelaire, Mallarm, and Rimbaud. For everyone else: nary has a Frenchman with a soul not been profoundly shaken and then shaped by the poets of the Second Empire and Third Republic. These poems are the cruel, delicious, salacious counterparts to prim and verdant English poetry, and the writing in this class draws from fields far beyond hermetic textual analysis.
 Absolutely, whether you only know English or are fluent in French and German, hardcore STEM major or literature major, this class is very rewarding and far from intimidating
 Yes! I great and approachable writing credit with really interesting lessons about history and literature.
 I definitely would recommend this course to another student!!!! Prof Connolly is an interesting character that I respect a lot. This is not your typical class which is why I loved it so much. Learning about mad poets entails being encouraged to be a bit mad yourself in your writing and analysis of the texts we read, which I am totally behind. It is also a great opportunity to read the works of fascinating German and French poets. (This class really piqued my interest in French poetry actually.)
