 strengths: professor was very engaged in course and very willing to help weakness: very fast paced
 The strengths of the course can definitely be attributed to Professor Sargent. She was incredibly receptive and make awesome note sheets for us to fill-in/follow along within the lecture. I think the weaknesses are potentially the gap between what we learn in the videos and what we cover in the lecture. I feel as though sometimes John would say important stuff in the video lectures, but it wasn't necessarily reiterated in the lecture, so I wasn't sure if it was important/we were expected to know it. Additionally, I never really felt like the Friday workshop sessions added to my understanding of the material, and they often took up so much time. Luckily, I had a very engaged workshop group so we were able to get through most workshops with the help of the TF/Professor, but I had classmates who were the only ones who spoke in their workshop group, thus never allowing them to complete the main problem in time. I also wish we could've gone over the main problem after everyone had a chance to try it, especially because the main problem was usually pretty difficult and would show up on Psets/tests.
 The online aspect certainly makes the class harder as the material is crammed into 5 weeks and you are unable to dive as deeply into each topic as you would if the course was in person. That being said, YSS did a decent job of organizing the course in a way that made it easier to learn remotely.
 The strength of this course is definitely the professor, Pamela Sargent. She was incredibly clear, helpful, knowledgeable and always there for her students. Maths has never been my strongest subject so I was concerned going in. However, Professor Sargent was amazing and I had a wonderful time in the course.
 It was very well run but we often ran out of time to go over all of the material for that day. I think we should have spent less time in breakout rooms.
 Strengths: there were a lot of resources available to help make sure that we really understood the content. The material provided was very clear and Prof. Sargent made great practice problems for us to check our understanding and prepare for exams. I also really liked the notes that she prepared for us to fill out during class and the pre-class assignment/video set upthey were super helpful. Weaknesses: the only thing I didn't love about the course was the fact that it was a lot of content to go through in a short period of time, but I think that must be a limitation of all summer courses, considering how there is a lot of material and not enough time to go too deeply into all of it.
 Watching videos before each class about the content to be covered the next day was very helpful. It allowed you to be fully prepared and primed for the content of the class and to ask any questions that had arrisen. On the other hand, the problem sets were multiple degrees more difficult than any of the content covered in class or on the examinations. This system didn't seem to make sense.
 The strengths of this course are organization, amiability, and engagement. This course was easy to follow and easy to understand the expectations of the students because of how the course material was organized in Canvas (every lesson had its own Module). Moreover, the course resonated with amiability, which stemmed from the professor. The professor was very happy, friendly, and relatable to the class. We also had Friday workshops which promoted group work and collaboration - this was great because we were able to establish friendships and become comfortable working together. Moreover, the level of engagement with the class was excellent. Every class, the professor asked us questions and patiently and humorously waited for a reply. On Fridays, we were exposed to more difficult concepts and had the time to truly dissect the question and concepts involved, which consequently allowed us to understand the material more thoroughly. Also, the professor welcomed us to ask about any concerns or questions that we may have during, before, or after class; she acknowledged that the course moves very fast and encouraged us to email her if we have any concerns. This allowed for students to clarify all their questions and optimize their understanding of material. There were very little negatives of this course. The only weakness was that the pre-class supplemental videos were a bit long. However, these were optional so it is not truly a weakness of this course. This could be improved by stating explicitly that the only videos we are required to watch are those in the actual quizzes.
 The main strength was lots of office hours and lots of chances to ask questions. I think it could be improved by having a few more practice problem sets available.
