 Yes, this was by far one of the top three classes I've ever taken at Yale. I looked forward to the course and it's readings every week. If you want to think about your life and experiences as a queer individual in a community of kind and generous thinkers take this class. Beans and Joanna are the absolute most wonderful human beings and generated such an important community for me especially during covid. yale has been deeply in need of this class -- it does critical work of critiquing stem/medicine/science, yale stem and makes space the urgent consideration of queer futures and queer possibilities for science.

 Yes, I would recommend this course to students looking for a science studies community on campus and who want to explore the intersection of science, queerness, and society.
 ABSOLUTELY! This class was absolutely amazing, and I could not recommend it enough. Joanna and Beans were fabulous instructors who were so willing to listen to students and work with us to make sure we met our goals as learners in the class. They also did such an amazing job of facilitating a class community that felt so welcoming and insightful; I can genuinely say that our meetings were one of my weekly highlights. This is all in addition to the deep discussions that we had. I learned so much from the readings, my classmates, and my instructors. 500/10 course
 Yes, 100%. This course was a true highlight of this past semester. The community that the course fostered is unlike any other I have been a part of on campus, and considering this was accomplished completely online is remarkable. The topics we discuss are fascinating and always stayed with me after class.
 I would 100% recommend this class to anyone who is a STEM student, a history or arts student, a humanities student, a queer student, a trans student, or anyone who is remotely interested in exploring and speculating about science, what science could do better, whether science can be ~queered~, what kinds of power dynamics have shaped the construction of 'biological sex', 'cisness', 'transness' and 'queerness' as concepts, and ways to think about the future of science. You will learn a lot without even feeling like you're having to memorize lots of things or revise content, because the course allows you to think about and sit with new and interesting concepts throughout. You will not regret taking this class (or any class with Joanna Radin). SO GOOD! TAKE THIS CLASS!
 Every single student at Yale who is studying in a STEM field should take this class. STEM classes do not put enough emphasis on the history of our field and the ways science has supported and upheld systems of oppression. If we want to be better scientists, we must understand this history so that we arent doomed to repeat it.
 Without a doubt! This is a low-pressure high-reward course with lovely peers and instructors that can teach you to think critically about science, technology and queerness.
