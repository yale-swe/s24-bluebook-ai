 I would absolutely recommend this course to another student. Anthropology is an exciting, challenging, complex, and fulfilling subject about which to learn. It teaches you to see the world in a new way, and, as Professor Harms pointed out throughout the semester, it is a self-correcting discipline, which strives to solve the very problems that, oftentimes, it historically had a hand in creating. By its very nature, anthropology is one of the most empathetic disciplines I've ever come across--and the most empathetic department. If nothing else, you must take this course for the teaching team. They are among the kindest, most thoughtful, and most passionate instructors I've ever had the pleasure to work with here at Yale. The subject matter, as well as the lessons I learned throughout the course, have continued to stay with me, shaping the way I view my surroundings every day.
