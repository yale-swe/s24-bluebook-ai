Â The professor for this course was excellent and very engaging. The discussion portion of the class was great, both in whole class discussion and smaller groups. However, there was some awkwardness due to zoom in the smaller group discussions, but I'm not sure there's a way to overcome that considering you've never met any of the people you're virtually grouped with in person. I also felt like it could be frustrating when a question would be asked and ten people would 'raise' their hands. If you were the tenth person to raise your hand, you would be last to speak, that is if you had anything relevant and original left to say at that point anyway. In-person classes avoid this problem because when many students raise a hand, one person is typically chosen, the rest put down their hands, giving people who didn't initially raise their hand a chance to jump and have more time to formulate a response to the initial question. For example, after one person would speak, I might have something of note to say, but if there were 5+ hands already 'raised' I didn't usually raise my, knowing that we would not likely have the time to hear me speak, nor would it likely be relevant. Additionally, this would sometimes result in a discussion in which people reiterated points or took a good discussion off topic just to get participation in. Though, I'm not sure this can really be fixed in a zoom format. Back to strengths, the readings were all super interesting as well as the ancient texts. Sometimes the reading was a bit overwhelming, but it was doable and well worth it in the end. The powerpoints were very clear and nicely laid out, and the professor nearly always was clear and asked discussion-provoking questions. The narrative of the class was clear and well-outlined and I was glad we read up to the Metamorphoses. It came full circle in a lot of ways from the Hesiod. The variety of activities were also really great and helpful to my learning of the material. Overall, this was one of the best classes I've taken and certainly one of the best, if not the best, zoom class I've done.
