2018-Summer ENGL S247 01 Travel Writing

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

ID: SU109 CRN: 30125 Term: 201802

Back123
 I would recommend this to any student who is serious about the craft of nonfiction, and who can fully throw themselves into the complete experience. I think some students who were less invested in the internet ban and in the daily production of creative work struggled a bit.
 I absolutely would. It is an incredible experience. It is a once in a lifetime opportunity that anyone is lucky to have. I would tell them: The course will significantly improve your writing. It will also make you a more critical reader and a better editor. You will read interesting materials and write many pieces that you're proud of. You will have a culturally immersive experience. Living in Auvillar is a wonderful experience. You will meet interesting locals, eat delicious food, and explore an incredible region of the world. All of these experiences will give you more inspiration for your writing. You will have an absolutely amazing professor and course assistant.
 I would, without a doubt, recommend this course to any student at Yale. Effective writing is an essential skill in any career field and this course, while focusing on travel writing and writing about place, helps all writers find their voice and explain themselves in ways that add a human element readers can relate to. Regardless of the issue students feel most passionate about, the course helps students express the importance of their interests to others in the most effective way. Sharing our writing among the student group was also an incredible way of meeting new friends in an intellectual environment without the pressure of competition and the dangers of comparison since every person has a different writing style and is evaluated on different grounds. This is made possible by the careful attention of Professor Kinder and the small group size. The course assistant also did an incredible job of planning interactive activities and diversifying the weekend itinerary in a way that allowed us to learn more about France without losing our focus on the work at hand. Irina Markina was an unforgettable aid to our understanding of Auvillar and each city we visited afterwards, and she was an essential aspect of our immersion in a completely new place.
 Yes, but only to students who are willing to actively engage with the curriculum. I think the program is the most amazing month I've spent as a traveller, student, friend, writer but I think that was dependent on my wanting to partake in a program like this. I think I would recommend this course to any student who loves writing and wants to spend hours improving that craft. It's an amazing opportunity to hone in on one focus and work with a cohort of similarly-driven peers to create mass content and develop as writers.
 Yes!! This has been an eye-opening experience in writing, and living together. It has made me conscious of just how engaging learning can be, and also of how one writes, or communicates about places that they are strangers to. This course has had a large impact on how I write and see the world, and I would love for every student to be able to experience something like this over the course of their semester.
 I would highly recommend this course. It is a truly exceptional and unique academic experience!
 Yes absolutely. It is definitely a commitment, but one that is worthwhile!
 I would highly recommend this course to other students. It is certainly a gem of the Yale College experience, to be able to travel to a location as beautiful as Auvillar and to focus on one thing and one thing only. It's a refreshing break from the school year without losing the best parts of what it means to be a Yale student.
 Absolutely! Especially if you have a fear of writing on a strict deadline, you should take this course! While you will certainly become a better writer, more than that, you will befriend a dozen other students whose bright and infectious energy will inspire you everyday to get up an write and who you will always feel comfortable turning to for help. This is the most academically and emotionally supportive class I have ever taken at Yale.
 If you are reading this because you have the chance to go to Auvillar, GO. I really don't know any other way to say it. This course will make you a better writer you will see your sentences smooth and your ideas clarify before your eyes. And you get to write about yourself! About yourself and the places you've been and the people who have been there with you! Who gets to do that??? And it doesn't stop thereyou get to do this while sitting on a terrace in the June sun in southwestern France while eating a nectarine from the corner store or a fresh baguette from the bakery, or you can go down and write by the river, or you can go to the lookout and and write while watching the vineyards. You eat three-course meals every weekday and travel on the weekends. Professor Kinder is a life-changing professorshe has such an eye for editing and such an ability to do so compassionately (and incredible dedicationyou get your essays back so fast, and you have one-on-one conferences with her every week). Irina (your course assistant) is one of the most lovely and rigorously organized people I've ever metshe makes your relationships with people in town happen, she makes your weekend trips happen, she makes your cultural education happen. If you're scared of the Internet sabbatical, don't beit turns out to be much easier than you expect it to be, and it's one of the greatest gifts this course gives you. I left a slice of my heart in Auvillar. GO!
 Yes! This class will change your life, your writing, your perspective. Living and writing in this place, with strangers who become friends and experiences that become essays, is easily the highlight of anyone's college experience.
