 Only if it is a requirement.
 As one of my friend's put it, the summer Biochemistry course is like ripping off a band-aid. The amount of information is intense, overwhelming at times, and fast-paced, but in the end, it is satisfying to see all you have learned. It is also enjoyable to solely focus on the one subject. But this class is definitely not for the faint of hearted. If you want to do well, you're going to have to push yourself to deeply understand the information and always stay on track. There are no dropped quizzes or assessments, so every little bit counts. That being said, the information itself is very interesting. If you are medically-inclined, as most of the students who take this class are, you will find the bits on metabolism, human physiology, and disease interesting. Professor Clay makes an effort to point things of that nature out and will even highlight what is important on the MCAT. I liked Professor Clay and thought she was a good teacher, but I know many of my classmates did not like her teaching style. She sometimes makes mistakes on small facts which bogged other students down, but I think if you are prepared to learn yourself, you will be fine.
 I do think the content is interesting, but the pace of new material can make it difficult to do well. It might be wise to credit/d/fail this class to be able to enjoy the learning and not have to worry about the letter grade.
 I would recommend the course because of the interesting material. However, try to not take it with Professor Clay. She is very bad at explaining concepts and does not seem to care about students deeply understanding the material. Even though she had good intentions, her explanations often did not make sense. It felt as though she was saying something she had memorized once, without really understanding the material. sometimes she would give us explanations that were blatantly false. the class would speak up, saying that the explanation did not make intuitive sense. She would then emphatically repeat the wrong thing she had said, without actually thinking through the problem at hand. only days later did she send us an email saying she was wrong. professor clay also very much buys into the premed mentality of grade-obsession. this is helpful because she is pretty transparent about grades, but she also doesn't seem to really care about students deeply understanding the material. If you asked her for feedback on an exam and you scored at or above the median, she would dismiss your questions saying: "but you did well!" it seemed that professor clay did not believe students could want to learn for the sake of learning. Prof clay was also very dismissive of questions in class. When she would answer a question, she would either give a cursory explanation or not answer the question at all. This was especially problematic in a flipped classroom where the whole point of class is for students to ask questions. Her lecture slides with audio were incredibly confusing. They did not seem to have any clear structure. I often found myself writing down lists of things that seemed to have nothing to do with each other. Her verbal explanations were slow and unhelpful.
 NEVER. The class was terribly run. The material could have been interesting if it were not flipped classroom with terrible slides and audio clips. The quizzes were not fair assessments. Nicole Clay is an awful professor, in terms of her knowledge, her ability to explain things, and her general caustic and unnecessarily rude personality.
