 strengths: detailed lecture slides, TA help sessions, practice exams
 Strengths: I would really ask the teaching team to seriously consider recording their lectures even next year. I cannot fathom how I would have understood anything in this class if I did not have the recorded lectures. I consider the recorded lectures to be a huge strength. Weaknesses: The sheer volume of material. There is so much stuff. One of the professors mentioned this, and I think it's true, so it's worth repeating here -- some members of the teaching team have been teaching this material for several decades (not an understatement). As a result, they know the material quite well! Unfortunately, this is my first time seeing the material. If the teaching team could try to put themselves in a first-time learner's shoes, I hope they might consider slowing down the presentation of the material.
 occasionally going to TA office hours is hard; a lot of people are competing to ask questions and most of the time TAs will not know the answer. a strength is that the instructors have made this course friendlier by eliminating the curve & encouraging cooperation, which has been a huge relief on my mental health. they are really here to help you & their OH are very helpful as well.
 Strengths: the amount of material covered is astounding. The most up-to-date science is presented, and, by the end of the course, we were able to deduce mechanisms provided experimental data. The lac of a curve made for a much more collaborative atmosphere in the course, and the professors genuinely seemed to care about our learning and impressions of them. The professors are very willing to answer questions during office hours. Weaknesses: the p-sets are often outdated-- not relevant to this year's material-- and take a significant amount of time but are only worth three points. They require attending peer tutor office hours as they cover content not previously studied.
 Wow. So much information. I cant imagine doing this course without being able to rewatch the lectures. I like that they listen to students. It would have been great if the problem sets included some simpler problems that were just content based instead of all test-like questions.

 I thought that this course was great. Weaknesses: I think it would have been useful to put the biology of eukaryotes and prokaryotes closer together. We learned about transcription, translation, etc. in prokaryotes, then took a few lecture break, then learned about it in eukaryotes.
