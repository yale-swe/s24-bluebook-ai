Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 12772 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 I can't think of anything that should be done differently. Under the circumstances, the instructor and the teaching fellows did a phenomenal job in terms of virtual engagement.
 The "Coffee with JT" appointments were a nice idea to replace the normal, casual interaction that you get with a professor in an in-person class.
 Everything was fair.
 The recording of lectures was especially helpful. If for some reason I was unable to attend, or if I simply wanted to go back for clarification I could watch the lectures that were uploaded within a day. Learning on zoom is frustrating and causes me to disengage; however, for the most part, the professor was able to keep class interesting.
 I was hesitant at first when JT announced a mandatory camera-on policy for Zoom (with exceptions granted for extenuating circumstances of course), but looking back now, I am very grateful that JT implemented this rule and enforced it. For myself, it forced me to pay attention and remain engaged during lecture, which meant that I was able to focus and have an easier time with learning the new material. For the class as a whole, it increased student participation and made it feel as if we were actually part of a class with fellow peers, as opposed to just mindlessly staring at an instructor on a screen. I hope that any virtual classes that I take next semester will implement a similar policy.
 Discussion sections and JTs teaching methods were very engaging. In lecture, I felt encouraged and comfortable to ask questions and participate. Outside of lecture, JT was extremely helpful in office hours office hours and section is how I understood theory. I got lucky and found a study group, but without one I think the class would be much more difficult. I recommend a survey to match people who want PSET partners is sent out early on in the semester.
 The Coffee-times with JT were really appreciated. Getting to know your professor beyond just class is a really neat thing to do, and I hope other Yale courses do this as long as school is conducted online.
 During the lectures, there was often a switching between the powerpoints and a sketch board to draw what was happening. There certainly is a use for the sketch board, but the frequency of the switching did convolute things for me. I think making the slides more robust to include what is often sketched out would be of great value.
