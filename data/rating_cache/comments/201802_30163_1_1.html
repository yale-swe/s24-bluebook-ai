2018-Summer SPAN S242 01 Lang, Cult, Soc Spain

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30163 Term: 201802

Back123Forward
 Strengths: good balance of work and free time so that we were busy but still had time to explore on our own. The online homework helped me remember a lot of small grammatical points that I had forgotten. The blogs were a good way to practice writing without *too* much stress, although sometimes it was hard to find time to write them. Weaknesses: The expectations were not always clear. Much of the information was on the syllabus/canvas, but sometimes the syllabus was hard to interpret and sometimes what happened in class differed from what the syllabus said. Also, I have little idea of what my grade will be in this course. 50% of our grade was determined in the last four days of the program, and another 15-30% on top of that was "participation" or "blog" assignments, for which we were not (numerically) updated on how we were doing. Realistically, my grade could be anywhere between a B/B- and an A.
 The homestay model, amount of work, and class schedule were all perfect. Some of the excursions weren't that worthwhile simply because they were so far away and I felt like I spent all day on the bus (the excursion to el parque natural, for example). Also, it was kind of unclear throughout if this was meant to be a literature course, grammar course, culture course, or other type of language course. I think it is possible for all of these types of courses to be combined into one, but sometimes these objectives got in the way of each other. If this is a course to improve our fluency, maybe it doesn't always behoove the students to interrupt them to correct minor grammar mistakes. If we want to truly study Spanish literature, maybe let the discussion move beyond vocabulary and clarification questions.
 I love how this course encourages students to be highly engaged, including blog posts, optional museum visits, and biweekly excursions to towns and cities around Spain. We are given more than enough time to make the most out of the long days, and there isn't an overwhelming amount of homework. I would, however, improve our use of class time. We never got around to discussing any of the texts in depth, which was disappointing because I know my classmates and I had a lot more to say. I would much rather focus on a few texts in detail than many texts on a surface level. I also thought the grammar for the exams was not totally relevant to the course.
 Strengths: interesting readings, good speaking and listening practice in class, incredible location in Valencia Weakness: classes held in room designed for lecture despite being a seminar
 Strengths: As oxymoronic as it sounds, the strength of the course comes from the activities outside the course. One can learn a lot in their own way from the excursions to localities inside and outside of Valencia, guest speaker lectures, and day-to-day experiences with host families. Weaknesses: This course serves as an introduction to many facets of Spanish culture, from the environment to the politics. Students usually become "jack of all trades, master of none." While this allows students to gain knowledge in a wide range of areas, this also leads to a rather cursory understanding of the studied topics. One suggestion to improve this course would be to shorten the time dedicated to introductory material and allow students to devote the last two weeks on a topic of their choice, allowing them to gain a deeper understanding of a topic they are particularly interested in.
 This course provides a well-developed glance into all facets of Spanish culture: food, art, literature, environment, economy, politics, sports, government, society, architecture-- it is all covered. However, I walked away with the feeling that none of these subjects were covered in depth. Just about the moment when I wanted to learn more about something in class or in our weekly excursions, we were asked to move on to another activity or discussion. The course load is manageable, but some of the graded assignments will occur by surprise, or when you're unready for them. In general these issues do not destroy the integrity of the program, but they are certainly nuisances that one has to bear.
 This course was an excellent introduction to the history and culture of Spanish. That being said, this course failed to truly dig deep into contemporary Spanish culture and events due to other subject matter that was given greater importance. The course touched lightly on several different topics but failed to bring them together in the end or even connect those sub-themes to the Spain we students were experiencing in our day-to-day.
 Professor Asensio truly made the course accessible to the many levels of Spanish-speaking ability we had in the class. The resources on MySpanishLab really helped me with brushing up on grammar review. We were constantly immersed in the language and I feel like my Spanish improved.
 This course did a great job of catering to each student's interests and background. Since students in the class had different levels of experience, Pilar helped each student improve based on their own needs. In addition, we were able to choose topics for our final assignments and blog posts based on our own interests and experiences in Valencia.
 One of the strengths of this course was the variety of materials and the topics they covered. Learning about so many different topics through several mediums (guest talks, plays, articles etc.) made it much easier to integrate new vocab into both our class discussions and our everyday lives during the trip. One weakness of the course was the pacing. It was somewhat unclear when we were moving onto a new topic. Often times our discussions felt cut short, so we would continue them the following day, which caused us to fall behind on the material, which we could have understood better.
 I thought the course was set up well. I did think that some of our readings in class seemed very disjointed and I was never sure what exactly was the point of the particular reading.
