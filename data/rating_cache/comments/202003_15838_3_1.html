Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

 I thought the professor did a good job overall
 Professor Manning frequently used Breakout Rooms. Honestly, I enjoyed Breakout Rooms more than our larger discussions, since the class could sometimes draw ca. 35 people. At the same time, I don't think that all the time spent reporting back what each group discussed was that valuable (it felt repetitive). In order to accommodate people in different time zones, every lecture was recorded, which seemed to work out well.

 I think that this class worked fine the way it was structured now. I liked the fact that there were no discussion sections. I think perhaps grading rubrics and expectations could have been made a bit more clear.

 I really liked the more discussion based format of the course, even though it was listed as a lecture. I was much more engaged over zoom than in other classes and it was very interesting and not a boring slog like many zoom lectures are.
