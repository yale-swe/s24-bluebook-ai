What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

Â I loved how the course was set up.
Â This course did a good job of keeping every student engaged with a variety of assignments, the best of which included daily discussions and individual presentations to engage in constant practice of the language. The professors also provided thorough feedback on written assignments that were completed weekly throughout the course, which was very helpful towards honing the understanding of the written language. Sometimes, however, the difficulty of the course and its formal assessments turned my focus too much towards the formalism of the language and how memorizing rules and terms would get me ahead on the assessment, instead of continuing to inspire my natural love of the language through a better understanding of the "feel" of the use and grammar of the language.
Â This courses principal weakness was in large part tied to uncontrollable factors: the size of the class and the level of engagement demonstrated by the students. As one who actively sought opportunities to push the advancement of my learning of Italian inside and outside the classroom, I found the course to be rather easy, and while I understand the impossibility of a course progressing at a rate comfortably tailored to the needs of each individual student, I cant help but consider this a weakness. With fewer classmates or with classmates who shared my eagerness to learn, far more could have been accomplished. Instead of being forced to review multiple times the same grammar topics to ensure understanding, we could have read authentic Italian news articles and other texts to facilitate advanced group discussions about pertinent topics. Instead of frustratingly wasting time waiting for comprehension and slow answers to verbal prompts, we could have covered more detailed examples or listened to presentations on specific ideas, such as Sandros work with Dante. I truly do understand that these issues in large part could not be helped and are not inherent to the structure of the course. In reflecting upon my experience this summer, however, these are the areas that I realized were left with the greatest room for improvement. The strength of the course was the engagement and patience of the two instructors. I benefitted greatly from Sandros and Giovannis respective teaching styles, and I actually think it was more effective to have two different instructors than a single perspective guiding the classs language development. I admired their patience and ability to explain and clarify almost completely in Italian, and I am grateful for the work they put in to quickly bring us students clear, instructive feedback and to engage us in dialogue outside the specified class time about ideas interesting to both us and them. This course could be improved by limiting its enrollment. This change would allow for more concentrated attention on individual students and eliminate the need to encourage participation from each member of a large group, thereby freeing up time for longer or deeper discussions, more focused activities, and prolonged direct interaction between students and the instructors.
