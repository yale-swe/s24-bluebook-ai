2021-Spring PHYS 261 01 Intensive Introductory Physics

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 Strengths: Professor Harris was very knowledgeable, and his lectures moved in a way that was internally consistent and made sense (sans the occasional typo). As for PSETs, they were much more manageable than last semester--but I think that was mostly a function of Griffiths being a really good textbook. Weaknesses: Grading and feedback came very slowly, and that was especially frustrating when trying to figure out where my understanding was incomplete and where I stood in the class. Also, when they happened, most of the TF office hours I went to were not the most helpful.
 Strengths - covers a good selection of topics from E+M and manages to have time to introduce some more advanced/interesting topics Weaknesses - some pset problems required knowledge from unassigned chapter sections (fortunately these never appeared on any exam but were very annoying, nonetheless
 Strengths: 1. the E&M content was chosen at the right level of difficulty for someone with AP Physics C background, and was not too challenging but not too boring either. 2. the grading was generous, which is a much-needed plus given the difficulty of the material. Weaknesses: 1. special relativity being taught in 261 and not 260 meant that our exploration into QM was shorter than one would've liked; it would have been better to cover special relativity in more detail in 260 instead. 2. Prof Harris's delivery of content in lectures can get dull occassionally, especially since his slides while helpful are extremely dense - although that might just be the Zoom fatigue speaking.
 I don't know. It's an intro physics class. You do the intro physics stuff. There's nothing to write home about, and I think that's just sort of the way it is with this kind of class.
 Strengths: professor Harris as a lecturer, class culture, rigor of homework assignments Weaknesses: tests mostly consisted of problems with simple ideas but messy and brutal mathematics (e.g. find the magnetic field inside an n-gon of constant current). I would have liked to see problems focused on subtlety of physical intuition.
 This class provides a solid background in electromagnetism while introducing special relativity and quantum mechanics, two pillars in modern physics. Additionally, the material is presented with some sophistication and mathematical methods are developed as needed including vector calculus, differential equations, the Dirac delta, and complex vector spaces. The attention given by Professor Harris to motivate relativity and quantum mechanics was a particular strength. I found it to be a unique perspective that builds intuition. In terms of improvements, it would be helpful to have more time on tests (scanning was always rushed and stressful). Furthermore, the TAs were generally not very helpful and would not take time to explain concepts; working with other students was super beneficial.
 I found the lecture format slightly difficult for notetaking, and while lectures were interesting I wish there had been slightly more focus on problem solving. Reading the textbook was definitely what taught me how to do the homework and the exams.
 The material in this course was fascinating and compelling, and the focus on building intuition provided me with a stronger foundation for exploring more complicated physics in the future. However, the course required knowledge of advanced techniques in math that weren't addressed thoroughly in class. Since students had varied levels of preparation from high school calculus classes, lacking a multivariable background would greatly decrease the difficulty of this course. For this reason, more worked examples and resources might improve this course.
 Very nice course overall, extremely enjoyed the motivations for relativity.
 I really enjoyed this class, more than I enjoyed 260 last semester. Professor Harris introduced some topics in a truly beautiful way, especially deriving the Theory of Relativity from Maxwell's equations. The class was well structured and taught, and the exams and homework were pretty fair. Overall a great class.
 This course is well organized and covers the challenging content clearly. Lectures usually discuss and develop all content required for assigned problems, except a couple instances were problems were not relevant to the lecture content. The thorough treatment of the chain of discoveries and amendments to the laws of E&M and how from them special relativity can be deduced is particularly helpful and illuminating, as well as the thorough discussion of quantum states and the spaces that represent them.
 The strength of this course was, arguably, the textbook for Electromagnetism (Griffiths), and the lectures were better than last semester. I also want to make a shoutout to one Peer Tutor (Eliza), who was incredibly helpful and patient during office hours. The weaknesses of the course include poor feedback that usually came late, and perhaps lectures that were unengaging because of extensive mathematical manipulations. Perhaps this could be improved by giving students a handout with the mathematical dirty work and show the results and some key steps instead of the whole derivation. Another weakness was the organization of many office hours during the course. I wish the office hours of the instructor had been put in a day farther from the day in which PSets were posted since this meant that I was unable to ask questions about the material to the instructor. Another issue that I would like to point out is that exams seem to be testing how well we can apply examples from class and the book as opposed to testing whether we actually understood the material. Throughout the academic year, both for 260 and 261, I felt that I was learning how to solve tests prepared by this instructor instead of learning physics.
 The lectures were clear and concise. The information conveyed by them was very helpful in improving my understanding of the subject. One thing that could be improved is that the lecture slides occasionally double up the panels, and some of the things written become special characters when uploaded to Canvas. If the could be fixed, then the slides would be easier to use.
