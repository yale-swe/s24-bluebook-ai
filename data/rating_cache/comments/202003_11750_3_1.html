2020-Fall MATH 350 01 Intro to Abstract Algebra

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 11750 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 The use of breakout room and jamoard in class is innovative. I love this part of class.
 Online lectures worked quite well. Miki's virtual preclass components also were very helpful and kept us from having to cram too much material into lectures. Office hours with Miki and the ULAs were often and clearly advertised. Obviously, collaborative work and finding study groups is more difficult, but this is no fault of the class.
 The breakout rooms were not fun and were rarely helpful. I liked the in-class polls and pre-class quizzes. The bi-weekly ULA study groups were not very helpful (although they were optional after the first one).
 Of all the classes I took this semester, this one (thanks to Miki) handled the new methods of teaching the best. Class was made as engaging as I think it could have been, with plenty of polls, healthy amounts of low stakes group work (using a shared whiteboard on Google Jamboards). I found bi-weekly small group tutorial particularly nice, and I think more math courses should work to incorporate something like this.
 Classes and peer tutor worked well. I just think that because it was online, much more was expected outside of class time. Pre class videos, stopping to take notes and understand them could take up to 20-30 minnow which 3 times a week really adds up on top of the other stuff. I just felt like the fact that it was online added a bunch of components to the class, which made it feel suffocating at times.
 The pre-class component was a useful inclusion; I also believe that the usage of breakout rooms was done very well in this class, allowing for some guided mathematical discussion.
 They were a lot of work, but I appreciated having the preclass videos and quizzes. It's definitely easy to fall behind in a course when everything is online and lectures are recorded (a feature which undoubtedly saved me in this class), but the preclass components made it necessary to at least keep up with the material for every class. I always sort of knew what was going on because of them. I think otherwise I would have been a lot more confused during class and needed to rewatch the recorded lectures many more times.
 Of all my professors and courses this semester, Miki handled the remote teaching the best. It was fantastic. Her combination of pre-written slides with live working-out was a perfect balance, and she did a fantastic job of integrating questions into the lecture as easily as in an in person setting. I dont think that anything needs to change.
 All lectures were recorded which was very helpful. Breakout rooms went well and were helpful for getting immediate practice with material.
 Miki made sure that class was engaging all the time. Breakout rooms and examples helped with this. I think there is no way this class could be improved on an online format.
 Miki put us into breakout rooms of 3-4 people roughly once per lecture to do group work, which I really liked, since it was one of the only times that I got to talk and work directly with classmates this semester.
 Breakout room problem solving sessions were a success! This is my only class that did live Zoom-proctored exams, and although I see the need for a high-pressure test to promote rigorous studying of the material, I felt that the time limit on these exams was a bit over the top. Other classes did take home exams with deeper, more involved questions, and I thought these exams allowed me to engage with the material in a more rewarding way than the timed 350 exams (which mostly involved drilling basic examples for speed).
 Lectures were great and exam review sessions were really helpful. Not sure if the preclass quizzes were actually helpful in terms of my understanding of the material because I usually just tried to get over with them late at night instead of actually trying to process them, but I appreciate how they made me keep up with the material to certain degree.
 I think the tutorial sessions were a great addition during covid times. But if they were more frequent that might be even better.
 Although COVID-19 presented unique challenges to the class environment, I think this class was not severely impacted (since it's a large lecture to begin with, there is not that much interaction during class sessions). That being said, I think there was a lack of communication and relationship-bonding within the class due to being remote, something I think could be worked on.
 This course was actually very well COVID-adjusted. I felt very cared for by Miki (who sends many emails and canvas messages) and the ULAs. I didn't like having to pre-class components, but that's because I'm lazy and prefer to compartmentalize work. The classes were often a bit too short, which the groupwork didn't help. I would have preferred to have had less group work over zoom.
 I thought that office hours and ULA study groups functioned very well. However I thought that the ULA tutorial system should have been a bigger component of the course, with more meetings and mandatory attendance.
 The pre-class assignments helped me keep on top of the material.
