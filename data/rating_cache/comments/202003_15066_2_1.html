2020-Fall AMST 375 01/HIST 102J 01/ER&M 302J 01 Asian Americans and U.S. Law

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

 Strengths: There was always clear feedback on assignments. Professor Lui made herself very available to students, despite her own busy schedule. I also enjoyed the community of our class, which Professor Lui did a great job facilitating! Places for improvement: The amount of reading was a little challenging, especially since we were reading long judicial opinions almost every week. The assignments were also quite challenging for me both in terms of frequency and expectations. Even though we had a lot of small 3-page assignments, the expectations for what we should cover in the 3-page assignments felt more suited to a 5-6 page assignment, so I found even the small assignments to be quite stressful.
 Strengths: Professor Lui made this course amazing; meeting 2x a week instead of meeting for one longer seminar a week (it was easier to stay engaged, I was able to contribute comments on Thursday that didn't come to me during the Tuesday course); the READINGS!!!!! (particularly all the Mendez v. Westminster readings, the readings on affirmative action, the readings on Sing Shen v. Southwood, the readings on Fong Yue Ting, and the readings on the cultural defense and environmental justice, the I-Hotel... actually basically every reading was so fascinating); the class environment was great and such a fun group of students; also we did fun intros for each other for our final presentations and that was SO fun
 Strengths: It covers a lot of legal content, and does so in a way that is approachable, while pairing it with useful secondary sources. Professor Lui is also an amazing professor who creates a welcoming and supportive environment in her seminars, even in Zoom world, and provides a lot of feedback on your work throughout the semester (even on shorter response papers!). I found myself really looking forward to class. You also have the opportunity to engage with everyone else's work through presentations, which is fun. Weaknesses: A lot of reading, that it simply isn't always possible to finish!
 Professor Lui has to be the biggest strength of this course. She fostered a learning environment such that everyone in the class could critically engage with and participate in each of our seminars, and she really helped us be a community in a challenging time. Most importantly, the breadth of her expertise in Asian American history is immense we all learned so much not only in class, but through her guidance in our independent projects. No weaknesses.
 Strengths: engaging discussions, amazing classmates, encouraging professor, manageable assignments Weaknesses: sometimes the readings were a little long/dense
 Great class overall! I loved the content, environment, and assignments. Professor Lui led the seminar effectively and was very knowledgeable about the material. The readings were also paired together very nicely-- we usually had a mix of primary and secondary sources spanning legal documents and journals, political and cultural history articles, and even some readings from other disciplines (excerpts from memoirs, etc.) that really enhanced the units. The assignments built up to the final paper effectively by helping us make incremental progress toward the research project throughout the semester. I appreciated that she brought in a research librarian and that he pulled together several primary and secondary source databases according to our topic proposals (these resources ended up helping me with my final papers from other classes as well). The final presentations were also a great way to end the class (learning about each other's research and doing fun bios on each other).
 Strength - Prof Lui and the other students in the class. Lui is one of the best professors I've ever had, and I was very lucky to be able to take this class. Everyone in the class was extremely fun and open. Discussions were great. Only mild weakness was not knowing which readings we were going to discuss each day, so you had to frontload all of the reading and try to get it done before the first class (and there was a lot).
 This was one of the best classes I've taken at Yale, even during the time of the pandemic. Mary Lui did a very good job in making sure that our classroom environment was one filled with laughter, caring people, wonderful insight, etc. I always laughed so much during class while also being challenged to go further with the point I brought up.
 One strength of this course is Professor Lui. She is incredibly knowledgeable and cares a lot about the material and her students' learning. Given that this was the first time the course was being taught, she was flexible and developing it along the way, but I think it all came together very well. Another strength of this course was the guest speakers. Kao Kalia Yang, in particular, was one of the most beautiful, eloquent, and inspiring speakers I've ever heard. Finally, this course is well-organized and has a comprehensive reading list that balances legal cases with other primary and secondary sources. A weakness of the course was that for some weeks, the readings were really long and sometimes harder to understand. I think splitting the seminar up into two sessions was beneficial, especially for online classes but could possibly also be helpful for in-person. However, in terms of improvement, I think having a clearer sense of which readings we were going over on Tuesday and which we could save for Thursday would allow us to focus more attention on each reading.
 The course was very engaging and the topics we discussed demanded our careful attention. Though the readings were long, the nuance in the legal documents and yielded thorough discussions and an expansion of our understanding of the tensions between asian identity and the rights of citizenship within the law. The seminar format is the classs greatest strength and potential weakness. Professor Lui helped make the class a lively place to be during online learning and her moderation of discussions enabled us to cover most if not all of the desired subjects. This iteration of the class shared in this collaborative energy, which lead to a fantastic learning experience.
