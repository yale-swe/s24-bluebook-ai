2020-Fall PHIL 281 01 Infinity

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401 CRN: 10607 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Critical thinking on philosophical concepts
 interesting discussions of infinity and related concepts, touching on different math (proof techniques, set theory, analysis, topology, probability)
 Discussion of paradoxes of the infinite, changed my way of thinking about the world, the nature of reality etc
 I learned about various paradoxes involving infinity, some theories on counting and natural numbers, theories on space
 I learned how to think philosophically about assumptions in sciences that I have always assumed to be correct. It was incredibly interesting and satisfying to be able to think about the topic of infinity in a philosophical context and to be able to rigorize thoughts processes through experiments. I learned about the history of understanding differences in infinity, and how gradually certain assumptions became popularized in fields of science.
 I developed my thinking skills -- ie. in pinpointing flawed arguments. I learnt about interesting paradoxes and the concept of infinity.
 Learned to have intelligible conversation on the topic of infinity and how to approach a variety of arguments.
 Understanding the concept of infinity and its application to different disciplines
 This course really helped me develop my analytical reasoning. It was a fascinating introduction to the topic of infinity, and helped me expand my critical thinking and analytic capacities, along with being very fun!
 Concept of infinity and potential infinity, zeno paradoxes, probability stuff etc
 I learned much more about philosophical argumentation through reading, discussing, and especially writing. I also learned a lot about the history and underlying theory behind everyday concepts (e.g. counting, measuring). I got my first introduction to proofs in this class, which will be helpful for other math classes. And, of course, I gained a deeper understanding of the concept of infinitywhat is actually meant by it, the differing types/sizes of infinity, the mathematical rules applicable to infinities, the paradoxes surrounding it, and so on.
 This class taught me a lot of the historical and philosophical underpinnings of mathematical ideas that I have learned in other math classes.
 I developed a basic understanding of the philosophical concept of infinity, various related paradoxes and elementary set theory.
 Considered the various paradoxes related to infinity, gained some understanding of basic real analysis.
 This course was my first dive into Philosophy, and I learned much about the field through the medium of mathematics. This class was a very effective blend of the two fields, and really challenged my ability to think logically and write in a clear and concise manner about paradoxes and other examples of infinity.
 This course allowed me the opportunity to practice creative thinking, pursuing avenues of thought outside of our normal accepted ways of thinking. I was taught to question how exactly we know things and how much we can stretch our knowledge in order to understand the concept of infinity. I learned how to craft arguments and explanations in a succinct and organized way. This course also provided a review/investigation into mathematical topics that was quite enjoyable and applicable. Discussion section allowed me to engage in fully interactive conversation and practice crafting positions/opinions on the spot.
 Critical thinking of large mathematical issues and ability to argue my point using mathematical facts.
