Towards the end of the course, however, I found myself treating the lectures as a slide show because the words on the slides were repeated almost word-for-word anyhow, leading me to think that maybe adding the slides themselves might also be helpful.
 Virtually, it's a bit harder to engage with the material, but the professor did a really good job at presenting it.
 I feel like the online lecture format is not very helpful. Although the professor answers questions during lecture, the lectures themselves are not very helpful. They jump many steps and leave it to the student to figure out how to get from point A to point B many times, which can be extremely frustrating. As for strength, the TA's are incredible and definitely put in the time and effort to help the students. I would improve it by having the professor work out the problems during class rather than just read them from the powerpoint and give more example relevant to the psets.

Very easy to get behind or lost if a concept is misunderstood. Lastly, the lecture slide format was sometimes hard to follow, leaving a chalkboard or live problem solving to be desired.

 The strengths include the engagement with the material via the homework and the TAs. The weaknesses include lack of class participation and general lecture style (i.e. simply reading the slides).

 Course moves way too fast in the summer. Dont take it in summer session, especially not with another course.
 I think this course has the potential to be more engaging/interactive.
 Strengths of this course included the live classes and the opportunity to set up meetings with the Professor and TAs in order to discuss content. The material was clearly taught in class and lectures were very engaging. The problem sets covered an appropriate range of problems that was reflective of the material taught and provided good practice. Weaknesses of the course include the lack of solutions provided for the problem sets and exams and the limited time for office hours. The course could be improved by making sure to provide clear solutions to problems sets and exams. Solutions are always very helpful to me in understanding content, studying for exams and the overall learning process.
 The strengths are that Prof Smooke posted videos of the lectures beforehand, which meant I could watch as many times as I needed, take notes, and figure out where I was struggling before I even got to class. This was SUPREMELY helpful and I wish all my teachers and classes would do this! It takes a lot of pressure off of lecture time, which I then treated as almost a review, where I could clear up any questions or concerns and hear my classmates ask their questions too. The weakness of this course is that they required us to schedule exams outside of class time. No other class did that, and actually because of the videos beforehand class times were ALWAYS cut at LEAST AN HOUR short, so we had plenty of scheduled class time to take the exams. I really dont understand why we could not have done the lectures in tues-weds and the exams Thurs during class time, or something like that. During the school year exams are scheduled outside of class but they are also usually presented that way before class even starts so we can schedule our lives around that. It was a little frustrating, as I spent a lot of time taking care of family members and working this summer outside of class times and I had to move things around when I didnt feel I should have needed to. We also had to find a completely silent room where we could be alone for the 60-120 min of the exam, which is great except that I have 8 other people living in my house and that was really difficult to do and I took 3 other classes this summer that didnt require that so Im not sure what the deal was with that. I hope Professor Smooke will take such circumstances into consideration in the fall and possibly spring if we are still online. I know we are all still figuring it out so I dont hold any animosity towards him of course but I do think he could do better.
 I liked watching the lectures ahead of time, but I would prefer if he wrote out the problems instead of just posting powerpoints.
 This class was primarily a self-study course, and although you could get help from the TA and instructor, the scheduling due to it being a summer course made it very difficult to receive help apart from certain designated times. Homework took a very long time, and the textbook will become your best friend in explaining the material. Most of tests were fair, except the final. There were four 1-hour exams, and for the most part, these exams were doable if you studied the material. In the final, however, the averages for the exams dropped from a mid-range B to a D. This had to do with a couple of factors. The last week of material was somewhat rushed through and there was little actual class time covering the material (which was significantly more difficult than the first few weeks of class). Also, the instructions on the exam were vague, and many students missed points for not "completing" the solutions. Canvas was very well organized for this class, and all lectures were recorded!
