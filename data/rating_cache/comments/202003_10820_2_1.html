Â This course covers a lot of material, which is one of the reasons it appealed to me during the selection process. There were lectures on such broad topics as gender, the body, and sleep however, these lectures sometimes seemed surface level. For example, in the gender class, we used most of the time to cover introductory ideas like the difference between gender, sex, and sexual orientation, which didn't seem like an effective use of our time. There was one question brought up about whether there is a real sex binary (given the existence of intersex people and the construction of sex characteristics to fit gender norms, not the other way around). I think that one way to structure the class to make it more interesting would be to give each lecture one of these big questions to open for discussion, covering basic info in the beginning but using most of the time to let students apply the sociological framework that we're learning about to approach debate. I also think we could flip the material that is covered in lecture and section-- use section for students to clarify basic ideas and establish a foundation of knowledge that we can draw upon for debate in the main lecture, rather than using lecture to establish knowledge and leaving the complex discussion for section.
