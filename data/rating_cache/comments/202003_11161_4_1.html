2020-Fall MENG 361 01 MechEng II: Fluid Mechanics

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 Yes, it is a core class for the MENG major, so you mostly likely have to take it, but if you ever get the chance to take a course with Professor Smooke, I highly reccomend.
 This was my favorite mechanical engineering course I have taken so far. The material is incredibly interesting and the way to apply everything you've learned thus far (differential equations, multivariable calculus, statics, thermodynamics). The material is very complex so be wary of the teacher you are taking with, but Smooke is an amazing professor. He really wants students to succeed so he teaches you well, but also gives very straightforward problem sets and exams. Nothing is surprisingly hard; nothing is surprisingly easy. This means it is a nice middle ground course. Extremely rewarding and very interesting. AVOID TAKING WITH GOMEZ. He is supposed to be extremely challenging.
 Required for mechE, take it with Smooke.
 I would recommend this to anyone who would like a greater understanding of the mechanics in the air or liquids around you, such as how airplanes fly or why cruise ships float.
 This is a difficult class, but can be very rewarding if you are interested in fluid mechanics.
