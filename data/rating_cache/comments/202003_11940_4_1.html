There are also two flaws in the class that I found. FIrst is the grading system which varies a lot between TAs (I had a B+ based on the assignments/quizzes alone but was curved to an A since my TA was a harsh grader) and you don't know how you're doing until after the final. Second is the assignments that need to be done outside of class. The experimental plans and lab notes seem tedious at times and usually you spent 2-3 hours before lab for each EP and another 2-3 hours after for the LN (though this might be on the higher end since my TA was very picky). Also the lab reports (there are two) took a decent bulk of time and you often get points taken off for reasons that you don't really know. However, overall, the lab I found to be a pretty great course. Just be wary going in that you might get a lot more work depending on your TA (not more assignments just how much time you spend), you won't really know how well you are doing throughout the semester, and your final grade rests heavily on the final and two lab reports.
