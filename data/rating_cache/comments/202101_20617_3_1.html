2021-Spring SCIE 031 01 Current Topics in Science

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409 CRN: 20617 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 Really great gut science course that covers a whole bunch of niche topics; can get boring pretty easily tho on lecture days
 I think this is one of the best ways to get a science credit at Yale. If you do the work, you will get an A or A-.
 If you want a science credit done by the end of your first year, are looking for a half credit to tack on each semester, and are willing to sit through some pretty boring material, then yes. Also, this class is technically labeled a seminar, but it is really a lecture course with student involvement once every three weeks.
 I honestly don't know what to think of this course -- whether it was easy or hard, good or bad. I don't feel like I took away a ton of content but it did inspire me to look into future courses in certain areas of science.
 I probably would not recommend this course to another student, especially if they aren't able to get another first-year seminar in addition to this one. I was lucky to get another first-year seminar in the fall, which I really enjoyed. If I had had to pick between this seminar and the other, and had chosen this one, I would have kicked myself for it by the end of the year because my other first-year seminar was awesome and this one simply was not. I took it to get a science credit, so at least I got that, but even so I feel like there must be other science credits for non-STEM majors in which you can get the credit without too much stress but still learn something and be engaged with the class. I did not learn as much as I wished in this class and I was not very engaged. Part of that was certainly my fault, but the class definitely didn't facilitate learning and engagement as well as it could have. I just wasn't very excited to go to class. Maybe it's a lot better in person as opposed to on Zoom during Covid, but I can't be sure. On the positive, the workload is very very light so if you have four credits already and want to add a half credit to each semester, it might be a good class to take for that reason. Be aware, though, that in order to earn credit for this class, you have to take it for the entire year (both semesters), and you only get one credit total half a credit per semester.
 I think this course would be much better as an in-person seminar, would not suggest if it stays online.
 I would recommend this course to another student with an interest in the STEM fields. A basic familiarity with biological concepts definitely helps in understanding the material. But even if you come in with little biological knowledge, as I did, you can read up on the fundamental biological aspects between modules and still be successful. Be prepared to put in some work into preparing presentations and studying for quizzes. These assignments are not too difficult, but should better not be put off to the last moment. You'll come out of this course feeling like you can hold an informed conversation on topics you might have never thought about (such as the age and composition of the universe).
 I would certainly recommend this course as a first year seminar in a low stress setting. Very happy with how the year went.
 Yes, because much of the material is very relevant to the modern world.
