2020-Fall MCDB 320 01/NSCI 320 01 Neurobiology

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 11845/11846 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 I like the structure of the lectures and the discussion sections helped clear up confusions.
 The course was pretty well-organized, and covered a lot of material that was also well-taught.
 One thing I absolutely did not like about the course was that I was confused half the time because I did not know how different topics were related to each other and it seemed like we were just jumping around a lot. It was only after studying for the final that I somewhat understood how things were interconnected. In my opinion, the exams (especially exam 1) were way too time crunched, and was basically testing how fast we could type instead of what we actually understood. A lot my answers (and I'm sure of others) were rushed and not very specific because of this.
 There is a lot of content, but it is unclear how to prioritize what to learn. I felt that the exams tested on certain things that were less relevant or required knowing a small detail, which didn't seem to be the best thing to test. I also think the teaching styles of each professor is pretty different so that is somewhat of a harsh switch midway through the semester.
 Some of this course's strengths were the incredibly fascinating material/content, the TF's (who were incredibly kind and helpful), and the Professors (who were extremely knowledgeable and accessible). The entire teaching staff was immensely supportive and provided us with many resources to not only understand the material (which was definitely challenging), but also succeed in the course. The only weakness I would say that this course has is the fact that it has no assignments in between the exams. I felt like sometimes I only realized that I didn't fully understand a concept when I came across it in an exam. While the Professors did provide us with optional PSets that we could do for practice which were really helpful and had questions that were definitely similar to what we saw in tests! I almost wish that we had PSets more often so that we could practice more questions based on the content.
 A strength I think is the availability of resources, including all of the review worksheets and the review sessions with the TAs.
 Make sure you go to section!!!!! The course is very interesting and you learn a lot in class, but without section it is very difficult to turn all of the ideas into concrete information that you should study for the tests.
 I was (and am) nervous about my grade the entire semester because there are no weekly problem sets and the average on the first exam was in the 60s. I did really enjoy the content, though.
 I think that this course had extremely interesting content and covered more ground than I was expecting--which I appreciated. I think that the weak point of this course was its exam structure, relying entirely on two midterms and a final. Other MCDB courses with high content volume utilize additional teaching methods (e.g. a weekly quiz system) to facilitate greater interaction with each lecture and parcel the content in to manageable amounts. Student performance on the first exam had an extremely wide range, and I was puzzled why this wasn't acknowledged or used to guide changes to the teaching of the remaining units of this course. Given how interesting this course material was, I felt like consternation over the exams ended up being an unnecessary impediment to engaging with the content.
 the first half of this course is extremely difficult and I still don't understand it, despite studying it harder than the second half. Thant might just be me, though, I'd like a more organized lesson plan if possible with the key points highlighted that way if I don't understand the explanation in lecture, i can search secondary resources on myown.
