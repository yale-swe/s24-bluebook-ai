 The strengths are definitely the psets, the weekly quizzes, and the lectures. The psets do a really great job of reinforcing the material learned in lecture. Fore example, when implementing checkers, we knew that we would be building off it for chess so I was extremely motivated to utilize the design patterns discussed in order to ensure an easier chess assignment. The weekly quizzes do a great job of keeping students up to date with lectures. If they hadn't been there, I'm afraid I would have definitely gotten behind on lectures and the quizzes force you to keep up which is great! Lastly, the lectures are extremely clear and always motivated by actual code examples to reinforce the material right after a general discussion of the topics. It's difficult to come up with many weaknesses. Perhaps having one more assignment may have been nice on concurrency but other than that this course was fantastic.
 The lectures are easy to follow, the slides are concise with all the most important information, and the assignments greatly help me solidify the different design patterns we learn in class.
 I felt like the last two Psets were a bit rushed in. If possible, spreading out the Psets more would make it a lot less stressful and easier to handle for students.
 Professor Barron was (a) great lecturer, (b) very helpful and listened to students and was understanding, and (c) wise in teaching the class in a non-C language. I think that is everything one can hope for in a CS class, and I am super glad to have taken it. Only weakness, as other reviews in past semester have said, is that the class is LOTS of work in the last three weeks of the semester, but manageable otherwise, so make sure to plan accordingly.

 Course material is good but the professors organization and teaching was not great
 Spread out the homework assignments more, so that the harder ones (in my opinion, HW2 and HW4, have more weeks to complete them). Use a queue system (like CS201 or CS223) for office hours.
 The strength of this course is the practicality of the subject. You learn very important concepts that are used in many software engineering settings, such as unit testing, asynchronous functions, and object-oriented design problems. Seeing live coding examples and working through them through lectures was also incredibly helpful. The weaknesses of this course were the weekly quizzes. While they seem like a good idea, the questions selected were kind of random and didn't really serve as a good evaluation of what was taught in the corresponding lecture. As a result, they ended up being annoying to complete. Another weakness was the pacing of the course. I felt like the beginning of the course went by extremely slowly, and we were given way too much time for some of the assignments and to learn some of the concepts. This made the end of the course feel super rushed, and the workload on the assignments ramped up suddenly.
