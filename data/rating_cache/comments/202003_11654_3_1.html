2020-Fall ENAS 118 01 Engineering Innovation &Design

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 11654 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Generally speaking, the course's COVID practices were excellent. Getting in person in the CEID with social distancing was great.
 Being able to have an in person lab was a huge bonus and made it the class to look forward to every week. The lectures were online but those were more content based for the most part which was fine.
 The lab protocols were managed very well, the class still spent a significant time in the CEID. Lectures had a lot of engaging material.
 Even when we attended in-person lab, everyone still had to log on to the Zoom, which didn't really seem necessary. Other than that, the professors did a great job of mixing virtual and in-person styles of learning, and were always very conscious of our health and safety. Office hours was offered both in-person and online, which I found very helpful (especially during the second half of the course) and allowed for much more flexibility than most of my other classes.
 The socially distanced labs worked out great, and Dean Vince + Professor Wilen + TAs put in a lot of effort to be accommodating when some people couldn't be there in person. It was a little bit difficult to work on projects in teams when we needed to all be six feet apart and when we had to leave campus early, but the professors handled it well and made sure that we were able to get everything done in the end.
 It was incredible that the professors and staff were able to incorporate such a big in-person part. I think the course truly demonstrated how in-person classes can be done during a pandemic.
 I think the adjustments made for COVID 19 were very well implemented. I do not think I missed a significant amount of information by listening to the lectures online but doing the labs in person was very important
 For the most part, the practices used for learning due to Covid19 were pretty successful. Virtually, breakout rooms were used whenever we needed smaller group discussions or when we needed to meet and work with our project teams. At the end of the semester, we were all able to present our projects through Zoom screen share. At the CEID, social distancing measures were strictly enforced.
 It was a little annoying not being able to work next to each other in the CEID and it was a little annoying not being able to go to the CEID 24/7, but not unmanagable.
 The in-person lab and all the safety measures were an absolute success. Students could work in the CEID without posing risk to others by constantly sanitizing their hands, keeping distance, and sticking to adhering to the attendance limit.
 I think keeping the in-person laboratory component was essential to this course, and I feel that the professors managed it very responsibly, with proper social distancing, occupancy, and sanitation measures. Recording the lectures also allowed our classmate who was studying remotely to join us along practically every step of the way. The Zoom/in-person hybrid office hours also worked really well, because the CEID usually wasn't too full at night, but it was also convenient enough to just hop on a Zoom and debug code using the share screen function. It was understandably difficult to work together as a team on our final projects in the lab while adhering to strict distancing guidelines, though I can't imagine any way around it. One thing that could be improved was communication between the teams and the professors, which usually just happened through email, but sometimes not all members would get the email or the email might not be opened in a timely manner which was difficult for time-sensitive things. I would suggest using a platform like Slack to have teams in different channels that professors could easily check and access while keeping all members on the same page.
 The instructors of this course were incredibly cautious and really careful with COVID-19. Although many of the classes were virtual, we were able to work on labs and our projects in person in the CEID. This of course, came with strict social distancing regulations. It was a little difficult to work on the same code or wiring as my team mates at times. After a while, however, you learn to just take turns or use a separate zoom call to look at someone else's computer screen.
 The in-person lab was super well organized and all of the covid safety measures were followed. I felt safe and was never concerned. The online lectures also ran very smoothly.
 I was one of the only people in my class taking this course remotely, but my professors and classmates were very accommodating. I watched recorded lectures and attended live zoom classes when I could. The professors mailed me engineering supplies and I was able to perform all lab activities remotely over zoom from my bedroom. Doing the final group project was tricky because of time zone differences and because I wasn't there in person to physically work on our prototype, which is something I was bummed about, but we made it work. It is possible to do this course remotely, but if you have the option to I highly recommend taking it in person instead.
