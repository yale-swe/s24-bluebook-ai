2020-Fall MUSI 050 01 20th & 21st Century Music

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409 CRN: 13625 Term: 202003

Back12345
 Definitely! This course is a perfect fifth class for any first-year students. Although the workload is extremely low (listening quizzes on a more or less weekly basis, two papers, one score-copying exercise during the break), I've learned so much in this class. As a musician myself, this class has completely changed my opinion on the twentieth century music which I absolutely despised before Trevor enlightened me. I even used the musical analysis skill I learned in this class on writing a literature paper in another class. Even if you are not that much of a musician, this class can still introduce you to listening to music like a pro without a steep learning curve as Trevor is very nice and considerate of people's background in music. Write about harmonies in your paper if that's your thing, or you can write about history/philosophy/virtually anything as long as you can tie it to the piece in question. Again, a highly recommended fifth class to anyone regardless of background.
 YES!!!!! A million times yes. I cannot recommend this course enough!! I can not emphasize enough how incredible of a person, a professor, Trevor is. He is super knowledgable about course material and stuff beyond it. Anything to do with music, and I mean, anything at all, you could have a fun and exciting and insightful conversation with him. Even if you do not have a music background, this course is really fun to take; you will learn a lot and appreciate music more for its sonic qualities but the deeper implications of it! For people with a music background, this course is equally beneficial to take since you learn to see the music in radically different ways than you likely have been taught before.
 Yes! I feel that I learned and grew so much throughout the class!
 I would definitely recommend this course to another student because I gained many new insights about modern classical music in this class. The workload was not stressful whatsoever and the professor is very understanding and extremely knowledge about the subject. It is fascinating to hear about his take on different pieces and composer throughout the 20th and 21st century.
 Absolutely. If you have ever listened to music you will get something out of this course.
 Yes! As long as you are passionate about music, and it's very helpful that you already play an instrument. Professor Baca's course expands your ideas of what music is and changes the way you listen to music.
 Definitely. This course is aimed at students with no musical background (even though I was the only one in my class with no formal musical training). In the class, you listen to various compositions from the 20th century and analyze them. There are also essays and music quizzes each week. The essays are fairly short and require you to analyze certain musical compositions you listened to up to that point in the class. The quizzes are not difficult, as long as you give a good effort at listening to them. By the end of this class, you will have a greater appreciation for music.
 Most certainly, a resounding yes! For any lover of classical music, this course is an absolute must. It's a fascinating dive into the soundscapes of contemporary music. The works I've listened to and the discussions in class throughout the semester have revolutionized my understanding of music, and I think the same will apply to any other student. Most of all, Professor Trevor is an extraordinary guide through all the crazy works and brought tremendous enthusiasm and insight into each and every discussion.
