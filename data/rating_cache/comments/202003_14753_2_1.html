2020-Fall HUMS 115 01 Purposes of College Education

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

 The course's readings are pretty interesting, and both the TAs and Dr. Lewis showed a genuine interest in the course material. Additionally, the writing assignments were geared toward helping students reflect on what they learned. I most loved the final 5 page paper because I got to reflec t on my first semester at Yale and include texts I had read in the class. The class could be improved if the TA sections were better prepared for discussion and if the class content was revised just a little. I think most students take the class because they hoped to read influential writers and were much less interested in the purpose of college education.
 Everything in this course worked very well for me.
 The strength of this class is a geographically and temporarily diverse selection of texts. The weakness of the class might be that lectures rarely offered new material and mostly reiterated what we have already read for the class.
 Strengths: Its pass/fail and theres hardly any work assigned (three one-page papers through the semester and a final five-page reflection), making for the perfect fifth/sixth course. Weaknesses: The lectures can get a little dull in their content, especially for some of the texts that you dont vibe with. Also, the discussion sections do get somewhat awkward when no one contributes.
 The strengths of this course would have the ability to attend either an in-person or virtual lecture. Along with this, there was a ton of flexibility for each student in terms of discussion sections and writing essays. When writing the essays assigned to us during the course, it was fun since the questions asked and topics brought about like cancel culture were relatable. Those essays, especially the last essay, were nice for reflection and helped make a person feel better, especially during the 1st semester of Yale. I wouldn't change anything other than having everything in-person.
 Strengths - low workload, interesting reading materials and discussions and pertinent questions + a global outlook. Weaknesses - discussion session

 This was a great class! The readings were really rewarding and not particularly long. Professor Lewis is a great lecturer and made the course interactive with in-class discussions. It was honestly the highlight of my week since the class was in person. The assignments were really easy and fun to write.
 Overall, the course was fantastic! The content was very interesting and the discussions were engaging. Professor Lewis is an incredible professor who is passionate, eloquent and evidently highly-knowledgeable about the course content. The mixed structure of the class was effective in maximizing learning, and the workload was good. What could be improved is the fact that the course is only 0.5 credits, and is Pass/Fail; I wish the course could have been at least 1 credit and could have provided some grade or fulfilled a distributional requirement, such as for the Humanities.
