The longest pset we had included a design portion for our final project (~2hrs of work), a written portion with 42 parts (the same 14 parts repeated three times with almost no change ~3hrs of work), and a coding portion. Almost every pset had a coding portion. This would not have been so bad since everyone in the class is an upperclassperson who should have taken ENAS 130 to learn how to do the coding. However, this was consistently awful. Joran is an actual idiot (more on this later) and designed his code consistently with errors. One night I ended up on a zoom call with eight people trying to figure out code at 4am. Turns out it was literally impossible to get correct. On average, the coding took 7hrs. I have taken CS 223 and this code was more tedious and frustrating than that. Onto the lectures. Like I said, Joran is an idiot and he doesn't plan his lectures in advance. He for some reason takes pride in this and lets the students know. This means you go through lecture with him deciding to do something and backtracking before it even makes sense. Worse than that, when he does actually have an idea of what he wants to do, you get to watch him flounder through the math and eventually never get it right. The final project had no advanced analysis done by Joran, so whenever you had to ask him a question, he would say he didn't know and have us ask a TA. The most time consuming problem set was one where we had to CAD our pistachio crusher. MENG 400 is not a prerequesite so many people had no idea what to do and he only gave an unplanned lecture on how to do CAD once. This made the assignment very difficult. Joran decided to make this part even harder by telling us two seperate times after the assignment was done that the parts we had available were limited so everyone had to redesign the structure multiple times. I would say I spent 30hrs CADing this stupid structure. Everyone knows that Fluid Lab is the hardest MechE class. The seniors in this class said it rivaled fluids, but at least they learned things in the latter. Joran just had a hard class because he is incompetent. Â I liked the content and lectures were very clear. Professor was always accessible by email/Zoom to discuss assignments. I loved getting to prototype at the end of the semester -- definitely a welcomed change during a semester of Zoom! And Joran is such a nice person to talk to in general. The only downside on my end would be assignment workload. For most of the problem sets, we had both a coding and a paper portion. Sometimes assignments were a bit disorganized. At the end, it felt like a push to pull my designs together and order parts, especially because random aspects changed. Prof/TA's did their best, though, and were accommodating with extensions. They also allowed multiple opportunities to make up work and gave thorough feedback which was really helpful.
Â One of the great strengths of the course was the varied methods of lecturing material that Prof Booth employed.
