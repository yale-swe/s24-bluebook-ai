Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30071/30070 Term: 201802

Back123Forward
 It felt disorganized and jumped around which was very confusing. It was, however, such a compressed summer course that I understand to some extent why it was felt so choppy, but I felt it could've been more efficient.
 Strengths:The teacher will adjust the teaching method according to the Suggestions of the students. A visit to libraries for archives is a very impressive experience.
 Strength: Discussion section can let people really get involved and the screenings are nice. (Dr. Strangelove Yeahhhhhh) Weakness: No PPT or other way to remind students the key point, so that sometimes it's hard to catch up.
 the instructor is very kind; readings are illuminating and thought-provoking, present history from different perspectives. but it would be better if the content of lecture could be sent to us before the class, and in the class the instructor could focus on some important or interesting points instead of just reading the manuscript word by word.
 Strength:it really helps me a lot with all the reading and discussion that I am more interested in this history and learn a lot Weakness:the lectures are kind of boring
 The course is a little compacted in a month.
 I would say that the main weakness of the course is its lenght. If we had had another week or two we could have covered the cold war during the 80s a bit further. We only had one class left for that topic.
 There may no a coherent logic between each events, so it is quite confused about why we are talking about such kind of things.
 I really appreciated the broad scope of the course in terms of the different geographic areas and perspectives that we covered. The lectures were a bit hard to follow, unfortunately, because there was so much information to learn. I would recommend having (minimal) powerpoint slides or writing all the key terms on the board before lecture. Alternatively, it would be helpful to have handouts with the outline of the lecture and key terms to make the lectures easier to follow. Also, there was an incredible amount of reading, and it would be great if we could focus in on a few readings.
 Overall it is good... The professor is charismatic and his teaching is fascinating. He is perfect.
 The course is very lecture-heavy. This is clearly the correct way to teach an information-heavy history class, but the lectures were at times hard to follow due to a lack of visual accompaniment.
