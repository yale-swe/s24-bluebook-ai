 I had heard really good things about this course, but my experience with it honestly wasn't too great. The lectures were super winded and confusing, and it was hard to articulate what we were actually learning. I had no idea what we were supposed to be writing down for notes, and it seems like neither did anyone else. We were ELEVEN lectures into the semester and Elihu was still receiving emails from students asking him how they should be taking notes. At that point, I really don't think that's a reflection on the students. Section felt extremely disconnected from what we were learning in lecture, and although we were told that "the big picture" is all that mattered, the study guide for the final exam was nothing short of 46 pages of individual buildings and pictures that we need to be ready to discuss. If the study guide had been released at the beginning of the semester, my experience with the class would've been much less frustrating. Don't get me wrong the material itself was interesting and Elihu is deeply passionate about what he does. He's a cool guy, but this class was NOT structured well for online learning. Based on what people have told me, it's much better in-person.
