 I would not recommend this course unless you are required to take it. It is definitely not the type of course to take as an elective because youre just curious about diving into quantum mechanics. It is, essentially, a math course, and students must be rigorously prepared in linear algebra, differential equations, and general mathematical intuition. I envisioned this course as one that would illuminate many of the fascinating principles of the quantum world, which governs a substantial portion of the technological innovation driving changes in our world today. To that end, I was sorely disappointed. Most of the work is quite theoretical and, as I said, math-intensive. Perhaps my expectations were just nave and unrealistic for a first course in quantum mechanics, but I felt that we spent a lot of time on concepts that didnt quite live up to my mental impressions of them. In short, this was not a fun or light and casual course that would serve as an engaging elective. It was time-consuming and, at times, dull. The general field of quantum studies remains fascinating and I dont regret taking this course. But it does not stand out in my mind as one that was 'hard but exhilarating' - its quality was not high enough to balance out its rigor and tedium. As such, it would be appropriate for students singularly fascinated by quantum mechanics, who envision themselves using this course as a springboard for further study in particle physics or electrical engineering.
