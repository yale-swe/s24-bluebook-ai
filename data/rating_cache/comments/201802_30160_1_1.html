2018-Summer SPAN S130 02 Intermediate Spanish I

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30160 Term: 201802

Back123Forward
 I really enjoyed that my understanding of Spanish language was improved through various activities, but especially conversation. Taking this course during the summer, before the trip to Bilbao, Spain, is the best opportunity to practice the language and strengthen it before diving into another country's faster-paced conversation. I think some of the readings can be difficult, but in a good way, as that pushes you to look up words that you don't know, and try harder to understand the writing and find the grammar/structures that you learn in the class.
 Strengths: There were a lot of worksheets, a lot of opportunities to share in class, and great teachers. Weaknesses: Nothing in particular
 This course is very quick and the grammar and vocabulary builds quickly, but the book exercises and homework help.
 Strengths: intensive Weaknesses: class is very early
 The professors for my section, Sebastian Diaz and Maripaz Garca, are terrific. It is clear that both professors care about their students' development and learning. Office hours were very accessible and plenty, as this summer course goes quickly through material. I believe the textbook presented the present subjunctive poorly, however with the help of Sebastian and Maripaz, one was about to workout issues and confusions.
 The strength of the course was the passion of the teachers and their honest desire for the students to do well. The weakness was probably the amount of homework outside of the classes that made it much less desirable to be engaged because it was just too much to enjoy it.
 Strengths: Variability of lecture materials and resources Weaknesses: Severely less effective then the 140 Abroad Counterpart. I would consider adjusting the itinerary of this program to more closely resemble that of Portuguese or Russian language programs, with 5-weeks abroad, or even mirroring Italian, with the full 8-weeks spent in Bilbao.
 The strengths are that it reviews things from previous levels of Spanish, allowing for a nice transition into this new course. The only weakness I encountered was that there wasn't quite as much writing practice as I would have hoped.
 I love how we were able to review everything from the previous two Spanish courses (building comfort with the use of the language) before going abroad to Bilbao, Spain. Well organized and appropriate difficulty.
 The strengths of S130 during the summer program is that class is every day for a long period of time, meaning the level of engagement and learning increase much greater than probably during a semester. The professors were great and made class enjoyable. Their knowledge on the subject and their ability to communicate effectively allowed my classmates and me to learn Spanish very quickly. The only weakness of this course could be that class is a little too long, and if we do not get a proper amount of time for a break, then our level of engagement could decrease.
 The strengths of the course include vast vocabulary and grammar taught throughout the period and great support from the professors
