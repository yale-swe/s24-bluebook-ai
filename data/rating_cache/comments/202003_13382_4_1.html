2020-Fall PLSC 837 02/PLSC 220 02/WGSS 220 02 Gender Politics/Women & Politics

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 This course has been my favorite one in the Fall Semester, I would definitely recommend it. I gives a great overview of the complex issue of the underrepresentation of women in politics, which is undoubtedly crucial to understand the current challenges in politics all over the world. Professor Aldrich is amazing. She makes sure everyone understands and gets involved in the class. In addition, she manages to foster juicy discussions between the students.
 Yes! Professor Aldrich presents big ideas in an approachable manner. In introductory political science courses I have certainly felt intimidated, so I came in thinking a seminar would be more intimidating, but it was very accessible information and Professor Aldrich does a wonderful job leveling conversation between all her students and making sure everyone understands the material/papers before them, while also giving students a personal reflective outlet to encourage personal conceptualizations of what could seem like distant topics.
 Yes, if you're prepared to learn about women in politics in a rather narrow sense (mostly in the U.S., and not looking at LGBTQ+ people in politics). As a humanities-geared person who's mostly taken English and History classes, I was surprised by how much of political science is quantitative, esp. interpreting graphs and charts, and found the readings pretty dry, but if you're already a political science person and you want to learn about women's representation, this is the class for you! Professor Aldrich is great and fosters a fun classroom community, even in the virtual space.
 I definitely would if they're looking to learn more about women in the political world; the only caveat is that we limit most our discussion and analysis to the gender binary, so while we do analyze LGBTQ women in politics, the research we look at is often not inclusive of other genders.
 For sure! This is a really important and informative course, to understand more about why women are underrepresented in public service careers and what potential improvements to the pipeline might look like, now and in the future.
 Absolutely - this class leaves students with clear takeaways about gender in politics, as well as with excellent understanding of how to engage with future research on the subject.
 Yes! This is a great course for students of all backgrounds. Very accessible and interesting content.
 Definitely!! Professor Aldrich organized all of the assignments in a very understanding fashion which prevented students from cramming last minute to meet assignment deadlines. In addition, the assignments that we did helped us apply our class concepts to real-world examples such as when we gathered data on women running in the 2020 elections.
 Yes! I learned so much about women and politics, and truly enjoyed hearing from such an educated and experienced individual regarding the field.
 I would recommend this class in a heartbeat. The professor is phenomenal and the content is relevant, probative, balanced, and fascinating.
