Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

 I liked that we did section within lecture time!
 I really loved the implementation of visual resources like ppt/videos/music videos throughout the lectures. When at least one of these was not present, I noticed myself losing engagement with the lecture. For discussion sections, I really liked the breakout rooms; it gave me and other students more of a chance to debrief and participate.
 The combined lecture and discussion section worked well.
 I think the course would have been a bit more engaging in person
 Professor Camacho was so kind and supportive, adapting the syllabus and assignments to the difficult semester which we all greatly appreciated
 The discussion sections were incredibly helpful. Professor Camacho was also incredibly understanding and caring during this time. She truly cared for our mental health, and not only did she have concerns for us, but she actively acted on her care, and made changes in the course to ensure we were okay.
 This class was the perfcet class to take during this time. The professor and the TFs were always extremely kind and understanding while also teaching us very important topics. This class is a great example of how educators can be compassionate while also promoting learning!!
 Zoom lectures were fine, the professor is not the most engaging though. If we weren't constantly kept over time that would have been great.
 I think the class did an overall good job of dealing with the virtual format. Professor Camacho and the TFs were very patient and understanding whenever they faced difficulties AND whenever we faced difficulties, and they were very committed to making class accessible via lecture guides, recordings, and closed captioning.
 Hosting section during the lecture as opposed to at a different time was very helpful for ensuring I attended the lectures.
 I really liked the fact that there were small group discussions alongside the lectures. Also within these small groups, doing breakout rooms with even smaller groups initially was helpful to gather one's thoughts. I also liked sharing music/video through zoom and thought it was a good way of taking advantage of this new virtual learning world.
 my discussion was rather big, but breakout rooms did help a bit.
 Although the discussion sections after each lecture helped me stay focused during the lecture and engaged throughout the course in general, there was often minimal participation and commentary from peers. Although breakout rooms were incorporated to get people discussing the themes in a smaller and potentially more natural environment, there was often little talking in those as well. I think the online nature of the discussion sections made it hard for people to get excited about contributing, and I don't have a clear way to fix this, but I think narrowing the question might make it easier to answer. Or, it might be helpful to treat the discussion part of the class as a mini class itself where students can go through a video or article and talk about that specifically rather than the huge themes discussed the hour before.
 I am not sure how the class would have been in-person, but sometimes the lectures took up most of the class time and didn't leave much time to have group discussions.
 Having discussion sections will always be difficult in the time of COVID, but breaking up the time so only half of the hour and 15 minutes were lecture, the other half section, helped a lot with engagement
 The closed captioning and recording of lectures were very helpful.
 The attempt at doing the Global Playlist was good, but it was difficult to get 17 people to work together over Zoom. I think in person the assignment would've been fine.
 more all in person discussion was good but a lot of the engagement was negatively affected by the virtual format.
 Online lectures with a 100 person class did not feel very interactive. However, Professor Camacho's lectures were very good.
 I wished the lecture outlines were posted before class so we can prepare for lectures better. The lectures were recorded in the beginning of the semester, but Professor Camacho stopped doing that later during the semester. Some of the lectures were quite long and took up discussion time. I really wished we had more time to discuss with small groups since there were a lot of people in the class. A separate discussion section (not part of class time) would be helpful for the class and will allow students to understand the material better.
 There could be more opportunities for students to actively discuss and participate in smaller groups
 Succeeded: small breakout rooms during discussion section helped to encourage student engagement. Could be improved: getting students to participate in the large lecture setting.
 Professor Camacho made it interactive over zoom and she was also cautious of Zoom fatigue!
 Including a seminar portion of the class made it far more engaging than it otherwise would have been with only the larger lecture component as the smaller setting mitigated anxieties about speaking in classes.

 I think all practices succeeded.
