2020-Fall S&DS 425 01 Statistical Case Studies

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409 CRN: 12411 Term: 202003

 Yes, it was useful to refine my R skills and become a cleaner programmer.
 Yes. This was one of the best and most engaging courses Ive taken at Yale, especially since I know I want to work with data upon graduation. I would highly recommend it for anyone in a similar position, since I think it is one of the few courses at Yale (in my experience, at least) that truly emulates real-world duties and responsibilities. The instructors place more value on a students process and the quality of his/her code and technique than they do on the correct outcome, a philosophy that encourages students to master the fundamentals and to actually learn from each assignment rather than doing the bare minimum or flying through them. Such a philosophy, in conjunction with less than typical emphasis on the importance of grades, was a stark reversal of the way basically all of my previous Yale courses were structured. I think I benefited from this approach because I became personally invested in the assignments for the sake of learning and because I find data cleaning and analysis quite interesting in general. The case studies took such self-engagement to the next level, allowing students to thoroughly immerse themselves in projects using real data and confronting the types of challenges - pertaining to data manipulation, research question formulation, and general problem solving - that would likely crop up in the workplace or in academia. I really enjoyed the opportunity to collect, clean and analyze data from nearly a dozen independent sources in order to model the spread of COVID-19 and its resulting deaths across the United States. These are the types of projects I hope to which I hope to contribute during my career, and this course offered me a controlled, guided setting from within which to understand how such projects come to fruition.
 I would only recommend it to S&DS students who need it to fulfill the major requirements.
