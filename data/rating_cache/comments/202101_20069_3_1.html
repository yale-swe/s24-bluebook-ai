2021-Spring ASTR 030 01 SearchForExtraterrestrialLife

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409-YCWR CRN: 20069 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 I think I'd recommend this course to a student who's interested in space but doesn't know a whole lot about it. The concept of the course is interesting, and you end up knowing things you never imagined you would think about. I don't know if Prof Fischer will be teaching this course in its next rendition, but it's cool to take a class with such an accomplished person.
 Yes!! Professor Fischer is an amazing lecturer and really tries to get to know you. I had practically no knowledge of space, but I definitely can at least give my (more educated after this class) opinion on extraterrestrial life! Great course for writing as well :)
 Absolutely. Even if you're not typically a STEM person, this class will fascinate you.
 Yes!! Even if you aren't particularly interested in extraterrestrial life, I highly recommend this work. I think this is one of those courses that anyone at Yale should take if they get the chance--it's hard not to love the professor and the way it expands your understanding of space and time. To get the most out of it, I recommend taking notes during pre-lectures, even if it takes you longer to watch lectures that way. i found that I retained information much better that way. Also, start your blog posts early! You kind of blow your own mind with the claims you make and support with data as you begin writing, and it's such an incredible feeling every time.
 Absolutely! I had no idea what to expect when I enrolled in a course on aliens, but this ended up being a fundamentally life-changing course. The content was utterly fascinating, and we delved into a range of topics from exoplanet detection to the Drake equation to the viability of extraterrestrial, intelligent life forms. The workload is not exceedingly rigorous, and there is plenty of flexibility within the course. Professor Fischer is tremendously accomplished in her own right, and it was a true privilege to attend seminars and office hours where we could learn from her decades of experience and research; not all professors have discovered exoplanets. It's a shame that this course is only reserved to a select few first-years in the seminar lotteryeveryone should try to take this course!
 Yes, definitely would recommend this class. The topics are all interesting, even if you aren't a science type of person, and you'll learn a lot.
 Yes, absolutely! It's a rather easy Sc/Wr credit and it is also super interesting. I particularly like how the prof added the discussion of climate change at the end to evaluate the possible duration of a technological civilization.
 I definitely would!! The course content was fascinating and the professor was great! She was clearly an expert in her field (and had all sorts of interesting facts and anecdotes to share), and she was incredibly kind, understanding, and approachable. Also, the grading was not super stressful, because there were a bunch of small assignments spread throughout the semester, rather than a few large tests. Take one of Prof. Fischer's classes, you won't regret it!
 I would recommend this course to anyone who has even a remote interest in space, aliens, or thinking about our role and position in the Universe. You absolutely do not need to be a science nerd or have any background knowledge to do well in this course, and are all but guaranteed to have your mind blown repeatedly.
 Yes, yes, yes! This course is a true hidden gem, and I absolutely recommend every first-year student to apply for this course. The professor is amazing, the topic is super interesting, and the work is meaningful.
 Yes to anyone who likes ET or wants to practice their writing. The professor is great, and one could know a great deal about extraterrestrial life and be proficient at writing at the end of the course.
 Yes, if you are interested in the topic. Even if you consider yourself a non-STEM person, you can enjoy the material if you listen to the lectures on the weekly basis and participate in class discussions.
