2021-Spring MCDB 205 01 Cell Biology

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 Yes! This class gives you so much information but the teaching team is incredibly organized and does a great job with providing students resources to help study. The weekly quizzes are relentless and it's important to stay on top of the material week to week. Sometimes this class can feel overwhelming, but there are no "surprises" to this class or in the grading scheme and professors and TAs are consistent in grading and posting lectures/slides.
 Yes, I'd recommend it to anyone interested in biology at virtually any level. There's plenty of overlap with other courses, but I thought MCDB 205 provided an excellent overview of how cells function as the basic unit of biology. It's not particularly easy (especially since it probably won't be open-note in future years like it was for the online version), and there's a ton of content; however, it's well organized so that attending lecture and reviewing the handouts should prepare you for the quizzes, with the textbook available to help with the finer details for the curious.
 I would only recommend this course to other MCDB majors and pre-med students. In terms of workload and material covered, this course has definitely been one of my tougher courses. However, it is very clear that I learned a lot from it and I think overall, the material was very interesting to learn given that cell biology is also such a foundation topic to understand.
