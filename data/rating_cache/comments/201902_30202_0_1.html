2019-Summer ENGL S247 01 Travel Writing

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

ID: SU100-SA CRN: 30202 Term: 201902

123Forward
Â Learned nonfiction narrative techniques, how to use constraints to one's writing advantage, how to portion time to writing, how to devote concentrated and long periods of time to writing, the benefits of disconnecting from the internet to pursue creative endeavors, and experimentation with form and structure. Writing about place and about one's own position in place was also a useful skill, especially for travel and travel related writing. Also learned about pitching stories to publications and how to pursue a career in writing.
Â I not only learned how to become a better writer in the craft sense but also how to read like an editor. We were encouraged to read each others papers and give feedback, not only on spelling and grammar but also on co tenet, flow, structure, rhythm. Further more it dispelled the myth that you need a burst of inspiration to write. You can do just as well by really working and crafting a piece. Truly a lovely experience.
Â I learned to notice. To pay attention. To take in my surroundings fully and be able to communicate evoked thoughts on paper. I have never been a strong writeror a writer at all, actually. I didn't think I could improve and I looked at essay writing as something that would be difficult. But being in a foreign environment, surrounded by brilliant classmates and under the guidance of such a passionate professor, showed me the creative side of my mind. We wrote every day. We bounced interpretations of texts off each other in seminars. We learned how to capture a "sense of place" and write about it, telling the story that every city, every street, and every person has and wants to tell.
Â I learned how to be a more effective storyteller, how to be more deliberate with word choice and narrative structure. I learned how to tell stories that feel true about places Ive only just gotten to know.
Â Through writing every day, I feel that my writing became much stronger all around. My voice, word choice, and storytelling skills all improved through thorough edits, discussions, and reading for craft. The writing partner program also greatly strengthened my editorial skills and the way I give feedback. This course made me confident in my choice to be an English major.
Â One of the most important things I learned during this course was not to fear writing. I often get paralysed by the pen and the sheer volume of work we had to turn in meant that I could no longer afford to stare at a blank page for days and give up on the whole exercise. I couldn't claim I was too busy or distract myself with the internet--I just had to write, and what came up surprised and excited me. I've never workshopped a piece before and the terror of someone not only reading my work but also giving feedback turned out not to be such a terror at all; in fact, it felt wonderful to share pieces I was proud of and have others affirm that. Reading the work of my peers helped me learn how to edit my own pieces better and I also appreciated how beautiful their writing was. I also really really enjoyed the course packet and the variety of travel writing we had to read--almost every essay struck me in some way and I'm so excited to have found certain writers whose style I love and who I plan to read a lot more of.
Â My writing skills improved immensely. I learned how to notice things in my environment, how to form experiences into essays and stories, and how to write these stories in a way that feels true to the experience. I gained a whole new perspective on writing and art and how they can be integrated into my own life.
Â Over the past month, I grew as a thinker and a writer. Admittedly, I didn't become a perfect writer. That's impossible, anyways! But, my editorial instincts improved dramatically. I see my writing, my peers' writing, and writing out in the world, with a whole new eye. More than any other Yale class, this course has given me the tools to edit (and I mean thoughtfully edit) my own writing regardless of where I am starting. I am a better editor, which makes me a stronger writer. Personally, the past month has given me the opportunity to look at the places of my past with a new lens. I am coming out of Auvillar with a whole new conception of my personal geography. This month gave me time to breathe and think about where I'm from, where I've been, and where I want to go. For this, I'm especially grateful.
