 Strengths: Very clear lectures during which the professor was always willing to answer questions or clarify content. This was especially true in the inequality section, where although the information was quite interesting, it felt somewhat detached from the other two parts of the course.  I do not think I got as much out of this class as I could have. Now I have a lot of sympathy for professors teaching during the pandemic and I'm sure it must be hard to hold lectures over zoom, but there were some issues I had with the class. First, I think Fabrizio has a very indirect way of lecturing where he will introduce a model, but then 3 slides later, say that the model is incorrect and the real model is as follows. This will effectively make all of my notes up until that point useless. This is something he does for almost every model he introduces in the class (sometimes re-teaching a model multiple times within the same class), and as a result when I went over my notes to study for the final, I found them to be very disorganized and almost useless. I ended up rewriting my notes in a more organized manner just for the final. On a similar note, I found the lecture slides to be rather disorganized and difficult to distinguish the key concepts from the less-important material (eg. scratch work for deriving an equation). For example, we would often spend a ton of time examining some line graph (which ended up never being mentioned again in the rest of the semester), before rushing through 10 slides about a concept that was crucial to the problem set. I think this could be improved by periodically including summary slides or key concept slides to highlight the important takeaways (this is something he started doing later on in the semester, and I think that was a step in the right direction). The final for this class is also worth 50% of the grade, which is understandable since it is cumulative, but I personally thought it was too much since combined with the midterm it made the problem sets in the class worth almost nothing.
