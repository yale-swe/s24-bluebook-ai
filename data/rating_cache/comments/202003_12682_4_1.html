2020-Fall PHIL 202 01/RLST 277 01 Existentialism

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409 CRN: 12683/12682 Term: 202003

Back12345
 Yes. It is a rare course at Yale in the kind of texts and methods with which it lets you engage. Professor Khawaja is also an excellent teacher: this is not the kind of course you should think you shouldn't take because you could read the existentialist canon yourself. The texts she selects are unexpected and fascinating; additionally, she challenges you to think deeply and personally about them in a way I've never previously encountered.
 Yes! I would definitely recommend this course to any other student - regardless of your background in philosophy - to ponder some extremely interesting questions about the self/identity/who we are alongside passionate classmates from all disciplines.
 Yes! The syllabus is amazing, we read a wide variety of authors (there's sure to be at least one that you really enjoy) from a variety of disciplines. Professor Khawaja and Alison were also great at helping guide the discussion each week to help you glean deeper insights.
 I would definitely recommend this course to other students interested in some of the following questions: How do we pursue a way of understanding the world, when we ourselves are transformed in the process of asking that question? How can we pursue truth in which ambiguity is not the enemy, but the condition? How do we relate to one another, express love, or question socially-imprinted identities? How can we understand the racialized and colonialized mind? What does quantum physics have to teach us about social theory? You'll find many more to reflect on and have your mind blown by. With that said, you might get more out of the class if you take an intro philosophy, if only to be familiar with the other philosophers and concepts these ideas wrestle with.
 YES!!!! If you get into this course, take it without a doubt!! The material is incredibly challenging, but Prof. Khawaja and Alison are so patient, generous, and willing to help students develop our understanding of the texts and work through our ideas! This is one of those rare courses at Yale that will actually change the way you think, and will push you to reflect critically and think more deeply than you ever thought possible. I believe that very few professors could pull off such an ambitious project as the one of this class, but both Prof. Khawaja and Alison worked very hard to build a space that felt supportive rather than destructive and argumentative, and that made such a difference!!!
 I cannot recommend this class (or really any other with Professor Khawaja) enough. This course was unlike any other Philosophy class I have taken at Yale and I loved its orientation towards questions of being and living in the world. A combination of really engaging material, great classmates, and dedicated instructors made for an awesome classroom space.
 I would recommend this course to any student interested in thinking through the self, freedom, our limits, the ways in which we have been shaped by society, and more. If you need lots of definitions given to you, I wouldn't recommend this course. That's not to say that Professor Khawaja won't give you wonderful explanations if you ask. But this is a course of doing the work, of thinking through the questions of existentialist philosophers. It's not a course, where you just memorize what each philosopher said about something with neat terms and dates and prescriptive definitions. In that sense, if you are excited about getting to ask the same questions that great philosophers asked, in a very supportive and wonderful enviornment, I highly recommend it!
 Absolutely!! Existentialism is such a powerful and thought provoking philosophy that will definitely change the way you see the world and think about yourself. It seems super meta and at times it is but you also come to understand how many areas/fields existentialist thought can be applied to
 Yes! Prof. Khawaja and Alison were wonderful and engaging, and the material was engaging. As someone who hasn't taken many philosophy or religious study seminars, this course was accessible while still being a fun intellectual challenge. The material ended up feeling relevant and came up a lot in my conversations outside of class in ways I would never have expected.
 Yes!
