2020-Fall PLSC 270 01 Reading the Constitution

Q:

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC409 CRN: 14555 Term: 202003

Back12345
 Without a doubt. Don't pass up an opportunity to learn with Professor Amar.
 I would recommend this seminar, but only is the student knows what they are getting themselves into. There is no structure, class always went at least 15 minutes over (one time 45!). You kind of just sit through Amar's train of thoughts and see where they lead. The gems of this class occur when it coincides with current events and you get to hear Amar's insider view (this course was during the RBG death and ACB confirmation).
 Absolutely; There's a reason why akhil amar is akhil amar. He's a brilliant scholar who can only be appreciated by studying with him. Most of the reasons people give for not wanting to study with amar are true, but they do not justify not learning with him. Even if you come out disagreeing with amar or even disliking him, you'll be a much much better person for studying with him. He has a lot to offer and really wants to offer it to undergrads. An amar education is part of a yale education.
 I would absolutely recommend this course to another student. The opportunity to learn constitutional law with the leading scholar of his generation is one of the key benefits of a Yale education, and this course also connects students with peers who share an interest and passion in the subject. The workload of the course is quite manageable, yet the readings and discussion posts are highly informative and educational. One should be aware that this course does not heavily discuss caselaw--although many cases are mentioned and discussed--and focuses more on constitutional first principles.
 YES! I would definitely recommend this course to other students! Professor Amar is amazing and so is the TA whoever it is. Be warned though, you get as much out of this course as you put into it, so please make sure to really be engaged every single class, do all of the readings, go to the lunches, schedule phone calls with him or the TA, post on Canvas some, etc. Make it a point to really connect with the class and you'll do fine. It's a great course for those interested in law school and those that aren't. The paper can be hard though if you've done constitutional law before, but I did fine on mine. I just had to really work harder compared to some of my other classmates (the ones that already took PLSC 233, ConLaw lecture) to understand the basics conlaw, but that's part of the reason why the TA is in the seminar. To be honest, it might be better in the long run to take this course first and then take Amar's lecture course because this really gives you the basic foundation, and the lecture is curved.
 I would not recommend this course unless someone had already taken Professor Amar's Con Law lecture and had dedicated a significant amount of leisure time to constitutional law. It is definitely not an entry-level course and it is not a supportive environment for students who are only starting to learn about constitutional law.
 No, I would not recommend this course. If you want to learn about the Constitution, this course provides a fine overview of the document and its history. However, as conducted by Professor Amar, the course is not conducive to student development. Students are not supported in sharing their viewpoints during class and do not receive feedback on their written work.
