2021-Spring AFAM 395 01/ER&M 294 01/ENGL 296 01 Writing the Self in the World

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401-YCWR CRN: 21011/21012/21010 Term: 202101

12345Forward
 I familiarized myself with the genre of auto-criticism and in general received so much helpful feedback on my original piece, which I produced many iterations of throughout the semester. I think I became a more thoughtful, vulnerable writer through this process and through getting to give and receive feedback. I also developed a better understanding of the process of writing about oneself through our q&as with various guest speakers.
 how to ask meaningful questions. how to read and understand the world more intimately and use this reading to sustain our collective continuance. workshopping our 'autocriticism' throughout the semester not only enriched each of our writing abilities but improved our capacity to engage editing in the long-term, a skill that is neglected in most Yale writing courses.
 In this course, I gained personal reflection and memoir writing skills. I also think that I grew as a constructive critic through the course's workshops, in which I learned more about giving good feedback.
 learned about different forms of auto-criticism, about how writers discussed their relationship with their parents, friends, partners, cultural identities, gender identities, etc in relation to different art forms
 I learned what it means to write auto-critically which was incredibly powerful and healing for me.
 What auto-criticism means, personal writing, how to close read a memoir
 I was completely new to autocriticism before taking the course. Through reading various models of autocriticism and working on my own piece, I developed an understanding of what can happen when a writer brings the self into contact with a piece of media, another person, a place or a larger system in the world. The course strengthened my close, critical reading skills, my ability to recognize and articulate craft in writing, and my comfort giving feedback in workshops. I also loved getting to spend the second half of the semester working on one piece of writing, which I don't think I've gotten the chance to do in other writing classes at Yale. It taught me how to think deeply and slowly about an idea, revise multiple times, and incorporate suggestions to make something I ultimately feel proud of.
