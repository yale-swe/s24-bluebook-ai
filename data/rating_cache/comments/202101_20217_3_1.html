2021-Spring ENGL 194 01/WGSS 194 01 Queer Modernisms

Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 Yes!! Professor Richards is amazing at engaging students and creating a lively class, even during zoom times.
 Yes! It's not too difficult and it's a fun writing credit if you want to learn more about queer fiction!!
 This is a great English class for those who enjoy modernist literature, especially literature written by queer authors, and for those who might not be experts in close reading or writing literature papers. The structure of the assignments (shorter, mid-length, then longer paper) provided a gentle introduction to how to close reading, and then expanded upon those skills as the semester went on. Professor Richards provided examples in class of how they might close read certain passages, which was helpful for understanding what was expected in our papers.
 I feel like this course was just a little bit misleading. I was expecting more of a history class where we learn about queer history through reading books but this got really English-y. Like a lot of close readings, examining art, etc. instead of learning about queer people and broadly how they lived. This just isn't what I expected - if you want to read old books that are queer/queercoded and analyze them, this is the class for you! If you are looking for more of a history class, however, then you should not take this.
 I would recommend this course to those who are comfortable with modern literature and with reading a couple hundred pages a week. But it's not a hard course necessarily, so anyone interested should at least shop it.
 yes- I took this course, unaware at first that it was an English course, and LOVED it! The selected works that are read are very insightful pieces of queer literature, and the course is very entertaining.
 Yes! Wide and great selection of queer modernist literature and taught with engaging lectures. However, do note that it is fast paced: often will be moving to a new or two new books every week which in turn leaves not a lot of time to get in depth with the material. Thus, some of the discussions often are surface level.
 I would recommend this course to another student. I think that this is a great course in the English department that gives a good introduction into important queer literature while still being accessible to those that are not English majors. It is well organized and while there is a lot of reading, the reading list is interesting and varied. It was very enjoyable to go to lectures and be exposed to numerous ideas and perspectives about the books.
