Â The course successfully covers the basics of several important domains of environmental engineering and gives the student a flavor of what environmental engineers are concerned with, how they work, what problems they tackle and what tools they use. The course is well structured, tying the units together with common ways of thinking in an order that makes sense. However, I think the course would benefit from deeper, more qualitative and freeform explorations of the complexities involved in environmental problems, maybe allowing students to research challenges or solutions interesting to them and discuss with the class pros and cons of several approaches to a big problem. I would say the course relies a bit too much on applying simplified entry-level mathematical models to give a general taste for how real-world issues are tackled, without fully exploring the various factors and competing interests that go into an environmental solution.
