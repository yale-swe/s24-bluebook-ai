 Strengths - clear, methodical lectures Weaknesses - some of the assignments lacked detail initially, but all questions were quickly answered
 Strengths- good mix of assignments; broad overview of the field; readings were varied and interesting. Weaknesses- at least in the beginning, many of us felt the exams were too long for time allotted; sometimes exam questions did not directly relate to what we learned in class.
 The lecture itself was very minimally interactive and could be difficult to pay attention to, but the discussion section entailed a large amount of participation, which helped make the class more interesting. The exams were much more difficult than the material presented in class, meaning that they were confusing at points.
 The course provided a broad range of generally interesting assignments, which was good. In terms of topics covered, it felt like we were given an extremely broad overview of many different things such that I feel as if I have not learned much about any specific thing. It might be helpful in the future to go a bit more in-depth on a few topics. Even though this is an introductory course, I feel that it could benefit from being slightly less elementary.
 Strengths: discussion sections were very helpful Weaknesses: too many big assignments
 strengths: Professor Puth and all the TA's are phenomenal. And this course has some of the best-structured tests I've ever taken. They did a very good job making sure that test questions left room for our own creativity re: conservation strategies while making sure we incorporated the big ideas of each unit. Creative assignments like the debate also helped. The writing assignments were good alternatives to labs, for example, for a non-stem major like me. weaknesses: none I can think of. great course
