 Definitely! This course is suitable for you even if you're not a global affairs/history/poli sci major, and it provides a lot of interesting background on US foreign policy
 Yes, I would definitely recommend this class!! If youre even remotely interested in intelligence, national security, government, foreign policy, the FBI, the CIA, the DHS, etc. then you should take it! We use a lot of interesting, historical events as case studies, in order to understand what makes something an intelligence failure or success. I had almost no knowledge about the Intelligence Community prior to taking the class, so I really enjoyed learning about the various types of intelligence, common challenges faced by intelligence analysts, reforms made to the IC after 9/11, etc. I definitely think that I gained a new perspective on current events and will start analyzing them like an intelligence officer does. Professor Wittenstein also invited several interesting guest speakers to the class: Fiona Hill, James Clapper, etc. It was such an amazing opportunity to hear about their life experiences, and the guest speaker always related to the topic that we were discussing for the week! Professor Wittenstein is super friendly and engaging lecturer, and the workload is very low (midterm, final, memo assignment, weekly readings), but you still learn a lot. I don't think you can find a better combination for a class!
