Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

My Berlin host family and the ease of transportation in the city (two things that I felt could have been improved in Jena) were both fantastic, and the city is full of excitement and history. Â The trips during this half of the program seemed even more ill-advised uses of time and money than the first half, which was largely very successful, entertaining, and educational. The night stay in Dresden comes to mind we were put up in a hostel 20 minutes by foot from the center of the city, we were not reimbursed or provided a stipend for travel (that is, for the street car that shortens the commute to town), and the city itself does not offer enough such that an entire night stay is warranted. The fact also that it took so long to get there from Berlin, and then that we were to leave the next morning after we arrived, made the entire trip seem useless and ill-planned. It would have made much more sense to travel to Dresden from Jena (which is a much shorter trip) and to allow some money to subsidize travel cost if the students are made to stay there for a night. This is all aside from the absolutely horrible instruction provided by Dr. Gehlker, which actively confused the students about topics we already knew and did not effectively instruct us in new topics (or rather, did not instruct us at all). Dr. Gehlker regularly laughed at language mistakes made by students while in class with other students present, most often when a student would ask her a question. She rarely if ever actually answered questions posed by students, instead automatically forwarding any question to the class (her go-to answer was, translated from German, "Well, what the others think?") or to our TA, Alina, who was thankfully on hand to actually teach a bit of language and maybe even clarify what exactly we were supposed to be doing. The course suffered greatly because of Dr. Gehlker's instruction, and would be greatly improved with another professor from the German department. I recognize also that it could have been that no other professors were available to teach abroad, but I strongly recommend against Dr. Gehlker's instruction, as it made the course nothing short of miserable to attend.
