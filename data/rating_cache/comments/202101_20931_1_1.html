2021-Spring MENG 185 01 Mechanical Design

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 20931 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: Gave an overview of required materials for MechE in a deep enough way to lay a proper foundation, but not too deep as to be too difficult for an introductory design class.
 Strengths: good intro, all around course Weaknesses: lectures were sometimes unclear & slow
 I liked how the labs were adapted for a virtual environment, lectures were very relaxed I tend to like learning from a textbook and this course didn't have that so it would have been nice to have some more organized lecture slides/notes uploaded to Canvas for the second half of the course
 The course explained all fundamental areas with equal importance and drew connections which helped me understand them better.
 This course expected/assumed some prior knowledge of mechanical engineering which at times made it difficult to fully understand the material.
 If you take MENG 280, the second two-thirds of the course is just a general review. In the first third of the course, we went over everything too quickly.
 The labs in this course were very engaging and the assignments were interesting but not overly-long, which I appreciated. I felt like this course was taught at a pace that expected us to have some experience with material properties, but I came in having none and it took me a little while to get comfortable with the topic because of that.
 The course is a little bit scattered but that is given as a warning in the beginning. The way it was separated into 3 different modules was helpful though to keep it organized.
 Keep it like it is! Loved professor Dollar and Vatsal!
 Strengths of this course include the labs which were engaging and generally covered the real world applications showing how the class applied to everyday objects. Some weaknesses of the course include the lectures with example problems. These lectures were generally more freeform and occasionally the example problems would be skipped or the steps would be drawn live virtually so the lecture slides when looked back upon didn't show the work. A way to improve this could be having a solution plan in mind or pre-written on the slides so there's not as confusing of an example.
