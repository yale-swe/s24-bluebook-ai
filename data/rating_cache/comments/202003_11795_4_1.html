Â 100%. Prof. Harms is truly one of a kind. I was excited to go to class every morning at 9:25, something that cannot be said about any other class I've ever taken (and I've taken 37 college courses). I barely had any knowledge of Vietnamese society at all going into the class, and now I have a thorough comprehension of so many postwar aspects of a constantly modernizing country that I can't shut up about it. The topics are so relevant and the readings are never too many. He even offered to put us in contact with actual Vietnamese people, and our first reading was a novel?? SO COOL! First day of class he said "you see those discussion posts and leader things on the syllabus? yeah, let's get rid of those." He treated it like we were chatting in a caf in a Vietnamese city, or a "boutique seminar," where we bounced ideas off of each other. I hate it when professors turn to students to lead discussion, so I was overjoyed when Prof. Harms would take the reins. If there were 5+ readings one week, he told us we could pick the ones that were most interesting and only read those, and then be the "expert" on that reading-immensely helpful when all my other classes had so many readings already. And, the only assignment the entire semester was a final research paper which was so well scaffolded throughout the semester that by the time we sat down to write, words just flowed onto the paper. This class has inspired me to take the Vietnamese language, travel to Vietnam, and even maybe double major in ANTH. Even with 7 people (mix of Vietnamese-American, non-Viet, and an auditing grad student from a dif university), class was lively and even sometimes entertaining. We didn't shy away from sensitive topics like sex work and we also covered modern issues like erosion and environmental catastrophe. And, he NEVER BRAGGED about his research either, which so many profs just looove to do; we read his articles when necessary, and he even allowed us to critique ~his~ style?? When do you ever get the opportunity to give your input on whether you understood an academic article to the author's face (hint: never!). Email responses were thorough and written with care, material was engaging, and feedback was fantastic and quick-he even copy edited our final papers to ensure they sounded good and were clear and not clunky. Fantastic hidden gem.
