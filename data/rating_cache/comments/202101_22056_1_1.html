 Strengths: Professor Glenn is meticulous and the problem sets and exams are well-set. Exams are challenging enough to produce a curve that actually looks like a proper curve (whereas in other classes, you have 80% of people getting 90+ on an exam), so you don't have to worry about getting perfect answers or getting a low raw score. In the remote setting, the ability to pause and rewatch lectures makes it easier to understand and follow the lectures, which I think was a problem students faced in past years. Weaknesses: Professor Glenn speaks like a robot in his recorded videos, which is very unfortunate because it makes it really difficult to understand and follow his train of thought. He spends a lot of effort in making the videos, but I suggest that he not follow a script while teaching. The natural intonations that come with teaching and explaining are lost, which seriously affects the intuitive understandability of the recorded lectures. I have struggled to follow the recorded lectures and find them a pain to watch. It might be better to do more explaining and teaching live. Also, problem sets were not returned on time (HW 4-6 returned after final). There is also inconsistency in grading the homeworks, with some students getting off with mistakes, while some students getting really strict and nitpicky graders.

 Strengths are that it exposes students to the fundamentals of algorithm design.
