Â The main strength of the course is Professor Schenker. She is the most animated professor I have ever had at Yale, and she makes class feel so fun, even when we're learning about obscure topics, like Middle German literature. She went above and beyond to adapt the course for the Zoom format, and there are plenty of activities to break up the schedule. The material of the course is also a huge strength -- everything is just really interesting to learn about. The only weakness of the course was the senior citizen interviews. They were difficult to schedule (especially on the weekends), and they didn't really give me much insight into the German language because most of the senior citizens weren't that interested in talking about dialects or German itself. The only other weakness of the course is that I would have liked a bit more feedback on our reading reflections; it would have been nice to get corrections every once in a while (even if it wasn't graded) just to know how we can improve our written German.

She is an absolutely wonderful professor -- she is engaging, extremely knowledgeable and interesting, and very kind to and accommodating of her students. Â The main strength for any course taught by Theresa Schenker is Theresa Schenker herself. She is clear, organized, effective, and also very understanding of extenuating circumstances. She clearly cares a lot about her students. Another strength of the course is that, especially toward the end, the content very much broadens your view of what German is and gives a better idea of what the language concretely looks like in day-to-day use than most German language courses I've taken. I also appreciated how the final project was very spaced outhaving it due in pieces rather than all at once made it feel much more approachable and lessened stress around finals season. As for weaknesses, the course felt quite lopsided in terms of which skills were practiced regularly. Although we had to write up short summaries of the readings, it felt like our reading and speaking skills (class participation + weekly video responses + the class presentation) were very much emphasized, and there weren't many opportunities to practice or get feedback on our writing, aside from on the final project. Additionally, the material can be quite hard to grasp toward the beginning of the course the texts were very academic and it was really easy to get lost or to get nothing out of them after spending a lot of time on them. The readings got easier as the topic material changed, and Theresa started sending reading questions to help guide us, but before that happened, it was really frustrating to have to get through a bunch of readings about Old German and linguistics in the first couple weeks of the course.
