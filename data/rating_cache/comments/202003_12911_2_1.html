2020-Fall ENGL 455 01 Writing about Oneself

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 12911 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: The one-on-one time and attention your writing gets, not only from Anne, but from everyone in the class. A huge part of this class is supporting one another's work by reading it and commenting on it each week -- unlike other writing classes/seminars where you only get to read people's work when it comes to workshop. Anne also does individual editing meetings with everyone and goes through your piece with you, which is invaluable and helps you understand what your specific strengths and weaknesses are as a writer. The collaborative and supportive atmosphere of this class is also amazing. You get to know everyone really quickly, and it never feels competitive. It's really exciting to read everyone's new work each week, and to be challenged to write so often and at such a high level. The readings are also really fun to read -- and for once, you don't have to take detailed notes on the reading! Weaknesses: Not necessarily a weakness, but just be aware that this is less of a seminar than a really intimate lecture. It gives you a lot of time to learn from Anne's expertise and perspective, which is nice, and there's a lot of information jam-packed in that you would lose with more discussion. But as students, you definitely don't spend *that* much time getting to have back-and-forth.
 This questions sucks because I truly cannot think of a weakness. This class was my life for a semester, idk what to even say. The community was so incredibly strong, even at such a huge distance. The classmates--UGH. Wildly talented, interesting, and kind. Don't even get me started on Anne. I simply cannot. Take this class if you possibly can. Put a lot of thought into your application and you statement of interest, it's worth it.
 The biggest strength is the course's emphasis on becoming the best version of the way you write. You never feel pigeon-holed into one version of being a great writer, and in fact, Prof. Fadiman equips the class with many devices through which they can evaluate their writing styles. The only weakness is discussion. I wish it had been a bit more free-flowing. I think this course is better thought of as a lecture course with some room for classroom participation than a true discussion seminar.
 Anne is very open to tweaking the syllabus over time to accommodate suggestions from students. This semester, she made a particular effort to diversify the voices we were reading, which all of us appreciated. It's a lot of work, but Anne is up front about this going into the semester.
 Anne has designed this course expertly. She's been teaching it for many years and takes it more seriously than any professor I've ever had has taken any course I've ever taken. The workload is calculated to be challenging but not impossible. She really gets to know her students and their writing styles, and she makes sure there's space for students to build meaningful creative relationships with one another.
 It's a very intensive semester, which is a strength and a weakness. The strength is that you learn a lot, the weakness is that there's not a lot of room to step back from it. You have to buy in.
