2021-Spring MCDB 450 01 The Human Genome

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 21483 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 I think the course was pretty perfect as is--the only thing that could make it better is having it be in person, but that will come in time.
 Strengths: There were a lot of opportunities for group discussion, and the professor and TA were very helpful when they explained sections of the paper that the students struggled to understand. In addition, I liked being split into breakout rooms in the beginning of the class to discuss one figure/table with a few of my classmates; this helped me understand the figure/table better and be able to present it during our whole class discussion.
 This is a fantastic course! Prof Dellaporta puts in a ton of effort to keep the course engaged. You learn from the professor and your peers.
 Strengths: small seminar discussion format is awesome, really gives you a chance to get to know everyone, and the diversity of backgrounds the students have adds to the discussions. Professor Dellaporta strikes a great balance between contributing his own very extensive knowledge and leaving it to the students to contribute. At the same time, the class is well structured, so it never feels like we are on our own. I really liked having the last two weeks to present many different papers to get a taste of many different topics, and I also loved how Prof. Dellaporta really kept the reading reasonable so we could focus on diving deep into each paper rather than trying to get to too much. Weaknesses: none
 Dr. Dellaporta and Matt (our TA) were awesome - two of the strengths of the course! Another strength was the papers we read throughout the semester. Dr. Dellaporta picked relevant, thought-provoking publications that are at the forefront of the field of genomics, leading to fruitful discussions of the data and its implications in future research and our current lives. Dr. Dellaporta and Matt were very accommodating and often acknowledged the fact that the course was challenging, making it less stressful when trying to learn how to get through the material. The only weakness of this course is the initial learning curve of understanding how to interact with the material. This is an artifact of a course such as this, however, and I think it is unavoidable. Even though learning how to interpret the figures and content of the papers was difficult at first, the class is set up in a way to allow this understanding to occur with little pressure.
 The only weakness of this class was the virtual format and that's something that was unique to this year. Especially when we had a rather challenging paper, sitting in class for 2 hours puzzling over advanced methods became a bit tedious. Strength was Professor Dellaporta. He cares so much about the topic, and is always there to help students further their understanding.
 This is an amazing course and Professor Dellaporta is extremely invested in your learning and making sure that you understand each topic and are able to have meaningful discussions around the papers. I loved the class and can imagine it wouldve been so much better in person.
 Strengths: tremendous sense of community, Dr. Dellaporta and our TA, Matt, were simply incredible. Take this class if you can get in!!
 The course was all discussion based, so being completely remote was difficult. I think the course will be much stronger next year when it returns to in-person learning.
 Strengths: Dr. Dellaporta he's a phenomenal professor and really cared about the subject. He was also so understanding of student needs during the pandemic. Could not have asked for a better professor. The content was hard, but very manageable. It was a great balance of challenging but not overwhelming.
 The strengths of this class are the instructor as well as the format. The instructor did a great job being available to answer any questions. The format makes the class quite laid back and fun to participate in. There's less of an emphasis on getting a bunch of assignments done and more in-depth reading of a single paper.
 Strengths: the professor, the TA, the students of the class are all so welcoming! The papers were challenging at times, but they were all so relevant and current which really open your eyes to how advanced the field of genetics is! The best part of the class was the student presentation where we got to present on a topic of interest that was still related to genetics. Although I never personally used it, another strength is how flexible both the professor and TA are with office hours, so I am sure if I ever needed help understanding the paper, I can easily reach out to one of them or attend one of the student-run office hours
 Strengths: Fair work load, challenging material but just the right amount of difficulty and support to get students into that slightly uncomfortable growth zone. Weaknesses: None
 Strengths: The format of this course is my favorite of all the science classes Ive taken in my four years at Yale. Weekly discussions of 1 paper, no examinations. Such a structure enables students to really focus on the literature, and keeping it to 1 paper a week enables us to thoroughly digest a particular topicany more and it probably would be overwhelming. Prof. Dellaporta also makes a strong effort to present students with the latest and greatest papersso the science we are learning is quite fresh. Another great strength of this course, compared to other seminars Ive taken, is that there is reduced emphasis on getting through the whole paper on any given week. Rather, Prof. Dellaporta emphasizes question-asking, which slows us down as we go through the paper, but actually, in my opinion, helps us learn a lot more. In other classes I might feel bad for slowing down discussion by asking a question, but as Prof. Dellaporta clarifies, we dont need to feel bad for doing so here. Weaknesses: As far as weaknesses go, I can only think of logistical things. It would be helpful to get a bit more time to prepare for the presentation on the week that we are assigned. Furthermore, Prof. Dellaporta helpfully gives students a few pointers that they should capture during the presentationbut it may be even more helpful if those pointers came a little bit earlier, because I spent some time preparing material that didnt fall under those points before I got the email. Another note is that students sign up for introduction slots not knowing which paper (and in what field) they are going to present (this to me is not a big deal though).
