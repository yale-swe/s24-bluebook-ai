Â One strength is how immersed in Auvillar the course requires you to be. The lack of access to wifi and our continual engagement with the community forced me to be more present. In turn, I was able to reap more creative fruits. Another strength was the writing partner program. I had never received such a volume of feedback on my work before, and the extra pairs of eyes challenged me in ways I could have never anticipated. I also believe the hectic pace helped me evade potential creative ruts and exemplified to me that I can do this, I can produce so much work and not fixated on whether theyre good enough. One weakness lies in certain texts chosen for the syllabus. Seminar discussions made clear to me the impact Top of the World and the piece on Liberia could have, and I believe future students should have ample warning/context. I support the idea of cordoning off more controversial texts and placing them into a separate section, specifying that they are not model essays. I also believe future years schedules should always include a serious discussion on the homogeneity of Auvillar.
Â Prof Kinder did an incredible job giving feedback and staying organized on such a fast timeline. Weekly conferences improved my writing a lot, and she provided prompts that I was always excited to write about. Seminars were always passionate and I enjoyed doing the readings. Being disconnected from the internet was a major plus of this course, as it allowed me to truly focus on my writing, as well as form connections with other students that will extend well beyond the trip. Some readings in the syllabus seemed dated in the way they talk about foreign countries, and I think the syllabus could be updated, regardless of who is currently in the travel writing canon.

The course was extremely well run and gave us exposure to great essays, while really pushing us to draw on our own experiences and write. The writing partner system was one of the best parts and the no internet policy was very effective in making the whole group bond. We got to see a lot of France as well and think really critically about our place as travelers. Weaknesses: Some of the course readings could have been updated because their authors views conveyed prejudice towards place and people.
