2020-Summer ARBC S150 02 Adv Modern Standard Arabic I

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

ID: SU100 CRN: 30359 Term: 202002

123Forward
 Arabic 150 with Professor Elbousty allows you to improve your writing, speaking, reading, and understanding. It truly is a comprehensive course that allows you to improve in all areas.
 developed my arabic writing, reading, speaking, and vocabulary especially pertaining to current events and politics in the arab world
 My arabic reading and presenting skills really improved considerably in this class. I become so much better at reading news articles and opinion pieces regarding what's going on the Middle East. I also learned a whole lot about the politics and history of various countries in the Middle East. I also learned how to respond extemporaneously to other students' comments made in class
 I was able to develop a new appreciation for the Arab world and middle eastern politics. This was unlike any other language class I have taken because we spent a lot of time talking about contemporary issues in the language, which was much more beneficial for me to grow as an Arabic student.
 This is a phenomenal course. Take it if you want to learn more Arabic but also take it to engage in a high-quality Middle East current events and affairs seminar. Professor Elbousty has designed this course such that each week students focus on a theme related to the Middle East. In this session we each chose a country to focus our studies on and spoke about: Refugees and Statelessness, Women and Society, Islam and Democracy, Climate Change in the Middle East, and Authoritarianism (especially in the time of Covid-19). Unlike any other Arabic course you will take, this one will help you learn how to speak about important issues. You will be able to debate in Arabic, present policy proposals in Arabic, do public speaking in Arabic. This is truly so much more than just a language course... it's an enlightening experience. Professor Elbousty has designed it in a way that really helps you to develop and share your own personal beliefs on these important issues. The nearly 7 hours of in-class instruction every week were never dull, by the end of each meeting you won't want to leave.
 Coming from Arabic 140, my language skills improved immensely. Before this class, I could not read more than half a page of Arabic in a two hour session; now, I am up to two and a half pages. My speaking skills skyrocketed, and I got better at listening, though I still need more improvement in that regard. Thanks to Professor Elbousty, I am way more confident in my Arabic than I ever have been, and I am thrilled at the progress I have made. I am excited to continue progressing in the future.
 Speaking, reading, and listening to comprehension. The course focused on complex and relevant topics.
 This course offered an opportunity to really develop a lot of Arabic skills and expand my knowledge of the Middle East (as colonial as that term is, I am not aware of a better alternative). Our readings were all from various news outlets in the Arab world, which really helped strengthen my reading abilities beyond the previous textbook readings of L1-L4. Because we did multiple written reflections a week, this course also saw a marked improvement in my writing. I think that I wrote more for this class than I did in perhaps an entire semester of L3. And, that's not a bad thing, because it really was a way to improve my writing and the feedback we received was really detailed and very timely, so it never felt like I was going at it alone. Professor Elbousty's insistence on MSA in speaking, while difficult, definitely forced me to try to formulate better sentences and communicate at a higher register. And, of course, as we progressed through the course, my lexicon expanded dramatically.
 I improved in my reading, speaking, and writing skills in Arabic.
