2018-Summer ANTH S110 01 Intro to Cultural Anthropology

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30213 Term: 201802

Back123Forward
 It's a highly condensed course of a plentiful field. Professor and TA made it possible for us to understand clearly without too much effort. Readings are of high quality, high representativeness and can be approached by a stage 0 student in this field like me. TA is super patient and nice and can point out what good points prof made in class that we may have taken for granted. Classes are filled with laughters and witty comments and interactions. For me it's a perfect class. If there is anything that I would like to change, perhaps is to have a 'map' for all the notions and points that we might encounter in advance to make the process of thinking easier.
 The strength was adding value to the unknown, and the weakness was it only being a five week class.
 The strengths of this course are that it has interesting subject matter that is relevant to daily life in terms of observing other's anthropological means of communication. The material was taught and explained well and thoroughly in a way that encouraged thought. There are no weaknesses of this course.
 Strength: Compared to others, I do want to study anthropology in University and I got to know this subject before the summer session since I have an activity related to local culture heritage protection. Therefore, this course can help me to build some new perspective and I am willing to experience the change about my previous understanding, making my mind more open. Weakness: The Introduction to Culture Anthropology course itself demonstrates the meaning of the culture. In the class, since lack of the American culture background for understanding and knowing some idiom, customs, I got confused many times when professor or classmates from USA provide some examples into the discussion. I cannot get their points, why they said this, what is the point of mentioning this information. It would be better to ask them to provide some basic background to the rest of class. And the time for office hour is so short that I cannot have enough time to raise my doubts and have some conversations with professors.
 Things are generally mentioned in the answer of the first question. I will list them again in a very simple and concrete form. Strength: Cultural diversity of the students The great teaching methods and prodigious knowledge base of the professor The uniqueness of the class contents per se (no course like this in high school) The thorough understanding delivered between students etc. Weakness: I didn't come up with one yet. If I've got a chance, I would like to experience this whole thing again :)
 The course is very various in terms of the materials we got to look at. It covered not just old-school anthro but also some modern ones and ideologies. It could maybe include some works from foreign anthropologists, just to see how anthro is approached differently.
 I think if the course was designed so that the students could discuss class room readings and topics on a forum, or in a discussion thread on Canvas, it would've encouraged more of the students to be more engaged, and allow students to bounce ideas off of one another, to agree and disagree. Alot of the concepts are not hard and fast, there's alot of grey space, so I would've preferred it to be more interactive between students either inside the classroom, or online.
