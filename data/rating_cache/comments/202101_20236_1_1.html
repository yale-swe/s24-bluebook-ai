2021-Spring ER&M 200 01 IntroToEthnicityRace&Migration

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 20236 Term: 202101

 Strengths: Exceedingly welcoming environment (seriously - the most welcomed I've felt at Yale). Truly discussion-based, as opposed to pushing one view or opinion. Dedicated teaching fellows. Wonderfully kind and encouraging professor who was able to achieve student engagement despite the limitations of virtual learning. Weaknesses: Live lectures did not engage students enough. Only a select few students consistently participated. (Not true for breakout/discussion sections.)
 Strengths- everything!! professor camacho!!!! great workload, wonderful readings, interesting assignments, material is perfect. Weakness- I guess we couldn't go into depth of some of the things that we learned about, but that's expected in a introductory course!
 strengths -interesting and engaging readings/topics -fairly engaging lectures -phenomenal professor (I love Prof. Camacho!!) weaknesses -discussions felt a bit forced at times
 This course is a good survey. However, it was difficult to engage with, as a student with little to no background in these topics, because so many of the students in the class were ER&M majors at the end of their Yale careers who already had a good grounding in the subject. It was really interesting to listen to their discussions, and I learned a lot, but I didn't feel like I could really contribute anything. I'm not sure if that's really a weakness of the course, though.
 Discussion sections were too big, and lectures started to feel a bit repetitive. I think this class would have been better served by the traditional format of 2 50 minutes lectures and a 50 minute discussion section each week, rather than two 75 minute classes that rolled it all together
 strengths: Professor Camacho is wonderful and the TFs were so helpful and responsive; well-adapted to pandemic times; engaging lectures weaknesses: because it is a survey course, the class prioritizes breadth over depth
 This class is a great introduction into these key and relevant topics as it covers a broad range of racial and social issues. I think my only complaint, if you can even call it that, was that I would have liked to explore more in-depth on a couple of topics more. However, given the scope of the issues we needed to cover, I guess this would have been logistically very challenging.
 The class is extremely fast paced, so sometimes it feels as though we don't get to really understand certain concepts because we can only learn so much about a time in history in one week. The lecture was also not engaging enough, and it made it very easy to lose interest. The discussion sections were incredibly helpful.
 The readings were very great and varied. I wish we had had more time in section rather than in lecture, because I found that section was the way I got the most out of the readings.
 There is so much content and it is never clear how much or what we need to retain. The first few lectures are deceivingly confusing, it gets better afterward. The professor and TFs were nice. I think there needs to be more clear communication on what we need to know after each lecture. The Professor also went over time constantly. I think it would be helpful to get more direction on how we should write our assignments and what they're looking for. Maybe even just providing a sample for each would be great. It felt like there was just so much that was just unclear, even though it was a manageable course and the content was interesting and important.
 A huge strength is the breadth of topics covered, respecting so many important historical events as well as different peoples. I appreciate the mix of lecture and discussion section because lectures are super informative but can be overwhelming because of the intensity of the content, so it is good to have a space to digest that in smaller groups. It should have more time, though! We often did not have enough time between lecture and discussion, so maybe the way we split up the two components could be improved.
 i wish that the lectures themselves offered more opportunity for class engagement. it was hard for me to do nothing but listen for up to 75 minutes. otherwise the professor (and all of the TFs) were always very kind and helpful, the readings were always applied well, and discussions were very generative.
 Strengths: a wide range of interesting topics discussed, lectures were always engaging, light workload, AMAZING professor Weaknesses: discussion sections were not as lively sometimes
 Strengths: The material is so interesting and so important. Professor Camacho is absolutely amazing! One of the most caring professors I've ever met Weaknesses: sometimes due to the intensity of the material, and to the online format, participation in our small group sections was lacking which made them less enjoyable.

 With short readings and large discussion sections, it is easy for students to get through the course without learning a lot. With effort, though, it served as an insightful class.

 This class connects many elements of the ERM major, and gives brief overviews of lots of things related to ethnic studies, it provides a discussion space for people to learn about them for the first times
 Strengths: Class assignments allow you to be very creative, class readings build on each other, though this course is a survey course, it allowed me to gain robust understandings of some key concepts which I can look more into in future ER&M classes Changes: I think it would be helpful to have a few more slides with images!
 I wish the Canvas page would have been updated with lecture overviews consistently.
 The professor is very smart and knows a lot about a wide range of topics, but at times discussions in class weren't very smooth.
 I thought that this class worked really well as an overview of the topics. However, I think a few things that could make it better are making more connections between topics and reading more fundamental readings. For the first of those, I think that there were connections that were made across weeks, but they were less specific to the topics of the class like capitalism, resistance, etc. I would be interested to have traced more of the specific ideas like indigineity in conversation with the Middle Passage or anti-Semitism or Orientalism and terrorism or Orientalism and colonization of the continents currently known as the Americas. As for the second suggestion, I specifically remember that a lot of references to the book 'Orientalism' by Edward Said were made but that we had other readings those weeks instead. These occasional weaknesses speak to the strength of flexibility in this course though. This was a very, very challenging semester to learn and to exist, and Professor Camacho was very attuned to the difficulties students were facing. Fingers cross future semesters will contain less trauma in the globe, and these changes can have the space to be considered.
 Good class overall! Definitely harder to be engaged over Zoom but the discussion section component was helpful.

 Professor Camacho was a great professor and the class was well structured with clear units and readings.
 One major strength of this course was Professor Camacho who is special, intelligent, kind, humble, and so accommodating towards students. She made the course accepting and a safe place for students. I also like how the course is organized starting from race, the overarching theme in the class, then to indigenous studies and then so on in a timely order until the present.
 Professor Camacho introduces students to a wide variety of racial relations but theres a specific focus on only a few groups (Black Americans and migrants from South America). Most of the classes are in a lecture style and there is little time for students to actively participate.
 Strengths: the variety of topics that were covered, the professor's profound understanding of the course material, the relevant weekly readings Weaknesses: communication over assignments and expectations were not always clearly stated.
 Strengths: This course was phenomenal. The entire teaching team is incredibly understanding and wants to see you thrive and learn. No weaknesses.
 strengths: The content is interesting, engaging, and relevant and lectures weren't too long. The readings are also very manageable and interesting. weaknesses: sometimes section felt a bit unnecessary
 I wish we had more time in discussion section but this course was a great survey course.
 i thought the setup of this course was very good, especially the conversations we got to have in discussion section.
 I think this course could improve from a more structured time frame. At times, the live lecture would go over, and we would have little time during the virtual seminar. Discussing our readings was my favorite part of the class, and a better balance and management of time would be a great improvement.
 The overall organization of the layout of the course was a major strength as it is what allowed me to make connections between material of various weeks at all points of the semester.
