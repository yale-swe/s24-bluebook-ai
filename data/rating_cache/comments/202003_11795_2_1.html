2020-Fall ANTH 378 01 Postwar Vietnam

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 11795 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Professor Harms is extremely knowledgeable in this field and he did an amazing job of leading discussion.
 This course has many strengths and no weaknesses. Each week focused on a specific topic, and each reading could be tied back to previous readings. This structure in conjunction with the many mediums of knowledge (books, papers, documentaries, music videos) helped us develop an in-depth understanding of postwar Vietnam. Even though each class met for about 2 hours every week, our engaging discussions always made time fly by.
 Strengths: Prof. Harms as a seminar leader, and as an understanding professor. Prof. Harms was more aware of his students' challenges this semester than any other professor I had this semester, and he adjusted the syllabus to make sure that we were never overwhelmed with work.
 It was the perfect course. The readings were fascinating and we would just talk and share insights
 STRONG: this class was. so. cool. It was so unique. And Harms is so open-minded and understanding and helpful and great and he really makes the class (even when some students were a bit condescending, albeit likely unintentionally). All of us had different backgrounds: stats, erm, east asian studies, history, film and media, anth, etc. and not once did it feel like nobody had nothing to contribute. The class was such a low workload it was almost relaxing, but I was so motivated to do the readings that I found myself pushing off other classwork to do these readings! And, we had the option to do our research paper on COVID-19 in Vietnam (or not, which many chose) and he put us in contact with 2 contacts and then 2 more who were both super perceptive. In essence, you could write about whatever you wanted and be as argumentative as you wanted (i.e. take risks, like make a claim that may not have been entirely proveable given your lack of evidence, but b/c you really just wanted to say something). Grading was super clear and very fair, and he was so incredibly helpful every step of the way. I've never received clearer feedback or more help from a professor before so this was phenomenal. WEAK: the timeline assignment felt kina pointless. It wasn't really part of our grade, it didn't go anywhere, and without constant reminders, after like week 4, no one was filling any of it in. I did my part, and then never visited the doc again. Harms had us lessen the workload a few times by assigning us to take notes on individual chapters, becoming the designated spokesperson for that section so as to not have to read as intensely, but the amount of notes one took varied widely. There wasn't really any guidance--would've been nice to be like "find the thesis, organize into chunks, keywords, key quotes" (and maybe even offer a critique? wasn't an option there, kinda just rote reiterated what the authors said without considering the implications and validity, i.e. I really do not agree with some of Natalie Porter's far-fetched avian flu propaganda poster analyses, they were very "out there" so to speak) b/c some students went overboard and took like 5 pages of notes (single spaced) on like 5 pages which could've been avoided. A few classes felt a bit worthless; while it was nice to be given a week's worth of readings off to focus on our papers, coming to class to ask non-expert peers what to do about your research question was not conducive to learning, especially when some people spoke for 25 minutes and others only got 3 because there weren't any time limits and we ran out of time. Or, the last class was "discuss movie, listen to music, talk about papers" which not everyone had finished or even started writing, and we didn't even really get to talk about the movie? Discussion questions were also a bit dry sometimes, like "what'd you think" I mean idk man, I'm compelled to believe the author but I'm not an expert so please for the love! of! all! that! is! holy! ask a more exciting question and provide a more riveting answer instead of "nice." I mean, sure it was an ego boost to get the validation of "wow [student x] you really got it down" but was it conducive to learning? Nope lol.
