Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 Incredibly interesting course content, structured in a logical way by Professor Smith. The lectures are interesting, the discussions are generally thought provoking.
 Strengths: Professor Smith is deeply knowledgeable. He does a great job of picking out Lincoln material and pointing to important moments in his speeches/writings. And the material itself is very engagingLincoln was a truly special writer/thinker, and it was enjoyable to see how his thinking evolved from the 1830's through his presidency. Jack was also a great TA. Weaknesses: None.
 Strengths were the discussions because we talked about very interesting topics in Lincoln's speeches. A weakness was that near the end of the semester the seminars didn't line up with the readings we did for that week, so it was kind of confusing to go back and forth between weeks.
 Strengths: the Professor and TA were great, discussions usually went pretty well, the readings are incredibly interesting Weaknesses: the class was a bit large, maybe 15ish students would be more conducive, but it wasnt a big problem bc we broke off halfway through the seminar into two groups, one led by the Professor and the other the TA, not every reading gets discussed, some were not mentioned at all, at two ten-page papers and a final exam the work was a bit much
 Biggest strengths were the TF and Professor and the readings. In a moment where politicians have gone so low, reading Lincoln was quite refreshing. Biggest weakness was the amount of writing. Two 10-12 pages papers for a non writing credit class seemed a bit steep. I feel like not much would be lost reducing that requirement to 6-8 pages.
 The class has a large (but great) amount of reading, fun assignments, a great structure, and a fantastic syllabus. The only way it could be improved would be by getting rid of the final exam -- a final, long paper would be more rewarding. An otherwise splendid class.
 The strengthens were Professor Smith and Jack (the TA) who were both extremely helpful with essay feedback and understanding the content. Also, the discussions and lectures by Professor smith were great
 This course was very strong in virtually every aspect.
 I very much enjoyed this course
 The instructors are very passionate about the material. The readings are great. I definitely learned something interesting every class that gave me greater perspective into historical characters as American history as a whole. I left wanting to take more classes about particular historical characters. Eventually, if you understand to repeat what the professor has said in your writing (writing that he's grading, not the TF, who had a different style), then you learn a particular mode of thinking - one which is valuable but does not really forgive competing insights to his... Also, the lectures at the beginning of the semester were sort of a rehearsal of the background history we had been reading, more than a study of rhetoric, which was left up to the seminar half of the class, though it was added on to the end of each essay question. This was a weakness, because the seminars were a bit uneven, with different instructors. While both the professor and the TF were really nice, one section was more about students bringing up what they thought, and the other invited participation but was more about accepting the instructor's perspective. In the end, the former gave me a false sense of latitude in my writing, without enough formal knowledge of what they expected to see. However, both instructors were also extremely available for guidance during the writing process and for feedback afterward. I think that the class could benefit from some clarity about what kind of insight and analysis students are supposed to be learning from the class and eventually write about. Also, there should be narrower essay questions.
 strengths: prof smith does a great job w the two hour format: half is a lecture placing the speeches in context, the other half is a discussion seminar, which makes the class far more enjoyable/less tiring, smith's knowledge is really comprehensive, reading is relatively light, themes are hammered into you, TF Jack Greenberg is GREAT weaknesses: I did not like the final exam, would rather have written another paper, just felt sort of repetitive improvement: I think we should have read more primary sources responding to Lincoln to place him in context w his peers - e.g. more stephen douglas, seward, herndon, etc
