Â Fabian is very helpful, so I would definitely count him as a strength. He was flexible but kept me on track to meet the deadlines, and he gave good advice. Honestly, while I understand the need for deadlines, I didn't find the assignments very helpful for actually propelling me forward in my thesis planning. I hear from a lot of History students, this year and past years, that they can get through the fall semester without doing much work at all on their thesis, and I definitely found that to be true for me as well. I wrote the several short papers, I met the deadlines, but I would have found a different structure more helpful. Perhaps future students could instead have weekly deadlines for which they have to read, say, 1 book and 1 article and write summaries of them for every Sunday at midnight. Papers like the prospectus and annotated bibliography didn't force me to do any considerable reading/research, but if I had been forced to do assignments all semester like the ones I just described, I would be so much farther along in my thesis process. I have to hold myself accountable too, of course...I could/should be farther than I am. I just think a different structure could benefit a lot of students like me (and I think there are many, from what I've gathered) who feel overwhelmed by such a huge project and struggle to hold themselves accountable. I think the idea is that we are seniors and should be able to handle this project mostly on our own. But the reality is that we are burnt out, just trying to finish college, and the thesis is so daunting. We are not too old to have some structure imposed on us. If the existing structure doesn't change, a word of advice for other students: Don't meet the bare minimum requirements like I did, just because you can get away with it. Try to push yourself to set your own deadlines for reading every week.
