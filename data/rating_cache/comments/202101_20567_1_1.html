What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 I really enjoyed the course and there are not really many things that I would change. Winston is an incredible human being and a great professor. Unfortunately this was his last semester at Yale so the next time this course is taught may not be as good as the experience I had. The readings were well balanced and I really enjoyed the conversations that we had. I really liked the weekly journal idea as it got you to actually do the readings. My only complaint is that the workload was not very well scattered throughout the semester. We started off pretty lightly, having one assignment every two weeks, but during the end of the semester we had an assignment every single week. Also, I found it a bit unnecessary to have a fifth pset. It had by far the longest readings of any assignment in the semester and it was due right in the middle of reading week. Still, the psets did not take too much time and they were enjoyable to go through. Also Winston provided incredible feedback in each of the assignments that was really insightful.
 Strengths: Winston is so kind and cares about his students a lot. I liked that this class focused on learning about methods and reading/analyzing papers rather than computational work, it was a nice departure from the other classes in the department. Weakness: lectures were long and kind of dry sometimes, it was definitely hard to pay attention over zoom.
 Strength: Winston is by far one of my favorite professors at Yale. He is incredibly generous and humble the man can recall just about any date, page number, and author from hundreds of causal inference papers. But, he's not intimidating in the slightest! He's very soft spoken and always willing to let his students learn. I also had a reading group that was very helpful in discussing key concepts from the homework this was very helpful. Weakness: There was a "front row" in the virtual class that Winston would cold call to answer questions. It was a little nerve-wracking being in the front row, but I understand the rationale behind the engagement. I wonder if a less scary idea would be to have a few people in "charge" of the reading each week, and we can have them stir up discussion and answer questions?

 Winston is an amazing professor and does a really great job of explaining concepts in class. I enjoyed the balance of learning the theory of causal inference techniques and reading/critiquing papers that applied these methods.
 Strengths: Winston is probably THE biggest strength of this class. He is an enthusiastic professor that created a great class atmosphere for learning. One of the things that most helped with this was the "Journal Entry" assignments, which were low-stakes and a great opportunity for learning free from pressure. I thought the readings were well-selected and well-contextualized by Winston. This class is unique in the S&DS department in that the main focus is understanding techniques of causal inference and their assumptions, then critiquing and discussing the limitations of their application, often by reading journal articles/newspaper articles that rely on them. I feel WAY more comfortable and equipped reading these types of articles after this class. I think another huge strength is that we were able to gain insight into the causal inference community and how it's changed over time. We were able to read from scholars with competing views and get a sense of some of the debates. It's been exciting to see the same names show up in other areas too! Winston is extremely well-read in the area and always did a fantastic job in giving credit and referencing the works of others, often sharing optional supplementary readings ranging from newspaper articles to academic journal papers to blog posts. I don't think I've ever read as many optional readings as I have in this class! Weaknesses: I think offering this class in an earlier time slot in the day would improve engagement. 4-5:15 PM on Zoom presented a challenge at times, especially after several classes worth of Zoom already that day. I think this would have been less of an issue with in-person classes.
