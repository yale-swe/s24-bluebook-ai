Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 I took this class for fun/for my QR, which I don't recommend. Take this class if you're a stem kid and you have to. This is not a for fun kind of class. You will regret it, I promise.
 Only if they have to. John Hall is great and all, but you still have so much self studying to do, especially since you're competing with kids who already took multi in high school. Ximera is your friend. Find good peer tutors.
 Yes, I needed it to fulfill my major and pre med requirement.
 If you are looking at the class, you will most likely take it at some point in the future... It is not easy but I would definitely recommend taking it with Professor Hall!
 It's not a crazy course and it's definitely a manageable QR, especially if you've complete calc BC or multi in high school. I completed the course without taking multi in high school, so it was a bit challenging at times, but there are so many resources available to you that it's alright in the end. Not sure if I would've taken this course if it wasn't a prerequisite, though; the content is pretty cut and dry and not super enthralling.
 If you have to take it, take it. Be aware that it felt like a very grindy class, and make sure you read the reviews carefully to find a good professor that teaches it.
 If that student can take it with John Hall, yes. He runs the whole MATH 120 program, and he's an excellent professor. However, I've heard from friends in other sections that the other professors are very hit-or-miss. The course material isn't particularly exciting, but it's an important foundation for a lot of majors / paths of study. If you liked calculus I / AB calculus, you might like this class as well.
 Only if you need multivariable for your major. I can't say much about the comparison to ENAS 151 but people have said it is much more engaging is a slight bit easier. The class wasn't the most engaging and a lot of the concepts you had to self-learn. The exams were fair (though the true/false section worth usually a 1/5 of the exam kills people's grade) and were always just easier versions of the weekly psets. Though note that the class is usually not curved so you have to score in the A range on exams to get an A in the course. The psets did take a decent amount of time but weren't unreasonable.
 Yes! Professor Hall challenged us to think critically about the problems presented before us. Because of his class, I also have a much greater understanding of how vectors and multivariable calculus present themselves in space.
 Yes. I think this course can be very challenging if you're coming in without a very solid math background though considering the pace and difficulty of some of the topics. I enjoyed the course and thought the topics I learned were interesting
 I enjoyed the subject matter. If the student was interested in math and not afraid of a larger time commitment, I would recommend the course to them for sure.
 Yes, I would recommend this course to students who have done some Calculus before, and would like to study Mathematics or other related fields.
 Yes, although it requires lots of work, I was able to master basic concepts of Multivariable calculus.
 Yes - it is a good foundation for any STEM or math-based major.
 Yes, a good QR credit for those who felt comfortable with calculus in high school
 I don't plan on pursuing any STEM-related major. I took Math 120 anyway, and I'm glad I did. Working through long integration problems really did develop my quantitative reasoning.
 100%! But please try to have some familiarity with the topics covered....either from Khan or somewhere else........just to make ur semester smoother.
 I would recommend this course to any student seeking to major in STEM, or otherwise looking for a challenging QR course. I would not recommend it to anyone who does not fall within those categories.
 It depends. Math 120 had classmates with all kinds of math backgrounds. There were some fresh out of Math 115/Calculus II/BC (me included), and others who already took Multivariable. Math 120 might not be a good first exposure to Math at Yale, since there is a lot of self-studying involved. Those who already took multivariable might be engaged in applications that were not learned before, but they may find the material a little repetitive. Others who have never taken multivariable before may find this material much more engaging but also very difficult. If you are interested in more application-based maths, Math 120 is a good course. If you like calculus, Math 120 will be a fun challenge for you, but if you don't enjoy learning calculus, don't take this course.
 I would recommend this course to another student. While it requires a lot of time outside of class, it is a class that leaves you with that much knowledge. I took it because it was needed in a major that I am interested in, but if it were for QR credit, I'd look to other classes.
 I would not recommend this course to a student unless they have to take it. The instructors were kind and seemed to enjoy teaching, but, at the same time, I experienced a lack of care about students' learning and progress. I have read accounts of other students' experiences of this class in more normal years, and it is the same. This course can be a gatekeeper course for students, rather than one that gets them excited about math. I genuinely enjoy and find interesting the math that I learned, but wish that the class was structured in a way that did not just impart the information, but aimed to really have you learn it and enjoy doing so.
 Yes, Math 120 was an excellent class to take. It establishes a lot of fundamental mathematical theories that are important in almost every STEM subject.
