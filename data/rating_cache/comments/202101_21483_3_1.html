Â ABSOLUTELY!!! I would consider this a must-take for all MCDB majors (it would be very hard to get a spot for non-majors). The course is a fantastic balance of challenging concepts but limited work, with great instruction from Professor Dellaporta. The course is a small seminar that discusses a genomic paper each week. The only assignments are reading the papers and participating in class, which is perfect because you can then focus on diving deep into the material rather than worrying about tests or busywork. To some, reading a paper each week might sound tough, but the papers chosen for the class are always really well written and interesting, so it's not hard at all, and I found myself really enjoying it. Discussions were always excellent and quite enjoyable, Prof. Dellaporta guides students and contributes his vast knowledge while also not overstepping and this creates an awesome, student-driven conversation each week. You'll learn a huge amount about genomics, from a variety of perspectives (including how genomics are used in cancer research, rare disease research, ancestry studies, population sciences, and even public health) There are no downsides to this class whatsoever, and I couldn't recommend it more. Take it!!! Make sure to email Professor Dellaporta early in the fall semester to get the application (due in December).

In addition to weekly discussions, our final involved choosing a paper of our own interest and presenting it to the class, which was an extremely rewarding and exciting way to end the semester. Overall, this course, if you are able to get a spot, is one of the best MCDB courses available - and you should take it 10000%!
