Â The biggest strength of this class is the professor. Professor Muravnik is one of the best professors at Yale, he is very kind and attentive to his students. He is always very engaged in the seminar and flexible in terms assignments. As for the material, reading primary documents and comparing different points of view is a great way to learn history. The biggest weakness was that we focus to much on the readings and spent a lot of time in class just answering the questions, which were directly related to the text. I think it would be better if readings only served as a background and we were able to learn more new material in class and discuss share our own thoughts on the subject instead of reciting the assigned readings.

Â The main weakness of this course is that all of the knowledge is taught based around primary readings. Â This course is extremely well-organized, interesting, and facilitates student learning greatly. The readings, assignments, and in-class discussions really worked very well together. The course was split up into four units, each covering a few months of the revolution. After each unit, there was a quiz and exam, which really helped with solidifying the knowledge of the material before moving on to the next unit. All of the excerpts that we read added something interesting to my understanding of the Russian Revolution and they complemented each other well because it gave us the opportunity to see and understand different perspectives of the same events. The professor was wonderful and very supportive, always giving us clear feedback on all of our assignments. Although this is not a weakness, I think that the student presentations sometimes were not as helpful as our in-class discussions and breakout room conversations. Although they were interesting and it was great to hear how other students understood the text, I think that sometimes the presentations took up a lot of class time and there was a lot of material to cover so sometimes we did not get to everything. However, overall, the course is really wonderful and I would recommend it.
