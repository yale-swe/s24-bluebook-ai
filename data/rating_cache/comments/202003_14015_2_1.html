Â The strengths of this course were the guest speakers (a wide range of organizers from different organizations and backgrounds that all eloquently spoke about their own political biography and the work of their organization), the accompaniment project placements (allowed us to pursue our interests and gain skills working for a community organization), the wide range of organizations we discussed and the different perspectives we were introduced to, and the small group discussions in breakout rooms during class. Finally, a strength is that this course taught practical and applicable skills as opposed to just theory. I appreciate that the course didn't have typical papers and exams but instead gave us real-world experience working for a community organization that we reflected on in an end of term paper. The weaknesses of this course were that it sometimes felt rushed or that we were trying to cover too much in a short amount of time. But I can also appreciate the value of being introduced to so many different organizations and organizers to learn from them. Some of the readings were also dense and long and felt more theoretical than practical. The accompaniment projects, while a huge strength, were also varied and because of that, the workload each student had was different. Based on one's accompaniment project placement and how established or active the organization was, some students had significantly more work or were more involved than others in addition to the weekly classwork and readings.
