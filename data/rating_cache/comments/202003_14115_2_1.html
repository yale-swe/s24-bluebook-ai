2020-Fall HLTH 240 01/GLBL 193 01 Epidemiology & Public Health

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 14115/14116 Term: 202003

 No weaknessses! You can tell Prof. White enjoys teaching the class. I thought the way assignments were weighted and divided was fair and I enjoyed working on the variety of weekly cases with other people. I also loved her idea of the mental health pset it actually made a huge difference in how I carried and maintained my health throughout the semester, and I thank her for that!
 This is a great courseDr. White is incredible, and one of those professors who genuinely cares about her students and her students' learning. She's the most important strength of this course. It's also generally thoughtfully designedit touches on public health/epidemiology from lots of different angles, and there are a lot of different opportunities for learning (ie, time spent on SPSS, time spent working in groups on cases, reading, low-pressure quizzes, the health case competition group project, the daily health goals assignment). We engaged a lot on current issues (obviously...) and I also really appreciated that. Honestly, I think that one of the only ways this course could be improved were if it could meet in person, which will hopefully happen next year. Dr. White showed us a lecture or two of her teaching in person, and I really hope future students get to experience that energy.
 strengths- professor amazing, so sweet, loved her, so smart weakness- not really any, but i think an exam or something may have helped me retain the info a little better, because there was no active recall
 One of the greatest strengths of this class is the group case studies. They help reinforce the knowledge gained from lectures and also encourage collaboration, which is important in public health (or any field, actually)! They can be a bit long and sometimes challenging, but the TAs and the professor are always there to offer help at any moment. However, if you do not work well with groups, this may be a little stressful (but also helpful to sharpen your group skills!). Also, Professor White made self care an assignment! This helped me actually put effort into my well-being throughout the semester, which was really important especially due to Zoom fatigue and all the stress from the pandemic. One potential downside is learning SPSS. It's a little challenging, but Professor White walks you through everything that you have to know, so it's definitely doable (I had no coding experience going into the class and I was fine).
 Strengths: Professor White and the TAs- They make the energy of the class so comfortable and fun which makes engaging in the class feel so natural and easy. Real-world applications- everything in this class has a real-world application that immediately made my understanding of public health much more sophisticated and gives a general context to all of my future learning in public health and epidemiology. 20% of our grade was dependent on us committing to wellness practices and taking care of ourselves... amazing Statistics!!! We learn some statistics which is super helpful to those who haven't taken statistics or want to soon. However, you absolutely do not need any stats background to do really well. The days we worked on cases were really fun (even on zoom) and you learn a lot from your peers and make friends! Weaknesses: A lot of information in the lectures! I came up with a system to take notes and return to lectures again later to understand them more deeply later OR I focused on understanding more information in lecture and returned to take brief notes later as it was a little difficult to do both because lectures moved quickly. Makes sense though because we had only one lecture a week with case studies on Wednesdays.
 The course was perfect me, although it's important to note that I had a background in stats and public health
 I really like the format of the course which was a mixture of lectures and group work. Doing hands-on problems was the best way to learn the material. The only thing that could be improved is instructions for some of the questions. I felt that sometimes instructions for projects/problem sets were a bit vague. But that is also consistent with the nature of public health where there are many correct answers.
 Strengths- case days were very engaging and hands-on, professor and TAs were always available to answer questions Weaknesses- lecture days were often dry; attendance not mandatory, so many students would not show up
 The course was organized in a way that allowed the material to build on itself, with the perfect amount of practice! I enjoyed Dr, White's ability to make the topics in the class enjoyable and relate them back to real-life examples.
 I liked that the course was focused more on discussions and applications of the material that we learn, instead of just the lectures. I also appreciated the focus on mental health in the course.
