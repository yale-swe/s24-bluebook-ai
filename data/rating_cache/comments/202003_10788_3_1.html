2020-Fall ARCH 150 01 Introduction to Architecture

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC602 CRN: 10788 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 We had live lectures where the cameras were required to be on. This made the class more engaging than others where live attendance was optional and/or cameras could be off.
 They literally forced us all to have our videos on throughout (even though it didn't say we had to in the syllabus). This was not helpful at all and did not put into consideration that not all students are able to do so.
 The section! Even though it was held over Zoom, the TAs were still so nice and helpful so I still felt super engaged with the course.
 The lectures were excellent, even virtually. There was come confusion about whether students needed to be in attendance synchronously and if so, whether their cameras needed to be turned on at all times, and I would really encourage the instructors to clearly state these expectations at the beginning of the semester. Students cannot be told, past midterm, that they will receive failing grades without their cameras on, with absolutely no knowledge of such a policy. Many earnest, hardworking students had not done so, and this was a very strange complication that felt quite unfair to the students. These complications over grading felt really hard for students, as this course did require many assignments every week, and did not quite follow through in giving feedback until it felt too late, and believe this is symptomatic of the online reformatting.
 Not much more could have been done, in my opinion, the Professor and TAs did an incredible job being accommodating to the students and tailoring the assignments and lectures to the students.
 They were really strict about attendence considering this was a lecture course. You were marked absent if your camera was off. I don't think that's fair, given that they don't know what people's homes lives are like, plus since the lectures are recorded people could just watch them later.
 The lectures are prerecorded, but we must be present in class to watch them. I think the lectures should either be live (recorded for students to review if needed) or prerecorded without the expectation that the students be present during a specific time.
 Access to recorded lectures was extremely helpful, especially for students who could not attend the live lectures. However, there was a lack of clarity regarding attendance. At first, I assumed attendance would not be strictly graded, though it was mentioned later that students are required to attend live lectures. It was a bit confusing for students in different time zones who could not attend.
 The group project was sort of a disaster. I think its really unreasonable to expect students to draw together when they cant meet up.
 I think the recorded lecture component was the most effective in making this semester less difficult. I appreciated having the opportunity to go back and rewind the lectures; however, the two reviews of the semester were announced a week prior. The short notice could have proved difficult for my schedule, given that the final review was 3 hours.
 More one on one check-ins, even if it were to be virtually.
 I'm not sure the lectures had to be mandatory (attended synchronously) - there was not much engagement anyways since we were just watching prerecorded lectures anyways. A better format could be to watch the lectures on our own time and then optionally come together to discuss or ask any questions regarding the lectures.
 Showing work over zoom/online was harder and not as conducive for appreciation of the work
 We used a virtual whiteboard called Miro that allowed everyone to look at their group's drawings better, that was a helpful improvement than just using a PDF with everyone's drawings.
 I wish that Teaching Assistants, as well as the professor in the future, is a little more lenient when it comes to engaging with the course, especially if its an online semester.
 All of it worked! Perhaps we could have gone on a section-wide socially distanced walk?
 I believe this class is meant to be taken in an in-person setting, consequently the virtual version of the course often lacked in conversation.
 Lectures and online review of our work was very well executed.
 Being required to keep our videos on in class felt unnecessary. We're watching a recorded lecture, not participating in a discussion. I'm not sure what keeping our videos on really accomplished? Certainly I can understand being encouraged to keep our cameras on, but sometimes it is unnecessarily uncomfortable or challenging (personally I had a back injury that made it really difficult to sit upright for the whole lecture, but this is definitely more of a challenge for people in other time zones).
 Class sections were a huge success.
 I really enjoyed using Miro as a pin-up board for our sketchbook assignments, as it made it very helpful to view when one of the TF's was sharing their screen. The pre-recorded lectures often ran over time, but they were more focused than live lectures. However, sometimes the professor would want to ask a question to the students (or we would want to ask a question to the professor), and either the question never gets asked or there is confusion in what it is in reference to.
 Online lectures were successful, as they were also posted on Canvas which allowed for future viewing. I would say that online sections could be improved a bit. Maybe the TAs could have been more interactive in teaching us sketching methods and techniques, for example.
 The recorded lectures were very helpful for our learning during the pandemic.
 I really enjoyed the sketchbook and final reviews, I think the entire teaching staff did a great job adapting those events in the online format.
