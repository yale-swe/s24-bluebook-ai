What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

 A more detailed and fundamental understanding of chemical mechanisms, principles, and syntheses.
 I increased my knowledge of chemical systems and strengthened my understanding of carbonyl-based mechanisms.
 Alkyne rxns, Diels Alder, carbonyl chemistry
 This class actually teaches you how to THINK about organic chemistry!! Unlike other memorization-heavy chemistry courses, this course is focused on teaching us major chemistry concepts and allowing us to apply these concepts. I feel like I've walked away from the course knowing so much more about fundamental organic chemistry, and ways to apply these principles through synthesis/mechanisms/etc.
 So many reaction mechanisms--and also their patterns that make them similar. Being able to draw out arrow pushing mechanisms for mechanisms and propose reagent pathways for retrosynthesis.
 We went through Diels-Alder, carbonyl chemistry, enolates, amines, and many many other chemical reactions. We learned how to do retrosynthesis efficiently, and also the underlying concepts behind the reactions, such as structure-reactivity trends, acidity and how it all relates to bio in some way.
 Built well off of what I learned in Orgo I. Biggest thing I took out of it was developing a better intuition with orgo that I didn't really have down-pat from 174. In Orgo II I learned about the Diels-Alder reaction, alkynes, a lot about carbonyls, enolate chemistry, and the nitro group (which was an extra topic this year because we had time leftover).
 Organic chemistry II concepts

 I learned a lot about carbonyl chemistry and some very fancy, complicated, chemical reactions.

 Expansion of organic chemistry knowledge from CHEM 175. We learned many different chemical reactions grouped in categories: carbonyls, enolates, carboxylic acids, amines, etc
