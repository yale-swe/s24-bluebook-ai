2020-Fall MENG 361 01 MechEng II: Fluid Mechanics

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

 Core concepts about fluid mechanics.
 Fluids is probably one the coolest thing you learn as a mech e. You learn the skills that can let you be an aerospace engineer or an F1 designer and tons of things in between. The class is very math heavy and you need to learn some proofs, but in general it is a lot of ideal gas law and Navier-Stokes integrals.
 Study of fluids from subsonic to supersonic conditions.
 I learned about fluid mechanics, from hydrostatics, control volume theory, and differential governing equations to dimensional analysis, pipe flow, potential flow, and compressible flow.
 Analyzing fluid flows.
 I learned basic fluid mechanics - how liquids/gases move through pipes, or in a more aerodynamic setting.
 I was able to gain a fundamental understanding of fluid mechanics, as well as skills to analyze a different types of engineering problems and apply the knowledge learned in order to solve them.
 I learned a whole lot about fluid mechanics as well as some very interesting applications and stories related to the field that helped solidify my knowledge.
 Fluid mechanics; really interesting topics ranging from vortices to hydrostatic pressure
 I learned about fluid mechanics. Pretty standard engineering course.
 evaluating systems with moving fluids, dimensional analysis, hydrostatics, Bernoulli's equation, pressure, volume and temperature relations, applications and history of fluids mechanics
 This was a standard engineering lecture class. We learned about fluid mechanics while reinforcing some of the things we learned in physics, chemistry, and thermodynamics. Only time will tell if this will properly prepare me for MENG363L, but I feel like I learned a substantial amount.
 Learned about different characteristics of fluid dynamics, such as ideal gas relations, influence of the Reynolds number, and streamlines
 A wide spread of fluid dynamic situations, primarily with liquids. Key topics being the Naiver-Stokes EQs and Bernoulli's EQ
 Loads of knowledge regarding fluids mechanics as well as some fun history facts associated with it
 Learned about fluids and how to analyses them. Also how to apply differential equations.
 An overall understanding of fluid dynamics
 Understanding of fluid mechanics equations, Navier Stokes, incompressible flows, basic compressible flows, vortices and sources/sinks
 laminar flow, pressure elevations, (conservation of mass and momentum) continuity, various other mathematical model theories for fluids along with non-dimensional analysis.
 the mechanics of fluids and gaseous elements
 The fundamentals of fluid dynamics. Pressure, hydraulics, viscosity, turbulence, speed of sound, Mach numbers, Reynolds numbers
 I learned how to answer simpler quantitative questions about the fluid flows. Ive learned how to perform dimensional analysis (using the Buckingham Pi Theorem and other techniques), apply and derive formulas like those of Bernoulli and Navier-Stokes, and to characterize and solve simpler issues in boundary layer flows.
