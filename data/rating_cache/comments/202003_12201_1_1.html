2020-Fall ER&M 241 01/SOCY 138 01/ANTH 140 01 The Corporation

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401 CRN: 12202/12203/12201 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 New outlook on my daily interaction with the corporate world.
 This class offers much literature and material regarding the evolution of capitalism as well as corporate America.
 I learned a lot about the history of corporations and how they became the entities we know now; I also developed a new language for them and leanred about the various ways they have intervened with our ideas of individual personhood, the creation of the "economy" and the wellbeing of a populace, sovereignty and race/gender. It was really really helpful, insightful, and I think every econ and business major should take this class. It's a new way of looking at things.
 I've developed an ability to understand and think about corporations through a variety of lenses that aren't necessarily natural, and to think about the historical formation of corporate power and how it relates to corporations and corporate power today. This also relates to corporation production, personhood, racial formation, and culture. I also learned how to read ethnographic research and write my own (including ethnographic interviews).
 Learned to think critically about corporations through various different lenses and in various contexts.
 I developed knowledge about the ways corporations and people interact with one another from the early modern era to the present. Concepts like corporate sovereignty, Fordism and post-Fordism, corporate social responsibility (CSR), corporations and the legal system, and the integration of finance with corporate governance were all topics we explored. Additionally, despite coming in with no knowledge about what anthropology or ethnographies were, I left the course knowing the basics of the ethnographic method and with an appreciation of the anthropological field. This class is so interdisciplinary and cross-cutting and relevant to everyone's daily life anyone will benefit from taking this course.
 Many! I enjoyed learning to write ethnographies.
 An understanding of the corporation's evolution throughout time, not only that but origins of "the economy," capitalism, etc. Such a good course that changes the way you see and relate to these things that we take for granted.
 How to evaluate a corporation, corporate form, the history of a corporation, relationship between corporations and persons in various settings and the relationship between ethnicity and indigenous groups and corporations
 I learned a lot about the corporate form, but my real takeaway from this class was how much I enjoyed anthropology as a field of academic study.
 I learned a lot about anthropological research methods and about corporations.
 An intellectual toolkit for analyzing corporations and their effects.
 I gained extensive knowledge about the ways in which corporations and modern capitalism interact with the people that navigate and create these structures. So fascinating!!!
 An understanding of the evolution of the corporate form over time, including consumption, production, and critiques, and how to use ethnographic tools to evaluate corporations.
 I learned about the evolution of corporations and their forms over time, as theyve been used as a force not only to structure labor, but which create difference and sameness and reinforce hierarchies of gender, race, sexuality, and more.
 I gained a lot of insight into the legal formation of corporations, the different types of corporations, and broader concepts like corporate sovereignty, personhood, and responsibility.
 Knowledge of legal and social theory and improved my anthropological writing skills.
 I gained a historical and anthropological understanding of the modern corporate identity and its origins. The insights this class has equipped me with has helped situate me in the professional world.
 A foundational understanding in analyzing corporate entities. This goes from their social responsibilities and beyond, with an excellent breadth of analysis.
 Developed a critical toolbox that I can use from here on to analyze corporate forms under our current economic framework

 Definitely all the different modes through which one can analyze corporations. Also some basics of anthropology
