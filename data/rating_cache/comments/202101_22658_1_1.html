 Strengths: The course readings and lecture content give you a broad understanding of many aspects of Middle Eastern politics that were impacted by the Arab Spring. This is a really thorough introductory course. The weekly live lectures, where Professor Nugent would interview experts on the MENA region, was so fascinating.  Good class
 The readings were quite interesting, and I really enjoyed section. My TA, Drew, was great and we always seemed to have lively discussions. The lectures were generally interesting as well. Overall, this was a great course for understanding the cultural and political aspects of the Middle East as a general region. I wasn't a fan of the weekly guest speaker. It was difficult to stay focused, especially on Zoom. I think if this class had happened in person the guest speaker component would have been quite engaging, but I often found myself working on other things during this time slot each week. A better way to format the class would be to provide more lecture material and go a bit deeper on a narrower range of topics; this could be compensated by limiting guest speakers to every other or every third week, rather than each week. This type of format would simultaneously address what I feel are the two biggest weaknesses of the course: the surface-level coverage of a wide array of topics (as opposed to a more confined focus) and the outsize presence of guest speaker presentations as a pillar of the course.
 There was very little student engagement in this course. There were only pre-recorded podcast-style lectures and town halls with guest speakers where our questions were mandatory, submitted before the town hall, and asked by the professor, not us. Everyone had their camera off for almost the entire semester. It s a nice course if you're looking for an easy grade without having to do much work. But if you want to learn and engage, I'm not so sure.

 I think the strengths of the course is its structure. We had prerecorded lectures as well as townhalls with many interesting guest speakers. The workload is really light, and the readings, videos, lectures, and townhalls are all really interesting. Professor Nugent is very knowledgeable about the region, and does a great job of making the material accessible to students who have never studied the Middle East before. I also think the fact that the course covers such variety of topics makes the course interesting for students across all fields of study at Yale. The one weakness of the course is that the guidelines for the final paper were extremely vague (i.e. no rubric), and the very strict word limit and source number requirement felt tedious and unnecessary.
