2018-Summer ANTH S110 01 Intro to Cultural Anthropology

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

ID: SU100 CRN: 30213 Term: 201802

123Forward
 The whole course is a multi-flavored pie of cultural-linguistic anth in general. Everybody can find something they are found of. The key methodology are Semiotics, Social status-role, ontology(basic)-inference relations, which are all elaborated and instanced. Professor also showed us the whole development path of anth in the past 50 years from classic ethnographies to modern, unusual sites that might trick you into a research, as well as abstract notions that are disclosed through research and the ontology of such research (or the enclosure process of the researcher, after all anth is a subject with high self-awareness).
 Never before have I viewed the world as complicated as I have before I took this class. The Sign-object-interpretant relationship is mind-blowing, and I am a different person for learning it. This course simply changes the way you view the world for the better.
 I developed a new way of thinking about anthropology and I was able to apply the skills learned in class to daily observations. I learned more about and understood the relationships between everyday objects and their relation to different cultures. It was a class that opened my perspective to the world around me and as a result of this class, I am able to perceive the world in a different way with the skills about observation that I acquired through this class.
 sign, object, interpretant etc. semiotic terms
 Though this course is about anthropology, professor contained knowledge of economy, physics, semiotics and etc in his lecture. All in all, he taught me to think interdisciplinarly, to see the connection among seemly different subject and joint them to tackle the problem in human society.
 In the Introduction to Cultural Anthropology course, I learned a lot of anthropological concepts and ideas such as semiotic process, status-role relationship through a lot of deep reading and exploration of ethnography works written by famous anthropologists. This course widens my understanding of some commonly used daily words, such as culture. It allows me to catch the sign, object and other subtle yet important things in the world, building anew perspective to my observation of the daily world.
 There are four different divisions of the study of Anthropology. Choosing cultural Anthropology class could be one of my wise decision, because I learnt so much as a future undergrad of Humanities. First of all, knowledge. I learnt specific knowledge about ancient cultures, whenever and wherever these cultures originate and locate, they brought me beyond my own country, beyond the U.S. For instance, the belief in witchcraft of the Azande people, and the essential role of cattle in the culture of the Neur. Indeed, knowledge within text is closed connected with the ideology of the authors. In this course, I analyzed more than ten documents and studies the mindset of those writers, including our professor Mr. Kockelman, who wrote professionally regarding the concept of Anthropology. Specific term include semiotic background, indexicality and so on. His words and realistic examples assist our class to a passionate and interesting one. Secondly, skills. The vision taken by anthropologist and me is the biggest obstacle that prevent me from becoming one of them (LOL). This course taught me to better understand some social phenomena around me, as well as better understand the actions of my intimate family members or friends. Its unnecessary to break things down into sign, interpretant, and object, but it will sometimes assist me to draw links and connections through figuring out the function of certain cultural mediums. Moreover, the proper way to write an academic essay with ethnographic examples is also what I learnt from our classes. Finally, insights. I learnt different people and various cultures, but I still need to reflect how to apply them into everyday lives and plentiful fields. The discussion between students with distinctive cultural background on class is also what contribute to my increasing interest, in another word, I will be able to learn insights from other students and take advantage from the platform provided by YSS instead of only the professor per se. Application of Ethnography is the principal method I observed in the study of Anthropology. In this sense, thanks for further raising my cultural pride, and making me cherish the glory of my own cultural background. I will probably access into a more thorough understanding about my own culture afterwards.
 I'm new to the concept and disciplines of anthropology, so taking this intro course really helped me learn and have kind of an insight into what anthro is about. Definitely get to learn about a bunch of different cultures I didn't know before, but more importantly I get to be familiarized with the mindset of how anthro, especially ethnography, examines cultures and worlds.
 I learned how to read critically, and to apply the examples from the readings to abstract concepts of the class, and to carefully construct an essay.
