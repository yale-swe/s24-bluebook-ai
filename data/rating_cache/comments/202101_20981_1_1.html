 I think the strength of the course is the lecture style. A lot of people complain about dry lectures that you can read off the lecture notes but this is probably the first lecture class I've taken where traditional lecture notes would really not do you much good. It's all about the approaches you take to a particular issue, and the interesting ideas and claims that Prof. Easterling makes along the way that you want to pay attention to. The one improvement I would make is that Prof. Easterling has slides with numbers on them to mark "interplays" and it would be nice to have an updated powerpoint with each one to better track the themes throughout the course.
 The lectures were phenomenal and the readings were incredibly interesting. For some reason, however, engagement of others in discussion section and lecture was incredibly weak. Maybe there need to be stronger incentives to get people to read and participate? Also, we never get a single grade in this course until the very end -- that accords well with the culture and fit but makes it very unpredictable and feel less like an academic class which demands academic engagement.
