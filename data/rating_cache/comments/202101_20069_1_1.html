2021-Spring ASTR 030 01 SearchForExtraterrestrialLife

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 20069 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 The strengths were that the class was really organized and Professor Fischer really spent time focussing in on the important topics that help us think about the search for extraterrestrial life. It was not a high-stress class and all the lecture videos and content were really manageable, especially for a non-stem major like me! The only weakness I can think of is that I wish we spent more time on the specific missions for the search for ET life rather than on the mathematical calculations.
 I think the interdisciplinary approach to the course was a strength, I feel like I've gained competency in a lot of different areas. The format of the course was a little weird, it wasn't really a seminar but it also wasn't a lecture or a lecture+seminar, it honestly felt most like a discussion section.
 Strengths: Great professor, engaging classes, reasonable workload. Really makes you think.
 Dr. Fischer is by far the biggest strength of this course. She is one of the best professors I have had at Yale.
 Strengths: Professor Fischer. She's one-of-a-kind. I've never met someone so humble and accomplished who has never failed to speak her mind--and thank goodness she did! I also think her flexibility and willingness to accommodate people's remote learning statuses were super considerate. The weekly assignments (Sunday discussion questions, homeworks, quizzes, and blogs) sometimes felt overwhelming but when I came out of the course, I seriously felt that they all played non-negotiable roles in my learning. The discussion questions for motivating me to finish the week's pre-lectures earlier, homeworks to practice concepts in lecture, quizzes to know critical astronomical background info, and blogs to synthesize my own learning with that of the rest of the scientific community. Weaknesses: the pre-recorded lectures got hard to keep up with roughly 2/3 into the semester, especially when all the content for the week was longer than 1hr. I think the professor was aware of this because the viewership of lectures declined sharply. She made every effort to keep us engaged though and I recall that she would sometimes summarize important concepts and give us relevant breakout room worksheets to make the lecture content useful. Really, this more of a systematic weakness of online learning that anything of Professor Fischer's fault.
 Strengths: The content of the course was extremely engaging, and we delved into a variety of topics within the fields of astronomy and the search for extraterrestrial life. The writing assignments and homeworks lended us an opportunity to apply our learning, while also challenging our own perspectives. Breakout rooms enabled us to work with other students and engage with one another, which was especially beneficial during virtual learning. Weaknesses: I feel as though a larger portion of the content could have been focused on SETI, as there was a disproportionate focus on very specific scientific knowledge. Although understanding basic astronomy concepts was necessary, it sometimes felt as though we were expected to retain substantial amounts of technical knowledge that was difficult to grasp from a singular pre-recorded lecture (especially in quizzes). In the future, I would hope that the course not only focuses on quantitative and scientific reasoning, but perhaps more so on theory, ideas in the scientific literature, etc. For example, the Drake equation was only introduced very late in the course, and an entire week was spent on climate change models, which is certainly important but used up two whole classes that could've focused on SETI.
 Strengths: fascinating content, wonderful professor Weaknesses: sometimes the amount of reading and number of videos could get a bit heavy! Although it was always manageable
 Strengths: Prof. Fischer is a fantastic teacher and was really committed to this class. The subject matter will fascinate science nerds and other curious minds alike, and she has been directly involved in all of it. This class will change the way you think not just about our scientific endeavors and place in the Universe, but also the role of humanity more broadly. Weaknesses: The course tried to teach too many things at once - basic astronomy, basic biochemistry, the theories of the formation of life, climate change, in addition to diving deep into Kahneman's book "Thinking Fast and Slow" as a guide to scientific thinking - which became rather chaotic. The workload was also somewhat uneven throughout the semester: there was a huge crush of work towards the middle of the semester, followed by a lull and a relatively short period to construct a semester paper. At times, it seemed as if Prof. Fischer was constructing the course as she went along, but I'm confident this will get better in future iterations of this course.
 Strengths: Professor Fischer, the super interesting topic, the small class size, the choose-your-own writing approach, and pretty much everything else about the course! Weaknesses: Absolutely none.
 This is a great course. I particularly enjoy the wide range of topics this course covers, not just focusing solely on the search for ET life. The readings and videos assigned each week are very enriching. The group work activity in each class is also helpful for facilitating learning.
 Strengths: Prof Fischer really cares about her students and her unique experience with SETI makes the discussions very engaging. Also, the semester paper was a great final assignment! Weaknesses: sometimes the lectures were very content-heavy (especially in terms of numbers and formulas).
