What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

Â Professor Yeazell is a great lecturer who is exceedingly knowledgeable about these books: not just their contents, but their historical contexts. I am so glad to have been introduced to these texts through this class. Had I not, I would have missed so much. Also, this course benefits from the richness of the texts studied. Middlemarch and Great Expectations, in particular, are fantastic, and they were taught to perfection in this class. The only weakness I can identify in this class was the content of discussion sections. I really wished we had taken more time to talk about the content of both the texts and the lecture. Often, the discussion sections felt tangential in a way that wasn't helpful to my overall understanding of the books.
Â The strengths of this course were its fascinating curriculum and the teaching team that made it come to life. Despite the fact that we were sitting on opposite ends of the country each week, I loved the critical conversations and debates that my class continued to have even over the medium of Zoom. The connections we were able to establish over the screen were incredible, and I feel as if I really had the opportunity to speak with classmates throughout this course--which is such a rare phenomenon in Zoom learning. The weaknesses of the course were precisely that we were not able to meet in-person! It was a wonderful topic to explore with an equally wonderful group of people, and I am excited for future students of this class to be able to have those experiences face-to-face.
Â Although this course was challenging, it was very strong. The discussion section was so well-run and engaged that it felt like a seminar in its own right. I also didn't expect to enjoy asynchronous lectures as much as I did, and I felt that both halves of the class worked together well. At times the reading felt overwhelming, specifically towards the end of Middlemarch, but it did turn out to be do-able and it was incredibly rewarding to feel on top of a demanding class.
Â This course is great at connecting and condensing the entire timespan of the Victorian period into just one semester. The fast-paced timeline however does present some issues, as it has to jump over some important themes, names race and imperialism. Because the course met online this semester and the lectures were pre-recorded, it often felt like there just wasn't enough time to get through all of the material presented by the novels and I wished this class were a seminar-style course. Honestly, I wish we could have just read more books together!
Â The strengths of this course are that Professor Yeazell is wonderful and brilliant, and the texts included in the syllabus are some of the most well-renowned ones ever produced. The weaknesses of the course derive perhaps from its position as an introductory class of sorts. In this sense, I think the course is really only a good fit for students who have little previous experience with literary analysis. For others, the course's odd focus on teaching students to conform to traditional essay structures is limiting and confining, stymying intellectual engagement and growth rather than encouraging it. The asynchronous set-up for lectures was also rather ineffective, especially given that lectures were only online for one week, a rather silly decision that likely discouraged student engagement rather than encouraging it.
