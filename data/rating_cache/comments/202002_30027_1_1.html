2020-Summer CHEM S221 01 Organic Chemistry

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30027 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 The weakness is that of all summer courses- a huge amount of information in so little time. It might be improved by limiting the amount of reactions introduced in order to have a solid grasp on key reactions rather than a weaker grasp on many extraneous reactions.
 Strengths: Dr. Martinez led very helpful problem solving sessions, which made digestion of the material much easier Weaknesses: While Dr. Parr is clearly very familiar with all the material, the lectures felt a bit disorganized at times. Examples or reactions that were not in the textbook were also sometimes mentioned, but it was hard to tell which of these we needed to know for each exam.
 Would like if the instructor and the class notes were more explicit about how the reactions tie together and what were likely to be tested on; handouts that compile the reactions in terms of mechanism (for example, grouping nucleophilic carbonyl reactions) would be amazing Transition-metal catalyzed reactions felt like a curveball I wish that it was clearer how much we needed to know about them, since the mechanisms were a lot different than the ones we had learned before, and it wasnt clear if we just needed surface level understanding or in-depth understanding
 The professors were all fantastic. I appreciated the amount of time dedicated to office hours, which I took advantage of. The sequence was taught well, and the explanations were great for each reaction. It would have been nice to have a little more practice in the of synthesis questions before the last week. They sort of appeared on the tests without much forewarning.
 After a certain point in the course, classes just feel like a litany of reactions and mechanisms. This seems to be how organic chemistry is taught everywhere, and that's probably why it has such a bad reputation.
 The strengths were that overall, the live parts of the class did not take up too much time, so I was able to effectively understand the material with time. One weakness was that at times I felt that the live lecture felt a little long, especially because it was so early in the day. I would suggest a larger or entirely recorded version that is no more than an hour so that students are able to absorb the material without feeling overwhelmed.
 Dr. Parr is a horrible instructor. He is so disorganized and makes it so difficult to follow along or organize what we are learning. I don't think I learned anything in this class other than how to take a test using the book and your notes as the only source of knowledge (not your actual brain). There is literally too much to say about how bad this experience was.
 Lectures were not engaging, and were not well-suited for the online format.
 I think the weakest aspect of this course is the textbook. It's poorly organized, bad diagrams, etc. I would recommend any student taking this class to buy a textbook that offers clearer explanations, such as Klein's Organic Chemistry.
 The course was pretty well-taught, which is impressive considering the short time frame. One weakness was that because of the short time frame, there wasn't much time for review.
 The course instructor did not do a very good job at explaining the course material or teaching what was going to be on the test. A change of instructors or a significant change in the instructors teaching skills would most benefit the class.
 Include more problems worked in lecture.
 For an online course, they did a really good job of making sure that there were a lot of accessible resources. Problem solving sessions and classes were pretty promptly uploaded onto the media library, making it fairly easy to keep up remotely. I liked too that they were pretty consistent with the textbook, because that ended up being a really great resource for me. The biggest weakness of this course was that we did not have enough opportunities for practicing problems. We had a couple problems every day on Canvas, but I would have genuinely benefited from Sapling-like problems where after studying, I could work out a wealth of problems that helped me understand the material better than simply reading or watching a lecture. There were end of chapter problems in the textbook that I tried to use, but those questions didnt look like the ones that we were expected to answer, so it was difficult to feel prepared for exams. I also feel like the course staff could have been more communicative about course logistics, like when things were due, how or if the class would be curved, etc. I appreciate though that they were very responsive by email and I also appreciate that they were flexible in accommodations that I needed.
 I think this summer was an interesting one, but comparing it to Dr. DiMeglios S220, I thought her class was a little better done with the videos we watched outside of class time. I think it would have been a little more useful to have a shorter in-person class time supplemented with videos we could watch beforehand. I also know I have a hard time watching a lecture normally because its hard for me to pay attention for an hour+ so I think it would have been nice to take this opportunity to break it up a little bit and make it easier for people like me.
 Online school is pretty difficult, I had to rely on the textbook heavily in order to learn the material and still had a lot of questions. Getting my questions answered was difficult because there usually wasn't time at office hours or I didn't feel comfortable asking questions that might seem dumb at office hours with other students. Asking questions through email was also not ideal because there were often misunderstandings as to what the question actually was and not all of the questions asked were always answered. Also receiving feedback was sometimes confusing online and sometimes I never found out what the correct answers were for the quizzes.
 tough online, but professor made the best
 Strengths: Great professors who are very helpful and responsive. Weaknesses: Very fast paced and may be hard to keep up.
 The daily quizzes were very helpful to guide studying/main points we should have learned. One thing that could be improved was how all the course material was only available the day of instead of in advance.
 Enjoyable lectures, but sometimes it was hard to organize all the information in my head. Lecture slides were not used in the same order as presented on canvas files and a virtual black board was also used without saving the notes. Sometimes classes were split across large chapters which was difficult to manage.
