Â I have somewhat of a mixed view of this course. On one hand, the professor and TAs are extremely nice and excellent at engaging with and helping students during office hours. Class lecture sessions directly engage with the types of problems you recieve on the homework assignments, which is very helpful. I also very much want to congratulate the peer tutors for their excellent work. Their help on the homework assignments was invaluable. The course certainly accomplishes the goal of making you pretty strongly versed in calculus techniques in the biosciences, and does so in a way that fosters inclusivity rather than exclusive competition. At the same time, I noticed a set of things I think could be improved upon. Perhaps it is because of the pandemic, but I often felt like examination preparation and homework assignments were made more difficult than necessary given the substance of the exams and homework themselves. For example, the integration gateway is only considered passed when only one or two (maximum) minor mistakes is made. Although I can understand why this mode of test would be favored, I feel like this actively contributes far more stress than needed given the nature of the assignment. As someone who feels comfortable with how they did on exams, I still studied for the gateway far more than any of the three midterm examinations. I was thinking this through and feel that a solution might be reached in the following way: -keeping the three-midterms organization of this semester (this allowed me to focus a ton more on the last few topics, which definitely improved my learning and also reduced stress in a subject I and I know many others get scared about taking) -reducing the pass threshold for the integration gateway and/or one or more of the following: increasing the time from 25 minutes to 30+ minutes asking students who do not pass after more than four tests to pass an online course module instead
