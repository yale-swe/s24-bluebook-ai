2020-Fall CPSC 531 01/MUSI 428 01/CPSC 431 01 Algorithmic & Heuristic Comp/Algorithmic Computer Music

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 10164/10163/10161 Term: 202003

 Recorded lectures were convenient for time zone differences!
 This class was fun and the professor is super funny. My only qualm is with the language it is taught in, because it feels super clunky and documentation is bad, but there is not much that anyone can do about that. Not an issue with the class itself.
 This class functioned fairly well as a virtual lecture. I wish more people had participated in class, but it speaks to the class and not the instructor and the class structure itself. Would have run more smoothly as an in-person lecture due to technical difficulties with zoom.
 Strengths: the topic was fascinating, something I definitely hope to work on in the future and something I don't think I would have learned anywhere else. The lectures were engaging (even if often off-topic) and the projects were super open-ended and became a great creative outlet for me. Using the course blogs to force students to see everyone else's projects was great, as we saw what everyone else had worked on, and got feedback from our peers on our assignments. Weaknesses: The biggest weakness was the lack of feedback on projects and assignments. I understand that it's hard to give detailed feedback with only one TA and a 20-person class, but the work could sometimes feel a little wasted if it's not looked at thoroughly by anyone, and I felt that I was making the same syntactic mistakes in SuperCollider from the very beginning of the course. Our understanding of SuperCollider was also a little superficial. I would have really enjoyed perhaps one or two more homeworks strictly focused on SuperCollider syntax and working with SynthDefs and stuff, even if it meant one or two less creative projects.
 Overall this course did a great job of addressing the challenges of Zoom, using multimedia offerings, live coding and other interactive methods in addition to lectures. The course does a good job of balancing the wide musical knowledge of its participants, but having additional musical prerequisites or even a brief musical "placement quiz" at the beginning of the course might help.
 The projects were very fun and the material was also super interesting, especially live coding/seeing what cool things are happening in computer music today. It was great to see classmates' projects, everyone was very creative.
 The strengths of the course include the amazing instruction from Professor Petersen and the interesting projects. I felt like Professor Petersen was great at keeping each lecture lively while still providing students with enough information to perform well on the assignments.
 Instructor is very knowledgeable and passionate, homework were somewhat long especially in the middle but well structured as well to facilitate learning
