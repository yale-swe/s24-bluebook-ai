Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 Recorded lectures were very convenient!
 Strengths - strong list of guest speakers that conceptualized these ideas Weakness - open ended assignments
 This course was incredible! I learned so much about the built environment and how the world around me/current day has been influenced by the past. I gained a better sense of how to look around me and see history versus passively taking in my surroundings. And through the project, I explored an area that I had never thought about pursuing before. Having an in person option was also a lifesaver this semester. It got me up and out of my house/isolation depression. Elihu hosted a walking tour of New Haven and I had the most fun that I've had at all during the semester. Prof/TA's always made themselves available to students when needed. They were good at stimulating discussion and I liked how they responded to student comments. No negative feedback to give!
 Strengths: Professor Rubin and the teaching team's strong passion and enthusiasm for the course, the variety of speakers, and the open ended-ness of the assignments Weaknesses: going overtime often and sometimes the reading not correlating with the live discussion
 Professor Rubin is very passionate and knowledgable about the information he teaches which makes the class very fun and engaging for the students. Professor Rubin also invited lots of guest to class who have done so very interesting and important work so it was very nice being able to get to hear directly from them about their work. One weakness though was the structure of the class. Lots of time we would fall behind and all the assignments due dates ended up being pushed back a couple weeks because we were so behind.
 This is a phenomenal course. The strengths are the pace, material, guests, assignments, and professor. Prof Rubin was very understanding about our reading load and workload and tailored the class accordingly while ensuring that we were fully engaging with the material. This was by far my favorite class taken so far at Yale.
 Elihu cares about his students, also the scrapbook is a fun way to do the assignments.
 I loved the unique structure of this course! For nearly every lecture, we had a guest speak about their specific expertise in urban themes. Furthermore, we got to be extremely creative and create a scrapbook throughout the semester. The group project allowed us to explore a topic that we were particularly interested in and was a great way to end the semester.
 Strengths: Prof. Rubin / The TFs are super knowledgeable and really passionate about this subject. The diverse array of speakers that Prof Rubin brought in to speak during each class period always provided unique and interesting perspectives. Weaknesses: I think an important part of Urban Studies the planning / politics of cities which was touched on for sure but I would have liked to see more of it.
 Very engaging, great guests, interesting forms of assessments.
 I think everything about this class was its strength! It is so clear that Professor Rubin is very passionate about what he's teaching, which makes the class really dynamic and fun to engage with. Rubin is also just really smart! I thought he designed the class really well. He brought in such a variety of guest speakers that made every class different and exciting, and it really expanded the scope of my understanding of urban studies. I was very impressed with how, even though each class had a guest speaker, we still learned so much from Rubin himself. Rubin made this class the success that it is!

 loved the course - would love a better description of assignments on the syllabus
 The course was pretty easy to skip. I think this course was really a sample platter for urban thinkersa lot of interesting inspirational topics but nothing super practical, which I expect will come up in future URBN courses. The colloquium style was good, but again made it easy to engage in some sessions but not in others. Overall, great easy course and helpful in determining whether or not Urban Studies is a good major for you. The optional discussion sections were extremely optional
