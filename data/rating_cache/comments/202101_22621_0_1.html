2021-Spring PLSC 205 01 The American Presidency

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401 CRN: 22621 Term: 202101

12345Forward
 I think the course does a good job of taking students through a trip of the American presidency. It felt like a lot of theory, which was helpful context for my understanding of the presidency but I think it left a lot of the understanding of the executive as a whole to the students.
 I learned about the different political eras of presidential administrations and how certain trends repeat across time.
 I learned extensively about presidential power, American presidential history, and how this all applies to today's presidencies.
 I learned about the institution of the presidency, how it has changed over time, how leadership has changed over time, and what all of that can tell us about the presidency going forward.
 This course provides both an overview of presidential history in the United States and several models to analyze political authority over time. Professor Skowronek is a leader in the field and provides students with his model of political time, which finds that recurring historical patterns have a critical role in determining presidential authority. For example, Skowronek argues that Jackson and Lincoln were alike in their rise to power-- they both followed failed regimes and could serve as great repudiators of action. The class also details famous presidential power models, such as the one provided by Neustadt, and helps to elucidate the complex structure of the executive branch.
 The history and development of the American presidency
 I learned a lot about the history of the presidency, as well as ways to analyze past presidents.
 Gained a really great and interesting perspective on the American presidency
 As my first survey political science course, not only did I learn a lot about the subject (the American presidency), but I also learned much about how political science is studied in general. Learning from an expert like Skowronek is awesome because you get to hear some of his own original ideas in addition to widely accepted ideas. Material-wise I learned less about the inner workings of the president / presidency and a lot more about the presidency's interactions with surrounding branches of government / institutions / the electorate.
 I learned about the history of the American presidency and the forces that shape the modern presidency
 I learned about the different ways we can evaluate, examine and think about past and future American presidents. I felt like I became a stronger writer and was given opportunities to form my own opinions and defend them.
 Learned different frameworks for how to think about the American presidency
 I learned about the political development of the presidency, and how to write clear arguments about the presidency and political science in general.
 I learned a lot about the presidency. Got to learn about Different theories regarding presidential power.
 Learned about how to situate each president within history, how to predict future leadership patterns, evolution of the office over time.
 I developed a solid understanding of the constitutional basis and historical understanding of the American presidency. I also learned about Prof. Skowronek's influential theoretic framework for understanding the nature of presidential change over time.
 This course mainly taught me how to critically evaluate American presidencies from a non-biased, informed point of view. I think I found Professor Skowronek's theory of political time to be most interesting in political trends through the executive. I also learned how to pull from a number of resources in order to develop a coherent argument. I think learning about presidents through an interesting and less fact-driven perspective was most interesting to me.
 I learned a lot about how to evaluate the president and the presidency as an institution. And learned the history of this country's president sand the tools they used to govern.
 I see the presidency in a whole different light after taking this class; the Professor shows us how difficult it truly is to run our American Government and you find a new understanding of the president.
 How to write a well formulated argument and essay.
 I learned different ways of evaluating presidential leadership, success, and failure. I learned the theories that outline the role of the presidency as it has existed and as it should exist.
 I came away with an entirely different perspective on the development of the presidency. I also got better at writing political science essays.
 I gained a stronger understanding of the function of the institution of the presidency and the ways in which it developed through time.
