2020-Summer MATH S115E 01 Calculus 2

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30144 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 The pre-recorded videos are super clear, good for learning the content on your own. Instructor and TAs were all very nice (Ethan was especially helpful), clear notes, generally well-structured course. Practice exams were super helpful as well. The polls were a good way to keep students engaged, although the breakout groups were not very effective/efficient. Perhaps the class time could've covered a little more of the material.
 I think the course was structured really well and everyone definitely had the resources to succeed if they took advantage of them.
 I really liked the short times of an hour long for class. It wasn't too exhausting. Strengths: Prof. Smith's teaching and the TAs. Weaknesses: It felt kind of rushed because the classes are only five weeks long. When we had breakout room sessions, it felt too short to get anything done and because so, it was kind of a waste of time, especially when no one talked. I recommend we stick to breakout sessions for workshop sessions.
 Strengths of the course include the pre-class explorations and the workshops. I think the workshops were just as valuable, if not more valuable, than class time. Other strengths include the accessibility of the professor and TF's. A weakness was the fact that we received very little feedback on our problem sets.
 This course had many resources, and multiple ways of teaching including videos, explorations, class time, workshops, and office hours which all helped in teaching concepts and problems.
 The way in which Professor Brett Smith taught the course was a strength. He delivered clear lectures and was consistently available to help students during one-on-one office hours. In addition, his exams were fair--the questions pushed us to think but were always in line with what we covered, and were graded fairly and timely. I also liked that we had optional problem-solving workshops held by TAs on the days that we did not have class, because I found these workshops to be very useful. Finally, a particular strength of the course is that we had post-exam one-on-one discussions with the professor or TAs. I really appreciated this because it allowed me to get feedback on my work and in general, made me feel supported in my learning. A weakness is that the course met only three times a week, once an hour each time. During the school year, I think this schedule would be fitting, but given that we compressed a semester's worth of material into five weeks during this course, I found that we weren't able to cover material deeply with the course time allocated and I wish we had longer class time. I would have liked perhaps meeting 1.5 hours three times a week. I think this would have given us more time to learn about parametric and polar equations, which occurred all of during the last week of class and could have benefited from more in-depth treatment. In addition, I wish the Canvas files section was more organized and that it was easier to find certain files. I did, however, found the Canvas modules section to be extremely organized and helpful, covering the same content as is in the files section.
 It was a little rushed at times. The course could use more practice exams before each test.
 The software that Professor Smith used to teach us was very convenient and clear with the color coding and space the write the whole lecture's notes in sort of one white board. Furthermore, the online videos were very helpful in refining certain knowledge areas. One weakness would be that it is hard to communicate with other students, as there is no social media platform in which we can ask each other questions.
 This class is good at teaching fundamental Calculus 2 skills in such a short period of time, however lecture does not often tackle important problems that may show up on homework or exams. I also did not find group work in breakout rooms to be useful since many people are often quiet and discussing each other's work is difficult on a virtual call. It would also be helpful if lectures could sometimes explain the concepts behind what we are learning, although it is hard to make everything fit in an hour-long lecture.
 The course was too short. Three hours per week is not enough to help students master the informtion.
