Would you recommend this course to another student? Please explain.

 If you love research/want to learn more about biological research then this is the class for you. You will be surrounded by incredible peers that are highly motivated and intelligent and a professor that offers valuable insight.
 I would wholeheartedly recommend this course to another student--for someone like me, who didn't have experience in a lab and was not exposed to all the assays and experimental methods that we talked about that are so important for research such as that on SARS-CoV2, it was truly eye-opening. I came out of this semester with so much knowledge about the virus (past just the symptoms and such), and I have found myself talking about things I learned in this class like the ACE2 receptor, the spike protein, etc. to my friends and family because it was so fascinating.
 I would absolutely recommend this for budding researchers, biologists, or people who want to go into medical professions.
 Yes, this course is my favorite from my schedule due to the small group size, great conversations with Dean Chang, and the exposure to the latest research and news of anything bio-related. If you dream of discovering new antibiotics, cure different types of cancer, or are thinking about graduate or medical school, this is the perfect class for you.
 Definitely. It may not give them the most exciting work, but the discipline needed to succeed in this course is something I think ALL students should try and develop.
 Yes definitely! Firstly, you get the opportunity to learn from the one and only Dean Chang! He is a really great and funny person and truly loves his students. If you are interested in doing any research in the future, I would greatly recommend that you take this course. You learn to write a grant proposal (and get feedback on it), how to present scientific journals, and a load about molecular biology concepts and techniques. While a huge plus of the class is that you get guaranteed summer funding, do be aware that the first-year summer fellowship isn't that difficult to get and I personally would not take the class just for that fact. The workload expected of you will definitely be dreadful if you have no interest in doing biological research whatsoever in any form. You are expected to understand concepts that are often taught at the graduate level and think and perform like a graduate student. You are expected to be able to research beyond the weekly paper assigned to understand the concepts and techniques used in-depth as they are often needed to complete the weekly psets. For the final grant writing assignment, you will be reading many many papers and expected to formulate a novel experiment project and work through all the nuances. While this may seem intimidating, Dean Chang does an excellent job in helping guide you through the process both of reading academic papers and writing a grant. Also, for the first semester, you do everything (psets, presentation, grant writing) in groups of 3 which makes the experience so much more enjoyable (the second semester you still present and do psets in groups but you are expected to write a grant on your own with a mentor you identify). If you take this course, you will definitely be satisfied with how much you learn by the end. Do note that this is a one-year-long course and this review reflects only the first semester.
 Yes! This is a really fun and interesting class if you like reading about science and biology. Learning how to read scientific literature and write grant proposals is also an invaluable skill if you want to do scientific research. It also really helps improve your presentation skills!
 Absolutely!!! Take this if you can! You will learn so much about being a scientist in the real world and be given a very honest and fresh look into such a fascinating career. Even if science doesn't pan out for you, you will gain so many skills on how to interact with difficult topics and engage with a community to learn and discover together. Take this class!!!

 I would recommend this course to any student with a strong background in biological sciences and an interest in research. Additionally, the final was a grant proposal for a cure for coronavirus, so this course requires creative thinking, hard work, and motivation to research. Everyone in the course plans on majoring in STEM and working in a STEM profession, so if you aren't interested in that, the class's environment may seem intimidating.
 Yes, I believe this class offers exceptional research opportunities under Dean Chang's excellent guidance and also teaches important skills that can really help individuals grow as young scientists and spark their interest and path into research.
