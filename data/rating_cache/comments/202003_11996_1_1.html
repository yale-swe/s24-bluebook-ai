2020-Fall THST 110 01 Survey of Theater and Drama

Q:

What knowledge, skills, and insights did you develop by taking this course?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC401 CRN: 11996 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 I learned a lot about global theater traditions and developed a better ability to research and analyze ancient theatrical texts.
 I gained survey-level knowledge of ancient theater and performance traditions around the world, as well as the origins of theater itself. I also gained an understanding of the differences between theater, drama, and performance, and I learned to read both ancient and modern plays critically. One of the most useful skills I developed during this course was the ability to read entire plays more quickly.
 Overview of theater and performance practices from around the world and how they have changed over time. A deeper understanding on the differences and similarities between theater, drama, ritual, and performance.
 I think I covered a lot of my big takeaways in my Critical Reflection conversation, but one of my main takeaways from this course was the exposure it gave me to a wide variety of theater traditions and their influence on Western "mainstream" theatre. I really enjoyed looking at theatre and performance from a scholarly perspective, as well as hearing from all our guest speakers, especially the practicing theatre artists.
 I learned to think outside the box and discover new approaches to thinking about theater and its history. I also learned how to better manage my time in order to be prepared for class (watching videos, doing readings, writing short responses etc.).
 An understanding of different performance forms from different cultures throughout history. Also an understanding of what and why certain forms are studied and considered "theater" more than others. We also had frequent guest speakers who would provide insight on their areas of performance.
 I learned how to analyze theatre more closely and write about it. I've always been especially interested in theatre criticism and the discussion posts we had to do every week really helped me hone that skill.
 I gained a much broader understanding of what performance is and a much deeper appreciation of theater.
 This class review different method, reasons, and approaches regarding performance from across the world from different time periods before the 17th century. Plays are analyzed from a literary lens but from a cultural one as well and they are compared to what is considered theater in America today. In addition to written plays students analyze other aspects of performance such as religious rituals, organized protest, outfits and other attire and dance. This course intentionally decentralizes European theater as the foundation of all performance and looks at how other theater traditions have contributed to theater today.
