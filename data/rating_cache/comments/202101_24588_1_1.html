Â This class has a really strong reading list and a lot of potential if you take it. The topics covered are interesting and Prof Eidemiller brings in very interesting guest speakers who can provide a unique strategic or tactical perspective on issues (We got to talk to a deputy secretary of state and a bunch of the top SOCCOM leadership). The subject and the interplay of strategy and tactics as well as the different roles that soldiers and policymakers take on is stimulating. Prof Eidemiller truly unmatched potential resources to understand the subjects. The mentors he assigned us were very helpful in understanding the topics in class. Being able to discuss current natsec issues with a good group of fellow students was also a plus. There is a lot of material that Prof Eidemiller tries to cover in this class, so to fully absorb it you will need to set aside time. To this end, the class feels like it's trying to pack too much into a class period sometimes. I think it could improve by spending more time delving into the topics and concepts presented in the slides - they felt a bit rushed between guest speakers at times. This would help on some of the essay prompts, since they were sometimes on things we touched on but did not get to elaborate on too much in class. I thought the class did a very thorough job covering the tactical side of SF and infantry, but that we didn't really deeply explore operational and strategic ways of viewing things. This was a bit of a disconnect since the last couple of assignments were strategically focused. Overall, I think the class was truly unique in the caliber of the reading list it provided and the resources Professor Eidemiller provides you, but could be improved by being a bit more focused on key topics and touching more on the operational/ strategic level in addition to tactics.
