2021-Spring PHIL 326 01/RLST 402 01 The Philosophy of Religion

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 Discussion was a strong point, since it solidified and developed my understanding of different concepts presented in class.
 Strengths: great variety in religious thought and assessment, covering many of the major figures alongside more niche advocates foundational experience in philosophical paper writing with helpful guides and feedback very structured class with accompanying class notes (VERY appreciated!) Weaknesses: repeatedly off schedule (usually for the good reason of fielding student queries) moves pretty quickly from topic to topic, though synthesis between sources is helpful/encouraged
 Strengths: Covered wide array of topics so now I feel like I have a basic understanding of the Phil. Religion landscape of issues. Lectures were clear and compelling, often diving quite deep into dialectics or raising concerns which I had never before considered. Readings also were interesting often raising different points or giving clear presentations of central positions. I liked the exam and essay format, as the exams made sure you actually understood each issue discussed in the class while the essays gave you license to more fully engage with a topic. Prof. Pittard is super generous with talking in Office Hours, and this is a great way to learn further about topics that were interesting. Weaknesses: The main weakness I think was how disconnected lectures were from some of our readings. It felt at times like the readings were just tacked on to actually give us homework as opposed to actually central to the course, and this was because the lectures would essentially go over all important content from the readings and only then diving further into the dialectic. To improve this I would say it would be good to have the readings as touchstones in lectures which can be referenced as exemplary of positions or as ways to shortcut further into a dialectic.
 The live lecture is very helpful as is the discussion section.
 I loved this class. My fave class at Yale. John Pittard is amazing.
 Overall, this was an excellent class and I was really happy I took it. I appreciated the complexity with which arguments were approached in class. My one small piece of feedback is that, in assigning readings, it may be better to assign fewer, longer readings. For instance, some weeks we had to read 4-5 short readings per lecture day. Though the page count was reasonable, it is sometimes hard to think about/evaluate so many competing views for a single class, so I thought it would have been better to assign a similar number of pages but fewer readings.
 Strengths: dynamic lecturer, great teaching fellow, manageable syllabus

The assessments are numerous, but are ideal for an undergraduate student, since they were ongoing and didn't put much pressure on during finals period. Prof. Pittard is an AMAZING professor -- I don't think I would have enjoyed this class as much with anyone else. The TFs were also great, and the reading selection was great.
 I really enjoyed the texts we engaged with and our discussion of them. I also think Professor Pittard is an excellent lecturer and made the sub-optimal format still quite enjoyable. I think the lecture potentially could have benefited from some visual aids - we all had a handout online that we used for the class, but especially in the virtual format, some visual aids to the lecture could probably have helped with engagement and focus. Other than that, I thought the course was structured well for learning and very interesting.

 Strengths: Professor Pittard is such an original, interesting thinker. Steph (my TA) was a ton of fun and just a really empathetic, kind person. The content is incredibly interesting and every class I learned something new about how we might prove/disprove/understand God. Prof. Pittard's lectures are well-organized, with useful notes sheets to follow along with. The exams are very fair. Weaknesses: We were sometimes a day behind schedule so we missed out on the afterlife class, which is not a big deal. Other than that, I honestly can't think of any weaknesses. I loved this class and would give it a 10/10. One of my favorite philosophy classes I've taken, and definitely the most informative..
 I thought that the course was well-organized, and Professor Pittard did a great job of covering a lot of material in a small amount of time. Biggest weakness was the bare fact that it was on zoom, but hopefully we will never have to ever do that again. That said, Pittard's was one of the best-adapted-to-zoom classes I took this semester. I also actually really liked having the combination of having exams that required us to familiarize ourselves with the authors and their arguments, and having papers that helped us analyze those arguments and put forth our own.
