2020-Fall ER&M 431 01 Community Organizing

Q:

Among the practices in this course geared toward learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, which succeeded and which could be improved? Please comment on course practices that allowed for virtual engagement.

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

 It was a tradeoff because the powerpoints were great - they kept us on top of schedule, oriented the class around a common set of vocabulary/concepts, and allowed for the presentation of different forms of media like songs, videos, etc. However it made it somewhat harder to engage in some ways because you can't see the other people in the class/engage with them as well when the screen is being shared. so perhaps we could spend more time in breakout rooms.
 I think bringing and guest speakers worked really well. I also think breaking us up into small groups for the accompaniment projects was helpful. I wouldve liked more breakout rooms maybe.
 The format was perfect as a leminar
 This course was really good at leveraging the benefits of virtual learning by bringing in experts who would not have been otherwise accessible.
 The virtual format of the class made it possible to invite amazing guest speakers to class and engage in discussions with them. However, the class often rushed, making it difficult to really digest the content.
 The course's structure was unlike any other virtual class I've had before. The integration of media into the course sessions kept every class dynamic. Additionally, the courses had guest lecturers who talked about their writings and work with organizations. The virtual engagement of the course enabled us to have guest lecturers and engage with community organizations across the country.
 Professor HoSang decreased the class workload and focused on praxis which was refreshing and a creative way for students to form new connections in the middle of an incredible lonesome global pandemic.
 The guest speakers were really amazing and was a great way to learn virtually!
 the live components with guest speakers were super engaging. Overall we were doing a lot of work for the course and would have loved some more feedback and check-ins for it.
 The breakout rooms were successful and helpful in allowing us to talk about specific topics in-depth and allow us to more freely participate in a smaller group setting as well as getting to know each other more. The weekly tasks each accompaniment project group had also facilitated engagement with the course. Though the class was virtual, I think the weekly tasks gave us all something to focus on and helped us to engage with and understand the material better.
