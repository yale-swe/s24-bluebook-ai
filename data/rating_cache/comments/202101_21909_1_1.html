2021-Spring PHYS 201 01 Fundamentals of Physics

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 21909 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 The support staff and professors were invaluable. The only thing that could have improved this course was being in person.
 The office hours of the professors and TAs were excellent (easily the best of any class I took). I feel that this class went too fast through a lot of concepts. Going through quantum in like 2 weeks was too fast. Professor Tipton is vocal that he has to go through certain things quickly to stay on schedule, but it's still not ideal. I feel that my base of understanding for much of the stuff this semester is weak, as I had to rush to do problem sets rather than truly learn.
 Professor Tipton is pretty humorous and formats his lecture material really well -- in retrospect I probably would have appreciated this so much more if I paid more attention during class lol. 201 also covers quantum, which was super cool to learn about, and Tipton does a pretty good job explaining concepts that seem super out of reach at first in just 75 minutes. The highlight of this class was definitely the office hours / ULAs / TAs, though. They were helpful, super approachable, and always on top of it. Genuinely, go to office hours for this class. The professors do a great job organizing the curriculum and adjusting the course to accommodate for us, too. I took 260 last semester with Professor Harris and had a much better experience in 201 with regards to organization, helpfulness of peer tutors, and test / homework difficulty.
 The course was very well-designed. Professor Tipton gives great lectures and was very engaging throughout the semester. The problem sets were difficult, but office hours with the professors and TA's were incredibly useful. I do not think there were any weaknesses.
 This course does a great job of giving a broad overview of many concepts.
 Class was well taught, strengths could be more engagement from the classroom but it is probably just an issue with online learning.
 Incredible course
 I appreciated the placement of more computationally intensive questions on the homework with more conceptual questions on the exams. Along with homework making up 50% of the course grade, I thought this made the exams less stressful while still keeping the course challenging and rigorous. The lectures were also generally clear, although there were a few spots which were very math heavy (mostly during derivations) that I found hard to stay focused for. In terms of weaknesses, I felt like some lectures developed an idea that we didn't really come back to. For example, we spent considerable time in one lecture doing the math for AC RLC circuits, introducing the idea of complex impedance, but since it wasn't reinforced in any problems or other lectures I didn't feel like I ever mastered the topic. I think topics like this could either be reinforced elsewhere or shortened in order to give more time to other important topics.
 extremely well taught but lectures and lectures notes should be numbered better
 Strengths: Wide review of material students may have already learned but with a deeper dive into some topics, lots of professor and TA office hours, some really cool lectures on more modern topics in physics like SR, quantum mechanics, cosmology Weaknesses: I only felt that the course moved a little too quickly for me at some times. Other than that I don't have any suggestions for improvement, this was a great course.
 The problem sets were very difficult and required a lot of time to be spent in office hours to do the assignments. I often felt that the class was unnecessarily difficult and fast paced
 strengths: problem sets, weaknesses: test difficulty was highly variable
 Strengths: Interesting material, great professors and peer tutors. There were plenty of office hours either with Professor Schiffer, Professor Tipton, or TAs/TFs. Weaknesses: Some of the topics we learned about were very bare-bones and introductory, I think it could be better if we look at fewer topics but at a greater depth.
 I think the discussing problems in breakout rooms method in the beginning of the semester was very helpful and decreasing the time of students working on problems was one thing that could not have been done.
 Engagement was obviously quite low, but Tipton does a good job of pacing out his teaching and makes the class fun to attend.
 The course was well taught, well organized, and the problem sets and exams were good. The low engagement that I reported was no fault of the course of the professors but represents general zoom fatigue on my end. If in person this would be an excellent course.
 The lectures are on-topic and well-delivered, and the professors are very responsive to questions. The TA's provided a true wealth of office hours, which was very helpful especially as time zones make scheduling even harder. I also really appreciated that the exams were, in my eyes, faithful to the material we had learned in class and really testing the right concepts (which was not my experience in some other classes). I'm not really sure what I would suggest to improve this class going forward: I had no complaints!
