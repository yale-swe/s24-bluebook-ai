 If you are planning to take this course post-pandemic and if Professor Snyder eases back the reading load by at least 50% I highly recommend this course. The readings are fascinating and Professor Snyder is a world-class lecturer. His scholarship is incredible. That being said this class has been single-handedly the most work I have ever had to do in my Yale career. Readings were over 500 pages a week for at least 3 of our weeks, and rarely below 200 pages. That wouldn't have been so bad had Professor Snyder not required timely, handwritten notes by the time section met every week. Like any Yale student, I appreciate when my professors bring their passion, expertise, and enthusiasm to the classroom. However this workload was simply overwhelmingly and often felt unmanageable. When I spoke with other students in the class I was glad to not feel alone, but if you take this class in the same format as it was taught Spring 2021 I would only advise taking it Cr/D. You will learn so much but it often feels overshadowed by the sheer burden the graded notetaking for this class is. Alternatively, I suggest you just buy Professor Snyder's books (Bloodlands and Black Earth) since he teaches a lot from them and they cover like 50% of the class topics/lessons. Amazing content, amazing lectures, dismal workload.
