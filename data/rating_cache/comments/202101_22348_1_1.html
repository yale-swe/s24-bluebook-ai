The ROTC students have their OWN SECTIONS for ROTC students only, and these TA's are all SUPER EASYGOING. It's because this is a required class for the ROTC kids or something.  I think Professor Kennedy and his treasure-trove of knowledge is one of the biggest strengths of this course. However, I found that the biggest issue with this course is its method of 'teaching' the content to the students.  Professor Kennedy is fantastically charismatic. His lectures were great, albeit not as information dense as they could have been. The readings were phenomenal. The diversity of views (from Boot to Kennedy himself) was super useful. But I think there was too much reading, which made it difficult to focus in on anything. Rarely was I caught up on the material. I think Boot, McNeill, and the Kennedy readings, plus the occasional Canvas supplement (On War was fascinating), were more than enough. Howard was good for the pre-class readings as well. I think if we had stuck to these readings it would have been more manageable. Then for the discussion sections, I think this was a fluke, but mine was a bit small. Only 4 people! That made things slow at times.
 Strength: covers a sweeping geographic and timescale in history; interesting readings; engaging professor Weaknesses: learning dependent on section; sporadic lecture topics and high reading load can leave student confused; not as much in depth analysis/critical thinking involved as I would have liked.
 The lecture material is innately interesting and Professor Kennedy is a fantastic lecturer. I felt like there was a lot of overlap between the readings, which were often onerous, so streamlining the assigned readings would have been helpful. Also, I didn't like that the vast majority of the final grade was based on two exams. Having a paper worth ~20-30% of the final grade could potentially be useful, especially because it would allow for more creative/nuanced analysis of the course material (which is simply impossible in a short exam essay).
 Strengths: Prof. Kennedy is awesome. He is the archetype of a Yale history professor and makes the lectures engaging. Course structure was well thought out with many people supporting. Weaknesses: Sometimes easy to forget readings but easy enough to get by without them.
