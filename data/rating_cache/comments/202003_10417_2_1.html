2020-Fall EAST 303 01/HIST 303J 01 Hong Kong and China

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 10417/10389 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: The short papers (as opposed to one long term paper) allowed for more feedback from the professor early on in the course. In this way, I was able to use feedback from previous essays to improve future work. The small, seminar format of the course was also conducive to lively discussion. Weaknesses: The only (minor) weakness is that the "blog post" requirement sometimes led to redundancy in the discussions- occasionally, the discussions became a revisiting of what students had written in the weekly discussion post on Canvas.
 Strengths: well-organized, expectations for each week are laid out clearly Weaknesses: not much to be honest
 Some visual context would have been good, and some introduction to actual content/contexf
 STRONG: the content was what kept me surviving this class. Reading are super interesting. Ethnographies are fascinating (albeit more suitable for an anth/sociology class, so they felt out of place). Some materials were gathered from just a few months ago, if not last month. We talked about the resignation of the remaining pro-democracy members of the LegCo in HK and discussed the implications of that. !!WEAK!!: literally everything. No joke. I read a review that said "Ho has heard this feedback and will likely provide a bit more in terms of background lectures and/or overview-focused readings in the early weeks." This NEVER happened. Not once did she lecture, and only when someone made a blatant error in recounting Chinese history did she interject. I cannot tell you where Bao'an County is or point to Shenzhen on a map. I did not know why HK was British until I watched a Vox video that explained all of HK history better than our readings did in 10 minutes. In our discussion posts (which I'll get to soon), we were begging her for context by asking concrete questions. Never answered. And for the life of me I have no idea why we had to do those posts anyway. I wrote all of my thoughts about the readings in my post, so by the time I got to class, I had ~nothing~ more to say. At all. Then, inevitably, we'd get "could you talk about your selected quote?" Well, no, b/c if you'd read my post, you'd see I said everything there is to say. Might I suggest that we DON'T post the posts and instead write them so we can all share what we wrote in class instead of trying to recount what everyone's already read? They were useless. Even worse, in a 4-6 person class, there is no need to assign 2 discussion leaders. At this point, Ho's class was glorified book club, glorified because we're paying tuition for it. None of us actually knew anything about Chinese history to make an apt synthesis point or make an astute observation about trends of the past coming to the future. Comments were "it's so interesting" umm thank u, next? Most infuriating of all was the "how would you answer your own question" question. Prof. Ho, if you're reading this, for love of Cardinal Joseph Zen, I asked my question in the first place BECAUSE I DIDN'T HAVE AN ANSWER TO IT! Why on earth would I write a question I had an answer to? And why would I want to answer my own question? And she never failed to ask why a certain medium was good for studying. Idk, b/c it is? Like, I'm not learning anything from discussing the merits of participant observation for 45 minutes, but b/c this class is a waste of time anyway, might as well go with it. Don't even get me started on the papers, though. Incredibly unhelpful and condescending when you go for help, with half-assed preliminary commentary. Refusal to meet to discuss anything class related outside of the two office hours at very inconvenient times (she just won't meet at any other times. at all). Essay grading when there are only 10 students who wrote 5 page papers on the same things took 2 months, and then she used a rubric we didn't know existed. She sent two sample papers with students' names on them, too--way to inflate egos while making the rest of feel stupid and worthless. That's literally the worst possible thing you could do to tell someone how to write better. No thesis guidance. No argument guidance. Don't need outside research.. unless you want an A, or you want to do prompts 1, 2, 3--or wait, they're all the same prompt: create your own prompt after reading and watching the following things that were not on the syllabus! Spend an ungodly amount of time writing a 5pg paper to get back illegible feedback that says "improvement from last time!" or "you almost convinced me" or "I'm not sure you fully proved that" yes queen, give me absolutely nothing! What am I supposed to do with that feedback? Biggest letdown of 2020, let me tell you, and it had so much potential. Waste of time.
