Weakness - would not record review sessions which was unhelpful considering I used to look back on recorded lectures, disliked the cold-calling that Aaron had sometimes, discouraged me from attending class due to this anxiety since I do struggle with orgo work
 Strengths: adapted incredibly well to the online format. Virtual lectures were very helpful and easy to follow. Discussion sections were used to work through challenging problems that would resemble those seen on exams. Plenty of opportunities to work with TFs in office hours. Weaknesses: The pacing of this course was odd. I think too much time was spent on benzylic reactivity and related concepts, and far too little on carbonyl reactivity. I'm not sure whether or not it's necessary to include intro biochem stuff -- in talking with friends at other schools, it seems like learning about proteins/carbohydrates during organic was fairly unique (I did enjoy learning about these topics, but their inclusion may have contributed to the pacing issues). The NMR project included topics that had never been taught before, which made it a bit frustrating to work through. These are nitpicky complaints -- the strengths of this course far outweigh the weaknesses, especially relative to the previous semester (CHEM 220).
 Like most chem classes thus far, the exams are nothing like the p-sets and students are really sent into them completely blind and unprepared. The lectures and course itself are also poorly organized overall, and both could be improved significantly to help students figure out exactly what they need to learn and focus on.
 I can't think of a single strength for this course and I dreaded it every day this semester. The exams consistently took students more than 7 hours to complete and often contained information that we had never learned. The projects took over 10 hours to complete. The content taught in lecture oftentimes was centered on biochemistry and it was never clear which aspects were important or relevant to our understanding of organic chemistry.
 I found that course assignments (both the group projects and weekly Sapling assignments) aligned well with the course material and prepared us well for each examination. The abundance of review materials and practice problems was also helpful. Furthermore, the course material incorporated various concepts from both earlier and more advanced courses in the chemistry sequencethereby providing context. The weakest element of the course was its pacing. Lectures progressed through course content rather rapidly, and the exam schedule (consisting of three midterms and a two-part final) did not allow for much time between assessments, particularly at the end of the semester. While it is helpful that the lowest of the three midterms can be dropped, it may be beneficial to distribute the exams more evenly throughout the semester. Separately, while the group projects were incredibly formative, they took far longer than the teaching team estimated; either shortening the assignments or providing more time to do them would be helpful.
