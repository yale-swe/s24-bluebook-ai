2020-Fall ENGL 009 01 Literature of the Lonely

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 14727 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 BEAUTIFUL COURSE WITH BEAUTIFUL PEOPLE Professor Hickey is literally so kind and wonderful and articulate, and she helped foster a class environment where we could all connect and be heard. Readings were honestly often a delight (except Walden), and as a student of color, I felt like Professor Hickey created a diverse reading for the course and handled matters of race/gender/sexuality/class without ever feeling "fake woke" or insensitive. Rather, you could tell that she genuinely meant what she was saying and also had spent time learning and educating herself on all of these topics (without then trying to out-woke anyone or invalidate experiences). She is the type of teacher that if you say something makes you uncomfortable or if you need help/an extension, then she will actually listen and understand.
 My only qualm with this class was the similarity of essays. Paper 2 and 3 felt like I was assigned to do the same thing, and they were both pretty vague. I love having open topics, but sometimes, I think spending a little more class time homing in on a specific goal would be worthwhile. And that should go beyond just rehashing the prompt of the essay. That might be asking a lot because the essay prompts were well put and complete, but it's difficult to grasp what to do through just reading sometimes.
 Strengths: This course was incredibly fun to take, and I read genres of literature I had never read before. Professor Hickey is a wonderful teacher, and such a wonderful facilitator of discussion. She makes sure the class is a welcoming and encouraging place. The readings are reasonable every week, and the discussions we have about them go wherever we feel lead them as a class. The weekly discussion leader position was fun and relatively low stress. This course creates a very welcoming and friendly environment where I felt like I could learn and discuss what I felt, which is very important considering the personal nature of this course. Weaknesses: I cannot think of any large weaknesses, except that I would have an example for the papers. Professor Hickey is very open about what she is looking for, so it was not a large issue but I do wish I had an example to look at while I was writing.
 This course was awesome! Prof. Hickey is a great teacher! Maybe we could have used gather.town more?
 The way the course was structured was a definite strength. I loved how discussions were led by one student every class and the fact that these discussions centered around thoughtful questions about the text. This led to some really thought-provoking discussions and allowed for a conversation involving multiple perspectives on the text.
 This was an amazing class! Prof. Hickey understood the unique nature of being a student during a pandemic and provided for me, and I think also the rest of the class, a warm, open and engaging space to learn and also build friendships. Prof. Hickey took time to get to know all of her students and always engaged with the claims we made during class discussions. The texts chosen by Prof. Hickey were diverse and provoking. Among my favorite authors that we read were Claudia Rankine, Billy Ray-Belcourt and Emily Dickinson. As for weaknesses, I think Prof. Hickey could have made the connections between the texts we were reading and our class' focus on loneliness clearer. It would have been nice towards the end of the semester to have reviewed the texts we read and trace how our understanding of loneliness as a class had evolved from the first week of class to the last. Asides from that, I absolutely loved this class. This type of class is why I came to Yale. Especially during the pandemic, a class about loneliness was therapeutic for me. If you have a chance to take a class with Prof. Hickey, do it!
 Strengths: Closeness of students, the selection of students just worked perfectly and everything synced, promoting a collaborative, close-knit classroom environment. Keep it up in terms of choosing students that are the right fit. A very down-to-earth professor who encourages questions and discussions is also a big strength.
 Course was very engaging, syllabus was great, discussions were fruitful, not too many weaknesses.
 I think the virtual format is always difficult, but this class handled it well! I would've loved to spend more time on the readings though, I felt like sometimes we rushed through them.
 The "How were your day?" sessions at the beginning of each class were wonderful for getting comfortable with our classmates, which helped the flow of discussion later. The variety of assessment/project types were fantastic, breaking up the monotony of too many essays.
 This is an amazing first year seminar!! Professor Hickey is incredibly aware of our needs and feelings and not only addresses them but actively adapts our activities and curriculum to reflect that. We started every class with a check-in, which helped us not only just talk about ourselves a bit and get to know each other better, but also examine our private and public circles and how our readings' discussions on loneliness manifest in our lives. The readings, from a diverse selection of authors, were all very interesting and the breadth of material covered allowed us to understand loneliness through multiple lens. Often, they would reference each other, helping us to progressively build a more nuanced picture of loneliness. Our discussions were mainly led by us, but Professor Hickey would step in occasionally to add an interesting point, or give some more context, or introduce a new take. Our papers were also on broad prompts, but designed to elicit interesting and nuanced takes from us. My perception of loneliness and its importance changed so much from this class, and I don't think there were any weaknesses to mention.
 Strengths everything about this course. Weakness none.
