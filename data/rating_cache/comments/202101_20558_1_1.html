 Flexible online format
 Strengths: great Professor. He cares a lot about the class and the students. He also developed multiple ways of engaging with the class apart from the usual tools. For example, I liked how he used edstem.org to post questions. This made the course easier to follow.
 The class videos were far too long
 Topics toward the end of the class were not explained well. Student's should have more independence in learning R and there should be more of a focus on teaching statistics theory.
 I thought this course was very thorough and in depth. Professor Meyers made sure that he was clear and organized with all of the material and I appreciated that. I was frustrated more often than not with the course though. The Canvas quiz format with participation questions ensured that we were following along with lectures and understanding material, however, I saw no benefit from these survey questions. I received zero feedback on the work I did and found that these surveys resulted in lectures taking much longer than a lecture should in a normal live-lecture format. I wouldn't mind this if I received feedback for my work, however, with the lack of feedback I found I was just answering the questions just to answer them and really not processing information. The questions became an added pressure throughout the semester because they would hurt my participation grade if I didn't complete them. I think the questions had an adverse effect for me than what was intended and I hope Professor Meyers reevaluates using them in the future.
 This course was great! Professor Meyers was informative, caring, and genuinely wanted us to have an as-least-stressed-as-possible semester. The material was presented in a palatable way, allowing for a greater depth of understanding and ability to master the skills required in R to succeed in this course.
 Because of the asynchronous component of the class, I felt very disengaged. I found it difficult to make sure I was truly learning the material, even though I worked collaboratively with other students and asked questions on Ed Discussions.
 Strengths: The workload was not too rigorous, and the problem sets allowed for application of the lessons we learned in class. Weaknesses: The course as a whole was rather disorganized, and it did not seem to be structured to be conducive to student learning. The content was basic, which is understandable given this is the most introductory course in statistics, but at times, it felt like I was not really maximizing the opportunity to learn as a student. The midterm exam was scheduled the morning immediately after a break day, which was strictly advised against by the administration, and when asked on Ed, the teaching staff dismissed these concerns saying that the exam "had already been scheduled" (as had every single break day and the Yale academic calendar). Instead, the professor provided the option to take the exam two days early with less than 24-hours notice, the same day that our campus faced a major tragedy. Despite each Canvas quiz asking for student feedback, it felt as though nothing was changed with regards to the course, and frankly, I was immensely disappointed by the end of the semester. I wish I had better used 1 of my 36 credits at Yale.
