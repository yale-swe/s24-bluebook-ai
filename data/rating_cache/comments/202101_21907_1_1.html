Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

 Content is really interesting and presented well. Problem sets are challenging but give good preparations for exams. Lots of office hours make getting help easy.
 This course was taught in a relatively intuitive manner through examples and simple explanations which made me feel as though I was learning the material very well. Unfortunately, the pre-recorded lectures were often not very engaging but I think that was mostly due to the difficulty of staying focused for 90 minutes on a person on my computer screen.
 Strengths: Professor Steinacker. My favorite professor I've had at Yale so far. She is firm and sticks to her expectations, and yet so kind and genuinely cares about her students. She loves when you come and chat with her and really gets to know you. She cares about her students, frequently expressing concern about our well-being. At times in the semester, she removed questions from homeworks and decided to put less questions on exams than she had planned because she felt the stress level was high. She chose to make the exams 24-hour open-note because of the online learning environment and pandemic, which was the most wonderful decision any of my professors have made while teaching this last year. She loves to talk to you about your major and give advice on other courses to take. I wish I could take more courses with her! Weaknesses: None really. I would like if there could be office hours on the days that homeworks are due for last minute questions, as there are office hours every day besides the due-date days.
 The lectures in this course were very engaging and well-put together to help promote student understanding, but on occasion they could become mired in minutiae and fall behind the lesson plan detailed in the syllabus. The problem sets were an extremely appropriate level of difficulty, but as the lectures fell behind the problem sets increasingly were focused on content covered in lecture only shortly before the release of the problem sets themselves.
 One of my few complaints about this course is that everything was recorded lectures in terms of learning. There were office hours and such but I felt like through my learning experience I wish I could have interacted with the instructor more.
