2021-Spring MENG 363L 01 FluidMechancs&ThermodynmicsLab

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403-YCWR CRN: 20953 Term: 202101

Back12345Forward
 This is a terrible course. There are no strengths. Only weaknesses. The entire class needs to be critically reevaluated.
 This class requires you to think critically about experimental data, process, and assumptions. It definitely improves your knowledge of fluid mechanics and thermo in a very sink or swim type of way. That said, it is very time and energy consuming, so if you don't build your semester to have a lot of free time to devote to this class, you will sink.
 Strengths - I do feel like I learned a lot in this class about fluids, thermodynamics, and data analysis. Also Gomez and Kevin are good about giving students mostly constructive feedback on their work in a timely manner to make improvements Weaknesses - this class is...a lot. It is the hardest MechE class at Yale, and it's really intense. I feel like the time commitment and weekly expectations is just too much to handle. You are expected to write one lab report per week, which for me worked out to be about 3000 words plus a ton of data analysis that alone took 10ish hours. Essentially, I could easily expect anywhere between 15-25 hours of work to be done for this class outside of the weekly lecture and lab session. The few labs that Gomez gave a week extension for were great - there was more time to do the work, and I feel like I learned/understood more of the theoretical background during the weeks this happened. I think the class could be improved by reducing the number of lab reports so that one is done every other week. If, say, only F3-F5 were taught (the labs after F3 were my personal favorites and I feel like I learned the most/was more interested in those) then students would have more time to spend on each lab report and could get a lot more out of it.
 This course is fairly well structured to evaluate knowledge of fluid mechanics and thermodynamics, which wouldn't be a problem if we had previously covered any of the material we did labs on. However, most of the topics covered in these labs were either not covered in detail during MENG 361 or 211 or not covered at all, which meant that to answer the questions posed in the lab handouts required either substantial independent study or guessing.
 This course would be great If I actually knew thermodynamics. This course works on the assumption that all students have a through understanding of fluids and thermodynamic concepts which I think is a flawed approach. MENG 286L is better structured in my opinion since it provides a thorough understanding of the content that will be addressed in the class labs. Other than that the 383L labs are interesting and applicable, I just wish there was closer guidance of the students and less of an assumption that students know how to write a good report, analyze data, and understand the concepts. If this class is a writing credit then it should be less focused on the analysis of the lab and content of the report and more focused on the ay the student presents their work. I thinks 286L was overall a more well guided lab class that actually successfully taught me how to write a good lab report.
 Strengths: Alessandro and Kevin are very knowledgeable and, by the structure of the course, I learned a lot about fluid mechanics and thermodynamics. Weaknesses: There was far too much work, and since we were entirely virtual, we could not even do the hands-on labs that wouldve given us more insight into the experimental techniques that we used to collect data for the reports.
 This course tries to do too much in too little time. 10 labs in 10-11 weeks is simply not a feasible goal in a normal semester, let alone a virtual one marked by widespread student disengagement, burnout, trauma, etc.
 Strengths: This class pushes you in ways that previous MENG classes likely haven't. You may become a master at writing good lab reports, or you might barely tread water through the semester, but you sure as hell will learn how to write them fast for the sake of your sanity. So, it teaches you endurance. If we didn't have to get so technical and detailed, the labs would be more enjoyable because the concepts are cool. The professors did try at times to minimize the work we were doing with "ultra-short report formats", and the class has more resources available to students than in previous years. Weaknesses: Even with the adaptations made to some reports to be ultra-short bullet point format, the workload is killer. I have written more word content for this class than many people write on their thesis. Also, the depth of expectation and information you need to talk about is the worst part. I'm not worried about reaching the length, I'm worried if I'll have enough room to discuss everything I need to. Gomez is also hard to work with - you can't avoid it and you'll see for yourself. The best way to succeed in the class is to home in on what he's looking for in your reports by closely reading the rules and seeing what he focuses on in lecture.
 The workload is beyond absurd for the most part. Could be improved by reducing the number of labs to 7/8 and allowing more time and focus on individual reports. Also the lab handouts could be formatted in a more presentable and readable way
 This course requires a lot of work and a lot of proactiveness. I actually enjoy data analysis but there was just so much of it. I appreciate that this course was partially about application of concepts learned in MENG 361.
