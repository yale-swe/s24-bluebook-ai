Â I would highly recommend this course to any student who desires to take a challenging, fast-paced class in counterpoint, harmony, and form. The course opened my eyes to a whole new world of musical analysis that was largely absent from my general lesson studies. The class is very well taught, and by the end of the class, you will feel like you have learned a great deal about music theory and general music history. If you are planning to take this course, you should know that it is a lot of work, more than the typical course at Yale. Many nights were spent trying to find ideal solutions to counterpoint practice assignments and challenges, and the beginning of the course -- when you are trying to figure out how to use note processors, read different clefs, follow all the rules etc -- can be challenging. Do not be mistaken -- this is not a repeat of an AP music theory class. That being said, the course gets much easier as you practice more and more. Professor Harrison expects a lot from his students; however, the reward is deep understanding of the basic framework of music. It has changed the way I examine works of musical literature, and I very much enjoyed applying the learning to various pieces by Bach, Beethoven, and other Baroque/Classical composers. I highly recommend the class, just make sure that you have enough time in your schedule to allow for the workload it demands.
