2019-Summer SBS 505 01 Social/Behavioral Foundations

Q:

Looking back on this course, what is your overall assessment of the course: What are its strengths and weaknesses, and in what ways might it be improved?

ID: PH006 CRN: 30383 Term: 201902

Back1234Forward
 This course was conducive for the discussion of multiple controversial topics including politics and discriminations. The instructor acted a very good facilitator with leading great examples. As a class, we would have appreciated more rigorous historic structure to the course regarding the major milestone articles or figures in social behavioral sciences.
 Strengths - this course is the most important topic of public health. Made me think critically and reconsider some of my own opinions and open my eyes to things I hadn't previously considered. Lecturer great at stimulating interesting discussion. Areas of improvement - certain people in the class spoke a lot more than others, there wasn't much nomination of people to speak which I would have liked. Very poorly organised lecture slides - trying to make summaries of them was difficult. Would have liked lectures that were clear, concise, categorised with the ability to make good summaries of.
 Great course and the best teachers and best learning environment Strengths: Easy going class, camaraderie between teacher and students, great involvement Weaknesses- focus on race and American Black White disparities, there are international students who come from backgrounds with other disparities that also need to be focused on Would have liked a more international focus on each topic that was taught Especially welcomed the Teacher who came from another college and also Ms. Shannon for her approachability and her knowledge of the subject Enjoyed writing the op ed and the fact that there were not many assignments that took our time, so could devote the extra time to personal reading and keeping up.
 Strengths: It makes you think critically and think about different determinants of disease besides the usual pathway risk factor to outcome. It opens your mind to other important things in research and public health Weaknesses: I feel that most of the things taught were not applicable enough. The instructor provided useful examples of how the concepts were applicable to real life situations but I did not learn how to apply myself these concepts or how to integrate these concepts in my career as researcher. For my it was kind of abstract although useful and made me think differently
 The course has a lot of potential, however there are significant weaknesses. My main grievances with the course are: 1. Strong liberal political inclination 2. Failure to address multiple health disparities (e.g. rurality vs urbanity, veteran's status) with primary focus/emphasis on racism and socioeconomic status. 3. Lack of formal syllabus 4. Lack of in class discussion of lengthy assigned readings
 It was helpful to understand social and behavioral theories and research methods. However, I felt the topics discussed were quite broad and focused on public health issues in the United States. As an international student, it was sometimes difficult to understand the contexts of the issues and participate in the discussion. Still, the class discussion and assigned readings helped me to learn and critically think about various public health issues with the framework of social epidemiology.
 The course was certainly very engaging, and the professor did his very best to foster positive discussion. The topics were all very interesting and incredibly useful for the course. Some suggestions for improvement: creating more structured lectures as it was often hard to follow along the topic and difficult to draw back to the bigger picture of the course as a whole; allowing for more group activities and smaller written assignments in between; and being more transparent about the grading process for participation in class since that was not very clear.
 The course was well taught. The case studies were very helpful in relating coursework to real life situations. The only problem with the course was that the assigned readings were too long compared to available time.
 the use of videos (especially the Hill documentary) was an effective way to illustrate concepts and spark discussion. readings were at times long and very theory-heavy - probably could be streamlined
 Overall this course was a positive experience. It was thought provoking and was very beneficial to discuss with the professor and the class about these complex topics. The course was not clearly/logically laid out, did not have a defined structure or progression through material. Readings were not applicable to discussions many times. The major benefit to this class was the discussions and learning alternative perspectives on complex topics.
 This is a huge subject area so difficult to distill it into 6 weeks. Yusuf is passionate, experienced, and knowledgeable, and Shannon was a great facilitator. Weakness of the course was difficulty in ensuring I had covered the required material prior to the exams so a tad more structure and focused readings might be useful.
 I enjoyed the class, I just really wanted more discussion. I think it would have helped the material to come alive more for people. There was a strong need for talking out situations. It would have been great to hear different points of view on various issues.
 Super interesting class - talked about many interesting topics on how society and health interacts. Some of the topics really made me reflect on how I can decrease the health disparities through controlling social factors. I particularly liked the open discussion opportunities in every class session. It was an interesting learning experience to hear all types of opinions. I would like to have more summary slides at the end of each lecture. I felt that I understood the concept in class while talking about it, but when I needed to refer back to the slides to refresh, I was not able to recall the key words all the time. Having a summary slide including keywords and key concepts would have been really beneficial.
 It was a great course , there is enough time for discussion in almost every class . Always encourage every student to participate .
 This course could have been much more intellectually stimulating and informative than it was. Given that so many of us were taking the course after several years in the workforce or in health care training, there should have been more of a focus on experiences we already had and the relevance to our work. I also think a case-study method would have been much more instructive for a course like this one. Alternatively, a course that went into more detail on the history and terminology of the issues we were discussing would have been more useful. I feel like I came away having enjoyed some in-class discussions and the opportunity to think about certain social issues, but not having learned much new.
