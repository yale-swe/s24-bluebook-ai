Â As expected, this class moved very fast during the summer session. Strengths: 1) Engaging lectures (even if online) 2) CS50 shorts: great way to get more info on a specific concept I was struggling with 3) Enthusiastic staff: They made the class worth it. TA's checked in on students regularly, were always available to talk, and provided a lot of guidance!! I do have a lot of gripes with how the summer course that need to be fixed regardless of when it is taught: 1) Office Hours: they are a complete mess. I logged in at 9:03 AM and wasn't helped until 2:15 pm. This is absurd. There needed to be far more TA's for the class. Especially during the summer, when the material is moving so fast, students are going to need access to a lot of help and this wasn't even close to possible during the summer session. Like when I waited for 5 hours, I just had to sit by my computer because I had no idea when it would magically be my turn. Also, despite being told to not sign up for a spot in the "line" before the official start of office hours, TA's still helped the people who signed up, sometimes an hour before the start time. Furthermore, there was no protocol for what to do if students weren't helped by the end of a TA's office hours. They would have to sign back up and go to the back of the line for the next TA's office hours, which is incredibly unfair. 2) Starting in C. I feel like this made no sense. Python is a much simpler program to grasp, and would have led to a much smoother transition from Scratch. Also, by starting with C and then going to Scratch, a lot of students felt like they were over thinking things and inserting brackets and notation we no longer needed. Instead of building our base of knowledge, we had to completely forget most of what we did for C in order to successfully use Python.
