I think beginning the course with a a few lessons on the historical background of South Africa would be invaluable to our understanding of the texts. Often, we'd be referencing events like the Boer War, or the Afrikaaner Trek from East Africa, or the arrival of the Afrikaaners in the 17th century, and I'd feel a bit out of my depths. Also, though this is something Steph mentioned a few times that she should have been better about, I think most of the novels we read this semester were by white authors. I was hoping to have read a few more works by Black African authors discussing Black South African life, as those were the ones that were often most engaging/insightful. I don't think this is usually the case, or will be the case in future iterations of the course, but reading from more Black (as well as "coloured" South Africans mixed race Black people, as well as South Asians, East Asians, etc.) would have provided a better view of South African society. Overall though, this course was definitely a highlight of this semester, and I truly felt like I was reaping the rewards of all the work I put in.
