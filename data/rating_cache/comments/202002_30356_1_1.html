2020-Summer ASTR S120 02 Galaxies and the Universe

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30356 Term: 202002

Back123Forward
 There is a lot of material that needs to be learned, but the textbook readings were very detailed and useful. During class, there could have been more interaction and involvement, but overall, it was a decent class.
 Strengths: Good textbook, passionate teacher, well organised (with class instructions available) Weakesses:
 Strengths: Professor Zinn is excellent, lectures were recorded which was helpful, weekly quizzes helped to ensure no one slacks off, PSETs were relevant to the material covered in class and looked like the final exam, structure of the course was very well-thought-out (in a manner that facilitated understanding) Weaknesses: the readings assigned were too much; considering the pace of the summer session, it was nearly impossible to get the readings done in time, especially for people juggling 2 YSS classes or an internship with this class. It would have been ideal if we were assigned excerpts that were important. Also, the Wednesday deadline to turn in the PSET was too rushed, especially for students who can't get support in a discussion section because of clashes with other classes/internships or time zone differences, and so need more time to engage with the material and try the PSET problems.
 I think the problem sets, quizzes, and final exam were structured really well and did a good job covering the important information and concepts. I liked the polls, but they got less frequent towards the end of the course and they were a good level of engagement and participation in class and I wish they had continued to be incorporated.
 I felt like the questions on the homework sets were one of the most helpful parts of the course. Really helped to solidify the fundamentals of each chapter/lesson. A couple of the lectures could run a little ling and certain subjects are a little dry, but overall good class.
 I gotta say I was NOT excited when I saw the incredibly dense schedule of classes, but in the end I was really glad we got to spend so much time learning and discussing, and I'm also amazed by the professor and TFs' willingness to be present and engaged for every second of it!
 The course is a manageable science credit for non-STEM majors. The content was interesting, and the calculations were not too hard, even for someone who hates math. The schedule of the quizzes and homework made for a helpful structure. I stopped doing the textbook readings after the first week and just looked to the textbook for clarification while doing the homework, because the content in class seemed repetitive of the textbook, and class discussions were the basis of our assessments. The short breaks in the middle of the class helped me to stay focused.
 If you are genuinely curious about astronomy and are willing to read the material from the textbook and beyond, I think this is a good course because the professor is extremely knowledgable and can explain any phenomenon in depth. However, I found the lectures to be a little bit incoherent in a sense that I often observed how some foundational and basic building blocks in our discussions were ommited by the professor during the lectures, which I am sure made the comprehension of this new material more difficult for people who took their first astro class. I often wondered "How did we arrive to this conversation? Did we even define what the phenomenon at hand was?". If you read the textbook in advance (as you are actually meant to do), there will be no issue with this since lectures build on the material very well, but without doing the reading in the textbook in advance, you might feel lost from the lectures alone, which is not great for people who just want to fulfill their SC requirement. But if you do the readings, the professor's lecture will add depth to what is said in the textbook, and you will have no problem. Just start doing all the readings from the very beginning. Besides that, the professor and the TAs were really nice and were always willing to help.
 The strength of the course is that its really fascinating and the material is really cool. The classs weakness is that it just covers too much material for an introductory course.
 There was nothing in particular that I can say are really strong or weak about this course, but the only thing I think should be reconsidered is the misleading course name: 'Galaxies and the Universe". There should at least be some mention of an introduction to astrophysics, and a much greater emphasis on the mathematics part of this course; I did not mind that there was math involved, but a few other students have also mentioned during study group session that they were surprised by the ratio of math to concepts in both quiz and homework assignments. I also think that Canvas should be updated; like every other course, the day to day agenda tends to get off track. However, this was never updated (and we were a few days behind for a while), but the textbook readings were never moved back so it kept us all guessing which class corresponded with which chapter/class coverage.
 Great professor. Great topic. Not enough time.
 All of the lecture material was presented in detailed and clear PowerPoint presentations that were easy to follow and very helpful for reviewing. Prof. Zinn was a really good professor. He seems to know a bit of everything with regards to astronomy, whether it be the history of the field or the current attempts at solving the dark matter problem. The only weakness of this course is a lack of depth with regards to the extent certain topics were covered. This is very much an introductory course and I almost wish that some of the physics we learned was more advanced.
 I really liked this course, although the lectures could be dry at times.
