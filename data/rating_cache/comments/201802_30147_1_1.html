2018-Summer ITAL S140 01 Intermediate Italian II

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

ID: SU103 CRN: 30147 Term: 201802

Back123Forward
 The structure of the course strongly facilitated learning and was very organized. Lessons we long and on the daily, but the demand to speak for a least a few hours a day helped to advance my skills in the best manner possible. The amount of homework was overwhelming for a summer abroad and a lighter-load would have allowed for a more enjoyable international experience, however I belief the homework was helpful for our studies. The course was designed to be taught by two professors and I really appreciated the class time being split between them. This switch created a different feel in the classroom and was helpful for maintaining focus for such a longtime.
 This class was very well organized and taught. I would only change one thing and that would be to reduce the amount of online workbook assignments before tests and instead add more review sheets for us to do by hand similar to those in class since they are more clear and similar to the test format.
 I liked the structure of having two instructors, as it broke up the 3 hours of class time, but I wish it could have been more of a seminar-style.
 It was very engaging all throughout the term. Not only did the professors help with the material, but they were able to answer questions that may not have been included in the textbook but still pertained to the class.
 Like the L3 course, the only weakness I could think of was the online workbook and one or two mistakes in the textbook.
 I really loved almost everything about the course. The exercises were helpful, the teaching was great, and I feel like I really cemented in my mind what I had learned during the past year in Italian L1 and L2. I also really enjoyed the essays and "zibaldoni", which helped me remember my time in Italy even as I grew more familiar with vocabulary and how things are said in Italian (idioms, uses of words, etc.). The detailed corrections were incredibly helpful. We also, even though this was not the culture class, were immersed in the culture to an extent through Italian music, the reading of Bocaccio, etc. I did dislike the online workbook: not only is it extremely expensive, but it is not nearly as helpful as the pen-and-paper exercises we do, which we check in class. With these latter we have the chance to ask questions about what we don't understand, while with the online workbook you are left in the lurch.
 Strengths: met every day, good environment
 The course was well-organized and structured so the class was very informative and interactive. Although class was lengthy, the structure kept me engaged. As it progressed, so did the ease in speaking and writing the language, but there was always more to learn.
 Fast learning but fortunately not a lot of homework because class time was long
