2020-Fall GLBL 101 01 Gateway to Global Affairs

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

(Your anonymous response to this question may be viewed by Yale College students, faculty, and advisers to aid in course selection and evaluating teaching.)

ID: YC403 CRN: 12771 Term: 202003

Back12345Forward
 Strengths: Very broad range of topics covered, great experience with TFs, consistent interaction with peers, encouragement to be engaged in class, variety of ideas and understandings welcome, diverse lecturers and journalists in materials Weakness: Not all students did the readings each week because there was no way to verify if they had been done; weekend homework was almost overwhelming if I did all the readings, since my reflections were due Tuesday 4 am and materials weren't posted until 5 pm the preceding Fridays.
 The discussion sections were an amazing resource of the course.
 There could be more organization, but it was well taught.
 I'd like to have a wider variety of readings. Sometimes, it felt like we dedicated A LOT of time to Middle East but skimmed over many other areas of the world.
 The section on the Middle East (roughly the first half of the course) is very strong. Professor Sky's experiences make this the highlight of the class. However, the rest of the class consisted of online videos and articles.
 It was very difficult to remain engaged in the course after the professor stopped posting virtual lectures and instead posted Youtube documentaries. We also only had the privilege of speaking directly to our professor two times this semester. The level of reading was very manageable and engaging, however, and I highly recommend trying to get into a small discussion section. The midterm was particularly frustrating because I read the professor's book twice, yet still found the prompts difficult to decipher and adequately answer.
 Strengths: spectacular lectures from Professor Sky and excellent discussions in section. Impactful readings Weaknesses: lack of consistency as semester went on. Sky's lectures stopped and eventually lectures stopped altogether. I really missed these later in the semester. They were a highlight of my experience at Yale thus far.
 A strength is that the course content was interesting and professor brought fascinating real world experience. A weakness is that for the second half of the semester we didn't even have lectures. We would just watch youtube videos and read articles.
 The course was very strong in getting students engaged with discussions. A lot of emphasis on the middle east so maybe more time spent on other events
 Strengths: manageable workload; passionate TAs and professor regarding the topics we talk about in class; both a strength and a weakness but putting giving students vague guidelines and expectations allowed us to have some creativity with our work Weaknesses: not enough guidance for the students, kind of just like "here's the work, do it". as a first-year, this was kind of difficult; rules and expectations were kind of vague; extremely disliked the "wow factor" as part of the midterm rubric --> i do not believe that 4 out of 20 points of our midterm papers should be part of our grade. If anything a "wow factor" should only be a bonus point or two. I also felt like most of the information we learned in this class could've been done on our not, not necessarily at Yale. Not really a weakness, but I think the students just really wished we could meet with the professor more.
 The course content is very engaging. However, only interacted with Professor Sky three times this semester: her greeting during the first section, her book discussion, and during my final presentation. I would've appreciated seeing more of her much more.
 We had a weekly discussion section, in which we talked about the lecture and readings. The discussions were informative and allowed me to hear and understand different perspectives. I think the course was disproportionally focused on the Middle East, as we devoted more than half of the semester to affairs in the Middle East.
 The breadth of topics is both a strength and weakness as it was all very interesting and great to see how everything connects, but it also meant that lectures were very long and there were a LOT of readings every week. It was kind of hard to keep up! Loved the final project
 Some strengths of this course were the amount of information presented and the opportunity to hear from guest lecturers and people at the Jackson Institute. However, this course was heavily skewed on the political spectrum and the required articles for weekly reading lacked variety in political thought and bias. In addition, some of the information presented in recorded lectures was either historically inaccurate or left out crucial details when attempting to retell history, particularly in modules on the MENA region. This created an echo chamber of a discussion section and likely provided students with an inaccurate account of history on certain conflicts or events.
 TA was very good at facilitating discussion sections
 This being a survey course brings unavoidable weaknesses such as the speed at which material is covered and the lack of depth that results from that. However, there's not really a way to change that. That being said, I think this course found a good balance since the Middle East was extensively covered while the other topics were quickly skimmed.
 The first half was good. I really enjoyed Prof. Sky's lectures. As soon as she stopped giving it the lectures it went downhill. Watching a youtube seminar or something wasn't as good as what she could've done. I really liked her history lessons about the middle east conflicts as well as her book, so I wish there was more of that.
 The global issues we learned about were very well selected!
 Strengths - Section was fun and a new topic was covered each section Weaknesses - Could be too broad and lectures fell off at the end of the course.

 This course's subject matter was fascinating--I only wish that Prof. Sky was more involved as her viewpoint is incredibly unique and valuable.
