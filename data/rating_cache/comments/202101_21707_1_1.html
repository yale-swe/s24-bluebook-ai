2021-Spring CHEM 251L 01 Inorganic Chemistry Laboratory

Q:

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this course and how could it be improved?

Â Strengths - This class is well-organized, teaching you lab and writing techniques you'll use in your future as a scientist. I enjoyed the labs themselves (lots of pretty colors), I enjoyed the lab lectures (lots of fun science trivia), and I enjoyed writing the reports and digging deep into the literature. The written feedback on reports is incredibly thorough, thoughtful, and helpful, pointing out what you did well and where you could improve in the future. I grew a lot as a writer. Weaknesses - Experiment 3 Report was one of the most difficult things I've ever put together (and this is including pchem lab reports). I think this report needs to be split up into 2 separate lab reports or require less data analysis. Or maybe more context needs to be given in the packet or in lecture. It was a lot to have to learn both how to use the computational clusters and also to figure out Tanabe-Sugano diagrams, not to mention that the workload overall was already x4 just bc of the sheer number of complexes we made and had to talk about. I'm proud of this report, and I learned a lot, but it was easily x4 the amount of hours put into any other report in this class.
Â The major strength is in learning how to write a lab report in the ACS style, which is far more complex and time consuming than typical lab reports for non-advanced classes. The major weakness was the lack of clear information in the syllabus. For all of the writing assignments in the course, there was no information about them or very little information given until they were about to happen. What the writing class consists of should be clearly demarcated at the beginning of the term so students know whether they want to take this class WR or not. The deadlines often changed for all assignments, but the communication was never made clear in advance. I also think Friday afternoon lecture is perhaps not the best time to have an engaging lecture with students, especially in a zoom format.
Â The strengths are the structure of these experiments. It is evident that these have all been carefully chosen and crafted by Professor Parr. I think lab 3 could be improved by being more explicit in what was required. I recall being overwhelmed by all the information we had to analyze and trying to write its narrative. A weakness is that there were also times when Professor Parr assumed I knew concepts but too much time had passed since I had learned them or I never actually did. Overall though, probably the best lab class I have taken at Yale.
